<h2 id="all-will-fall-oil-rig-guide-core-rules">
  All Will Fall Oil Rig guide: the core rules that make this scenario different
</h2>

Oil Rig looks like a normal survival start for the first few minutes, but it is actually a **resource conversion puzzle**.

Your real loop is:

- gather just enough basics to stabilize the colony
- unlock access to **upper metal**, **tools**, and blocked routes
- conserve **fuel** for the raise
- start the raise before the map takes away too much safe space
- repeat without overbuilding

That is why this scenario punishes three habits especially hard:

- building broad decorative neighborhoods too early
- spending fuel casually on non-raise uses
- using edge tiles like ordinary interior land

### The edge-tile rule

One of the most useful practical points in Oil Rig is that the **edge of the structure is premium real estate**.

You want those sea-facing edges for:

- fishing structures
- docks
- catchers / blockers for floating resources
- later boat-based collection

So the best Oil Rig layouts usually keep:

- **housing, lab, and storage in the interior**
- **utility and sea collection on the edge**

That one rule alone makes a big difference in how clean your midgame feels.

  All Will Fall Oil Rig best build order
</h2>

If your goal is a stable first raise, this is the cleanest early route based on the transcripted run:

### Step 1: place Storage and Food Storage first

The first storage unit shown has **200 capacity**, which is enough to let your workers stop wasting time on tiny hand-carry loops and start accumulating the opening junk, wood, food, and water efficiently.

### Step 2: build the Small Lab as soon as possible

The **Small Lab costs 20 junk**. That is the first exact tech gate shown in the run, and it matters because Oil Rig gets much easier the moment your opening stops being pure manual gathering.

### Step 3: rush Demolition Hut research

This is the most important early unlock shown in the route. The reason is simple: visible resources are not the same thing as **accessible** resources.

Demolition Hut lets you start clearing the blockers that are sitting between you and:

- upper metal
- tool caches
- crates
- routes that later support cranes or better gather tools

The exact research cost is **not visible in the transcript**, so do not publish a hard number for it unless you have direct gameplay confirmation.

### Step 4: build only the shortest walkways you need

Do not sprawl. Use short walkways and stairs only to:

- touch the first food and water nodes
- reach junk that keeps research moving
- open the first route toward metal

This is where most failed Oil Rig starts go wrong. Players start building for comfort instead of for **access**.

### Step 5: make a compact housing block instead of a wide settlement

A tight housing block is better because it:

- uses fewer materials
- shortens travel
- leaves edge tiles free
- supports the Rain Catcher plan later

### The practical raise rule

Start the raise when these are true:

- you already stabilized your absolute basics
- you have a route to keep gathering while the timer runs
- waiting longer is more likely to cost access than to create value

### What usually goes wrong

The transcript shows exactly the kind of failure pattern that makes Oil Rig punishing:

- a raise is started
- a complication delays the completion
- the player misses a stockpile target by a tiny margin
- loyalty drops because the economy was balanced too tightly

That is why the right mindset is:

- do not build a fancy base first
- do not hold fuel back for vanity systems
- do not wait until the last safe moment to start the raise

### A simple early raise checklist

Before you press the raise button, ask:

- do I already have the **40 metal**?
- do I really have spare **fuel**, or am I pretending I do?
- am I about to lose routes to tools, lower debris, or support space if I wait?
- can the colony survive the next 24 hours without a panic rebuild?

If the answers are mostly yes, raise.

<h2 id="oil-rig-mistakes-to-avoid">
  Oil Rig mistakes to avoid
</h2>

### Building wide instead of building useful

Every extra platform, bridge, and decorative extension costs materials, adds clutter, and can make later platform raises less forgiving.

### Wasting the shoreline

If you spend your best sea-facing edge tiles on random houses or unnecessary structures, your fishing and boat options get worse later.

### Delaying Demolition Hut logic

You do not beat Oil Rig by staring at resources. You beat it by opening routes to them.

### Treating Rain Catcher as a vague water building

The reason the Rain Catcher is good here is **not** that it exists. It is that the transcript shows a **specific roof-fed layout** hitting **500% efficiency**.

### Pushing population before food is ready

The run repeatedly shows how quickly food becomes the next crisis once housing and recruitment start working.

### Ignoring political recovery after a near miss

A failed stockpile or a delayed raise can cost loyalty fast. If you recover the economy but ignore the people layer, your colony still slows down.
