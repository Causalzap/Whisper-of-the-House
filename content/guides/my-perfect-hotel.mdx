## Top 8 My Perfect Hotel Tips and Tricks for Faster Progression

These are the most useful strategies to prioritize in the early to mid-game to avoid common beginner traps.

### 1. Upgrade Existing Rooms Before Unlocking New Ones
This is the biggest beginner mistake. **Upgrading rooms you already own is often cheaper and more profitable than rushing to unlock too many new rooms at once.** Before saving up massive amounts of cash to unlock Room 4 or 5, ensure Rooms 1-3 are fully maxed out on bed, TV, and bathroom upgrades. Higher-level rooms yield significantly more cash per stay, making your overall cash flow much easier to manage.

### 2. Hire Staff Early and Keep Upgrading Them
Good staff does more than save your thumbs from running around; it directly improves your hotel cycle. Upgrading a cleaner from Level 1 to Level 2 might cost a chunk of your early earnings, but the reduced room downtime pays for itself in less than 10 minutes of active play. 

### 3. Fix Bottlenecks Instead of Upgrading Evenly
If guests are always waiting in the lobby, improve reception speed first. If rooms stay unavailable for too long after checkout, prioritize cleaning staff. If guests take too long to move between floors, look at elevator speed. **Always invest your cash into the specific choke point that is slowing the whole hotel down.**

### 4. Save Diamonds for High-Impact Upgrades
Diamonds are the premium currency, so spend them wisely. Save them for upgrades that permanently improve efficiency, such as:
* Permanent movement speed boosts for your character.
* Key facility upgrades that shorten global waiting times.
Try not to waste diamonds on small, short-term cash injections. You will always regret that later when high-tier upgrades demand them.

### 5. Watch Ads Only When the Reward is Worth It
Time is money. If an ad gives you **the VIP Guest, temporary extra staff, or a massive flying cash drop**, it is highly worth taking. If the reward is a minor cash bump, skip it. A simple rule: **watch ads only when they help you immediately break through your current bottleneck.**

### 6. The Lobby Matters More Than You Think
The lobby is not just for decoration. It affects guest waiting comfort and your overall hotel flow. Don't ignore the vending machines or seating areas; passive income generated from the lobby is a lifesaver when you are saving up for expensive room expansions.

### 7. Complete Daily Missions and Limited Events
Daily tasks are often one of the easiest ways to farm extra cash and diamonds. They help you level up faster and unlock your next stage of growth earlier. Always check the mission tab before you start manually running around the hotel.

### 8. Expand at a Controlled Pace
Management games become chaotic when you unlock too much content before your staff can handle it. A better rhythm is:
1. Make your current area stable and fully upgraded.
2. Expand to a new room or floor.
3. Immediately reinforce staff (hire a new cleaner or receptionist) to handle the new workload.

## Overcoming Progress Walls

Most early slowdowns come from one of these three problems:

* **Guests are waiting too long:** That usually means your reception flow is too slow. Check front desk speed first.
* **Rooms are empty but no one is entering:** This is almost always a cleaning issue. Grab the mop yourself, or hire more cleaners immediately.
* **You are always short on money:** Stop expanding. Go back and max out the internal upgrades of your existing rooms to rebuild your cash flow per check-out.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### How to Get Money Fast in My Perfect Hotel?
The fastest way to earn money in My Perfect Hotel is to max out your current room upgrades (beds, TVs, bathrooms) before opening new rooms,then keep cleaners upgraded so turnover never stalls.

### What to Spend Diamonds on in My Perfect Hotel?
Use diamonds on permanent account upgrades that improve your overall management efficiency, such as character movement speed or crucial facility unlocks that noticeably speed up the hotel flow. Avoid exchanging diamonds for regular cash.

### Should I unlock new rooms or upgrade existing ones first?
In 90% of cases, **upgrade existing rooms first**. Only open more rooms when your current setup is already running smoothly, fully staffed, and highly profitable.

### Are the in-game ads worth watching?
Yes, but be selective. Ads that grant temporary extra staff, double income buffs, or VIP guest drops are incredibly valuable. Small flat-cash rewards are usually a waste of your time.
