<h2 id="cooper-guide-overview">
  Rogue Core Cooper Guide Overview
</h2>

Cooper is one of the most important tools for solo players in Deep Rock Galactic: Rogue Core.

It replaces the empty feeling of playing alone by helping with combat, mining, target pressure, and upgrade scaling. The key difference is that Cooper is tied into Rogue Core’s run system. It can receive Cooper-specific upgrades, and in the right situation it can turn a risky solo floor into a much cleaner one.

This page is not a general solo guide. It answers the Cooper questions players actually search for:

- how to command Cooper,
- how Cooper upgrades appear,
- what to do with Expenite Gems,
- when to use Cooper for mining,
- when to use Cooper for focus fire,
- and which classes benefit most from Cooper.

If you want the full solo route plan, read the <Link href="/deep-rock-galactic/solo-guide">Rogue Core solo guide</Link>. If you are still new to the whole game loop, start with the <Link href="/deep-rock-galactic/beginner-guide">beginner guide</Link>.

<h2 id="what-is-cooper">
  What Is Cooper in Rogue Core?
</h2>

Cooper is the helper drone you get when playing solo.

It can fight, assist with tasks, and receive upgrades during the run. Think of Cooper as a run tool, not a full teammate. It is strongest when you give it specific jobs: mine that Expenite, shoot that dangerous target, help me finish this deposit, or reduce pressure while I move.

The biggest beginner mistake is letting Cooper act in the background and never giving it commands.

  How to Command Cooper
</h2>

Use your **laser pointer** to direct Cooper.

This is the practical answer to “how do you use Cooper?” Point at the thing you want Cooper to help with. In solo, that usually means one of two jobs:

- point at a mineral deposit, especially awkward or out-of-reach Expenite,
- or point at a dangerous enemy so Cooper helps focus fire.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to command Cooper in Rogue Core solo runs."
  columns={commandColumns}

The rule is simple:

**Command Cooper onto things that improve the run right now.**

A high-threat enemy matters. An Expenite deposit matters. A route-critical task matters. A marked target matters if your build benefits from focus fire. A harmless straggler or low-value mineral usually does not, especially when the aggression meter is already climbing.

<h2 id="best-cooper-upgrades">
  Best Cooper Upgrades to Pick
</h2>

There is no single best Cooper upgrade for every run.

Pick the upgrade that fixes your solo bottleneck.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to choose Cooper upgrades during solo runs."

For example, a **rocket radius** upgrade is better when grouped enemies interrupt mining or deposits. A **damage** upgrade is better when Cooper is already shooting the right targets but not helping enough. A **reliability** upgrade can beat raw damage if Cooper keeps wasting pressure on the wrong things.

</h2>

If you find an **Expenite Gem**, deposit it immediately.

This is one of the most important Cooper-related solo tips. A normal Expenite deposit pushes you toward the next upgrade. An Expenite Gem is much stronger because it can trigger an **Epic-tier upgrade for both you and Cooper** at the same time.

That makes it a major power spike.

Do not carry it around while farming small rewards. Bank it, take the power, then use that stronger setup to clear the next fight or floor.

Cooper is especially useful for awkward mining.

If an Expenite deposit is out of reach, high on a wall, or sitting in a bad position, use the laser pointer and send Cooper to mine it. This keeps you free to watch enemies, hold a safer position, or prepare the next move.

The key is not “mine everything.”

The key is “mine what changes the run.”

Good Cooper mining targets:

- Expenite,
- Expenite Gem routes,
- mineral deposits that are hard to reach,
- deposits near enemies you do not want to face directly,
- and resources that help trigger the next upgrade before the floor gets worse.

The power cable is your route anchor in Rogue Core.

A simple solo pattern works well:

1. Follow the cable until you understand the floor.
2. Leave the cable for Expenite, an Expenite Gem, a Workbench, a Bio Booster, or a crate you need.
3. Command Cooper onto the useful task.
4. Return to the cable before the floor turns into a time sink.

Cooper makes side tasks easier, but the power cable keeps the run disciplined.

<h2 id="cooper-vs-bosco">
  Cooper vs Bosco
</h2>

Players coming from Deep Rock Galactic naturally compare Cooper to Bosco.

That comparison is useful, but it is not exact.

Bosco in Deep Rock Galactic helps you complete a mission by responding to pings, mining, fighting, lighting areas, and reviving you in solo. Cooper fills the solo-helper role too, but Rogue Core ties that helper more directly into the run system.

The practical difference:

**Bosco helps you complete a mission. Cooper helps you scale a solo run.**

Cooper can receive upgrades through R.E.P.D. / processor drone choices and Bio Booster events, and Expenite Gems can power up both you and Cooper. That means Cooper does not feel like a static background helper. It becomes part of your solo build as the run develops.

That is why Cooper upgrades, Expenite Gems, and laser pointer commands matter so much.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

<h3 id="what-is-cooper-in-rogue-core">
  What is Cooper in Rogue Core?
</h3>

Cooper is the solo helper drone in Deep Rock Galactic: Rogue Core. It can help with combat, mining, focus fire, and solo task flow, and it can receive Cooper-specific upgrades during the run.

<h3 id="how-do-you-command-cooper">
  How do you command Cooper?
</h3>

Use your laser pointer. Point Cooper at useful mineral deposits, out-of-reach Expenite, or high-threat enemies you want it to focus.

<h3 id="can-cooper-mine-expenite">
  Can Cooper mine Expenite?
</h3>

Yes. Use the laser pointer on Expenite deposits, especially ones that are awkward, out of reach, or unsafe to mine yourself.

<h3 id="does-cooper-get-upgrades">
  Does Cooper get upgrades?
</h3>

Yes. Cooper upgrade options can occasionally appear during R.E.P.D. / processor drone upgrades or Bio Booster events in solo runs.

<h3 id="what-does-an-expenite-gem-do-for-cooper">
  What does an Expenite Gem do for Cooper?
</h3>

An Expenite Gem can trigger an Epic-tier upgrade for both you and Cooper. Deposit it immediately instead of carrying it through more danger.

<h3 id="what-are-the-best-cooper-upgrades">
  What are the best Cooper upgrades?
</h3>

The best Cooper upgrade depends on what is hurting your run. Rocket radius helps against groups, damage helps kill pressure, reliability helps focus fire, and utility helps mining or solo task flow.

<h3 id="is-cooper-like-bosco">
  Is Cooper like Bosco?
</h3>

Cooper fills a similar solo-helper role, but it is built for Rogue Core's run-based structure. Cooper upgrades, R.E.P.D. choices, Bio Boosters, and Expenite Gems make it feel more tied to the roguelite loop.

<h3 id="can-cooper-carry-solo-runs">
  Can Cooper carry solo runs?
</h3>

No. Cooper helps a lot, but solo still depends on your route, upgrade choices, Expenite timing, positioning, and when you leave the floor.

<h3 id="what-class-is-best-with-cooper">
  What class is best with Cooper?
</h3>

Guardian is the safest class to pair with Cooper for new solo players. Slicer has the higher aggressive ceiling, and Falconer is a flexible option with extra pressure but less solo revive value.
