<h2 id="solo-guide-overview">
  Rogue Core Solo Guide Overview
</h2>

Deep Rock Galactic: Rogue Core works solo, but solo is not just an easier version of co-op.

The game reduces enemy health and baseline spawn pressure for one player, but it does not remove the real solo problem: **the timer keeps moving while every job is yours**. You mine alone, deposit alone, pick up objects alone, choose upgrades alone, command Cooper alone, and defend the elevator alone.

That is why solo often feels harder than the numbers suggest. In a squad, one player can mine while the others defend. In solo, even a simple Expenite deposit can become dangerous if the aggression meter is already climbing.

This guide focuses on solo-specific decisions:

- why solo pressure feels different,
- which Reclaimers work best alone,
- why Falconer needs a warning,
- how Retcon can work for experienced players,
- which upgrades matter most,
- how to use Cooper commands,
- how to pace each floor,
- and how to survive elevator defenses.

If you are still learning the basic game loop, read the <Link href="/deep-rock-galactic/beginner-guide">Rogue Core beginner guide</Link> first. If your main question is Cooper, use the <Link href="/deep-rock-galactic/cooper-guide">Cooper guide</Link>.

The enemies may scale down, but the run still pressures you forward. That creates a bottleneck that does not exist in the same way in a full squad.

<h2 id="guardian-solo">
  Guardian Solo: Safest First Pick
</h2>

Guardian is the safest starting point because it gives you time before a mistake becomes fatal.

Use **Concussive Barrage** before enemies reach you, not after you are already surrounded. A good stun window lets you reload, mine the last bit of Expenite, repair the elevator generator, or create space for a clean retreat.

Use **Repulsion Field** when enemies are already too close and you need immediate breathing room. In solo, this is often stronger than it looks because there is no teammate peeling enemies away from you.

Use **Armor Beacon** before chip damage becomes health damage. If you keep waiting until your armor is already gone and the room is collapsing, Guardian starts feeling weaker than it should.

For upgrades, prioritize:

- longer stun duration,
- more Concussive Barrage value,
- Repulsion Field uptime,
- Armor Beacon cooldown, duration, or radius,
- and damage against stunned enemies only when your weapon can use the control window.

Guardian does not need to clear the fastest. It needs to keep your run stable enough to finish.

<h2 id="slicer-solo">
  Slicer Solo: Highest Aggressive Ceiling
</h2>

Slicer is strong solo because it can turn enemy density into momentum.

Slice deals heavy melee damage, and Slicer gets much better when kills chain together. Each confirmed kill inside an active chain increases the payoff of later hits, which is why Slicer can feel awkward in sparse rooms but overwhelming in dense waves.

Upgrade around that identity. Slice damage helps start chains faster. Kill-chain duration and shorter chain trigger windows make the class more forgiving. Defensive Fighting-style upgrades and melee safety windows matter because you are choosing to fight close.

The rule is simple:

**Enter with a chain plan, leave with an exit plan.**

<h2 id="retcon-solo">
  Retcon Solo: High-Ceiling Timing Pick
</h2>

Retcon is not the easiest solo class, but it has real solo potential.

Time Rewind can act like a planned escape, a pseudo-revive, or a way to undo a risky push if you set it up before the danger. That makes Retcon valuable for experienced players who can predict when a room is about to turn bad.

Use Retcon well by setting your rewind state while you still have armor, ammo, and a safe position. Then take the risk. If the push fails, roll back before it becomes a wipe.

Retcon is weak when played as a late panic button. It is strong when played as a planned reset.

<h2 id="falconer-solo">
  Falconer Solo: Playable, But Do Not Pick It for Revive Value
</h2>

Falconer is where solo advice needs a caveat.

In co-op, Falconer's remote revive is a major strength because it can save a downed teammate without forcing you to cross the room. In true solo, that signature value disappears. You can still use Falconer for drone pressure and electric damage, but you are not getting the class's biggest team tool.

That does not make Falconer unplayable. It means Falconer is not the safest solo recommendation if your goal is survival.

Pick Falconer solo if you like:

- drone pressure,
- electric damage,
- active ability management,
- and flexible ranged fights.

Avoid Falconer solo if you wanted a safety net. For that, Guardian is easier and Retcon has more planned recovery potential.

<h2 id="armor-mechanics">
  Armor Matters More in Solo
</h2>

Armor is your warning layer.

When armor is gone, every mistake becomes more dangerous. Do not assume armor will simply fix itself over time, and do not assume every supply source will cover the problem. In solo, losing armor often means losing your chance to interact safely with Expenite, Workbenches, Bio Boosters, or the elevator generator.

That is why defensive upgrades are not boring in solo.

Take armor recovery, damage reduction, suit ability immunity, or health-protection effects when the run is unstable. If you keep taking health damage, a Damage Dam-style effect can be stronger than another damage roll because it stops one bad hit from becoming the end of the run.

The aggression meter is the easiest way to tell when a floor is becoming too expensive.

Use it as a decision tool:

- if the reward is major, finish it fast;
- if the reward is minor, skip it;
- if an upgrade is close, deposit first;
- if the elevator is ready, start preparing to leave;
- if armor and ammo are already low, stop opening new problems.

This is where most solo runs fail: not because the first mistake was huge, but because the floor kept adding pressure while you tried to do one more thing.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

<h3 id="can-you-play-rogue-core-solo">
  Can you play Rogue Core solo?
</h3>

Yes. Rogue Core supports solo play, and solo runs give you Cooper as a helper. Solo still has real pressure because the timer keeps moving while you handle mining, deposits, fights, upgrades, and routing alone.

<h3 id="is-rogue-core-solo-harder-than-coop">
  Is Rogue Core solo harder than co-op?
</h3>

Solo can feel harder because every interaction falls on you. Enemy scaling is reduced, but co-op players can split jobs while solo players must mine, fight, deposit, and move by themselves.

<h3 id="what-is-the-best-solo-class-in-rogue-core">
  What is the best solo class in Rogue Core?
</h3>

Guardian is the safest solo class for new players. Slicer has the highest aggressive ceiling if you can manage kill chains and close-range pressure. Retcon is strong for experienced players who can use Time Rewind before risky pushes.

<h3 id="is-falconer-good-solo">
  Is Falconer good solo?
</h3>

Falconer is playable solo, but you should not pick it for remote revive value. That tool is much stronger in co-op. In solo, Falconer depends more on drone pressure and electric damage.

<h3 id="is-retcon-good-solo">
  Is Retcon good solo?
</h3>

Retcon can be very good solo for experienced players. Time Rewind works best when you set it up before a dangerous push, then use it as a planned reset if the room turns bad.

<h3 id="what-upgrades-are-best-for-solo">
  What upgrades are best for solo?
</h3>

Solo players should prioritize self-sustain, armor recovery, damage reduction, movement, ammo help, and simple damage triggers. Damage Dam-style effects and defensive suit upgrades are valuable when health or armor keeps collapsing.

<h3 id="how-should-i-use-cooper-solo">
  How should I use Cooper solo?
</h3>

Use the laser pointer to command Cooper onto specific jobs: mine awkward Expenite, focus high-threat enemies, help during deposits, or support route-critical tasks. Cooper upgrades can appear through R.E.P.D. or Bio Booster interactions.

<h3 id="what-should-i-do-with-an-expenite-gem">
  What should I do with an Expenite Gem?
</h3>

Deposit it immediately. An Expenite Gem can create a major upgrade moment for both you and Cooper, so carrying it through more danger is usually not worth it.

<h3 id="should-i-full-clear-solo">
  Should I full clear floors in solo?
</h3>

Usually no. Take the rewards that change the run, such as Expenite thresholds, Expenite Gems, Workbenches, Bio Boosters, and needed crates, then return to the route before the floor becomes too expensive.
