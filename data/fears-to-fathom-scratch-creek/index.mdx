<h2 id="co-op-basics">
  Co-op basics: Marcus and Tessa do not always do the same job
</h2>

Scratch Creek is built around Marcus and Tessa as separate playable roles. It is not structured like the earlier solo-style Fears to Fathom episodes. The route is based on two characters, shared timing, and co-op interactions.

Sometimes both players carry furniture together. Sometimes one player drives while the other watches the phone. Later, Marcus and Tessa split during the parsonage sequence, and they may not be focused on the same prompt at the same time.

That means the safest co-op rule is:

<strong>When the game separates you, do not force both players to solve the same problem.</strong>

At the house, both players can help with furniture and trailer setup. During the road trip, one player should focus on the road while the other watches the GPS. At the parsonage, Marcus can be sent away for the towel route while Tessa stays in the room sequence.

For the intended experience, pay attention to the game's own communication and prompts. If you are only trying to clear the route or clean up achievements, use whatever helps your team coordinate.

  Ending: why the trailer matters
</h2>

The final escape is the biggest Scratch Creek stuck point. The car cannot escape normally because the trailer is still attached.

The correct ending route is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>reach the car area,</li>
  <li>do not try to leave on foot,</li>
  <li>have one player unhook the trailer,</li>
  <li>get both players into the car,</li>
  <li>and drive away immediately.</li>
</ul>

If the unhook prompt does not appear, back away from the hitch, stop button-spamming, re-approach from a cleaner angle, and wait for the prompt. For the exact ending solution, use the <Link href="/fears-to-fathom-scratch-creek/ending-guide">Scratch Creek Ending Guide</Link>.
