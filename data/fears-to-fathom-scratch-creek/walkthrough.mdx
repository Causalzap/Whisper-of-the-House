<h2 id="fears-to-fathom-scratch-creek-walkthrough">
  Fears to Fathom Scratch Creek walkthrough
</h2>

**Fears to Fathom - Scratch Creek** is not hard because the route is long. It is hard because the game often expects one specific player, one specific prompt, or one specific timing. Marcus and Tessa do not always need to do the same thing, and several optional achievements can be missed if you follow the story too quickly.

This walkthrough is written for a first clear, but it also includes achievement windows so you do not need to keep another page open for every small unlock.

If you only need the final escape, use the <Link href="/fears-to-fathom-scratch-creek/ending-guide">Scratch Creek Ending and Trailer Guide</Link>. <h2 id="checkpoints-and-reloads">
  Checkpoints and reloads
</h2>

Scratch Creek does not play like a manual-save puzzle game. Treat saves as **checkpoint-style progress**, usually around major transitions such as the drive, trail, parsonage, and other scene changes.

That matters because a bad co-op bug or missed scripted step can push you back farther than expected. If a scene is truly stuck, reloading the nearest checkpoint is often cleaner than fighting the same prompt for ten minutes.

Use checkpoint reloads for:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>voice or input issues after a transition,</li>
  <li>objects getting awkwardly stuck during the moving section,</li>
  <li>parsonage dialogue failing to advance,</li>
  <li>the bathroom / towel sequence not lining up between players,</li>
  <li>or repeated final escape failures.</li>
</ul>

Do not rely on checkpoints for every achievement. If you miss an early window like **Out of Sight**, **Who's Watching?**, or **The Drifter**, you may need to replay from an earlier point.

<h2 id="start-as-marcus-and-tessa">
  Start as Marcus and Tessa
</h2>

Scratch Creek is built around two roles: **Marcus** and **Tessa**. You do not need a perfect plan at the start, but you should understand that some later sections depend on one player doing a specific job.

The game also leans into its own communication systems. Pay attention to phone messages, voice moments, and prompts instead of assuming both players always see the same thing.

Do this carefully:

<h2 id="faq">
  Fears to Fathom Scratch Creek walkthrough FAQ
</h2>

### How long is Scratch Creek?

A normal first clear usually takes about **1.5–2.5 hours**. A cleaner replay can be closer to **60–90 minutes**, while achievement cleanup can push the run toward **3–4 hours**.

### What do I do first at the house?

Load the important items, connect the trailer, put the suitcase in the trunk, and leave when both players are ready.

### Why will the trailer not connect?

The car is probably not close enough to the hitch. Back up slowly and line the car up with the trailer until the connection registers.

### Where is the Who's Watching? achievement window?

After the fallen-tree stop, look toward the roadside house / porch area. If you see the figure watching you, approach and confront it before continuing the drive.

### Where do I go after the car breaks down?

Follow the route to the shop / gas station area first. After the repair situation fails, the route points you toward Bill and then Miss Julia's parsonage.

### Should I explore the abandoned gas station?

Yes, if you want **The Drifter**. Talk to the man living at the abandoned gas station before leaving the area.

### What do I do at Miss Julia's parsonage?

Let the dialogue finish, go through the room setup, and continue until the missing towel sequence starts. Slow down for staff chatter if you want **Eavesdropper**.

### Where are the towels?

Miss Julia sends Marcus to get clean towels from the temple / church basement area out back.

### How do I get Down in the Dark during the walkthrough?

During Marcus's towel objective, check the area under the stairs in the church basement route before returning to Tessa.

### Should Tessa open the door for Henry?

For a cautious first survival route, wait and confirm what is happening. For the **Come In** achievement, Tessa needs to let Henry into the room.

### Can I leave on foot at the end?

No. The intended survival route uses the car after the trailer is disconnected.

### What should I do if the game soft-locks?

Reload the nearest checkpoint if the scene truly stops advancing. Checkpoints can send you back farther than expected, so use them for real progression problems rather than every small mistake.
