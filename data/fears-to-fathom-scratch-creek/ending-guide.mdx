<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
To get the survival ending in **Fears to Fathom - Scratch Creek**, do **not** run away on foot and do **not** keep driving with the trailer attached. One player needs to move to the hitch, **unhook the trailer**, then both players must get into the car and drive away.

If the unhook prompt does not appear, back away from the hitch, stop button-spamming, re-approach from a cleaner angle, and wait for the prompt. If the scene truly stops progressing, reload the nearest checkpoint and repeat the final escape with one player assigned to the hitch. 
</GuideQuickAnswer>

The ending of **Fears to Fathom - Scratch Creek** is confusing because the car seems like the answer, but it still fails if the trailer is attached. If you reached the final escape and the car feels broken, the missing step is almost always the same:

<strong>Unhook the trailer before trying to leave.</strong>

<h2 id="why-the-car-does-not-escape">

</h2>

If the car will not leave, the issue is not only the driver seat. The loaded trailer is still connected, and that keeps the car from completing the escape.

Do not keep opening doors, restarting the same drive attempt, or running away from the car area. The answer is at the hitch between the car and the trailer.

<h2 id="do-not-leave-on-foot">
  Do not try to leave on foot
</h2>

Running away from the car area feels natural because the vehicle fails at first. That is the wrong read. The ending is built around using the car after removing the thing holding it back.

If you leave the car and keep testing the road or woods, you are not solving the actual escape. Return to the vehicle, find the hitch, and disconnect the trailer.

<h2 id="how-to-unhook-the-trailer">
  How to unhook the trailer
</h2>

Send one player to the connection point between the car and the trailer. Look for the hitch interaction prompt, use it once, then return to the car immediately.

If the prompt does not appear, do not mash the button while standing in the same bad angle. Back away from the hitch, re-approach from the side or center of the connection point, and wait for the interaction prompt to appear.

If both players crowd the hitch, the attempt can become harder to read. Let one player handle the interaction while the other stays ready at the car.

<h2 id="checkpoint-and-prompt-fixes">
  If the prompt still does not work
</h2>

The final escape can fail in two different ways. The first is not knowing that you need to unhook the trailer. The second is knowing the answer but failing to make the prompt appear.

Try this before resetting:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Step away from the hitch and stop pressing the interact button.</li>
  <li>Re-approach the connection point slowly.</li>
  <li>Face the hitch directly instead of clicking from the side of the trailer.</li>
  <li>Let only one player stand at the hitch.</li>
  <li>Interact once when the prompt appears, then return to the car.</li>
</ul>

If the scene truly stops advancing after several clean attempts, reload the nearest checkpoint. Checkpoint reloads can send you back farther than expected, so use them for real prompt or progression problems, not every small mistake.

<h2 id="what-happens-after-you-escape">
  What happens after you escape
</h2>

After the trailer is disconnected and both players escape in the car, Marcus and Tessa make it back toward the main road before the car gives out again. They call for help, but the ending does not turn into a clean explanation where every threat is neatly solved.

The important gameplay answer is simple: if you escaped after disconnecting the trailer and reached the ending text, you solved the survival route.

<h2 id="ending-explained">
  Scratch Creek ending explained
</h2>

The ending works because it turns the move itself into the trap. Marcus and Tessa spend the episode depending on the car and trailer to reach their new life. At the end, that same trailer becomes the thing that slows them down.

The final escape is not a dialogue choice or a morality choice. It is a survival check:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Can you stay calm when the car does not work?</li>
  <li>Can both players understand that the trailer is the problem?</li>
  <li>Can one player take the risk outside while the other is ready to drive?</li>
  <li>Can you commit once the hitch is disconnected?</li>
</ul>

That is why the final sequence feels harsher than a normal chase. The solution is not just “run.” It is “drop the thing holding you back, then run.”

<h2 id="faq">
  Scratch Creek ending FAQ
</h2>

### Why do I keep getting caught after unhooking the trailer?

You are probably taking too long after the interaction. Once the trailer is disconnected, the hitch player should return to the car immediately and both players should leave.

### What should I do if the unhook prompt does not appear?

Back away from the hitch, stop button-spamming, re-approach the connection point from a cleaner angle, and wait for the prompt. If the scene truly stops advancing, reload the nearest checkpoint.

### Is there a different ending if I fail?

Failed attempts usually lead to death, a restart, or a checkpoint retry. This guide focuses on the survival ending route, not a full all-deaths catalog.

### Is this an all endings guide?

No. This page covers the survival ending and common final escape failures. If distinct alternate endings are confirmed later, they should be handled in an all-endings guide or added here as a separate section.
