<h2 id="fears-to-fathom-scratch-creek-achievements-guide">
  Fears to Fathom Scratch Creek achievements guide
</h2>

**Fears to Fathom - Scratch Creek** has a short achievement list, but several unlocks are easy to miss because this episode is co-op. Some achievements can be done by either player, while others depend on **Marcus** or **Tessa** being in the right place at the right time.

The main trap is rushing the story. If you play only for survival, you can finish the episode and still miss the drifter, Henry, staff chatter, the trailer hiding spot, the watching figure, or Marcus's dark chore.

If you are still stuck in the story route, use the <Link href="/fears-to-fathom-scratch-creek/walkthrough">Scratch Creek Walkthrough</Link>. If you are stuck at the ending, use the <Link href="/fears-to-fathom-scratch-creek/ending-guide">Ending and Trailer Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="texter">
  Texter
</h2>

**Unlock condition:** Send a manual text to the other survivor.
**Who should do it:** Either player.
**Unlock rate:** 65.4%.

Open the phone and send one manual text to your partner. Do this early, before the drive and parsonage sections start demanding more attention.

You do not need a special story moment. The important part is that the message is sent manually, not just read from the automatic story texts.

<h2 id="all-hitched-up">
  All Hitched Up
</h2>

**Unlock condition:** Connect the trailer before leaving the house.
**Who should do it:** Either player.
**Unlock rate:** 44.9%.

This unlocks during the opening moving-out section. Back the car close enough to the trailer hitch until the trailer connects.

If the trailer does not connect, the car is usually too far away or angled badly. Back up slowly and line up with the hitch instead of driving off early.

<h2 id="out-of-sight">
  Out of Sight
</h2>

**Unlock condition:** Hide in the trailer.
**Who should do it:** Either player.
**Unlock rate:** 27.1%.

When the trailer is available, climb inside and use it as a hiding spot. This is easy to miss because most players only think of the trailer as a place for furniture.

If you want to clean this up quickly, do it before the road trip fully moves on. Once the episode leaves the early trailer sections behind, you will need to replay back to that point.

<h2 id="holy-cow">
  Holy Cow
</h2>

**Unlock condition:** Reach 100,000,000 in Cow Clicker.
**Who should do it:** Either player.
**Unlock rate:** 12.5%.

Cow Clicker is the longest achievement in Scratch Creek. It is not mechanically difficult, but it becomes painful if you start too late or miss later upgrades.

Plan around **30–45 minutes of steady Cow Clicker progress** if you start early and keep buying upgrades. Treat that as a practical planning range, not a fixed timer: your result depends on how often you open the phone, whether you keep the app progressing during downtime, and whether you scroll down to buy stronger upgrades.

The important upgrade breakpoint is when passive income starts carrying the run. Once cow-per-second upgrades are doing most of the work, stop obsessing over manual clicks and focus on keeping upgrades moving during safe moments.

Do not play Cow Clicker while you are responsible for driving, hiding, opening doors, or handling the final escape. Let the less-busy player manage it.

  Who's Watching?

**Who should do it:** Either player.
**Unlock rate:** 30.6%.

This achievement happens after the road section where a fallen tree blocks the path. After you move the tree and the car trouble continues, look toward the nearby house / porch area. You can spot someone watching you.

If you are playing with a friend, call out the achievement before the scene starts. The worst way to miss an achievement is to let one player advance the story while the other player is still doing the required interaction.

<h2 id="eavesdropper">
  Eavesdropper
</h2>

**Unlock condition:** Eavesdrop on all the staff chatter in the parsonage.
**Who should do it:** Usually Tessa, or whoever is near the chatter.
**Unlock rate:** 5.4%.

This is one of the rarest achievements because players move too quickly through the parsonage. Whenever you hear staff chatter, stop and listen until the conversation finishes.

The safest rule is:

<strong>If someone starts talking nearby at the parsonage, do not leave the area until the chatter is fully done.</strong>

This is especially important before and during the room / bathroom portion. If Marcus is doing the towel route and Tessa is still inside, Tessa should stay patient instead of rushing every prompt immediately.

<h2 id="down-in-the-dark">
  Down in the Dark
</h2>

**Unlock condition:** Marcus's last chore.
**Exact route:** During the towel objective, use Marcus and check the area under the stairs in the church basement route.
**Who should do it:** Marcus.
**Unlock rate:** 6.5%.

This is the biggest achievement trap in Scratch Creek. The vague description makes it sound like you only need to finish the towel task, but the part players miss is the **under-stairs check** during Marcus's route.

When Tessa is in the bath / room sequence and the towel problem starts, Marcus needs to call Miss Julia. Miss Julia sends Marcus to get clean towels from the temple / church basement area out back. During that route, do not rush straight back after finding the obvious path. Check the area under the stairs before returning to Tessa.

<h2 id="come-in">
  Come In
</h2>

**Unlock condition:** Let Henry into the room.
**Who should do it:** Tessa.
**Unlock rate:** 17.3%.

During the parsonage room sequence, Henry knocks and asks to come in. If you are playing safely, your instinct may be to refuse or stall. For **Come In**, Tessa needs to let Henry enter.

<h2 id="survivor">
  Survivor
</h2>

**Unlock condition:** Survive through this episode of Fears to Fathom.
**Who should do it:** Both players.
**Unlock rate:** 44.8%.

This unlocks after you survive the ending. The final escape is not just reaching the car. You need to disconnect the trailer first.

The final route is simple: reach the car area, do not leave on foot, unhook the trailer, get both players into the car, and drive away immediately.

If the car feels stuck or too slow, the trailer is still attached. Use the <Link href="/fears-to-fathom-scratch-creek/ending-guide">Scratch Creek Ending and Trailer Guide</Link> for the full escape route.


<h2 id="can-you-get-all-achievements-in-one-run">
  Can you get all achievements in one run?
</h2>

You can plan for most achievements in one run, but it is not the cleanest first playthrough. **Holy Cow** needs early Cow Clicker management, **Come In** requires a riskier Henry choice, and **Down in the Dark** requires Marcus to slow down during the towel route instead of rushing back.

The best practical approach is:

<strong>Survive once, then do a focused cleanup run where both players already know their roles.</strong>

If you still want a one-run attempt, follow the cleanup priority table near the top of this guide. Do not try to memorize a second route here; the key is to stop at the right missable scenes instead of autopiloting the story.

<h2 id="faq">
  Fears to Fathom Scratch Creek achievements FAQ
</h2>

### Which Scratch Creek achievement is easiest to miss?

**Down in the Dark** and **Eavesdropper** are the easiest to miss. Down in the Dark needs Marcus to check under the stairs during the towel objective, while Eavesdropper needs you to slow down and listen to staff chatter in the parsonage.

### Which player should do Down in the Dark?

Marcus. During the towel objective, Marcus must go through the church basement route and check under the stairs before returning.

### How long does Holy Cow take?

Plan around 30–45 minutes of steady Cow Clicker progress if you start early and keep buying upgrades. If you start late or miss stronger upgrades lower in the list, it can take longer.

### Why is Eavesdropper so rare?

Most players leave parsonage chatter points too quickly. Stay close until each staff conversation fully finishes.

### Should I do Come In on my first run?

Only if you are comfortable choosing the risky option. For a blind first survival route, you can skip it and return later for cleanup.
