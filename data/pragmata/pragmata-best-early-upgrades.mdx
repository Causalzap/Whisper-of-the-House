Pragmata’s opening hours can feel like a slog if you treat it like a traditional shooter. Your guns aren't meant to carry fights alone—if you aren't opening up targets with hacking first, your raw damage is going to feel abysmal. That is exactly why so many players think their starter weapons are underpowered.

Before you start burning materials at the Shelter, here is the optimal path for your early game. We break down exactly what to prioritize at the Firmware Updater and Unit Printer, and which early mod upgrades actually give you the best return on investment.

Keep these two golden rules in mind before you spend anything:

- **Upgrade Components are strictly limited early on**. Wasting them on the wrong tech will immediately bottleneck your build and delay your power spikes.
- **Firmware Updater choices are foundational.** These aren't minor stat bumps; they dictate how you scale into the mid-game, so do not treat them casually.

<h2 id="why-your-guns-feel-weak-in-pragmata">
  Why Your Guns Feel Weak in Pragmata
</h2>

The biggest early misunderstanding in Pragmata is thinking that combat is a pure shooter. It is not.

Enemies become properly vulnerable after hacking opens them up. Until then, your **Primary Unit** can feel underpowered, especially if you are trying to brute-force encounters without opening them first. That is why early upgrades that improve hacking performance often feel stronger than raw weapon upgrades.

![Pragmata enemies become vulnerable after hacking opens them up](/images/pragmata/pragmata-why-guns-feel-weak-hacking-open-state.webp)

If you are struggling in the opening areas, think of combat in this order:

- Open enemies with hacking
- Use your weapon while they are exposed
- Use **Attack Units** or **Tactical Units** to extend openings or burst them down
- Upgrade mobility and healing so you can survive mistakes

This matters a lot in early large-enemy fights. If you have not improved your hacking flow or your movement, you often end up doing weak chip damage while also failing to create enough distance to reset the fight cleanly.

<h2 id="best-early-upgrades-to-buy-first">
  Best Early Upgrades to Buy First
</h2>

These are the upgrades I would prioritize first if I were starting over.

### 1. Hacking upgrades in the Firmware Updater

If you only upgrade one category first, make it hacking.

Hacking is what turns enemies into easy targets, and the early footage strongly supports that improving hacking gives better results than only buffing the basic gun. Once you lean harder into hacking power, enemy damage windows become more reliable, and later nodes like **Decode** become even stronger.

This is the single best answer if your damage feels low.

### 2. Thruster upgrade

Thruster upgrades are one of the best early quality-of-life buys because Pragmata constantly asks you to dodge, jump, hover, and reposition.

More mobility means:

- safer fights
- easier platforming
- better recovery after mistakes
- smoother boss handling

This is one of the most practical early pickups because it helps in both combat and traversal.

In early large-enemy encounters, this is where the value becomes obvious. If your **Thruster Gauge** feels too short, you often cannot dodge, jump, and re-angle fast enough before the next attack comes in.

### 3. Object Scan

**Object Scan** is one of the best early utility upgrades because it improves how efficiently you read a space.

It helps with:

- spotting useful objects
- finding upgrade materials
- highlighting cleanup targets
- reducing how often you miss optional resources

Even if it does not look flashy on paper, it directly improves your overall upgrade pace because it helps you stop leaving useful pickups behind.

### 4. Auto-hacking

**Auto-hacking** is one of the best quality-of-life upgrades for players who want smoother fights.

It is strong because it reduces the amount of manual hacking pressure in the middle of combat. That means you can spend more time:

- dodging
- repositioning
- maintaining damage
- reacting to incoming attacks

This is especially useful once fights get busier and enemies start forcing you to multitask more aggressively.

### 5. Shockwave Gun

The **Shockwave Gun** is one of the best early **Attack Units** because it is usually the first one that feels immediately powerful instead of merely situational.

It is strong for two simple reasons:

- it is one of the first Attack Units you gain access to
- a huge amount of early combat in Pragmata happens in tight spaces where close-range burst is naturally effective

That makes it a much better fit for the early game than a weapon that needs long sightlines or very safe spacing. In real encounters, the game keeps pushing you into short-to-mid-range rooms, so the Shockwave Gun ends up matching the actual rhythm of the campaign.

### 6. Harden Suit mod

The **Harden Suit** mod gives you a flat max health increase, which makes it one of the cleanest early survivability upgrades in the game.

![Pragmata Harden Suit mod increases max health by 10 percent](/images/pragmata/pragmata-harden-suit-mod-max-health.webp)

Early on, that kind of upgrade matters a lot because the game is still teaching you enemy patterns, hack timing, and movement management. If you are taking hits while learning, extra health is immediately useful.

### 7. Cartridge Holder

**Cartridge Holder** is extremely strong early because more healing capacity gives you more room to learn encounters without resetting as often.

![Pragmata Cartridge Holder increases the number of heals you can carry](/images/pragmata/pragmata-cartridge-holder-extra-heal.webp)

This is one of those upgrades that may not look flashy, but it is the kind of upgrade that makes the rest of the game smoother. If you are dying before you can finish an encounter, extra healing is often better than one more small damage bump.

### 8. Critical Shot

**Critical Shot** is one of the best early offensive unlocks because it gives you a much stronger payoff after successful hacking and low-health pressure.

Once you understand the hack flow, Critical Shot turns openings into much bigger momentum swings. This is the kind of upgrade that starts making combat feel much more explosive.

### 9. Extra Mod Slot

**Extra Mod Slot** gets better the more useful mods you find, and early footage already shows enough good mods to make the upgrade worth considering.

Once you have both a survivability mod and a hacking-oriented mod, an extra slot starts to feel much more valuable than another tiny stat bump.

<h2 id="what-decode-does-and-why-it-matters">
  What Decode Does and Why It Matters
</h2>

**Decode** is one of the first really important **Hacking Nodes** because it directly improves how much damage you get out of a successful hack sequence.

In simple terms, Decode helps turn a good hack into a much more meaningful punish window against a single target. That is why it stays relevant for so long: it does not ask you to change how you play. It just makes the hack-first combat loop hit harder.

If your early fights feel slow, Decode is one of the clearest examples of why hacking upgrades scale better than raw gun upgrades in the opening stretch.

<h2 id="the-best-weapon-and-hacking-priorities-early">
  The Best Weapon and Hacking Priorities Early
</h2>

If you are choosing between **Primary Unit** upgrades and hacking upgrades early, lean toward hacking first.

That does not mean Primary Unit upgrades are bad. It means the game seems designed so that weapons perform best after a successful hack opens the enemy. In practice, that makes hacking upgrades feel more important in the early game than small base weapon increases.

![Pragmata shelter lets you upgrade weapons, hacking nodes, and abilities](/images/pragmata/pragmata-shelter-upgrades-loadout.webp)

A good early priority order looks like this:

- Hacking performance
- Thrusters
- Healing and survivability
- Key weapon improvements
- Mods and extra slots

If you want a weapon-focused early choice, the best ones are usually the ones that either:

- make enemies easier to stagger
- give you more follow-up pressure
- work well during open windows

<h2 id="why-some-players-prioritize-primary-unit-first">
  Why Some Players Prioritize Primary Unit First
</h2>

There is a real point of disagreement here.

Some players prefer upgrading the **Primary Unit** first because it feels like the most direct way to improve combat. That logic makes sense on the surface: if your gun feels weak, improving gun damage sounds like the obvious fix.

We still do not think that is the best first priority for most players.

The reason is practical, not theoretical. Even when you raise raw weapon output, early elite fights still feel slow if:

- enemies are not being opened reliably
- you cannot stay safe long enough to capitalize
- your movement is too weak to create clean punish windows

So yes, a stronger Primary Unit is useful. But in real encounters, raw gun damage often fails to solve the actual problem. The problem is usually that your **hack windows are too weak**, your **mobility is too limited**, or your **resource route is too inefficient**.

That is why we treat early Primary Unit upgrades as good support picks, not the best first fix.

### Extended Breach

### Relay Amplifier

If you already have an Extra Mod Slot, running a survivability mod together with a hacking-support mod looks stronger than going all-in on niche damage too early.

<h2 id="what-to-buy-first-at-the-shelter">
  What to Buy First at the Shelter
</h2>

Before spending anything here, remember one important thing:

**Your early Upgrade Components are limited, and your core Firmware Updater path is not something you should treat like throwaway experimentation.**

If you are standing in the Shelter and do not know what to buy first, use this order:

1. **A hacking upgrade in the Firmware Updater**
2. **Thruster upgrade**
3. **Object Scan**
4. **Auto-hacking**
5. **A survival upgrade like Harden Suit or Cartridge Holder**
6. **Critical Shot**
7. **Extra Mod Slot**
8. **Shockwave Gun or another weapon upgrade that matches what you actually use**

That is the safest upgrade path for most players because it fixes the biggest early pain points:

- low damage
- weak survivability
- clunky movement under pressure
- missed resources
- too much manual combat load

<h2 id="training-simulations-and-upgrade-efficiency">
  Training Simulations and Upgrade Efficiency
</h2>

Do not ignore **Training Simulations**.

They matter because they are not just practice rooms. They can also contribute useful progression rewards, including:

- **Upgrade Components**
- **Pure Lunum**
- **Cabin Coins**

That makes them important for two different reasons at once:

- they improve your familiarity with hacking and combat flow
- they help your actual upgrade economy

So if you skip Training Simulations completely, you are not only skipping practice. You may also be delaying some of the resources that support your best build path.

<h2 id="what-not-to-prioritize-too-early">
  What Not to Prioritize Too Early
</h2>

Do not overcommit to tiny raw damage bumps if you are still struggling with the core combat loop.

In the early game, a pure stat increase can look tempting, but if you are not consistently opening enemies with hacks first, the payoff is smaller than it appears. Mobility, healing, scanning efficiency, and hacking support often do more for your real performance than one isolated damage node.

That is also why this is a resource management article, not just a shopping list. The danger is not merely buying the wrong thing once. It is slowly locking yourself into an inefficient build path while your best upgrades arrive late.

<h2 id="final-verdict">
  Final Verdict
</h2>

The best early upgrades in Pragmata are the ones that support the game's real combat flow, not just the ones that look strongest on a menu.

If your guns feel weak, the answer is usually:

- improve hacking first
- upgrade thrusters early
- unlock Object Scan and Auto-hacking
- use Decode to get more value from hack windows
- pick up survivability tools like Harden Suit and Cartridge Holder
- use Shockwave Gun as one of your best early offensive tools
- unlock Critical Shot once available
- add an Extra Mod Slot once you have strong early mods

If you only want one sentence to remember, use this:

**In early Pragmata, hacking, mobility, scanning efficiency, and survivability are usually better first investments than blindly stacking Primary Unit damage.**
