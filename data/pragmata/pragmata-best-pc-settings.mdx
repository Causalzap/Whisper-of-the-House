Pragmata runs well overall, but the best settings are not just about using the highest preset. The real decision is whether you want extra reflections from ray tracing or a cleaner, more stable image with less visual noise. This guide covers the best Pragmata PC settings for most players, 8GB GPUs, 16GB GPUs, and anyone deciding between RT, Path Tracing, DLSS, and FSR.

<h2 id="quick-answer">
  Quick Answer
</h2>

For most players, the best Pragmata PC settings are simple:

- Use the **Quality** preset if you have a **16GB GPU** and want the best overall image.
- Turn **Ray Tracing Off** if the visual noise bothers you more than the extra reflections help.
- Use the **Balanced** preset if you have an **8GB GPU**.
- If you want to use **upscaling**, it generally looks better with **RT Off** than with **RT On**.
- Treat **Path Tracing** as an optional enthusiast setting, not the default best setting.

In short, Pragmata usually looks better when you prioritize image stability over noisy reflections.

<h2 id="best-pc-settings-for-most-players">
  Best PC Settings for Most Players
</h2>

If you just want the best settings without overthinking every toggle, this is the setup most players should start with:

- **Graphics Preset:** Quality
- **Ray Tracing:** Off
- **Path Tracing:** Off
- **Upscaling:** Off at 1080p if performance is already good; DLSS Quality or FSR Quality at 1440p if needed
- **Frame Generation:** Optional, only if your base frame rate already feels stable
- **V-Sync:** Personal preference
- **Motion Blur / Film Grain / Chromatic Aberration:** Personal preference

This is the safest starting point because Pragmata already performs well, and turning RT off usually gives you a cleaner image without giving up much performance.

<h2 id="why-the-quality-preset-is-not-always-the-best-looking-option">
  Why the Quality Preset Is Not Always the Best-Looking Option
</h2>

Pragmata’s **Quality** preset is the maximum preset, and it enables **ray tracing by default**. That sounds ideal on paper, but it does not automatically mean it looks better in every scene.

![Pragmata Quality preset with RT enabled by default, showing visible noise on reflective floor surfaces](/images/pragmata/pragmata-quality-preset-rt-default-noise.webp)

At 1080p, the biggest issue is not raw performance. It is **visual noise**, especially on floors, metal grates, and reflective surfaces. In other words, Quality can be the highest preset while still looking less clean in motion than you might expect.

If you are sensitive to shimmering, noisy reflections, or unstable image detail, do not assume the highest preset is automatically the best-looking option for your setup.

<h2 id="should-you-turn-ray-tracing-off">
  Should You Turn Ray Tracing Off?
</h2>

For most players, **yes — at least try it**.

The important thing here is that turning RT off does **not** produce a massive performance jump. The gain is real, but small enough that the real choice comes down to image quality preference.

![Pragmata RT off only gains about 8 percent performance compared to RT on](/images/pragmata/pragmata-rt-off-only-gains-8-percent.webp)

Turning RT off usually gives you:

- A small performance boost
- Slightly lower VRAM pressure
- Fewer reflections
- A cleaner and more stable image

That is why RT Off is the best default recommendation for most players. You lose some reflective detail, but you often gain a better-looking image during actual gameplay.

<h2 id="rt-on-vs-rt-off-what-actually-changes">
  RT On vs RT Off: What Actually Changes?
</h2>

This is the most useful side-by-side comparison for anyone trying to decide whether ray tracing is worth keeping on.

![Pragmata RT On vs RT Off comparison on floor reflections and noise](/images/pragmata/pragmata-rt-on-vs-rt-off-floor-reflections-noise.webp)

With **RT On**, you get:

- More reflections
- More accurate reflective detail
- More visible noise on some surfaces

With **RT Off**, you get:

- Fewer reflections
- A cleaner and more stable image
- Slightly better performance

That means the choice is not really about whether RT is "better." It is about what bothers you more: missing reflections or noisy reflections.

For most players, RT Off is easier to recommend because it looks more stable in real gameplay, especially when you are moving through environments instead of stopping to inspect puddles and polished floors.

<h2 id="rt-on-vs-path-tracing-which-one-is-better">
  RT On vs Path Tracing: Which One Is Better?
</h2>

Path Tracing is not the best default setting. It is the optional visual experiment for players who want to test a heavier rendering mode.

![Pragmata RT On vs Path Tracing visual tradeoff comparison](/images/pragmata/pragmata-rt-on-vs-path-tracing-visual-tradeoff.webp)

Path Tracing can improve:

- Reflections
- Shadow quality
- Some lighting detail
- Overall consistency in certain reflective scenes

But it also comes with major downsides:

- It needs aggressive upscaling to be usable on mid-range GPUs
- It produces a softer image than native RT
- It costs much more performance

So the real trade-off is not simply "Path Tracing looks better." It is this:

- **RT On:** Sharper and faster, but noisier
- **Path Tracing:** Cleaner in some scenes, but softer and much heavier

If you are on a mid-range GPU, Path Tracing is something to test for fun. It is not the setting most players should leave on full-time.

<h2 id="best-settings-for-8gb-gpus">
  Best Settings for 8GB GPUs
</h2>

If you are playing on an 8GB card, this is the simplest recommendation in the whole guide:

**Use the Balanced preset.**

![Pragmata Balanced preset works better for 8GB GPUs](/images/pragmata/pragmata-balanced-preset-best-for-8gb-gpus.webp)

The Balanced preset is one step below Quality and automatically turns Ray Tracing off. That makes it a much safer fit for 8GB hardware, especially if the higher preset causes memory-related issues or less consistent performance.

### Recommended 8GB Setup

- **Graphics Preset:** Balanced
- **Ray Tracing:** Off
- **Path Tracing:** Off
- **Upscaling:** Optional
- **Target Resolution:** 1080p
- **Frame Generation:** Optional, depending on your base frame rate

If you are on 8GB VRAM, do not waste time trying to force the fanciest settings. Balanced is the practical answer.

<h2 id="best-settings-for-16gb-gpus">
  Best Settings for 16GB GPUs
</h2>

If you have a 16GB GPU, you have more flexibility, but the same visual trade-offs still apply.

### Recommended 16GB Setup for Image Stability

- **Graphics Preset:** Quality
- **Ray Tracing:** Off
- **Upscaling:** Off at 1080p if performance is already strong
- **1440p:** Use DLSS Quality or FSR Quality only if needed

### Recommended 16GB Setup for Visual Testing

- **Graphics Preset:** Quality
- **Ray Tracing:** On
- **Upscaling:** Prefer native resolution first
- **Path Tracing:** Optional test setting only

A 16GB GPU gives you room to experiment, but that does not mean RT or Path Tracing automatically becomes the best choice. Pragmata’s main graphics decision is still about noise versus reflections, not just raw GPU power.

<h2 id="why-upscaling-looks-worse-with-rt-enabled">
  Why Upscaling Looks Worse with RT Enabled
</h2>

This is one of the most useful takeaways from the footage.

![Pragmata upscaling with RT enabled increases visible noise](/images/pragmata/pragmata-rt-upscaling-increases-visual-noise.webp)

In Pragmata, using upscaling while Ray Tracing is enabled can make visual noise more obvious. Once internal render resolution drops, the RT solution has less image data to work with, and reflective surfaces can look messier.

That leads to a simple rule:

- **If RT is On, try native resolution first**
- **If you want to use upscaling for performance, it often looks better with RT Off**

This is unusual because many games encourage players to turn on RT and then use upscaling to recover frames. In Pragmata, that can create a worse-looking result than simply disabling RT and using DLSS or FSR.

<h2 id="final-verdict">
  Final Verdict
</h2>

The best Pragmata PC settings are not about maxing everything out. They are about understanding the game’s visual trade-off:

- **Ray Tracing adds reflections**
- **Ray Tracing also adds visible noise**
- **Path Tracing can look cleaner in some scenes, but it is much heavier and softer**
- **Balanced is the best answer for 8GB GPUs**
- **RT Off is the best default recommendation for most players**

If you only want one sentence to remember, use this:

**Start with Quality + RT Off on 16GB GPUs, or Balanced on 8GB GPUs, then only turn RT or Path Tracing on if you personally prefer the extra reflections over a cleaner image.**
