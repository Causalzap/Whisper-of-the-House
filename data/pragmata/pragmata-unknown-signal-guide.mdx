**Unknown Signal** is one of the most important postgame systems in *Pragmata*, but most guides undersell what it actually does.

It is not just a bonus challenge mode you sample once for a weapon or outfit. It is the game's real **post-story completion route**: the place where you finish sector cleanup, unlock the full set of simulation pods, collect some of the best rewards in the game, and close out the last layer of challenge content.

It is also where players make their biggest postgame mistake. A lot of people clear the 10 pods, assume the mode is finished, and miss the final step that actually closes out the route.

So this guide is built around the questions that matter most:

- how Unknown Signal unlocks
- how all 10 pods open up
- what counts as true postgame completion
- what happens after all 10 pods are done
- and what trophy-focused players should be careful not to miss

<h2 id="quick-answer">
  Quick Answer
</h2>

If you only want the short version:

- **Unknown Signal unlocks after you beat the main story once**
- It is not just a reward mode; it is Pragmata's **real postgame cleanup and challenge path**
- You use it to finish **sector cleanup, enhanced boss progression, simulation pods, and late postgame rewards**
- **Not all 10 pods are available immediately**
- The full route is closer to this: **main story clear → sector 100% progress → enhanced bosses → all 10 pods → special Mod reward → final boss rematch**
- If you are aiming for the extra ending payoff, **clearing the pods alone is not enough**
- The most important mistake to avoid is thinking **10 pods = true completion**

<h2 id="unknown-signal-full-route-at-a-glance">
  Unknown Signal Full Route at a Glance
</h2>

If you want the cleanest way to understand the mode, think of it as a chain rather than a single activity:

1. Beat the main story once
2. Unlock Unknown Signal
3. Return to earlier sectors and push them toward full completion
4. Defeat the relevant enhanced postgame bosses
5. Open up the full set of Unknown Signal simulation pods
6. Clear all 10 pods
7. Earn the special post-pod Mod reward
8. Return for the final boss rematch
9. Bring the correct Mod setup into that fight if you want the full ending payoff

That is the part many players never see explained in one place. Unknown Signal is not just "10 challenge rooms after credits." It is a **structured postgame route** with a real endpoint.

<h2 id="how-to-unlock-unknown-signal">
  How to Unlock Unknown Signal
</h2>

Unknown Signal unlocks after you complete the main story once.

After your first clear and the credits, Pragmata opens up its postgame layer, and Unknown Signal becomes the system you use for both cleanup and higher-end challenge progression.

The simple rule is this:

**Beat the campaign once, then treat Unknown Signal as your main postgame route rather than a side distraction.**

That matters because the mode sits at the center of several different goals at once:

- finishing missed exploration content
- unlocking postgame challenge progression
- earning rare rewards
- and moving toward true full completion

<h2 id="what-unknown-signal-actually-is">
  What Unknown Signal Actually Is
</h2>

Unknown Signal is Pragmata's postgame challenge layer, but calling it only a "challenge mode" is too narrow.

It is built around tougher encounters, harder platforming, more demanding puzzle execution, and a sequence of simulation pods that ask more from your weapons, hacking tools, movement, and resource management than the main story usually does.

![Pragmata Unknown Signal postgame mode overview](/images/pragmata/pragmata-unknown-signal-postgame-overview.webp)

It is also where completion-focused players tie together several systems that the story only partially finishes:

- late cleanup
- enhanced boss clears
- postgame reward farming
- rare mod collection
- and final route closure after the 10 pods

That is why Unknown Signal matters so much. It is both a **reward layer** and a **completion layer**.

<h2 id="how-all-10-pods-unlock">
  How All 10 Pods Unlock
</h2>

Unknown Signal contains **10 simulation pods** in total, but the key thing to understand is that **they do not all open the moment the mode unlocks**.

This is where a lot of lighter guides become too vague. The pods are not just "extra missions that appear over time," and they are not only about replaying bosses either.

The full set is tied to **two different postgame unlock tracks**, and this is where players most often get confused.

Some pods unlock when you push a sector to **100% completion**. Others unlock when you defeat the relevant **enhanced postgame bosses** tied to the earlier sectors. In other words, these are **two separate unlock conditions for different pods**, not a case where every missing pod requires both at the same time.

That distinction matters because it changes how you troubleshoot missing challenges. If a pod has not opened yet, the right question is not just "Did I replay the boss?" or just "Did I finish the sector?" It is:

**"Which unlock track is this pod tied to: sector completion or enhanced boss progression?"**

<h2 id="the-biggest-unknown-signal-mistake">
  The Biggest Unknown Signal Mistake
</h2>

The biggest Unknown Signal mistake is thinking that **clearing all 10 pods means the postgame route is over**.

It is not.

The 10 pods are the mode's biggest milestone, but they are **not** the true endpoint. They are the setup for the final postgame closure.

That is the single most important idea in this guide:

**10 pods is the unlock point for the finale, not the finale itself.**

Many players stop at the exact point where the mode is about to ask for one last important step.

<h2 id="what-happens-after-all-10-pods">
  What Happens After All 10 Pods
</h2>

Clearing all 10 simulation pods is a major accomplishment, but it does not fully finish Unknown Signal.

After that, you still need to go back and handle the **final boss rematch** that closes out the postgame path.

![Pragmata Unknown Signal final boss rematch](/images/pragmata/pragmata-unknown-signal-final-boss-refight.webp)

This is also the point where the route becomes easiest to misread. Finishing the pods is the big milestone, but it is **not** the same thing as properly resolving the postgame finale.

After the 10-pod clear, you earn a **special Mod**, and for players chasing the full ending payoff, that reward is not something you should treat as automatically counted just because it dropped. The important step is to make sure your **final boss setup actually includes that special Mod** when you go into the rematch.

So the post-pod sequence is really this:

- clear all 10 pods
- collect the special post-pod Mod reward
- save before the final boss rematch
- confirm that the special Mod is actually equipped
- then clear the final boss to properly close out the route

That is the mistake point most players never realize until it is too late. They think the reward was the endpoint, when in practice it is part of the setup for the mode's real final step.

<h2 id="can-you-clean-up-collectibles-in-postgame">
  Can You Clean Up Collectibles in Postgame?
</h2>

Yes, and that is one of the biggest reasons Unknown Signal matters.

For most players, the practical rule is this: **most standard collection progress is still recoverable after the main story**. Once you are in postgame, you can use the Shelter and its travel systems to return to earlier sectors and finish what you missed.

That usually means postgame cleanup can still cover much of the usual exploration content, including:

- Mini Cabins
- Read Earth Memories
- Upgrade Components
- Mods
- Chests
- unfinished Red Zones
- unfinished Training Simulations

This is what makes Unknown Signal so valuable for completion planning. It is not just an arena mode or a reward farm. It is the practical bridge between story completion and real cleanup.

<h2 id="what-you-cannot-fix-in-postgame">
  What You Cannot Fix in Postgame
</h2>

Even though most collectible progress is recoverable, postgame is **not** a universal undo button.

The main danger is **trophy or achievement logic tied to specific boss conditions**, especially around late-game and final-boss-related triggers. Items may still be collectible later, but trigger logic is not always as forgiving as inventory cleanup.

That means the safest completion habit is still:

- keep a manual backup save before the final boss
- do not assume every trigger can be repaired in postgame
- treat New Game Plus as a fallback route, not your main safety net

For trophy-focused players, Unknown Signal should be planned like a **route**, not a cleanup junk drawer.

**Unknown Signal is where your cleanup route and your ending-sensitive route start overlapping.**

That overlap is exactly why so many players miss something late.

  Should You Rush to the End of Each Pod?

Usually, no.

A common Unknown Signal mistake is treating each pod like a straight sprint to the exit. These simulation missions often contain useful extras, including collectible pickups, rare mods, cabin coins, and Pure Lunum.

![Pragmata Unknown Signal sim mission rewards with cabin coin and Pure Lunum](/images/pragmata/pragmata-unknown-signal-sim-mission-rewards-cabin-coin-pure-lunum.webp)

- clear safely
- look around before leaving
- open reward chests when the pod gives you time
- do not treat every mission like a speedrun unless you are returning later for a specific challenge goal

Unknown Signal is most rewarding when you play it like a **route with layered value**, not a checklist you race through once.
