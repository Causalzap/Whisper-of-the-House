<h2 id="quick-summary">

If you only want the short version:

<h2 id="what-pure-lunum-actually-is">
  What Pure Lunum Actually Is
</h2>

Pure Lunum is one of Pragmata's rarest upgrade materials. It is not part of your normal upgrade loop like blue Lunafilament. You do not find it everywhere, and you do not casually spend it on routine progression.

That is why players search for it by name. It is a tracked, limited, high-value resource that matters once your build starts hitting higher-end upgrade thresholds.

<h2 id="can-you-farm-pure-lunum">
  Can You Farm Pure Lunum?
</h2>

Not in the normal sense.

Pure Lunum is **not** the kind of resource you grind from regular enemies in a repeatable loop. Public guide coverage consistently treats it as a **fixed collectible-style resource** that comes from specific containers, paths, and challenge rewards rather than a standard mob drop. That is also why location guides exist for it in the first place.

So if you are looking for a fast "best farming route," the better question is:

**Which sectors, White Chests, and Red Zones have the pieces I missed?**

<h2 id="white-chests-vs-safe-boxes">
  White Chests vs Safe Boxes
</h2>

This is the first thing your article needs to get right.

Pure Lunum is mainly associated with **White Chests**, not generic “gold chests.” Public guide coverage also separates those White Chests from **red Safe Boxes**, which are usually used for items like mods and Cabin Coins instead.

That means when you are searching a sector, your mental checklist should be:

- **White Chest:** high chance of Pure Lunum
- **Red Zone reward:** possible Pure Lunum
- **Red Safe Box:** usually not your Pure Lunum target

<h2 id="how-many-pure-lunum-are-there">
  How Many Pure Lunum Are There?
</h2>

Current public collectible coverage reports **13 Pure Lunum total** across the game, spread over **4 sectors**. One breakdown published this week lists them like this: Sector 1 has 1, Sector 2 has 4, Sector 3 has 6, and Sector 4 has 2.

That number matters because it changes how the guide should be written:

- this is not a farmable economy loop
- this is a collectible route problem
- the player wants to know how many are left, not just what they do

<h2 id="where-pure-lunum-comes-from">
  Where Pure Lunum Comes From
</h2>

Most Pure Lunum comes from three sources:

- **White Chests**
- **hidden side paths**
- **Red Zone completion rewards**

Public guides also note that some pieces are hidden behind red and blue crystal barriers, which means exploration alone is not always enough. Sometimes the real blocker is story progression or an ability unlock.

So if you feel like a sector is “fully explored” but your count is still short, the answer may be:

- you skipped a White Chest
- you did not clear a Red Zone
- you do not yet have the right ability to open the path

<h2 id="ability-gates-and-why-you-might-miss-one">
  Ability Gates and Why You Might Miss One
</h2>

This is one of the most important things missing from your previous version.

Some Pure Lunum-adjacent routes and sector collectibles are blocked by story abilities, especially **Cleansing**. The best current example is Lunum Mines, where collectible guides explicitly warn that some items are locked behind Cleansing and should be picked up later through backtracking or Unknown Signal cleanup.

That means if you cannot reach something on your first pass, do **not** automatically assume you missed a jump or failed to solve a puzzle. Sometimes the correct answer is simply:

- mark the spot
- come back when you have the required ability
- clean it up later in Unknown Signal Mode

If you are short on Pure Lunum, these are the kinds of places to check first.

### 1. Warehouse Red Zone in Lunum Mines

One public Lunum Mines collectible guide specifically notes that the **Warehouse Red Zone** rewards **Pure Lunum 1**. If you skipped that Red Zone, it is one of the first places worth revisiting.

### 4. Area 4 Red Zone reward that counts toward Nexus Tower

Another easy-to-misread one in Lunum Mines: a Red Zone physically located in **Area 4** is credited toward **Nexus Tower / Area 5** in the tracker, and that reward includes **Pure Lunum 4**. If your tracker count looks wrong, this is the sort of thing that causes confusion.

<h2 id="can-you-come-back-for-missed-pure-lunum">
  Can You Come Back for Missed Pure Lunum?
</h2>

Usually, yes.

Public collectible guides consistently treat **Unknown Signal Mode** as Pragmata's cleanup mode, and at least the current Lunum Mines coverage says nothing there is permanently missable because you can come back after the story.

That is one of the best things about Pure Lunum routing: even though it is limited, it is generally not a one-chance-only resource. If you missed a White Chest because you lacked Cleansing or skipped a Red Zone, postgame cleanup is usually the answer.

<h2 id="what-pure-lunum-is-used-for">
  What Pure Lunum Is Used For
</h2>

Pure Lunum matters because it starts showing up in **higher-tier Shelter upgrades**.

The safest way to explain it is this:

- regular progression leans heavily on **Lunafilament**
- Pure Lunum starts appearing when upgrades move into higher-value tiers
- public guide coverage specifically ties it to higher-end upgrades for weapons, hacks, mod slots, hacking slots, and abilities like Thrusters or Critical Shot

One terminology note matters here. Official PlayStation coverage confirms that the Shelter's two central stations are the **Firmware Updater** and the **Unit Printer**. The Firmware Updater is used to enhance Hugh's suit, primary weapon, and Diana's hacking abilities, while the Unit Printer is where you spend Lunafilament to unlock weapon units and hacking nodes permanently for your loadout.

So unless you have a direct UI screenshot proving otherwise, it is safer to write that Pure Lunum is used for **high-tier Shelter upgrades** rather than hard-writing a single station name.

<h2 id="what-to-upgrade-first-with-pure-lunum">
  What to Upgrade First with Pure Lunum
</h2>

Once the player knows where Pure Lunum is, this is the next question they actually care about.

For most runs, the safest priority order is:

1. **your main weapon's high-tier upgrade**
2. **the next major combat or hacking enhancement you actively use**
3. **extra slots or late-game utility upgrades**
4. **everything cosmetic or lower-impact after that**

The reason is simple: Pure Lunum is too limited to spend on things that do not immediately improve your main build.

<h2 id="best-upgrade-priority">
  Best Upgrade Priority
</h2>

### 1. Main weapon output

This is the best first use in most runs because late-game fights and enhanced encounters are where weapon breakpoints start to matter most.

### 2. Core hacking or combat tools you actually use

If your loadout leans heavily on one hacking route or one key combat ability, Pure Lunum should strengthen the things you already rely on, not experimental side gear.

### 3. Important slot or ability upgrades

Once your core damage plan is online, extra mod or hacking slot value starts to rise.

### 4. Low-impact or cosmetic spending last

Do not turn Pure Lunum into a vanity resource before your actual postgame build is finished.
