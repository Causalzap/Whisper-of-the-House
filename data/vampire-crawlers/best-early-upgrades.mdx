<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best early upgrades in Vampire Crawlers are Reroll Rank 1, Recovery Rank 1, Might, Max Health, Duration, and then Greed if you can already farm safely.**

A safe first-clear route is:

```text
Reroll -> Recovery -> Max Health -> Might -> Armor
```

A faster farming route is:

```text
Greed -> Might -> Recovery -> Reroll -> Growth
```

A combo-heavy route is:

```text
Reroll -> Might -> Cooldown -> Magnet -> Amount
```

The two most important early prices are:

- **Reroll Rank 1:** 250 coins
- **Recovery Rank 1:** 500 coins

Do not stop at the Power-Up shop, though. Good progression also means unlocking Arcana, saving Floor Chicken until you need it, using Cash Out instead of taking bad cards, avoiding early Blacksmith overspending, and not burning your last 0-cost starter at a blue Bing Upgrade machine.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="how-the-power-up-shop-works">
  How the Power-Up shop works
</h2>

The Power-Up shop is the main permanent stat system in Vampire Crawlers.

Cards, gems, and evolution rewards are temporary. Power-Ups are different because they carry into future runs and make every character stronger.

The best early question is not:

```text
What is the strongest upgrade?
```

It is:

```text
What problem is ending my runs right now?
```

If you are dying, buy survivability.

If you are surviving but killing too slowly, buy damage.

If your deck is messy, buy Reroll or Skip.

If you are stable and farming, buy Greed.

The shop also has a refund system, so do not treat early purchases like permanent mistakes. Your first-clear setup, farming setup, combo setup, and evolution-forcing setup can all be different.

<GuideCallout title="Best shop rule">
Use refunds as part of progression.

Buy what solves the current wall, then move Gold around when your goal changes.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="recovery-vs-greed-what-should-you-buy-first">
  Recovery vs Greed: what should you buy first?
</h2>

This is the biggest early upgrade debate because both answers can be correct.

### Buy Recovery first if you are dying

Recovery is best when runs end because of chip damage across long floors.

Buy Recovery first if:

- you reach bosses at low HP
- you are still learning enemy intent
- awkward hands make you take repeated hits
- Floor Chicken drops are not enough
- you want the safest first-clear route

### Buy Greed first if you are already stable

Greed is an economy upgrade. It does not help you win the current fight, but it can accelerate every future purchase.

Buy Greed first if:

- you already clear early floors
- you are farming easier maps
- your deck decisions are consistent
- you can use Cash Out and map pickups well
- you are comfortable refunding later

<GuideCallout title="Best Recovery vs Greed rule">
If your problem is dying, buy Recovery.

If your problem is slow account growth after stable clears, buy Greed.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="cooldown-magnet-and-crawler-slot">
  Cooldown, Magnet, and Crawler Slot
</h2>

These are not always your first upgrades, but they are some of the biggest mid-game power spikes.

### Cooldown

Cooldown gives **+1 Mana per rank**.

That means it directly changes your turn structure. More starting Mana lets you play longer chains earlier:

```text
0-cost starter -> 1-cost bridge -> 2-cost payoff -> 3-cost finisher
```

Buy Cooldown when your hands are good but too expensive. It is especially strong for Arca-style mana builds, evolved payoff cards, and any deck that wants to play multiple setup cards before a finisher.

### Magnet

Magnet improves draw-related effects.

Draw is a combo stat because it helps you find the next card in the chain. Once your deck has strong cards, Magnet-style draw value makes those cards show up together more often.

### Crawler Slot

Crawler Slot is expensive, but strategy-changing.

A second or third Crawler can combine effects that a single character cannot provide alone. This matters once you care about party shells instead of only solo character strength.

Do not rush Crawler Slot before you have enough unlocked Crawlers to make use of it. But once your roster is deeper, it becomes one of the most important upgrades in the game.

<h2 id="arcana-are-not-power-ups-but-they-change-upgrade-priority">
  Arcana are not Power-Ups, but they change upgrade priority
</h2>

Arcana are a separate progression layer from the Power-Up shop.

They matter enough that an upgrade guide should mention them because they can change which Power-Ups are best for your build.

First, unlock the Fortune Teller by finding the **Polentír Relic** in Furious Forest. After that, you can equip Arcana from the village.

<GuideTable
  caption="Early Arcana choices that change Vampire Crawlers upgrade priority."
  columns={arcanaColumns}
  rows={arcanaRows}
/>

### Practical examples

**Gennaro + Over the Top** wants Duration, Might, Reroll, and Cooldown because repeated Crawler access means more red-card damage and more low-cost spam.

**Imelda + Over the Top** wants Duration, Growth, Reroll, and Magnet because repeated Crawler access means more XP scaling and more chances to find the right cards.

**Your Shield My Liege** makes Armor much more attractive because armor can persist instead of disappearing at the end of a turn.

**Sharp Mind** makes Cooldown, Tome cards, and mana support better because unused Mana can carry forward into a larger future turn.

<h2 id="field-economy-floor-chicken-cash-out-and-bing-machines">
  Field economy: Floor Chicken, Cash Out, and Bing machines
</h2>

Not every upgrade decision happens in the village.

Some of the best early economy choices happen during a run.

<GuideTable
  caption="In-run economy and resource decisions that affect early upgrades."
  columns={fieldEconomyColumns}
  rows={fieldEconomyRows}
/>

### Save Floor Chicken

Floor Chicken is free healing, but it is easy to waste.

If you are healthy, leave it on the ground. Clear the floor, fight the boss, then return for the chicken before descending.

Do not eat a chicken at full HP just because it appears.

### Use Cash Out

If every reward is bad, do not take a bad card.

Cash Out turns a weak reward into coins. A few extra coins are often better than a card that makes your deck worse for the rest of the run.

### Be careful with Bing Upgrades

Blue Bing Upgrade machines can burn a card for a permanent in-run stat bonus.

That sounds good, but it can wreck your deck if you burn the wrong card.

The biggest warning:

```text
Do not burn your last 0-cost starter.
```

0-cost cards are the foundation of your combo chain. If you remove too many, your deck becomes full of bridges and payoffs with no reliable way to start 0 -> 1 -> 2.

<GuideCallout title="Best Bing rule">
Only burn a card if your deck still works without it.

A permanent stat bonus is not worth destroying your last starter, your only bridge, or a key evolution piece.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="blacksmith-is-a-late-game-investment">
  Blacksmith is a late-game investment
</h2>

The Blacksmith is powerful, but it is a common early Gold trap.

Once unlocked, the Blacksmith lets you permanently add empty Gem Sockets to discovered cards. That can eventually create extremely strong cards.

The problem is cost.

Adding a slot can start around **500 Gold** even for basic cards and can scale into the thousands for stronger cards. Early on, that Gold usually does more in the Power-Up shop.

A slotted Magic Wand is still weak if your account has low Might, no Recovery, no Reroll, and no economy.

Use this rule:

```text
Early game: Power-Ups first
Mid game: Power-Ups + key Arcana
Late game: Blacksmith once Gold is stable
```

Blacksmith becomes much better after your Greed and damage economy are already healthy.

<h2 id="when-to-refund-upgrades">
  When to refund upgrades
</h2>

Refund when your goal changes.

Use this pattern:

```text
First clears: Reroll / Recovery / Max Health / Might
Gold farming: Greed / Might / Growth / Reroll
Combo builds: Cooldown / Magnet / Reroll / Amount
Evolution forcing: Reroll / Skip / Growth / Might
Crawler synergy: Duration / Crawler Slot / Cooldown
```

Refunding is useful when:

- you bought Greed but cannot survive
- you bought survival but now want to farm
- you unlocked Cooldown and need Gold for it
- you unlocked Fortune Teller and want Arcana synergy
- you are chasing a specific evolution
- you are testing a new Crawler party

Do not be afraid to move Gold around.

The shop is designed to let you adapt.

<h2 id="characters-vs-power-ups-what-to-buy-first">
  Characters vs Power-Ups: what to buy first?
</h2>

New Crawlers are exciting, but Power-Ups usually make your whole account stronger.

A character buy is worth it early when it gives you a clear build direction:

- **Imelda** for XP and scaling
- **Gennaro** for red-card damage and Amount-style pressure

A Power-Up is worth it early when it fixes a problem every run:

The safe approach is:

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What are the best early upgrades in Vampire Crawlers?

The safest early upgrades are **Reroll**, **Recovery**, **Might**, **Max Health**, and **Duration**. If you can already clear early floors, **Greed** becomes a strong economy purchase.

### What should I buy first?

Buy **Reroll Rank 1** and **Recovery Rank 1** early. Reroll is cheap and improves reward choices, while Recovery helps you survive long floors.

### How much does Reroll cost?

Reroll Rank 1 costs **250 coins**.

### How much does Recovery cost?

Recovery Rank 1 costs **500 coins**.

### Is Cooldown good in Vampire Crawlers?

Yes. Cooldown gives **+1 Mana per rank**, making it one of the best upgrades for combo-heavy builds once you can afford it.

### Is Greed worth buying early?

Yes, if you can already survive. Greed is excellent for farming, but it does not help you win the current fight.

### Should I unlock Arcana early?

Yes. Arcana are separate from Power-Ups, but they can reshape builds. Over the Top, Your Shield My Liege, and Sharp Mind are especially important early examples.

### What does Sharp Mind do?

Sharp Mind lets unused Mana carry into the next turn, up to its cap. It pairs well with Cooldown, Tome cards, and expensive payoff cards.

### Should I use Bing Upgrade machines?

Use them carefully. Do not burn your last 0-cost starter, your only bridge, or a key evolution piece just for a stat bonus.

### Should I spend Gold at the Blacksmith early?

Usually no. Blacksmith slots are strong later, but early Gold usually does more in the Power-Up shop.

### Should I eat Floor Chicken immediately?

No, not if you are healthy. Leave Floor Chicken on the ground and return when you actually need healing.

### When should I refund upgrades?

Refund when your goal changes: first clear, Gold farming, combo build, evolution forcing, or Crawler synergy can all prefer different setups.

### What upgrades help combo builds most?

**Cooldown**, **Magnet**, **Reroll**, **Might**, **Amount**, and **Hand / Draw-style support** help combo builds most.

### What upgrades help evolution builds most?

**Reroll**, **Skip**, **Growth**, **Might**, **Cooldown**, and later **Area / Amount** help evolution builds most.
