<h2 id="what-counts-as-a-secret-character-in-vampire-crawlers">
  What counts as a secret character in Vampire Crawlers?
</h2>

A secret character in Vampire Crawlers is **not** part of the normal “unlock at the Inn and recruit with gold” flow used by the standard roster.

Normal crawlers are generally tied to:

- map progression
- coffins
- bosses
- enemy kill counts
- card-use milestones
- or clear account-wide requirements

Secret characters are different.

They are tied to **hidden or challenge-style conditions**, which usually means:

- an unusual one-run restriction
- a burst or overkill test
- an overdraw or glitch condition
- or a hidden challenge route that most players would never discover by accident

That is why hidden characters feel less like ordinary progression rewards and more like puzzle unlocks.

<GuideCallout title="Best way to think about secret characters">
Regular crawlers ask, “Did you reach the right map, boss, or milestone?”

Secret characters ask, “Can you deliberately build the right weird run and execute it correctly?”
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-to-unlock-leda-the-dealer">
  How to unlock Leda The Dealer
</h2>

The most commonly reported condition for **Leda The Dealer** is:

**defeat 50 enemies in a single Turboturn**

This makes Leda the hidden unlock that is easiest to understand conceptually.

It is not a normal survivability test.
It is a **burst-turn test**.

### What that means in practice

A Leda run wants:

- high **Area**
- strong **Amount**
- enough **Mana** to keep the sequence alive
- a combo chain that does not stall halfway through
- and a turn explosive enough to wipe a huge board all at once

So the real question is not:

> “Can I survive a long time?”

It is:

> “Can I build one giant turn that deletes 50 enemies in a single burst window?”

### Why players would chase Leda

Even without full launch-week consensus on every hidden character detail, Leda is attractive because it rewards a very clear kind of run:

- one big board-wipe turn
- one dramatic combo payoff
- one obvious challenge goal

### Best kinds of crawlers for a Leda attempt

The most helpful traits are:

- **damage**
- **Area**
- **Amount**
- **Mana**
- stronger finishing turns

So if your account is still developing, characters like **Gennaro**, **Pugnala**, and **Arca** make much more sense here than niche support-only picks.

<h2 id="how-to-unlock-missingn0">
  How to unlock MissingN0
</h2>

The most commonly reported condition for **MissingN0** is:

**trigger a Glitch State by overdrawing 100 cards in one run**

This is the hidden unlock most players will probably search for, because the condition sounds strange at first.

But its build logic is actually pretty readable:

### MissingN0 is the overdraw secret

You are not trying to build a generic “best run” deck.

You are trying to build a deck that draws so aggressively and so often that it crosses into overdraw territory.

That means MissingN0 runs want:

- **draw**
- **hand expansion**
- **Mana**
- clean combo sequencing
- turns that stay alive long enough to keep drawing

### Why MissingN0 is attractive

MissingN0 is interesting because it is one of the hidden paths most directly tied to the game’s deeper systems:

- combo flow
- card draw
- hand structure
- engine building

So even though it is a secret unlock, it also doubles as a kind of “have you really learned how this game works?” challenge.

### Best kinds of crawlers for a MissingN0 attempt

The most useful traits are:

- **Draw**
- **Hand**
- **Mana**
- stable color triggers that keep turns moving

That makes characters like:

- **Poe**
- **Pasqualina**
- **Arca**
- and often **Pugnala**

look much better here than they do in a random normal run.

<GuideCallout title="Best MissingN0 rule">
Do not build MissingN0 like a generic strongest-deck run.

Build it like a draw engine whose real purpose is to push the run into overdraw territory.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-about-red-death-card-form">
  What about Red Death (Card Form)?
</h2>

This is the part of the hidden roster that needs the most caution.

Publicly, the safest thing to say is:

**Red Death (Card Form) should be treated as a hidden challenge-layer reward path, not as a fully ordinary standard-roster entry.**

That wording matters because launch-week reporting is less clean here than it is for the normal 21 crawlers.

### What is safe to say

Red Death is tied to the hidden layer.

That hidden layer overlaps with:

- boss language
- extreme challenge language
- reward language
- and in some discussion, playable-form language

### What is not safe to say too strongly

Do not write this as if it were already documented exactly the same way as Antonio, Imelda, or Pugnala.

The better launch-window framing is:

- this is a **hidden path**
- it is **not** part of the standard 21-character Inn roster coverage
- it may overlap with a boss or boss-style event
- and it should be treated more cautiously than MissingN0 or Leda until more direct proof is documented

### Why players still care

Even with the ambiguity, Red Death matters because it is the hidden unlock most likely to confuse players:

- Is it a boss?
- Is it a secret character?
- Is it both?
- Is it part of MissingN0?
- Or is it a separate reward path?

That is exactly why this guide keeps it in the hidden layer, but does not oversell certainty.

<h2 id="how-combo-and-trigger-colors-affect-secret-runs">
  How Combo and Trigger colors affect secret runs
</h2>

Hidden runs still obey the same basic logic as normal runs.

You still want to:

- play cards in ascending Mana order
- keep turns alive long enough to scale
- choose crawlers whose Trigger colors support what the run is trying to do

That means hidden attempts still care about deck identity.

If a hidden run wants:

- **burst** → you want damage, Area, and Amount
- **draw abuse** → you want Draw, Hand, and Mana
- **restricted consistency** → you want cleaner triggers and fewer dead turns

So even in secret content, choosing a crawler still means choosing a **color package and turn structure**.

<h2 id="do-you-need-a-full-party-for-secret-runs">
  Do you need a full party for secret runs?
</h2>

Not always, but it helps a lot.

Once you have extra **Crawler Slot** power-ups, hidden attempts get much easier because you can build cleaner shells:

- one crawler for safety
- one crawler for engine support
- one crawler for the actual challenge payoff

That matters especially for:

- **MissingN0**, where draw and Mana support are huge
- **Leda**, where burst support matters
- **Red Death**, where every bit of stability helps

<GuideCallout title="Best party-system rule for hidden runs">
Secret characters get much easier once your roster stops being one crawler deep.

If your account is still underdeveloped, grow the normal roster first.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="the-trickster-hidden-boss-explained">
  The Trickster hidden boss explained
</h2>

**The Trickster** matters because players often mix it up with secret-character content.

But it is better treated as a **hidden boss**, not as one of the currently tracked secret characters.

### Why The Trickster causes confusion

It lives in the same general category as hidden character content:

- obscure trigger conditions
- challenge-style discovery
- special rewards
- community-first documentation

So players naturally group it together with Leda, MissingN0, and Red Death discussion.

### The important distinction

The Trickster is valuable hidden content, but it is **not** the same thing as saying:

- “this is a hidden roster entry”
- “this adds a new standard crawler”
- “this belongs on the ordinary 21-character list”

### Why it is still worth mentioning

If a player is searching hidden content broadly, they usually do not care only about recruitable characters.
They also care about:

- hidden bosses
- hidden rewards
- hidden triggers
- and whether any of those are required for completion or progression

So The Trickster belongs in the hidden-content conversation even if it is not a standard secret-character unlock.
