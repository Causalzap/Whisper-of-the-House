<h2 id="how-to-pick-the-best-beginner-character-in-vampire-crawlers">
  How to pick the best beginner character in Vampire Crawlers
</h2>

A beginner-friendly character in Vampire Crawlers usually does at least one of these things well:

- gives you value immediately when played
- supports simple colors you are already likely to use
- makes bad early draws less punishing
- helps your first party setups come together faster
- does not need a strange unlock chain or advanced combo plan to feel useful

That is why the best beginner picks are **not** always the same as the best endgame or tier-list picks.

The most important mechanic behind that choice is the **Combo** system.

- Play cards in **ascending Mana cost order** each turn: **0 → 1 → 2 → 3**
- Each correctly sequenced card powers up the **next** card in the chain
- That means deck flow and hand quality often matter more than raw card rarity

Character choice matters because every crawler also has a **Trigger color**.
If a character says **“when a red card is played”** or **“when a purple card is played,”** that is telling you which color package that crawler wants your deck to lean toward.

So when you choose a beginner character, you are really choosing three things at once:

- the crawler card effect itself
- the color your deck should prioritize
- the kind of combo turns that will feel easiest to build

Early on, the most practical traits are:

- **Armor**
- **Mana**
- **Amount**
- **Hand**
- **Draw**
- **easy damage scaling**
- **easy unlock value**

<GuideCallout title="Best beginner rule">
Do not ask, “Which character is strongest at full power?”

Ask, “Which character helps me build cleaner turns, progress my account, and unlock the next useful character fastest?”
</GuideCallout>

- how quickly the character helps your account grow

- **Antonio** = safest first few runs

<h2 id="best-overall-beginner-character-imelda-belpaese">

**Imelda Belpaese** is the best overall beginner character because she improves both your **runs** and your **account progress** at the same time.

Her value is simple:

- **When Played**: gain **15 XP**
- **Trigger**: gain **1 XP Growth** when a **yellow** card is played

That makes her extremely forgiving for new players.
Even if a run is messy, Imelda still helps it become productive.

Why Imelda is rank 1 in this guide:

- she unlocks almost immediately after clearing **Mad Forest**
- she only costs **10 gold**
- she helps every future run level faster
- she directly supports your broader account progression
- she is also part of the unlock path for **Pasqualina**

**Unlock method:** clear **Mad Forest**

**Recruit cost:** **10 gold**

**Best for:** players who want the most efficient “fresh account” recommendation, not just the safest first fight

<h2 id="best-safe-starter-antonio-belpaese">
  Best safe starter: Antonio Belpaese
</h2>

If you want the most straightforward answer to **“Who should I actually use in my first few runs?”**, the answer is still **Antonio Belpaese**.

His value is extremely clean:

- **When Played**: gain **3 Armor**
- **Trigger**: increase damage **10%** when a **red** card is played

Why Antonio stays near the top:

- he is free from the tutorial
- Armor helps cover beginner mistakes
- red-card decks are easy to understand
- he teaches combo order without asking you to build around a narrow engine

That is why Antonio is one of the most common **#1 beginner** recommendations in other lists.
The only reason he is **#2** here is that this article weights **total account progression** more heavily than pure first-run safety.

**Unlock method:** tutorial character

**Recruit cost:** **free**

**Best for:** first runs, clean fundamentals, and players who want the least complicated entry point

<h2 id="best-easy-damage-character-gennaro-belpaese">
  Best easy early damage character: Gennaro Belpaese
</h2>

If you want a crawler that feels immediately stronger in combat, **Gennaro Belpaese** is one of the best beginner pickups in the game.

His effects are exactly the kind of thing new players benefit from:

- **When Played**: gain **2 Amount**
- **Trigger**: deal **60 damage** when a **red** card is played

Why Gennaro is such a strong early unlock:

- direct power with almost no setup
- strong fit in simple red attack shells
- great follow-up after Antonio or Imelda
- easy to slot into early party builds that still want straightforward damage

**Unlock method:** defeat **Mantichana** in **Mad Forest**

**Recruit cost:** **700 gold**

**Best for:** beginners who want their next unlock to feel immediately stronger in combat

<h2 id="best-first-major-milestone-character-pugnala-provola">
  Best first major milestone character: Pugnala Provola
</h2>

**Pugnala Provola** is one of the first characters that feels like a real roster milestone instead of a small upgrade.

Her effects are excellent for stabilizing stronger runs:

- **When Played**: increase damage by **20%**
- **Trigger**: **draw 1 card** when a **yellow** card is played

That mix of damage plus draw is exactly why Pugnala is so valuable:

- she raises your floor by making turns hit harder
- she raises your ceiling by helping you keep drawing into better lines
- she rewards learning coffin routes early
- she stays relevant far beyond the beginner phase

**Unlock method:** find the coffin in **Berserk Wood** and defeat the flying skeleton guardians

**Recruit cost:** **1,440 gold**

**Best for:** players ready for their first real power spike and first meaningful coffin chase

<h2 id="best-next-step-character-pasqualina-belpaese">
  Best next-step character: Pasqualina Belpaese
</h2>

Once you understand why **Hand** and **purple combo turns** matter, **Pasqualina Belpaese** becomes one of the smartest early unlocks in the game.

Her effects are:

- **When Played**: gain **10 Area**
- **Trigger**: gain **1 Hand** when a **purple** card is played

Pasqualina is where many beginners start to understand that the strongest crawler is not always the one with the biggest raw stat number.
Sometimes the best crawler is the one that makes your **whole hand** function better.

That is also why some competing guides rank her first or near the top.
A strong Pasqualina run can snowball very hard once your deck supports purple sequencing and hand expansion.

Why she is still below Imelda and Antonio here:

- she asks more from your deck construction
- she is much better once combo logic already clicks
- her real value is in smoother turn structure, not immediate beginner comfort

**Unlock method:** reach **level 20 with Imelda** in **Inlaid Library**

**Recruit cost:** **110 gold**

**Best for:** players whose early runs are already stable and who want stronger combo structure

</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="best-later-beginner-upgrade-arca-ladonna">
  Best later beginner upgrade: Arca Ladonna

**Arca Ladonna** is where beginner lists and general tier lists often split the hardest.

His effects are extremely strong:

- **When Played**: gain **3 Mana**
- **Trigger**: gain **1 Mana** when a **purple** card is played

That is a huge deal in a game where better combo turns usually come from being able to keep playing cards without running dry.

Why advanced or general tier lists rate him so highly:

- Mana is one of the best stats in the game
- better Mana means smoother combo turns
- smoother combo turns mean better scaling on almost every deck
- he supports a system-level strength, not just a single card color payoff

Why he is only **#7** in this article:

- he is better once you already understand combo sequencing
- his strength is more obvious to players who think in deck flow rather than single-card value
- he is not the easiest character for a fresh account to immediately appreciate

**Unlock method:** play **Fire Wand** **100 times**

**Recruit cost:** **420 gold**

**Best for:** players moving from “beginner survival” into “I want my whole deck to function better”

<h2 id="power-ups-to-buy-before-you-force-character-unlocks">
  Power-Ups to buy before you force character unlocks
</h2>

Character rankings matter less if you cannot afford to recruit the characters.
That is why your first real progression layer is not only the roster — it is also the **Power-Up** board.

The most important early beginner reminder is this:

- buy **Greed** early to accelerate gold income
- buy **Might** early so your baseline combat improves faster
- buy **Crawler Slot** early once you can afford it, because that is what lets party recommendations actually matter

**Crawler Slot** is especially important because you can eventually bring **up to three Crawlers into one run** once you buy the expansion.
That is what turns “good solo character” thinking into “good party synergy” thinking.

<h2 id="how-party-system-changes-beginner-character-choice">
  How the party system changes beginner character choice
</h2>

One of the biggest beginner mistakes is judging every crawler as if they only matter in isolation.

That stops being true once you buy extra **Crawler Slot** power-ups and start bringing multiple crawlers into the same run.

At that point, you care about:

- who adds Armor
- who adds Mana
- who adds Draw
- who increases Hand
- who helps a color package function more smoothly

That means some characters are strong not because they carry by themselves, but because they make a whole run feel cleaner.

For example:

- **Antonio** stabilizes sloppy early turns
- **Gennaro** adds easy direct pressure
- **Pasqualina** improves hand scaling
- **Poe** improves draw consistency
- **Arca** helps expensive turns stay playable
- **Imelda** keeps progression value high even on imperfect runs

That is why the best beginner roster is usually a mix of:

- one safe stabilizer
- one straightforward value or damage crawler
- one support or engine crawler

- Antonio stabilizes the front end

This is one of the most appealing early growth-plus-consistency setups.

- Pugnala gives damage and draw pressure
- Imelda keeps progression and yellow scaling relevant

This is one of the best beginner parties for players who are done learning basics and want more reliable engine turns.

This is the best early combo-engine learner party.

Why it works:

- Pasqualina increases hand size
- Poe draws more cards
- Arca gives the Mana needed to actually convert that extra hand quality into bigger turns

This trio does not look as “safe” as Antonio teams, but it teaches the real logic of stronger Vampire Crawlers decks faster than almost anything else.

<h2 id="which-characters-are-not-the-best-first-pick">
  Which characters are not the best first pick
</h2>

Some crawlers can be strong without being the best early beginner target.

### Christine Davain

Christine is more awkward for a fresh account because the **Pentagram** chain is not an immediate early unlock.

You first need Pentagram to appear, which usually means pushing to roughly **level 35** in a single run before it starts showing up consistently.
Once you can find and play it, Christine becomes much more realistic.

That makes Christine a better later target than a first-wave beginner goal.

### Lama Ladonna

Lama can be powerful, but finishing a dungeon with **10% or more Curse** is not the smoothest ask for newer players.

### Secret characters

Secret characters are cool, but they are challenge goals, not your first stable roster upgrades.

<h2 id="why-crawler-card-effects-matter-more-than-raw-rarity">

Beginners often make the mistake of assuming the hardest unlock must also be the best beginner pick.

That is not how this game works.

The better questions are:

- does the character help me survive?
- does the character help me play my hand?
- does the character support a color I actually use?
- does the character improve my combo turns?
- does the unlock cost make sense for where my account is right now?

That is why early beginner favorites are usually characters that give:

- Armor
- Mana
- Amount
- Hand size
- Draw support
- easy damage scaling
- or progression value

Not every strong character is a strong beginner character.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Who is the best beginner character in Vampire Crawlers?

**Imelda Belpaese** is the best overall beginner character if your goal is total early progression.
If you only care about the safest first few runs, **Antonio** is the simpler starting pick.

### Why do some guides rank Antonio or Pasqualina above Imelda?

Because they are using a different lens.
Antonio is the safest pure starter, while Pasqualina can become stronger in a single run once you understand purple combo turns and hand scaling.
This guide ranks **Imelda** first because it values **account progression and unlock efficiency** most.

### How does the Combo system affect character choice?

A lot.
You want to play cards in **ascending Mana order** so each card powers up the next one.
That makes draw, hand size, Mana, and color-trigger synergy much more important than they first appear.

### What does Trigger color mean in Vampire Crawlers?

It means the crawler gets extra value whenever you play a card of that color.
So choosing a crawler is also choosing which color package your deck should naturally prefer.

### Is Antonio still good for beginners?

Yes.
Antonio is one of the best pure beginner picks because he gives Armor, scales off red cards, and is extremely easy to understand.

### Is Pasqualina worth unlocking early?

Yes, once your early runs are stable.
She is one of the best characters for learning why hand size and purple sequencing matter.

### How do you unlock Poe Ratcho?

First unlock **Garlic** by picking up **5 Floor Chickens**.
After that, play **Garlic** **25 times** to unlock **Poe Ratcho**, then recruit him for **360 gold**.

### How do you unlock Arca Ladonna?

First unlock **Fire Wand** by destroying **20 light sources**.
Then play **Fire Wand** **100 times** to unlock **Arca**, then recruit him for **420 gold**.

### Why is Arca lower here than in some tier lists?

Because Arca is one of the strongest **high-ceiling** characters, not necessarily the cleanest **fresh-account** recommendation.
He is amazing once you understand how Mana drives combo turns.

### What Power-Up should beginners buy before chasing more characters?

Start with **Greed** so gold income ramps faster.
Then add early combat power like **Might**, and buy **Crawler Slot** once you can so party-based recommendations actually become relevant.

### What is the best beginner party in Vampire Crawlers?

A very safe beginner party is **Antonio + Gennaro + Imelda**.
A stronger combo-learning party is **Pasqualina + Poe + Arca** once your roster and Power-Ups can support it.
