<h2 id="how-character-unlocks-work-in-vampire-crawlers">
  How character unlocks work in Vampire Crawlers
</h2>

Character unlocks in Vampire Crawlers are not tied to just one system.

Some characters unlock because you:

- reach a new map
- open a coffin
- defeat a boss
- farm a specific enemy type
- collect enough coins
- recover enough total HP

But others unlock because you:

- play a specific card enough times
- clear a dungeon with a modifier
- complete a run under a special challenge
- push the game’s combo, Wildcard, or overdraw systems far enough

That is why the roster can feel slow at first and then suddenly open up once you understand how the game tracks progression.

<GuideCallout title="Best way to think about the roster">
Do not treat Vampire Crawlers characters like one big checklist.

They are really four different unlock systems:

1. early map and resource unlocks  
2. coffin characters  
3. boss and enemy-kill characters  
4. card-based and secret challenge characters
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="party-system-and-why-it-changes-your-unlock-priority">
  Party system and why it changes your unlock priority
</h2>

One of the biggest mistakes players make is treating character unlocks like they only matter one at a time.

That is not how Vampire Crawlers works once the **party system** starts to matter.

As your account grows, unlocking more characters is not just about finding your next main. It is about building a roster that can **cover each other’s weaknesses**.

That changes unlock priority in a big way.

A character that looks average on its own can become much better in a party because its Crawler card offers:

- free armor
- extra mana
- hand size
- area
- amount
- draw support
- or a passive that scales off a color package you already want to play

This matters because party building pushes the game away from “Who is the strongest single crawler?” and toward “Which three crawlers create the cleanest engine together?”

That means unlocking a new crawler is not always valuable because that character is individually busted.

Sometimes it is valuable because that character unlocks a **better three-crawler shell**.

So when you read a character unlock guide, do not just ask:

- is this character strong?

Also ask:

- does this character improve my best party?
- does this character add a color trigger I can actually use?
- does this character patch armor, mana, draw, or reach problems?
- is this a solo carry, or a party enabler?

<GuideCallout title="Best party-system rule">
Do not judge a crawler only as a solo pick.

In Vampire Crawlers, a good unlock often becomes a great unlock once it gives your party a missing piece like armor, mana, hand size, or better color scaling.
</GuideCallout>

</h2>

Unlock conditions tell you **how** to get a character.

Crawler card effects tell you **why unlocking that character matters right now**.

That is the extra layer a lot of character guides miss.

In Vampire Crawlers, a character is not just a roster checkbox. It is also:

- a **When Played** effect
- a **color-trigger passive**
- a possible **party slot upgrade**
- and sometimes a better resource engine for your next set of runs

That is why the table above includes both the unlock condition and the Crawler card information.

Two characters with equally easy unlocks can have very different practical value depending on whether they add:

- Armor
- Mana
- Hand
- Amount
- Area
- draw support
- or stronger scaling for a color package you already use

<GuideCallout title="Best way to judge a new unlock">
Do not just ask whether a character is easy to unlock.

Ask whether that character improves your current deck engine, party setup, or color package immediately.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="do-you-need-specific-maps-relics-or-kill-conditions">
  Do you need specific maps, relics, or kill conditions?
</h2>

Yes, and this is one of the biggest reasons the roster feels layered.

### You need specific maps

A lot of the roster is tied to the main progression maps:

- **Mad Forest**
- **Inlaid Library / Library Sanctum**
- **Dairy Plant / Milk Factory**
- **Gallo Tower**

### You often need specific kill conditions

Some unlocks require:

- a boss kill
- a specific enemy type
- a cumulative kill total
- or a run cleared under a special condition

### Relics matter more for secret content

Most regular characters do not need relic-heavy setup.

But the secret side of the game often does, especially when the unlock depends on:

- Wildcard-only clears
- overdraw loops
- glitch states
- extreme combo or overkill setups

That is why the regular roster and secret roster should not be treated as the same kind of content.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you unlock all characters in Vampire Crawlers?

You unlock characters through tutorial progress, map progression, coffins, total resource milestones, enemy or boss kill counts, card play counts, and special run conditions. Most characters still need to be bought with gold after you meet the unlock condition.

### How do you unlock Imelda in Vampire Crawlers?

Play a run in **Mad Forest**.

### How do you unlock Pugnala Provola?

<p>Find the coffin in <strong>Berserk Wood</strong>. If you want the full route, use the <Link href="/vampire-crawlers/secret-characters-how-to-unlock  ">Pugnala unlock guide</Link>.</p>

### How do you unlock Bianca Ramba?

Defeat the **Milk Elemental** boss in **Dairy Plant**.

### How do you unlock Arca Ladonna?

Play the **Fire Wand** card **100 times**.

### How do you unlock Poe Ratcho?

Garlic must be unlocked first by picking up **5 Floor Chickens**. After that, play **Garlic** **25 times** to unlock Poe Ratcho.

### How do you unlock MissingN0?

<p>Trigger a <strong>Glitch State</strong> by overdrawing <strong>100 cards</strong> in one run. For the full setup, use the <Link href="/vampire-crawlers/secret-characters-how-to-unlock">MissingN0 unlock guide</Link>.</p>

### Are there secret characters in Vampire Crawlers?

Yes. Vampire Crawlers has a hidden challenge layer with secret characters like **Leda The Dealer**, **Red Death**, and **MissingN0**.

### What is the easiest character to unlock early?

Imelda is one of the easiest early unlocks, followed by natural early progression targets like **Gennaro** and **Pugnala**.

### How do you unlock Christine Davain?

Pentagram only appears once you reach around level 35 in a single run; push to 40 to be safe. Once Pentagram starts appearing, find it and play it to unlock Christine Davain.
