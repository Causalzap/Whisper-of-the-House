<h2 id="voidling-bound-golden-egg-locations">
  Voidling Bound Golden Egg locations
</h2>

Golden Eggs are some of the most valuable collectibles in **Voidling Bound** because they do not just fill a checklist. Each one hatches into a secret Voidling variant, and those variants can matter later for Breeding, Splicing, rare traits, or Voidpedia completion.

The route logic is different from normal mission clearing. Many eggs sit behind a side platform, a building detour, a puzzle interaction, or a route that you would skip during a fast objective clear.

<h2 id="centennial-bloom-golden-egg">
  Centennial Bloom Golden Egg
</h2>

**Hatch result:** Qwebeck Kwipeck

Centennial Bloom is the best first Golden Egg route because the landmark is easy to remember. Follow the plant-platform section until you can reach the upper cliff route, then look along the cliff path instead of dropping straight back to the main objective.

This is a good mission to practice egg cleanup because the route is short, readable, and not too punishing. If you only came for Qwebeck Kwipeck, grab the egg and return to the ship.

<h2 id="secret-stronghold-golden-eggs">
  Secret Stronghold Golden Eggs
</h2>

**Hatch results:** Experimental Gwigoon / Telluric Gilick

Secret Stronghold has two Golden Eggs, so do not treat it like a quick single pickup. The first route cue is the lower area near the start. Drop down and check the side route before you commit too far into the stronghold.

The second egg is tied to the large antenna building. Go inside, move upstairs, and search the upper interior path instead of only clearing enemies outside. This mission is worth doing as a dedicated two-egg route because the landmarks are clear once you know them.

<h2 id="search-and-rescue-golden-eggs">
  Search and Rescue Golden Eggs
</h2>

**Hatch results:** Andromeda Gilick / Charcoal Kerapin

Search and Rescue is larger than the early Vireo routes, so plan it as a focused cleanup run. After opening the puzzle route, climb toward the upper path and hook left instead of following only the obvious objective direction.

The first egg is tied to that upper-left route. The second is later, near the building around the final cleansing area. If you are only missing one egg, use the mission checklist to avoid replaying the entire mission blindly.

<h2 id="cleaning-the-eyrie-golden-eggs">
  Cleaning the Eyrie Golden Eggs
</h2>

**Hatch results:** Arcing Gwigoon / Galvanic Ur-Sek

Cleaning the Eyrie has two Golden Eggs, but the route is easier if you break it into two landmarks. Start by checking the first building carefully before moving too far into the mission. Do not assume the egg route is always outside; one of the key pickups is tied to the early structure.

The second egg depends on the nearby fire crystal interaction. Use the fire crystal, then inspect the newly opened path behind the structure instead of returning immediately to the main route. This mission is a good example of why Golden Egg cleanup should be slower than a normal objective clear.

<h2 id="subterranean-expedition-golden-egg">
  Subterranean Expedition Golden Egg
</h2>

**Hatch result:** Transcendent Anami

Subterranean Expedition has one Golden Egg, but it is easy to miss because it is tied to a puzzle-style route. Explore the cave rooms until you reach the fire crystal interaction, then use it to melt the larger ice blockage.

After the blockage opens, search the side path before leaving the chamber. If Transcendent Anami is your only goal, this is a return-to-ship-friendly pickup once you know the ice-door route.

<h2 id="storm-breaker-golden-egg">
  Storm Breaker Golden Egg
</h2>

**Hatch result:** Argon Anami

Storm Breaker has one Golden Egg and a very clear environmental cue. Clear the first area, follow the route until the upper path opens, then check the red pond side area instead of moving straight toward the finish.

This is another good return-to-ship-friendly egg if you are only collecting Argon Anami. Once you know the red pond landmark, the route becomes much easier to repeat.

<h2 id="navigate-the-maze-golden-eggs">
  Navigate the Maze Golden Eggs
</h2>

**Hatch results:** Golden Nugget Kwipeck / Hellfire Packuran

Navigate the Maze has two Golden Eggs and should be treated as a dedicated collectible route. Near the start, turn right and climb toward the high route. The first egg is tied to that elevated path, so do not rush into the lower maze immediately.

For the second egg, drop down later and follow the lower side-wall route. This mission also contains several Mutagens and a Quacky, so it is tempting to do everything at once. If your build is not comfortable yet, focus on the Golden Eggs first and clean the rest later.

<h2 id="pull-the-plug-golden-eggs">
  Pull the Plug Golden Eggs
</h2>

**Hatch results:** Chthonian Morfang / Heavenly Packuran

Pull the Plug has two Golden Eggs and is better handled as a late Aulis cleanup route. The first egg is on the high purple-platform route. If you see the purple-platform path, slow down and check the elevated route before dropping back into the main flow.

The second egg is lower in the later part of the mission. Continue through the route after the first pickup and watch for a lower hidden path instead of assuming the mission only has the high platform egg. Bring a build you trust, because this is not the best place to learn Golden Egg routing for the first time.

<h2 id="end-to-the-unknown-golden-eggs">
  End to the Unknown Golden Eggs
</h2>

**Hatch results:** Locust Packuran / Galvanic Ur-Sek

End to the Unknown is the final Golden Egg cleanup target and should not be rushed. The first egg is on the hidden mountain climb, so look for the route that pulls you away from the obvious forward path. If you are only missing this egg, focus on the climb and leave safely once you have it.

The second egg appears later, after the lift-side route. Search the side room and final hidden corner before leaving the mission. This is a good final sweep once your build is strong enough to explore without pressure.

<h2 id="faq">
  Voidling Bound Golden Egg FAQ
</h2>

### What do Golden Eggs do in Voidling Bound?

Golden Eggs hatch secret Voidlings outside the normal genetic tree. These variants can have rare Natures, unusual traits, unique looks, and future Splicing value.

### Can I return to the ship after grabbing a Golden Egg?

Yes. If the Golden Egg is your only goal, grab it and return to the ship instead of forcing a full mission clear.

### Should I release Golden Egg Voidlings?

Not immediately. Hatch them, inspect their Nature, stats, traits, and possible Splicing value first.

### What does the Centennial Bloom Golden Egg hatch?

The Centennial Bloom Golden Egg hatches Qwebeck Kwipeck.

### What does the Secret Stronghold Golden Egg hatch?

Secret Stronghold has two Golden Eggs. They hatch Experimental Gwigoon and Telluric Gilick.

### What does the Search and Rescue Golden Egg hatch?

Search and Rescue has two Golden Eggs. They hatch Andromeda Gilick and Charcoal Kerapin.

### What does the Cleaning the Eyrie Golden Egg hatch?

Cleaning the Eyrie has two Golden Eggs. They hatch Arcing Gwigoon and Galvanic Ur-Sek.

### What does the Subterranean Expedition Golden Egg hatch?

The Subterranean Expedition Golden Egg hatches Transcendent Anami.

### What does the Storm Breaker Golden Egg hatch?

The Storm Breaker Golden Egg hatches Argon Anami.

### What does the Navigate the Maze Golden Egg hatch?

Navigate the Maze has two Golden Eggs. They hatch Golden Nugget Kwipeck and Hellfire Packuran.

### What does the Pull the Plug Golden Egg hatch?

Pull the Plug has two Golden Eggs. They hatch Chthonian Morfang and Heavenly Packuran.

### What does the End to the Unknown Golden Egg hatch?

End to the Unknown has two Golden Eggs. They hatch Locust Packuran and Galvanic Ur-Sek.

### Should I collect Golden Eggs during timer runs?

Usually no. Golden Egg routes often pull you away from the fastest objective path, so it is better to separate timer runs from egg cleanup.
