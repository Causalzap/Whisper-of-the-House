<h2 id="voidling-bound-all-collectibles-guide">
  Voidling Bound all collectibles guide
</h2>

**Voidling Bound** has three main collectible goals that matter for mission cleanup: **Mutagens**, **Golden Eggs**, and **Quackies**.

They are not equally important at the same point in the game. If your Voidling is weak, Mutagens matter first because they unlock evolution paths. If you want secret Voidlings, Golden Eggs matter. If you are finishing the collection, Quackies become the cleanup target.

The best way to plan a route is:

<strong>Check what upgrade or collection goal you actually need, then replay the mission that solves that problem.</strong>

For focused routes, use the <Link href="/voidling-bound/golden-egg-locations">Golden Egg Locations Guide</Link> and the <Link href="/voidling-bound/quackies-locations">Quacky Locations Guide</Link>. This page is the combined mission checklist and resource planner.

Use this table as a route selector.

caption="Voidling Bound Mutagen type farming lookup."

These missions can contain multiple element types, so use the table as a route selector rather than a guarantee that every pickup in the mission is the same element. If you are not sure which element you are missing, **Navigate the Maze** is the best mixed-element cleanup target because it can cover all five Mutagen types in one replay route.

Do not use Navigate the Maze as your first farming mission, though. It is better after your build is stable. For early farming, start with the more specific missions listed first, then use Navigate the Maze when you need a broad catch-up route.

<h2 id="faq">
  Voidling Bound all collectibles FAQ
</h2>

### What collectible should I prioritize first?

Prioritize Mutagens first if your next evolution node is blocked. Then collect Golden Eggs for secret Voidlings, and save Quackies for completion cleanup.

### Where should I farm Organic Mutagens?

Start with Centennial Bloom, Cleaning the Eyrie, and Navigate the Maze. For the full route selector, see the <a href="#mutagen-type-lookup">Mutagen type lookup table</a>.

### Where should I farm Pyro Mutagens?

Check Centennial Bloom, Storm Breaker, and Navigate the Maze first. For route cues by element, use the <a href="#mutagen-type-lookup">Mutagen type lookup table</a>.

### Where should I farm Cryo Mutagens?

Secret Stronghold, Subterranean Expedition, and Navigate the Maze are the best missions to check first. See the <a href="#mutagen-type-lookup">Mutagen type lookup table</a> for the full breakdown.

### Where should I farm Cyber Mutagens?

Start with Secret Stronghold, Navigate the Maze, and Pull the Plug. Use the <a href="#mutagen-type-lookup">Mutagen type lookup table</a> if you need the route cues.

### Where should I farm Plasma Mutagens?

Navigate the Maze, Pull the Plug, and End to the Unknown are the best late-game checks. For the full list, jump to the <a href="#mutagen-type-lookup">Mutagen type lookup table</a>.


### How do I know which Mutagen I need?

Open the Evolution Chamber, choose the Voidling you are building, and check the next locked evolution node. Farm the element required by that node.

### Are Golden Eggs worth collecting early?

Yes. Golden Eggs hatch secret Voidlings, and some variants may become useful later for traits, perks, or Splicing plans.

### What does the Centennial Bloom Golden Egg hatch?

It hatches **Qwebeck Kwipeck**, one of the earliest secret Voidlings you can collect.

### What does the Secret Stronghold Golden Egg hatch?

Secret Stronghold has two Golden Eggs. They hatch **Experimental Gwigoon** and **Telluric Gilick**.

### What does the Navigate the Maze Golden Egg hatch?

Navigate the Maze has two Golden Eggs. They hatch **Golden Nugget Kwipeck** and **Hellfire Packuran**.

### Are Quackies useful for builds?

No. Quackies are mainly for completion and ship-side collection display. They are worth collecting, but they do not replace Mutagens for power progression.

### Should I collect everything during a time-star run?

Usually no. Hidden collectible routes and timer routes often conflict. Do the time star separately after you know the mission layout.
