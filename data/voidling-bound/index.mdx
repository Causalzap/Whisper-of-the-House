<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Voidling Bound**, your early goal is to build one reliable Voidling, clear missions, collect Mutagens and Research Points, then replay missions for missed stars, Golden Eggs, Quackies, and upgrades.

Start on a comfortable difficulty, bind with **Kwipeck**, learn dash timing and healing, use the **Evolution Chamber** for Mutagen upgrades, spend **Research Points** at the **Cerebrum Enhancer**, and unlock the **Atlas Terminal** so you can choose missions and replay old ones safely. Save deep Breeding, Splicing, Catalyzer, and Abyss planning until you have more species and resources.

<h2 id="voidling-bound-guide">
  Voidling Bound guide
</h2>

**Voidling Bound** is a monster-taming action RPG built around short mission runs, ship-side upgrades, creature evolution, and long-term build crafting. You choose a mission, bind with one Voidling, cleanse corruption, collect resources, return to the ship, then use those resources to make your lineup stronger.

The game is not a traditional open-world monster collector. Most progression happens through repeatable missions and upgrade systems. That means replaying old missions is not wasted time. It is how you farm resources, train Voidlings, collect hidden items, and prepare for harder content.

The most important early concepts are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>build one reliable Voidling first,</li>
  <li>use Mutagens for individual evolution choices,</li>
  <li>use Research Points for species-wide upgrades,</li>
  <li>use the Atlas Terminal to replay missions and check missing objectives,</li>
  <li>collect Golden Eggs for secret Voidlings,</li>
  <li>save Quackies for completion cleanup,</li>
  <li>and treat Breeding, Splicing, Catalyzers, and Abyss planning as later goals.</li>
</ul>

This page is the hub for the full Voidling Bound guide cluster. Use it to choose the right next guide, understand the basic route, and avoid early resource mistakes.

<h2 id="what-to-do-first">
  What to do first in Voidling Bound
</h2>

The early game is easier when you treat it as a learning route, not a race. Your starter can carry the opening missions, but it still needs upgrades before you keep pushing harder routes.

<GuideTable
caption="Voidling Bound early progression route."
columns={startColumns}
rows={startRows}
/>

The safest early mindset is simple: **do not treat every new mission as the next required push. Replaying old missions is part of progression.**

<h2 id="difficulty-and-first-stats">
  Difficulty and first stat choices
</h2>

Pick the difficulty that matches how much action pressure you want. If you mainly care about exploration, collection, and creature building, start easier. If you want a more punishing action game, go higher. The best first difficulty is the one that lets you learn without making every mistake feel fatal.

For your first stat points, do not overcomplicate the choice. Early Voidling Bound rewards survival and consistency more than perfect optimization.

<GuideTable
caption="Voidling Bound first stat priorities."
columns={statColumns}
rows={statRows}
/>

A simple first rule works well: pick **Vitality** if you are dying, **Strength** if fights take too long, and **Recuperation** if movement or recovery feels uncomfortable.

The **Evolution Chamber** is your first major creature-building system. It uses Mutagens to unlock new abilities, elements, body changes, and combat options for one Voidling.

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Evolution is an individual Voidling upgrade path. Spend Mutagens on the build you actually plan to use.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Do not spend Mutagens just because an option is available. Ask what your current build needs:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>more safe ranged pressure,</li>
  <li>more damage,</li>
  <li>better movement,</li>
  <li>a stronger element for upcoming missions,</li>
  <li>or a playstyle that is easier for you to control.</li>
</ul>

A simple build you can pilot safely is better than a fancy build that gets you killed.

<h2 id="cerebrum-enhancer">
  Cerebrum Enhancer and Research Points
</h2>

The **Cerebrum Enhancer** is easy to undervalue because Mutagens feel more exciting at first. Do not ignore it. Research Points can improve a species more broadly, which means future Voidlings of that species can benefit too.

Good early upgrade targets usually include:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>primary attack damage,</li>
  <li>primary attack fire rate,</li>
  <li>cooldown or mobility improvements,</li>
  <li>survival upgrades,</li>
  <li>and upgrades that improve the ability you use most often.</li>
</ul>

If you are mainly using Kwipeck, invest there first. If another species immediately feels better, shift your Research Points toward that species instead of forcing the starter forever.

<h2 id="atlas-terminal">
  Atlas Terminal, mission replay, and stars
</h2>

The **Atlas Terminal** is where Voidling Bound opens up. Once it is available, you can choose missions, replay previous routes, check missing objectives, train Voidlings, and farm resources without forcing the next main route.

</figure>

Replay missions when you need missing Mutagens, time objectives, Golden Eggs, Quackies, Research Points, eggs, new species, or more practice with your current build.

If the next mission feels bad, go back, upgrade, and return stronger.

<h2 id="quackies-and-collectibles">
  Quackies, Mutagens, and 100% cleanup
</h2>

Quackies are hidden duck collectibles. They are useful for completion, but they are not power upgrades. Mutagens, Golden Eggs, and Research Points usually matter more if your build still feels weak.

A good cleanup plan separates goals:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>one run for timer stars,</li>
  <li>one run for missing Mutagens,</li>
  <li>one run for Golden Eggs,</li>
  <li>and a slower run for Quackies when the route is awkward.</li>
</ul>

Use the <Link href="/voidling-bound/quackies-locations">Quackies Locations and Checklist</Link> for all Quacky names, colors, landmarks, and cleanup notes. ### What should I do first in Voidling Bound?

Pick a comfortable difficulty, bind with Kwipeck, clear the first mission, use Mutagens at the Evolution Chamber, and spend Research Points at the Cerebrum Enhancer.

### Which difficulty should I pick first?

Pick the difficulty that lets you learn combat without getting punished every mission. Start easier if you mainly want creature collecting and completion. Go higher if you want more action pressure.

### What are Mutagens used for?

Mutagens are used in the Evolution Chamber to unlock new abilities, elements, body changes, and combat options for individual Voidlings.

### What are Research Points used for?

Research Points are used at the Cerebrum Enhancer for species-wide upgrades. These can help future Voidlings of the same species, so they are very valuable.

### How do I know which mission to replay next?

Replay the mission that fixes your current bottleneck. If your evolution path is blocked, replay a mission with the Mutagen element you need. If your build feels weak, farm Research Points or safer upgrade routes. If you want completion, replay missions with missing Golden Eggs, Quackies, Mutagens, or stars.

### Are Golden Eggs important?

Yes. Golden Eggs hatch into special Voidlings outside the normal genetic tree. They can matter for completion and later Splicing options.

### Can I collect a Golden Egg and leave?

Yes. If you only need the Golden Egg, you can grab it and return to the ship instead of finishing the whole mission.

### Are Quackies power upgrades?

No. Quackies are completion collectibles. They are worth collecting for cleanup, but they do not replace Mutagens, Research Points, or Golden Eggs for progression.

### When does breeding matter?

Breeding matters after you unlock the Pheromone Nest and have useful same-species Voidlings to compare. Before that, focus on clearing missions and upgrading your main species.

### When should I care about Splicing?

Care about Splicing once the Splicing Station opens and you have enough discovered genes, Golden Egg variants, species, and mutated perks to make meaningful choices.

### What is the best Voidling?

Early on, the best Voidling is the one you can use safely. Reliable movement, steady damage, and a playstyle you understand matter more than an untested tier list.

### Should I make an Abyss build early?

No. The Abyss and Void Strikes are later content. Build a strong progression Voidling first, then prepare for deeper runs once your upgrades and playstyle are stable.

Yes. For now, this hub and the beginner guide cover the basics. Separate pages make more sense after there is enough reliable testing for matchup examples, status behavior, and breeding inheritance.
