<h2 id="best-early-weapons">
  Best early weapons and where to get them
</h2>

The biggest improvement over your starting state is not a fancy purchase. It is getting specific named weapons from the right early routes.

<GuideTable

/>

The **Old Sword** is the first weapon most players should grab. **Drax's short bow** is the best free early ranged reward. **Rider's Bow** is stronger but requires the right route and Dexterity planning.

<h2 id="old-sword-location">
  Old Sword location
</h2>

The Old Sword is the first weapon you should care about.

From the starting road:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Move down from the exchange area toward the Old Camp road.</li>
  <li>Pass the abandoned mine area.</li>
  <li>Check the left-side mine entrance area for small loot.</li>
  <li>Follow the road left and uphill toward the bridge.</li>
  <li>Cross the bridge.</li>
  <li>The dead tree with the hanging corpse is on the right side of the path.</li>
  <li>The Old Sword is on the ground at the base of that dead tree.</li>
</ol>

Do not skip this. It is the difference between crawling through the first route and actually being able to handle small fights.

<h2 id="free-short-bow">
  Drax's free short bow
</h2>

Drax is near the hunter camp off the road before the Old Camp. His hunting knife is on the ground nearby. Pick it up and return it to him.

He rewards you with a free short bow. That is a big deal because bows usually compete with maps, training, lockpicks, and armor for your early ore.

<h2 id="riders-bow">
  Rider's Bow location
</h2>

The Rider's Bow is hidden near the route toward the New Camp. Watch for the water area before you reach the New Camp proper. Leave the main road, look for the underwater opening, dive in, and loot the hidden cave.

This is not as obvious as the Old Sword route. If you only follow the road, you can miss it.

The Rider's Bow is worth grabbing if you are leaning into Dexterity. If you do not meet the requirement yet, keep it for later instead of selling it too quickly.

<h2 id="digger-dress">
  Best early armor: Digger's Dress
</h2>

The most important early armor decision is **Digger's Dress**.

You have two routes:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Free route:</strong> go to the Free Mine south of the New Camp, talk to <strong>Swiney</strong>, and agree to join the Scrapers.</li>
  <li><strong>Paid route:</strong> buy Digger's Dress from <strong>Fisk</strong> for <strong>313 ore</strong>.</li>
</ul>

The free route is much better value. 313 ore is a huge early cost, and that money could go toward maps, lockpicks, training, or faction route expenses.

The catch is travel. If you cannot safely reach the Free Mine yet, delay the armor instead of bankrupting yourself immediately.

<h2 id="hunting-income">
  Drax hunting skills: free bow into income
</h2>

Drax is not only useful because of the free short bow. He also teaches hunting skills.

Once you can kill animals safely, hunting skills turn animal kills into sellable materials. That means teeth, claws, fur, and skins become ore instead of wasted bodies.

The order matters:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Find Drax and return his hunting knife.</li>
  <li>Take the free short bow.</li>
  <li>Use the bow and safer fights to kill animals more reliably.</li>
  <li>Learn hunting skills when you can actually use them.</li>
  <li>Sell animal materials for ore.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="blacksmithing-income">
  Blacksmithing can be a stable early ore route
</h2>

Blacksmithing is worth considering if you want a repeatable income plan instead of only looting and quest rewards.

Around the Old Camp smithing route, you can learn to craft simple weapons from cheap materials and sell them. The cheapest recipes can start early, and even small profit matters when lockpicks, maps, armor, and training compete for the same ore pool.

Blacksmithing is not mandatory, but it fills an important gap: it gives you something productive to do when you are too weak to safely farm harder enemies.

<h2 id="whistlers-sword">
  Should you keep Whistler's Sword?
</h2>

Whistler's Sword is not just a weapon question. It is an Old Camp reputation decision.

Whistler gives you 100 ore and sends you to Fisk. Fisk asks for 110 ore, and you should not mention Whistler during the purchase. If you want clean Old Camp progress, get the sword and return it to Whistler.

<GuideTable
caption="Should you keep or return Whistler's Sword?"
columns={whistlerColumns}
rows={whistlerRows}
/>

If you are trying to join the Old Camp, returning the sword is the safer recommendation because it gives you experience and helps with Whistler's support. If you are deliberately playing a greedy or non-Old-Camp route, keeping value for yourself is possible, but do not pretend it is the clean path.

<h2 id="map-before-expensive-gear">
  Buy or get a map before expensive gear
</h2>

A map can save more resources than a small armor or weapon upgrade. Without one, it is easy to walk into forests, ruins, caves, or monster paths that cost you healing items, time, and reloads.

Once you reach the Old Camp, look for Graham and prioritize the main colony map. Do not buy every small local map before you know where you are going.

<h2 id="new-camp-weapon-route">
  New Camp has a strong Chapter 1 weapon route
</h2>

New Camp can give strong Chapter 1 value, but it takes setup. Do not expect the payoff the moment you leave the Old Camp.

You need to keep Mordrag alive, use his ring, work through Lares, handle the Herb Merchant route, and progress far enough for the rewards to matter. That is why New Camp feels slower at first, even if it becomes very rewarding.

If you are choosing a faction based on rewards, read the <Link href="/gothic-1-remake/best-faction">Best Faction Guide</Link> before locking yourself into Old Camp, New Camp, or Swamp Camp.

<h2 id="faq">
  Gothic 1 Remake early gear FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best early armor in Gothic 1 Remake?

Digger's Dress is the best early armor target. Get it free from Swiney at the Free Mine south of the New Camp if you can, instead of buying it from Fisk for 313 ore.

### Where is the Old Sword?

After the abandoned mine area, follow the road left and uphill to the bridge. Cross the bridge, then check the dead tree with the hanging corpse on the right side of the path. The Old Sword is on the ground at its base.

### How do I get a free bow early?

Find Drax near the hunter camp off the early road before the Old Camp. Pick up his hunting knife nearby and return it to him for a free short bow.

### Where is Rider's Bow?

Rider's Bow is hidden in an underwater cave near the route toward the New Camp. Watch for the water area, leave the main road, dive through the hidden opening, and loot the cave.

### How much do lockpicks cost?

Fisk sells lockpicks for 13 ore each. Buy a few if you plan to open chests, but do not spend your whole early budget on them.

### Should I buy Digger's Dress from Fisk?

Only if you cannot safely reach the Free Mine yet and urgently need the armor. The better value route is getting it free from Swiney.

### Should I return Whistler's Sword?

Yes if you want clean Old Camp progress. Returning it gives you experience and Whistler's support. Keeping it is a greedier route and can hurt the clean Old Camp path.

### Is blacksmithing worth it early?

Yes, if you want stable income. Simple crafted weapons can be sold for ore, and crafting can be safer than fighting enemies you are not ready for.

### Is mining good for early ore?

Mining helps, especially around the Free Mine route, but it is slower than good quests, crafting, and route rewards. Use it as extra income.
