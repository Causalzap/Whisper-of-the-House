  Gothic 1 Remake lockpicking guide
</h2>

Lockpicking in Gothic 1 Remake is valuable, but it is easy to misunderstand because the game uses a visible slider puzzle.

The first thing to learn is the mechanism, not the trainer list.

<h2 id="how-lockpicking-works">
  How lockpicking works
</h2>

Each lock is made of several sliding plates. Each plate has **7 holes**. Your goal is to move every bronze pin into the **fourth hole**, which is the center position.

When a pin is correct, it rises / pops up instead of dropping. That is your feedback.

The puzzle becomes tricky because plates can be linked. Moving one plate may also move another plate, and the second plate may move in the same direction or the opposite direction.

<GuideTable
caption="How the Gothic 1 Remake lockpicking puzzle works."
columns={mechanicColumns}
rows={mechanicRows}
/>

Before you are trained, a lockpick breaks after **2 failed slider moves**, and the lock fully resets. That is why lockpicking feels brutal if you spam guesses.

<h2 id="how-to-lockpick">
  How to lockpick without wasting picks
</h2>

Use this method:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Stand at the chest and make a manual save.</li>
  <li>Look at the pins and sliders before moving anything.</li>
  <li>Move one plate and watch which other plate moves with it.</li>
  <li>Aim each pin for the fourth hole in the center.</li>
  <li>Use the rising pin as confirmation that a position is correct.</li>
  <li>If you break too many lockpicks, reload instead of burning your ore budget.</li>
</ol>

The goal is not to brute-force every lock. The goal is to understand how the sliders are connected, then move them in an order that leaves the pins centered.

<h2 id="lockpicking-training-levels">
  Lockpicking training levels and costs
</h2>

Fingers can train lockpicking, but the key question is whether the upgrade solves your current problem.

<GuideTable
caption="Gothic 1 Remake lockpicking training levels."
columns={trainingColumns}
rows={trainingRows}
/>

The best early upgrade is usually **Trained**, not Master. Trained is where lockpicking becomes much less punishing because you get more mistake room and a broken lockpick no longer resets the whole lock.

Master is strong, but **20 LP and 200 ore** is a major early investment. Wait until you know you are playing a serious thief / chest route.

<h2 id="where-to-buy-lockpicks">
  Where to buy lockpicks
</h2>

Buy from **Mordrag** first if he is still in Old Camp. He sells lockpicks for **13 ore each**, making him the cheapest early source.

After Mordrag, use Fisk or Dexter as backup sources. They sell lockpicks for about **15 ore each**.

<GuideTable
caption="Where to buy or find lockpicks."

rows={sourceRows}
/>

Do not buy a giant stack immediately. Early ore also needs to cover maps, training, arrows, quest costs, and gear.

<h2 id="is-lockpicking-worth-it">
  Is lockpicking worth learning early?
</h2>

Yes, but not automatically as your first LP purchase.

<GuideTable
caption="When lockpicking is worth learning."
columns={worthColumns}
rows={worthRows}
/>

The best timing is after you have a basic weapon, a stable route, a few lockpicks, and a reason to open several chests. If you are still dying to early animals, spend your first LP on survival or combat instead.

<h2 id="lockpicking-vs-pickpocketing">
  Lockpicking vs pickpocketing
</h2>

Lockpicking and pickpocketing are not the same decision.

**Lockpicking** is safer to start with because it targets chests. You still need to watch ownership and NPC sightlines, but the risk is more controllable.

**Pickpocketing** targets people. That means failure can create more serious consequences, especially around important NPCs, trainers, guards, and faction figures.

For a first run:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>learn lockpicking before pickpocketing,</li>
  <li>practice on safe chests first,</li>
  <li>save before theft,</li>
  <li>do not pickpocket key NPCs unless you accept the risk.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="stealing-owned-chests">
  Owned chests, real-time risk, and Sneak
</h2>

The lockpicking interface runs in real time. If a guard or NPC sees you while you are solving the puzzle, they can interrupt and attack you before you finish.

Owned chests are more dangerous than abandoned supply chests. If someone sees you open a chest that is not yours, they may warn you, attack, knock you down, or create trouble with that area.

<GuideTable
caption="Owned chest and real-time lockpicking risks."
columns={stealthColumns}
rows={stealthRows}
/>

If you want to steal more safely, look into **Sneak** training from **Cavalorn** west of Old Camp. Sneak does not make theft consequence-free, but it can help you approach chests without immediately alerting people.

<h2 id="lockpicking-and-old-camp">
  Lockpicking and Old Camp admission
</h2>

Lockpicking matters more if you plan to join the Old Camp.

Fingers is one of the easier Shadow supporters, and his task is close by. You do not need to travel across the map, fight a boss, or commit to a faction route. You just need to prove you can handle the chest.

If your Old Camp route needs four supporters, Fingers is a good target alongside Whistler, Sly, and Scatty.

<h2 id="faq">
  Gothic 1 Remake lockpicking FAQ
</h2>

### How does lockpicking work in Gothic 1 Remake?

Each lock uses sliding plates with 7 holes. Move the bronze pins into the fourth hole, the center position. A correct pin rises instead of dropping. The challenge is that moving one plate can move other plates too.

### Why do my lockpicks break so fast?

Before training, a lockpick breaks after 2 failed slider moves and the lock fully resets. This is why manual saving before chests is so important early.

### What does Trained lockpicking do?

Trained lockpicking costs 10 LP and 100 ore. It lets you make up to 4 failed slider moves before the lockpick breaks, and a broken pick does not reset your lock progress.

### What does Master lockpicking do?

Master lockpicking costs 20 LP and 200 ore. When a lockpick breaks, one slider connection is removed, making the remaining puzzle simpler.

### Who sells the cheapest lockpicks?

"Mordrag can sell lockpicks while he is still in the Old Camp, before you send him away for Thorus. Check his stock first, then use Fisk or Dexter as backup sources."

### Where is Fingers?

Fingers is in the Old Camp outer ring. From the castle gate, head left toward the arena side and castle wall.

### Is lockpicking worth my first 10 LP?

Only if you have a chest route or are using Fingers for Old Camp support. If you are still struggling in combat, one-handed training, Strength, or Dexterity may matter more.

### Can guards interrupt lockpicking?

Yes. Lockpicking happens in real time, so guards and NPCs can catch you during the puzzle and attack or interrupt you.

### What is Cavalorn's Sneak training for?

Sneak can help you approach owned chests without being noticed as easily. It does not remove theft consequences, but it supports a safer thief route.

### Should I learn pickpocketing with lockpicking?

Not automatically. Lockpicking is usually the safer first thief skill because it targets chests. Pickpocketing targets people and carries higher risk around important NPCs.
