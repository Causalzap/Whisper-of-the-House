<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Gothic 1 Remake**, talk to **Diego**, grab the first sword near the bridge before the Old Camp, use NPC escorts for safe XP, and delay your faction choice until you have visited more than one camp.

Your first 10 Learning Points matter. Do not spend them randomly. Meet early trainers first, then choose between Strength, Dexterity, one-handed combat, lockpicking, hunting, or saving the points for a clearer build.

<h2 id="beginner-guide">
  Gothic 1 Remake beginner guide
</h2>

Gothic 1 Remake starts you as a nobody. You have no reputation, weak gear, no map, no faction, and almost no money. That is the point.

The first hours are not about clearing the road. They are about learning how the Colony works before it punishes you. A good beginner start gives you five things:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>a real weapon,</li>
  <li>safe XP,</li>
  <li>food and ore,</li>
  <li>a map or at least a clear route,</li>
  <li>and enough information to delay your faction choice.</li>
</ul>

Use this guide if you are asking what to do first, why you keep dying, whether to follow Diego, how to spend your first Learning Points, or when to choose a camp.

For a more exact item-by-item opening route, use the <Link href="/gothic-1-remake/perfect-start">Perfect Start guide</Link>. For early weapons, ore, armor, and bow rewards, use the <Link href="/gothic-1-remake/early-ore-weapons-armor">Early Gear Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="follow-diego-and-escorts">
  Follow Diego, then use other escorts
</h2>

This expands Step 1 and Step 7 from the opening route.

Yes, you should follow Diego on your first run. He is your safest introduction to the Old Camp route, and his dialogue explains the three camps, the barrier, the Fire Mages, and why you need a weapon before you act brave.

<strong>Important: when an escort kills enemies during travel, you can still benefit from the XP.</strong> That makes guided routes much stronger than they look. Diego, Mordrag, and Baal Parvez are not just directions on legs. They are safe travel, free enemy kills, loot opportunities, and world knowledge.

Use these escort routes carefully:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Diego</strong> helps you understand the first Old Camp route.</li>
  <li><strong>Mordrag</strong> can guide you toward the New Camp if you do not kill him for Thorus.</li>
  <li><strong>Baal Parvez</strong> can guide you toward the Swamp Camp instead of making you wander there alone.</li>
</ul>

If you already reached the Old Camp and are choosing where to commit, read the <Link href="/gothic-1-remake/best-faction">Best Faction Guide</Link> before joining anyone.

<h2 id="get-first-weapon">
  Where to find your first weapon
</h2>

This expands Step 3 from the opening route.

You should get a weapon before testing real fights. The early sword route is one of the most important beginner details because the game does not place a giant marker over it.

From the starting road:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Move down from the exchange area toward the road leading to the Old Camp.</li>
  <li>Pass the abandoned mine area.</li>
  <li>Check the left-side mine entrance area for small loot, such as a pickaxe or supplies.</li>
  <li>Follow the road left and uphill toward the bridge.</li>
  <li>Cross the bridge.</li>
  <li>The dead tree with the hanging corpse is on the right side of the path.</li>
  <li>The sword is on the ground at the base of that dead tree.</li>
</ol>

Do not treat this as your final weapon. Treat it as the tool that lets you survive long enough to reach real choices.

<h2 id="first-learning-points">
  How to spend your first Learning Points
</h2>

Each level gives you **10 Learning Points**. The trap is thinking you should spend them immediately.

Gothic 1 Remake uses trainers. You do not open a simple menu and pick upgrades in isolation. You need to find the right person, and some training also requires ore or access.

Use this table before spending your first 10 LP:

<GuideTable
caption="How to think about your first Learning Points."

/>

For most new players, the first real decision is this:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Pure Strength or Dexterity</strong> helps you meet weapon requirements and improve basic damage direction.</li>
  <li><strong>Weapon training</strong> can change how combat feels by improving practical control and attack options.</li>
</ul>

If you are struggling to survive melee fights, do not only ask “how much Strength do I need?” Ask whether you should train one-handed combat when Scatty becomes available. If you want to use bows after getting Drax's free short bow, Dexterity becomes more attractive.

Once you reach the Old Camp, look for Graham. A colony map should be one of your early priorities because it helps you understand:

<h2 id="drax-hunting-knife">
  Where to find Drax and why his hunting knife is worth doing
</h2>

This expands Step 5 from the opening route.

Drax is near the early hunter camp off the road before the Old Camp. After you move down from the starting area and follow the main route toward the first camp, watch for the hunter area near the road instead of rushing straight past it.

His hunting knife is on the ground nearby. If you find the knife and return it to Drax, you can get a free short bow. That matters because early ore is tight, and a bow gives you a new way to approach fights without buying one immediately.

Drax also matters because he can teach hunting skills. Once you can kill animals safely, those skills can turn teeth, claws, fur, and skins into sellable income instead of wasted animal kills.

<h2 id="faq">
  Gothic 1 Remake beginner FAQ
</h2>

### Can I steal from NPCs early?

Yes, but it is risky. If someone sees you steal, open a chest that is not yours, or enter a restricted place, they may warn you, attack you, knock you down, or take back stolen items. In the Old Camp, guards and stronger NPCs can punish you long before you are strong enough to fight back. Save before testing theft.

### Can I pickpocket early?

Pickpocketing is tied to training and opportunity. Do not build around it before you understand trainers, ore cost, and how much risk you are willing to take. If you fail around important NPCs, you can damage the route more than the stolen item is worth.

### What happens if I attack a guard or NPC?

You can get knocked down, robbed, killed, or locked out of useful interactions. Some NPCs are important enough that attacking them can damage your route. Save first if you are testing anything aggressive.

### Does sleeping change NPC locations?

Yes. Sleeping advances time, and many NPCs follow routines. If a trader, teacher, or quest NPC is missing, try checking during the day or near their usual work area.

### Why is an NPC not where the quest implied?

They may have moved because of time, weather, work, sleep, or routine behavior. Look nearby first, then try another time of day before assuming the quest is stuck.

### Do I still get XP if Diego or another escort kills enemies?

Yes, escort routes can still reward you while the guide handles enemies. That is why Diego, Mordrag, and Baal Parvez are so valuable early.

### Should I spend my first 10 LP right away?

Usually no. Meet trainers first. Your first 10 LP can go into attributes, one-handed training, lockpicking, hunting, or be saved until your build is clearer.

### Should I fight wolves early?

No. Even a single wolf can be too much for a fresh character. Come back after better gear, more health, and training.

### When should I choose a faction?

After you have visited more than one camp, reached Level 5, completed useful prerequisite tasks, and made a manual save before the final join step.

### Is Old Camp the best beginner faction?

Old Camp is the easiest first-playthrough route to understand, but it is not automatically best for every build. New Camp and Swamp Camp are worth visiting before you commit.
