<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Gothic 1 Remake**, each level gives **10 Learning Points**, and there is **no respec**. Every LP you spend is permanent for that run, so do not spread points across too many systems early.

For a safe first run, use the trainer that solves your current problem: **one-handed training** if melee feels clumsy, **Diego** if you need Strength or Dexterity for a weapon, **Fingers** if you are opening chests, **Huno** if you want blacksmithing income, **Drax** if you can already hunt animals, and **Cavalorn / Wolf** if you are building around bows. 
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="skills-trainers-guide">
  Gothic 1 Remake skills and trainers guide
</h2>

Gothic 1 Remake does not let you become stronger from a simple menu. You gain **Learning Points** by leveling up, but you spend them through trainers. Many trainers also require ore, reputation, quest progress, or faction access.

The most important beginner rule is simple:

<strong>There is no way to respec in Gothic 1 Remake. Every LP you spend is permanent for that run.</strong>

That means your first question should not be “what stat is best?” It should be:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Which trainer have I reached?</li>
  <li>How much LP and ore does this training cost?</li>
  <li>Which weapon, chest route, income route, or faction path does it support?</li>
  <li>Am I fixing a real problem, or just spending because I leveled up?</li>
</ul>

If you are still in the first hour, read the <Link href="/gothic-1-remake/beginner-guide">Beginner Guide</Link> first. If you need specific weapons and armor before spending LP, use the <Link href="/gothic-1-remake/early-ore-weapons-armor">Early Gear Guide</Link>.

Do not spend your first 10 LP in a vacuum. Spend it to support a build direction.

<strong>Meet the early trainers first, then spend.</strong>

A melee player should compare one-handed training against raw Strength. A bow player should check Dexterity and bow access. A thief player should understand Fingers and lockpicking costs. A crafting player should find Huno before assuming hunting is the only ore route.

<h2 id="diego-strength-dexterity">
  Diego: Strength and Dexterity
</h2>

Diego is the cleanest early stat trainer. He can help you raise Strength or Dexterity, which matters for weapon requirements and build direction.

Use Diego when you have a specific goal:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Need a melee weapon requirement? Train Strength.</li>
  <li>Need a bow requirement? Train Dexterity.</li>
  <li>Not sure what you are building? Save your LP.</li>
</ul>

Do not buy Strength only because melee feels bad. If your issue is slow attacks, bad recovery, or awkward timing, the better answer may be one-handed training.

<h2 id="one-handed-arena-trainer">
  One-handed training: Scatty, Dog, and the Old Camp arena
</h2>

The Old Camp arena area is where melee players should look for early one-handed training. Be careful with names here: **Scatty is the arena figure**, but depending on route state and NPC layout, the actual one-handed training option may be tied to an arena-side trainer, often found around the hut east of the arena.

If Scatty does not offer the training you expect, do not assume the guide is wrong or the route is broken. Check the nearby arena-side hut and talk to the weapon trainer there.

One-handed training matters because it changes combat directly.

<h2 id="huno-blacksmithing">
  Huno: blacksmithing and safer ore income
</h2>

Huno is the Old Camp smith and the trainer to remember if you want crafting income instead of relying only on hunting or risky fights.

Blacksmithing can cost around **10 LP + 50 ore**, but it opens a repeatable economy route. That matters because early ore is always under pressure: maps, lockpicks, arrows, training, armor, and quest costs all compete for the same wallet.

Blacksmithing is best when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you have a stable Old Camp route,</li>
  <li>you can reach the forge safely,</li>
  <li>you want income without fighting stronger enemies,</li>
  <li>and you are willing to spend LP on economy instead of immediate combat.</li>
</ul>

Do not take blacksmithing if you still cannot survive basic travel. Take it when your route is stable enough to turn crafting into real ore.

<h2 id="bow-trainers-cavalorn-wolf">
  Bow trainers: Cavalorn early, Wolf later
</h2>

If you want a bow route, do not wait until New Camp to think about archery.

**Cavalorn** is the important early name near the Old Camp side of the world. He can support early bow training before you fully commit to New Camp. This matters if your route uses Dexterity, arrows, and ranged safety from the beginning.

**Wolf** becomes more important once you reach New Camp. He is useful if you already have a bow plan and want to improve it rather than simply carrying a bow as a backup.

Before buying bow training, check four things:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Do you have a bow worth using?</li>
  <li>Do you meet its Dexterity requirement?</li>
  <li>Do you have enough arrows?</li>
  <li>Can you create space before enemies reach you?</li>
</ul>

Cavalorn-style early bow training can cost around **10 LP + 50 ore**, so do not buy it unless ranged combat is actually part of your plan.

<h2 id="theron-climbing">
  Theron: climbing and Swamp Camp utility
</h2>

Theron is easy to miss because climbing does not look like a damage upgrade. But movement skills can matter a lot in Gothic 1 Remake, especially if you are exploring, searching for routes, or trying to avoid bad fights.

Theron's climbing training can require **5 LP**, but the route may also require you to give **6 Northern Dark herbs** to Fortuno first.

This makes climbing a planned utility investment:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

<h2 id="faq">
  Gothic 1 Remake skills and trainers FAQ
</h2>

### Is there respec in Gothic 1 Remake?

No. There is no respec, so every LP you spend is permanent for that run. Save before major training choices and avoid spreading LP across too many systems.

### How many Learning Points do I get per level?

Each level gives 10 Learning Points. You spend them through trainers, often with an additional ore cost or route requirement.

### What should I spend my first 10 LP on?

Spend your first 10 LP on the trainer that solves your current problem. For melee, consider one-handed training. For weapon requirements, use Diego. For chests, use Fingers. For crafting income, use Huno.

### Who teaches one-handed weapons?

Check the Old Camp arena area. Scatty is the arena figure, but if he does not offer one-handed training, check the nearby arena-side trainer or east-side hut.

### Is Strength or one-handed training better first?

Strength is better if you need a weapon requirement. One-handed training is better if your problem is clumsy melee control.

### Who teaches blacksmithing?

Huno in the Old Camp smith area is the key early blacksmithing trainer. Blacksmithing can cost around 10 LP + 50 ore.

### Who teaches bow skills early?

Cavalorn is the early bow trainer near the Old Camp side of the world. Wolf becomes important later in New Camp.

### Is lockpicking worth learning early?

Yes if you have a chest route or want Fingers' Old Camp support. Trained lockpicking costs 10 LP + 100 ore, while Master costs 20 LP + 200 ore.

### When should I learn hunting skills?

Learn hunting only after you can kill the animals that drop the materials. Buying teeth, claws, fur, or skin skills too early wastes LP.

### Who teaches climbing?

Theron in Swamp Camp can teach climbing. Expect a 5 LP cost and a possible requirement involving 6 Northern Dark herbs for Fortuno.

### Who teaches mana or magic?

Torrez helps with Old Camp / Fire Mage direction, Cronos supports New Camp / Water Mage direction, and Baal Cadar supports Swamp Camp / Brotherhood magic.
