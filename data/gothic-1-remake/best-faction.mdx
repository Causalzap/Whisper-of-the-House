<h2 id="short-answer">
  Which camp should you join?
</h2>

Pick **Old Camp** for the easiest first playthrough. It has the clearest early route, the most obvious structure, and a strong starting armor path through Shadow progression.

Pick **New Camp** for freedom. The early route takes more work, and the first economy can feel rougher, but Mordrag, Lares, and the New Camp quest chain give the faction a strong payoff.

Pick **Swamp Camp** for Brotherhood identity. It is the strangest route and not the strongest pure mage route, but it has Novice / Templar flavor and useful visitor benefits before you join.

The best practical move is to delay the final choice until you have reached Level 5, visited all three camps, and made a manual save.

<h2 id="old-camp">
  Old Camp: safest first playthrough and Fire Mage direction
</h2>

Old Camp is the most natural first faction because the game pushes you toward Diego, Thorus, the castle gate, and Gomez early. It is the best camp for players who want structure, status, and a clear early route.

Old Camp is best for:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>first playthroughs,</li>
  <li>Shadow progression,</li>
  <li>Fire Mage interest,</li>
  <li>clear early armor direction,</li>
  <li>players who want the most guided Chapter 1 route.</li>
</ul>

The basic joining route is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Talk to Thorus at the castle gate.</li>
  <li>Ask Diego how to earn trust.</li>
  <li>Get enough Shadow support.</li>
  <li>Complete Diego's Old Mine list test.</li>
  <li>Reach Level 5.</li>
  <li>Talk to Thorus and Gomez when Diego says you are ready.</li>
</ol>

<strong>Story warning:</strong> Old Camp players are scripted to be exiled in Chapter 4. This is not a punishment for choosing wrong, and it does not mean your Fire Mage route was a mistake. It is part of the story and your route continues after exile.

For the full route, read the <Link href="/gothic-1-remake/old-camp-walkthrough">Old Camp Walkthrough</Link>. For Diego's list specifically, use the <Link href="/gothic-1-remake/old-mine-diegos-list">Old Mine / Diego's List guide</Link>.

<h2 id="new-camp">
  New Camp: best for freedom and mercenary-style rewards
</h2>

New Camp is the better choice for players who dislike Old Camp authority and want a freer route. It fits a mercenary-style run, practical rewards, and a stronger feeling of working outside Gomez's system.

New Camp is best for:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>players who want more freedom,</li>
  <li>Mercenary direction later,</li>
  <li>weapon-focused builds,</li>
  <li>players who want a stronger New Camp payoff in Chapter 1,</li>
  <li>players who want Water Mage direction later.</li>
</ul>

The early warning is important: **New Camp can feel poorer and slower at first than Old Camp**. You need time for Mordrag, Lares, Diego's list, Herb Merchant money, Rice Lord tasks, and the later raid path before the route starts paying off.

The biggest beginner mistake is killing Mordrag too early. Thorus wants Mordrag gone, but that does not mean you should turn him into a corpse. Convince Mordrag to leave instead. He can guide you to New Camp and give you his ring, which helps with Lares.

A simple New Camp sequence looks like this:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Talk to Thorus about Mordrag.</li>
  <li>Tell Mordrag to leave instead of killing him.</li>
  <li>Let Mordrag guide you to New Camp.</li>
  <li>Keep Mordrag's ring for Lares.</li>
  <li>Use Diego's list carefully before handing it in.</li>
  <li>Work through Lares and the New Camp errands.</li>
  <li>Reach Level 5 before the final joining step.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="swamp-camp">
  Swamp Camp: best for Brotherhood and Templar flavor
</h2>

Swamp Camp is the strangest faction, and that is its appeal. The route is built around the Sleeper, Baal approvals, Cor Kalom, Novices, and later Templar identity.

Swamp Camp is best for:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Brotherhood roleplay,</li>
  <li>Novice armor and Swamp Camp identity,</li>
  <li>Templar direction later,</li>
  <li>players who want a less conventional Chapter 1 route,</li>
  <li>players who enjoy magic flavor but do not need the strongest pure mage route.</li>
</ul>

The safest way to see the camp early is to use Baal Parvez from the Old Camp area. Blindly walking there alone is a bad first-run idea.

Swamp Camp also has value even when you do not join it. **Theron can teach climbing for a fee, and daily free swampweed is available to visitors.** That makes the camp worth visiting even for Old Camp or New Camp players.

The Swamp Camp join route eventually asks for enough Baal support and Level 5 before Cor Kalom accepts you.

<h2 id="when-you-get-locked-in">
  When do you get locked into a faction?
</h2>

You are not locked in just because you talk to a camp member or complete an early task. You can visit camps, do useful errands, and gather information during Chapter 1.

The lock-in happens when you formally join a camp.

The practical advice is simple: make a manual save before the final joining conversation. That save is the easiest fix if you choose a camp and regret it five minutes later.

<h2 id="chapter-1-decision">
  At the end of Chapter 1, how should you decide?
</h2>

When you are finally ready to join, stop asking which camp sounds coolest and check your actual run.

Choose **Old Camp** when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you want the clearest first run,</li>
  <li>you care about early Shadow armor,</li>
  <li>you are aiming at Fire Mage direction,</li>
  <li>you do not mind the scripted Chapter 4 exile later.</li>
</ul>

Choose **New Camp** when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you kept Mordrag alive,</li>
  <li>you used Diego's list carefully with Lares,</li>
  <li>you can tolerate a slower early economy,</li>
  <li>you want Mercenary / Water Mage direction later.</li>
</ul>

Choose **Swamp Camp** when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you like the Brotherhood identity,</li>
  <li>you want Novice and Templar flavor,</li>
  <li>you enjoy Baal approval routes,</li>
  <li>you care more about faction style than the easiest early armor route.</li>
</ul>

Before you click through the final join step, make a manual save. That save is your safety net.

<h2 id="faq">
  Gothic 1 Remake faction FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best faction in Gothic 1 Remake?

Old Camp is the safest first-playthrough choice, New Camp is best for a freer mercenary-style route, and Swamp Camp is best for Brotherhood and Templar flavor.

### Which faction has the best early armor?

Old Camp has the clearest early armor path because joining as a Shadow gives you access to Shadow Armor. New Camp and Swamp Camp have strong identity routes, but their early armor progression is less straightforward for a first run.

### Does Old Camp exile ruin my Fire Mage route?

No. The Chapter 4 exile is scripted story progress, not a punishment for choosing Old Camp. Your route continues after exile.

### Is New Camp harder for beginners?

It can be. New Camp has a slower early economy and more setup before the rewards start paying off. Keep Mordrag alive, use his ring, and work carefully through Lares.

### Is Swamp Camp useful even if I do not join?

Yes. Theron can teach climbing for a fee, and visitors can take daily free swampweed. That makes Swamp Camp worth visiting even on Old Camp or New Camp routes.

### Can you join all factions?

No. You can work with all three camps early, but you formally join only one.

### When should I choose a faction?

Choose after you have visited the camps, completed useful prerequisite quests, reached Level 5, and made a manual save before the final decision.

### Should I kill Mordrag?

No, not on a flexible route. Mordrag can guide you to New Camp and give you his ring, which is more valuable than removing him early.

### Which camp is best for magic?

Old Camp is best for early Fire Mage direction. New Camp is better for Water Mage direction later. Swamp Camp has Brotherhood magic flavor, but it is not the strongest pure mage route.
