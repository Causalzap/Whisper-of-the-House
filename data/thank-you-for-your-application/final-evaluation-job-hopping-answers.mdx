<h2 id="job-for-life-vs-refuse">
  Job for Life vs Refuse Permanent Employment
</h2>

These two outcomes share the same clean Day 30 setup. The split is the final offer choice:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Accept permanent employment</strong> — Job for Life.</li>
  <li><strong>Refuse permanent employment</strong> — refusal route.</li>
</ul>

If you never see the clean choice, the problem is probably earlier: rating failed, or a recorded violation overrode the clean branch. For the full route context, use the <Link href="/thank-you-for-your-application/endings-guide#day-30-final-evaluation">Endings Guide Day 30 section</Link>.

<h2 id="illegal-layoff-diagnosis">
  Illegal Layoff diagnosis: good rating but still fired
</h2>

If you had a good rating but still got **Illegal Layoff**, check recorded violations. The two important causes are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>H11 scanner violation,</li>
  <li>shock misuse.</li>
</ul>

A clean-looking rating does not erase those records. If either one is on your file, the final evaluation can punish the route.

<h2 id="failed-performance-review-diagnosis">
  Failed Performance Review diagnosis
</h2>

**Failed Performance Review** means the issue is work performance, not the final offer choice. If you expected a clean permanent-employment branch but got this result, your rating likely landed in the C/D range.

Use earlier workdays to repair accuracy. Most rating problems come from wrong approvals, wrong rejections, missed document rules, or incorrect Thanks Letter reasons.

<h2 id="faq">
  Thank You For Your Application final evaluation FAQ
</h2>

### I had an A or B rating but still got Illegal Layoff. Why?

A recorded H11 scanner violation or shock misuse can override the clean Day 30 branch. A good rating does not erase those records.

### I expected Job for Life but got Failed Performance Review. What went wrong?

Your final rating likely landed in the C/D range. Repair earlier work accuracy, especially wrong approvals, wrong rejections, missed document rules, and incorrect Thanks Letter reasons.

### I reached the job-hopping interview but failed. Why?

One of the answers probably did not match the successful chain. The job-hopping route wants loyal motivation, excitement to work, belief in Minato Communications, and cross-department teamwork.

### Should I choose the no-purpose work answer for job-hopping?

No. The no-purpose answer belongs to **What’s the point of working?**. Job-hopping success uses the loyal-worker answer chain.

### Why did I get Job for Life instead of The Meaning of Work?

On the A02 Day 29 branch, choosing **(Take A01's hand)** points toward Job for Life. For The Meaning of Work, choose **(Do not reach out)**.

### Why did Escape not appear on Day 29?

You probably missed or answered K88's Day 27 work-shift Confidential Mail differently. The Escape setup starts during the work shift, not from apartment mail after work.

### Where are the exact Day 30 rating thresholds?

Use the rating table in the Endings Guide. This page focuses on diagnosing the final result after the game has already chosen a route.
