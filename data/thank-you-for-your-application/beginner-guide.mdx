For a first run in **Thank You For Your Application**, use **Subsidy Mode** if you want less pressure: it gives **30P per day** and removes the interview time barrier, which lets you learn the desk rules without rushing.

Your safe beginner loop is: read the **Job Requirements tab**, check the resume and required documents, compare identity fields, look for forged documents, select the exact **Thanks Letter** reason before rejecting, then check bills, A02, and breakdown index after work.

Do not treat this like a free manual-save game. It uses automatic checkpoints, so test risky achievements and overtime only after checking what your Load / Continue point actually returns to.

<h2 id="thank-you-for-your-application-beginner-guide">
  Thank You For Your Application beginner guide
</h2>

This **Thank You For Your Application beginner guide** is for players who are still learning the desk, the apartment, and the early money pressure. The game is not only about dragging resumes into a green or red folder. Your decisions affect pay, bills, probation, mental state, achievements, and late route options.

The biggest beginner mistake is rushing interviews before understanding the rule panel. The second biggest mistake is spending money before checking bills.

<h2 id="subsidy-mode">
  Subsidy Mode: the best option if you want less pressure
</h2>

**Subsidy Mode** is the easiest recommendation for new players who want to learn the system first. It gives **30P every day** and removes the interview time barrier, so you can focus on checking documents and understanding the story instead of racing the clock.

Choose Subsidy Mode if:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you mainly want to see the story,</li>
  <li>you are missing details because the timer pressures you,</li>
  <li>you keep losing money from rushed document checks,</li>
  <li>you want to learn Thanks Letter reasons before doing a normal route.</li>
</ul>

Normal mode is still better if you want the full pressure of pay-per-application work. But for a first playthrough, Subsidy Mode is the cleanest way to learn without turning every mistake into a money spiral.

<h2 id="forged-documents">
  Forged documents and mismatched fields
</h2>

This is where the game becomes closest to Papers, Please. Do not ask only, “Does the candidate have the document?” Ask, “Do the documents agree with each other?”

The checking mindset is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>social number mismatch between resume and certificate,</li>
  <li>name mismatch across documents,</li>
  <li>school mismatch between resume and graduation certificate,</li>
  <li>invalid or altered Psychological Evaluation Report date,</li>
  <li>breakdown index above the allowed requirement,</li>
  <li>missing internship or work experience even when education looks eligible.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="breakdown-index">
  Breakdown index: check it before ending the day
</h2>

Breakdown index is your warning that a normal workday can still turn into route damage. The most concrete early example appears in document checks: one Psychological Evaluation Report rule says candidates’ breakdown index must not exceed 40%.

For C89’s own breakdown risk, do not guess from vibes. Check the apartment status before ending the day, especially after overtime, stressful choices, or route scenes.

<h2 id="checkpoint-risk">
  Checkpoints and risky choices
</h2>

**Thank You For Your Application** uses automatic checkpoint saves rather than a free manual multi-save system. Your checkpoint is usually the day-start or route-state autosave the game gives you.

This matters most when you are testing achievements or late routes.

<h2 id="faq">
  Thank You For Your Application beginner FAQ
</h2>

### Should I start with Subsidy Mode?

Yes, if you want less pressure. Subsidy Mode gives 30P per day and removes the interview time barrier, making it easier to learn document rules and follow the story.

### What should I check first at work?

Open the Job Requirements tab first. Then check the resume, required documents, school eligibility, identity fields, and any Thanks Letter reason before approving or rejecting.

### What do the green and red folders do?

The green folder is the Interview Pass Folder for approved candidates. The red folder is the Interview Reject Folder for failed candidates.

### What is the Thanks Letter for?

The Thanks Letter explains the rejection reason. After it becomes required, you need to select the exact failed requirement or invalid field before rejecting a candidate.

### What counts as a forged or invalid document?

Common signs include mismatched social numbers, mismatched names, wrong school details, invalid report dates, and Psychological Evaluation Reports that fail the required value.

### How long is probation?

C89 starts with a 7-day probation period.

### How much do successful interviews pay early on?

The opening contract shows 10P per successful interview. Your daily report can also show subsidy and bonuses.

### What should I do after work?

Check mail, bills, A02, personal status, subsidy status, and breakdown index before sleeping or spending money.

### Does Thank You For Your Application have manual saves?

Treat the game as an automatic-checkpoint game rather than a free manual multi-save game. Check your Load / Continue point before testing risky achievements or late route choices.

### Should I work overtime on my first run?

Only if you need the money and your condition is safe. Do not chase five consecutive overtime days on a clean first route.
