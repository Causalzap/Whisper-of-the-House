<h2 id="how-to-handle-wrong-turns-without-wasting-time">
  How to handle wrong turns without wasting time
</h2>

The biggest mistake here is not taking one wrong turn.

The biggest mistake is **panicking after a wrong turn**.

This section is much easier if you follow a simple rule:

### Wrong-turn rule

If a line clearly dead-ends, back out immediately and keep testing the coast route.

Do not:

- stop to over-loot
- turn the whole bay into a wide exploration sweep
- assume the destination must be somewhere else
- retreat all the way back mentally into “maybe I missed another clue in town”

You probably did not.

The route is just designed to force a bit of trial and error before the ship reveal.

### What success looks like

Eventually, the route stops feeling like shoreline confusion and starts feeling like approach.

That is when the chapter pays off with the first strong visual confirmation of the ship.
