**After talking to Terry in The Long Dark Episode 5, go to Desperation Bay and investigate the cargo ship.**

The important thing to understand is that **Desperation Bay** is the next **area**, but the **cargo ship** is the real **destination**.

If you are stuck after Terry, do not keep searching **Perseverance Mills** for another town clue. Terry already gives you the handoff you need:

- he confirms the strange signal came from a **ship**
- he places that signal in **Desperation Bay**
- that makes the coast your next real story route
- the ship is where the next major answers start

If you only want the shortest answer:

**leave town, head toward Desperation Bay, and keep moving until you reach the frozen cargo ship.**

<h2 id="the-cargo-ship-is-the-real-target">
  The cargo ship is the real target
</h2>

A lot of players stop at the right area but still feel uncertain because **Desperation Bay** sounds broad.

That is because the bay itself is not the final answer.

The real target after Terry is the **cargo ship**.

That is what the distress signal is tied to, and that is where the story wants you to go next.

So the route logic is:

- **Terry** gives the signal clue
- the clue points to **Desperation Bay**
- inside that area, the real objective is the **frozen cargo ship**

If you remember only one thing from this page, remember this:

**Desperation Bay is the zone. The cargo ship is the destination.**

After Terry, do not ask “What town clue did I miss?”

Ask: **“How do I get to the cargo ship in Desperation Bay?”**

<h2 id="what-terrys-clue-actually-means">
  What Terry’s clue actually means
</h2>

Terry is important because he does more than repeat the quarantine story.

He gives Astrid a usable lead.

What he tells you, in practical terms, is:

- the strange signal was real
- it came from a **ship**
- the source was **Desperation Bay**
- the next important evidence is no longer in town

That is why this is not just flavor dialogue.

Terry is the NPC who converts the investigation from a town mystery into a destination problem.

Before Terry, the question is:

**“What is happening in Perseverance Mills?”**

After Terry, the question becomes:

**“How do I reach the ship tied to that signal?”**

**radio hut → Desperation Bay → cargo ship**

<h2 id="why-players-still-get-stuck-after-terry">
  Why players still get stuck after Terry
</h2>

This part is where a lot of players lose momentum, even though the actual next objective is straightforward.

### 1. They stay mentally locked in Perseverance Mills

They have spent so much time in town that they expect one more town-side answer.

That is usually the wrong instinct.

### 2. They think Desperation Bay is the whole answer

It is only the area name.

The ship is the real goal.

### 3. They treat Terry like optional lore

He is not. Terry is the cleanest route handoff in this part of the chapter.

### 4. They drift into broad exploration instead of goal-based movement

The more useful mindset is not:

> “I’ll wander and see what I find.”

It is:

> “I am following the signal lead to the cargo ship.”

<GuideCallout title="Fast answer">
If you are stuck after Terry, the mistake is usually not that you missed another clue.

The mistake is that you have not fully committed to **Desperation Bay → cargo ship** as the next route.
</GuideCallout>
