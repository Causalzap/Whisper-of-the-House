<h2 id="what-search-party-requires-in-episode-5">
  What Search Party requires in Episode 5
</h2>

**Search Party** is one of the easiest Episode 5 achievements to fail for a very simple reason:

it looks like a normal story objective, but it is really a **timed route problem**.

To get the achievement, you need to find all three missing people fast enough during the **Search the Wilds** stretch of the Missing People investigation.

That means this is not the run where you should play slowly, loot every structure, or treat the area like a normal survival sandbox.

You are solving one thing only:

**find all three bodies before the real-time clock beats you.**

<GuideCallout title="What makes Search Party hard">
The difficult part is not the three locations themselves.

The difficult part is that each one wastes time in a different way:
- one is hidden in a **basement**
- one is hidden **behind a waterfall**
- one looks blocked off by a **broken bridge**
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="when-the-search-party-timer-starts">
  When the Search Party timer starts
</h2>

The safest way to think about this achievement is:

**assume the timer begins as soon as the game pushes you into the Search the Wilds part of the Missing People investigation.**

In practice, that means you should treat the clock as live once the story has moved you through the early town investigation and clearly sends you out into the wilds to search for the missing townspeople.

### The important rule

Do **not** play loosely with the timer.

Even if different players describe the exact trigger point slightly differently, the safe approach is the same:

- leave town ready
- know your route before you start
- do not stop for side looting
- do not sleep during the attempt

### Why sleeping is a bad idea here

This is a **real-time** achievement, not a relaxed story checkpoint objective.

So even if you are tired, cold, or tempted to reset with sleep, this is not the run for that.

For safety, do the whole attempt in one continuous push.

<GuideCallout title="Safe rule for the timer">
If you are chasing Search Party, do not try to outsmart the timer.

Treat it as already running the moment the game sends you into the wilds, and do the whole route in one clean run.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="best-search-party-route">
  Best Search Party route
</h2>

For a normal story run, the cleanest order is often just “whatever you find first.”

For **Search Party**, that is not good enough.

The best route is the one that:

- avoids extra backtracking
- keeps the most confusing detours under control
- leaves the awkward **Constable** crossing for last

### Recommended order for Search Party

1. **Waterfall**
2. **Basement**
3. **Constable**

Why this order works:

- the **waterfall** is fast if you already know the correct side path
- the **basement** is efficient once you commit to the lake area and do not wander
- the **Constable** is the most annoying route, so finishing there prevents one more big reposition

If you already have a personal route that works, keep it.  
But if you want one recommendation for a first serious achievement run, this is the order I would use.

<h2 id="stop-1-waterfall-route">
  Stop 1 — the waterfall route
</h2>

The second missing person from the normal guide makes a very good **first stop** for the achievement run.

The reason is simple:

once you know the trick, this location is actually faster than it looks.

### Where this target is

The body is at the **far north end of the western river**, **behind the waterfall**.

### What wastes time here

Most failed attempts do not lose time because the waterfall is far away.

They lose time because players approach it from the wrong angle.

If you walk straight at the waterfall from the front and expect the body to be visible, you will waste precious seconds circling.

### The correct approach

Use the **left-side path** and come into the waterfall area from the side.

That is the whole trick.

Once you know this, the waterfall section becomes a fast checkpoint instead of a time trap.

<h2 id="stop-2-basement-route">
  Stop 2 — the basement route
</h2>

The basement body is the one that most clearly punishes “normal search behavior.”

Why?

Because it is not out in the open at all.

### Where this target is

The body is in the **lake area**, inside the **basement under the trap door cabin**.

### What wastes time here

Players lose time in this section for three reasons:

- they search the lake area for an open-air body
- they do not recognize the **trap door cabin** fast enough
- they hesitate around the lake because of wildlife pressure

### The only thing that matters here

Do not treat this as a broad search.

Treat it as a direct move toward one landmark:

**the cabin with the trap door.**

If you know the area already, this stop is not slow.  
It only becomes slow when you search it like a wide open zone.

<h2 id="stop-3-constable-route">
  Stop 3 — the Constable route
</h2>

The **Constable** is the best final stop because he is the route that causes the most hesitation.

This is the part of the run where players either finish cleanly or bleed away the last few minutes.

### Where the Constable is

He is on the **southeast side** of the search area, past the **broken bridge**, near a **small fishing pier**.

### Why this location kills runs

The problem is not the body itself.

The problem is that the **broken bridge** makes the whole side of the map look inaccessible.

That is what causes panic, rerouting, and wasted movement.

### The correct mindset

The bridge is **not** a dead end.

It is a route puzzle.

Use the terrain around the crossing properly, commit to the southeast route, and keep moving. Once you get through, the final body is straightforward.

<h2 id="what-wastes-the-most-time-in-search-party">
  What wastes the most time in Search Party
</h2>

If you fail this achievement, it usually comes down to one of these mistakes.

### 1. Playing it like a normal loot run

This is the biggest mistake.

Search Party is not the time to:

- open every structure
- grab extra supplies
- wander for comfort loot
- search “just in case”

### 2. Fighting every wolf encounter

You do not need to turn this into a combat route.

If wildlife slows you down, the real problem is usually hesitation.

Stay focused on movement first.

### 3. Misreading the waterfall

If you approach the waterfall from the front, you are losing time for no reason.

### 4. Forgetting the basement is underground

This is the classic “I know the lake area is right, but I still cannot find the body” failure.

### 5. Letting the broken bridge break your confidence

The southeast side looks like a trap for a reason.

If you hesitate too long there, your run starts bleeding out.

<h2 id="how-to-prepare-before-you-start-search-party">
  How to prepare before you start Search Party
</h2>

You do not need a full expedition loadout for this achievement.

What you need is a **fast** loadout.

### Good preparation for the run

- travel light
- enter with a route already chosen
- bring only what helps you keep moving
- use emergency stamina tools if you rely on them
- avoid anything that encourages slow looting behavior

### What mindset works best

The wrong mindset is:

> “I’ll search carefully and see how it goes.”

The right mindset is:

> “I already know where all three are. I am only here to execute the route.”

That one mindset change is worth more than almost any single item in your inventory.

<GuideCallout title="Best preparation rule">
If an item helps you keep running, it matters.

If it tempts you to slow down and loot more, it probably hurts the attempt.
</GuideCallout>
