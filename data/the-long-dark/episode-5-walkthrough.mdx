<h2 id="part-1-astrid-in-perseverance-mills">
  Part 1 — Astrid in Perseverance Mills
</h2>

This part of the **The Long Dark Episode 5 walkthrough** covers the opening route in **Perseverance Mills**: talk to **Jane**, go to **Cooper’s Blind**, get **Dr. Larson’s clinic key**, investigate the clinic, search for the **missing people**, complete the **autopsy**, and then follow the **Terry** lead to the **radio hut**.

Episode 5 opens with Astrid arriving in **Perseverance Mills**. Jane takes her in and explains the crisis:

- Freya is getting worse
- the treatment Astrid came to deliver is still in the hardcase with Mackenzie
- Dr. Larson set up a quarantine and then disappeared
- people in town have gone missing
- many residents think there is a murderer on the loose

Your first objective is clear:

**find Larson’s trail and figure out what is happening in town.**

### Step 1 — Talk to Jane, then go to Cooper’s Blind

After the opening conversation, head to **Cooper’s Blind**, the bar near the center of town.

This should be your first stop for two reasons:

- the bartender gives you the **spare key to Dr. Larson’s clinic**
- he points you toward the **constable’s office** and the **co-op**

Do not overcomplicate the early route.  
**Bar first, clinic second, then the other town leads.**

<GuideCallout title="Do Cooper’s Blind first">
If you skip the bar, you are skipping the cleanest route through the opening section. Get the clinic key before wandering anywhere else.
</GuideCallout>

### Step 2 — Investigate Dr. Larson’s clinic

Take the key and enter **Larson’s clinic**.

This is where the game starts giving you hard evidence instead of rumors. Search the clinic carefully and read Larson’s notes. The important takeaways are:

- his patients were showing symptoms he could not explain normally
- the quarantine was not random
- Larson involved the constable in the investigation
- Larson eventually left town to search for the missing and never returned

### Step 3 — Check the constable’s office and the co-op

After the clinic, check the **constable’s office** and the **co-op**.

These locations are there to support the same mystery:

- the town was already under pressure before Larson disappeared
- community notices mention missing dogs
- people are scared, confused, and making bad guesses about what is happening

You do not need to linger here too long. The main line is still Larson and the missing people.

### Step 4 — Talk to the mill workers

Next, go speak with the **mill workers**.

They will not let you in, but the conversation matters because it gives you the next name you need:

**Terry**

This is where the game tells you that Terry runs a radio station from the **old radio hut above town**.

Do not drop everything and sprint there yet.  
Finish Larson’s trail first.

### Step 5 — Search for the missing people

Your next objective is to search for the missing townspeople outside Perseverance Mills.

There are **three people** to find in this section, including the **Constable**. This is an important early objective because it confirms that the disappearances are tied to the larger mystery Astrid is investigating.

Once you find all three, return to the main investigation and continue through the **autopsy** and **Terry** sections.

If you are stuck on the exact routes, use the dedicated <Link href="/the-long-dark-episode-5/episode-5-missing-people-locations">Missing People Locations guide</Link>.

### Step 6 — Bring a body back to the clinic and do the autopsy

This is the most important step in the early Astrid section.

Once Astrid realizes the bodies need closer examination, take one back to **Larson’s clinic** and perform the autopsy.

The result is the major clue:

**there is salt water in the lungs.**

That is what blows up the ordinary explanations.  
The deaths are not just cold, not just wildlife, and not just simple accidents.

### What to do after the autopsy

Once the autopsy is done, go follow the **Terry** lead.

Jane and the people in town make it clear that Terry matters because:

- he has been broadcasting the quarantine message
- he may be the town’s only working radio link
- nobody has heard from him in a while

That makes the **radio hut** your next main objective.

<h2 id="part-2-astrid-radio-hut-and-desperation-bay">
  Part 2 — Astrid: the radio hut and Desperation Bay
</h2>

### Step 1 — Go to the radio hut and talk to Terry

Head to the **radio hut** and speak with Terry.

This conversation is what moves the story out of town and toward the coast. Terry explains:

- Larson asked him to broadcast a quarantine warning
- he kept the transmitter running with **car batteries**
- strange electrical behavior continued during the aurora
- he picked up a **distress signal from a ship**
- the signal came from **Desperation Bay**

That last point is the one that matters most for progression.

### Step 2 — Leave town and head to Desperation Bay

After Terry, stop searching Perseverance Mills for answers.

Your next job is:

**go to Desperation Bay and investigate the ship.**

This is the cleanest progression route through the chapter.

### Step 3 — Reach the cargo ship

The Desperation Bay route is cold and dangerous, but the story objective is simple: keep moving until you reach the frozen cargo ship.

Do not treat this like a long detour.  
If your goal is story progress, go straight for the ship.

### Step 4 — Search the ship properly

Once on board, move through the ship carefully and inspect what you find.

You are looking for:

- bodies
- tied-up survivors
- signs of struggle
- damaged radio equipment
- logs and notes from the crew

The ship logs are the key evidence here. They reveal that:

- the ship lost power
- it drifted badly off course
- the crew suffered insomnia and mental breakdowns
- some people were drawn toward the water and threw themselves into the sea

After Astrid understands what happened on the ship, return to **Perseverance Mills**.

She now has enough evidence to connect the town’s missing people to the same pattern:

- the aurora

- people being drawn toward the water
- deaths that do not look normal at first glance

### Step 6 — Push through the Mathis scene

After this scene, you switch to **Mackenzie**.

Astrid’s investigation section is over for now.

  Part 3 — Mackenzie and Jace through the wilderness
</h2>

When the perspective changes, you are now playing as **Mackenzie** with **Jace**.

Your immediate objective is survival and movement, not investigation.

### Step 1 — Escape the pursuers and regroup

Mathis’s people are still after you, so keep moving through the wilderness.

The broad structure here is:

- move fast
- split up briefly to confuse the convicts
- regroup near the logging camp
- keep heading for Atwood

### Step 2 — Head for the mine

Jace remembers the route back to Atwood’s observatory, and eventually the way forward becomes the **mine**.

If you are stuck here and wondering whether the mine is optional, it is not.

**Yes, you are supposed to go through the mine.**

If you are stuck in the mine in **The Long Dark Episode 5**, the route is always the same: restore the lights, clear the gas, solve the machinery section, and keep moving forward.

The mine is one of the places players are most likely to get stuck, so here is the simple version:

### Step 1 — Get the lights working

Early in the mine, Jace makes it clear she does not want to keep moving deeper in the dark.

If you feel stuck, the answer is usually not “I missed a hidden tunnel.”  
It is:

**restore the lighting and reopen the path forward.**

If the mine feels too dark to navigate properly, you are probably still before the next required objective.

### Step 2 — Keep moving through the newly opened path

Once the lights come on, keep advancing.

The mine is not an open map. It is a progression route with a series of obstacles. When one section is solved, move to the next one instead of backtracking too much.

### Step 3 — Clear the gas problem

Later in the mine, you run into **poison gas**.

This is another common point where players hesitate. The correct approach is not to brute-force your way through it. You need to interact with the environment and solve the immediate obstacle so the gas is no longer blocking the route.

If the gas is still active, you are not done with the puzzle in that area.

### Step 4 — Look for the key and crane route

The mine then turns into another mechanical progression sequence. Jace even hints that you are likely looking for **some kind of key**.

So if you are stuck in the later mine section:

- search for the key item
- unlock the next system
- use the machinery/crane route to keep moving

### Step 5 — Follow the only valid route out

Once the lights, gas, and machinery sections are dealt with, keep pushing forward until you reach the exit and continue toward **Atwood**.

<GuideCallout title="How to think about the mine">
The mine is not about choosing between many valid routes. It is about solving one obstacle at a time: lights, gas, machinery, then forward progression.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="part-5-reach-atwood-and-start-the-substation-objective">
  Part 5 — Reach Atwood and start the substation objective
</h2>

After the mine and mountain approach, Mackenzie and Jace finally reach **Dr. Atwood** at the Suzuki Radio Telescope.

Atwood explains the problem:

- the facility only has partial geothermal backup
- important data may still exist in the system buffers
- they need the full power system back online
- Mackenzie has to restore it

That leads directly to the next major gameplay objective.

### Step 1 — Accept the three-substation task

Atwood points out **three substations** tied to the geothermal plant.

Your job is to go to each one and:

- **prime** the switches
- flip the station to **active**
- keep moving fast enough that system pressure does not build too far

### Step 2 — Use the steam pipes to find the next station

This is the most important tip in the whole substation stretch:

<GuideCallout title="Best tip for the substation section">
After you activate a substation, **follow the steam pipes**. They guide you to the next station.
</GuideCallout>

If you are lost after getting one green light, do not guess.  
Follow the pipes.

This section stacks several pressures on you:

Do not linger too long after each one.

### Step 4 — Return to Suzuki after all three are online

Once all three substations are green, head straight back to the facility.

That begins the next big set piece.

After the power comes back online, you and Jace begin the telescope sequence.

### Step 1 — Listen to Jace carefully

Jace runs the program and calls out **bearings**.

Your job as Mackenzie is not to search randomly. It is to move to the correct telescope control point and match the bearing she gives you.

### Step 2 — Find the right wheel fast

The telescope controls are built around large wheel/control points.

So if you are stuck here, the logic is:

- hear the bearing
- find the correct wheel
- set it quickly
- move to the next one

The sequence is about **speed and response**, not hidden-item hunting.

### Step 3 — Finish the scene and watch Atwood’s explanation

Once the telescope sequence succeeds, Atwood explains what the chapter has been building toward:

- Wintermute was a real person
- the aurora event is much bigger than a temporary power crisis
- the world has already entered a major geomagnetic disaster
- Mackenzie now needs to get back to Astrid

That sends the story into its final stretch.

<h2 id="part-7-astrids-sar-station-and-train-section">
  Part 7 — Astrid’s SAR station and train section
</h2>

The perspective shifts again, and now Astrid has a very practical objective:

**get the trauma kit from the SAR station.**

### Step 1 — Head north along the old logging roads

Astrid is told the **search and rescue station** is north of Desperation Bay along the old logging roads.

If you are wondering what to do after Donner’s medical crisis becomes urgent, this is the answer.

### Step 2 — Inspect the old steam engine

On the way, Astrid finds an old train that could make the trip much faster.

But it will not work immediately.

First, inspect it and confirm what is missing.

### Step 3 — Find the missing control part

This is the first real train puzzle.

Astrid notes that the engine is missing a control part, and the nearby **maintenance shed** is the best place to search first.

So the solution here is straightforward:

- search the nearby structures
- find the missing train part
- return and install it

### Step 4 — Light the firebox

Installing the part is not enough.

Next you need to:

- light the **firebox**
- get steam pressure building

If the train still is not moving, that usually means you have not fully completed the fire-starting step yet.

### Step 5 — Build pressure and release the brake

Once the fire is going, let the pressure build.

Then release the brake and start moving.

### Step 6 — Add coal when pressure drops

This is the biggest sticking point in the whole train section.

If the engine stops pulling properly or loses pressure, the fix is usually simple:

**add more coal.**

Do not waste time looking for a hidden mechanic when the answer is just fuel.

### Step 7 — Switch the tracks at dead ends

The second common problem is the route itself.

If you hit the wrong line or a dead end:

- get out
- switch the tracks
- try the route again

### Step 8 — Reach the SAR station and grab the trauma kit

Once the train section is done, the route to the **SAR station** opens up.

Go inside, take the **trauma kit**, and prepare to head back immediately.

That is the only goal here.

<h2 id="part-8-mackenzie-in-occupied-perseverance-mills">
  Part 8 — Mackenzie in occupied Perseverance Mills
</h2>

This is the final major gameplay stretch.

When Mackenzie returns to town, **Perseverance Mills** is under convict control.

### Step 1 — Move through town carefully

This section is more about controlled movement than open fighting.

The best approach is:

- avoid open sightlines
- move between cover
- take the cleanest route forward
- do not waste too much time trying to clear every enemy

### Step 2 — Find Astrid

Keep pushing until you reach Astrid.

This is the reunion scene the chapter has been building toward:

- Mackenzie finds Astrid
- he gives her the hardcase
- the immediate next priority becomes survival and escape

### Step 3 — Move toward the radio telescope

Once the reunion happens, keep the objective simple:

- get moving
- leave town
- continue toward the radio telescope and the ending scenes

At this point the gameplay is mostly giving way to story payoff.

<h2 id="episode-5-ending-summary">
  Episode 5 ending summary
</h2>

Episode 5 starts as a quarantine investigation in Perseverance Mills, expands into the Desperation Bay ship mystery, then turns into a race through Atwood’s observatory, the substations, and the radio telescope. By the end, Mackenzie and Astrid reunite, the hardcase is finally delivered, and Astrid broadcasts a warning telling anyone still alive to take shelter, avoid the aurora, and prepare for a darkness that may last far longer than anyone expected.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### What do you do first in The Long Dark Episode 5?

After talking to Jane, go to **Cooper’s Blind** and get the spare key to **Dr. Larson’s clinic**. That is the cleanest first step.

### What do you do after the autopsy in The Long Dark Episode 5?

Follow the missing-person investigation, complete the autopsy, then go to the **radio hut** and speak with **Terry**.
### Where are the missing people in The Long Dark Episode 5?

There are **three missing people** to find outside Perseverance Mills, including the **Constable**. They are spread across the search area, and one of the routes involves a basement while another requires getting past the broken bridge on the southeast side.

If you want the exact routes for all three, use the dedicated <Link href="/the-long-dark-episode-5/episode-5-missing-people-locations">Missing People Locations guide</Link>.

### Where do you go after Terry in The Long Dark Episode 5?

Go to **Desperation Bay** and investigate the cargo ship. That is the next main objective.

### How do you get through the mine in Episode 5?

Restore the lights, clear the gas-blocked section, solve the machinery/key obstacle, and keep following the only open route forward.

### What is the key to the three-substation section?

**Follow the steam pipes.** They guide you from one active station to the next.

### How does the telescope sequence work?

Jace calls out bearings, and Mackenzie has to reach the correct wheel/control point and match them quickly.

### How do you complete Astrid’s train section?

Find the missing control part, install it, light the firebox, build pressure, add coal when pressure drops, and switch the tracks when you hit dead ends.

The hardcase contains experimental medication — possibly a cure — for the cognitive symptoms tied to what Astrid believes is coming with the Aurora, including symptoms like insomnia, memory loss, and fugue-like states.

### What do you do near the end in Perseverance Mills?

As Mackenzie, sneak through the convict-controlled town, find Astrid, give her the hardcase, and move toward the radio telescope and final scenes.
