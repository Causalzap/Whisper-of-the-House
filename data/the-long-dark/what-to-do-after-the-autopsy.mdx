<h2 id="why-the-autopsy-changes-the-route">
  Why the autopsy changes the route
</h2>

The autopsy is not just another clue pickup.

It is the point where the investigation stops feeling like a local missing-person case and starts pointing to something bigger.

Before the autopsy, the town still has room for bad explanations:

- cold exposure
- wildlife
- random panic
- a possible murderer

After the autopsy, Astrid finds the thing that breaks those explanations:

**salt water in the lungs.**

That is why this moment matters so much. It tells you the game is done with ordinary town theories. The next step is no longer “search another building.” The next step is to follow the person most connected to the quarantine message and the town’s outside radio contact.

That person is **Terry**.

<GuideCallout title="Core judgment">
The autopsy is the handoff point.

It is not a “keep searching Perseverance Mills” moment. It is a “go to Terry now” moment.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="why-terry-is-the-next-objective">
  Why Terry is the next objective
</h2>

A lot of players hesitate here because Terry can sound optional at first.

He is not.

Jane makes Terry important for three separate reasons:

- he has been broadcasting the **quarantine message**
- he may be the town’s only useful **radio link**
- people in town have **not heard from him in a while**

That means the Terry step is not just a side conversation. It is the bridge between the autopsy mystery and the next major lead.

If you skip him and keep wandering around town, you are delaying the actual route forward.

### What Terry does for the story

Terry is the character who takes the investigation out of **Perseverance Mills** and points it toward the coast.

Without him, you do not get the clean push toward the next major area.

That is why “what to do after the autopsy” has such a short answer:

**go to Terry.**

<h2 id="where-to-go-after-the-autopsy">
  Where to go after the autopsy
</h2>

After the autopsy, head for the **old radio hut above town**.

This is Terry’s position, and it is the next main objective the story wants you to follow.

### The important part

Do not overthink this transition.

You do **not** need to:

- re-check every house in Perseverance Mills
- keep circling the clinic for another clue
- go back into a full Missing People search loop
- treat the mill workers, co-op, and constable office like fresh major objectives again

Those places help build the opening mystery, but after the autopsy the route narrows.

### What to look for

If you are heading the right way, you are looking for:

- the route out of town toward the **radio hut**
- the hut itself on higher ground
- visible signs that someone is still there, like the **smoke from the chimney**

That is the confirmation that you are back on the main line.
