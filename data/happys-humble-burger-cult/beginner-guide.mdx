<h2 id="tutorial-and-controls">
  Finish the Tutorial and learn the essential controls
</h2>

I would not skip the Tutorial on a new save.

The burger is useful practice, but the larger lesson is that normal service,
Closeout, and elevator escape are three separate stages. That distinction is
exactly what I misunderstood during my first real attempts.

These are the default PC controls I use:

- **Left click:** interact or pick up;
- **Right click:** discard the held item;
- **E:** use the held item;
- **F:** flashlight;
- **Tab:** open the Tablet;
- **R:** shove;
- **Q:** punch;
- **X:** spit.

R, Q, and X control shove, punch, and spit, but I avoid them near active customers during a serious run.

All inputs can now be rebound in Settings. I still memorize Right Click, Tab, and
F before the first real shift. Right Click clears a bad object from my hands,
Tab shows the active objectives, and the flashlight becomes important when the
restaurant enters the escape phase.

<h2 id="solo-or-coop">
  Solo or Co-op: which is easier for the first clear?
</h2>

Solo and Co-op are separate modes, not the same lobby with empty player slots.

  <Image

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Solo is supported and became fairer after the July 18 patch, but Co-op still
    gives me more room to learn the kitchen and the route back to the elevator.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

For the easiest first completion, I choose Co-op.

Two players can complete shifts, but only when both stop duplicating work. Three
players feel much more comfortable because the third person can handle sides,
drinks, hazards, or the register while the sandwich player stays at the grill
and assembly station.

Solo is more reasonable after the balance patch. A fresh Closeout begins with
one duty, and a clean service phase gives me more time before Joy arrives.
However, Toe is not a full second player. In my shifts, he prepared drinks,
pies, and other secondary items, but I still had to collect his work, move it to
the active bag, and complete the closing objective myself.

My practical rule is:

> I use Co-op to learn the station route. I return to Solo after I can move from
> the register to every kitchen station and back to the elevator without opening
> the Tablet just to find the room.

<h2 id="station-route">
  Learn the first restaurant route before serving
</h2>

The first restaurant felt confusing because I tried to learn every shelf at
once. I only need one repeatable loop.

From the entrance, I memorize this route:

<div className="my-4 space-y-1 font-mono text-sm leading-7">
  <div>entrance and shift lever</div>
  <div>→ lobby register</div>
  <div>→ recipe display and burger assembly station</div>
  <div>→ freezer and grill</div>
  <div>→ fryer and baked-goods area</div>
  <div>→ drinks and shakes</div>
  <div>→ bagging / fulfillment station</div>
  <div>→ entrance elevator</div>
</div>

Before starting the shift, I physically face each station once. I do not spend that setup time reading every recipe, checking optional menus, or searching for collectibles.

<h2 id="contract-and-modifier">
  Best contract and modifier for the first shift
</h2>

The shift will not begin until a contract is selected. After that, the modifier
can make the first restaurant either manageable or unnecessarily chaotic.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/happys-humble-burger-cult/happys-humble-burger-cult-first-shift-modifier-choice.webp"
    alt="Happy's Humble Burger Cult modifier choice between twice as many events per shift and faster Joy movement"
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Busted Stabilibussy doubles the events in a shift, while Mad Cow makes Joy
    move faster. I avoid both when I am still learning the first restaurant.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

I read the complete card instead of choosing from the title or positive effect.

For a first clear, I avoid modifiers that:

- create **twice as many events per shift**;
- make Joy move faster;
- remove or disrupt Toe when I planned around his help;
- add Pickup, Drive-Thru, or another order queue;
- or make the restaurant harder to see before I know the layout.

I prefer effects that reduce the workload or increase the margin for error. One
Night Crew effect I encountered started the shift with **one quarter of the
quota already filled**. That is valuable for a first clear, but I still read its
paired condition before selecting it.

I do not gamble on a high-reward contract while learning the first kitchen. A
small, completed payout is more useful than a larger contract that never reaches
the elevator.

<h2 id="first-order">
  First order walkthrough: register to bagging station
</h2>

The first customer is where the themed menu names become overwhelming.

In one first order, the customer asked for:

- **Deep Sea Sammy**
- **Hot Pie**
- **Strawberry Jacuzzi**

I translate that into station language before pressing Submit:

> One sandwich, one baked item, one drink. Three items total.

  <Image

    alt="Happy's Humble Burger Cult first lobby order for a Deep Sea Sammy, Hot Pie, and Strawberry Jacuzzi"
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    I split the order into its three station categories before leaving the
    register instead of trying to remember three joke names in the kitchen.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

My first-order route is:

1. Accept and read the complete order at the lobby register.
2. Count the requested items and name their categories.
3. Submit the order to the kitchen.
4. Check the written sandwich recipe before taking a patty.
5. Go to the Freezer, take the correct patty, and close the door.
6. Turn to the Grill and cook the patty.
7. Build the sandwich from the bottom bun upward.
8. Let Toe or a teammate handle the baked item and drink.
9. Collect every requested item at one bagging station.
10. Check the sandwich name and the total item count before delivery.

I start with the sandwich because it has the most failure points. The pie and
drink can finish while the patty cooks, but I do not let those items enter a bag
until the sandwich owner confirms which order the bag belongs to.

That short check prevents a correct drink from entering the wrong bag and stops

<h2 id="wrong-order">
  How I avoid Weird Sandwiches and wrong orders
</h2>

The recipe picture is useful for recognizing the sandwich shape, but I read the
written list for sauces and repeated toppings.

Mayo was the ingredient I missed most often because it was difficult to see in
the picture. Once I started reading every ingredient from the bottom bun to the
top bun, I could identify exactly which step failed.

My sandwich check is:

1. correct bun;
2. correct patty;
3. correct number of vegetables, cheese, and bacon;
4. every listed sauce;
5. correct top bun.

If the result becomes a **Weird Sandwich**, I discard it and rebuild. In my early
runs, one wrong bun was enough to create the label, and I could not reliably
remove a single bad layer.

I also check the order name before checking every topping. A Burger Burger can
look complete and still be wrong when the customer ordered a Biggly Piggly.

<h2 id="simulation-stability">
  What Simulation Stability does after the July 18 patch
</h2>

Simulation Stability is no longer only a threat meter during service. The amount
left when I clock out now buys extra time before Joy arrives.

That changes my first-shift decision:

> Once quota is secure, I preserve the remaining Stability instead of spending
> it on one more uncertain order.

The training information in my launch-week run stated that completing an order
restored **2% Stability**. I treat that as a launch-week value and a small
recovery, not a reason to rush bad orders.

My service priorities are:

1. finish the active order correctly;
2. extinguish a fire that blocks the Grill, bagging station, or main route;
3. close the Freezer if it is making the floor slippery;
4. ignore optional exploration;
5. stop taking new work once quota is secured.

I do not punch, shove, or throw food near an active customer during a serious
run. Those actions can interrupt the register process, and one Co-op order
disappeared after a teammate assaulted the customer being served.

<h2 id="after-quota">
  What to do after quota: Closeout and elevator escape
</h2>

Quota is not the end of the shift.

### Clock out after reaching quota

For my first clear, I use this sequence:

<div className="my-4 space-y-1 font-mono text-sm leading-7">
  <div>quota met</div>
  <div>→ decline Overtime</div>
  <div>→ stop accepting orders</div>
  <div>→ return to the same shift lever</div>
  <div>→ clock out</div>
</div>

I do not run directly to the elevator after quota. Pulling the lever ends normal
service and begins Closeout; it does not unlock the exit immediately.

I also skip Overtime on the first successful attempt. A rushed bonus order can
consume the Stability that would otherwise become extra time before Joy arrives.

### Complete the duty and reach the elevator

After the July 18 patch, a fresh completion streak starts with **one Closeout
Duty**. The duty count then increases with consecutive completed shifts.

Closeout Duties in my tested runs included:

- turning off appliances;
- resetting rat traps;
- taking out trash;
- restocking the kitchen;
- disposing of grease;
- calibrating thermostats;
- extinguishing fires.

I open the Tablet, complete the duty currently listed for that shift, and wait
for the objective to change to **Escape** or **Get to the Elevator**. Only then
do I return to the entrance.

<h2 id="troubleshooting">
  First-shift troubleshooting
</h2>

### The shift will not start

Select the contract and required modifier, then make sure the lobby is ready.

### The first customer times out while I am exploring

Learn the key stations before starting service. Once the shift begins, return to
the register instead of continuing the tour.

### I cannot find the bagging station

Look beside the soft-drink and shake equipment on the fulfillment side of the
first kitchen.

### The sandwich becomes a Weird Sandwich

Check the bun, written sauces, and duplicate toppings. Discard an invalid build
instead of adding more layers.

### I reached quota but the elevator is still locked

Clock out at the shift lever and complete the active Tablet duty. Quota alone
does not unlock the elevator.

### Joy arrives before I finish Closeout

Clock out with more Stability remaining. The current patch converts it into
additional time before Joy arrives.

### Two players keep making the same item

Name one sandwich owner and one sides/drinks/bag owner before anyone takes a
patty.

<h2 id="faq">
  Happy's Humble Burger Cult First Shift FAQ
</h2>

### Why did my Closeout have more than one duty after the balance patch?

The number scales with consecutive completed shifts. A fresh streak starts at
one duty, then the workload increases as the crew keeps completing shifts. This
means a later run can have more duties even on the same restaurant.

### Can I use Pacifist Mode to learn the first restaurant?

Yes. The launch hotfix added a Pacifist Mode without enemies or threats, which
makes it useful for learning station locations and the cooking route. I have not
confirmed whether it changes achievements, progression, or rewards, so I would
not assume those behave identically without checking the current build.

### Can another player cancel the customer I am serving?

Yes. In one Co-op run, a teammate attacked the customer during order entry and
the active customer disappeared. I keep punches, shoves, spit, and thrown food
away from the register until the order has been submitted.

### Can I change the default controls?

Yes. Input rebinding was added in the first launch hotfix. The controls listed
above are the defaults I learned with, not mandatory bindings.

### Why did the order disappear before I finished cooking?

The customer may have timed out, or another player may have interrupted the
customer at the register. Check whether the order reached the kitchen queue
before continuing to build food for it.
