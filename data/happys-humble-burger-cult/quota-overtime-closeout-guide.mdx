<h2 id="order-categories">
  Translate menu names into kitchen jobs
</h2>

The themed food names are harder to remember than the work they create.

When a customer asks for a **Deep Sea Sammy**, **Hot Pie**, and **Strawberry
Jacuzzi**, I do not carry those three names around the restaurant. I translate
them into:

> One sandwich, one baked item, one drink. Three items total.

    I classify the three themed names before pressing Submit. That tells the
    kitchen which stations must work and gives the bag owner a final count of
    three.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

The four terminal categories lead me to four station groups:

- **Burgers & Sandwiches:** recipe display, Freezer, Grill, and assembly station;
- **Fried Foods:** Fryer and the Fried Foods storage;
- **Baked Goods:** the oven or baked-goods station;
- **Drinks & Shakes:** the matching drink machine and its nearby cups.

The player at the register becomes the **order caller**. That player reads the
request once, assigns the sandwich, names the extra categories, and repeats only
corrections. Four people repeating every item produced more duplicate food than
useful information.

<h2 id="kitchen-stations">
  My kitchen route after the order reaches the queue
</h2>

I use the same sequence for every standard Lobby order:

<div className="my-4 space-y-1 font-mono text-sm leading-7">
  <div>kitchen order queue</div>
  <div>→ written recipe display</div>
  <div>→ Freezer for the correct patty</div>
  <div>→ Grill</div>
  <div>→ burger assembly station</div>
  <div>→ Fryer, oven, or drink machine</div>
  <div>→ one named bag</div>
  <div>→ the order's Lobby, Pickup, or Drive-Thru submission point</div>
</div>

I do not take a random patty before checking the recipe. Beef, chicken, pork,
and fish are easy to confuse in a dark kitchen, and the wrong patty invalidates
an otherwise correct sandwich.

On the first restaurant, the Freezer and Grill are nearly opposite each other.
I take the patty, close the Freezer, turn to the Grill, and then return to the
assembly side. I do not circle the entire kitchen.

The Bagging Station is the final station, not an optional staging shelf.
Finished food does not enter the order automatically.

<h2 id="weird-sandwich">
  Follow the recipe and stop a Weird Sandwich early
</h2>

My bottom-to-top check is:

1. correct bottom bun;
2. correct patty or meatless base;
3. every topping in the listed order;
4. duplicate ingredients in the listed count;
5. every sauce;
6. correct top bun.

When Complex Orders are enabled, I read the base recipe first and the customer's
modification second. A visible condition such as **No Bacon** or **No Tomato**
overrides the normal stack for that one order.

I do not build the standard sandwich and attempt surgery afterward.

### I rebuild immediately when

- the bun or patty is wrong;
- the station labels the result `Weird Sandwich`;
- I cannot remember whether a hidden sauce was added;
- a required duplicate ingredient is missing;
- or another player added an unknown item to my stack.

### I keep the current build when

- the sandwich itself is correct and only waiting for a side or drink;
- the patty is ready but has not burnt;
- or the order caller catches a missing topping before I close the sandwich.

<h2 id="cooking-states">
  How to recognize cooked and burnt food
</h2>

I do not publish a universal number of seconds for patties, fries, nuggets,
pies, or cookies yet. The available runs did not isolate each station cleanly
enough to separate actual cook time from pauses, events, and delayed pickups.

What I can verify is more useful during a live shift:

- the Tutorial says a patty changes color when it is cooked;
- patties can continue past ready and burn;
- Fries and Salmon Nuggets can burn when nobody owns the Fryer;
- pies and cookies should be checked while the sandwich is being assembled;
- and leaving a finished item unattended is more dangerous than starting it a
  few seconds late.

My low-risk timing rule is:

> Start the patty and one requested side or baked item together. Assign a person
> to each active appliance, then remove the food as soon as its ready state is
> visible.

I prepare only one small spare batch of a common side when the team is stable.
I do not fill the kitchen with speculative fries or cookies while an active
order is still missing its sandwich.

<h2 id="sides-and-drinks">
  Salmon Nuggets, baked goods, drinks, and cups
</h2>

### Salmon Nuggets

Salmon Nuggets are a prepared Fried Food item stored near or below the Fries
area. I do not take a raw fish patty and search for a cutting station.

One team spent more than a minute carrying raw fish around before finding the
actual Salmon Nuggets beside the Fryer supplies.

### Baked goods

I start a requested Hot Pie or Cookie while the patty cooks, then give that
appliance to one named player. In one kitchen, the team lost Fries twice because
everyone assumed somebody else was watching them.

### Drinks and shakes

I read the exact drink name and use the cup supplied beside the matching machine.
I do not move an empty cup from one drink setup to another and assume it will
work.

I have not added a hard red-cup-versus-clear-cup table because the available
launch-week material did not show both cup types and machines clearly enough for
a version-safe rule. The station layout or UI should be treated as the current
source until that pairing is retested.

Toe prepared drinks and baked items in several shifts, but Toe did not complete
the bag. I still collected the item, checked it against the active request, and
placed it in the correct order.

<h2 id="bagging-errors">
  Bagging and fixing incorrect deliveries
</h2>

A correct sandwich can still become a failed order at the Bagging Station.

I keep one physical bag beside the player who owns the order. The bag is named

- `Lobby`;
- `Pickup`;

<h2 id="order-channels">
  Lobby, Pickup, and Drive-Thru are separate queues
</h2>

I never treat the three delivery channels as interchangeable.

### Lobby

The customer orders at the front register. I return the completed bag to the
Lobby fulfillment point.

### Pickup

Pickup adds a separate order section. The first time my team enabled it, we
finished the food and then lost time asking who should receive the bag.

    Pickup is a separate queue, not another Lobby customer. I assign it to one

### Drive-Thru

Drive-Thru orders use their own service side. I do not carry a Drive-Thru bag to
the Lobby just because the sandwich name matches another active request.

The available English material did not provide a clean enough frame to publish
a precise Drive-Thru submission-point diagram, so I keep the rule narrow:
identify the Drive-Thru queue and its service window before enabling the lever.

I leave Pickup and Drive-Thru disabled when:

- the team still expires normal Lobby orders;
- nobody knows the extra submission point;
- the kitchen does not have a fixed bag owner;
- or two players are already duplicating food.

<h2 id="kitchen-roles">
  My two-player split and how I use Toe
</h2>

Two players can finish shifts, but both players cannot own the same sandwich.

### Player 1: order caller, sandwich, and bag

- reads the request and names the categories;
- checks the written recipe;
- cooks the patty and assembles the sandwich;
- keeps the active bag beside that station;
- performs the final item-count call.

### Player 2: sides, drinks, and kitchen control

- prepares Fries, Nuggets, Cookies, Pies, and drinks;
- watches the active Fryer or oven;
- closes the Freezer;
- handles a Grill fire that blocks the route;
- brings each completed item to the named bag.

When two sandwich-heavy orders arrive, I use the second prep station and give
each station one owner and one bag.

<h2 id="faq">
  Happy's Humble Burger Cult Orders and Kitchen FAQ
</h2>

### Can I remove one wrong ingredient from a Weird Sandwich?

I did not find a reliable one-layer removal method. Because the wrong bun
immediately created a Weird Sandwich in the Tutorial, I rebuild before adding
more ingredients rather than risk losing the entire order timer.

### Can I reuse food after an order expires?

Only when the item exactly matches a new live order and has no customer-specific
Complex Order modification. Otherwise, I clear it from the active station so it
cannot enter the next bag by mistake.

### Does one extra item make the whole bag incorrect?

It can. One Co-op bag was rejected after the team discovered too many items
inside. I match the exact item count instead of treating extra Fries or a spare
drink as harmless.

### Why can I not find Salmon Nuggets beside the raw fish?

Raw fish is a sandwich patty. Salmon Nuggets are a separate Fried Food supply
near or below the Fries storage, so I search the Fryer side rather than the
Freezer.

### Can Toe replace the second kitchen player?

No. Toe can reduce secondary-item work, but a player still has to identify the
finished item, collect it, prevent duplicates, place it in the correct bag, and
submit the order.

### When should I enable Pickup or Drive-Thru?

After the team can clear Lobby orders without expiry and one player can point to
the extra queue's submission point before service starts. I do not learn an
extra channel while an active bag is already waiting.
