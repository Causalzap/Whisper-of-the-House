<h2 id="faq">
  Timberborn guide FAQ
</h2>

### Which Timberborn guide should I read first?

Start with the Beginner Guide if you are new. If you are already losing to a specific problem, jump directly to the First Drought, Dam, Irrigation, Badwater, or Automation guide.

### Why do some Timberborn guides use 2.1, 2.5, or 3 water per beaver per day?

Use 2.1–2.5/day for normal planning and 3/day only as a conservative stress buffer. The First Drought Guide explains the calculation in detail.

### Why do my crops dry when my beavers still have water?

Drinking water and soil irrigation are different systems. Tanks keep beavers alive, but crops need nearby water that is close and deep enough to keep land green.

### Should I always build a dam before the first drought?

No. Build an early dam only if the site is realistic. If the river is wide, far, shallow, or awkward, stored water and better access may be safer first.

### When should I use floodgates instead of a dam?

Use floodgates when you need adjustable water height, controlled reservoir filling, drought release, badwater isolation, or future automation.

### When should I start badwater planning?

Start once basic drought survival works, and earlier if a badwater source or Badtide Drain can reach your clean river, pumps, farms, or irrigation route.

### Should I automate early?

No. Test the route manually first. Automation is best when it improves a working system, not when it hides bad routing.
