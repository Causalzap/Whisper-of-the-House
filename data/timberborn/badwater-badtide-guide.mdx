<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
To stop **badwater and badtides in Timberborn**, separate toxic water from clean water before it touches your farms, pumps, reservoirs, or worker paths.

Badwater is dangerous because contamination can spread through connected water and leave terrain unsafe even after the visible flow recedes. A good defense is not just “block it.” You need to identify the source, give badwater a safe exit, protect farm borders, avoid trapped toxic pockets, and keep beavers out of contaminated paths.

Once the colony is safe, badwater can become useful through Badwater Pump, storage, Centrifuge, and Extract production. Defense comes first. Production comes later.

<h2 id="timberborn-badwater-badtide-guide">
  Timberborn badwater and badtide guide
</h2>

This **Timberborn badwater and badtide guide** is for the point where drought survival is no longer the only problem. Once toxic water enters the map, a river that used to save your colony can become the thing that kills it.

The core rule is:

**Do not let badwater share your drinking, farming, or irrigation route unless you control exactly where it enters, where it leaves, and what land it touches.**

A strong badwater plan answers five questions:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Where does toxic water enter?</li>
  <li>Is there a Badtide Drain or unexpected source on this map?</li>
  <li>Where can toxic water leave safely?</li>
  <li>Which farm borders or clean-water routes need protection?</li>
  <li>Can beavers build the defense without walking through badwater?</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="badwater-vs-badtide">
  Badwater vs badtide: what you are defending against
</h2>

**Badwater** is the toxic water itself. **Badtide** is a toxic-water season that can push badwater through routes that may normally carry clean water.

This distinction matters because a badwater defense is not only about the source. It is about the full route.

<GuideTable
caption="Timberborn badwater, badtide, terrain and map threats."
columns={threatColumns}
rows={threatRows}
/>

On default settings, badtides can start around a 40% chance, then become more likely after repeated droughts. On easy settings, a badtide may be short. On hard settings, you should plan for much longer events, often several days at a time.

<GuideTable
caption="Badtide timing and planning pressure."
columns={timingColumns}
rows={timingRows}
/>

The exact pressure depends on map, settings, difficulty, and cycle history. The planning lesson is stable: **a temporary manual gate may survive the first badtide, but a long colony needs a real toxic-water route.**

<h2 id="why-badwater-is-dangerous">
  Why badwater is dangerous: contamination spreads
</h2>

Badwater is not just ugly water flowing past your town. One bad connection can poison a clean river, ruin crops, contaminate terrain, and sicken workers before you finish fixing the gate that caused it.

That is why badwater deserves a real route, not a temporary panic wall. If toxic water touches your main river or farm channel, the damage can spread much faster than a new player expects.

<GuideTable
caption="How badwater contamination hurts a Timberborn colony."
columns={contaminationColumns}
rows={contaminationRows}
/>

This is why badwater deserves immediate attention. A colony can survive a missed comfort building. It may not survive a poisoned farm belt, dead crops, contaminated drinking water, and sick workers at the same time.

<h2 id="badtide-drains">
  Check for Badtide Drains before you trust the map
</h2>

Some maps have **Badtide Drains**. These can stay inactive during normal weather and drought, then release badwater during badtides. That makes them easy to forget during early town planning.

If badwater appears from an unexpected place, do not assume the game bugged out. Pause and trace the flow. Look for extra drains, side channels, or terrain routes that only matter during toxic seasons.

A good pre-badtide map check is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>follow every source and drain near your farm district,</li>
  <li>check whether multiple badwater routes merge downstream,</li>
  <li>avoid placing farms in a basin with no toxic-water exit,</li>
  <li>protect low ground where badwater can pool,</li>
  <li>plan an outlet before the first long badtide.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="extract-production">
  Badwater is not only a threat: turn it into Extract later
</h2>

Once your colony can safely handle badwater, the next step is using it. Badwater can feed an Extract production chain instead of being treated only as waste.

The basic idea is:

The common Centrifuge conversion is:

**4 badwater + 0.1 logs → 1 Extract per 0.75-hour cycle**

That means one Centrifuge can process roughly **5.3 badwater per hour** before you account for logistics and downtime.

Do not rush this chain before defense. Extract production is valuable, but it should come after:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>farms are protected,</li>
  <li>clean-water pumps are safe,</li>
  <li>badwater has a controlled route,</li>

  <li>overflow has somewhere safe to go.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">
  Timberborn badwater and badtide FAQ
</h2>

### How do I stop badwater in Timberborn?

Give badwater a separate route away from your main river, farms, pumps, and clean irrigation. Use levees, floodgates, barriers, and safe outlets.

### Why is badwater so dangerous?

Badwater can contaminate connected water and terrain. If it reaches your farm or drinking route, the damage can spread far beyond the first tile.

### What is a Badtide Drain?

A Badtide Drain is a map feature that can release badwater during badtides. It may not matter during normal weather, so check the map before building downstream.

### How long can badtides last?

It depends on settings and difficulty. Easy badtides can be short, while hard badtides can last several days. Long colonies should plan for longer events over time.

### Can contaminated terrain still hurt me after badwater leaves?

Yes. Soil can remain unsafe after the visible badwater recedes, and contamination can continue affecting crops and nearby land.

### Should I block badwater completely?

Not always. Toxic water still needs somewhere to go. A controlled diversion is usually safer than trapping it.

### How do I use badwater for Extract?

Use Badwater Pump and storage, then process badwater in a Centrifuge. A common conversion is 4 badwater plus 0.1 logs into 1 Extract per 0.75-hour cycle.

### What is different for Folktails and Iron Teeth?

Folktails can use Contamination Barriers to protect farmland. Iron Teeth use different irrigation tools and can also use badwater discharge for power setups.

### When should I automate badwater control?

Automate only after the manual route works. A simple starter circuit is Contamination Sensor above 5% OR Badtide Weather opening the badwater outlet.

### Why is badwater trapped behind my floodgate?

The gate or terrain may have created a dead-end pocket. Check the inlet, outlet, and any terrain lip that prevents water from flushing away.
