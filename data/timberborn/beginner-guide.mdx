<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
For a safe **Timberborn beginner start**, pick Folktails on a forgiving map, pause on day 1, then build short paths, wood, water, food, storage, a conditional first dam, an early Inventor, and a Forester.

Use numbers instead of guesses: each beaver needs about **3 water per day**, a **Small Water Tank stores 30**, and a **Large Water Tank stores 100**. If your nearby river is narrow, start the first dam early. If the river is wide or awkward, delay the dam and prioritize filled tanks first.

Protect green land for farms and trees, build most structures on dry brown ground, and start replacement tree planting before the starting forest disappears. 
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="timberborn-beginner-guide">
  Timberborn beginner guide: first 30 days
</h2>

This **Timberborn beginner guide** is written for the first 30 days, where most failed colonies die from one missing system rather than one dramatic mistake.

If you are new, start with **Folktails** on a forgiving map. They are the cleaner learning faction because early farming, tree planting, and water storage are easier to understand before you add specialized systems.

Your first colony has five jobs:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>store drinking water,</li>
  <li>turn green land into food,</li>
  <li>finish or postpone the first dam based on the map,</li>
  <li>replace the trees you cut,</li>
  <li>start science early enough for the second cycle.</li>
</ul>

Do those five things and the game becomes much easier to read.

<h2 id="day-one-paths">
  Day 1: pause, plan paths, then unpause
</h2>

Pause immediately. The first mistake is letting beavers wander while you decide where everything goes.

Build short paths to your first jobs:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Lumberjack Flags near reachable trees,</li>
  <li>Gatherer Flag for berries,</li>
  <li>Water Pump on reachable river water,</li>
  <li>small storage near work areas,</li>
  <li>an early route toward the first dam site if that site is realistic.</li>
</ul>

<figure className="my-8">

Paths are not cosmetic. A beaver on a path moves about 30% faster than on bare ground, so a short path network can be the difference between finishing survival work on time and watching it stall.

<h2 id="green-land">
  Green land rule: farms on green, buildings on brown
</h2>

Green irrigated land is valuable because it grows food and trees. Use it carefully.

A simple rule works well:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Use green land for carrots, berries, trees, and future crop space.</li>
  <li>Use dry brown land for housing, storage, science, and industry whenever possible.</li>
  <li>Use paths to connect those zones instead of covering your best fertile strip.</li>
</ul>

This rule is especially important on starts with limited irrigated ground. Losing a few green tiles to housing can feel harmless on day 2 and painful when your food margin is thin during drought.

<h2 id="forester">
  Forester: do not let the wood economy silently collapse
</h2>

The Forester is easy to delay because your starting forest looks large. That is the trap.

Trees take time to regrow. If you only cut the starting trees and never plant replacements, the colony can feel stable and then suddenly run out of logs around the early midgame.

Build the first Forester around **day 4–5** if your opening allows it. Then mark planting zones before the nearby trees are gone.

Use the Forester for:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>replacement trees near your starting district,</li>
  <li>future log supply for dams, tanks, levees, and housing,</li>
  <li>stable construction after the first drought,</li>
  <li>avoiding emergency long-distance wood runs.</li>
</ul>

A Forester fixes the future, not the current empty stockpile. Build it before the shortage becomes visible.

<h2 id="inventor-and-science">
  Inventor and science: start earlier than you think
</h2>

The first Inventor should not wait until every survival structure is perfect. Build it around **day 3–5** and assign a worker when the colony can spare one.

At a 14-hour workday, a single Inventor can produce about **14 science per day**. That means the first science building can meaningfully push you toward early unlocks before the second drought.

Good early science goals include:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Large Water Tank if storage is the weak point,</li>
  <li>Floodgate if river control is the weak point,</li>
  <li>Lumber Mill if planks are blocking stronger structures,</li>
  <li>map-specific unlocks only after water and food are safe.</li>
</ul>

Do not overstaff science if pumps, farms, builders, or lumberjacks are empty. The point is to start the science clock early, not starve survival jobs.

<h2 id="food-and-farms">
  Food and farms: carrots first, storage before drought
</h2>

Carrots are a safe first crop because they are simple and fast enough for early survival. Berries help while the farm gets started, but do not rely on berries alone.

Your early food plan should be:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Gatherer Flag for berries,</li>
  <li>Farmhouse on or near green land,</li>
  <li>carrots planted early,</li>
  <li>storage reachable by paths,</li>
  <li>no unnecessary buildings covering future farm tiles.</li>
</ul>

The danger is not only running out of food today. The danger is having crops in the ground when irrigation disappears.

<h2 id="faq">
  Timberborn beginner FAQ
</h2>

### Is Folktails still the best beginner pick in Timberborn 1.0?

Yes for most new players. Folktails make the early loop easier to learn because farming, tree planting, and water storage are straightforward.

### What if my starting river is too wide for an early dam?

Do not force the day 2–3 dam. Prioritize filled tanks, food, wood, and paths first, then build the dam when the site is reachable and affordable.

### Does Forester planting zone size matter?

Yes. A tiny planting zone will not replace your starting forest fast enough. Mark enough nearby land that your future log supply grows while you handle drought prep.

### Is it ever okay to build on green land?

Sometimes, if the map gives you more fertile land than you need or the layout demands it. As a beginner rule, avoid it until you understand your food margin.

### Should I run the Inventor during drought?

Only if stored water, pump work, food harvest, and builder assignments are already safe. If survival jobs are behind, temporarily move the worker and restart science after the crisis.

### What should I do if my first drought barely succeeds?

Do not add many new beds. Fix the bottleneck first: water storage, food, Forester coverage, science unlocks, dam access, or path efficiency.
