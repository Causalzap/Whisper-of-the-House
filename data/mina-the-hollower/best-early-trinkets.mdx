<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
The best early trinket in **Mina the Hollower** is **Proto Spark**, found through the Duke route in **Queensbury Crypt**. It saves Mina from death once, which makes it the strongest early safety pick after you clear that route.

Before you get Proto Spark, use **Primed Vial Pouch** if you need more healing or **Steady Soles** if terrain and knockback are causing problems.

You start with limited trinket slots, so your early choice matters. Look for **Trinket Bags** to equip more than one trinket, then use the boss loadout section below to decide your safest setup.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="best-early-trinkets">
  Best early trinkets by problem
</h2>

Do not pick early trinkets by raw power alone. Pick the trinket that fixes what is currently making your run worse: dying, running out of heals, losing Bones, moving badly through terrain or missing damage windows.

<GuideTable
caption="Best early Mina the Hollower trinkets by player problem."

rows={priorityRows}
/>

For most players, the early priority is simple: get through **Queensbury Crypt**, secure **Proto Spark**, then build around survival until you have more trinket slots.

<h2 id="trinket-slots">
  How trinket slots work early
</h2>

Before chasing every trinket, remember one important thing: you cannot equip everything at once. Early on, your trinket choice matters because your available slots are limited.

<GuideTable
caption="Trinket slots and Trinket Bags."
columns={slotColumns}
rows={slotRows}
/>

If you only have one slot, use it for the problem you are facing right now. If you are going into a dangerous route, use survival. If terrain is the problem, use movement. If you already have a second slot, start combining safety effects instead of swapping constantly.

<h2 id="boss-loadouts">
  Early boss and route loadouts
</h2>

Proto Spark is the best early safety trinket, but you may not have it before your first Queensbury Crypt boss attempt. Use your available slot based on the fight in front of you.

<GuideTable
caption="Early trinket loadouts by situation."
columns={loadoutColumns}
rows={loadoutRows}
/>

The safest general rule is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

  <li><strong>After Proto Spark:</strong> make it your default danger-route trinket.</li>
  <li><strong>With two slots:</strong> pair Proto Spark with Primed Vial Pouch for a very safe early setup.</li>
  <li><strong>If terrain is killing you:</strong> swap in Steady Soles instead of forcing a damage trinket.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="primed-vial-pouch">
  Primed Vial Pouch gives you more healing room
</h2>

Use **Primed Vial Pouch** when your biggest problem is running out of healing before the route is done. It lets Mina carry **2 extra Plasma Vials**, and using vials always restores some health.

To get it, head left outside the main gate of **Ossex**. Get past the two guards shooting beams, then go down and to the right. Burrow into the marked roof spot to enter the building below. From there, take the bottom-left stairs inside and claim the reward.

<h2 id="steady-soles">
  Steady Soles makes bad terrain easier
</h2>

Use **Steady Soles** when the environment is causing more damage than the enemies. It helps with movement penalties on rough terrain and helps resist knockback.

To get Steady Soles, start from the checkpoint near the eastern side of **Ossex**. Go up the stairs, head left onto the balcony route, then burrow through the rightmost attic opening. Work across the attic, check the Bone Stone chest while you are inside, then exit through the top-left stairs to reach the reward.

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Start from the checkpoint near the eastern exit of Ossex.</li>
  <li>Take the stairs up, then head left toward the balcony.</li>
  <li>Burrow through the rightmost opening to enter the attic.</li>
  <li>Cross the attic route and grab the Bone Stone chest if you want the extra value.</li>
  <li>Exit through the top-left stairs and collect Steady Soles.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="bell-of-grace">
  Bell of Grace helps if deaths are draining your Bones
</h2>

Use **Bell of Grace** if repeated deaths are costing you too many Bones. It is a resource-protection trinket, not a first-choice damage or boss trinket.

The important question is whether you should spend **1,000 Bones** on it now. If you still need the Isle Map or basic upgrades, buy those first. If Bone loss is already slowing your progress, Bell of Grace becomes much more attractive.

<h2 id="chain-capacitor">
  Chain Capacitor is for confident attackers
</h2>

Use **Chain Capacitor** when you can land hits consistently. It boosts damage for consecutive hits, but if you keep missing, you lose the value that makes it good.

To get it, leave Ossex from the east, go one screen north, then use a key on the building surrounded by grass.

The right trinket is not always the highest-damage trinket. Early in Mina the Hollower, survival and route comfort usually matter more than perfect damage.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best early trinket in Mina the Hollower?

The best early trinket is Proto Spark because it saves Mina from death once. You get it through the Duke route in Queensbury Crypt.

### Where is Proto Spark?

Proto Spark is in Queensbury Crypt. Take the left path in the red-carpet room before the Ancestral Chamber, spend two vials on the barrier, find the Duke and finish his escort route.

### Can I use Proto Spark before the first Queensbury Crypt boss?

No, unless you fully explore the crypt side route before triggering the boss. The Duke escort pays off after the boss room, so for most players Proto Spark becomes available on the way out of Queensbury Crypt, not before the first boss attempt.

### How do I equip more than one trinket?

You need Trinket Bags to increase how many trinkets Mina can equip. One early Trinket Bag comes from beating Maxi after Queensbury Crypt.

### What should I equip if I only have one trinket slot?

Before Proto Spark, use Primed Vial Pouch if healing is your problem or Steady Soles if terrain is the problem. After Proto Spark, use Proto Spark for dangerous routes.

### Is Primed Vial Pouch worth it?

Yes. Primed Vial Pouch is one of the best early survival trinkets because it gives Mina 2 extra Plasma Vials and makes vial use restore some health.

### How do I get Primed Vial Pouch?

From the main gate of Ossex, head left, get past the two beam guards, then go down and right. Burrow into the marked roof spot, enter the building below and take the bottom-left stairs to the reward.

### What does Steady Soles do?

Steady Soles helps with movement on difficult terrain like water, grass, stairs and ice, while also helping resist knockback.

### Is Bell of Grace worth 1,000 Bones?

Bell of Grace is worth it if deaths are costing you too many Bones. If you still need the Isle Map or core upgrades, buy those first.

### How do I get Bell of Grace?

Start at the station underside west of Ossex, meet Frey, stop him when he later tries to steal your Bones, then return and pay 1,000 Bones to receive Bell of Grace.

### Is Chain Capacitor good?

Chain Capacitor is good if you can land consecutive hits without missing. It is weaker if you are still learning enemy movement and whiff often.

### How do I beat the Chain Capacitor car challenge?

Use the weapon you control best, stay close to the target and come back after an Attack upgrade if the timer feels too strict.

### Should I buy Plasma Funnel early?

Buy Plasma Funnel early if healing keeps getting interrupted. It lets Mina drink Plasma Vials faster and prevents losing vials when hit.

### Is Seismic Belt worth buying early?

Seismic Belt is optional. It is useful if you like burrow attacks, but most beginners should prioritize Proto Spark, Primed Vial Pouch or Plasma Funnel first.

### What trinkets should I look for after the early game?

After the early survival picks, watch for hazard and build trinkets such as Pit Preserver, Spike Spurs and Uranium Bracelet, then move into a full trinket locations guide.
