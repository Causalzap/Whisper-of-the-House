<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
The best starting weapon in **Mina the Hollower** for most beginners is **Nightstar**. Its range matters because Mina attacks in four directions, so being able to hit from safer spacing makes the first routes less punishing.

You can try all three starting weapons before committing, so test each one for a few swings before choosing. Pick **Whisper and Vesper** if you like fast close-range attacks, and pick **Blaststrike Maul** if you enjoy charge timing and can use its roll safely.

Do not restart just because you picked the wrong weapon. Once you reach **Ossex City Center**, you can buy other weapons at **Legovich's Arms** and upgrade weapons with Bones.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="best-starting-weapon">
  Best starting weapon for most players
</h2>

For a first playthrough, pick **Nightstar**. It is the safest starting weapon because it gives you reach, and reach matters more in Mina the Hollower than it does in many action games.

Mina moves and attacks in four directions, not with free diagonal attacks. That means enemies sitting at awkward angles can be annoying early on. Nightstar gives you more room to line up hits without standing directly on top of danger.

You can also try all three starting weapons before making your final choice. Do that. Swing each one a few times, move around, test the timing, then commit to the weapon that feels safest under pressure.

<h2 id="nightstar">
  Why Nightstar is the safest first pick
</h2>

**Nightstar** is a crushing mace fixed to a flexible chain. In player terms, it lets you hit from farther away than the other two starters.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/mina-the-hollower/mina-the-hollower-nightstar-description.webp"
    alt="Mina the Hollower Nightstar starting weapon description showing a crushing mace on a flexible chain."

    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Nightstar's chain range helps you attack without always standing inside enemy contact range.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Nightstar is good because it lets you:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>hit enemies before they stand directly on top of you,</li>
  <li>play more safely around pits, water, stairs and corners,</li>
  <li>line up four-direction attacks with more room for error,</li>
  <li>learn enemy movement without always trading damage,</li>
  <li>handle awkward spacing better than short-range weapons.</li>
</ul>

It is not the fastest weapon, and aggressive players may find it less exciting than daggers. But if your goal is to survive the first few hours without losing piles of Bones, Nightstar is the easiest recommendation.

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

The downside is reach. Some early enemies are annoying when you only have short-range attacks. If you keep getting hit while trying to close the gap, the daggers may make the early game feel harder than it needs to be.

<h2 id="wrong-weapon-and-early-decisions">
  What if you picked the wrong weapon?
</h2>

Do not restart just because your first weapon did not feel perfect. Starting weapon choice changes how the first stretch feels, but it does not lock your whole run.

After you reach **Ossex City Center**, visit **Legovich's Arms**. You can buy the other two starting weapons there, along with additional weapons that become part of the broader weapon set. Plan around **2,000 Bones** if you want to buy a replacement weapon early.

That does not mean you should immediately spend everything on a new weapon. First ask what the real problem is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If you are lost, buy the <strong>Isle Map</strong> first.</li>
  <li>If fights take too long, upgrade <strong>Attack</strong> or your current weapon.</li>
  <li>If you die too quickly, buy <strong>Defense</strong> or survival upgrades.</li>
  <li>If the weapon itself feels awful, then buy a replacement at Legovich's Arms.</li>
</ul>

Weapon upgrades also cost Bones. The first upgrade costs **2,000 Bones**, and later upgrades cost **2,500 Bones** each. Trying to upgrade a weapon a second time triggers the **Armand duel**, so do not treat upgrades like a quiet shop menu forever.

<h2 id="all-weapons-later">
  What other weapons can you get later?
</h2>

There are **five weapons total**. The three starting weapons are **Nightstar**, **Whisper and Vesper**, and **Blaststrike Maul**. Later, you can also unlock weapons such as **Battery Buster** and **Guardian Casket**.

That is why this page focuses on starting weapon choice, not the full weapon list. Your first choice matters for comfort, but it is not your only weapon decision for the whole game.

  <li>Buy the Isle Map from Drillhardt when available.</li>
  <li>Put early Bone Ups into Attack and Defense.</li>

This setup helps any starting weapon. Even the safest weapon feels bad if you are under-upgraded, lost and carrying too many loose Bones.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best starting weapon in Mina the Hollower?

The best starting weapon for most beginners is Nightstar because its range gives you safer spacing while you learn four-direction combat and enemy movement.

### Can you try weapons before choosing?

Yes. You can test all three starting weapons before committing. Swing each one a few times and choose the weapon that feels safest under pressure.

### Should I restart if I chose the wrong weapon?

No. You can buy other weapons later at Legovich's Arms in Ossex City Center, so do not delete your save just because your first weapon feels awkward.

### Where do you buy other weapons after choosing?

Go to Legovich's Arms in Ossex City Center. You can buy the other starting weapons there and later build toward the full weapon set.

### How much do weapons cost in Mina the Hollower?

Plan around 2,000 Bones for early weapon purchases at Legovich's Arms.

### How much do weapon upgrades cost?

The first weapon upgrade costs 2,000 Bones. Later upgrades cost 2,500 Bones each.

### What happens when you upgrade a weapon a second time?

Trying to upgrade a weapon a second time triggers the Armand duel. Be ready before committing to that upgrade.

### Can you change weapons later?

Yes. You are not locked into the starting weapon forever, so do not restart just because your first pick feels awkward.

### Why is Nightstar good for beginners?

Nightstar has better reach, which matters because Mina attacks in four directions. It gives you more space to line up attacks without standing directly in danger.

### Is Blaststrike Maul safer than it looks?

Yes, if you use it correctly. While charging, Mina can roll with invulnerability, but the Maul is still risky if you mistime the charge or roll into danger.

### What other weapons unlock later?

There are five weapons total. Beyond the three starters, later weapons include Battery Buster and Guardian Casket.

### Should I buy a new weapon early?

Only if your starting weapon feels bad. Otherwise, buy the Isle Map and invest in Attack, Defense and survival upgrades first.
