<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
The best early way to get Bones in Mina the Hollower is to **collect safe Bone Stones first**, then use the repeatable enemy route **east of Ossex on the way toward Queensbury Crypt** when you only need a small buffer.

For serious farming, upgrade into the **Elizabeth → Deboning Wand → Stolenoid → Mourner's Mile** setup. Deboning Wand makes enemies reward far more Bones, Stolenoid pulls the bouncing Bones toward you, and Mourner's Mile becomes a strong repeat route.

Do not grind with a huge pile of loose Bones in your pocket. Spend, protect or bank value before taking a risky route. Your early Bones should usually go toward:

**Isle Map → Attack / Defense → survival upgrades → Deboning Wand setup / train funding later**.

<h2 id="elizabeth-deboning-wand">
  Elizabeth quest and Deboning Wand
</h2>

The real farming upgrade is **Deboning Wand**. This trinket makes enemies reward far more Bones, but it also causes the Bones to bounce around. That drawback is why you eventually want **Stolenoid** with it.

You start the Deboning Wand route through **Elizabeth**.

caption="How to get Deboning Wand from Elizabeth."

/>

Think of the 1,000-Bone loan as a farming investment. It feels expensive if you are still buying the Isle Map and basic upgrades, but it becomes worthwhile once you are ready to farm larger amounts.

<h2 id="stolenoid-deboning-wand-build">
  Deboning Wand + Stolenoid farming build
</h2>

**Deboning Wand** creates more Bones, but **Stolenoid** makes the setup much easier to use. Deboning Wand's downside is that the extra Bones bounce around; Stolenoid pulls pickups toward Mina so you miss fewer drops.

<GuideTable
caption="Best Bones farming build pieces."

rows={deboningBuildRows}
/>

Get Stolenoid from **Belvedere** in **Queensbury Crypt / Statue Head Hall** for **600 Bones**. If you only have one trinket slot, Deboning Wand is the income piece. If you can equip both, Deboning Wand + Stolenoid becomes the cleaner farming setup.

After you have Deboning Wand and Stolenoid, move your farming to **Mourner's Mile**. The best loop runs through Mourner's Mile toward the Queensbury Crypt entrance, where the enemies are valuable enough to make the trinket setup shine.

Use this route when you need thousands of Bones for upgrades, weapons, train funding or expensive trinkets. A clean loop can take about **2 minutes** and earn around **1,400 Bones**. Repeating it for about **10 minutes** can bring in roughly **7,000 Bones** if your route stays consistent.

This is the point where Bones farming becomes a real strategy instead of a small emergency top-up.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you farm Bones fast in Mina the Hollower?

For small early needs, collect Bone Stones and use the enemy loop east of Ossex toward Queensbury Crypt. For serious farming, unlock Deboning Wand from Elizabeth, pair it with Stolenoid, then farm Mourner's Mile.

### What is the best early Bones farming route?

The best starter route is east of Ossex near the path toward Queensbury Crypt. It is close enough to a checkpoint that you can reset enemies without traveling far, but it is only meant for small top-ups.

### What is the best mid-game Bones farming route?

The best upgrade route is Mourner's Mile with Deboning Wand and Stolenoid equipped. A clean loop can earn around 1,400 Bones in about 2 minutes.

### How do I get Deboning Wand?

Find Elizabeth in the upper-right grass path of the Radiant Manor Foyer courtyard, accept her 1,000-Bone loan, repay the debt by farming enemies around Mourner's Mile, then return to receive Deboning Wand.

### What does Deboning Wand do?

Deboning Wand makes enemies reward far more Bones, but the Bones bounce around. That is why it works best with Stolenoid.

### How do I get Stolenoid?

Buy Stolenoid from Belvedere in Queensbury Crypt / Statue Head Hall for 600 Bones. It pulls nearby pickups toward Mina.

### Why use Stolenoid with Deboning Wand?

Deboning Wand creates more Bones but makes them bounce around. Stolenoid pulls pickups toward you, which helps you collect the extra Bones instead of leaving them behind.

### Should I farm Bones before buying the map?

If you do not have 500 Bones, yes. Farm or collect enough to buy the Isle Map early. The map saves enough confusion to be worth the cost.

### What should I spend Bones on first?

Buy the Isle Map first, then invest in Attack, Defense and survival upgrades. Avoid buying a new weapon immediately unless your starting weapon feels bad.

### What are Bone Stones?

Bone Stones are condensed Bones that cannot be lost the same way loose Bones can. They are separate from Bone Up upgrades and are mainly used to protect value.

### What is Bone Up?

Bone Up is the Underlab upgrade system where you spend Bones on stats or systems such as Attack, Defense, Sidearms and Bone Stones.

### Can you lose Bones when you die?

Loose Bones are risky when you die. That is why you should spend, protect or convert value before entering dangerous areas.

### Is Bell of Grace worth it?

Bell of Grace is worth it if deaths keep draining your Bones. It helps reduce loss, but it does not create income like Deboning Wand.

### Should I save Bones for the train?

Yes, but not before your basics. Buy the map and stabilize your build first, then start saving toward train funding. If the cost feels too high, use the Mourner's Mile farming setup.

### Should I keep farming if enemies feel too strong?

No. If the route feels too hard, return to Ossex, spend Bones on upgrades and come back later. Farming only helps if you can do it safely.
