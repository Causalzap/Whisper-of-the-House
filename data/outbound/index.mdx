<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**In Outbound, your first goal is to choose a practical van and turn it into a working mobile base, not a perfect house.**

If you are new, choose a roomy starting vehicle, finish the tutorial area, download **Basic Tools**, craft the **Wrench**, build the **Recycler**, turn **Litter** into **Download Vouchers**, and keep unlocking the systems that move you forward: **storage**, **energy**, **blueprints**, **tools**, **workstations**, **bridge repairs**, **vehicle upgrades**, and finally the **Mossrocks** that lead out of the first map.

<h2 id="choose-starting-van">
  Choose your starting van carefully
</h2>

Before the tutorial really begins, Outbound asks you to choose a starting vehicle.

This matters more than it first appears. A vehicle with more interior space is usually easier for new players because you need room for the Recycler, Sawmill, Sewing Table, Bolt Press, storage, food tools, and later power systems. If you are unsure, choose the roomiest option, such as **Big One**, instead of a more cramped vehicle like **Pathmaker**.

Pathmaker can feel better to drive, but interior space becomes a real limitation once you start adding workstations. For a first playthrough, storage and workstation layout are usually more valuable than handling.

<GuideCallout title="Starting van rule">
If you are not sure what to pick, choose space. A roomy van gives you more room to make mistakes while learning storage, crafting, and upgrades.
</GuideCallout>

**Drive until you find something useful, stop the van, gather nearby materials, unlock a blueprint, craft what you can, deposit materials, then move on.**

<h2 id="core-loop">
  The Outbound core loop
</h2>

Outbound is built around one loop:

1. **Explore by van.**
2. **Stop at campsites, towers, buildings, farms, bridges, and landmarks.**
3. **Gather nearby resources.**
4. **Deposit everything into your van.**
5. **Use blueprints to unlock tools, workstations, and upgrades.**
6. **Upgrade your van and tools to reach the next area.**

The important part is that your van is not just a vehicle. It is your base, storage, workbench, power system, bed, building platform, and upgrade hub.

When you are stuck, ask:

**What system am I missing?**

<h2 id="energy-basics">
  Energy basics
</h2>

Your van battery drains as you drive. Early on, wood is your best emergency fuel, and Fiber should be treated as backup because it matters for Sewing Table and gear upgrades.

Do not wait until the battery is empty. Use charging stations when you pass them, keep fuel in the van, watch your build weight, and upgrade battery, motor, slope, and weight capacity as your mobile base grows.

For charging station cooldowns, passive power while parked, solar vs wind, rainwater / hydro placement, van weight, and co-op power management, read the <Link href="/outbound/energy-guide">Outbound Energy Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="workstations-and-van-upgrades">
  Workstations and van upgrades
</h2>

Do not try to memorize every workstation from the Hub.

The important idea is simple: if a recipe asks for Planks, Bolts, Metal, backpack upgrades, vehicle upgrades, or roof building, you probably need the workstation or research station that processes that material first.

For the exact priority order, read the <Link href="/outbound/blueprints-workstations-upgrades">Blueprints, Workstations and Upgrades Guide</Link>.

If you want to build upward, expand storage, or plan a larger mobile base, prioritize the Base Building Deck, building space, and weight capacity after the core workstations are stable.

<h2 id="food-farming-and-health">
  Food, farming, and health
</h2>

Outbound is not a harsh survival game, but hunger still matters.

Roadberries are useful early, Ginger helps with health, and water becomes more important once farming and cooking systems open up. As soon as you can process food, do it: cooked or processed food is usually more efficient than carrying raw ingredients. For example, Roadberries become more useful once turned into better food items like jam.

Carry enough food for the current trip, but do not let food clog your backpack.

<h2 id="co-op-crossplay">
  Co-op, crossplay, and platform notes
</h2>

Outbound supports online co-op for up to four players, but you have to clear the starting section first. After you repair the first gate with the Wrench, co-op becomes available.

Important notes:

- Co-op unlocks after the starting tutorial gate.
- The game supports up to four players online.
- Co-op progress is best treated as host-world progress unless your group has tested otherwise.
- Platform availability, Game Pass inclusion, and crossplay status can change, so check the latest store page or official update before planning a long co-op session.
- If you are playing with friends, assign jobs: driver, gatherer, tower runner, builder, and power monitor.

For most players, this Hub FAQ is enough. If co-op search volume grows later, it can become its own Spoke.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Outbound?

Choose a roomy starting van if you are unsure, finish the tutorial area, download Basic Tools from the first Signal Tower, craft the Wrench, repair the gate, gather basic resources, build the Recycler, and start turning Litter into Download Vouchers.

### Which starting van should I choose?

For a first playthrough, choose the roomiest vehicle you can. Interior space is valuable because your van quickly fills with workstations, storage, power systems, and building pieces.

### How do I unlock more blueprints?

Use Signal Towers. The first one is free, but later towers usually need Download Vouchers. Build the Recycler and process Litter to get more vouchers.

### Why is my backpack full so quickly?

Your backpack is only for the current trip. Deposit materials into the van often, lock important items like Download Vouchers, and read the <Link href="/outbound/storage-backpack-guide">Storage Guide</Link> if you cannot find the deposit panel or retrieve items.

### Why does my van battery keep running out?

Your van uses battery power while traveling. Early on, feed wood into the Power Unit, use charging stations when you find them, watch van weight, and upgrade battery / motor systems. The <Link href="/outbound/energy-guide">Energy Guide</Link> covers the full setup.

### How do I leave the first biome?

You need to repair bridges, lower the drawbridge from the far side, upgrade Motor I, craft Pickaxe II, and break the Mossrocks that block the tunnel. Use the <Link href="/outbound/leave-first-biome">First Biome Progression Guide</Link> for the full route.

### Where do I get a dog?

After leaving the first map, reach the coast and visit Paws & Whiskers Lodge. The dog pouch helps with resource runs, so it is worth doing early.

### Is Outbound co-op?

Yes. Outbound supports online co-op for up to four players after the starting tutorial gate.

### Does Outbound have crossplay?

Crossplay and platform compatibility can change over time. Check the latest official store page or update notes before planning a long session across platforms.

### Is Outbound on Game Pass?

Availability can change over time. Check the latest Xbox / Microsoft Store listing for current Game Pass status before relying on it.

### Is Outbound Steam Deck Verified?

Outbound has been listed as Steam Deck Verified during launch coverage, but verification and performance recommendations can change. Check the current Steam page if you are reading this later.

### How dangerous is Outbound?

Outbound is not a combat-focused or punishing survival game. Hunger, fall damage, heat, and hazards can still cause problems, but the main challenge comes from exploration, resources, energy, storage, blueprints, tools, vehicle upgrades, and biome progression.
