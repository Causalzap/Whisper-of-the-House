<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best early resource route in Outbound is to loop between campsites, Signal Towers, bridges, and mining-style POIs while constantly depositing materials into your van.**

Prioritize **Scrap Metal**, **Everwood**, **Litter**, **Fiber**, **Roadberries**, **Ginger**, and **Water** in the first map. Scrap Metal is especially important because one Metal Scrap pickup gives **3 Scrap Metal**, and tower-side Scrap Metal can respawn after time passes or after sleeping. For bridge progression, remember that the **Sawmill costs 10 Everwood + 2 Scrap Metal**, and **2 Everwood makes 1 Everwood Plank**.

Once you reach later areas, protect new biome materials such as **Silverbark**, **Redwood**, and **Clay** before burning or crafting with them.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="best-early-resource-route">
  Best early resource route in Outbound
</h2>

Outbound does not ask you to memorize a perfect farming map in the first hour. It asks you to build a habit:

**Stop often, grab everything near the route, deposit it into the van, then keep moving.**

The best early loop is:

1. Drive toward a campsite, tower, landmark, bridge, or question-mark POI.
2. Stop the van close enough that you can return quickly.
3. Collect Scrap Metal, Everwood, Fiber, Litter, food, and water.
4. Deposit materials into van storage.
5. Recycle Litter into Download Vouchers.
6. Use vouchers at Signal Towers.
7. Come back with the right tool or workstation when a resource is gated.

<h2 id="storage-panel-build-mode">
  Storage panel and Build Mode basics
</h2>

New players often lose time because the storage and build controls are easy to miss.

On keyboard, press **B** to enter Build Mode. On controller, use the right D-pad input. This is how you place counters, workstations, and van-building parts.

The storage / deposit panel is near the van, around the driver-side rear wheel area. It can blend into the vehicle because it uses similar colors, so if you cannot find it, walk near the rear wheel and look down / scan the side of the van.

Use this habit:

- enter Build Mode to place counters and workstations,
- use the storage panel whenever you return,
- lock Download Vouchers before depositing,
- start resource runs with an empty backpack.

Read the <Link href="/outbound/storage-backpack-guide">Storage and Backpack Guide</Link> if inventory is slowing you down.

<h2 id="scrap-metal">
  Where to find Scrap Metal
</h2>

Scrap Metal is the resource you will underestimate first.

You need it for tools, workstations, Bolts, Vehicle Research, motor upgrades, and repair-style progression. The important detail is that one Metal Scrap pickup gives **3 Scrap Metal**, so dense metal spots are worth stopping for even when your backpack is getting close to full.

The best early places to check are:

- around Signal Towers,
- supply boxes,
- roadside junk,
- abandoned points of interest,
- mining areas once you reach them.

Scrap Metal around Signal Towers can return after time passes or after you sleep. If you are short, do not drive randomly forever. Revisit tower routes, check the ground around them, and use the trip to collect Litter and food at the same time.

Fiber comes from bushes.

Small bushes can be harvested early. Larger bushes may require the **Sickle**, so if the game blocks you, do not keep clicking the bush. You need the right tool.

Fiber matters because it connects to:

- Homing Boots,
- early crafting,
- Sewing Table upgrades,
- backpack and clothing paths,
- emergency battery charging.

Fiber can be burned in the Power Unit, but it is usually better as an emergency fuel than a main fuel. Once Sewing Table or gear upgrades appear, Fiber becomes too useful to waste casually.

Everwood is one of the most important first-map materials.

You can find it from:

- supply boxes,
- loose logs near roads,
- campsites,
- wooded paths,
- larger wood sources after you unlock the Axe.

Everwood is used for basic crafting, campfires, Sawmill processing, planks, building, and early energy emergencies.

The key conversion is:

**2 Everwood → 1 Everwood Plank**

That means a bridge asking for 30 Everwood Planks is really asking for 60 Everwood before any other processing costs. Do not spend all your early wood on decoration before bridge progression is handled.

Litter looks like trash because it is trash, but mechanically it is one of the most valuable early resources.

The chain is:

**Litter → Recycler → Download Vouchers → Signal Tower blueprints**

That means every piece of Litter helps unlock more workstations, tools, decorations, building pieces, and upgrades.

Your early habit should be:

1. Pick up Litter at every stop.
2. Deposit normal materials into the van.
3. Put Litter into the Recycler.
4. Take the Download Vouchers after processing.
5. Lock vouchers in your backpack before visiting towers.

<h2 id="mining-coarse-ore">
  Mining areas and Coarse Ore
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

Coarse Ore is one of the easiest early materials to miss because it is not spread evenly across the first map.

If a recipe asks for Coarse Ore, do not waste time checking every normal rock. Look for a **mining-style pocket**, **ore area**, or **metal-heavy landmark**. These spots are also useful when you need Scrap Metal, smelting materials, Bolts, Vehicle Research parts, or heavier workstation chains.

Check mining areas when you start seeing:

- Metal Smelter recipes,
- Coarse Ore requirements,
- ore processing,
- heavier workstation chains,
- vehicle or tool upgrade materials,
- mid-progression crafting blockers.

If you have not found the mining area yet, keep exploring question-mark POIs and metal-heavy routes instead of assuming Coarse Ore should spawn beside every stone.

Clay is not a first-hour resource. It becomes relevant later, around the canyon biome.

The important thing is not to look for it too early. If a recipe asks for Clay and you have not reached the canyon-style area yet, keep progressing.

Once you do find Clay, collect more than the exact amount you need. Later crafting chains can ask for a lot of it, and backtracking for one missing material is one of the slowest parts of Outbound.

<h2 id="best-storage-habits">
  Best storage habits for resource farming
</h2>

Resource farming gets annoying when your backpack is full, your stamina is low, and your van is far away.

Use these habits:

1. **Park close to the resource area.**
2. **Deposit before leaving the van.**
3. **Lock Download Vouchers.**
4. **Keep one or two food items on you.**
5. **Gather in short loops.**
6. **Return before the backpack is full if the van is close.**
7. **Use the dog pouch later when available.**
8. **Do not carry heavy stacks of food, water, or decorations during resource runs.**

The van is your real storage. Your backpack is just the bag you use between the van and the resource node.

Read the <Link href="/outbound/storage-backpack-guide">Storage and Backpack Guide</Link> if inventory is slowing you down.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Where do I find Scrap Metal in Outbound?

Check around Signal Towers, roadside junk, supply boxes, abandoned stops, and mining areas. One Metal Scrap pickup gives 3 Scrap Metal, and tower-side Scrap Metal can respawn after time passes or after sleeping.

### Where do I find Fiber?

Fiber comes from bushes. Small bushes can be gathered early, while larger bushes may require the Sickle.

### Where do I find Everwood?

Everwood comes from loose logs, supply boxes, campsites, wooded paths, and larger wood sources once you unlock the Axe.

### How do I get Everwood Planks?

Build the Sawmill and process Everwood into Everwood Planks. The Sawmill costs 10 Everwood and 2 Scrap Metal, and 2 Everwood makes 1 Everwood Plank.

### Where do I find Coarse Ore?

Check the mining-style ore pocket or metal-heavy landmarks. Coarse Ore is not spread evenly across normal roadside rocks, so explore mining POIs if smelting recipes are blocking you.

### Where do I find Redwood?

Redwood appears after you leave the first map and reach the coast / second map area. It becomes important for later tools, campfires, and biome progression.

### Where do I find Clay?

Clay appears later, around the canyon biome. It is not something you should expect to farm in the first map.

### What is Litter used for?

Litter becomes Download Vouchers through the Recycler. See <Link href="#litter-download-vouchers">Litter and Download Vouchers</Link>.

### Do resources respawn?

Some resources around towers and common pickup areas can return over time. Scrap Metal around Signal Towers is especially worth rechecking when you are short.

### What should I burn for power?

Use common Everwood if you need emergency charge, use Fiber only as a backup, and avoid burning Silverbark or Redwood until you know your next recipe costs.
