<h2 id="best-blueprints-first">
  Best blueprints to unlock first in Outbound
</h2>

Outbound looks like a cozy van-building game, but progression is controlled by blueprints.

A blueprint is not just a recipe. It is usually the key to a new system: tools, workstations, vehicle upgrades, gear upgrades, power, farming, building, or decoration.

For a smooth early game, prioritize blueprints that unlock more progression before spending too much on cosmetic or comfort items.

<h2 id="signal-towers-reactivate">
  Signal Towers reactivate, so do not panic over blueprint choices
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/outbound/outbound-blueprints-signal-tower-reactivates.webp"
    alt="Outbound Signal Towers reactivate and cycle blueprint options so players do not permanently miss key unlocks."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    A tower that goes dark is not necessarily useless forever. Towers can reactivate later with more blueprint options.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

One of the most important things the game does not explain clearly enough is that Signal Towers can reactivate and cycle new blueprint options.

That means your first blueprint choice is not always a permanent disaster. If you pick Axe before Pickaxe, or food processing before sewing, you may have to route differently for a while, but you are usually not locked out forever.

There is also some randomness in how blueprint options appear. You may not see the exact same tower choices in the exact same order as another player. If your friend found Vehicle Research Station, Sewing Table, or Base Building Deck at a different tower, do not assume your save is broken.

Use this habit instead:

- revisit towers after exploring elsewhere,
- watch for new radio signals,
- keep Download Vouchers ready,
- check question-mark points of interest on the map,
- explore towers fully if they ask for local keys or items,
- do not build your whole plan around one fixed tower route.

<GuideCallout title="Signal Tower rule">
If the blueprint you want is not available yet, keep exploring towers and unknown POIs. Outbound often wants you to widen your route, not restart your save.
</GuideCallout>

  Download Vouchers and the Recycler recipe
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    The Recycler turns Litter into Download Vouchers, which makes trash one of the most important early resources.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Download Vouchers are the currency you use at many Signal Towers.

The early loop is:

**Pick up Litter → Build Recycler → Process Litter → Take Download Vouchers → Unlock blueprints at Signal Towers**

To build the Recycler early, place a counter inside your van and craft the Recycler on top of it. The early recipe is:

**Recycler = 2 Everwood + 1 Scrap Metal**

That is why your first resource stops should not ignore loose wood, Scrap Metal, or Litter.

  Lock your Download Vouchers before depositing
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/outbound/outbound-blueprints-lock-download-vouchers.webp"
    alt="Outbound how to lock Download Vouchers before depositing materials at the van storage panel."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Lock vouchers before using the deposit panel, or you may leave your blueprint currency behind in van storage.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

This is one of the easiest mistakes to make.

The van deposit panel is useful because it dumps materials into storage fast. But if you deposit everything without thinking, you can accidentally store your vouchers too.

Before a tower run:

1. Open your backpack.
2. Select your Download Vouchers.
3. Lock them.
4. Deposit everything else.
5. Walk to the tower with vouchers still on you.

This small habit saves a lot of backtracking.

If you find a broken bridge asking for Everwood Planks, gathering more loose Everwood is only half the solution. You need the Sawmill to process it.

<h2 id="player-upgrades">
  Sewing Table and Gear Research: backpack, stamina, and clothing upgrades
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/outbound/outbound-blueprints-sewing-table-backpack-upgrades.webp"
    alt="Outbound Sewing Table and Gear Research guide for backpack upgrade stamina upgrade and clothing upgrades."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Player upgrades matter because backpack space, stamina, and clothing upgrades make every resource route smoother.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Do not treat Sewing as a cosmetic blueprint.

The **Sewing Table** and **Gear Research Station** both help solve the same player-side problem: exploration friction. If you are constantly overencumbered, low on stamina, or walking back to the van too often, this upgrade path matters as much as a new vehicle part.

Think of them like this:

- **Sewing Table:** the crafting side of backpack and clothing upgrades.
- **Gear Research Station:** the research side of backpack, stamina, and health improvements.
- **Sickle:** the tool that makes Fiber gathering easier once larger Fiber bushes start blocking you.
- **Fiber:** the material that connects plant gathering to backpack and clothing progression.

This is why the Sickle is more important than it first appears. A player who skips Sickle and Sewing may still progress, but every resource run will feel slower for longer.

Prioritize this path when:

- your backpack fills before every useful trip ends,
- you cannot sprint often enough,
- larger Fiber bushes are blocking material collection,
- clothing or backpack recipes start asking for Fiber,
- you are spending more time walking back to the van than exploring.

If your main frustration is inventory, do not only look for van upgrades. Look for Sewing, Gear Research, Fiber, and backpack upgrades too.

Read the <Link href="/outbound/storage-backpack-guide">Storage and Backpack Guide</Link> if backpack space is your main blocker.

The Base Building Deck is one of the most important building blueprints because it lets you build upward on top of your van.

Without it, your van can feel cramped quickly. You are trying to fit the Recycler, Sawmill, Sewing Table, Bolt Press, power production, food tools, storage-friendly layouts, and decoration into a small space. The deck gives you a second layer to work with.

A common early route is to repair the bridge near the Fire Lookout path, reach the Treehut / upper building area, and look for the blueprint there. Because blueprint availability can vary, treat this as a high-priority POI route rather than a guaranteed fixed unlock.

Once you have the Base Building Deck, think of your van in zones:

- **Interior:** core workbench access, quick deposit, storage habits, essential machines.
- **Roof deck:** Sawmill, Bolt Press, Sewing Table, power tools, and bulky workstations.
- **Outdoor / upper area:** farming, turbines, water systems, decorative space, and future expansion.
- **Weight-limited sections:** anything heavy should wait until you research more vehicle weight or building capacity.

The deck is not just for making the van look cooler. It reduces workstation clutter and makes the whole mobile-base loop smoother.

This is where Outbound starts to feel less like “collect everything” and more like “process the right material.”

The best choice depends on your blocker:

- **Bolt Press** if motor upgrades, vehicle upgrades, or bolt recipes are blocking you.
- **Metal Smelter** if recipes start asking for Coarse Ore or smelted metal parts.
- **Nailing Station** if building and construction chains are your focus.

If you are trying to climb a steep slope, the Bolt Press usually becomes more important because motor upgrades often need Bolts.

<h2 id="vehicle-research">
  Vehicle Research Station: motor, battery, weight, and building space
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/outbound/outbound-blueprints-vehicle-research-station.webp"
    alt="Outbound Vehicle Research Station guide for motor upgrade battery capacity weight and building space."
    width={1600}

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Vehicle Research turns the van from a starter camper into a real progression machine.
  </figcaption>

The Vehicle Research Station is one of the most important mid-early unlocks.

It opens upgrades for:

- motor,
- slope handling,
- battery capacity,
- vehicle weight,
- building space,
- outdoor building,
- base expansion.

This matters because some routes are not blocked by your character. They are blocked by your van.

If a steep slope stops you, you do not need to drive better. You need vehicle research, processed parts, and a motor upgrade.

Do not expect the first biome to support every van upgrade. The early map mainly gives enough materials for the first stage of vehicle progression. Faster travel, larger builds, better power systems, and more furniture / workstation routes often depend on materials and blueprints from later maps and biomes.

The short version is:

**Scrap Metal → Bolt Press → Bolts → Vehicle Research → Motor Upgrade**

Once you understand this chain, a lot of Outbound becomes easier. When the game blocks you, it is usually not hiding one missing item. It is asking whether you built the whole chain.

<h2 id="co-op-blueprints">
  Co-op blueprint and save progress notes
</h2>

Co-op makes blueprint choices feel more important because one player's unlocks can help the whole group inside the host world.

The important warning is that co-op progression is tied to the save you are playing in. If you join someone else's game, you are helping progress that host world. Do not expect the host's repaired bridges, van upgrades, workstation layout, or blueprint progress to appear in your own solo save afterward.

  caption="Outbound co-op blueprint and progression notes."

<GuideCallout title="Co-op rule">
Before a long co-op session, decide whose world is the main save. Blueprint and van progress is most useful when the same host keeps running the shared world.
</GuideCallout>

### What are the best blueprints to unlock first in Outbound?

Prioritize Basic Tools, Recycler, Axe / Pickaxe / Sickle, Sawmill, Sewing Table, Base Building Deck, Bolt Press, Vehicle Research Station, and Gear Research Station.

### Can I permanently miss a blueprint?

Usually, no. Signal Towers can reactivate and cycle new blueprint options later. You may need to revisit towers, follow new radio signals, or explore question-mark POIs.

### Are blueprints random in Outbound?

Blueprint availability can vary by save and route. If another player found a blueprint at a different tower, keep checking Signal Towers and question-mark POIs instead of assuming your game is broken.

### How do I build the Recycler?

Build a counter inside your van, then place the Recycler on it. The early Recycler recipe is 2 Everwood and 1 Scrap Metal. Use it to turn Litter into Download Vouchers.

### What does the Sewing Table do?

The Sewing Table supports backpack and clothing upgrade paths. It connects Fiber gathering to better carry capacity, stamina, and health-style gear progression.

### How do I build on top of the van?

Look for the Base Building Deck blueprint. After crafting it, you can start placing roof floors and expanding upward, though weight and building-space upgrades still matter.

### Where is the Base Building Deck?

A common early route is around the Treehut / Fire Lookout side of the first map after repairing bridge access. Because blueprint availability can vary, check nearby POIs and towers if it is not exactly where another player found it.

### Why am I missing materials even after building Vehicle Research?

Some vehicle upgrades are designed for later progression. The first biome mainly supports early van upgrades; higher-tier materials and more advanced recipes often require later maps and biomes.

### How do blueprints work in co-op?

Blueprints, recipes, bridges, van upgrades, and workstation progress are tied to the host save. They help the group while playing in that world, but they should not be expected to transfer back to each guest's own solo save.
