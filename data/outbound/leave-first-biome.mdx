<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**To leave the first biome in Outbound, you need to follow the early unlock chain: build the Recycler, make Download Vouchers, unlock tools and workstations from Signal Towers, build the Sawmill for Everwood Planks, repair the first two broken bridges, lower the drawbridge from the far-side control room, upgrade to Motor I for the east-side slope, upgrade to Pickaxe II, then break the Mossrocks and drive through the tunnel.**

If you are stuck, you are probably missing one link in that chain: **vouchers, Sawmill, Planks, Bolt Press, Motor I, Pickaxe II, or the drawbridge switch**. The Pickaxe II details are covered in the Pickaxe II section below.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="how-to-leave-first-biome">
  How to leave the first biome in Outbound
</h2>

Leaving the first biome is not one single quest. It is a chain of small unlocks.

The game teaches you to drive, gather, craft, and decorate, but the real progression path is about solving a few blockers in order:

<h2 id="step-1-start-the-blueprint-loop">
  Step 1: start the blueprint and voucher loop
</h2>

Before worrying about Mossrocks, bridges, or slopes, make sure your basic progression loop is working.

You need:

- the first Signal Tower,
- Basic Tools,
- the Wrench,
- the Recycler,
- Litter,
- Download Vouchers,
- at least one or two extra Signal Towers.

The first Signal Tower gives you Basic Tools for free. After that, towers usually need vouchers. That means Litter is not junk. It is your blueprint currency once you process it through the Recycler.

Before you leave the van on foot, take vouchers with you and lock them in your backpack. Otherwise, you can accidentally deposit them into van storage and reach a tower with no voucher.

Read the full unlock order in the <Link href="/outbound/blueprints-workstations-upgrades">Outbound Blueprints and Workstations Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="step-2-get-sawmill-planks">
  Step 2: unlock the Sawmill and make Everwood Planks
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/outbound/outbound-first-biome-sawmill-planks.webp"
    alt="Outbound Sawmill guide showing how to make Everwood Planks for bridge repairs."

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    The Sawmill is the bridge-repair workstation. If you only have loose wood, you are not ready yet.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

One of the first real blockers is a broken bridge asking for **Everwood Planks**.

This is where many players get stuck because they keep collecting Everwood but never unlock the workstation that turns it into planks.

The fix:

1. Collect Litter.
2. Recycle it into Download Vouchers.
3. Visit more Signal Towers.
4. Download the Sawmill blueprint when available.
5. Build the Sawmill on your van.
6. Turn Everwood into Everwood Planks.
7. Bring the planks back to the bridge.

The key conversion is:

**2 Everwood → 1 Everwood Plank**

Do not spend all your Everwood on decorations too early. Bridges, campfires, workstations, and base pieces can drain your wood supply fast.

<h2 id="step-3-repair-bridges">
  Step 3: repair the broken bridges
</h2>

Broken bridges are not just optional map dressing. Some of them are part of the route that gets you around other blockers.

There are three important broken bridges to plan around in the first map:

<GuideTable
  caption="First biome broken bridge requirements in Outbound."
  columns={bridgeColumns}
  rows={bridgeRows}
/>

The basic bridge loop is:

**Everwood → Sawmill → Everwood Planks → Bridge Repair → New route**

The first two bridge repairs are manageable if you bring enough Planks. The third one is the trap: it is much more expensive and sits behind the east-side steep slope route, so you need **Motor I** before that bridge becomes realistic.

The drawbridge is a classic early-game trap.

You find it, walk up to it, and expect a simple interact button. But if you are on the wrong side, you are not supposed to lower it yet.

The actual solution is:

1. Leave the drawbridge for now.
2. Find another route around, usually by repairing bridges and exploring the far side.
3. Once you reach the opposite side, look for a small control room on the right side of the bridge.
4. Go inside and flip the switch.
5. The drawbridge lowers and stays down, so you only need to do this once.

Do not waste time trying to force the drawbridge from the front. It is a permanent shortcut unlock after you reach the correct side.

  Step 5: unlock Pickaxe II for Mossrocks

The first biome quietly teaches you that tools are progression keys.

You will see:

- large logs that need an Axe,
- big bushes that need a Sickle,
- rocks or blocked paths that need a Pickaxe,
- Mossrocks that need Pickaxe II.

Pickaxe II is the important one for leaving the first biome, but it is not just a simple “get more wood” upgrade. It can require a longer chain involving **Reinforced Planks**, **Metal**, **Nailing Station**, and **Metal Smelter**.

If Pickaxe II is blocking you, the missing piece is often not the Pickaxe blueprint itself. It is usually one of the processing steps behind it.

Check mining-style POIs when you need:

- Coarse Ore,
- Rock,
- Scrap Metal,
- Metal Smelter materials,
- Metal,
- Reinforced Planks,
- Nailing Station materials.

If you are driving random loops and still cannot craft Pickaxe II, stop looking only at tool recipes and start checking whether you have the **Metal Smelter** and **Nailing Station** chain ready.

For a wider material route, use the <Link href="/outbound/early-resources">Outbound Early Resource Guide</Link>.

</GuideCallout>

Some roads are blocked by your van, not your character.

If you reach a steep slope and the van cannot climb it, you need a vehicle upgrade. The usual chain is:

**Vehicle Research Station → Bolt Press → Bolts → Motor I → Try the slope again**

Motor I can ask for **10 Bolts**, so this often depends on Scrap Metal and the Bolt Press.

This matters because the east-side route and the expensive third bridge are tied to slope progression. If you keep trying to drive up the hill without Motor I, you are solving the wrong problem.

Mossrocks are the clearest sign that you are close to leaving the first map.

If you cannot break them, you are not missing a hidden button. You need Pickaxe II and the processing chain behind it.

Once you can break the Mossrocks:

1. Clear the rocks.
2. Keep the materials you get.
3. Return to your van.
4. Drive into the tunnel.
5. Continue to the next map.

After you leave the first map, do not ignore **Paws & Whiskers Lodge**.

A dog companion is not just cosmetic. It can give you a **10-slot pouch** right away, which helps with resource runs, and later training can help with rare material hunting.

Your first priorities after reaching the next map should be:

- unlock the dog companion,
- start collecting new biome materials such as Redwood,
- deposit before long scouting trips,
- check new towers and POIs,
- protect new resource types until you know their recipes.

<h2 id="recommended-order">
  If you are lost, reset your route like this
</h2>

If you feel like you have been driving in circles, do not repeat the full progression route from memory. Reset your state first.

Use this shorter reset:

1. **Return to the van.**  
   Deposit materials, check battery, eat food, and lock Download Vouchers.

2. **Identify the actual blocker.**  
   Is it a bridge, drawbridge, steep slope, Mossrocks, missing Metal, missing Bolts, or missing Coarse Ore?

3. **Check the workstation, not just the resource.**  
   Planks need Sawmill. Bolts need Bolt Press. Metal needs Metal Smelter. Reinforced Planks need Nailing Station.

4. **Prepare the exact material count.**  
   Do not return to a bridge with “some wood.” Bring the Planks or parts the repair actually asks for.

5. **Pick one route objective.**  
   Do not try to repair bridges, find Coarse Ore, lower the drawbridge, and break Mossrocks all in the same overloaded trip.

6. **Move the van closer if possible.**  
   Bad parking creates half of the backtracking pain in the first map.

The key is not to solve everything at once. Solve the next missing link.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I leave the first biome in Outbound?

Follow the route in <Link href="#how-to-leave-first-biome">How to leave the first biome</Link>: unlock vouchers and tools, repair bridges, lower the drawbridge from the far side, upgrade Motor I, craft Pickaxe II, then break the Mossrocks.

### How many broken bridges are in the first map?

There are three important broken bridges. The first two each need 10 Everwood Planks. The later east-side bridge needs 30 Everwood Planks and sits behind a steep slope route.

### How do I fix broken bridges in Outbound?

Unlock the Sawmill, turn Everwood into Everwood Planks, and bring the required Planks to the bridge. Remember that 2 Everwood makes 1 Everwood Plank.

### How do I lower the drawbridge?

Reach the far side first, then look for the small control room on the right side of the drawbridge. Flip the switch inside. After that, the drawbridge stays lowered.

### Why can’t I break Mossrocks?

You need Pickaxe II. If you have the blueprint but cannot craft it, check the Pickaxe II material chain: Reinforced Planks, Metal, Nailing Station, Metal Smelter, Coarse Ore, and Rock.

### How do I make Reinforced Planks?

Craft Reinforced Planks at the Nailing Station using 2 Everwood Planks and 1 Metal.

### How do I get Metal?

Smelt Scrap Metal in the Metal Smelter. The Metal Smelter itself requires Coarse Ore and Rock to build.

### Why can’t my van climb the steep slope?

You need Motor I. Build Vehicle Research, craft Bolts through the Bolt Press, and upgrade the motor before trying the slope again.

### Where is the lift in the first map?

The east side of the first map has a lift / elevator-style structure that helps you reach the hilltop route. If you are circling below the hill, look for vertical routing instead of another road.

### What should I do after leaving the first map?

Visit Paws & Whiskers Lodge early, unlock a dog companion, and start collecting new biome materials such as Redwood. The dog’s 10-slot pouch is especially useful for resource runs.
