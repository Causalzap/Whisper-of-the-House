The four puzzles fail for different reasons:

- Music Rails requires one fixed sequence.
- Iris Lake requires controlled attention and torch movement.
- Carousel hides its real clue on the horses.
- Trippy Grid rewards sound tracking and avoidance rather than room clearing.

I move the locomotive close before entering Iris Lake or Trippy Grid. Music Rails and Carousel need less preparation, but I still remove nearby pressure before starting a long interaction.

<h2 id="music-rails-solution">
  Music Rails solution: 1, 2, 5, 4, 3
</h2>

Music Rails uses a fixed five-note sequence.

Stand in front of the hanging chimes and number them from left to right:

- far-left chime: 1
- second chime: 2
- center chime: 3
- fourth chime: 4
- far-right chime: 5

Press:

<strong>1 → 2 → 5 → 4 → 3</strong>

The height of each chime does not affect its number.

### My Music Rails route

1. Park close enough to retreat without crossing another encounter.
2. Remove the enemy pressure around the structure.
3. Stand where all five chimes remain visible.
4. Press the far-left chime.
5. Press the second chime.
6. Move to the far-right chime.
7. Press the fourth chime.
8. Finish on the center chime.

Do not start the sequence while another player is down or while an enemy is hitting the person entering the notes. An interrupted interaction can make the correct solution appear to have failed.

<h2 id="iris-lake-solution">
  Iris Lake solution: place all 6 torches
</h2>

Iris Lake requires **6 torches**, one for each empty holder at the cave entrance.

The location also states its main rule:

<strong>Eyes move toward you when you're not looking at them.</strong>

### Where to search for the torches

I use the cave entrance and the nearby shop as the two main landmarks.

From the cave, I sweep:

1. the ground immediately around the entrance;
2. the lake edge between the cave and the shop;
3. the outer rear edge of the location;
4. the side paths behind the first visible torch positions;
5. the darker tree and eye routes around the lake perimeter.

Several torches are visible from the main approach. The harder ones require moving around the edge rather than crossing the open center without watching the eye.

When I lose my orientation, I return to the cave or shop before beginning the next carry.

### Duo route: Watcher and Carrier

With two players, I assign fixed roles.

**Watcher**

- keeps the giant eye visible;
- calls when it starts moving;
- handles smaller threats when possible;
- stays close enough to revive the Carrier.

**Carrier**

- collects one torch at a time;
- travels along the outside edge;
- places the torch in an empty holder;
- regroups before starting the next trip.

Do not send both players toward separate torches. The apparent time saving disappears when the giant eye advances and neither player can safely revive the other.

### Solo route

Solo takes longer because the same player must carry and control the eye.

1. Move the locomotive as close as the track allows.
2. Count the six empty holders.
3. Choose one visible torch.
4. Follow the outside edge.
5. Turn toward the giant eye every few steps.
6. Drop the torch temporarily when combat becomes the immediate threat.
7. Return to the cave after every placement.

I ignore optional chests and loose gold until all six holders are filled.

### Can I put a torch down temporarily?

Yes.

I stage a torch when:

- the giant eye has moved too close;
- smaller enemies have blocked the route;
- a teammate needs help;
- or I need both hands free for combat.

Drop it beside a clear landmark rather than in grass or dark terrain.

<h2 id="carousel-solution">
  Carousel solution: match horse symbols to emotes

Carousel has two separate activities:

- the horse-symbol puzzle;
- an optional gambling wheel.

The horse symbols complete the location. Gambling does not.

### How to choose the correct emote

The horse shows a body pose rather than a written action name.

I solve each symbol like this:

1. Walk behind or beside the horse until the full icon is visible.
2. Memorize the arm position, leg position, and body lean.
3. Open the emote menu.
4. Compare the animation silhouettes.
5. Stand close to the horse.
6. Start the closest matching emote.
7. Allow the animation to continue long enough to register.
8. Check the central lights.

Do not rely only on the emote name. The pose is the more reliable clue.

One tested symbol resembled a hand-clapping action, while other horses were solved by matching their visible silhouettes.

### How do I know the dance was accepted?

Watch the lights in the middle of the Carousel.

Every accepted action fills another part of the central display.

When no light changes:

- move closer to the horse;
- verify that you are reading the correct symbol;
- choose the animation with the closest pose;
- let it play instead of cancelling immediately.

Once the lights are full, the puzzle completes and the reward area opens.

### Is gambling required?

No.

The betting wheel can consume gold or items but does not fill the central puzzle lights.

I finish the horse symbols and collect the main reward before deciding whether spare resources are worth risking.

<h3 id="trippy-grid-blue-key">
  Blue Key: open the hidden wall
</h3>

The Blue Key route uses a lever.

Search the blue section for an interactable lever. Pulling it opens a hidden wall rather than a normal door.

After using it:

1. turn around and inspect the nearby walls;
2. enter the newly revealed space;
3. follow the key sound inside;
4. collect the Blue Key.

The most common failure is pulling the lever and continuing forward without checking what changed behind or beside the player.

<h3 id="trippy-grid-green-key">
  Green Key: follow the metallic sound
</h3>

Enter the green section and listen for a repeating metallic, grinding, or chiming noise.

Use sound instead of trying to memorize every turn:

1. Stop at an intersection.
2. Rotate slowly.
3. Identify the direction where the key sound is loudest.
4. Move one corridor.
5. Stop and listen again.
6. Check nearby side rooms when the sound becomes strong.

The sharper repeating noise is the key cue. Do not confuse it with the general music of the location.

<h3 id="trippy-grid-red-key">
  Red Key: knock down the clown and check the floor
</h3>

Follow the sound into the red section.

A clown guards the route, so I:

1. throw rocks to distract or damage it;
2. temporarily knock it down;
3. check the floor immediately;
4. collect the Red Key;
5. take the nearby Aid Kit when needed;
6. leave before the clown recovers.

<h3 id="trippy-grid-yellow-key">
  Yellow Key: use the narrow safe rooms
</h3>

Enter the yellow section and follow the same grinding sound used for the Green Key.

The useful feature of this area is its small doors and side rooms. The clown cannot move through every narrow opening cleanly, so I use those spaces as listening points.

1. Enter a side room.
2. Wait for the clown to move away.
3. Listen for the key sound.
4. Leave through the safest doorway.
5. Move toward the louder sound.
6. Repeat until the Yellow Key is found.

Do not remain in the wide main corridor while trying to determine the direction.

<h3 id="trippy-grid-exit">
  How to leave Trippy Grid
</h3>

After collecting all four keys:

1. return toward the central route;
2. throw rocks away from the corridor you need;
3. wait for the clown to investigate the sound;
4. crouch before leaving the safe room;
5. move directly to the exit;
6. claim the final reward chest.

Completing Trippy Grid awards one global Warden Key. The four colored maze keys do not fill four Warden-key slots.

<h2 id="faq">
  Forest Escape Last Train puzzle FAQ
</h2>

### What is the Music Rails chime order?

Number the five chimes from left to right and press **1, 2, 5, 4, 3**. Ignore their height.

### How many torches are required at Iris Lake?

The tested route requires **6 torches**, one for each holder at the cave entrance.

### Is gambling required to complete Carousel?

No. Match the pose symbols on the horses with the corresponding emotes. The gambling wheel is optional.

### What keys are required in Trippy Grid?

You need the Blue, Green, Red, and Yellow Keys. Blue is behind a lever-opened hidden wall, Green and Yellow are found by following the key sound, and Red is collected from the guarded red section.

### Can Trippy Grid clowns be killed?

No. They can be disabled temporarily, but they recover. Use rocks, crouching, and narrow side rooms to move around them.
