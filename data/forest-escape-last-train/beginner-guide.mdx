<h2 id="starting-points">
  How the three starting points change your opening
</h2>

Forest Escape: Last Train has **three possible starting points**. The Early Access launch build also contains **13 locations and mini-games**, so two runs do not necessarily present their first branches in the same order.

The starting point can change:

- the station layout you search first;
- which side structures are closest;
- where the opening railway branches lead;
- which named location appears early;
- and the order in which shops, coal stops, and puzzle areas become practical.

It does not change the basic opening job.

At every starting station, I still:

1. search the full local area;
2. locate the three train parts;
3. collect rocks and coal;
4. identify the brake and direction controls;
5. inspect the first railway branch;
6. and leave only after the train is prepared.

This is why I describe search zones instead of promising one exact coordinate for every train part.

<h2 id="difficulty-choice">
  Which difficulty should you choose?
</h2>

The game offers **Easy, Normal, and Hard**.

### Choose Easy when learning solo

Easy is the sensible first choice when:

- playing alone;
- learning the three train controls;
- testing how far you can safely explore;
- learning puzzle rules without a coordinated team;
- or introducing players who are unfamiliar with co-op survival games.

The purpose of the first run is to understand fuel, movement, revives, and route decisions—not to prove that the hardest setting is possible.

### Choose Normal for a first co-op run

Normal is my default recommendation for a new group.

It leaves enough pressure for the train, enemies, and locations to matter without making every early mistake consume the entire recovery supply.

Normal is especially appropriate when:

- the group has not played together before;
- nobody has committed to the Engineer role;
- players are still learning the item and interaction prompts;
- or the team wants to explore instead of rushing the route.

### Choose Hard for a coordinated group

Hard makes more sense after the group already understands:

- who controls the locomotive;
- who carries coal and healing;
- how the team handles a downed player;
- when to stop exploring;
- and how to regroup before named locations.

One early three-player review reported completing the game on the highest difficulty. The final boss initially felt overwhelming to that group, but became much more manageable after several attempts.

That is the right expectation for a first Hard clear: treat the opening boss attempts as learning runs instead of spending every resource in panic during the first exchange.

### How to throw rocks

The current mouse interaction is straightforward:

1. Collect rocks from stone deposits.
2. Equip the rock stack.
3. Aim at the balloon, enemy, or distraction point.
4. Click the left mouse button.

The rock launches immediately. Holding the mouse button does not currently increase throw power.

For balloons, I aim slightly above the hanging item instead of directly at the object below it.

### Does the game support inverted mouse?

An earlier build did not show a mouse-inversion option. The developer later stated that inverted mouse support was added in the following build.

Check the current Controls menu before assuming the option is unavailable. When it is missing, update the game and restart before checking again.

### What to do when one part is missing

Search in expanding circles:

1. Walk around the entire station building.
2. Check both sides of the railway and locomotive.
3. Enter every accessible room.
4. Look above for balloons or elevated loot.
5. Mine enough rocks to reach target-based pickups.
6. Check the nearest treehouse or side structure.
7. Read item names before dropping anything.

The Control Lever is easy to mistake for an ordinary tool or piece of scrap. Do not leave because the Front Light and Horn are already attached.

**Rocks** are the flexible early resource. I use them to:

- pop balloons;
- distract enemies;
- soften early attackers;
- trigger distant objects;
- and save ammunition.

**Coal** keeps the route alive. It is less flexible but more important whenever the locomotive reserve is falling.

My decision rule is simple:

> When the train needs fuel, coal comes before gold, optional houses, and speculative loot.

Gold has no value if the locomotive cannot reach the next shop.

<h2 id="coal-temperature">
  How much coal does the train need?
</h2>

To add fuel:

1. Put coal in an active inventory slot.
2. Stand in front of the locomotive furnace.
3. Aim at the furnace opening.
4. Click the left mouse button.
5. Watch the temperature display increase.

I aim for **roughly 100°C** before leaving the first station. This gives me enough operating room to reach the first branch without immediately stopping for another mining trip.

### When I stop for coal

I stop when:

- the display is clearly falling;
- coal is visible close to the track;
- the next named location is still far away;
- or collecting it now is cheaper than reversing later.

I do not stop for every deposit. One player should remain aware of the locomotive, enemies, and retreat route while the other mines.

My stopping procedure is:

1. Call the stop before the scout jumps.
2. Apply the brake.
3. Confirm the train is no longer rolling.
4. Check the map and surroundings.
5. Leave one clear route back to the locomotive.

Before reversing, make sure no teammate is standing behind the train or trying to board a moving wagon.

### Why the train will not move

Check these in order:

1. The Control Lever is installed.
2. Coal remains in the furnace.
3. The brake is released.
4. Forward or reverse has been selected.
5. A stopper hand is not holding the locomotive.
6. The train has not entered the derailment bug.

### What to do when the train derails

The developer's current temporary workaround is to operate the forward and backward lever until the locomotive begins moving correctly on the track again.

The bug can be associated with an unstable connection. When lever switching does not help:

1. stop both players from interacting with the train at once;
2. check the internet connection;
3. allow the host to operate the controls;
4. restart from the last checkpoint when the train remains unusable.

<h2 id="one-revive-distance">
  Use the one-revive-distance rule
</h2>

My exploration limit is not a fixed number of meters.

It is this:

> If I cannot reach a downed teammate, complete the revive, and get both players back to the locomotive, we explored too far.

Move the train closer before exploring when:

- the next building is beyond a safe sprint back;
- several enemies are between the scout and the railway;
- the route disappears behind terrain;
- or the engineer could not safely reach a downed player.

The locomotive is transportation, storage, retreat point, horn platform, and the center of every recoverable route.

### Buy an Aid Kit when health is the immediate risk

The 20-gold Aid Kit is my first purchase when:

- one player is already low;
- the next stop is a named location;
- the team recently used a revive;
- or no food remains.

A powerful weapon does not solve a run where the next trap causes a down.

### Buy a Respawn Token before a dangerous commitment

The 40-gold Respawn Token is most valuable before:

- a long solo push;
- a dangerous puzzle location;
- a route carrying important progress;
- or a section where a teammate may not reach the revive marker.

It is insurance, not a replacement for healing.

### Buy ammunition only after the gun matters

Six Revolver rounds cost 20 gold.

I do not stock ammunition without a Revolver, and I do not spend 200 gold on the Revolver while fuel and healing are still unstable.

The gun becomes reasonable after:

- the train has spare coal;
- health is covered;
- the team expects a longer combat route;
- and the purchase will not remove the last recovery option.

### Buy Dynamite for a known problem

Dynamite costs only 20 gold, but cheap does not mean urgent.

I buy it when the route has already shown a blockage, dangerous target, or interaction where explosives have a clear use.

<h2 id="early-threats">
  Early enemies and train events
</h2>

### Flying enemies

Use the Train Horn to drive flying threats away from the locomotive.

Do not spend bullets on every flying attacker when the horn can reset the encounter safely.

### Stopper hands

Large hands can grab and stop the train.

When this happens:

1. return to the locomotive;
2. locate the hand holding it;
3. destroy the hand;
4. confirm the brake and movement controls;
5. continue only after the train responds.

Do not let the entire team disappear inside a distant building while the train is being held.

### Bear traps

Watch the ground around houses, forest paths, loot, and narrow approaches.

One trap can remove enough health to make the next normal enemy dangerous.

### Suspicious containers and Mimics

Open unusual containers only when:

- the locomotive is close;
- the team has enough health to fight;
- and the route back is clear.

Do not open suspicious loot while one player is already near death.

### Normal attackers

Start with rocks when possible, then spread out when the enemy reaches melee range.

Do not stand directly behind a teammate using a gun or swinging a melee weapon.

My revive order is:

1. Identify what caused the down.
2. Kill it or pull it away.
3. Check for a second threat.
4. Hold `E` at the teammate marker.
5. Give the revived player food or an Aid Kit.
6. Return to the locomotive before continuing.

The revive does not return the player at full health.

Starting the interaction while an enemy is still standing on the marker is one of the fastest ways to lose both players.

<h2 id="checkpoints-and-death">
  Checkpoints, team wipes, and saved progress
</h2>

When every active player dies, the current attempt ends and the run must be restored from a checkpoint.

After loading:

1. check the current location;
2. inspect both inventories;
3. confirm recent shop purchases;
4. recheck newspapers and Warden-key progress;
5. and confirm that important rewards collected shortly before the wipe remain.

The launch build has shown slow checkpoint loading and cases where a rejoining player lost carried items. Treat recent inventory changes as something to verify rather than assuming every item survived the reload.

The game supports Steam Cloud, but that is not the same as having unrestricted manual save slots.

Before ending an important session, I prefer to reach a clear checkpoint or route transition instead of quitting immediately after a major reward.

<h2 id="solo-and-coop">
  Solo, co-op, and offline play
</h2>

Forest Escape: Last Train supports **1–4 players**.

Solo play is available, but the current build requires an internet connection. Offline play is not currently supported, although the developer plans to add it later.

### My solo route

When playing alone:

- move the train closer before every search;
- use shorter resource loops;
- carry healing on the active character;
- consider a Respawn Token before a dangerous location;
- avoid suspicious loot while low;
- and leave optional gold behind when the fuel reserve is falling.

Nobody can reposition the train or complete a manual revive after a solo down.

### My co-op role split

With two players, I use two clear roles.

**Engineer**

- manages fuel;
- controls forward, reverse, and brake;
- keeps the locomotive within revive distance;
- operates the horn;
- and calls when the team must return.

**Scout**

- searches buildings;
- mines nearby resources;
- collects balloons and loot;
- and reports threats before the Engineer leaves the train.

Both players regroup for named locations, shops, major combat, and anything likely to contain important progression.

With three or four players, keep one designated Engineer. More players do not help when everybody abandons the train.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  First-run mistakes that end good attempts
</h2>

### Leaving before searching the full station

Missing the Control Lever, coal, or balloons creates unnecessary backtracking before the route has properly started.

### Treating the first fuel load as permanent

The opening furnace target gets the train moving. It does not remove the need to collect coal throughout the run.

### Spending every hammer immediately

Opening barricades consume tools quickly. Protect one exposed section and preserve the rest.

### Buying the Revolver before stabilizing the run

A 200-gold weapon does not repair low health, an empty furnace, or a team with no recovery option.

### Chasing loot beyond revive distance

Move the locomotive closer before committing to a distant house, hill, or resource group.

### Reviving before removing the threat

The revive takes long enough for the second player to be downed during the interaction.

### Letting every player leave the train

One player should remain responsible for fuel, movement, braking, and the return route.

### Starting on Hard without assigning roles

Hard is much less forgiving when nobody owns the Engineer role or the group has no plan for coal, healing, and revives.

<h2 id="faq">
  Forest Escape Last Train beginner FAQ
</h2>

### Which difficulty should a beginner choose?

Choose Easy for a first solo learning run. Choose Normal for a first co-op run or a group that has not assigned clear roles. Save Hard for a coordinated team that already understands fuel, revives, and train positioning.

### How many starting points are there?

There are three. The starting point changes the opening layout and early branch order, so train parts should be found by searching the full station rather than following one fixed coordinate list.

### Can Forest Escape Last Train be played solo?

Yes. It supports solo play and online co-op for up to four players.

### Can Forest Escape Last Train be played offline?

Not currently. Solo play still requires an internet connection. The developer plans to add offline support later.

### Does Forest Escape Last Train support inverted mouse?

An earlier build did not include the option, but the developer later said inverted mouse support had been added to the following build. Check the current Controls menu and update the game when the option is missing.

### How do I throw rocks?

Equip a rock, aim, and click the left mouse button. The current interaction releases the rock immediately rather than charging the throw.

### Where are the Front Light, Horn, and Control Lever?

Search the entire starting station, including rooms, containers, balloons, elevated loot, side structures, and both sides of the locomotive. Exact pickup positions can vary with the starting point.

### Does the train need exactly 100°C to move?

No exact minimum is confirmed here. This guide uses approximately 100°C as a comfortable opening fuel target.

### Why will my train not move?

Check the Control Lever, furnace fuel, brake, direction control, stopper hands, and possible derailment bug.

### What should I do when the train derails?

Operate the forward and backward lever to help the locomotive return to normal movement. When that fails, let the host control the train, check the connection, or reload the last checkpoint.

### How do checkpoints work after a team wipe?

Load the last checkpoint and immediately verify inventory, purchases, newspapers, keys, and recent rewards because the restored state may be earlier than the moment of death.

### Is the final boss difficult?

The first attempts can feel overwhelming, especially on Hard. A launch player group that completed the highest difficulty reported that the fight became much more manageable after learning its mechanics over several attempts.
