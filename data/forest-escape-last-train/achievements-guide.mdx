<h2 id="achievement-roadmap">
  My three-pass achievement roadmap
</h2>

### Pass 1: progression, locations, and collectibles

Use the first committed run for:

- named location completions;
- every newspaper you find;
- Warden-key rewards;
- railroad switches and train upgrades;
- and the final route after all six keys are collected.

I do not slow down a strong progression run by deliberately farming nine revives, hoarding 200 coal, or refusing every useful purchase.

### Pass 2: controlled co-op counters

Use the second run for:

- `To Hell and Back`;
- `Long Live the King`;
- `Cat tale`;
- `Shopaholic`;
- `Regulator`;
- `Untouchable`;
- `Why So Serious?`;
- and missing hat or mimic counters.

These are safer to plan near the train than inside Iris Lake, Trippy Grid, or another location where a failed revive can end the run.

### Pass 3: long-run and resource cleanup

Leave these for dedicated cleanup:

- `Ring`;
- `Massacre`;
- `Monster Hunter`;
- `Rock and Coal`;
- `We Rich!`;
- `I Fear No One`;
- `Mythbuster`;
- and `Home Run`.

`We Rich!` and `I Fear No One` can technically be completed in the same run by reaching 500 gold first and spending afterward. I still prefer separate attempts because they encourage opposite spending strategies.

<h2 id="trippy-grid-warden-keys">
  Trippy Grid keys and confirmed Warden-key rewards
</h2>

Forest Escape: Last Train uses two key systems that are easy to confuse.

The four keys inside **Trippy Grid** are local puzzle keys:

- Blue Key
- Green Key
- Red Key
- Yellow Key

They are not four of the six global Warden keys.

### Blue Key

In the blue section, pull the lever that opens a hidden wall. Enter the newly opened room and collect the Blue Key.

### Green Key

Move through the green section and listen for the repeating mechanical key sound. Follow it as it becomes louder until you reach the Green Key.

### Red Key

Enter the red section and follow the key sound. Disable or distract the clown blocking the route, then check the room and floor carefully for the Red Key.

### Yellow Key

Enter the yellow section and follow the same mechanical sound cue until you reach the Yellow Key.

Crouch while moving through exposed corridors. The Trippy Grid rules explain that clowns react to sound, cannot be permanently killed, and can be distracted by throwing rocks away from your route.

After collecting all four colored keys, return to the exit and claim the final location reward. That reward includes **one Warden key**.

### Confirmed Warden-key rewards

Two location rewards are confirmed:

- **Iris Lake:** place all six torches, open the cave, and loot the final reward area.
- **Trippy Grid:** collect all four internal colored keys, escape the maze, and claim the final reward.

After completing both locations, the train objective page showed the blue and green Warden-key slots filled.

The current sequence does not clearly show which of the two locations awarded which Warden-key color, so I track filled slots on the train instead of assigning an unproven color to either location.

### How I avoid missing a Warden key

After every major location, I:

1. wait for `MISSION COMPLETE`;
2. enter the final reward area;
3. open the reward chest;
4. check the floor around the chest;
5. return to the train;
6. confirm whether another Warden-key icon has filled.

`The End of the Beginning` unlocks after collecting all six Warden keys. Before opening the Boss Door, confirm that all six icons are filled on the train objective page.

<h2 id="ring-eight-locations">
  Ring achievement: track eight named locations
</h2>

The official game description lists **13 unique locations and mini-games**, while the achievement list gives ten clearly named location objectives:

1. Upper Village
2. Trippy Grid
3. Mystic Room
4. Iris Lake
5. Carousel
6. Music Rails
7. Stepfall Bridge
8. Acid Pit
9. Tower
10. Bootball Court

These are not necessarily all 13 locations. They are the ten locations that can currently be tied directly to a named completion achievement.

`Ring` requires only eight completed locations in one run.

I mark a location only after its named objective reaches `MISSION COMPLETE`. I do not count:

- the starting station;
- houses;
- treehouses;
- shops;
- mining stops;
- or ordinary railway chests.

Because the game has **three starting points**, branch order can change between runs. I complete the first eight named locations the current railway route gives me instead of trying to copy one fixed left-and-right sequence.

Keep a written checklist because the game does not show live `Ring` progress.

<h2 id="revive-achievements">
  Revive achievements without losing the run
</h2>

The three revive achievements track different actions:

- `To Hell and Back`: perform five teammate revives in one game.
- `Long Live the King`: revive the lobby host.
- `Cat tale`: be revived nine times in one game.

My controlled setup is:

1. Park beside a weak enemy or known trap.
2. Put food or Aid Kits on the train.
3. Confirm which player needs the counter.
4. Let only that player go down.
5. Remove the nearby threat.
6. Hold `E` to revive.
7. Heal before repeating.

A revived player does not return at full health. Repeating the down immediately is how a controlled farm becomes a full team wipe.

For `Long Live the King`, verify which player created the lobby. For `Cat tale`, keep the same player as the revive target for all nine attempts.

Use manual teammate revives for all three achievements.

The achievement triggered after the golem was killed with the pickaxe.

My route is:

- keep the train close;
- let one player hold a safer angle;
- weaken the golem without spending every recovery item;
- stop using guns or explosives when its health looks low;
- land the final hit with the pickaxe.

### Home Run: kill a flying enemy with a bat

The horn drives flying enemies away, which is useful during normal survival and counterproductive for `Home Run`.

Keep a bat ready, avoid using the horn, let the flying enemy reach the locomotive, and make sure the killing hit comes from the bat.

### Why So Serious?: stagger one clown ten times

Trippy Grid confirms that clowns can be disabled temporarily but cannot be permanently killed.

One clown is enough:

1. Choose one near a colored safe room.
2. Stagger it.
3. Retreat while it recovers.
4. Repeat until the achievement triggers.
5. Throw rocks away from your path when it needs to be repositioned.

### Spy Detected: kill three mimics

Open suspicious containers only when the train is close and the team is prepared for a fight. Manually count the three kills instead of assuming every unusual chest was a mimic.

### Monster Hunter: use a route-coverage approach

`Monster Hunter` requires every monster type to be killed in one game, but the game does not provide a live checklist.

The safest route is:

1. Use the designated player for finishing hits.
2. Complete at least eight named locations.
3. Do not skip Mimics, Golems, flying enemies, or location-specific encounters.
4. Combine the attempt with `Ring` and `Massacre`.
5. Continue exploring after 100 kills if `Monster Hunter` has not triggered.

If the achievement does not unlock, If the achievement does not unlock, check for skipped location-specific encounters before farming more copies of ordinary enemies.

### Rock and Coal: hold 200 of each

In co-op, assign one player as the hoarder.

That player keeps rocks and coal. The other player fuels the locomotive, throws combat rocks, and handles normal spending.

Do not start this counter during an unstable opening. The train still needs enough coal to reach the next locations and checkpoints.

### We Rich!: hold 500 gold

Once I commit to this achievement, I stop:

- gambling;
- buying cosmetics;
- buying spare ammunition;
- and purchasing anything that is not needed to keep the run alive.

Keep the gold on the designated player rather than splitting it across the team.

### Shopaholic: make five purchases in one game

The shop has enough useful low-cost items to avoid buying the 200-gold Revolver only for the counter.

A practical route uses combinations of:

- Aid Kits for 20 gold;
- ammunition packs for 20 gold;
- Dynamite for 20 gold;
- Respawn Tokens for 40 gold.

Make all five purchases during the same game.

### I Fear No One: exact ammunition cost

The shop sells **6 revolver rounds for 20 gold**. The achievement requires buying at least 100 rounds.

You can complete `We Rich!` and `I Fear No One` in the same run by reaching 500 gold first, waiting for `We Rich!` to unlock, and then spending 340 gold on ammunition.

I still prefer separate runs because one route rewards saving while the other requires repeated spending.

<h2 id="cleanup-order">
  Final achievement cleanup order
</h2>

After normal progression, I use this order:

1. Missing named locations.
2. Missing newspapers.
3. Missing Warden keys and `Last Train Home`.
4. Co-op revive achievements.
5. `Ring`, `Massacre`, and `Monster Hunter`.
6. `Rock and Coal`.
7. `We Rich!`.

<h2 id="faq">
  Forest Escape Last Train achievements FAQ
</h2>

### How many achievements are in Forest Escape Last Train?

There are 41 achievements in the Early Access launch list.

### Are the Steam completion rates live?

No. The percentages in this guide were captured on July 10, 2026 and will change as more players progress.

### Can Forest Escape Last Train be completed 100% solo?

No. The lobby-host revive achievement and the two revive-count achievements require another player.

### Are the four Trippy Grid keys Warden keys?

No. Blue, green, red, and yellow are local maze keys. Completing the maze then awards one global Warden key.

### How many locations are in Forest Escape Last Train?

The official description lists 13 unique locations and mini-games. Ten of them currently have clearly named location-completion achievements.

### Do the three starting points change the achievement route?

They change which branches and locations appear first, but they do not change the achievement requirements. Track completed location names instead of following one fixed turn sequence.

### How much gold is required for I Fear No One?

You need 17 ammunition packs. At 20 gold per pack, the minimum cost is 340 gold.

### Can We Rich and I Fear No One be completed in the same run?

Yes. Reach 500 gold first, unlock `We Rich!`, then spend 340 gold on ammunition. Separate runs are usually easier to manage.

### How do I unlock Mythbuster?

Kill a golem and make sure the finishing hit comes from the pickaxe.

### What is the safest Ring route?

Complete any eight named locations and keep a written checklist. Houses, shops, treehouses, and ordinary railway stops do not count.
