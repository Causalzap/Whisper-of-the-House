<h2 id="where-theta-9-starts">
  Where Research Outpost Theta 9 starts
</h2>

Research Outpost Theta 9 starts when you follow the old **emergency survivor flag** toward the underwater station.

Theta 9 is not just another sealed room. It is a deep-sea research outpost tied to **Roth’s Anomaly**, so the station is built around old-world containment, research, and the aftermath of whatever happened there. That is why the route feels different from a normal loot stop: the outpost has locked doors, enemy rooms, terminals, power checks, and signs that the facility was sealed for a reason.

Before I enter, I want three things handled:

1. My boat is close enough for a safe return.
2. My inventory has room for station loot and route items.
3. I have a recent save before pushing into the deeper rooms.

Do not enter Theta 9 like a normal ocean pickup. The route is long enough that bad preparation makes every backtrack worse.

---

<h2 id="what-to-bring">
  What to bring before diving
</h2>

Before I enter Theta 9, I bring:

* **Tide Ripper or strong underwater mobility** for the approach and return;
* **weapons and ammo** for spiders, flying bugs, and nests;
* **healing or emergency recovery** in case a room goes badly;
* **inventory space** for loot and route items;
* **battery / energy safety** so the return trip is not a problem;
* **a recent save** before the station interior.

<GuideCallout title="Prep rule">
If you are already overweight, low on energy, or missing underwater mobility, leave and prepare first. Theta 9 becomes much harder when every mistake turns into a slow swim back.
</GuideCallout>

Tide Ripper is not always strictly required, but it makes the route much more comfortable. I would bring it before doing Theta 9 seriously.

<h2 id="enter-the-outpost">
  How to enter the outpost
</h2>

When you reach the signal, first find the actual station entry. Do not burn time swimming in random loops around the structure.

Look for:

* an airlock-style entrance;
* a roof or upper structure access point;
* broken or open station paths;
* door prompts that name the outpost;
* powered doors or terminals;
* lower passages that look easy to miss from the surface.

<h2 id="clear-enemy-rooms">
  Clear nests, spiders, and flying bugs
</h2>

Theta 9 has enemy pressure inside the station. The threat is not one boss; it is small enemies interrupting you while you are trying to read prompts, find terminals, and remember the route.

The main problems are:

* **nests** that keep the area dangerous;
* **spiders** that stay active in tight rooms;
* **flying bugs** that distract you while searching;
* dark or narrow rooms where it is easy to miss a door.

<h2 id="door-lock-colors">
  Door lock colors: red, yellow, and green
</h2>

Theta 9 uses door lock colors to hint at what is blocking you.

The simple way I read them is:

* **Green lock:** the route is available, or the condition is already met.
* **Yellow lock:** the door is not fully open yet, but it is waiting on a condition such as power, authorization, or another nearby step.
* **Red lock:** the route is blocked harder, usually because the required condition is not met yet.

A yellow lock is the confusing one. I do not treat it like a bug right away. I check nearby power, terminals, side rooms, and authorization prompts first. If the door also shows **No Power** or names **Ventilation 3.3 Authorization**, the color is telling me to solve the missing condition before trying the door again.

This helps a lot in Theta 9 because the station often tells you what is wrong, but it does not always point directly to the room that fixes it.

<h2 id="ventilation-33">
  Ventilation 3.3 authorization required
</h2>

The main Theta 9 blocker is **Ventilation 3.3**.

When the door screen says something like **Research Outpost Theta-9 - Ventilation 3.3 Authorization required**, the door is telling you the next step: you need authorization first.

At this point, do not keep clicking the same locked door. Check:

* whether nearby enemies are cleared;
* whether you missed a side room;
* whether there is a lower or vent-like passage;
* whether a terminal prompt is nearby;
* whether the door also needs power before it can respond properly.

If the screen shows both power and authorization language, solve the route in order: stabilize access, find the terminal, then return to the door.

<h2 id="terminal-authorization">
  Find the PECO / Pico terminal and get authorization
</h2>

After the Ventilation 3.3 blocker appears, backtrack and search for the station authorization terminal. The exact screen text may look like a PECO / Pico-style terminal depending on the prompt you are reading, but the goal is the same: get **Outpost Theta authorization**, then return to Ventilation 3.3.

I search in this order:

1. Return to the last safe room.
2. Clear any remaining nests or enemies.
3. Sweep side rooms you skipped during combat.
4. Check behind nest rooms, tight corners, and lower passages.
5. Inspect terminals before assuming they are decoration.
6. Use the authorization terminal and confirm the authorization message appears.
7. Return to the same Ventilation 3.3 door.

If the door still does not open after authorization, check:

* Did you use the correct terminal?
* Did the authorization message actually appear?
* Are you back at the same Ventilation 3.3 door?
* Does the door still need power?
* Is the lock still yellow instead of green?
* Are enemies interrupting the interaction?
* Do you need to save and reload after a stuck prompt?

The key is not to keep forcing the door. Theta 9 usually wants you to read the lock state, solve the missing condition, then return.

<h2 id="after-ventilation-opens">
  After Ventilation 3.3 opens
</h2>

Once Ventilation 3.3 opens, slow down again. The main blocker is gone, but the route can still continue into deeper rooms with enemies, loot, and new prompts.

Before pushing forward, I do this:

1. Save.
2. Reload weapons.
3. Heal if needed.
4. Drop or move heavy loot if I am overweight.
5. Confirm the way back.
6. Clear enemies near the door.
7. Move into the next section carefully.

The biggest mistake is opening Ventilation 3.3 and immediately running deeper with low health, low ammo, and a full inventory.

<h2 id="loot-and-return">
  Looting and leaving safely
</h2>

Theta 9 is tempting to overloot early, but inventory weight can turn the return route into the real problem.

I loot in two passes:

1. **Route pass:** open the station path, clear enemies, get authorization, and unlock Ventilation 3.3.
2. **Cleanup pass:** collect heavier loot after the main blocker is solved and I know the way back.

This keeps the run safer. If I pick up everything before solving the door, I may end up overweight during the hardest part of the route.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Common Theta 9 mistakes
</h2>

* **Entering without mobility:** deep water makes every mistake slower.
* **Ignoring nests:** small enemies become route blockers if left alive.
* **Forcing Ventilation 3.3:** the door needs authorization, not repeated interaction.
* **Missing side rooms:** the terminal route is easy to skip if you only run between the main doors.
* **Overlooting too early:** being overweight makes combat and backtracking worse.
* **Forgetting to save:** this is a station route, not a quick pickup.
* **Leaving before authorization:** get Outpost Theta authorization before assuming the station is finished.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Where is Research Outpost Theta 9?

Theta 9 starts from the old emergency survivor signal in New Horizons. Follow the marker, then look for the underwater station entry or airlock route.

### What should I bring before entering Theta 9?

Bring Tide Ripper or underwater mobility, weapons, ammo, healing, inventory space, energy safety, and a recent save.

### Why is Ventilation 3.3 locked?

Ventilation 3.3 needs Outpost Theta authorization. Find the station terminal route first, then return to the door.

### What does “No Power” mean on the Ventilation door?

It usually means the door is not ready to open even if you are standing at the right place. Check the station route, nearby terminals, and power state before assuming the door is bugged.

### Where is the PECO / Pico terminal?

After you find the Ventilation 3.3 blocker, backtrack and sweep nearby rooms, side paths, and terminal prompts. Clear enemies first so you can read prompts safely.

### How do I open Ventilation 3.3?

Acquire Outpost Theta authorization from the correct terminal, then return to the Ventilation 3.3 door. If it still does not open, check power, the exact door, and whether the authorization message appeared.

### Should I loot everything before opening Ventilation 3.3?

No. Open the route first, then do a cleanup pass. Looting too early can make you overweight before the real blocker is solved.

### Do I need Tide Ripper?

Not always, but I strongly recommend it. Theta 9 is an underwater station route, and good mobility makes the approach, mistakes, and return much safer.
