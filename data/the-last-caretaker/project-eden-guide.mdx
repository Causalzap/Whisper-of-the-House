<h2 id="where-project-eden-starts">
  Where Project Eden starts
</h2>

Project Eden starts when the Eden route points you toward **Pishon**. Once that marker appears, the first goal is to reach the platform, place the first Eden Frame, and build the first Farming Platform so the route can actually progress.

I treat Pishon like a construction site, not a normal loot stop. The route needs heavy materials, clean placement, and support systems. If my boat is low on power or short on Stainless Steel, I scout the marker first and return when I can build properly.

The first route is:

1. Sail to the **Pishon** marker.
2. Find the platform before jumping into the water.
3. Place **Eden Frame - Small 1**.
4. Craft or bring enough **Stainless Steel**.
5. Add the **Farming Platform** to the frame walkway.
6. Connect water and power.
7. Add soil and seeds so the first roots step can complete.

---

<h2 id="what-to-bring">
  What to bring before sailing to Pishon
</h2>

Do not sail to Pishon empty if you plan to build.

Before I commit to the first Eden setup, I want:

* **Stainless Steel** for the frame and Farming Platform;
* **Iron and enough oil** to craft more Stainless Steel at the Fabricator;
* **Connection Ports** for the frame and support routing;
* **Compression Pump** parts if your frame prompt asks for them;
* **Gas Tank / storage parts** if your frame prompt asks for them;
* cable or outlet support for power;
* enough battery reserve for the return trip;
* empty inventory space for heavy construction parts;
* a save before placing the frame.


<GuideCallout title="Build-trip rule">
Scout Pishon light if you only want to find the location. Bring real construction materials if you want to place the frame and start the Farming Platform step.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="stainless-steel">
  How to make Stainless Steel
</h2>

Stainless Steel is the first big material wall for Project Eden.

You make **Stainless Steel in the Fabricator**. The recipe is:

* **1 Iron**
* **10L oil**

That oil cost matters. If your oil storage is low, Project Eden can stall before the Farming Platform is even built. I check my oil supply before sailing to Pishon, especially if I have also been using oil for diesel, petrol, or other ship systems.

If the Eden Frame prompt asks for around **50 Stainless Steel**, do not try to force the route without it. Go back, craft the steel, clean your storage, and return when you can finish the build instead of leaving half a construction site behind.

<h2 id="find-the-pishon-platform">
  Find the Pishon platform
</h2>

When you arrive at Pishon, do not build in open water. Find the actual platform and get close enough that the build ghost snaps to the right surface.

The marker can be hard to read in poor weather. I slow the boat down, circle carefully, and look for the platform before jumping in.

If the Eden Frame refuses placement, check:

* Are you close enough to the platform?
* Are you aiming at the correct walkway or build surface?
* Is the build ghost clipping into an edge or support?
* Is the boat drifting while you are trying to place it?
* Are floating crates, panels, or loose debris blocking the build area?
* Do you need to approach from a lower angle or move around the support structure?

<h2 id="build-the-first-frame">
  Build Eden Frame - Small 1
</h2>

Once you have the parts, build the first frame carefully.

My build order is:

1. Park close enough that the platform is easy to reach.
2. Select **Eden Frame - Small 1**.
3. Confirm the build ghost is valid.
4. Add the missing parts to the build queue.
5. Wait for fabrication to finish.
6. Check the next platform and support prompts before leaving.

If the frame refuses to complete even though the prompt looks correct, check for floating objects near the platform. Loose crates, panels, or debris can interfere with the build area. Clear them, then try placement again.

This is not the place to rush. A clean first frame saves more time than trying to fix a bad placement later.

<h2 id="farming-platform">
  Build the Farming Platform
</h2>

After Eden Frame - Small 1 is built, the next practical step is the **Farming Platform**.

The Farming Platform snaps onto the frame walkway. This is the first part that makes Project Eden feel like a growing system instead of only a metal frame.

Plan around:

* **Stainless Steel x8** for the Farming Platform;
* a valid snap point on the Eden Frame walkway;
* water support;
* power support;
* soil;
* seeds.

If the Farming Platform will not snap, walk the frame slowly and look for the correct connection point. Do not try to place it like a random free-standing object. It is meant to attach to the frame.

Once the Farming Platform is placed, the route becomes about supporting growth: water, power, soil, and seeds.

<h2 id="water-power-soil-seeds">
  Water, power, soil, and seeds
</h2>

The Farming Platform needs support before it can become useful.

The important checks are:

* **Water:** connect the water route so the platform can be watered.
* **Power:** power the frame or platform setup through the available socket.
* **Soil:** add the soil needed for planting.
* **Seeds:** plant the required seeds so the first roots step can progress.

For water, connect a **Water Purifier** to the **Water Pump on the lower part of the Frame**. Once the freshwater route is connected to the main frame, watering is automatic; you do not need to keep watering the Farming Platform by hand.

For power, place a wind turbine or solar panel where the frame can use it, then connect the power into the frame socket. After water and power are both working, add soil and seeds, then wait for the first roots step to progress.

Do not leave immediately after placing the platform. Wait until the seed and support setup actually starts working. The first roots objective completes only after the growing setup is properly supported.

<h2 id="power-and-routing">
  Power and routing before you continue
</h2>

Before I continue deeper into Eden, I want the ship and platform support to be stable.

Project Eden becomes much easier when I already have:

* enough battery reserve;
* stable power generation;
* clear power routing;
* spare connection ports;
* organized storage;
* enough oil to craft more Stainless Steel;
* enough room to build support systems.

Wall outlets can help once the boat gets busy, but only if the route is readable. If I cannot tell what a cable powers, I do not want to add Eden support inputs on top of it.

Use the <Link href="/the-last-caretaker/power-fuel-wall-outlets">Power & Fuel Wall Outlets Guide</Link> if you need to clean up the ship before returning to Pishon.

<h2 id="what-can-wait">
  What can wait until after first roots
</h2>

Do not try to handle every New Horizons route during the same Pishon trip.

These can wait unless you are already nearby or the game gives you a clear reason to do them now:

* Project Jonah and the Oil Whale;
* Research Outpost Theta 9;
* deeper Eden modules after the first roots step;
* Samples and Holo Memories away from your current route;
* rocket or vehicle work if your ship is still unstable.

If you are preparing Project Eden, finish the frame, Farming Platform, water, power, soil, and seed checks first.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Common Project Eden mistakes
</h2>

* **Sailing to Pishon without Stainless Steel:** the first frame and Farming Platform can both stall.
* **Forgetting the Stainless Steel recipe:** craft it in the Fabricator with Iron and oil.
* **Ignoring oil supply:** Stainless Steel costs oil, so low oil can block Eden construction.
* **Building from a bad angle:** reposition if the frame ghost keeps failing.
* **Leaving floating debris near the platform:** crates, panels, or loose objects can interfere with placement.
* **Missing the Farming Platform:** the first frame alone is not the full first roots step.
* **Not connecting water or power:** the platform needs support before planting matters.
* **Leaving before seeds take root:** confirm the growth setup is actually working before sailing away.
* **Trying to do Oil Whale, Theta 9, and Eden in one run:** each route competes for power, inventory, and attention.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Where does Project Eden start?

Project Eden starts at Pishon. Follow the Pishon marker, find the platform, and place Eden Frame - Small 1 there.

### How do I make Stainless Steel?

Make Stainless Steel in the Fabricator with **1 Iron** and **10L oil**. Bring enough oil before you commit to the Eden build chain.

### Why can’t I place the Eden Frame?

Move closer to the platform, check the build surface, clear nearby floating crates or panels, and try a different angle around the support structure. Eden Frame placement can be sensitive.

### What comes after Eden Frame - Small 1?

Build the Farming Platform on the frame walkway. Then connect water and power, add soil, and plant seeds so the first roots step can progress.

### How much Stainless Steel does the Farming Platform need?

Plan around **Stainless Steel x8** for the Farming Platform, then confirm the current in-game prompt before building.

### Where do I connect power?

Use the available socket on the frame / platform setup. Place wind or solar nearby if needed, then connect the power route into the frame system.

### How do I water the Farming Platform?

Use the water support route. Connect the lower Water Pump / water input through the appropriate water system, such as a Water Purifier, so the platform can be watered automatically.

### Do I need the Oil Whale before Project Eden?

Not for the first frame or Farming Platform. Oil Whale can help later if you need more oil, but the early Eden route mainly needs Stainless Steel, support parts, water, power, soil, and seeds.
