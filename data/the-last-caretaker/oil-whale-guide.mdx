<h2 id="oil-whale-start">
  How to start the Oil Whale route
</h2>

The Oil Whale route is tied to **Project Jonah**, so do not sail around randomly looking for a whale first.

Before this route makes sense, you need your **Lazarus Complex** running well enough to produce a mature human. Bring a **human**, not just a seed, to **Omega Nursery / Platform Omega**. On the map, look for the **shark icon** around Platform Omega / Omega Nursery; that is the marker I use when I am trying to start the Whale route.

I would not bring my best human unless I am willing to risk losing access to them for a while. After the Project Jonah step, getting that human back out can be awkward, so I prefer using a duplicate, a lower-priority human, or someone I do not need for my main plan.

My start checklist is:

* Lazarus Complex is already working;
* I have a mature human ready;
* the map route points me toward the shark icon near Omega Nursery / Platform Omega;
* the boat has enough battery reserve;
* I have spare oil capacity on the boat;
* I have cable, outlet, or wall socket routing ready;
* I save before committing the human and starting the Whale setup.

This is the first real warning: the Whale route is not just “find oil.” It asks for a human, power, storage, and a boat that can handle a messy setup.

<h2 id="is-oil-whale-worth-it">
  Is the Oil Whale worth it?
</h2>

The Oil Whale is worth it when I want a prepared burst of oil and my boat can afford the setup.

I use it when:

* I am already near Omega Nursery / Platform Omega;
* I can spare about **75–100kW** of power;
* I have empty oil tank capacity on the boat;
* I can park beside the Whale safely;
* I want one or two large oil pulls before moving on.

I skip it when:

* my batteries are already low;
* my deck is full of temporary cables and random tanks;
* I only need a calm long refill;
* I do not have a spare human ready;
* a refinery or oil station is closer and safer.

The important tradeoff is simple: **the Oil Whale can give oil quickly, but it spends power quickly too.** If the boat is unstable, fix power and routing first.

<h2 id="approach-dock-and-power-switch">
  Approach, dock, and find the power switch
</h2>

When the Whale appears, approach from the side and slow down early. Do not charge straight into the front of the Whale, and do not try to park the boat on top of it.

The Whale has **side docking points**. I line up the boat close to the Whale’s body, find the docking prompt along the side, and climb onto the Whale when I need to reach the top oil setup.

There is also a **power switch on the Whale**. If the Whale is not starting even though you are close enough, check the Whale’s side rather than only checking your boat. A useful orientation is: when you are looking at the Whale from the front and the head is on your right, check the left-side area for the switch. You still need enough available power; finding the switch does not help if the setup is underpowered.

My docking order is:

1. Slow down before reaching the Whale.
2. Park beside the Whale instead of ramming the front.
3. Find the side docking point.
4. Climb onto the Whale if the oil setup needs direct access.
5. Find the Whale power switch if the setup does not start.
6. Connect power only after the boat is stable.
7. Start the oil process after checking free tank space.

If the Whale pulls away or the boat keeps drifting, reposition. Forcing a bad angle usually wastes more time than docking again.

<h2 id="where-to-put-oil-tanks">
  Put oil tanks on the boat
</h2>

I prefer keeping useful oil storage on the **boat**, not permanently on the Whale.

The reason is simple: the oil only matters if I can take it with me. You may be able to place parts of the setup on or near the Whale, but if your main storage is not on the boat, you can create a new transport problem after solving the oil problem.

My practical rule is:

* keep the main oil tanks on the boat;
* power the Whale setup from the boat or local generation if your build allows it;
* transfer the oil into boat storage before leaving;
* avoid layouts that only work while the boat is parked perfectly;
* keep enough deck space to reach tanks, cables, sockets, and the docking point.

If your oil, diesel, petrol, or power routing is already confusing, clean it first. The <Link href="/the-last-caretaker/power-fuel-wall-outlets">Power & Fuel Wall Outlets Guide</Link> covers outlet placement, cable range, and fuel routing cleanup.

<h2 id="oil-amount">
  Oil amount: 10,000L, 20,000L, and transfer speed
</h2>

A normal Oil Whale pull gives about **10,000L** of oil.

With extra storage, I plan around a **20,000L temporary setup**. That does not mean the Whale gives 20,000L in one action. It means I can hold more oil across repeated pulls before moving it into the boat.

The useful numbers I plan around are:

* **10,000L:** one normal Whale pull;
* **20,000L:** a useful temporary storage target with extra tanks;
* **about 72,000L/hour:** a good transfer rate when the connection is stable and the tank route is working;
* **75–100kW:** the power range I want available before starting.

Treat **72,000L/hour** as a clean-setup number, not a guaranteed speed in every situation. If the boat drifts, the cable disconnects, the wrong tank is linked, or the Whale moves out of range, the real transfer is slower because the setup itself keeps breaking.

<h2 id="power-cost">
  Power cost and battery warning
</h2>

Power is the real price of the Oil Whale.

If my boat has around 100kW of stored power and the Whale uses most of it, I do not call that free oil. I call it oil bought with battery reserve. That matters because I still need power for lights, tools, pumps, charging, movement, and mistakes.

Before I send the Whale down, I check:

* Can I still recharge myself after the cycle?
* Can the boat keep essential systems running?
* Do I have enough tank capacity for the oil?
* Is solar or wind helping right now?
* Are decorative lights or nonessential machines draining power?
* Can I recover if the docking setup fails?

<GuideCallout title="Battery rule">
Do not start a Whale cycle just because the Whale is available. Start it when the boat can lose a large chunk of power and still safely move, charge, and transfer oil.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="transfer-oil">
  Transfer oil back to the boat
</h2>

After the Whale returns with oil, wait until the oil is actually inside your boat storage before leaving.

A clean transfer needs:

* the Whale close enough to the boat;
* a working cable or wall socket connection;
* the correct oil route;
* enough free tank capacity;
* time for the oil to move;
* a stable boat position while the transfer runs.

If the connection is working well, the oil can move at about **72,000L/hour**. If the cable disconnects, the Whale shifts, or the tank route is wrong, stop and fix the setup instead of assuming the Whale is empty.

Getting the Whale to surface is only half the route. Leaving with the oil is the part that matters.

<h2 id="oil-whale-vs-refinery">
  Oil Whale vs refinery
</h2>

I do not use the Oil Whale as my only oil plan.

The Whale is better when:

* I am near Platform Omega already;
* I can spare 75–100kW of power;
* I have tank capacity ready;
* I want a quick 10,000L or 20,000L run;
* I do not want to travel back to a fixed oil source.

A refinery or oil station is better when:

* I want a calmer refill;
* my batteries are low;
* I do not want to manage docking;
* I need a long, predictable oil session;
* my boat routing is not ready yet.

My verdict: **use the Whale as a prepared oil burst, not as a lazy default farm.**

<h2 id="can-you-tow-oil-whale">
  Can you tow the Oil Whale?
</h2>

I would not rely on towing the Whale.

You can experiment with connections and boat positioning, but towing is not a practical route for normal progress. The Whale can resist, spin, pull away, or make the boat awkward to control.

This is also why I do not plan Tower 4 or Pen 4 around Whale towing. If a door, pen, or tower does not clearly react to the Whale in your current build, treat it as unresolved and move on. Use the Whale for oil first.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Common Oil Whale mistakes
</h2>

* **Going to Omega Nursery without a human:** bring a mature human for the Project Jonah / Whale route, not just seeds.
* **Bringing your best human:** use a duplicate or lower-priority human if you can, because getting them back afterward can be awkward.
* **Missing the shark icon:** use the map marker around Platform Omega / Omega Nursery instead of searching randomly.
* **Ramming the Whale from the front:** approach from the side and dock cleanly.
* **Ignoring the Whale power switch:** if the setup does not start, check the Whale-side switch and your available power.
* **Leaving oil storage off the boat:** oil only helps if you can take it with you.
* **Starting with low power:** plan around 75–100kW before the cycle.
* **Adding tanks before generation:** bigger storage is useless if the Whale cannot be powered.
* **Leaving before transfer finishes:** wait until the oil is actually inside your boat tanks.
* **Trying to tow the Whale:** do not build your route around towing unless a future update makes it reliable.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What if the Oil Whale does not appear?

Check that you are on the Project Jonah route, that you are around Omega Nursery / Platform Omega, and that you brought a mature human. If you only brought seeds or are not at the shark icon route, you may be missing the trigger.

### Do I need Lazarus Complex before the Oil Whale?

Yes. You need the Lazarus Complex running well enough to produce a mature human for the Project Jonah / Whale step.

### Should I bring my best human?

I would avoid it. Use a duplicate or lower-priority human if possible, because recovering that human after the route can be awkward.

### Why will the Oil Whale not start even after docking?

Check the Whale-side power switch and make sure you have enough available power. Docking beside the Whale is not enough if the setup is still unpowered.

### Can I reuse the Oil Whale after one oil pull?

Yes, but each pull still costs power and setup time. Make sure you have enough battery reserve and tank capacity before repeating the cycle.

### Why is my oil transfer slower than 72,000L/hour?

That number assumes a clean, stable connection. If the Whale drifts, the cable disconnects, the tank route is wrong, or storage is full, the real transfer will be slower or stop completely.

### Can I build solar panels or wind turbines on the Whale?

Depending on your current build state, you may be able to place generation near or on the Whale. I still prefer planning around boat power first because the boat is what needs to leave with the oil.

### Can I tow the Oil Whale?

Do not rely on towing. It is awkward and not a reliable progression route.

### Does the Oil Whale unlock Tower 4 or Pen 4?

Not in a reliable way. Treat Tower 4 or Pen 4 Whale theories as unresolved unless your current build clearly gives you a working objective.
