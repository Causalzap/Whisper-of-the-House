<h2 id="what-to-do-first">
  What to do first in New Horizons
</h2>

The first thing I check after loading into New Horizons is not the newest marker. It is my boat.

Update 5 adds several systems that all ask for the same things: power, storage, clean routing, fuel planning, and enough room to carry parts. If the boat is already hard to read, Project Eden, Oil Whale, and Theta 9 make it worse.

My returning-player order is:

1. **Check power storage and generation.** Oil Whale and deep routes can drain more power than expected.
2. **Clean up the boat.** Wall outlets are worth using before adding more tanks, pumps, and cables.
3. **Visit Pishon for Project Eden.** This is the first big construction route.
4. **Start Project Jonah when you are ready for the Oil Whale route.**
5. **Prepare before diving into Research Outpost Theta 9.**
6. **Use Samples, Holo Memories, and cave paintings as exploration goals, not emergency priorities.**
7. **Test Tide Ripper / water jet upgrades before long underwater routes.**

That order keeps New Horizons from turning into one overloaded trip.

<GuideCallout title="Returning-player rule">
If the ship has low battery, messy cables, and no spare storage, fix that before chasing every new marker. New Horizons gives you more to do, but the boat still decides how safe each route feels.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="project-jonah-oil-whale">
  Project Jonah and the Oil Whale
</h2>

Project Jonah is the main Whale route in New Horizons. Do not treat the Oil Whale as a random free oil button.

To start the practical Whale route, plan around **Omega Nursery / Platform Omega** and bring a **human**, not just a seed. On the map, look for the **shark icon** around Platform Omega / Omega Nursery; that is the route I use when I am trying to start the Whale setup instead of sailing around blindly.

Once the Whale appears, approach carefully. It has docking points along its side, and the oil setup becomes much easier if your boat is close, stable, and already prepared with power and storage.

The important Oil Whale rules are:

* use a grown human for the Project Jonah / Whale trigger;
* approach the Whale carefully and park beside it;
* look for the side docking points;
* climb onto the Whale when you need to reach the oil collection setup;
* keep oil tanks on your boat when possible so you can actually take the oil with you;
* power the setup from the boat or place generation on the Whale if the game allows your current build;
* do not start the process with a weak battery bank.

<h2 id="wall-outlets-grid-scan">
  Wall outlets and grid scan
</h2>

Wall outlets are one of the most important practical changes in New Horizons because they let you clean up the ship before it becomes unreadable.

  <Image
    src="/images/the-last-caretaker/the-last-caretaker-wall-outlets-skill-unlocks.webp"

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Power, fuel, and gas wall outlets are useful because New Horizons adds more systems that need clean routing.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

The main mistake is thinking wall outlets are wireless routing. They are not. They still care about range, direction, input, output, and what each connector is attached to.

I use wall outlets for:

* cleaner power routing;
* oil, diesel, and petrol organization;
* gas and oxygen-style routing;
* Oil Whale transfer setup;
* Project Eden support inputs;
* reducing cable clutter near machines and tanks.

Grid Scan becomes useful once I can no longer tell what the ship is doing by looking at it. If power is disappearing, a machine is not receiving input, or a new outlet is not behaving, Grid Scan is worth checking.

For the detailed setup, use the <Link href="/the-last-caretaker/power-fuel-wall-outlets">Power & Fuel Wall Outlets Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="research-outpost-theta-9">
  Research Outpost Theta 9
</h2>

Research Outpost Theta 9 is the new underwater station route I prepare for, not a place I enter casually.

The station has an emergency signal, underwater entry, enemies, locked areas, and an authorization blocker. If you reach a screen that says **Research Outpost Theta-9 - Ventilation 3.3 Authorization required**, the answer is not to keep clicking the same door. You need the authorization route first.

* weapons and ammo for nests, spiders, and flying enemies;
* enough free inventory space;
* power and oxygen safety;
* a backup save before the deeper rooms.

<h2 id="samples-memories-music">
  Samples, Holo Memories, and music
</h2>

New Horizons also adds smaller systems that matter during exploration.

**Samples** give you a reason to collect, catalog, and send discoveries to space. I treat this as a long-term exploration system rather than something to finish before Project Eden or the Whale.

**Holo Memories** and cultural discoveries are worth checking when you are already exploring ruins, caves, or station routes. If a location looks like it has history attached to it, slow down and scan before leaving.

**Music cartridges and the portable player** are not progression blockers, but they are part of the update’s world-building. Pick them up when you find them, but do not let them distract from power, routing, or station objectives.

The practical rule: collect these systems while traveling, but do not let them replace the main route you came for.

<h2 id="water-jet-tide-ripper">
  Water Jet, Tide Ripper, and underwater movement
</h2>

Update 5 adds water jet propulsion equipment, and the Tide Ripper is the tool I care about before long underwater work.

The standard hand jet helps with underwater movement. The stronger Tide Ripper / Twin Jet version is the one I want before deeper routes because Theta 9 and other underwater objectives become slower and riskier without good movement.

I use water jet tools for:

* long underwater approaches;
* returning to the boat after a deep dive;
* station entry routes;
* reducing time spent exposed in open water;
* correcting bad positioning when the weather or waves make visibility poor.

If I am going to Theta 9, I want this tool ready before leaving the boat.

<h2 id="what-can-wait">
  What can wait until later
</h2>

Not every new name needs to become the next trip. I leave these for later unless I am already nearby or the game gives me a clear objective:

* full Project Eden completion beyond the first frame;
* deeper Committee progress;
* Project Jonah follow-up steps after the first Whale setup;
* rocket payload work if I am not already in that chain;
* Gyropad and vehicle expansion if the boat itself is still unstable;
* **Samples and Cultural Memories unless I am already exploring that location.**

This does not mean I ignore those systems. It means I do not interrupt a real route just to chase side discoveries. If I am already inside a ruin, cave, station, or Eden route and I see a Sample, Holo Memory, or cultural scan, I grab it. If I am preparing for Pishon, Oil Whale, or Theta 9, I finish that route first.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first after Update 5?

Check your boat power, storage, and routing. Then choose one route: Project Eden at Pishon, Project Jonah / Oil Whale, Research Outpost Theta 9, or wall outlet cleanup.

### Is New Horizons a fresh-start update?

No. New players can start normally, but returning players get the most value by using an old stable boat and choosing which new route to handle first.

### Where does Project Eden start?

Project Eden starts at Pishon. Find the platform, place the Eden Frame, then prepare stainless steel, connection ports, pump parts, gas tank parts, and later water / power / oxygen inputs.

### How do I start the Whale route?

Use the Project Jonah / Omega route and bring a human, not just a seed. Once the Whale appears, approach carefully, use its side docking points, and prepare power plus oil storage before collecting.

### Is the Oil Whale better than a refinery?

Only in the right situation. The Whale is useful when you are nearby and prepared, but it can drain power and take setup time. Refineries or oil stations can still be easier for calm long refills.

### What are wall outlets for?

Wall outlets clean up power, fuel, and gas routing. They help with oil, diesel, petrol, oxygen-style systems, Project Eden inputs, and Oil Whale setups.

### Why is Ventilation 3.3 locked in Theta 9?

It needs Research Outpost Theta authorization. Find the terminal route first, then return to the Ventilation 3.3 door.

### Are Samples required before Project Eden?

No. Samples are an exploration system. Collect and send discoveries when you find them, but do not delay Project Eden or Whale prep just to chase every sample first.

### Is Tide Ripper required?

Not always, but it makes underwater routes much more comfortable. I would bring it before Research Outpost Theta 9 or any long underwater objective.

### What should I read next?

Use the <Link href="/the-last-caretaker/project-eden-guide">Project Eden Guide</Link> for Pishon, the <Link href="/the-last-caretaker/oil-whale-guide">Oil Whale Guide</Link> for Project Jonah oil setup, or the <Link href="/the-last-caretaker/research-outpost-theta-9-guide">Theta 9 Guide</Link> if you are stuck on authorization.
