<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Cheap Car Repair**, your first goal is not to become a perfect mechanic. Your first goal is to finish jobs cheaply without making the customer angry.

Start by learning the basic loop:

**take job → check objectives → use the right tool → cut corners carefully → finish the checklist → call the customer → keep useful parts when the game lets you**.

The most important early things to learn are **how to drop items with G**, how **sloppiness** works, when to use cheap fixes, and which early missions introduce tires, fluids, batteries, air intake parts and dangerous customers.

<h2 id="what-is-cheap-car-repair">
  What is Cheap Car Repair?
</h2>

Cheap Car Repair is a repair shop sim about running a very questionable garage in 1990s Poland. This guide covers how to get started, how the repair loop works, and where to find help when a mission blocks you.

The game is not only about making cars perfect. A lot of the fun comes from deciding how cheap you can be before the job becomes a problem.

That means you will constantly ask questions like:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Do I really need to remove all the rust?</li>
  <li>Can I paint over this and get away with it?</li>
  <li>Is this old tire good enough?</li>
  <li>Can I keep this better part for myself?</li>
  <li>Will this customer notice if I cut corners?</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="basic-repair-loop">
  The basic repair loop
</h2>

Most early jobs follow the same pattern: inspect the objective list, pick up the right tool, remove or cover the problem, install the requested part, then check whether the job can be turned in.

<GuideTable
caption="How the main Cheap Car Repair job loop works."

rows={repairLoopRows}

The trick is knowing when "good enough" is actually good enough. If a job only needs the car to look usable, overworking the repair can waste supplies and time. If the customer is important, angry or connected to the story, being too sloppy can create bigger problems.

<h2 id="sloppiness">
  Sloppiness is profit until someone notices
</h2>

Sloppiness is one of the most important early systems. You can make money by doing bad work quickly, but only if the customer does not catch you.

That does not mean you should ruin every car. Think of sloppiness as a risk meter:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Small shortcuts can save time and supplies.</li>
  <li>Painting over bad spots can finish a cheap job faster.</li>
  <li>Loose parts, missing parts or obvious mistakes can come back to hurt you.</li>
  <li>Story customers and dangerous customers deserve more caution.</li>
</ul>

</h2>

These are the mistakes that are not always obvious from the basic repair loop:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Forgetting G:</strong> press G when you need to drop or throw the item in your hands.</li>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Is Cheap Car Repair a serious mechanic sim?

Not really. It uses repair sim systems, but the main loop is about cheap fixes, bad decisions, strange substitutes and trying to make money before customers notice.

### What should I do first in Cheap Car Repair?

Take Sebastian's first job, follow the objectives, remove rust, paint the car green, polish it and call the customer. After that, restore power, fix your own car and build your shop sign.

### How do I drop items in Cheap Car Repair?

Press **G** to throw or drop the item or tool you are holding.

### How do I save in Cheap Car Repair?

Use **Save Game** from the menu before stopping a long session. The game also shows saving progress during some early story steps. For more details, read the <Link href="/cheap-car-repair/controls-drop-items-save-tools/">Controls, Drop Items, Save and Tools Guide</Link>.

### What is sloppiness in Cheap Car Repair?

Sloppiness is the risk created by bad or cheap work. It can help profit if the customer does not notice, but it can also lead to trouble if the repair is too poor.

### Should I remove all rust?

Not always. For cheap customer jobs, follow the objective list and do enough to complete the job. For your own car or risky customers, be more careful.

### Why can I not finish Daddy's Little Girl?

The air intake pipe is a common issue. If it has been removed, reinstall it in the engine area and secure the clips with pliers before trying to finish the job.

### Can I keep customer parts?

Sometimes the game tempts you to keep useful parts. Be careful: taking a part may help your own car, but some parts are required before the customer job can be completed.

### How do I make money faster?

Take jobs where the payout beats your part cost, supply cost and time cost. Use found parts, finish only the required objectives and save good parts for your own car or higher-paying jobs.

### Should I use proper parts or cheap substitutes?

Use proper parts when the job requires them or when the customer is risky. Use questionable substitutes when the game allows it and the profit is worth the risk.

### Where should I go after this guide?

Use the mission guide if you are following the story, the money guide if you want profit, the controls guide if you are stuck with tools, and the parts guide if you need to understand vehicles and replacement parts.
