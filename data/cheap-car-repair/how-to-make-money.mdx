<h2 id="how-money-works">
  How money works in Cheap Car Repair
</h2>

Money comes from customer jobs, but the salary is only one part of profit. A job is worth doing when the final payout is higher than what you spent or sacrificed to finish it.

<GuideTable
caption="How to read money signals in Cheap Car Repair."
columns={payoutColumns}
rows={payoutRows}
/>

The main rule applies to every money decision in this guide:

<strong>Use cheap work on safe jobs. Use safer work on risky jobs. Save good parts when the current customer does not deserve them.</strong>

That rule is the reason you can paint only what matters on one car, keep a better battery for your own car on another, and still avoid the worst shortcuts on dangerous story customers.

<h2 id="best-jobs-for-money">
  Best jobs for making money fast
</h2>

The best-paying job is not always the job with the biggest salary. A big job that forces you to buy expensive parts can be worse than a smaller job you finish with supplies you already own.

<GuideTable
caption="Which Cheap Car Repair jobs are best for money?"
columns={jobValueColumns}
rows={jobValueRows}
/>

For early money, look for jobs with one or more of these traits:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>You already have the required part.</li>
  <li>The replacement can be found nearby.</li>
  <li>The job has only one or two repair systems.</li>
  <li>The customer is not a dangerous story character.</li>
  <li>The job allows a cheap substitute without blocking completion.</li>
</ul>

Avoid calling a job "high value" just because the number looks bigger. First check whether you need to buy tires, rims, filters, pulleys, belts or fluids.

<h2 id="cheap-shortcuts-that-save-money">
  Cheap shortcuts that actually save money
</h2>

A shortcut is only good if it saves more than it risks. The goal is not to ruin every car. The goal is to use the cheapest repair that the job accepts.

<GuideTable
caption="Cheap shortcuts and when they help profit."
columns={shortcutColumns}
rows={shortcutRows}
/>

Do not stack every shortcut on the same car. One bad part or cheap fluid mix may pass. A bad tire, missing cap, loose bolt, weak battery and wrong filter on the same job is how a profitable repair becomes an obvious scam.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best way to make money in Cheap Car Repair?

Take jobs where the payout is higher than your part cost, supply cost and time cost. Use found parts, finish only the required objectives and save good parts for your own car or higher-paying jobs.

### What jobs pay the most in Cheap Car Repair?

Large multi-part jobs can pay more, but they are only good profit if you already have the parts or can find them cheaply. Early on, quick fluid, tire, battery and filter jobs can be better money than a big job that forces you to buy expensive parts.

### What is the fastest early money route?

Finish small jobs quickly, use driveway clients for repeat cash, search hinted locations before buying parts and avoid long repairs until you know the missing parts.

### Should I repair my own car?

Yes, when the story requires it or when the part gives long-term value. Do not over-invest in cosmetic repairs too early if those parts could finish a paying customer job.

### What does sloppiness bonus mean?

A sloppiness bonus means your cheap repair worked in your favor. You saved value through bad or cheap work, and the customer did not notice enough to punish you.

### What does sloppiness detected mean?

Sloppiness detected means the customer noticed the bad work. The shortcut can reduce your payout, remove the bonus or create a follow-up problem.

### Should I use proper parts or cheap parts? 

Use cheap parts only when the job accepts them and the saved value is worth the risk. For the full decision framework, use the cheap shortcuts section above.

### Is painting over rust good for profit?

Only when the job accepts it. Painting too early can save time, but if the objective still wants rust removal or filler work, it can create sloppiness instead of profit.

### Are regular clients worth doing?

Yes. Regular driveway clients are your repeat cash source once they start appearing. They are useful between story jobs, especially when their repair uses parts you already have.

### Is the scrapyard worth using?

Yes. The scrapyard can save money on common replacement parts. It is most useful when a job needs a part that would cost too much at the store.

### Should I buy parts or search first?

Search first when the game gives a hint or the part is likely nearby. Buy only when the missing part is specific, cheap enough or the job payout justifies saving time.

### Why am I losing money?

You may be buying too many parts, wasting supplies, getting sloppiness detected, using good parts on low-value jobs or starting big repairs without checking the missing parts first.
