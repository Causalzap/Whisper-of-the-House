<h2 id="mission-objectives">
  Daddy's Little Girl mission objectives
</h2>

Daddy's Little Girl is the first job where Cheap Car Repair starts combining several systems in one car. It is not just a paint-and-polish job anymore. You need to handle wheel work, fluids, a battery and the rear engine area.

For a first playthrough, expect the mission to take about **15–25 minutes**. Most of that time comes from finding the correct rear engine access, understanding RL tire placement and learning that the air intake pipe has to be reinstalled.

  Replace the rear-left tire
</h2>

Start with the tire because it teaches you several controls that matter later. Move the jack into place, raise the car to the maximum height, then use the wrench on the rear-left wheel.

Do not forget to tighten the bolts after the tire is mounted. Loose parts can raise sloppiness and may stop the next step from behaving properly.

<h2 id="air-filter-decision">
  Should you replace the air filter in Daddy's Little Girl?
</h2>

The air filter is the flexible part of this rear engine step. The air intake pipe is required, but the air filter is where the game starts teaching you the cheap-repair choice.

If your route lets you keep, skip or substitute the air filter, the job can still be completed as long as the checklist accepts the result and the **air intake pipe is reinstalled**. The trade-off is sloppiness: a worse filter, missing filter or cheap substitute can save value, but it also increases the chance that the repair gets noticed.

Use this rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Required:</strong> reinstall the air intake pipe and secure the clips.</li>
  <li><strong>Flexible:</strong> the air filter can be part of the cheap-repair decision if the objective accepts it.</li>
  <li><strong>Risk:</strong> a bad or missing filter may increase sloppiness.</li>
  <li><strong>Safe route:</strong> keep the intake system complete if you are not sure what the job will accept.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How long does Daddy's Little Girl take?

Daddy's Little Girl usually takes about **15–25 minutes** on a first playthrough. It is faster once you know that RL means rear left and the Mini Fiax engine is in the rear.

### How do you complete Daddy's Little Girl in Cheap Car Repair?

Replace the rear-left tire, replace the battery, refill brake fluid, fill the petrol objective, open the rear engine area, remove and reinstall the air intake pipe, then secure the clips with pliers.

### What does RL mean in Daddy's Little Girl?

RL means **rear left**. The tire objective wants the rear-left tire, not the front wheel or the right side.

### How do I replace the tire?

Raise the car with the jack, click the wheel so the bolts are highlighted, unscrew the bolts with right mouse, remove the wheel, keep the rim, add the replacement tire, mount the wheel and tighten the bolts.

### Where is the battery?

Look around the garage area for a spare battery. Remove the old one from the car, place the replacement and tighten it.

### How do I refill brake fluid?

Open the brake fluid reservoir cap, pick up the brake fluid, fill the reservoir and close the cap again.

### How do I fill up petrol?

Use the fluid mixing table, prepare the required mixture, then pour it into the tank until the objective is accepted.

### Where is the engine in Daddy's Little Girl?

The engine is in the rear. Remove the trunk hatch or liftgate so you can reach the air intake pipe and air filter area.

### How do I remove the air intake pipe?

Pick up the pliers and use them on the clips holding the air intake pipe. After it is removed, make sure you reinstall it before finishing the job.

### Why won't the air intake pipe install?

The pipe has to be placed before it can be clipped. If it will not install, use the reset steps in the air intake fix section, then place the pipe and secure the clips with pliers.

### What happens if I skip the air filter? 

Skipping, keeping or substituting the air filter can save value only if the job accepts the result. The risk is sloppiness: the customer may notice the cheap repair, while the air intake pipe itself still has to be reinstalled.

### Can I finish the job without the air intake pipe?

No. If the intake pipe is removed, the car still needs it reinstalled before the job can be completed.

### Why can't I call Angelica?

Check the job list. You may have the wrong tire, loose bolts, an open cap, missing battery work, incomplete petrol, or the air intake pipe still removed.

### Can I make more money on Daddy's Little Girl?

Yes, but only on flexible parts. Battery quality and air filter choices can create savings, while the air intake pipe, tire bolts, fluid caps and petrol objective must still be completed.
