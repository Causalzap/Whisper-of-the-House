<h2 id="walkthrough">
  The Adventures of Elliot walkthrough
</h2>

This walkthrough is built for the opening route, not a final all-endings route. The goal is to get you through the first major systems cleanly: Faie, Southern Caves, guideposts, clean combat, Warp to Faie, Ignite, Hammer, the Golden Bell, cats, Shards of Life, Shrine of Life progress, Magicite, and time travel.

If you want a broader overview first, read the <Link href="/the-adventures-of-elliot">The Adventures of Elliot Guide Hub</Link>. If you are still learning basic systems, use the <Link href="/the-adventures-of-elliot/beginner-guide">Beginner Guide</Link> before following this route.

<h2 id="opening-faie-and-hurick">
  Opening: Faie joins Elliot and Hurick points the way
</h2>

After the opening event, follow the dialogue until Faie joins Elliot. The route objective becomes simple: get back to the village and find a way toward the doorway of time.

Do not grind the opening area. This part is about setting up Faie, the village route, and the reason you need to move forward.

Once you regain control, look for Hurick near camp. He tells you that the way back to the village runs through **Southern Caves**.

<h2 id="southern-caves-route">
  Southern Caves route
</h2>

Southern Caves is where the walkthrough starts turning into real exploration. Expect enemies, side paths, chests, Magicite, cracked walls, rocks, and routes that are not ready yet.

This is the only rule you need for the opening route:

<strong>Clear what is open now, write down what is blocked, then return when a tool, weapon effect, Faie ability, or age change gives you a new answer.</strong>

Use this habit while moving through the caves:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>open reachable chests,</li>
  <li>fight enemies that block the route,</li>
  <li>check nearby side rooms,</li>
  <li>touch guideposts as soon as you find them,</li>
  <li>use dash for spacing and hazard movement,</li>
  <li>use shield and parry instead of taking easy hits,</li>
  <li>and test Warp to Faie when positioning looks useful.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="combat-and-movement-basics">
  Combat and movement basics: dash, shield, parry, and Warp to Faie
</h2>

Clean fights matter in the walkthrough because every unnecessary hit makes the next cave, side room, or trial more expensive.

<GuideTable
caption="Early combat and movement tools."

You do not need perfect parries to progress. You only need to stop treating every fight like a damage race. Dash away from bad angles, block when you do not know the timing, and practice parries on safer enemies before relying on them in harder rooms.

Warp to Faie is especially useful during exploration because it changes your positioning. If Faie is in a safer or more useful spot, warping can be faster than forcing Elliot through a messy route.

<h2 id="weapons-and-exploration">
  Weapons and exploration: 7 weapon types matter
</h2>

The game has **7 weapon types**, and they matter for more than damage. Some tools and weapon effects can change exploration by interacting with environmental objects, opening safer angles, or giving you a better way to handle enemies around a route check.

If you find a new weapon, test it in a safe room first. Do not learn a new weapon during a dangerous room unless you have to.

For now, the early rule is simple: rotate weapons naturally, but use your safest weapon when the route is dangerous.

<h2 id="shards-of-life-and-shrine-of-life">
  Shards of Life and Shrine of Life
</h2>

Shards of Life are health-upgrade progress, not just side collectibles. When you collect a Shard, note the area, age, room name, and nearest landmark. A Shard from a grassy side path, cave entrance, mirror room, bomb-rock route, or cracked-floor room is much easier to find again when you remember the route clue.

<h2 id="doorway-of-time">
  Doorway of time and four ages
</h2>

Once the Golden Bell is recovered, the route returns to time travel.

This is where the map starts asking you to think differently. The same location can matter across **four ages**, and routes may change depending on the age, story state, or tool access.

A blocked route may need:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>a new tool,</li>
  <li>a new Faie ability,</li>
  <li>a weapon effect,</li>
  <li>story progress,</li>
  <li>or the same area in another age.</li>
</ul>

If you cannot solve something in the current age, do not assume you missed a hidden switch. Keep progressing, then check the same region later when the route changes.


<h2 id="moon-mirror-western-marge">
  Moon Mirror route: Western Marge
</h2>

After returning to the kingdom, the story reveals the **Moon Mirror**, a shield carried by the hero king and crafted in the age of magic. It is hidden in the **Western Marge**, and Elliot is sent to find it as the next answer to Kyifrey's light staff.

This is a main-route objective, not ordinary side cleanup. Once the Golden Bell loop brings you back to the kingdom, the Moon Mirror becomes the next major story direction and pushes the walkthrough beyond the opening route.

  The Adventures of Elliot walkthrough FAQ
</h2>

### Is this a full-game walkthrough?

No. This is the confirmed early-route walkthrough. Later areas, endings, bosses, and full 100% cleanup should be added after more route information is verified.

### Where do I go after Faie joins Elliot?

Talk to Hurick near camp. He points you toward Southern Caves, which is the route back toward the village.

### Should I clear every chest in Southern Caves immediately?

No. Open what you can reach, but leave obvious tool-locked chests, rocks, cracked walls, and blocked paths for later cleanup.

### How do Shards of Life and Shrine of Life work?

Every 4 Shards of Life unlocks 1 Shrine of Life upgrade, increasing max HP by 1. Track Shards by route clue, room name, age, and nearby landmark.

### What does Self-Destruct Resistance do?

Self-Destruct Resistance costs 5 and reduces damage taken from your own bombs by 50%. It is useful if bomb routes or tight rooms make you hit yourself.

### What does Pulse Throw do?

Pulse Throw costs 10. It delivers an electric shock to nearby enemies when status-afflicted enemies are hit by the matching attack, and it creates a 10% chance of stunning enemies hit by electric shock.

### What does Ignite do?

Ignite lets Faie light objects and solve early magic route checks. After you unlock it, test it near the trial and remember earlier objects that looked lightable.

### What should I do after getting Heavy Hammer?

Check earlier rocks, breakable-looking objects, and blocked routes. Heavy Hammer is one of the first tools that turns old obstacles into new paths.

### How should I fight cleanly early?

Use dash for spacing, shield when you are unsure, and parry after you understand an enemy attack. Clean fights save resources for later caves and trials.

### What is Warp to Faie used for?

Warp to Faie can reposition Elliot to Faie's location. Use it when Faie's position gives you a safer path, better angle, or faster recovery during exploration.

### How many weapon types are there?

The game has 7 weapon types. Test new weapons in safe fights and remember that some tools or weapon effects can also matter during exploration.

### Why does the cat have the Golden Bell?

Follow the village event with the cat and Caleshia. It leads you back to the Golden Bell and moves the story toward time travel again.

### Should I track cats during the walkthrough?

Yes. Cats are tied to rewards and completion, so start tracking them as soon as the game introduces them.

### What if a route is blocked in one age?

Keep progressing and check later. Some routes need tools, story progress, or the same location in another age.

### Where should I check for bonus rewards?

Once you reach a safe hub or orphanage area, check whether Prologue Demo progress, save-data bonuses, or preorder rewards can be claimed.
