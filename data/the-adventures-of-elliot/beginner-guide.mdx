<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In your first hours of **The Adventures of Elliot: The Millennium Tales**, choose a comfortable **difficulty**, continue your **Prologue Demo save** if you already played it, then follow the story until **Faie** joins you. Ask Hurick about the village route, head through **Southern Caves**, touch every **guidepost**, open nearby chests, break obvious pots and barrels, talk to marked NPCs, unlock **Ignite**, and use tools like **Heavy Hammer** to revisit blocked routes.

The safest beginner rule is: **build clean habits early**. Avoid unnecessary hits, collect nearby resources, test new weapons, track cats and Shards of Life, and return later when a route clearly needs a tool, weapon, magic power, or another age. 
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="beginner-guide">
  The Adventures of Elliot beginner guide
</h2>

The opening hours of **The Adventures of Elliot: The Millennium Tales** are not only about following the story. Before you settle into the route, the game asks you to make a few decisions and learn several small habits that affect the rest of the adventure.

This guide focuses on what actually helps a new player:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

  <li>how to build resources before harder routes,</li>
  <li>why marked NPCs and breakable objects are worth checking,</li>
  <li>how to read blocked paths,</li>
  <li>when to backtrack,</li>
  <li>and what to track for later completion.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="difficulty-demo-save-and-bonuses">
  Difficulty, demo save, and bonus rewards
</h2>

Your first real decision is **difficulty**. Pick the setting that lets you learn the game's route logic without making every room feel like a punishment. If you enjoy careful combat, a higher difficulty can be fine. If you mainly want to learn exploration, tools, and collectibles first, start lower and focus on clean play.

If you played the **Prologue Demo**, continue that save instead of starting over unless you specifically want a fresh route. Demo progress can carry into the full game, which matters if you already explored, collected early items, or learned the first areas.

Also check for early bonus rewards once you reach the correct safe hub or reward area. Players with demo progress, save-data bonuses, or preorder items may have rewards to claim.

<h2 id="guideposts">
  Guideposts: touch them before the route gets messy
</h2>

Guideposts are important route markers and checkpoint-style points. The game reminds you to stop by guideposts when you spot them, and that advice matters once the route starts looping through caves, side paths, and time travel areas.

Do not skip them just because the next chest or cave looks close.

<h2 id="southern-caves">
  Southern Caves: learn the route, not every chest
</h2>

Southern Caves teaches the main exploration pattern. You will fight enemies, open chests, find useful pickups, and see objects you cannot solve yet.

This is the core framework:

<strong>Follow the open route, note blocked rewards, unlock new tools, then return when the route changes.</strong>

Use that framework once here. After this, each new tool or age simply gives you a new reason to revisit old paths.

<h2 id="shards-of-life">
  Shards of Life: track route clues, not only item popups
</h2>

Shards of Life are worth caring about early because they improve survivability. The mistake is remembering only the final item popup and forgetting where the Shard came from.

When you spot a Shard, note the **area name, age, room name, and nearest landmark** so cleanup is easier later. A Shard from a cave entrance, mirror room, cracked-floor route, or bomb-rock path is much easier to find again when you remember the route clue.

For confirmed health-upgrade routes, use the <Link href="/the-adventures-of-elliot/shards-of-life-locations">Shards of Life Locations guide</Link>.

<h2 id="time-travel-and-four-ages">
  Time travel and the four ages
</h2>

Once the Golden Bell and doorway of time become important, you need to think about areas across **four ages**. The same location can have different terrain, access, routes, and rewards depending on the age.

This is the simple beginner version:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If a route is blocked now, it may open in another age.</li>
  <li>If a chest is visible but unreachable, the path may come from a different version of the area.</li>
  <li>If a story event changes the world, revisit important landmarks.</li>
  <li>If a puzzle looks impossible, check whether you are in the right age or need a later tool.</li>
</ul>

Do not try to solve every age-based route the first time you see it. Just start thinking of each location as something that can change.

<h2 id="local-coop">
  Local co-op
</h2>

The Adventures of Elliot supports local co-op, where a second player can control Faie. This does not change the route structure, but it can change how puzzles, magic use, and support feel moment to moment.

For guide purposes, the important point is simple: the same cats, Shards, tools, weapons, and story routes apply whether you play solo or local co-op.

<h2 id="faq">
  The Adventures of Elliot beginner FAQ
</h2>

### What difficulty should I choose?

Choose a difficulty that lets you learn exploration, tools, Faie's magic, and weapon switching without turning every cave into a wall. You can play harder if you enjoy careful combat from the start.

### Does the Prologue Demo save carry over?

Yes. If you played the Prologue Demo, continue that save if you want to keep your early progress.

### Where do I claim bonus rewards?

Check the early safe hub or reward area once the story lets you move around again. Save-data or preorder rewards may be claimable there.

### What should I do first after Faie joins Elliot?

Talk to Hurick near camp. He points you toward Southern Caves, which is the first real route back toward the village.

### Should I break pots and barrels?

Yes, when they are on your way. Break obvious pots, barrels, and similar objects for small resources, but do not slow the whole route just to clear every corner.

### Why should I talk to marked NPCs?

Marked NPCs may point to side quests, route clues, follow-up dialogue, or rewards after story progress. They are worth checking when you pass through town.

### How do guideposts work?

Guideposts act as important route markers and checkpoint-style points. Touch them whenever you see one, especially before entering a new cave, trial, or long side route.

### Should I explore every chest and cave right away?

No. Check reachable side paths, but leave obvious tool checks for later. Many early rewards need Ignite, Hammer, bombs, weapon abilities, another route, or another age.

### When do I get Ignite?

Ignite comes from an early magic trial. After that, you can use Faie's power to light objects and solve early route checks.

### How many weapons are there?

The game has multiple weapon types. Test new weapons in safe fights and avoid using only one weapon for the entire game.

### Do weapons matter outside combat?

Yes. Some tools and weapon effects can interact with environmental objects or route checks. Hammer is the clearest early example, but ranged attacks and other weapon effects can also matter during exploration.

### Should I worry about Magicite early?

Collect it, but do not force a final build yet. Early Magicite is useful, but you need more weapons and enemy types before deciding what is best.

### How does time travel work for exploration?

The game uses four ages, and the same location can change across ages. If a route is blocked now, it may open after story progress, a new tool, or another age.

### Does The Adventures of Elliot have co-op?

Yes. Local co-op lets a second player control Faie. The main route and collectibles stay the same.

### Are cats important?

Yes. Cats are tied to rewards and completion. Start tracking them early instead of leaving every cat for the end.

### What are Shards of Life?

Shards of Life are health-upgrade collectibles. Track them by route clue, room name, and nearby landmark so cleanup is easier later.
