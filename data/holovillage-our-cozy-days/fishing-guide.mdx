<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**To fish in holoVillage: Our Cozy Days, talk to Kuro, craft a Wooden Rod, equip it, and stand near a fishing spot where fish gather.**

You **cannot fish anywhere**. When you are close enough to a valid fishing spot, a **yellow exclamation mark** appears. Press confirm to start the fishing mini-game.

For bigger fish, attach bait, reel while the fish is calm, and stop pulling when it thrashes. If you keep pulling during the thrashing phase, the line can snap. Early on, Kuro asks you to catch **three carp**, then introduces bait, koi, Fisher Rank progression, and eventually the Freshwater Crab objective beyond the blocked bridge.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="how-to-unlock-fishing">
  How to unlock fishing
</h2>

You unlock fishing by starting Kuro's early job quest in the village. After the first village setup steps, the game points you toward three job categories: **adventuring**, **farming**, and **fishing**. Kuro is the fishing expert, and his tutorial is the one that teaches you how to use the rod.

The first thing Kuro asks you to do is simple:

```text
Craft a Wooden Rod.
```

Use the crafting table, make the Wooden Rod, then return to Kuro. If you are short on materials, chop trees first. Fishing does not properly begin until you have the rod equipped and are standing at a valid fishing spot.

<GuideCallout title="Do this first">
Before you run around testing every pond, make sure you have actually crafted and equipped the Wooden Rod. Without the rod, you will not get the correct fishing prompt.
</GuideCallout>

---

<h2 id="where-to-fish">
  Where to fish in holoVillage
</h2>

The most important fishing rule is this:

**You can only fish at spots where fish gather.**

That means you cannot stand beside any random river, pond, or stream and expect fishing to work. Some water is decorative or simply not a fishing spot. This is why early players often feel like fishing is broken: the rod is equipped, but nothing happens.

To check whether a spot works:

1. Equip your fishing rod.
2. Walk near a fish-gathering spot.
3. Look for the **yellow exclamation mark**.
4. Press confirm when the prompt appears.

If no prompt appears, move to another section of water. The game is not asking you to fish anywhere; it is asking you to find the correct spot.

<GuideCallout title="Why you can't fish">
If you are holding the rod and nothing happens, you are probably not at a valid fishing spot. Look for the yellow exclamation mark instead of trying to cast into every body of water.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-to-catch-three-carp">
  How to catch three carp
</h2>

Kuro's first real fishing request is to catch **three carp**. This is the task that teaches the basic loop:

```text
Find a valid spot -> wait for the prompt -> start the mini-game -> land the fish
```

Stay near the starting fishing area and keep using valid fish-gathering spots. You may catch other items along the way, including trash, but that does not mean you are in the wrong system. Keep fishing until the carp objective is complete.

For this first task, do not worry too much about bait or rare fish. Your goal is to understand the prompt and land basic catches consistently.

<h2 id="how-bait-works">
  How bait works
</h2>

After the carp task, Kuro introduces bait and larger fish.

The key rule is:

**You can use fish you catch as bait to go after bigger fish.**

This changes how you should treat small fish. Selling everything immediately can feel good early, but keeping some fish gives you more flexibility for later fishing tasks. Bigger fish are usually more valuable, but they also require more careful reeling.

<h2 id="how-to-catch-bigger-fish-without-snapping-the-line">
  How to catch bigger fish without snapping the line
</h2>

The fishing mini-game gets stricter once bait and bigger fish are involved. If you simply mash confirm the whole time, you can lose the catch.

The safe rhythm is:

```text
Reel when the fish is calm.
Stop or ease off when the fish thrashes.
Watch the pattern.
Finish the catch when the button prompt appears.
```

The important part is the **thrashing phase**. When the fish resists, your line is under pressure. If you keep pulling at the wrong time, the line can snap.

<GuideTable
  caption="holoVillage fishing mini-game phases and the safest response for each one."
  columns={miniGameColumns}
  rows={miniGameRows}
/>

Do not treat bigger fish like basic carp. Carp teaches the hook and reel flow. Bait fish teach timing.

<h2 id="how-to-catch-koi">
  How to catch koi
</h2>

Koi appears early as the first step beyond the basic carp task. Kuro asks for a koi after introducing bait, which makes this a good checkpoint for whether you understand the bigger-fish system.

To catch koi:

1. Finish the first carp task.
2. Let Kuro introduce bait.
3. Attach bait at a valid fishing spot.
4. Start the fishing mini-game.
5. Reel carefully and stop pulling when the fish thrashes.

Do not assume koi means you need an endgame fishing area. In the early quest flow, koi is part of the first fishing progression. The real difficulty is learning how bait and the line-snap timing work.

<GuideCallout title="Koi tip">
If you are losing the fish, the problem is probably not the spot. It is usually the timing: stop forcing the reel while the fish is thrashing.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="fisher-rank-2-and-job-abilities">
  Fisher Rank 2 and job abilities
</h2>

After you clear Kuro's early fishing tasks, you are promoted to **Fisher Rank 2**.

This matters because Fisher Rank is not just a title. Rank 2 lets you raise your fishing job level further and start learning job abilities. A better fishing rod also becomes available for sale after this point.

In other words, fishing progression has three rewards:

- better access to fish
- job level growth
- job abilities and better tools

This is why you should not ignore fishing even if your main goal is money, villagers, or combat. Fishing feeds into multiple early-game systems.

<h2 id="freshwater-crab-and-the-blocked-bridge">
  Freshwater Crab and the blocked bridge
</h2>

After the first fishing tasks, Kuro eventually mentions a new fishing spot beyond a blocked bridge in the northern part of Hollow Grasslands. That route leads toward the Ancient Ruins, and the next fishing target is **Freshwater Crab**.

At this point, do not waste time searching the starting pond forever. The Freshwater Crab objective is tied to progression beyond the blocked bridge.

The basic flow is:

```text
Finish early fishing tasks
Reach Fisher Rank 2
Progress the bridge route
Enter the later ecosystem
Catch Freshwater Crab
```

If the bridge is still blocked, continue your main and adventuring progression. Kuro's hint is telling you where the next fishing stage is, not asking you to solve it from the starting village pond.

<h2 id="what-to-do-with-fish">
  What to do with fish
</h2>

Fish have several early uses:

- completing Kuro's fishing quests
- acting as bait for bigger fish
- selling for money
- cooking dishes once the kitchen system is available
- helping your early shop or restaurant economy later

This is why you should avoid selling every fish the moment you catch it. Selling trash is usually safe, but fish can become more useful when cooking and shop systems open.

A simple early rule:

```text
Keep a few small fish for bait.
Keep valuable fish until you know whether you need them for cooking or quests.
Sell extra fish when you need quick money.
```

Once you unlock the shop or restaurant, fish become more than a fishing reward. They become part of your early money loop.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you fish in holoVillage?

Talk to Kuro, craft a Wooden Rod, equip it, and stand near a fishing spot where fish gather. When the yellow exclamation mark appears, press confirm to start fishing.

### Why can't I fish in holoVillage?

You are probably not standing at a valid fishing spot. You cannot fish in every pond, river, or water tile. Equip the rod and look for a yellow exclamation mark near a fish-gathering spot.

### Where do you catch carp?

Carp is part of Kuro's first fishing task. Use the starting fishing spot in the village area and keep fishing until you catch three carp.

### How does bait work?

Bait lets you go after bigger fish. You can use fish you catch as bait, so do not sell every small fish immediately.

### How do you catch koi?

Finish the first carp task, attach bait when Kuro introduces it, then fish at a valid spot. Reel carefully and stop pulling when the fish thrashes so the line does not snap.

### Why does my fishing line snap?

The line can snap if you keep pulling while the fish is thrashing. Reel during calm windows, ease off during resistance, and finish the catch when the button prompt appears.

### What does Fisher Rank 2 unlock?

Fisher Rank 2 lets you raise your fishing job level further, learn job abilities, and buy a better fishing rod.

### Where is Freshwater Crab?

Freshwater Crab is tied to the later fishing objective beyond the blocked bridge in the northern Hollow Grasslands route toward the Ancient Ruins. If the bridge is still blocked, progress the main and adventuring tasks first.

### Should I sell fish early?

Sell extra fish if you need quick money, but keep some small fish for bait and save valuable fish until you know whether a quest, recipe, or cooking route needs them.

### Is this an all fish locations guide?

No. This guide focuses on early fishing mechanics: where to fish, how bait works, how to catch carp and koi, and how to progress toward Fisher Rank 2 and Freshwater Crab. A full fish database should be handled separately only if there is enough confirmed data.
