<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**In your first day of holoVillage: Our Cozy Days, focus on building a stable village routine instead of rushing one system.**

A clean early route is:

1. Place your first house.
2. Use the chest before your bag fills up.
3. Craft a **Wooden Axe**.
4. Craft a **Pickaxe** and keep a **Sword** ready.
5. Start farming with a field, hoe, seeds, and watering can.
6. Unlock fishing with Kuro and craft a **Wooden Rod**.
7. Start adventuring with Bubba.
8. Sell only surplus items while saving useful materials.
9. Build toward your first villager house.
10. Use quests whenever you do not know what to do next.

The best beginner mindset is simple: **farm, fish, gather, fight, sell carefully, sleep, and use that routine to unlock villagers and stores.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="what-to-do-first-in-holovillage">
  What to do first in holoVillage
</h2>

The opening hours of **holoVillage: Our Cozy Days** are not about doing one perfect thing. They are about building a repeatable routine.

The game starts by giving you a place in the village and then slowly introduces the systems you will use every day: crafting, gathering, farming, fishing, adventuring, selling, inviting villagers, and eventually opening stores.

Your first priority is not to get rich immediately.

Your first priority is to answer:

```text
What can I do every day that moves the village forward?
```

Early on, that means:

```text
build house
-> craft tools
-> gather materials
-> learn farming, fishing, and adventuring
-> earn money
-> invite villagers
-> unlock jobs and stores
```

That loop is the backbone of the early game.

<GuideCallout title="Best beginner rule">
When you feel lost, do not wander randomly.

Check your quests, then improve one part of your daily routine: tools, crops, fish, combat, villagers, or money.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="place-your-first-house">
  Place your first house
</h2>

After the intro, Nodoka and Mayor Kumarin ask you to help develop the village. Your first major action is placing your own house.

The house matters because it gives you a home base. It also connects to sleeping and storage, which become important almost immediately.

When placing your house, do not overthink the perfect layout. The early game is about function, not beauty. You can worry about perfect village design later once you understand how much space buildings, fields, stores, and villager houses need.

If you want a practical beginner placement, put your house somewhere close to the village office and early facilities. You will be running back and forth a lot.

---

<h2 id="use-your-chest-before-your-bag-fills-up">
  Use your chest before your bag fills up
</h2>

Your bag fills up quickly in holoVillage.

In the first hour, you can pick up wood, stone, ore, crops, fish, food, monster drops, tickets, potions, broken weapons, and other materials. It is very easy to start selling items just because you need space.

That is a mistake.

Use your chest early. Store materials that you do not understand yet, especially:

- ore and ingots
- broken weapons or staffs
- better fish
- crops you may need later
- food ingredients
- special drops
- crafting materials

Selling is still important, but early panic-selling can slow you down later.

If you are unsure whether an item matters, put it in storage first and sell surplus copies later.

<h2 id="craft-your-first-tools">
  Craft your first tools
</h2>

Crafting starts at the crafting table in the village office. Your first crafted tool is the **Wooden Axe**, but the early game quickly expands into other tools.

<GuideTable
  caption="holoVillage early tools and what each one unlocks."
  columns={earlyToolColumns}
  rows={earlyToolRows}
/>

The most important early tool is the Wooden Axe because wood is used constantly. You need it for crafting, building, fuel, and progression.

The Pickaxe is next because mining gives stone and ore, which lead into tool upgrades, gear, and the Smitty economy later.

The Sword matters because the areas outside the village are not just peaceful gathering zones. Enemies can appear, and combat becomes part of progression.

<GuideCallout title="Best tool rule">
Do not treat tools as optional side items.

Each tool unlocks a different part of your daily routine, and the village grows faster when all of those routines are active.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="start-farming-early">
  Start farming early
</h2>

Farming begins when you receive a field ticket and place a field in the village.

The basic loop is:

```text
place field
-> craft or use a hoe
-> till soil
-> plant seeds
-> water crops
-> harvest when ready
```

Watering is important because it helps crops grow faster. Rainy days can save you work, but do not rely on rain as your entire farming plan.

Crops are useful because they can become:

- direct money
- cooking ingredients
- quest items
- Restaurant stock
- villager job support

You do not need a huge farm immediately. A small stable farm is better than a giant field you forget to maintain.

If you are already working toward the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/shop-money-guide">Shop and Money Guide</Link> route, farming becomes one of your best Restaurant support systems.

---

<h2 id="unlock-fishing-with-kuro">
  Unlock fishing with Kuro
</h2>

Fishing is one of the strongest early systems because it gives you food, bait, cooking materials, and sellable items.

To start fishing, talk to **Kuro** and craft a **Wooden Rod**. The first fishing lesson teaches an important rule:

**You cannot fish anywhere.**

You need to find fishing spots where fish gather. When the rod is equipped and you are near a valid spot, the game gives you a prompt.

Early fishing objectives include catching carp, learning bait, catching koi, and raising Fisher Rank.

For the full breakdown, use the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/fishing-guide">holoVillage Fishing Guide</Link>. That page explains fishing spots, bait, line snapping, carp, koi, Fisher Rank, and the freshwater crab quest.

---

<h2 id="start-adventuring-with-bubba">
  Start adventuring with Bubba
</h2>

Adventuring begins with Bubba and introduces the combat side of holoVillage.

Your early combat goals are simple:

- learn how to swing your sword
- gather wood and stone
- fight basic enemies
- collect drops
- open chests when you find them
- use potions or food when needed
- prepare for gates and stronger errors later

Do not assume holoVillage is completely safe just because it looks cozy. The outside areas can hurt you, and early enemies can interrupt gathering if you are careless.

You do not need to grind combat nonstop on day one. But you should learn enough to survive and collect materials safely.

<h2 id="follow-main-job-and-daily-quests">
  Follow main, job, and daily quests
</h2>

Quests are your best guide when you are not sure what to do next.

<GuideTable
  caption="holoVillage quest types and what they teach in the early game."
  columns={questTypeColumns}
  rows={questTypeRows}
/>

The early game is built like a tutorial chain. Main quests unlock the next major village step. Job quests teach farming, fishing, and adventuring. Daily quests give you repeatable tasks so that each day has direction.

If you feel stuck, check:

```text
main quest
job quests
daily quest board
pinned quest details
```

This is especially useful when a system feels incomplete. Often the missing piece is not hidden. It is simply the next quest in the chain.

<h2 id="sell-items-carefully">
  Sell items carefully
</h2>

You need money early, but selling everything is not the same as making progress.

A safe early approach is:

```text
sell common surplus
store unknown materials
cook or craft when possible
save rare drops
keep some fish and food
```

This matters because many items have multiple uses. Fish can be bait, food, quest progress, or Restaurant stock. Ore can become tools, gear, or Smitty materials. Crops can be sold raw, cooked, or used for future objectives.

If you need quick money, sell simple surplus items. If you are not sure what something does, store it.

Once stores unlock, your money plan changes. At that point, read the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/shop-money-guide">Shop and Money Guide</Link> to decide whether Restaurant or Smitty fits your village better.

<h2 id="build-toward-your-first-villager-house">
  Build toward your first villager house
</h2>

The next major step after learning basic jobs is inviting villagers.

To invite a villager, you need a vacant villager house. That means you should start saving money and materials for your first villager house instead of spending everything as soon as you earn it.

Villagers matter because they are not just decoration. Once they move in, they can help with jobs such as fishing, farming, adventuring, and shopkeeping. They can also use supplies from the workbox.

If a visitor you want appears before you have a house ready, do not panic. Build your system first, then invite villagers properly once you have the house and points.

For details, read the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/how-to-invite-villagers">How to Invite Villagers guide</Link>.

<h2 id="what-to-prioritize-after-the-first-day">
  What to prioritize after the first day
</h2>

After your first day, your goal is to make your routine stronger.

<GuideTable
  caption="holoVillage early priorities after your first day."
  columns={earlyPriorityColumns}
  rows={earlyPriorityRows}
/>

The best second-day plan is usually:

```text
water crops
check quests
fish for food and money
gather wood and stone
mine ore
fight safely for drops
sell surplus items
save for villager houses
```

Once villagers and stores unlock, your routine becomes more powerful:

```text
villagers gather or work
-> you cook or craft
-> stores sell processed goods
-> money funds more village development
```

That is the real early-game progression loop.

<h2 id="early-upgrades-and-progression-goals">
  Early upgrades and progression goals
</h2>

Do not chase every upgrade immediately. Focus on upgrades that improve your daily loop.

Good early progression goals include:

- more reliable storage
- better tools
- first villager house
- job rank upgrades
- a useful fishing rod upgrade
- basic food or potion supply
- first Restaurant or Smitty
- stronger weapon or staff
- better armor before dangerous areas

Tool upgrades are especially important because they save time. Better tools make gathering smoother, and better gear makes adventuring safer.

A strong early village is not built by one lucky item. It is built by making every day easier than the last.

<h2 id="common-beginner-mistakes">
  Common beginner mistakes
</h2>

Most beginner problems come from treating holoVillage like a pure cozy sandbox or a pure combat game. It is both slower and more interconnected than that.

<GuideTable
  caption="Common holoVillage beginner mistakes and what to do instead."
  columns={commonMistakeColumns}
  rows={commonMistakeRows}
/>

The easiest way to avoid these mistakes is to keep one question in mind:

**What does this item or system feed into later?**

If the answer is unclear, store the item, follow your quest, and come back later.

---

<h2 id="best-early-game-loop">
  Best early-game loop
</h2>

The best beginner loop is:

```text
Morning:
- check crops
- water if needed
- check quests

Day:
- fish, farm, gather, mine, or fight based on current quest

Evening:
- craft tools or gear
- cook food if available
- sell surplus items
- store useful materials

Night:
- plan the next unlock
- sleep before pushing too late
```

This loop keeps you moving without forcing you into one activity forever.

When your village grows, the loop expands:

```text
villagers work
-> workbox supplies support jobs
-> shops sell processed goods
-> money funds new buildings
-> better tools and gear unlock deeper areas
```

That is when holoVillage starts to feel less like a tutorial and more like a working village.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in holoVillage?

Place your house, use storage, craft basic tools, then start farming, fishing, and adventuring through the early job quests.

### What is the best first tool to craft?

The Wooden Axe is the first key tool because wood is needed constantly for crafting, building, fuel, and progression.

### Can you fish anywhere in holoVillage?

No. You can only fish at valid fishing spots where fish gather. Equip your rod and look for the fishing prompt.

### Should I sell all my items early?

No. Sell surplus common items, but store materials you do not understand yet. Fish, ore, crops, broken weapons, and food can all become useful later.

### How do I know what to do next?

Check your main quests, job quests, and daily quests. The early game is designed to teach systems through quest chains.

### When should I build a villager house?

Build a villager house after you have a basic routine and enough money. A vacant house is required before you can invite a visitor to move in.

### What do villagers do?

Villagers can take jobs such as fishing, farming, adventuring, and shopkeeping. They can also use supplies from the workbox.

### Should I choose Restaurant or Smitty first?

Choose Restaurant if you are fishing, farming, and cooking often. Choose Smitty if you are mining and crafting equipment often.

### What happens if I stay up too late?

If you work too late into the night, you can faint and be sent to bed. Use late-night time carefully.

### Is holoVillage only a cozy farming game?

No. It has farming and fishing, but also crafting, combat, mining, job ranks, gates, equipment, stores, villagers, and village development.
