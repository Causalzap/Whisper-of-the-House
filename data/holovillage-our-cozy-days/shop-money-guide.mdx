<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best early way to make money in holoVillage: Our Cozy Days is to stop selling every raw item immediately and start turning your daily routine into a store loop.**

Early on, gather materials through **fishing, farming, adventuring, and mining**. Once stores unlock, choose between a **Restaurant** and a **Smitty**:

- Pick **Restaurant** first if you are catching fish, growing crops, and cooking often.
- Pick **Smitty** first if you are mining ore, crafting equipment, and replacing weapons or tools often.
- Set up your store with a **register, shelves, stock, and a sales clerk**.
- Use villagers to support the loop through jobs and shopkeeping.

For most new players, the safest early route is:

**Fish or farm -> cook food -> stock the Restaurant -> assign a sales clerk -> sell for bonus profit.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="how-money-works-in-holovillage">
  How money works in holoVillage
</h2>

Early money in **holoVillage: Our Cozy Days** starts simply: gather materials, sell what you do not need, and use the money to build more facilities.

That works at the start, but it is not the real economy.

The stronger early-game money loop begins when you stop treating every item as raw cash and start asking:

**Can this item become something more valuable first?**

A fish can be sold, but it can also become bait or food. A crop can be sold, but it can also become an ingredient. Ore can be sold, but it can also become tools, weapons, armor, or Smitty stock. A monster drop can look useless until it appears in a recipe.

The first big shift is:

```text
raw item -> processed item -> store sale
```

That is why the Restaurant and Smitty matter. They turn your normal daily actions into a repeatable village economy.

<GuideCallout title="Best early rule">
Do not sell everything just because your bag is full.

Sell the items you can replace easily. Save materials that are used for cooking, crafting, tools, gear, quests, and store stock.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="when-you-unlock-stores">
  When you unlock stores
</h2>

After you invite villagers and start developing the village, the game pushes you toward opening a store. At this point, the village needs more funds and more traffic, so the next objective becomes building either a **Restaurant** or a **Smitty**.

You do not have to treat this as a permanent one-time choice. The game makes it clear that you can build the other store later. The important decision is which one helps your current routine first.

If you are mostly fishing, farming, and cooking, the Restaurant usually feels better first.

If you are mostly mining, fighting, smelting, and crafting equipment, the Smitty may fit better.

  How to open a store
</h2>

To open a store, you need to buy the correct ticket and place the building, similar to how you place other village facilities.

Once the building exists, the work is not over. A shop needs a proper setup before it becomes useful.

You need:

- a **register**
- **shelving**
- goods to use as **stock**
- a villager assigned as a **sales clerk**

<h2 id="how-store-sales-and-bonus-profit-work">
  How store sales and bonus profit work
</h2>

Selling through a store is different from simply dumping items at a basic vendor.

Once the store is set up, selling through the correct shop can give **bonus profit** compared to normal selling. This is why the store system becomes one of your main income sources after it unlocks.

The idea is simple:

```text
normal selling = quick cash
store selling = better long-term village income
```

That does not mean every item should go into the store. It means the right items should go into the right store.

Use the Restaurant for food and dishes.

Use the Smitty for equipment and crafted gear.

Use direct selling only when you need quick cash or when the item is easy to replace.

---

<h2 id="how-the-restaurant-works">
  How the Restaurant works
</h2>

The Restaurant is built around cooking.

To cook, you need a **kitchen table**. Once you have one, you can combine ingredients into dishes. Dishes can be sold, and they can also be used as food buffs for adventuring.

This makes the Restaurant valuable because it gives your fish, crops, meat, eggs, and other ingredients two possible uses:

```text
sell cooked dishes for money
or
eat cooked dishes for temporary buffs
```

Cooked food is often more valuable than raw ingredients, especially when sold through the Restaurant. This is why fishing and farming pair so well with the Restaurant.

The best early Restaurant loop is:

```text
fish or farm
-> cook simple dishes
-> keep a few buff foods
-> stock the rest
-> sell through the Restaurant
```

If you already followed the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/fishing-guide">Fishing Guide</Link>, the Restaurant is the natural next step. Extra fish become money, bait, or food instead of just cluttering your bag.

<h2 id="cooking-tips-for-early-money">
  Cooking tips for early money
</h2>

Cooking is not just a flavor system. It changes the value of your materials.

A few early tips:

- Cook surplus fish instead of selling every fish raw.
- Keep some buff food for caves, bosses, or dangerous areas.
- Do not waste rare ingredients on low-value dishes unless you know the recipe is worth it.
- Try simple repeatable dishes first before experimenting with expensive ingredients.
- Pay attention to recipe memos when you find them outside the village.

Special recipes can be stronger and more valuable than normal dishes, but early on you do not need to solve every recipe. Your first goal is to create a repeatable money loop, not complete a cookbook.

<GuideCallout title="Best Restaurant rule">
If you can turn a common ingredient into food, try cooking before selling.

If the ingredient is rare or needed for a quest, save it until you know what it does.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-the-smitty-works">
  How the Smitty works
</h2>

The Smitty is the equipment side of your economy.

It connects to:

- mining
- ore and ingots
- weapon crafting
- armor crafting
- broken weapons and staffs
- enhancement materials
- selling crafted equipment

The Smitty is useful because equipment can be worth more than raw materials. It also supports your combat progression, since stronger weapons and armor make it easier to gather better materials.

<h2 id="should-you-sell-or-keep-enhanced-gear">
  Should you sell or keep enhanced gear?
</h2>

Be careful with enhanced gear.

The game explains that enhanced equipment can gain extra abilities, but the cost can be high. That makes enhanced gear better for personal use than quick profit most of the time.

A good early rule is:

```text
basic crafted gear = okay to sell
strong enhanced gear = usually keep
```

If a weapon, staff, armor piece, or accessory has a useful effect, think twice before selling it. Better gear helps you clear caves, survive stronger enemies, and gather materials faster. That can be worth more than the one-time sale price.

Sell duplicates, outdated weapons, and simple crafted gear first.

Keep anything that helps your current build.

<h2 id="best-early-money-loops">
  Best early money loops
</h2>

You do not need one perfect money method. You need a repeatable loop that matches your daily play.

  rows={moneyLoopRows}

The easiest beginner path is:

```text
Fishing Guide progress
-> spare fish
-> cook food
-> Restaurant stock
-> sales clerk sells it
```

The more combat-focused path is:

```text
mine ore
-> smelt materials
-> craft extra gear
-> sell through Smitty
```

The farming path is slower but stable:

```text
plant crops
-> water or let rain help
-> harvest
-> cook or sell surplus
```

All three loops are valid. The best one is the one your village can repeat without draining rare materials.

<h2 id="how-villagers-help-your-economy">
  How villagers help your economy
</h2>

Villagers are part of the money system.

After a villager moves in, you can assign jobs such as fishing, farming, adventuring, and shopkeeping. That means villagers can help create the materials your stores need or help run the store itself.

This connects directly with the villager system:

- fishing jobs can support Restaurant stock
- farming jobs can support cooking ingredients
- adventuring jobs can bring materials for crafting
- shopkeeping lets a villager help with sales
- the workbox can hold supplies villagers need

If you have not set this up yet, read the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/how-to-invite-villagers">How to Invite Villagers guide</Link> first.

The biggest upgrade to your economy is not one expensive item. It is turning villagers into a supply chain.

<h2 id="early-store-build-order">
  Early store build order
</h2>

A clean early build order looks like this:

```text
1. Build your own house
2. Start farming, fishing, and adventuring
3. Build your first villager house
4. Invite a villager
5. Assign jobs and use the workbox
6. Unlock the store objective
7. Choose Restaurant or Smitty based on your inventory
8. Set up register, shelves, stock, and sales clerk
9. Sell processed goods through the store
10. Build the second store later
```

This keeps your village from feeling scattered. Each step feeds the next one.

Fishing gives food.

Farming gives crops.

Adventuring gives drops.

Mining gives ore.

Villagers give labor.

Stores turn that labor into money.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best early way to make money in holoVillage?

The best early method is to gather repeatable materials, process them when possible, and sell them through the correct store. Fish and crops pair well with the Restaurant. Ore and crafted gear pair better with the Smitty.

### Should I choose Restaurant or Smitty first?

Choose Restaurant first if you are fishing, farming, and cooking often. Choose Smitty first if you are mining and crafting equipment often. You can build the other store later.

### How do I open a store?

Progress through village development until the store objective appears, buy the correct store ticket, place the building, then set up the register, shelves, stock, and a sales clerk.

### Why is my store not making money?

Check whether you have stocked items and assigned a villager as a sales clerk. A store building alone is not enough.

### What should I sell early?

Sell common surplus items, trash, extra cooked food, and replaceable goods. Be careful with ore, broken weapons, better fish, rare drops, and materials used in recipes or upgrades.

### Are cooked dishes better than raw ingredients?

Usually, yes. Cooked dishes can sell for more than raw ingredients and can also provide food buffs for adventuring.

### Should I sell all my fish?

No. Some fish are useful for cooking, bait, quests, or later money loops. Sell your surplus, but keep a small reserve.

### Is enhanced gear good for selling?

Not usually early on. Enhanced gear can be expensive to make and is often better for personal use. Sell basic duplicates or outdated gear first.

### Do villagers help with money?

Yes. Villagers can support your economy through jobs and shopkeeping. They can help gather materials or run the store, depending on how you assign them.

### Can I build both Restaurant and Smitty?

Yes. The game indicates that you can build the other store later, so your first choice is about early priority, not a permanent lockout.
