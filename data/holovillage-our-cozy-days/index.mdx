<h2 id="early-game-progression-route">
  Early-game progression route
</h2>

holoVillage is easiest to understand as a chain of connected systems.

You are not just farming. You are not just fishing. You are not just inviting cute villagers. Each system feeds into the next one:

```text
tools give resources
resources build facilities
facilities unlock villagers
villagers work jobs
jobs create materials
materials become food, gear, or shop stock
money funds more village development
combat unlocks deeper progression
```

<GuideTable
  caption="holoVillage early-game progression route."
  columns={earlyGameFlowColumns}
  rows={earlyGameFlowRows}
/>

This is why the best early strategy is balanced. If you ignore fishing, you miss food and bait. If you ignore villagers, you lose job support. If you ignore shops, your money loop stays weak. If you ignore combat, gates and materials can block you later.

<h2 id="shop-and-money-guide">
  Shop and Money Guide
</h2>

Read the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/shop-money-guide">Shop and Money Guide</Link> when you start wondering what to sell and how to make more money.

The early money system changes once stores unlock.

At first, selling raw items is enough. Later, the better loop is:

```text
gather materials
-> cook or craft
-> stock the right store
-> assign a sales clerk
-> sell for better profit
```

The money guide explains:

- when stores unlock
- Restaurant vs Smitty
- how to open a store
- register, shelves, stock, and sales clerk
- bonus profit from store sales
- cooking and food buffs
- equipment crafting and the Smitty
- what to sell and what to keep early
- how villagers support your economy

For most new players, Restaurant first is the safer choice if you are already fishing, farming, and cooking. Smitty first makes more sense if you spend most of your time mining and crafting gear.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Can you play holoVillage offline?

holoVillage: Our Cozy Days may require an online connection depending on the current build and Steam behavior. If the game does not start offline, reconnect to the internet, launch the game once, then check the latest Steam notes or community reports before assuming offline play is fully supported.

### Why does holoVillage have an always-online requirement?

Some players may run into online-check behavior when launching the game. If you see an always-online issue, treat it as build-specific behavior: launch while connected, check for updates, and watch the Steam community or patch notes for changes. This hub does not assume a permanent offline or online-only status unless the developers confirm it.

### Why is holoVillage stuck at the opening loading screen?

If holoVillage is stuck at the opening loading screen, restart the game first. Then verify the game files through Steam, launch with an internet connection, update your graphics driver, and check whether a new patch has been released. If the issue started after an update, wait for community reports before deleting saves or reinstalling.

### Does holoVillage have Steam Cloud saves?

Steam Cloud support can vary by build and Steam configuration. If you do not see your save syncing between devices, treat your save as local for now and avoid switching PCs without checking Steam Cloud status on the game's Steam page or in your Steam library properties.

### Does holoVillage support controllers?

holoVillage includes controller-style prompts and basic control options, but controller behavior can depend on your device and Steam Input settings. If your controller is not working correctly, enable Steam Input, reconnect the controller before launching the game, or switch temporarily to keyboard and mouse.

### Does holoVillage support ultrawide monitors?

Ultrawide behavior may depend on your resolution, display mode, and the current build. If the image looks stretched, cropped, or forced into windowed mode, try a standard 16:9 resolution first, then check whether a later patch improves ultrawide support.

### Why is my villager stuck in holoVillage?

If a villager appears stuck, leave the area, sleep until the next day, or return to the title screen and reload your save. If the villager is assigned to a job, also check the workbox and job assignment in case they are waiting on supplies or a valid task.

### Why can't I invite a visitor?

You need a vacant villager house before inviting a visitor. If a visitor appears before you have a house ready, keep progressing and invite them later once you have enough points and an empty house. Read the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/how-to-invite-villagers">How to Invite Villagers guide</Link> for the full system.

### Why can't I fish in some water?

You cannot fish everywhere in holoVillage. Equip your fishing rod and look for valid fishing spots where fish gather. If no prompt appears, that water is probably not a usable fishing spot. Read the <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/fishing-guide">Fishing Guide</Link> for bait, carp, koi, and Fisher Rank help.

### What should I do first in holoVillage?

Start by placing your house, using storage, crafting basic tools, then unlocking farming, fishing, and adventuring through the early job quests. After that, save for villager houses and build toward stores. The <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/beginner-guide">Beginner Guide</Link> has the full first-day route.

### Should I sell everything early?

No. Sell surplus common items, but save fish, ore, crops, broken weapons, food, and strange materials until you know whether they are used for crafting, cooking, bait, quests, or store stock. The <Link href="/holovillage-our-cozy-days/shop-money-guide">Shop and Money Guide</Link> explains what to sell and what to keep.
