<h2 id="how-villagers-work-in-holovillage">
  How villagers work in holoVillage
</h2>

Villagers in **holoVillage: Our Cozy Days** are not just collectible characters. They are part of your early progression loop.

The basic chain is:

```text
Build a house -> meet visitors -> gain points -> invite a villager -> wait until the next morning -> assign jobs
```

This is why the villager system can feel confusing at first. The game may show you visitors before you are ready to recruit them. Seeing a visitor does not mean you can instantly add them to the village.

The most important rule is simple:

**A visitor needs an empty villager house before they can move in.**

Once a villager actually lives in your village, they can help with jobs like fishing, farming, adventuring, and later shopkeeping. That makes villager recruitment one of the first systems you should understand after fishing and basic resource gathering.

<GuideCallout title="Best early rule">
Do not chase every visitor immediately. Build a vacant house first, then use points to invite the villager you actually want.
</GuideCallout>

---

<h2 id="why-you-cant-invite-villagers-at-the-start">
  Why you can't invite villagers at the start
</h2>

If you meet a visitor early and the game will not let you invite them, the likely reason is that you do not have a **vacant villager house** yet.

The opening hours focus on your own setup first:

- build your first house
- learn crafting
- gather basic materials
- start farming, fishing, and adventuring
- save enough money and resources
- build a house for a new villager

Only after that does the invite system start to make sense.

A visitor can appear before you have the right house. That does not mean you failed the game or permanently missed them. It usually means you need to keep progressing until you can build housing and gather enough invite points.

<h2 id="how-to-build-a-villager-house">
  How to build a villager house
</h2>

To build a villager house, progress until the game points you toward inviting people into the village. The game explains that you need houses before you can invite residents, then sends you back into the normal resource loop: gather materials, sell items, and buy the right construction ticket.

The process is:

1. Follow the main quest until villagers are introduced.
2. Gather materials through farming, fishing, and adventuring.
3. Save enough money for a villager house ticket.
4. Buy or receive the correct ticket.
5. Place the house in an open spot in your village.
6. Use that vacant house to invite a visitor.

<GuideCallout title="Placement tip">
Leave extra room around your early buildings. You will need space for more houses, fields, stores, and work facilities later.
</GuideCallout>

If you cannot place the house, check three things:

- the area may be blocked by trees, buildings, or terrain
- you may be using the wrong ticket
- you may need to progress a village objective first

Do not spend all your early money on random facilities before you understand what the current main quest wants. Villager houses and store buildings can get expensive as your village grows.

<h2 id="visitors-exchange-points-and-invite-points">
  Visitors, exchange points, and invite points
</h2>

Visitors are the characters who appear in your village before they become full residents. Talk to them whenever you can, even if you are not ready to invite them yet.

Talking to visitors gives you points. Depending on the menu and translation, you may see this described as **exchange points** or **invite points**. The purpose is the same: points help you target a specific visitor instead of waiting for random appearances forever.

A safe way to think about the system is:

```text
Random visitor appears -> talk to them -> gain points -> use enough points to invite a desired visitor
```

Some visitors may require more points than others. The cost can also vary depending on the character or stage of progression, so always check the actual number shown in the menu before planning around a fixed price.

<GuideCallout title="Do this even if you are not ready">
If a visitor you like appears before you have a house, still talk to them. You may gain points that help you invite them later.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="when-invited-villagers-move-in">
  When invited villagers move in
</h2>

Invited villagers move in **the following morning**.

That means you should not expect the house to fill instantly after sending the invitation. Once you confirm the invite, finish your day, sleep, and return to the village in the morning.

When the new villager arrives, the game plays a short welcome scene. After that, the villager becomes part of your village and can be assigned work.

This timing is useful to remember because it affects your planning:

- invite before sleeping if you want the villager tomorrow
- do not assume the invite failed just because nothing changes immediately
- check job options after the move-in scene, not before

<h2 id="what-the-workbox-does">
  What the workbox does
</h2>

The **workbox** is the shared supply box villagers use for jobs.

The game specifically points out that you can store supplies such as seeds for villagers to use. This matters most for jobs like farming, where the villager cannot do useful work without the right materials.

Think of the workbox like this:

```text
Your inventory = items for you
Workbox = supplies for villagers
```

If a villager is assigned to a job but does not seem to be producing much, check whether the workbox has the supplies they need.

For farming, that usually means seeds. For fishing or adventuring, the result may be checked later rather than appearing instantly on screen.

<GuideCallout title="Workbox rule">
Assigning a job is only half the system. If the job needs supplies, put those supplies in the workbox.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-if-the-villager-you-want-appears-too-early">
  What if the villager you want appears too early?
</h2>

This can happen: a visitor you want may appear before you have a vacant house or enough points.

Do not panic.

Visitors rotate, and the point system exists so you are not forced to rely only on lucky timing. If someone appears too early, talk to them if possible, keep progressing, then invite them later once you have the housing and points required.

The worst response is to spend randomly or rush a messy village layout just because a visitor appeared once. A cleaner plan is:

```text
Talk to the visitor -> keep earning points -> build the house -> invite when ready
```

This is especially important if you are waiting for a specific hololive talent. Do not turn the page into a checklist of every character. Focus on the invite system first.

<h2 id="best-early-villager-jobs">
  Best early villager jobs
</h2>

For most players, the best early job order is:

1. **Fishing** if you want food, bait, and easy selling/cooking materials.
2. **Farming** if you are planting enough crops to justify the workbox setup.
3. **Adventuring** if you are actively pushing combat and need more outside materials.
4. **Shopkeeping** once you unlock stores and need a sales clerk.

Fishing is the easiest job to understand early because fish feed directly into several systems: bait, cooking, selling, and quests. Farming can be just as strong, but it asks you to manage seeds and workbox supplies. Adventuring is better once you have a clearer reason to gather combat materials.

If your goal is early money, the villager system connects naturally into stores later. Villagers can help gather the raw materials, and your Restaurant or Smitty can turn those materials into better profits once the shop system opens.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you invite villagers in holoVillage?

Build a vacant villager house, talk to visitors to gain exchange or invite points, then use the invite option when you have enough points or when the visitor is available. The villager moves in the next morning.

### Why can't I invite a visitor?

You probably do not have a vacant villager house yet. A visitor can appear before you are ready to recruit them.

### Do villagers move in immediately?

No. After you invite a villager, they move in the following morning. Sleep and check the village the next day.

### What are exchange points or invite points?

They are points earned by talking to visitors. You can use them to invite a specific visitor instead of waiting only for random visits.

### What happens if the villager I want appears before I have a house?

Talk to them if possible, then keep progressing. Visitors can return, and points help you invite specific villagers later.

### What jobs can villagers do?

Early jobs include fishing, farming, and adventuring. Later, villagers can also help with shop systems such as sales clerk work.

### What does the workbox do?

The workbox stores supplies for villagers to use while working. For farming, put seeds in the workbox so the assigned villager has materials to use.

### Should I assign my first villager to fishing or farming?

Fishing is usually easier early because fish can be used for bait, cooking, quests, and selling. Farming is good if you already have seeds and enough fields to justify the setup.

### Can I invite a specific hololive talent?

Yes, the invite system lets you target specific visitors once you have the required points and housing. Exact point costs can vary, so check the in-game menu.

### Is this an all-villagers list?

No. This guide focuses on how the villager invitation and job systems work. For an early-game progression cluster, this is more useful than a full character database.
