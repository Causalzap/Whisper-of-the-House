<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**For your first Everything Is Crab clear, use a build that has five parts: safe food, one main attack, movement, sustain, and a safe fight location.**

A minimum first-clear setup looks like this:

**Cheek Pouch or feeding distance → Trunk / Horns / Jaws / Body Slam as your main attack → Legs or speed → Shell / Plating / regeneration → safe boss terrain and low Carcinisation.**

Do not force every shiny mutation. If you die before the first boss, fix food and movement. If Trunk feels weak, balance Physical and Ability. If you die after taking crab points, you took Carcinisation too early. If you die after your first clear, check the Codex and push one goal at a time.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="cheek-pouch">
  Cheek Pouch: capacity, trigger, and why it is strong
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/everything-is-crab/everything-is-crab-cheek-pouch-capacity-tooltip.webp"
    alt="Player checking the Cheek Pouch tooltip showing food storage and capacity 2 in Everything Is Crab."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Cheek Pouch has a capacity of 2 and lets stored food keep being consumed while you move.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

**Cheek Pouch stores nearby food inside your cheeks. Stored food is consumed at the same rate, and the tooltip shows a capacity of 2.**

That means Cheek Pouch is not just a convenience upgrade. It changes how safely you can level. Instead of stopping for every food pickup, you can store food, keep moving, and let the food convert into progress while avoiding enemies.

The reason it is so strong is simple:

**food becomes less dangerous.**

Cheek Pouch is especially valuable when your run is underleveled, when enemies are forcing you away from food piles, or when you are using a slower attack like Trunk and need more time to reposition.

**Trunk is a swiping, slow hybrid attack. Its tooltip shows 1-1.4x damage based on Physical or Ability, using the lowest value. It has a 1.5s cooldown and gives +0.66 Feeding Distance.**

This makes Trunk very different from a pure physical attack. If your Physical damage is high but Ability is low, Trunk is held back by Ability. If your Ability is high but Physical is low, Trunk is held back by Physical.

The basic Trunk build logic is:

**balance Physical and Ability → add size / attack area → reduce cooldown or improve tempo → add movement → use the +0.66 feeding distance to level more safely.**

  rows={trunkRows}

Trunk works best when it is both your damage tool and your food-routing tool. The +0.66 feeding distance matters because it lets you collect food from safer positions, which pairs well with Cheek Pouch, Tail / Prehensile Tail, and movement speed.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How many food slots does Cheek Pouch have?

Cheek Pouch has a capacity of 2. It stores nearby food inside your cheeks, and stored food is consumed at the same rate while you keep moving.

### What is Trunk's exact damage multiplier?

Trunk deals 1-1.4x damage based on Physical or Ability, using whichever value is lower. It also has a 1.5s cooldown and gives +0.66 Feeding Distance.

### What is the easiest first-clear build?

Use Cheek Pouch or feeding distance, one main attack, movement speed, Shell / Plating / HP, and low Carcinisation. Trunk hybrid and big physical builds are the easiest early routes to understand.

### What should I do if I die before the first boss?

Fix food and movement first. Take Cheek Pouch, feeding distance, speed, or a simple main attack before chasing rare evolutions.

### What should I do if I die to bosses?

Enter the fight with one attack that hits reliably, movement speed, HP or defense, and safer terrain. Do not fight bosses in water, snow, heat, or cramped areas if you can move away first.

### What should I do if I die after taking crab points?

Take fewer Carcinisation points. Stay at 0-1 while learning, push 2-3 only when stable, and save 4+ for strong runs or challenge goals.

### Is Pacifist good for beginners?

Pacifist is not a good first-clear route. It becomes useful after you understand food routing, movement, boss patterns, and non-combat scaling.

### Is Scavenger good?

Yes, Scavenger-style XP value becomes stronger when runs are longer, but you still need one attack or survival plan for bosses.

### How do I unlock Stinger?

Stinger is tied to dealing over 200 poison damage in a single run. Build poison early and add enough survival for poison damage to stack.

### What should I do after my first clear?

Check the Codex, read new genetics, push one Pressure level with a stable build, and pick one unlock target instead of testing everything at once.
