<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best builds in Everything Is Crab are not just lists of good evolutions. They are routes with timing.**

For a first clear, the safest route is **Cheek Pouch Safe Scaling** or **Trunk Hybrid**. For simple damage, use **Big Physical**. If the game gives you HP, Shell, Plating, Revenge, and Body Slam, pivot into **Body Slam Tank**. If you see Spines, Poisonous, Detachable, HP, and defense, go **Spines / Reflect Poison**. Social Pack is strong only when charm tools, pack damage, ally scaling, and a backup boss attack appear together.

Use this rule:

**Evolutions 1-3 decide your direction. Evolutions 4-6 confirm the build. Before the first boss, you need one attack, movement, and survival.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="trunk-hybrid-build">
  Trunk Hybrid build
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

    alt="Player checking the Trunk tooltip showing hybrid damage, cooldown, and feeding distance in Everything Is Crab."

**Trunk Hybrid** is one of the best builds because Trunk solves two problems at once: it gives you a wide swiping attack and improves feeding distance.

The mistake is building it like a pure physical weapon. Trunk uses the lower value between Physical and Ability, so the weaker stat limits the attack. Check the key numbers table above if you need the exact multiplier, cooldown, and feeding-distance value.

<GuideTable
  caption="Trunk Hybrid pieces and timing."
  columns={trunkColumns}
  rows={trunkRows}
/>

A strong Trunk route is:

**Trunk → balance Physical and Ability → add Mass / Alpha / size → add cooldown help → add movement → add Cheek Pouch or Tail for safer food.**

If Trunk does not appear early, do not wait forever. If the game gives you Mass, Alpha, HP, or Physical damage, pivot to Big Physical or Body Slam Tank and keep Trunk as a possible later bonus.

**Body Slam Tank** becomes strong when your run already wants HP.

Do not force Body Slam on a fragile build. Its damage scales with **Max HP**, so the build needs bulk before the attack becomes a real win condition. Use the key numbers table above when you need the exact damage and cooldown.

The ideal Body Slam route is:

**HP / Mass / Shell / Plating → Body Slam → Revenge → cooldown support → movement → final boss defense.**

This build is best when the game keeps giving you tank pieces. If you are not seeing HP or defense, Body Slam is probably a trap pick.

**Big Physical** is the easiest damage route to understand because it does not need hybrid stat balancing, poison timing, or charm setup.

Start with one physical attack or size piece. Then add Physical, Mass, Alpha, HP, attack area, and enough movement to keep your body from becoming a liability.

The clean route is:

**physical attack → Mass / Alpha → Physical damage → HP → attack area / reach → movement → Shell / Plating → Body Slam if HP is high.**

Big Physical is also one of the best pivot routes. If you wanted Trunk but the game keeps giving you Mass, Alpha, HP, and Physical damage, stop waiting and become a big physical build. If Body Slam appears after HP support, pivot into Body Slam Tank.

A Social stat by itself is not a build. The build starts when you have a charm trigger, ally support, or pack damage that converts allies into real value.

**Tail / charm tool → Social support → pack damage → ally count scaling → backup attack → defense / movement.**

The main weakness is bosses. Allies and charm can be excellent in normal waves, but boss fights may still require personal damage. If you do not have a backup attack before the first boss, do not go all-in on Social yet.

<h2 id="cheek-pouch-safe-scaling">
  Cheek Pouch Safe Scaling
</h2>

Cheek Pouch is covered in detail in the main <Link href="/everything-is-crab#cheek-pouch">Everything Is Crab guide</Link>, so this section focuses only on build timing.

Cheek Pouch Safe Scaling is not a damage build. It is a **tempo build**. It gives you safer food, which gives you more evolution choices, which lets you choose the real damage route later.

The correct route is:

**Cheek Pouch → simple attack → movement → defense → choose Trunk, physical, Body Slam, poison, or Social based on what appears.**

Do not stay in “food build” mode forever. By the first boss, Cheek Pouch should already be helping you reach a real combat route.

<h2 id="recommended-build-order">
  Recommended build order for new players
</h2>

If you are not sure what to play, use this order:

1. **Cheek Pouch Safe Scaling**  
   Learn food tempo and how to reach more evolution choices.

2. **Trunk Hybrid**  
   Learn hybrid scaling, feeding distance, cooldown timing, and size.

3. **Big Physical**  
   Learn simple damage, body size, attack area, and boss positioning.

4. **Body Slam Tank**  
   Learn HP scaling, defense, contact range, and tank mistakes.

5. **Spines / Reflect Poison**  
   Learn contact punishment, poison contact, dodge triggers, and sustain.

6. **Social Pack**  
   Learn charm, allies, pack damage, and backup boss planning.

This order works because each build teaches a different part of the game without requiring perfect RNG.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best build in Everything Is Crab?

For first clears, the best build is usually Cheek Pouch Safe Scaling into Trunk Hybrid or Big Physical. For safer boss fights, Body Slam Tank is strong if you already have HP and defense.

### What is the easiest beginner build?

Cheek Pouch Safe Scaling is the easiest opener because it fixes food tempo. You still need to turn that extra progress into a real attack before bosses.

### What is the best damage build?

Trunk Hybrid and Big Physical are the easiest damage builds to recommend. Trunk gives hybrid damage and feeding distance, while Big Physical gives simple scaling through size and Physical damage.

### What are Trunk's important numbers?

Trunk deals 1-1.4x damage using Physical or Ability, whichever is lower. It has a 1.5s cooldown and gives +0.66 Feeding Distance.

### Where should I check Body Slam's numbers?

Check the <Link href="#confirmed-build-numbers">Key build numbers</Link> section for Body Slam's damage, HP scaling, and cooldown. Then use the Body Slam Tank section to decide whether your current run has enough HP, Mass, Shell, or Plating to support it.

### Is Body Slam good?

Body Slam is good when you already have HP, Mass, Shell, Plating, or other tank pieces. Do not force it on a fragile build.

### Is Spines good?

Spines is good when you have survivability. Spines Lv. 4 can return +180% damage on contact impacts, but the build still needs HP, Plating, Shell, Revenge, regeneration, or movement.

### How does Poisonous work with Spines?

Poisonous applies a poison stack on contact impacts taken and deals 0.3x Ability Damage. It works best when you can survive contact and have enough Ability scaling.

### What does Detachable do?

Detachable makes your Spines trigger on Dodge. This is strong when you are already using Spines, movement, dodge, poison, or reflect support.

### Is Social Pack good?

Social Pack can be good, but only when charm tools, ally support, pack damage, and a backup attack appear together. Do not go all-in on Social if you have no boss plan.

### When should I pivot builds?

If your first three evolutions suggest one route but choices four to six support a different route, pivot before the first boss. For example, if Trunk never appears but HP and Body Slam support do, move into Body Slam Tank.

### What should I build for Pressure levels?

For early Pressure, use Trunk Hybrid, Big Physical, or Body Slam Tank. For Pressure 6 and higher, value movement, sustain, and boss consistency more than normal-run greed.
