<h2 id="how-achievements-and-challenges-work">
  How achievements and challenges work
</h2>

Achievements and challenges in **Everything Is Crab** are not all the same type of goal. Some are natural progression goals, some are build-routing goals, some are numeric thresholds, and some are collection goals.

The most important rule is:

**one run should have one main goal.**

If you try to farm poison, push Pressure, collect cosmetics, force Pacifist, stack Carcinisation, and beat the final boss all in the same run, you will usually build something unfocused.

<h2 id="poison-goals-and-stinger">
  Poison goals and Stinger unlocks
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/everything-is-crab/everything-is-crab-achievements-stinger-poison-unlock.webp"
    alt="Player checking Stinger, which unlocks after dealing over 200 poison damage in a single Everything Is Crab run."
    width={1600}

    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Stinger is one of the clearest early poison unlocks because it gives poison routing a specific target.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

**Stinger** is the cleanest poison route because it gives poison builds a specific target instead of a vague “use poison” objective. The exact trigger is listed in the known unlock table above, so this section focuses on how to route the run.

The mistake is treating poison like instant burst. Poison needs time and enemy uptime. Your job is to apply poison early, keep the run stable, and avoid dying before the damage total matters.

For this route, the unique priorities are:

- get a poison source early,
- keep enemies alive long enough for poison damage to tick,
- avoid overbuilding fragile damage,
- use mutagen and rerolls on poison pieces only when they move the target forward,
- keep enough personal boss damage so the run does not stall.

<GuideCallout title="Stinger route">
For Stinger-style goals, the known threshold matters more than generic poison advice. Build toward the target, then stabilize the run so you can finish instead of dying after the unlock attempt.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="mutagen-and-rerolls">
  Mutagen, rarity, and rerolls for unlock farming
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/everything-is-crab/everything-is-crab-achievements-mutagen-reroll.webp"
    alt="Player learning that starred enemies give mutagen points for rarity upgrades and rerolls in Everything Is Crab."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Mutagen points are especially useful during unlock runs because they let you chase the pieces your goal actually needs.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

When you are farming an unlock, mutagen is more important than usual because it controls how often you can fix the route.

Use mutagen when it does one of three things:

1. finds the required goal piece,
2. upgrades the required goal piece,
3. fixes the weakness that would make the goal fail.

Do not reroll only because an option is not legendary. A normal poison, reflect, Social, food, or survival upgrade that supports the goal can be better than a rare option that sends the run in a new direction.

<GuideCallout title="Reroll rule">
In an achievement run, the best reroll is the one that moves you closer to the target. Rarity is secondary.
</GuideCallout>

Reflect goals are tempting because they sound passive: let enemies hit you and return damage. The real route is more controlled than that.

Use **Spines** as the identity piece, then build the run around surviving the contact that triggers the value. That means Plating, Revenge, Shell, HP, regeneration, and movement all matter because they keep the reflect route from becoming a suicide route.

<GuideCallout title="Reflect rule">
Reflect is not an excuse to stop dodging. It is bonus damage for a build that is already durable enough to survive contact.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="pacifist-runs">
  Pacifist runs and low-damage goals
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/everything-is-crab/everything-is-crab-achievements-pacifist-genetic.webp"
    alt="Player hovering the Pacifist genetic before starting an Everything Is Crab run."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Pacifist-style runs are much easier after you already understand food, movement, enemy patterns, and boss survival.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Pacifist is not just “normal mode with worse damage.” The important mechanic is that you can route around **outlasting** a boss instead of killing it. If the boss gives up, it can drop special fruit that supports a different evolution path.

That route has a cost: by not killing normally, you can lose meat and DNA / mutagen-style resources, which means fewer rerolls and less upgrade control later. Build around food access, feeding speed, feeding distance, movement, HP, defense, regeneration, Social tools, and indirect value from the start.

<GuideCallout title="Pacifist rule">
Outlasting the boss is a real strategy, not a failure to kill fast enough. The tradeoff is resource control: fewer meat and DNA / reroll drops can make the next stage harder if the build is unfocused.
</GuideCallout>

Pressure goals are the long-term challenge path.

**Pressure 7 and above is the point where boss difficulty, environmental pressure, and build gaps start stacking more noticeably.** Before that, weak routes may still survive through food luck or safe bosses; after that, missing movement, damage, or defense becomes much harder to hide.

The safest way to approach Pressure is to stop treating it as a place for random experiments. Use stable builds first. Once you can clear consistently, then start adding more specific achievement goals.

For Pressure clears, the unique question is not “what is the strongest evolution?” It is “what removes the most failure points?” Usually that means food safety, one reliable attack, movement, defense, boss preparation, and cautious Carcinisation.

Final boss and Cataclysm goals should be treated as full-run goals because you cannot fix a bad endgame build at the final boss.

A good final boss route answers four questions:

1. Can I damage the boss reliably?
2. Can I move when Cataclysm starts?
3. Can I survive one or two mistakes?
4. Did Carcinisation add more danger than my build can handle?

During Cataclysm, do not chase unsafe damage just because the boss is low. The achievement route is to finish the fight, not to prove the last hit was greedy.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Is this a complete all achievements guide?

No. This is an early achievements and challenges guide. It focuses on practical routes, known triggers, and high-value challenge paths rather than pretending every achievement threshold is already documented.

### What should I unlock first in Everything Is Crab?

Get a stable clear first. After that, push early Pressure, check the Codex, test new genetics, then start farming specific poison, reflect, Social, boss, or challenge goals.

### How do I unlock Stinger?

Deal over 200 poison damage in a single run. Detachable Tail damage does not count, so use a real poison route and keep the run alive long enough for poison damage to stack.

### Can you skip killing bosses in a Pacifist run?

Yes. Pacifist-style routing can focus on outlasting a boss until it gives up, but the tradeoff is losing normal meat and DNA / reroll resources.

### What is the best Social achievement target?

The clearest Social target is having 12 charmed allies at the same time. Tail, Tail Wag, Alpha, Antennae, and Social stacking are the main route pieces.

### Are social builds good for bosses?

Yes, but they still need a backup plan. Allies can split pressure and add value, but bosses may still require personal damage, movement, and survival.

### Are Pacifist runs good for beginners?

No. Pacifist-style runs are better after you understand food, movement, boss patterns, and safe scaling.

### Should I take Carcinisation during challenge runs?

Only if the run is already stable. Do not take Carcinisation while behind, low on HP, slow, underleveled, or about to fight a boss unprepared.

### Do Supporter Pack cosmetics affect gameplay?

No. The Supporter Pack cosmetics are visual only. They do not improve survival, progression, stats, or achievement routing.

### Are all cosmetics paid DLC?

No. The Supporter Pack contains paid Ghost Crab cosmetics, but the game also has free cosmetics unlockable through normal gameplay.
