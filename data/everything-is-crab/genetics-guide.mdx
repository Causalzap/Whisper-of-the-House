<h2 id="what-are-genetics">
  What are Genetics?
</h2>

Genetics are starting modifiers that change how a run begins. Some give early power, some change enemy pressure, some alter branch timing, and some push you toward unusual challenge routes.

The important part is that Genetics are **not** the whole build. A Genetic shapes the opener. Your level-up evolutions still decide whether the run becomes poison, Chonky, Predator, POI farming, Social, or something else.

<GuideCallout title="Genetics rule">
Pick Genetics for the first six evolutions, not for the fantasy final build. If the Genetic does not help you reach a stable attack, food plan, movement tool, or survival layer before the first boss, it is probably too greedy.
</GuideCallout>

Use the <Link href="/everything-is-crab/evolutions-list">Evolution Picker</Link> once the run starts. Use <Link href="/everything-is-crab/best-builds">Best Builds</Link> when you need full route plans.

<h2 id="how-splice-works">
  How Splice works
</h2>

Splice lets you start with two Genetics instead of one, but only if your previous run reached the first boss. If the previous run ended before the first boss, you only choose one Genetic. Challenge Mode does not provide Splice opportunities.

When Splice is available, the selection happens in two rounds: you see three Genetic options, choose one, then see another three options and choose a second. Both Genetics are active, so the run becomes stronger but also more complex.

A good Splice pair has one of these patterns:

- **One power Genetic + one stabilizer.**
- **Two Genetics that support the same build route.**
- **One unlock / challenge Genetic + one consistency Genetic.**

A bad Splice pair gives you two unrelated problems. For example, Spawn of Chaos asks you to pivot from random stats, while Minimalist removes Dash. That can work, but it is dangerous if you start in bad terrain with low HP.

<GuideCallout title="Splice rule">
Do not Splice two Genetics just because both are strong. Splice them because they make the same first-boss plan easier. If you died before the first boss last run, treat the next run as a single-Genetic recovery run.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="unlock-priority">
  Which Genetics should you unlock first?
</h2>

Do not chase the hardest Genetics first. Unlock the ones that make future runs easier, then use those to chase the weird challenge names.

Recommended order:

1. **Chosen** — win any run, then use the early Legendary spike.
2. **Pioneer** — discover 7 POIs, then use extra POIs for safer routing.
3. **Expert** — select 5 Specializations, then reach build payoffs sooner.
4. **Grandiose / Minimalist** — learn size manipulation from both directions.
5. **Challenger** — defeat Alphas across runs, then use alpha pressure for mutagen.
6. **Independent / Precocious** — use Branching routes once you understand branch planning.
7. **Pressure Genetics** — Simple, Stubborn, Spawn of Chaos, Envoy of Chaos, and Precocious belong later.
8. **Challenge Genetics** — Vegan, Bullseye, Opportunistic, Underdog, and similar goals are best after you can route intentionally.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What are the best Genetics in Everything Is Crab?

The best general Genetics are Chosen, Pioneer, Grandiose, Independent, Precocious, Challenger, and Expert. Chosen is best for early flexibility, Pioneer for POIs, Grandiose for Chonky builds, Independent and Precocious for Branching, Challenger for Alphas, and Expert for specializations.

### What does Chosen do?

Chosen makes your third level-up offer three Legendary Evolutions. It is one of the best general-purpose Genetics because it gives the run an early direction spike.

### What does Pioneer do?

Pioneer increases the number of Points of Interest by 50%. It is excellent for exploration, POI level farming, mutagen, recovery, and flexible build routing.

### What does Grandiose do?

Grandiose makes damage scale with Size, so it rewards builds that are already becoming large. It is best with Chonky, Body Slam, Imposing, big Physical, More Mass, HP, and defensive layers. The common mistake is stacking size without movement; if terrain or bosses are already punishing you, take Legs, Wings, Slithery, Leap, or adaptation before more greed.

### What does Independent do?

Independent makes every 12th level-up a Branching Evolution, but bosses no longer drop Boss Fruits. It is best for POI level farming and long-run branch planning.

### What does Spawn of Chaos do?

Spawn of Chaos randomizes starting stats. Check your stat screen immediately and pivot based on size, HP, Ability, Physical, speed, senses, and terrain. New players often fail this Genetic because they force their planned build instead of reading the opener they actually received.

### What is the best Splice combination?

The safest general Splice is Chosen + Pioneer because Chosen gives early direction while Pioneer gives more POIs and recovery options. For Chonky builds, use Grandiose + Chosen. For POI Branching routes, use Independent + Pioneer. If your previous run did not reach the first boss, you will not get a Splice choice and should plan around one Genetic instead.

### Should beginners use Splice?

Yes, but keep it simple. Beginners should avoid combining two risky modifiers. Use Chosen, Pioneer, Standard, or another stable Genetic before testing volatile Splice combinations.

### What Genetics are best for Chonky or Body Slam?

Grandiose, Chosen, Expert, and Spawn of Chaos with high starting size are the best Chonky / Body Slam Genetics. Look for More Mass, Chunky, Body Slam, Shell, Plating, and movement.

### What Genetics are best for poison builds?

Poison builds usually prefer Chosen, Spawn of Chaos if Ability starts high, Expert, or Pioneer. After the run begins, look for Stinger, Leech, Cortex, Dextrous, Immune System, and movement.

### What Genetics are best for POI level farming?

Pioneer, Independent, Precocious, and Spawn of Chaos are the main POI farming Genetics. Pair them with More Eyes, Legs, Wings, Slithery, Leap, and terrain adaptation.

### How do I unlock Minimalist?

Reach 50% size or smaller. Minimalist gives strong damage value but removes Dash, so you need a movement plan before using it seriously.

### How do I unlock Bullseye?

Kill any regular boss with a thrown stone. The usual mistake is letting poison, contact damage, reflect, or a normal attack take the final hit first. Lower the boss safely, then make the thrown stone the finishing hit.

### How do I unlock Patient?

Deal 100 or more damage with a single hit. Use routes that create one large hit, such as big Physical, Body Slam, size scaling, or a damage-window setup. Poison, allies, and many small attacks are usually worse for this goal.

### What Genetics should I use for high Pressure?

Use tested Genetics that support movement, sustain, defensive consistency, or a proven damage route. Chosen, Pioneer, Grandiose, and Expert are easier to stabilize than risky combinations like Spawn of Chaos + Minimalist.
