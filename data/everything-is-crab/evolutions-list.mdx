<h2 id="how-to-use-this-picker">
  How to use this Evolution Picker
</h2>

Most bad evolution choices happen because the player asks the wrong question.

Do not ask, **"Which evolution is strongest?"** Ask:

1. **What is killing this run right now?**
2. **Which stat does my main attack scale with?**
3. **Do I have enough food tempo to reach the next boss?**
4. **Can I move through the next biome safely?**
5. **Am I building toward a real route, or just taking good-looking pieces?**

This guide is built around that player decision. A raw data table can tell you what an evolution does. This picker tells you when to take it, when to skip it, and which build actually wants it.

<GuideCallout title="Picker rule">
If two evolutions look good, pick the one that fixes your weakest checkpoint before the next boss. Damage, movement, food, and defense all matter, but not at the same time.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="affinity-drift">
  Why you keep getting the same type of evolutions
</h2>

If you keep seeing the same family of evolutions, it is usually not a bug. It is **affinity drift**.

Every time you pick an evolution tied to an affinity, your build is pushed further toward that affinity. After two or three picks in the same direction, that affinity starts showing up more often in your evolution pool. For example, if you keep taking Gregarious tools like Tail Wag, Social, Antennae, or charm support, you should expect more social-style evolutions to appear.

<GuideCallout title="Affinity drift rule">
Affinity is not just flavor text. It nudges the future evolution pool. Consecutive Predator picks make Predator-style options more common; consecutive Gregarious picks make social options more common; consecutive Imposing picks make size and tank options more common.
</GuideCallout>

This is why the first few picks matter so much. Your early choices do not only help the current level-up; they influence what the game is likely to offer next.

<h2 id="branching-evolutions">
  Branching Evolutions vs normal evolutions
</h2>

Branching Evolutions are not the same as normal level-up evolutions.

Normal evolutions appear from the regular level-up pool. Branching Evolutions are route-shaping choices that appear from boss reward moments and major branch points. That means you cannot always force them by rerolling normal level-ups. You prepare for them by pushing your build toward the route that wants that branch.

<GuideCallout title="Branching rule">
Plan your build before the Branching Evolution appears. If your route wants Body Slam, big-body tools, Arms, Legs, Wings, or other branch payoffs, your earlier picks should already support that direction.
</GuideCallout>

This matters because some evolutions are build-around pieces only when the rest of the route is ready. Body Slam is much better after HP and size support. Wings are much better when terrain is costing you the run. Arms are stronger when your build can use the physical, ability, or reach tools that follow.

The core pieces are:

**Stinger → Leech → Cortex → Dextrous → Poison Spores → Immune System → movement or Reach.**

Stinger gives the route its poison identity. Leech gives close-range sustain. Cortex makes Ability-scaling attacks hit harder. Dextrous helps the build apply damage more often. Immune System and poison resistance keep mushroom and poison-heavy routes from turning against you.

The biggest poison mistake is taking poison without a way to safely apply it. If your Stinger is short range and your build has no HP, no mobility, no cooldown, and no healing, the poison plan is not online yet.

Pick poison when your run already has at least two of these signals:

- Ability scaling is showing up.
- You have Leech or another sustain tool.
- You have cooldown reduction.
- You can apply poison without taking lethal contact damage.
- You have movement, Reach, or terrain adaptation.
- You are seeing mushroom / poison support.

If only one poison piece appears, treat it as a side pick until the route proves itself.

Big Physical is the easiest route to understand: get larger, hit harder, and add enough movement that your size does not trap you.

The core pieces are:

**More Mass / Chunky → one Physical attack → Muscular Tissue → HP → Plating / Shell → movement → Body Slam if HP is high.**

Body Slam is the clearest payoff. It is not just a random close-range attack; it becomes a route when Max HP, size, defense, and Physical damage are already appearing. If you take Body Slam before HP support, it can feel underwhelming. If you take it after HP and size are online, it can become your main damage plan.

Jaws is different. Jaws can be used as a Predator food engine, especially when you can finish enemies and convert kills into meat progress. The best Predator runs combine kill power with movement so enemies do not simply run away from your giant body.

<GuideCallout title="Big body rule">
Size is not enough. The bigger you become, the earlier you should ask whether you still have speed, terrain adaptation, or a way to reach food safely.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="best-predator-evolutions">
  Best Predator evolutions
</h2>

Predator evolutions reward aggression, Physical damage, meat progress, and contact attacks. They are best when your run can actually secure kills.

Prioritize:

- **Jaws** when you can finish enemies and use meat drops.
- **Carnivore** when meat is your main food source.
- **Muscular Tissue** when your main attacks are Physical.
- **Sharpen** when you have enough Predator affinity and contact attacks to make the active buff matter.
- **Legs / Wings** when your prey keeps escaping.

Predator routes fail when they are too slow. If you cannot catch enemies, Jaws and Carnivore lose value. Take movement before greed if the map is outrunning you.

<h2 id="best-mobility-poi-evolutions">
  Best food, movement, and POI evolutions
</h2>

This is where the old beginner and food economy sections merge. Food tools and movement tools are not separate early-game ideas; together, they decide how fast you reach the next build checkpoint.

Prioritize:

- **Cheek Pouch** for moving while food continues to convert.
- **Saliva** when feeding speed is the bottleneck.
- **Scavenger** when enemies or bosses leave meat you can safely steal.
- **Legs / Wings / Slithery / Leap** when terrain or enemy speed is the bottleneck.
- **More Eyes** when POIs, alphas, and map awareness are the bottleneck.
- **Jaws / Carnivore** when kills are your food engine.

Do not stay in food economy forever. Food tools create more evolution choices; they do not replace damage. Once your food loop is stable, convert that advantage into a real attack route.

<h2 id="best-reflect-spines-evolutions">
  Best Spines, reflect, and contact poison evolutions
</h2>

Spines-style builds are powerful only when they reverse enemy pressure. The route is not "let enemies hit you." The route is "survive contact, then make contact expensive."

The core pieces are:

**Spines → HP / Plating / Shell → Poisonous or Detachable → Revenge → Regeneration → movement or dodge.**

Use Spines when your build already has a survival plan. Add Poisonous when Ability and poison contact matter. Add Detachable when dodge is frequent enough to become a damage trigger.

Skip Spines as a main plan if you have no HP, no defense, and no recovery. Reflect damage does not help if the contact kills you first.

<h2 id="affinity-picker-notes">
  Affinity picker notes
</h2>

Use affinity as a direction signal, not as a prison. A build can take off-route evolutions when they solve an immediate problem, but your main scaling should still make sense.

### Predator

Predator is best for Physical damage, aggression, meat progress, Jaws, Carnivore, and contact attacks. Each Predator pick makes future Predator-style options more likely, so do not chain Predator picks unless you are ready to commit to kills and meat progress.

### Prey

Prey is usually safer, more defensive, or more food-oriented. Consecutive Prey picks push the pool toward survival, food, and lower-risk routes. That is useful if you are under pressure, but it can leave you short on boss damage if you never pivot.

### Imposing

Imposing supports size, HP, mitigation, Body Slam, Plating, Shell, and big-body routes. Each Imposing pick makes the run more likely to keep offering tank and size tools. Take movement early so the route does not become too slow.

### Trickster

Trickster is best when your run wants dodge, cooldown, Ability tools, evasive play, or unusual routes like poison / leech / dexterity setups. Multiple Trickster picks can pull the pool toward skill tempo, but the build still needs a safe way to apply damage.

### Gregarious

Gregarious supports senses, social effects, charm, allies, and map awareness. Consecutive Gregarious picks can explain why you keep seeing social and charm options. It can be strong, but it needs a backup boss plan.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What are the best evolutions in Everything Is Crab?

The best general evolutions are usually Cheek Pouch, Saliva, Legs, Shell, More Eyes, and one reliable attack for beginners. For specific routes, Stinger and Leech are better for poison / Ability, while Body Slam, Chunky, More Mass, Jaws, and Muscular Tissue are better for big Physical builds.

### Does this cover all evolutions?

This picker covers the major evolution decisions across the game's 5 affinities and the evolutions most likely to change your build. It is not trying to clone a raw 125+ row data table. Instead, it organizes the important picks by route, priority, take condition, and skip condition.

### Why do I keep getting the same evolutions?

You are probably seeing affinity drift. When you keep choosing evolutions from the same affinity, that affinity becomes more likely to appear in future evolution choices. If you keep taking Gregarious or Social tools, expect more charm, ally, and senses options.

### What is affinity drift?

Affinity drift is the way your early picks push the future evolution pool. Predator picks pull you toward aggression and meat routes. Imposing picks pull you toward size and tank routes. Gregarious picks pull you toward social and senses routes.

### What are Branching Evolutions?

Branching Evolutions are route-shaping choices that appear from boss reward moments and major branch points, not just from normal level-up rolls. Plan for them by building the stats and route that make the branch useful.

### How many attacks can you have at once?

Plan your loadout around limited slots: usually two normal attacks, one Ultimate-style ability, and one Movement tool. If a new evolution replaces a slot, make sure it improves your actual build instead of breaking your current damage or movement plan.

### What is the max evolution level?

Not every evolution scales the same way. Some evolutions have multiple levels and become build-defining only after upgrades, while others are one-time tools or branch choices. Check the tooltip before spending mutagen or reroll value on an upgrade.

### Should I always pick the highest rarity evolution?

No. Rarity helps, but the best pick is the one that solves your current problem. A rare off-route evolution can be worse than a common evolution that gives your build food, movement, defense, or the correct damage stat.

### Is Cheek Pouch good?

Yes. Cheek Pouch is one of the safest early picks because it improves food tempo. It helps you keep moving while food continues to convert into progress.

### Is Stinger good?

Stinger is excellent when you are building poison, Ability damage, cooldown, or sustain. It is weaker when your build cannot safely land short-range attacks or when you are already committed to pure Physical.

### Is Leech good?

Leech is good in Ability and sustain routes because it deals Ability damage and helps you heal through fights. It is less valuable in pure Physical builds unless you have enough Ability support.

### Is Body Slam good?

Body Slam is good when you already have Max HP, size, Shell, Plating, Chunky, or More Mass. Do not force it before your build has enough bulk.

### Is Jaws good?

Jaws is good in Predator routes because it supports Physical damage and meat-based progress. It is best when you can use it to finish enemies reliably.

### Are Points of Interest worth chasing?

Yes, if your build has movement, senses, and enough safety to reach them. POIs can give huge progress, but chasing them through lethal terrain can still lose the run.

### When should I pivot evolutions?

Pivot when your first plan does not appear by the first boss checkpoint. If you wanted poison but the game gives HP, Mass, and Body Slam, pivot to Body Slam. If you wanted big Physical but the game gives Leech, Cortex, and Dextrous, consider Ability instead.

### What changes at higher Pressure?

At Pressure 1-5, food tempo and simple scaling are usually enough. At Pressure 6+, random biome starts make movement and terrain adaptation more valuable. At Pressure 10+, sustain, poison resistance, and defensive consistency become much more important.

Start with this one Evolution Picker page. Split into separate pages later only if Search Console shows meaningful impressions for specific routes like poison evolutions, Body Slam, Predator / Jaws, Branching Evolutions, or POI level farming.
