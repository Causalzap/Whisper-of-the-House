<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Progression in Everything Is Crab has two layers: run progression and account progression.**

Inside a run, you progress through food, evolutions, mutagen, bosses, and Carcinisation. Between runs, you progress through **Pressure levels, genetics, Codex entries, evolution unlocks, challenge goals, cosmetics, and eventually Endless Mode preparation**.

The best progression order is:

**first clear → early Pressure → Codex check → test one genetic → farm one build-specific unlock → prepare for Endless Mode → push harder Pressure and challenges.**

Do not test everything at once. If you unlock a new genetic, use it in a controlled run. If you push Pressure, use a stable build. If you farm poison, reflect, social, or boss goals, build around that one target.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="progression-loop">
  How progression works
</h2>

Everything Is Crab has two different progression layers.

**Run progression** is what happens inside one run: food, evolutions, mutagen, bosses, Carcinisation, and your current creature.

**Account progression** is what changes after the run: Pressure levels, genetics, Codex entries, new evolutions, cosmetics, challenge routes, and long-term goals like Endless Mode.

<h2 id="endless-mode">
  Endless Mode: what it is for
</h2>

Endless Mode should be treated as a late progression goal.

A normal run is about reaching and beating the end condition. Endless-style progression is about testing how long your build can keep scaling after a normal clear route is already reliable.

Use the Codex or mode screen to check the exact Endless Mode unlock condition when it appears. For planning, the important point is simple: **Endless Mode needs a build that scales longer than a normal clear build.**

<GuideTable
  caption="Endless Mode planning guide."
  columns={endlessColumns}
  rows={endlessRows}
/>

Good Endless Mode candidates are builds that can keep solving four problems:

1. food access,
2. scalable damage,
3. movement,
4. sustain.

A build that barely survives the final boss is not an Endless build yet. Before focusing on Endless Mode, clear normal runs, push early Pressure, test genetics, and make sure your final boss route is consistent.

<GuideCallout title="Endless rule">
Endless Mode is not where you fix a weak build. It is where you test a build that already clears.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="unlock-priorities">
  What to unlock first
</h2>

Your first unlock target should make future runs easier to understand.

<GuideTable
  caption="Recommended unlock order in Everything Is Crab."
  columns={unlockPriorityColumns}
  rows={unlockPriorityRows}
/>

Do not try to unlock everything in one run. Pick one target:

- a Pressure clear,
- one new genetic,
- one poison goal,
- one reflect goal,
- one boss goal,
- one cosmetic / collection goal,
- one Endless Mode preparation run.

The more focused the goal, the easier it is to understand why the run worked or failed.

<h2 id="build-specific-unlocks">
  Build-specific unlocks
</h2>

Build-specific unlocks are where progression becomes more interesting.

Instead of asking “what is generally strong,” ask “what unlock am I trying to trigger?”

Good examples:

- For poison goals, build around Stinger, poison effects, survival, and enough time for poison damage to stack.
- For reflect goals, build around Spines, Plating, Revenge, HP, and recovery.
- For size goals, build around Mass, Alpha, Trunk reach, Body Slam, and movement.
- For social goals, build around charm, allies, pack damage, and a backup boss plan.
- For Carcinisation goals, build around food, HP, defense, movement, and final boss readiness.

Use <Link href="/everything-is-crab/achievements-challenges-guide">Achievements and Challenges Guide</Link> for challenge and achievement routing.

Cosmetics and evolution visuals are long-term collection rewards.

They matter because Everything Is Crab is built around creating strange creatures, but they do not make a run stronger. Keep power progression and collection progression separate.

A clean collection plan is:

1. clear with a stable build,
2. check locked cosmetics,
3. pick one visual or collection target,
4. build a safe run,
5. farm that target without ruining the clear.

The biggest mistake is turning every new unlock into a new run at the same time.

A better approach is:

**one new genetic, one new Pressure level, one unlock goal, or one challenge route per run.**

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do after my first clear?

Check the Codex, read any new genetics, push one early Pressure level with your safest build, and pick one unlock target instead of testing everything at once.

### What is the main progression system in Everything Is Crab?

The main progression systems are food and evolutions inside a run, then Pressure levels, genetics, Codex entries, challenge goals, cosmetics, and Endless Mode preparation between runs.

### How do I unlock new Pressure levels?

Clear your current run or current Pressure level. For example, clearing Pressure 6 unlocks Pressure 7.

### What changes at Pressure 6?

Pressure 6 adds random biome starts, which makes your opening more dependent on the biome you spawn in.

### What changes at Pressure 7?

Pressure 7 adds boss protection at the start of fights and after each enragement, while environmental damage increases.

### Which genetic should I use first?

Use the baseline / Standard start for your first clear and early learning. After that, test one new genetic at a time.

### Is Pacifist a good first genetic?

No. Pacifist is better after you understand food routing, movement, boss patterns, and non-combat scaling.

### When should I try Grandiose?

Try Grandiose when you want a large-body route with Mass, Alpha, Trunk reach, Body Slam, HP scaling, or big physical damage.

### What is Endless Mode for?

Endless Mode is a late progression target where your build needs to keep scaling longer than a normal clear build. Focus on it after stable clears and early Pressure progression.

### Is Endless Mode important early?

No. First learn normal clears, early Pressure, genetics, and final boss consistency. Endless Mode becomes useful when you already have builds that can clear.

### How do I track unlocks?

Use the Codex after each clear or death. Check genetics, evolutions, enemies, creations, cosmetics, and any new entries before starting another run.

### What should I unlock first?

First clear normal runs, then early Pressure levels, then test one new genetic, then farm one build-specific unlock such as poison, reflect, size, boss, or social goals.
