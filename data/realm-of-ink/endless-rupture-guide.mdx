<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Endless Mode** unlocks after you reach the **true ending**, then opens through the **Archive / Sanctum** system using a completed run's final build. If Endless is not available yet, keep progressing endings first.

**Rupture** is the extra modifier system near the run entrance. Early Rupture starts with a limited cap, then higher levels open after clears. Push it for challenge, achievements, character progression, and reward milestones such as **Rupture 12, 21, and 30**. After that, every 5 Rupture levels improves the chance of Epic-or-higher Curios / perks and Elixirs.

Do not push Endless, Dragon Hair Brush, or Rupture with a build that barely cleared. Bring a finished route like **Violeta Primordial Status / Curse** or a complete **Oread Curse Butterfly / Laser** setup.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="endless-rupture-overview">
  Endless, Dragon Hair Brush, and Rupture explained
</h2>

Realm of Ink's endgame is not just about clearing one more run. Once you start pushing higher difficulty, Rupture modifiers, and Endless, the game tests whether your build actually scales.

A normal run can be cleared with a messy setup if you dodge well and get decent rewards. High difficulty is less forgiving. Rupture makes your weak points louder. Endless asks whether your final build can keep going after the normal route is already finished.

<h2 id="how-to-unlock-endless-rupture">
  How to unlock Endless mode and Rupture
</h2>

The two most common endgame questions are simple: **why is Endless missing, and how do I start Rupture?**

<GuideTable
  caption="How to unlock Endless mode and Rupture in Realm of Ink."
  columns={unlockColumns}
  rows={unlockRows}
/>

For Endless, the key requirement is the true ending. If you have not reached the true ending yet, do not waste time looking for the Sanctum option. Keep progressing the ending route first.

For Rupture, look for the doctor / old man near the run entrance once the system is available. Early Rupture does not immediately let you push every level. You start with a smaller cap, then unlock higher Rupture levels through clears.

If you are still working on endings, read the <Link href="/realm-of-ink/final-boss-true-ending">Final Boss and True Ending Guide</Link> first.

<h2 id="difficulty-7">
  Difficulty 7: Dragon Hair Brush
</h2>

Dragon Hair Brush is difficulty 7, the highest normal difficulty tier. The reward is better, but the game also becomes much more demanding.

The important changes are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>monster attack and HP are increased,</li>
  <li>some levels can feature empowered enemies,</li>
  <li>mid-chapter bosses and end-chapter bosses can gain enhanced moves,</li>
  <li>Fox Blood drop increases,</li>
  <li>and high-rarity rewards and legendary Elixirs become more common.</li>
</ul>

That sounds like a good deal, but the catch is simple: if your build is weak, the better rewards may not save you.

A build that clears lower difficulty by surviving messy fights can feel awful on Dragon Hair Brush. Bosses live longer. Bad dodges hurt more. Low damage becomes more obvious.

Use Dragon Hair Brush when you already understand your build, can clear normal runs consistently, and have stable boss damage. Do not use it just because you want better drops.

<h2 id="rupture">
  What is Rupture?
</h2>

Rupture is an extra challenge layer. You can add modifiers through the doctor / old man near the run entrance, then push harder versions of a run for more challenge, achievements, unlocks, and character rank progress.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/realm-of-ink/realm-of-ink-rupture-modifier-doctor.webp"
    alt="Realm of Ink doctor near the run entrance used to increase Rupture modifiers."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Rupture is where you add extra pressure. Start low, clear the current cap, then climb higher.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Rupture does not magically create a new game. It takes the problems you already have and makes them harder to ignore.

If your build lacks boss damage, Rupture makes bosses feel worse.  
If your defense is thin, Rupture punishes mistakes harder.  
If your economy is weak, Rupture makes bad shops more painful.  
If you do not understand your attack loop, Rupture turns every room into noise.

Before adding Rupture, ask:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Can I clear without needing perfect reward luck?</li>
  <li>Can I beat bosses without every fight becoming a panic dodge marathon?</li>
  <li>Do I know which stats my build actually wants?</li>
  <li>Can I recover if I enter a Way Station hurt?</li>
</ul>

If not, fix the normal build first.

<h2 id="rupture-rewards">
  Rupture rewards and why you should climb
</h2>

Rupture is not only for bragging rights. It also has reward checkpoints and better high-rarity reward odds as you push deeper.

<GuideTable
  caption="Realm of Ink Rupture reward milestones."
  columns={rewardColumns}
  rows={rewardRows}
/>

This reward structure matters because it gives you a real reason to climb beyond low Rupture. You are not only making the game harder. You are also increasing the chance that future runs see stronger Curios / perks and Elixirs.

The important 1.0 terminology note: older update notes or discussions may say **perks**, while current in-game language uses **Curios**. When players talk about Epic-or-higher perks, they usually mean Epic-or-higher Curios.

<h2 id="endless-mode">
  How Endless mode works
</h2>

Endless mode unlocks after the true ending and is accessed through the Archive and Sanctum system. The key detail is that you enter using a completed run's build.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/realm-of-ink/realm-of-ink-endless-archive-sanctum.webp"
    alt="Realm of Ink Archive screen with Enter Sanctum option for Endless mode."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Endless uses a finished run's build. Do not enter with a setup that only barely cleared the normal route.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

That changes how you should think about your normal clears. A normal clear is not always an Endless-ready clear.

A build is good for Endless when it ended with:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>strong damage scaling,</li>
  <li>good boss damage,</li>
  <li>multiple defensive layers,</li>
  <li>a clear attack loop,</li>
  <li>and enough synergy to keep going without needing one more lucky pickup.</li>
</ul>

A build is bad for Endless when it only survived because:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you got lucky with healing,</li>
  <li>bosses barely died,</li>
  <li>your damage fell off late,</li>
  <li>or your upgrades did not multiply each other.</li>
</ul>

If Endless is still locked, keep working through endings instead of trying to force the Archive / Sanctum option early.

<h2 id="faq">
  Realm of Ink Endless and Rupture FAQ
</h2>

### How do you unlock Endless mode in Realm of Ink?

Endless mode unlocks after you reach the true ending. After that, you can access it through the Archive / Sanctum system using a completed run's final build.

### Why is Endless mode missing?

If Endless is missing, you probably have not finished the true ending route yet. Keep progressing endings before looking for the Sanctum option.

### Can I re-enter Endless with the same build?

Yes, if that completed run is saved in the Archive / Sanctum system. Endless uses completed-run builds, so the important part is finishing a run with a build worth bringing back.

### Does Endless use my last run or can I pick a build?

Endless uses completed builds through the Archive / Sanctum system, so you are not only thinking about your most recent room clear. Pick a finished run that ended with strong damage, defense, and scaling instead of a run that barely survived.

### What is Dragon Hair Brush in Realm of Ink?

Dragon Hair Brush is difficulty 7. It increases challenge, improves rewards, increases Fox Blood drops, and can give bosses enhanced moves.

### What is Rupture?

Rupture is an extra modifier system that adds more challenge for achievements, unlocks, rewards, and character rank progression.

### How do you unlock higher Rupture levels?

Rupture starts with a limited early cap, such as Lv.5. Clear the available levels, then return to push higher caps as more Rupture levels unlock.

### What are the Rupture reward milestones?

Rupture 12, 21, and 30 are important reward checkpoints. After that, every 5 Rupture levels improves the appearance chance of Epic-or-higher Curios / perks and Elixirs.

### Should I push Rupture early?

No. Push Rupture after your normal clears are stable. If you add Rupture before you understand your build, it will mostly make your mistakes more punishing.

### What is Rupture 50?

Rupture 50 is a high-pressure endgame setting where you need a finished build with real scaling, boss damage, and defensive support.

### What build should I use for Endless?

Use a completed run that ended strong. Violeta Primordial Status / Curse is a strong tested direction. Oread Curse Butterfly / Laser can also work if the right pieces are online.

### What is the best build for Rupture?

The safest tested route here is Violeta Primordial Status / Curse because it stacks multiple damage layers into one engine.

### Is Oread good for high difficulty?

Yes, but Oread needs the correct curse, butterfly, laser, array, ability damage, and defensive pieces. She can feel weak if the setup has no payoff.

### Why does my damage fall off in high difficulty?

Your build probably has too many unrelated rewards. Pick one damage engine and stack Ink Gems, Curios, Elixirs, and abilities that multiply it.

### Why do bosses take so long?

Your build may have good room clear but weak boss damage. Add single-target scaling, burst, status damage, curse payoff, heavy attack lasers, or Ink Relic skill damage.

### Should I take more damage or defense?

If bosses are not dying, take damage. If you already have damage but keep losing runs to mistakes, take HP, shield, food, or damage reduction.
