<h2 id="violeta-status-curse">
  Violeta Primordial Status / Curse build
</h2>

Violeta's strongest tested setup is a **Primordial Status / Curse build** centered on **Viperous Beauty**. This route works because it turns Violeta into a multi-layer damage engine: status effects, curse effects, heavy attacks, Ink Relic skill damage, crit, and all damage bonus all start pushing the same direction.

<GuideTable
  caption="Violeta Primordial Status / Curse build priorities."
  columns={violetaColumns}
  rows={violetaRows}
/>

The practical playstyle is simple: get the Primordial route online, keep status and curse effects active, then shift your attack loop when your upgrades start rewarding heavy attacks, clones, shockwaves, or Ink Relic skill triggers.

Do not stay locked into light attack spam if your build has clearly moved into heavy attack or Ink Relic scaling. This is one of the biggest reasons a strong-looking Violeta run can feel weaker than it should.

Use this build if:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you have access to Viperous Beauty / Primordial Ink,</li>
  <li>you can stack burning, bleeding, poison, or other status effects,</li>
  <li>you have curse effect or curse-related Curios,</li>
  <li>your run is giving you heavy attack, Ink Relic skill, crit, or all damage scaling,</li>
  <li>and you want a build that can handle Dragon Hair Brush or Rupture-style pressure.</li>
</ul>

<h3 id="red-crit-build">
  Red Crit Build with Tiger Ink
</h3>

Start with Red Crit if you want the most straightforward default-character route. Look for **Tiger Ink**, crit rate, crit damage, **On Target** style talent support, **Croc Drum**, and **Tempest Ring**.

This route works because crit builds are easier to understand than many DOT or Primordial routes. You want frequent hits, enough crit chance to make the build consistent, and crit damage that makes those hits matter.

Use Red Crit if:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you are still learning the game,</li>
  <li>you want a direct damage plan,</li>
  <li>you find Tiger Ink or crit support early,</li>
  <li>you already have Curios that reward light attacks, fast hits, or crits,</li>
  <li>and you do not want to rely on poison / bleed / burn stacking yet.</li>
</ul>

<h3 id="red-dot-build">
  When Red should switch to a DOT build
</h3>

Red does not have to stay on crit every run. If your early rewards give you **Venom Ink**, **Ivy Ink**, poison, bleeding, burning, or status effect damage before your crit route is ready, it can be better to move into DOT instead.

Use Red DOT instead of Red Crit when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you find Venom Ink or Ivy Ink early,</li>
  <li>your Curios already improve status effect damage, status duration, or stack limits,</li>
  <li>you do not have enough crit rate to make Tiger Ink feel consistent,</li>
  <li>and you are comfortable letting poison, bleed, or burn keep ticking while you dodge.</li>
</ul>

Do not force DOT just because you found one poison source. Red DOT needs several pieces before it feels better than a straightforward crit route.

For the full setup, use the <Link href="#dot-build">Venom Ink + Ivy Ink DOT build</Link> section below.

<h2 id="dot-build">
  Venom Ink + Ivy Ink DOT build
</h2>

The strongest non-Violeta status route to watch for is **Venom Ink + Ivy Ink**. This build is easier to search, easier to recognize in-game, and more useful than a generic “status scaling” label.

<GuideTable
  caption="Realm of Ink DOT build pieces."
  columns={dotColumns}
  rows={dotRows}
/>

The DOT route wants layered effects. Poison alone can feel slow. Bleeding alone can feel incomplete. Burning alone can be fine, but it becomes better when it is one part of a larger status engine.

A stronger DOT route stacks:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Venom Ink</strong> for poison pressure,</li>
  <li><strong>Ivy Ink</strong> for status pairing,</li>
  <li><strong>Tri-Curse</strong> when your run can keep multiple debuffs active,</li>
  <li><strong>Glacier Claw</strong> for extra status / control value,</li>
  <li>status effect damage,</li>
  <li>status duration,</li>
  <li>status stack limit,</li>
  <li>and multi-hit attacks that keep effects applied.</li>
</ul>

This is the route to use when your run gives you status pieces early but not enough Primordial support for a Violeta-style build.

<h2 id="momo-pet-build">
  Momo Ink and pet builds
</h2>

Pet builds can work, but they need more commitment than one pet reward.

<GuideTable
  caption="Realm of Ink Momo and Ink Pet build pieces."
  columns={petColumns}
  rows={petRows}
/>

Look for **Momo Ink** or pet-specialized Ink when your run is already giving you pet value. A real pet build wants several pieces working together: Ink Pet damage, extra pets, pet attack speed, pet ultimate cooldown, pet crit, or pet inheritance.

Pet builds are useful because they keep dealing damage while you dodge. That makes them comfortable for players still learning boss movement. The downside is that pet builds can fall off in high difficulty if you do not keep scaling them.

Use pet builds if:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you find Momo Ink early,</li>
  <li>you have multiple pet Curios,</li>
  <li>you want safer damage while learning bosses,</li>
  <li>or your run gives you extra Ink Pets and pet scaling together.</li>
</ul>

Do not force pet builds if your best Curios are clearly pushing crit, DOT, Oread lasers, or Violeta Primordial damage.

<h2 id="wang-ding-ning-ye">
  What about Wang Ding and Ning Ye builds?
</h2>

Wang Ding and Ning Ye are not covered as full tested routes here yet, but they should not be treated like weaker copies of Red, Violeta, or Oread. Their best builds depend on how their base kit wants to deal damage and what the run gives you early.

For **Wang Ding**, think of him as a more weapon-forward character. If your run starts giving you heavy attack damage, burst windows, clones, shockwaves, single-hit payoff, or strong melee Curios, lean into that direction instead of forcing Venom + Ivy DOT just because status builds are strong elsewhere.

For **Ning Ye**, look for speed, skill use, multi-hit pressure, or safer hit-and-run damage. If the run gives you crit support, she can move toward a fast-hit crit route. If it gives you status application and enough scaling, she can move into DOT. The key is to read the first strong route the run gives you instead of copying Oread's curse laser plan or Violeta's Primordial status route.

A simple rule works:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Wang Ding</strong> — start by checking heavy attack, burst, clone, shockwave, or melee payoff.</li>
  <li><strong>Ning Ye</strong> — start by checking speed, crit, skill use, multi-hit, or status application.</li>
  <li><strong>Do not force another character's build</strong> unless the rewards clearly support it.</li>
</ul>

These two characters deserve dedicated build testing later, but this should keep you from making the biggest mistake: forcing them into a route that only works because another character's kit supports it.

  Best builds for bosses
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

Boss builds need more than room clear. Normal rooms reward wide damage, pets, and screen coverage. Bosses reward pressure on one target, safe burst windows, and enough defense to survive bad patterns.

Use this quick matchup logic:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Early bosses:</strong> Red Crit is usually enough if Tiger Ink, crit rate, or fast attack support appears early. You do not need a perfect Primordial route for the first real boss checks.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Tanky mid-run bosses:</strong> Venom + Ivy DOT becomes better if you already have status effect damage, stack limit, or several ways to apply poison, bleeding, or burning.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Book Spirit:</strong> Violeta Primordial Status / Curse and Oread Curse Butterfly / Laser are the strongest directions here because both can keep pressure active through longer phases.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Ink God:</strong> bring a finished build with a defensive layer. Raw damage is not enough if you cannot survive long enough to use your burst windows.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Dragon Hair Brush bosses:</strong> use builds with real scaling, not only early room clear. Violeta is the safest tested route here, while Oread needs her curse, laser, and defense pieces online.
  </li>
</ul>

If your boss damage feels low, check whether your build has single-target payoff. A pet build with no pet scaling, a DOT build with no status damage, or a crit build with low crit rate will all feel fine in rooms and weak against bosses.

The most important habit is to read named rewards as part of a route. **Tiger Ink** suggests a different run than **Venom Ink**. **Momo Ink** suggests a different run than **Viperous Beauty**. **Power of Prayer** changes Oread differently than a basic curse pickup.

<h2 id="faq">
  Realm of Ink Best Builds FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best build in Realm of Ink?

The strongest tested build here is Violeta Primordial Status / Curse with Viperous Beauty, status effect damage, curse effect, heavy attacks, Ink Relic skill damage, crit, and all damage bonus.

### Which Red build is easiest to start with?

Red Crit Build is the easiest starting route. Look for Tiger Ink, crit rate, crit damage, On Target-style support, Croc Drum, Tempest Ring, and fast light attack pressure.

### Is Red Crit better than Red DOT?

Red Crit is easier to start with because the goal is direct: crit more often and make crits hit harder. Red DOT can scale well with Venom Ink + Ivy Ink, but it needs enough status application and status effect damage to feel good.

### What is the best Oread build?

Oread's best route is Curse Butterfly / Laser. Use Butterfly Roosting Call, Six-Line Secret Art, Power of Prayer, Spiraling Light Dance, Butterfly Manipulation Art, curse effect, ability damage, and laser payoff.

### Is Venom Ink good?

Yes, Venom Ink is good when your run can apply poison often and support it with status effect damage, status duration, stack limit, or other status pieces like Ivy Ink.

### Is Tiger Ink good?

Tiger Ink is good for crit-focused builds, especially on Red or other fast-hit routes that can use crit rate and crit damage consistently.

### Can pet builds work at Dragon Hair Brush?

Yes, but only with real pet support. Momo Ink, pet damage, extra pets, pet attack speed, and pet inheritance need to stack together. A single pet bonus usually falls off in high difficulty.

### Does Wang Ding have a recommended build?

For Wang Ding, look for heavy attack, burst, clone, shockwave, or single-hit payoff support. Do not force him into a Violeta-style status build unless the run clearly gives you those pieces.

### Does Ning Ye have a recommended build?

For Ning Ye, start from the first strong route your run gives you: crit, status, skill use, or safer multi-hit pressure. Build around the rewards that appear instead of copying Oread or Violeta directly.

### Should I always take Primordial Ink?

No. Primordial Ink is strongest when it becomes the center of your route. Do not take it if your current two-Ink-Gem setup and Momo form are already carrying the run.

### Why does my build lose damage late?

First check your named pieces. If your Curios reward heavy attacks but your Ink Gems are pushing pets, your build is split. If you have Venom Ink but no status damage, your DOT route is unfinished. If you have Tiger Ink but no crit scaling, the crit route is incomplete.
