<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
For your first Realm of Ink runs, learn **Ink Gems**, **Momo**, **Curios / perks**, **Way Stations**, and basic combat before chasing advanced builds. Ink Gems are flexible because you can replace them during a run, but Curios are harder to undo, so pick Ink Gems that support the Curios and damage direction you already have.

In combat, use **dash + held heavy attack** as a forward lunge punish, keep ranged enemies on screen, and do not press every Ink skill the moment it comes off cooldown. At Way Stations, heal first if you are hurt, then buy Curios or upgrade Ink Gems. Skip purple challenge rooms when your build or HP is weak.

<h2 id="what-is-realm-of-ink">
  What is Realm of Ink?
</h2>

Realm of Ink is an isometric action roguelite where your run is built from fast attacks, dodges, Ink Gem skills, Momo, Curios, Elixirs, food, and character-specific abilities.

If you have played Hades or similar roguelites, the room-to-room loop will feel familiar. The part that can confuse new players is the reward logic. Realm of Ink gives you many upgrades quickly, but not every strong-looking reward belongs in your run.

<h2 id="combat-basics">
  Combat basics that new players miss
</h2>

Realm of Ink is not only about dodging and pressing attack faster. A few small combat habits make the early game much easier.

<GuideTable
caption="Realm of Ink combat tips for beginners."
columns={combatColumns}
rows={combatRows}
/>

The most useful early trick is **dash + held heavy attack**. After a dash, hold heavy attack to turn your heavy into a forward lunge-style punish. Use it when you need to close distance safely, hit a ranged enemy, or punish after dodging a boss move.

Do not press every skill the moment it is ready. Some Ink Gem skills are much better when enemies are grouped, when a boss is vulnerable, or when you have Curios that trigger from Ink Relic skill use.

Also keep ranged enemies on screen. If you chase one target too far, a projectile from off-screen can hit you before you even know what happened.

<h2 id="ink-gems">
  Ink Gems explained
</h2>

Ink Gems are one of the most important systems in Realm of Ink. Each Ink Gem can give you a passive bonus and an active skill. Ink Gems also have elements and rarities, and both matter.

For beginners, the key details are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Ink Gems can give passive bonuses such as pet damage, crit, bonus damage, status effects, or curse support.</li>
  <li>Ink Gems also give active skills, such as summons, lightning, poison, circles, curses, or burst effects.</li>
  <li>You normally use two Ink Gems at a time.</li>
  <li>Your two Ink Gem elements affect Momo's form.</li>
  <li>Ink Gem rarity affects Momo's progression and the strength of the gem.</li>
  <li>Ink Gems can be replaced during a run, while Curios are much harder to undo.</li>
</ul>

That last point is important. If you already have strong Curios, your next Ink Gem choice should usually support those Curios. Do not rebuild your whole run around a random new gem unless the new gem is clearly stronger than your current plan.

Example:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If your Curios apply curses, choose Ink Gems that add curse payoff, repeated hits, ability damage, or curse duration.</li>
  <li>If your Curios improve pet damage, choose Ink Gems that give Momo a useful form or improve Ink Pet value.</li>
  <li>If your Curios reward heavy attacks, choose Ink Gems and abilities that make heavy attacks easier to use or more rewarding.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="momo">
  How Momo works
</h2>

Momo is your Ink Pet, and he is always with you. What changes is his form and how much he matters to your build.

Momo's form depends on the elements of your equipped Ink Gems. His strength also improves with Ink Gem rarity. That means an Ink Gem swap can change your pet setup even if the new gem looks better in isolation.

Use these rules:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If Momo is dealing noticeable damage, keep an eye out for Ink Pet damage, pet attack speed, extra pets, or pet cooldown effects.</li>
  <li>If Momo feels weak, do not force a pet build unless the run starts giving you pet support.</li>
  <li>If a new Ink Gem changes Momo's form, check whether the new form actually helps your current run.</li>
  <li>If you see Momo Ink or other pet-specialized Ink, only commit if you can support the route with Curios or Elixirs.</li>
</ul>

Some Momo forms and Ink Gem effects can look dangerous at first, especially when pets or projectiles start filling the screen. Learn which effects belong to you and which belong to enemies. For visual clutter from Ink Gems, pets, circles, butterflies, lasers, or status effects, use the visibility section below.

If your own effects make the screen too busy, adjust Ink Gem visibility or transparency settings so you can still read enemy attacks.

<h2 id="visibility">
  Ink Gem visibility and visual noise
</h2>

Some builds create circles, projectiles, pets, butterflies, lasers, poison clouds, or other effects that can cover important enemy tells. This is especially common once your Ink Gems and Curios start triggering multiple effects at once.

If you are getting hit because you cannot see what is happening, the problem may not be your build. It may be visibility.

Use these settings and habits:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Lower Ink Gem effect transparency if your own effects cover enemy attacks.</li>
  <li>Learn which projectiles belong to Momo or your Ink Gems so you do not dodge your own effects.</li>
  <li>Keep ranged enemies on screen instead of chasing one target into visual clutter.</li>
  <li>During boss fights, prioritize reading the boss over watching your damage numbers.</li>
</ul>

This matters more for builds with magic circles, arrays, pet projectiles, Oread butterflies, lasers, or status explosions. High damage is not useful if the screen becomes impossible to read.

<h2 id="curios">
  Curios, perks, and why the name matters
</h2>

Curios are the 1.0 name for perks. Older Realm of Ink content, Steam discussions, and comments may still use the word **perks**. In current 1.0 language, those are Curios.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/realm-of-ink/realm-of-ink-curios-cursed-variants.webp"
    alt="Realm of Ink Curios and cursed Curios showing build effects and tradeoffs."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Curios are the 1.0 name for perks. They are harder to undo than Ink Gems, so build around the Curios that matter.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

This name change is easy to miss. If someone says “best perks,” “boss perks,” or “perk build,” they usually mean Curios.

Curios can give simple stats, but many of them are build-defining. They can add status triggers, extra pets, boss drops, cursed tradeoffs, economy bonuses, shield effects, or attack-based triggers.

Because Curios usually stay with you once picked, the smart choice is often:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>first, identify your strongest Curios,</li>
  <li>then choose Ink Gems that support them,</li>
  <li>then use Elixirs to scale the stats that route needs.</li>
</ul>

That is why “Curios should match Ink Gems” is only half the logic. In many runs, it is better to say: **your next Ink Gem should match your best Curios**, because the gem is easier to replace than the Curio.

<h2 id="way-stations">
  Way Stations: what each NPC does
</h2>

Way Stations are where many beginner runs are saved or ruined. Do not treat every vendor the same.

<GuideTable
caption="Realm of Ink Way Station NPCs and when to use them."
columns={wayStationColumns}
rows={wayStationRows}
/>

The most common mistake is buying a Curio while injured, then dying in the next room. If your HP is low before a boss, the chef / food seller usually comes first.

Use the Curio merchant when your build needs a real effect: damage scaling, defense, pet support, curse payoff, status triggers, economy, or boss damage.

Use the upgrade vendor when your current Ink Gem is worth keeping, or when you are preparing for Primordial Ink and need the right rarity.

<h2 id="challenge-rooms">
  Should you enter purple challenge rooms?
</h2>

Purple challenge rooms are not automatic. They are a risk/reward decision.

<GuideTable
caption="When to enter purple challenge rooms in Realm of Ink."
columns={challengeColumns}
rows={challengeRows}
/>

A good rule is simple: if your build already feels strong, challenge rooms can help you snowball. If your build is already behind, a challenge room often turns a weak run into a dead run.

Before entering, ask:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Is my HP safe?</li>
  <li>Can I kill normal enemies quickly?</li>
  <li>Do I have enough room clear?</li>
  <li>Will the reward help my current build?</li>
  <li>Am I entering because it is smart, or because I am greedy?</li>
</ul>

If the answer feels bad, skip it. Surviving to the next Way Station is often worth more than gambling a weak run.

<h2 id="fox-blood-talents">
  Fox Blood and early Talent priorities
</h2>

After your first runs, you will start making hub decisions with Fox Blood and Talent-style meta progression. This is where beginners often waste early progress on upgrades that look interesting but do not make future runs more consistent.

For your first 10–15 minutes in the hub, prioritize upgrades that make every run easier to control.

<GuideTable
caption="Realm of Ink early Fox Blood and Talent priorities."
columns={talentColumns}
rows={talentRows}
/>

The safest early order is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Take reroll or reward-control upgrades first if available.</li>
  <li>Add starting consistency, such as better starting shops or starting choices.</li>
  <li>Take survival passives if you are dying before builds come online.</li>
  <li>Add damage or economy support after your runs feel less random.</li>
  <li>Save niche upgrades for later, when you understand which builds you actually like.</li>
</ol>

This is not about finding the most exciting Talent. It is about reducing bad runs. The more control you have early, the easier it becomes to build around Ink Gems, Curios, and Momo.

  Primordial Ink explained
</h2>

Primordial Ink is powerful, but it is not an automatic pick.

To get Primordial Ink, you need the Primordial Ink Curio and two fine / rare-or-better Ink Gems. When you exchange them, your two normal gems are dismantled and you receive a form-exclusive Primordial Ink.

This is best when it gives your run a stronger identity. It is risky when your current two gems are already carrying your Momo form, active skills, or damage plan.

For beginners, the important lesson is not “always take Primordial Ink.” The lesson is to ask whether the Primordial route improves the build you already have.

If you want practical examples of characters that can build around Primordial Ink, read the <Link href="/realm-of-ink/best-builds">Best Builds Guide</Link>. That guide covers tested routes where Primordial Ink becomes the center of the run instead of a random late pickup.

<h2 id="elixirs">
  Elixirs and breakable objects
</h2>

Elixirs are stat and scaling upgrades. Some are simple, but they matter because Realm of Ink builds often need multiple small bonuses working together.

Elixirs can improve:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>light attack damage,</li>
  <li>heavy attack damage,</li>
  <li>Ink Relic skill damage,</li>
  <li>status effect damage,</li>
  <li>crit rate and crit damage,</li>
  <li>HP, shield, damage reduction,</li>
  <li>drop rate and economy.</li>
</ul>

Break pots, bushes, and objects when it is safe. They can drop healing, coins, and Elixirs. Most drops are small, but over a full run they help you buy more at Way Stations and survive more mistakes.

The key is to match Elixirs to your build. Heavy attack Elixirs are great for heavy attack builds. Status effect damage is great for burning, bleeding, poison, and curse-adjacent setups. Pet bonuses matter when Momo or Ink Pets are actually part of your damage.

Higher difficulty can give better rewards and more Fox Blood, but it also makes weak builds easier to expose. Do not rush difficulty just because you want better drops.

Rupture is an extra modifier system for more challenge, achievements, unlocks, and character progression. Treat it as an endgame pressure tool, not a beginner requirement.

Use this progression:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Clear normal runs consistently.</li>
  <li>Understand why your builds succeed or fail.</li>
  <li>Try higher difficulty after your boss damage feels stable.</li>
  <li>Add Rupture only when you want extra challenge.</li>
</ol>

You access Endless through the Archive and Sanctum system. The important detail is that Endless uses the build from a completed run.

That means a normal clear is not always an Endless-ready clear. If your run barely survived, it may collapse quickly in Endless. If your build ended with strong scaling, good defense, and boss damage, it has a much better chance.

<h2 id="faq">
  Realm of Ink beginner FAQ
</h2>

### Are Curios the same as perks?

Yes. In Realm of Ink 1.0, perks are called Curios. Older guides, videos, and Steam discussions may still use the word perks.

### Should I choose Ink Gems first or Curios first?

During a run, Curios are usually harder to undo than Ink Gems. If you already have strong Curios, choose Ink Gems that support those Curios.

### How do I lunge forward with heavy attack?

Dash first, then hold heavy attack. This creates a forward lunge-style punish that helps you close distance more safely.

### Are purple challenge rooms worth it?

Enter purple challenge rooms when your build is strong and your HP is safe. Skip them when your damage is low, your HP is low, or the reward will not fix your current problem.

### Why do my own effects make fights hard to read?

Some Ink Gems, Momo forms, circles, pets, butterflies, lasers, and status effects create visual noise. Adjust Ink Gem transparency if your own effects hide enemy attacks.

### Can Momo projectiles hurt me?

Your own Momo effects may look dangerous, but they are part of your build. Learn which effects are yours so you do not dodge your own damage.

### What should I buy first at Way Stations?

If you are injured, buy healing food first. If your HP is safe, buy Curios, Elixirs, or Ink Gem upgrades that support your current build.

### What should I spend Fox Blood on first?

Prioritize upgrades that improve consistency, such as rerolls, starting options, and survival passives. Save niche upgrades for later.

### Should I always take Primordial Ink?

No. Primordial Ink is strong, but it replaces your normal two-Ink-Gem setup. Take it when it supports your current form and build.

### When should I increase difficulty?

Increase difficulty after normal clears feel stable. Dragon Hair Brush and Rupture are more enjoyable when you already understand your build and boss damage.

### What is Endless mode?

Endless mode uses a completed run's build through the Archive / Sanctum system. Enter with a strong final build, not one that barely cleared.

### Why does my damage fall off? 

First check your Curios. If two or more Curios reward the same attack type, status effect, pet damage, curse effect, or Ink Relic skill use, that is probably your real damage plan. Then check whether your Ink Gems and Elixirs multiply that plan or pull the run in a different direction. If your Curios say “heavy attack” but your Ink Gems and Elixirs only support pets or random status damage, your build will usually fall off before late bosses.
