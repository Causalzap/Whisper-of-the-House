<h2 id="how-the-codex-lumina-fragments-work">
  How the Codex Lumina fragments work
</h2>

The **Codex Lumina** is not an optional lore collectible in The Occultist.

It is a **main-story objective** inside **Redler Manor**.

After meeting **Amelia Redler**, Alan learns that **John Redler tore out the Codex’s core pages and hid them around the manor**. You must recover all four fragments and restore the book to understand how the curse can be ended.

That means this is not a side activity.

It is one of the most important late-game progression chains in the entire story.

<GuideCallout title="Why this guide matters">
The Codex Lumina fragment route matters for two reasons:

- it is required to continue the Redler Manor storyline
- it reveals the true structure of the ritual and the identity of the five sacrifices
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-many-codex-lumina-fragments-are-there">
  How many Codex Lumina fragments are there?
</h2>

There are **4 Codex Lumina fragments** in **Redler Manor**.

The damaged Codex makes this easy to misunderstand at first, because you are not collecting generic pages from random tables. These are the **central pages** of the book, and they are locked behind specific manor puzzles and hidden-room sequences.

So the clean way to think about this objective is:

**solve the four Codex fragment chains, then return and restore the book.**

<GuideCallout title="Do not confuse these with other collectibles">
The Codex pages are not medallions, altars, or optional mansion loot.

They are required story pages tied to puzzle completion.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="where-to-find-all-codex-lumina-fragments">
  Where to find all Codex Lumina fragments
</h2>

The four Codex fragments are found in these Redler Manor sequences:

1. **Clock-and-portrait room**
2. **Gabriel’s hidden childhood chamber**
3. **Painting-symbol puzzle room**
4. **Lion Key route and star-and-moon chamber**

If you are stuck, the important thing is not just knowing the room names.

It is knowing that each page is tied to a **specific puzzle solution**, not broad exploration.

<h2 id="fragment-1-clock-and-portrait-room">
  Fragment 1 — clock-and-portrait room
</h2>

The first Codex fragment is found in the **clock-and-portrait room**.

This is one of the earliest Codex gates in Redler Manor, and it is very easy to overcomplicate if you treat it like a vague mansion puzzle instead of a direct solution room.

### What you need

Before you can finish this room, you need:

- the **hour hand**
- the correct room access sequence
- the correct final clock setting

### Exact solution

1. Reach the portrait-and-clock room inside Redler Manor
2. Use the room clues and the hour-hand setup to complete the clock sequence
3. Set the clock to **10:00**
4. Once the time is correct, the room’s door opens as you approach
5. Collect the **first torn Codex page**

### What this page confirms

The first page begins revealing the sacrifice structure behind the ritual, including **Whitney** as the **disease sacrifice**.

6. Collect the **second Codex fragment**

### Why this room matters

This room does not just give you a page.

It explains why Gabriel ran, why the **Rebels** name matters, and why Alan is tied to the bloodline ending of the ritual.

<h2 id="fragment-3-painting-symbol-puzzle">
  Fragment 3 — painting-symbol puzzle
</h2>

The third Codex fragment comes from the **painting-symbol puzzle**.

This is the page that many players remember most clearly because it is one of the hardest puzzle gates in the Codex route.

### What you need

Before entering this room, you must complete the required access chain, including the **Mask** door sequence.

### Exact solution

1. Unlock the room that requires the **Mask**
2. Enter the painting room
3. Inspect the blank frame and the reference clues
4. Use the room’s puzzle logic to reproduce the correct **symbol**
5. Complete the drawing correctly
6. Collect the **third Codex fragment**

### What makes this puzzle hard

This puzzle is difficult because it does not test exploration.

It tests whether you can correctly identify and reproduce the required symbol without drifting away from the room’s actual logic.

<h2 id="fragment-4-star-and-moon-chamber">
  Fragment 4 — star-and-moon chamber
</h2>

The fourth and final Codex fragment comes from the **Lion Key route** and the **star-and-moon chamber**.

This is the last major page puzzle in Redler Manor and the one that completes the Codex.

### What you need

Before reaching the room, you need:

- the **Lion Key**
- access to the upper chamber route
- the **raven ability**
- the correct **star-and-moon button solution**

### Exact solution

1. Use the **Lion Key** to open the required upper path in Redler Manor
2. Enter the hidden **star-and-moon chamber**
3. Examine the planetary mechanism
4. Use the **raven ability** to trigger the mechanism correctly
5. Input the correct solution: **2 stars and 1 moon**
6. Open the mechanism and collect the **final Codex fragment**
7. Complete the Codex

### Why this fragment matters

This is not just the last page.

It is the final step that allows Alan to read the completed Codex and understand the full ritual structure.

<h2 id="what-the-completed-codex-reveals">
  What the completed Codex reveals
</h2>

Once all four fragments are restored, the **Codex Lumina** reveals the full structure of the ritual.

This is the real payoff of the entire Redler Manor sequence.

### The five sacrifices revealed by the Codex

The completed Codex identifies the ritual sacrifices as:

1. **Whitney** — **Disease**
2. **Rowan** — **War**
3. **Cole** — **Hunger**
4. **Borna / the death-linked sacrifice**
5. **Gabriel** — the **bloodline sacrifice**

This is the point where Alan fully understands what happened to his father and what his own role has become.

Gabriel was meant to be the final bloodline sacrifice.

Because Gabriel escaped, Alan is now the one tied to the ritual’s final bloodline logic.

<GuideCallout title="Why the Codex is such a big story moment">
The Codex does not just explain the past.

It also explains why Alan is no longer just investigating the curse — he has become central to ending it.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="why-players-get-stuck-on-the-codex-fragments">
  Why players get stuck on the Codex fragments
</h2>

Most players do not get stuck because Redler Manor is large.

They get stuck because the Codex route keeps changing its logic.

It moves between:

- hidden chambers
- name-based door solutions
- symbol puzzles
- key-gated routes
- raven-triggered interactions

### The most common mistakes

#### 1. Treating the Codex pages like passive collectibles

They are not.

These pages are **puzzle rewards**.

#### 2. Ignoring key-room logic

Each page is tied to a room that has to be solved correctly.

#### 3. Rushing past Gabriel’s room as “just lore”

That room is part of the actual fragment chain.

#### 4. Reaching the final chamber without the correct setup

The Lion Key route is not the end by itself. The chamber still has to be solved correctly.
