<h2 id="how-many-medallions-are-there-in-the-occultist">
  How many Medallions are there in The Occultist?
</h2>

There are **15 Medallions** in **The Occultist**.

This is one of the game’s main collectible lines, and it starts almost immediately. That matters because the game does **not** wait until the late chapters to begin hiding them. If you stop paying attention in the Farm, you can fall behind before the collectible route has even properly started.

The cleanest way to approach this page is simple:

**track Medallions by area and by running total.**

<GuideCallout title="Best tracking rule">
When the pendulum stops pulsing and no longer points anywhere useful, the immediate area is usually close to fully cleared.

That does not replace a proper sweep, but it is a strong signal that you can move on.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-you-need-before-hunting-medallions">
  What you need before hunting Medallions
</h2>

The Medallion route uses the same exploration tools as the rest of the game, but some matter much more here than they do in a story-only run.

### The tools that actually matter

- **Vera Visio / Sight of Truth** for revealing hidden pickups
- **Caecus Corvus** for grabbing Medallions that are out of reach
- the **Pendulum** for checking whether a local zone is mostly exhausted
- a habit of checking your count before leaving each chapter area

### Why players miss Medallions

Most misses do not happen because the collectible is brilliantly hidden.

They happen because of one of these three things:

- story momentum
- puzzle pressure
- assuming an area is “done” before it really is

<GuideCallout title="The biggest collector mistake">
The fastest way to lose a Medallion is to switch from “I am clearing this area” to “I am just following the next objective.”
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="farm-medallions">
  Farm Medallions
</h2>

The Medallion route starts immediately in the Farm, which is why this chapter matters more than most players expect.

If you miss collectibles here, you usually do not realize it until much later.

### Medallion 1/15
**Location:** The opening cave route near the house keys.  
**How to get it:** After entering the cave and reaching the box where you find the house keys, use **Vera Visio**. The Medallion is hidden near the keys and only becomes visible once the ability reveals it.

</h2>

The Orphanage is one of the easiest chapters to lose your Medallion count in.

Once **Caecus Corvus** enters the route, the collectible logic becomes more vertical, more indirect, and much easier to overlook.

**Location:** Outside stone statue seen through a wall opening.  
**How to get it:** After unlocking **Caecus Corvus**, look through the hole in the wall from inside the room. Send the raven toward the statue outside and retrieve the Medallion from it.

<h2 id="cemetery-medallions">
  Cemetery Medallions
</h2>

The Cemetery is where many collector runs go wrong.

This section mixes graves, ghost events, altars, catacombs, and maze routing, which makes it one of the worst places to trust memory alone.

### Medallion 9/15
**Location:** Tree above the sarcophagus near Gabriel’s crypt.  
**How to get it:** After opening the coffin section in the cemetery, look up toward the tree above the stone coffin near Gabriel’s crypt. Use **Caecus Corvus** to retrieve the Medallion from the branch.

**Location:** Drawer in the room near Gabriel’s post-cutscene area.  
**How to get it:** After the Gabriel cutscene triggers, move left and enter the **second door on the right**. Light the candles at the altar inside, then check the **left-side drawer**. The Medallion is hidden there.

Redler Manor is the chapter where story focus starts overriding collectible discipline.

If your count is wrong late in the game, this is one of the first places you should review carefully.

### Medallion 13/15
**Location:** Table downstairs after the clock puzzle.  
**How to get it:** Solve the manor clock puzzle and take the first Codex page from the pedestal. Then go down through the newly opened route and check the **table** below before climbing the metal ladder. The Medallion is sitting there.

<h2 id="how-to-find-the-last-missing-medallion">
  How to find the last missing Medallion
</h2>

If you are missing one Medallion late in the game, do not search the entire map randomly.

Use this order instead:

1. **Check the Orphanage first**
2. **Then check the Cemetery / Catacombs**
3. **Then check Redler Manor**
4. **Check the Temple approach last**

Why this order works:

Those are the areas where players are most likely to lose collectible discipline because route pressure starts competing with puzzle pressure and story urgency.

<GuideCallout title="Best last-Medallion rule">
The final missing Medallion is usually in the area where your priorities changed, not the area you remember best.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How many Medallions are there in The Occultist?

There are **15 Medallions** in total.

### Do I need Vera Visio for Medallions?

Yes. Some early Medallions are hidden until **Vera Visio** reveals them.

### Do I need Caecus Corvus for Medallions?

Yes. Several later Medallions cannot be collected without **Caecus Corvus**.

### What is the easiest Medallion to miss?

The easiest late misses are usually in the **Orphanage**, **Cemetery / Catacombs**, and **Redler Manor**.

### Where is the final Medallion in The Occultist?

The final Medallion is hanging from a **lantern before the Temple entrance**.

### What do you unlock for collecting all 15 Medallions?

Collecting all 15 unlocks **The Collector** achievement.
