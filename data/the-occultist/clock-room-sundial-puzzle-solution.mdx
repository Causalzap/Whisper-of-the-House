<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
To solve the **Clock Room — Sundial Puzzle** in **The Occultist**:

- use the **Hour Hand** and **Tempus Fugit** to set the **clock-faced door to 10:00**
- enter the **Clock Room**
- rotate the **two cranks** until the shadow matches the **Vera Visio clue**
- use **Tempus Fugit** to adjust the **minute hand length** so it matches the projection

The steps must be done **in order**. Solving the puzzle opens the opposite door and reveals a hidden floor compartment with **Codex Lumina Fragment 1**.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="where-is-the-clock-room-sundial-puzzle">
  Where is the Clock Room Sundial Puzzle in The Occultist?
</h2>

The **Clock Room** is in **Redler Manor on the first floor**.

Look for the **clock-faced door to the left of the piano area**. This is the entrance to the puzzle room.

This is not a free-standing one-step lock. It is a chained manor puzzle that starts outside the room and finishes inside it.

### What you need before this puzzle
- the **Hour Hand** from **Emilia’s room**
- **Tempus Fugit**
- **Vera Visio**

<GuideCallout title="Important requirement">
If you do not have the **Hour Hand** yet, you are too early for this puzzle. The Clock Room route starts at the locked clock-faced door, not inside the sundial area itself.
</GuideCallout>

---

<h2 id="clock-room-sundial-puzzle-solution">
  Clock Room and Sundial Puzzle solution
</h2>

This puzzle has **three linked steps**, and the order matters.

### Step 1 — set the clock-faced door to 10:00
Use the **Hour Hand** together with **Tempus Fugit** and set the clock-faced door to **10 o’clock exactly**.

That opens the way into the **Clock Room**.

### Step 2 — rotate the two cranks to match the Vera Visio projection
Once inside, use **Vera Visio** to check the correct shadow pattern.

Then rotate the **two cranks** until the sundial-style projection matches that clue.

### Step 3 — adjust the minute hand length
After the projection is aligned, use **Tempus Fugit** again to adjust the **minute hand length** so it matches the final projection correctly.

Once all three steps are completed, the puzzle resolves.

<h2 id="clock-room-puzzle-step-1-door-time">
  Step 1: Set the Clock Room door to 10:00
</h2>

Before you can solve the actual sundial puzzle, you have to unlock the room.

Use the **Hour Hand** you got from **Emilia’s room**, then apply **Tempus Fugit** to set the clock-faced door to **10:00**.

That is the correct entry setting.

<GuideCallout title="Door code rule">
The first part of this puzzle is not optional setup. If the door is not set to **10:00**, none of the room logic matters because you are still locked out.
</GuideCallout>

---

<h2 id="clock-room-puzzle-step-2-match-the-shadow">
  Step 2: Match the sundial shadow
</h2>

Inside the Clock Room, the puzzle changes from a lock puzzle into a **shadow-matching puzzle**.

Use **Vera Visio** to see the target pattern, then turn the **two cranks** until the projected shadow matches it.

This is the part most players spend the most time on, because the room looks more complex than it really is.

### What matters here
- the projection must match the **Vera Visio clue**
- both cranks affect the result
- this is a setup step for the final hand-length adjustment

---

<h2 id="clock-room-puzzle-step-3-adjust-the-minute-hand">
  Step 3: Adjust the minute hand length
</h2>

After you have the shadow lined up correctly, use **Tempus Fugit** to change the **minute hand length**.

This final adjustment has to match the projection you just created.

That is the last step of the puzzle.

<GuideCallout title="Common mistake">
A lot of players assume the crank alignment is the full solution.

It is not.

The puzzle is only complete after you also use **Tempus Fugit** to match the **minute hand length**.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="does-the-clock-room-puzzle-have-to-be-done-in-order">
  Does the Clock Room puzzle have to be done in order?
</h2>

Yes. This puzzle has to be done in **sequence**.

The order is:

1. set the door to **10:00**
2. match the shadow using the **two cranks**
3. adjust the **minute hand length**

You cannot skip to the last step first, and you cannot solve the interior projection before opening the room properly.

This is one of the reasons the Clock Room puzzle is more likely to stop players than a simple one-screen symbol puzzle.

<h2 id="what-do-you-get-from-the-clock-room-puzzle">
  What do you get from the Clock Room puzzle?
</h2>

Solving the Clock Room puzzle does two things:

- the **opposite door opens**
- a **hidden floor compartment appears**

Inside that hidden compartment, you get **Codex Lumina Fragment 1**.

That makes this one of the most important early manor puzzle pages to track, because it is tied directly to the **Codex Lumina** collectible route.

If you are cleaning up all Codex pages, use the <Link href="/the-occultist/codex-lumina-fragments/">Codex Lumina Fragments guide</Link> alongside this page.

<h2 id="why-players-get-stuck-on-the-clock-room-puzzle">
  Why players get stuck on the Clock Room puzzle
</h2>

This puzzle is harder than it first looks because it combines **three different mechanics**:

- door time setting
- shadow matching
- time-based hand adjustment

Players usually get stuck for one of four reasons:

- they do not realize the **door must be set to 10:00 first**
- they miss that **Vera Visio** gives the target projection
- they only adjust one crank and not both
- they forget the **minute hand length** is its own final step

### The short logic
- open the room first
- match the shadow second
- finish the hand length last

<h2 id="what-to-do-if-the-clock-room-puzzle-is-not-working">
  What to do if the Clock Room puzzle is not working
</h2>

If the puzzle is not resolving, check these in order:

1. **Make sure the clock-faced door is set to 10:00**
2. **Make sure you already have the Hour Hand from Emilia’s room**
3. **Use Vera Visio to confirm the target projection**
4. **Re-check both cranks**
5. **Make sure you adjusted the minute hand length with Tempus Fugit**
6. **Check the floor for the hidden compartment after the room resolves**

If the room still seems wrong, the most likely problem is not a hidden extra mechanic. It is usually that one of the three required stages was not fully completed.

<GuideCallout title="Best troubleshooting rule">
If this puzzle feels broken, do not restart from the middle.

Go back to the full sequence:

**10:00 door → shadow match → minute hand length**
</GuideCallout>
