<h2 id="how-cross-map-transfer-works-in-soulmask">
  How Cross-Map transfer works in Soulmask
</h2>

Cross-map play in **Soulmask 1.0** is real, but the first thing you need to understand is that the system works in **two completely different ways** depending on mode.

### There are two different transfer rulesets

1. **Single-player**
2. **Private / official servers**

That difference matters because a lot of players ask:

- Can I use my old save?
- Can I keep my tribe?
- Can I take my items?
- Do I need to restart?
- Can I bring ships back?

The answer changes depending on which mode you are using.

<GuideCallout title="The biggest Cross-Map mistake">
The biggest cross-map mistake is assuming that single-player and multiplayer follow the same transfer rules.

They do not.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="can-you-use-your-old-save-or-existing-character">
  Can you use your old save or existing character?
</h2>

Yes, but the answer depends on mode.

### Single-player answer

In **single-player**, seamless transfer is **not** available.

Instead, Soulmask 1.0 uses a **save inheritance** feature.

That means:

- you create a **new map**
- you choose whether to inherit from an existing save
- the game copies over **character data only**

### What single-player actually carries over

Single-player save inheritance carries over:

- your **main character**
- your **level**
- your **mask**
- your **technology tree**
- your **recorded tribesmen**

It does **not** carry over:

- buildings
- placed resources
- your old physical base state
- general world state from the original map

### Multiplayer / private server answer

On **private servers and official servers**, existing progression can transfer through the teleportation gate system.

That is the version people usually mean when they ask about “seamless transfer.”

<h2 id="single-player-cross-map-rules">
  Single-player Cross-Map rules
</h2>

Single-player is the easiest mode to misunderstand, so it is worth spelling out clearly.

### What single-player transfer is good for

Single-player transfer is good if you want to keep your:

- character progression
- mask progression
- technology progress
- recorded tribesmen data

and begin a **new world on a different map** without losing those systems.

### What single-player transfer is not

It is **not** a live world migration.

You are not carrying your whole settlement over.

You are not dragging all your stored resources into the new map.

You are not moving every placed object and structure with you.

### Best way to think about it

Single-player transfer is basically:

**character continuity, not world continuity.**

<GuideCallout title="Single-player reality check">
If you want your buildings, resource stockpiles, and placed world state to come with you, single-player transfer will disappoint you.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="private-and-official-server-cross-map-rules">
  Private and official server Cross-Map rules
</h2>

This is the version of cross-map transfer most players are hoping for.

### What servers allow

On private and official servers, the teleportation gate allows seamless transfer of:

- existing tribe members
- **tribesmen**
- **mounts**
- **masks**
- **regular items**

This is the real “travel between maps” version of the system.

### What happens to your tribe

Your tribe stays with you.

That means:

- you do **not** create a new tribe
- you do **not** rejoin it manually
- your tribe membership remains intact after switching maps

That is one of the biggest quality-of-life parts of server-based cross-map play.

  What carries over between Cloud Mist Forest and Shifting Sands?
</h2>

This is the part most players actually care about.

### Carries over in single-player save inheritance

- main character
- level
- mask
- technology tree
- recorded tribesmen

### Carries over on private / official servers

- tribe membership
- tribesmen

- regular items

- world resources as a complete transferred settlement

That is why Cross-Map transfer is best thought of as **character / tribe continuity**, not **whole-world duplication**.

The following are locked to **Shifting Sands**:

That means it is the opposite case: it is not your all-maps utility.

<h2 id="how-to-switch-maps-step-by-step">
  How to switch maps step by step
</h2>

The exact process depends on mode.

### Single-player step-by-step

1. Start the game
2. Create a **new save**
3. Choose the destination map
4. Use **save inheritance**
5. Select the source save
6. Confirm the inherited character data
7. Begin the new world on that destination map

### Private / official server step-by-step

1. Enter the current map
2. Control your **tribesman character**
3. Travel to the large **sea Stargate / Cross-Server Portal**
4. Interact with it
5. Choose the destination map / server
6. Transfer over
7. Unlock the **local scout** to fully reveal the destination map

  Do you need to start over for Shifting Sands?
</h2>

Not in the strict sense, but again it depends on mode.

### Single-player

You do need to create a **new map**, but you do **not** necessarily lose your character progression.

So the truthful answer is:

**new world, not full hard reset.**

### Private / official servers

No, not in the same way.

Server-based cross-map play is much closer to a real connected-world system.

That is why “Do I need to start over?” has two very different answers depending on where you play.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Can I play Shifting Sands with my old save?

Yes, but the answer depends on mode. In single-player, you use **save inheritance** when creating a new map. On servers, you can use the teleportation gate system.

### Do I need to start over for Shifting Sands?

Not completely. In single-player you create a new map but can inherit character data. On servers, transfer is much more seamless.

### What carries over in single-player?

Your main character, level, mask, technology tree, and recorded tribesmen.

### What carries over on private and official servers?

Your tribe membership, tribesmen, mounts, masks, and regular items.

### Can I bring ships back to Cloud Mist Forest?

No. Ships and their related systems are locked to **Shifting Sands**.

### Will my tribe come with me to Shifting Sands?

Yes. Your tribe membership is preserved when switching maps on servers.

### Do I need to unlock the map again after transferring?

Yes. After arriving, you still need to unlock the **local scout** to fully reveal the destination map.

### Is the Mysterious Mark cross-map?

No. The **Mysterious Mark** is locked to **Cloud Mist Forest**.
