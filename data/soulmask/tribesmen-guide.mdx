<h2 id="why-tribesmen-matter-in-shifting-sands">
  Why Tribesmen matter in Shifting Sands
</h2>

Tribesmen in **Shifting Sands** are not just backup bodies.

They are the difference between:

- doing every single task yourself
- and running an actual functioning settlement

The cleanest way to think about the system is simple:

**first make workers useful, then make them efficient, then make them specialized.**

That matters because early frustration usually comes from expecting full automation too soon.

The real progression is more like this:

1. **recruit your first worker**
2. **assign one simple job**
3. **fix missing materials and tools**
4. **stabilize farming and gathering**
5. **add animal and furnace loops**
6. **claim profession bonuses**
7. **build a 3-follower combat party**

<GuideCallout title="What this page covers">
This guide focuses on the **role system, assignments, worker loops, and deployed followers**.

If you later gather stronger direct Training Ground material, that can be added as a dedicated upgrade section rather than guessed at here.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-to-unlock-and-recruit-tribesmen">
  How to unlock and recruit Tribesmen
</h2>

The first big breakpoint is not some late tribe management menu.

It is simply getting access to **recruitment** early enough to stop doing everything alone.

### How recruitment starts

Once tribesmen recruitment is unlocked, you can begin taking enemies low, **deterring** them, and then recruiting them into your tribe.

That first recruit matters a lot because it turns the system from theory into actual labor.

### What happens after the first recruit

Your first tribesman immediately has:

- their own proficiencies
- their own skills
- their own separate body and stats
- potential job value you do not have to replace yourself

That is why the first recruit should not be treated as a disposable sidekick.

They are your first real production multiplier.

<h2 id="best-first-tribesmen-jobs">
  Best first Tribesmen jobs
</h2>

The best first assignments are not glamorous.

They are the boring jobs that remove friction.

### Best early jobs

The cleanest early jobs are:

1. **loom / linen**
2. **carpentry / planks**
3. **basic gathering**
4. **simple cooking support**

These work well because they feed the rest of the base.

If you solve those first, everything else becomes easier.

### Why linen and planks are so important

Early on, one worker is assigned to the loom for linen and another later gets pushed into making planks and hardwood planks.

That is exactly the right instinct.

These are not exciting jobs, but they are the jobs that unlock momentum.

<GuideCallout title="Early job rule">
Do not start with “best worker overall.”

Start with **the worker who removes the most friction from your current bottleneck**.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="why-tribesmen-stop-working">
  Why Tribesmen stop working
</h2>

This is the first big tribesmen trap.

If a worker looks broken, they often are not broken at all.

They are just missing something.

### Most common reason a worker stands still

The simplest example is the early loom assignment.

The worker is told to make linen, but nothing happens.

The reason is not AI failure.

The reason is **missing fiber**.

Once the materials are added, the worker starts doing the job properly.

### The four things to check first

If a tribesman is standing there doing nothing, check:

- **input materials**
- **tools**
- **job assignment**
- **work area / station access**

In practice, this solves a lot of “the worker system is bugged” moments.

<GuideCallout title="The biggest worker mistake">
The biggest early worker mistake is assuming a standing tribesman means bad AI.

Very often it means **you forgot the materials, tools, or job chain requirement**.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-farming-actually-works">
  How farming actually works
</h2>

Farming is where the system starts feeling like a real base sim instead of a survival checklist.

### What a farm loop actually needs

A proper farming setup is not just farmland.

You want:

- **farmland**
- **grainery**
- **seeds**
- **water access**
- eventually fertilizer support

Once those pieces are in place, one worker can start carrying a lot of the process.

### Farm manager role

The cleanest example here is assigning **Backpack Lady** as the farm manager.

Once set up properly, she can:

- plant
- water
- fertilize
- harvest

That is the kind of loop you should be trying to build.

Not “everyone does a little farming.”

One worker, one clear loop.

### What usually breaks the farm

Farm loops usually break because of:

- seeds not assigned properly
- missing water support
- missing storage support
- assuming the worker will improvise without the right setup

Once the watering problem is fixed with a water source and tanks, the farm starts functioning much more cleanly.

<h2 id="wild-collection-and-basic-resource-loops">
  Wild collection and basic resource loops
</h2>

Once farming is stable, the next clean win is **wild collection**.

### Gathering works best when the worker has a defined area

At first, one worker is placed at a collection yard.

Later, the setup improves by using the **bonfire as the work location** and directing gathering around that active area.

That change matters because it makes the collection loop feel much less random.

### What gatherers need

Gatherers still need:

- the correct tools
- a reasonable work area
- somewhere to deposit materials
- a clear purpose

Once that is in place, a gatherer can keep tree, bark, and other basic inputs flowing.

That helps every other production line.

<GuideCallout title="Gathering tip">
If your base is always short on wood, bark, fiber, or similar basics, the fix is usually **a better gathering loop**, not better combat.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="breeding-farm-and-animal-automation">
  Breeding farm and animal automation
</h2>

This is where the tribesmen system starts becoming genuinely high value.

### Why the chicken loop matters

Once you have a male and female pair, the game opens a clean low-tier animal loop that can produce:

- meat
- bones
- feathers
- other useful byproducts

That is strong because it feeds both survival and crafting.

### How the breeding setup works

The key pieces are:

1. capture and place the birds
2. make sure food and water are available
3. assign a worker to **breed and slaughter management**
4. define your population floor so the loop does not delete the whole flock

The setup shown here keeps a minimum number of males and females and only slaughters above that threshold.

That is exactly how you want to think about it.

### Why this loop is strong

This is one of the best examples of tribesmen doing real work for you.

Once it is stable, it becomes a steady material engine instead of a chore.

<h2 id="profession-bonuses-and-why-output-matters">
  Profession bonuses and why output matters
</h2>

This is where a lot of players underuse the system.

Workers do not just gain proficiencies.

They can become materially better producers.

### Why bonuses matter

One of the clearest examples is the carpenter worker whose output climbs high enough to produce **bonus planks**.

That is not a tiny quality-of-life perk.

That is a real multiplier.

### Best way to think about profession bonuses

Profession bonuses should be treated like this:

- early on, they are small improvements
- once a worker has been doing the same job for a long time, they become major efficiency gains

If one worker is loyal to one station for long enough, the long-term output starts stacking in a way you actually feel.

### When to claim them

Do not leave these bonuses unclaimed for too long.

Once a worker has proven they are part of a stable loop, claim the bonuses and make the loop stronger.

<GuideCallout title="Output bonus rule">
A stable worker with output bonuses is often more valuable than a fresh recruit with better-looking potential.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="position-based-production-and-furnace-work">
  Position-based production and furnace work
</h2>

This is the point where the base starts feeling responsive instead of clumsy.

### Why later production feels better

Once you start using proper **positions**, the system improves a lot.

A good example is finally understanding how the **kiln worker position** works.

Instead of manually telling someone to touch a station every time, the worker owns that production role.

That is a big quality jump.

### Furnace work

The same idea applies to smelting.

Once furnaces are online, they need dedicated labor.

That means tribesmen are not just optional helpers anymore.

They are part of the production chain.

A clean setup can split jobs like:

- one worker on tin
- one on copper
- one on bronze
- others on supporting stations

That is how you stop bottlenecking yourself on every ingot stage.

### Why this matters so much

The late lesson is simple:

**job positions scale better than one-off orders.**

If you are still micromanaging every station manually, your base will always feel worse than it should.

<h2 id="best-way-to-build-a-war-party">
  Best way to build a war party
</h2>

Tribesmen are not just for labor.

You can also deploy them as a small combat party.

### War party limit

You can have up to **3 tribesmen** fighting alongside you.

That matters a lot in Shifting Sands because even imperfect followers still provide:

- distraction
- extra damage
- breathing room in boss fights
- inventory support between fights

### What makes a good combat recruit

The run makes one important point very clearly:

not all tribesmen are equal.

Things that start to matter more later include:

- combat talents
- combat proficiencies
- max proficiency caps
- decent gear
- role fit

That means the best fighter is not always your first recruit.

### What deployed tribesmen are best at

Deployed followers are strongest when they:

- draw enemy attention
- survive long enough to keep pressure off you
- use decent spare gear
- support boss fights and camp clears

They are not always smart.

But they are still extremely valuable.

<GuideCallout title="Combat party tip">
A mediocre war party with gear and supplies is often better than a “perfect” war party you never actually finish building.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-tribesmen-still-do-badly">
  What Tribesmen still do badly
</h2>

The system is useful, but it is not magic.

### Common weak points

Tribesmen can still:

- path badly
- stop when inputs are wrong
- lag behind on follow behavior
- act confused in chaotic fights
- need babysitting during bosses

That is important because it keeps your expectations realistic.

The goal is not perfect AI.

The goal is a tribe that saves you enough time and pressure to be worth running.

<GuideCallout title="Best expectation rule">
Judge tribesmen by whether they make the base and combat **better overall**, not whether they behave perfectly every second.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you recruit Tribesmen in Soulmask: Shifting Sands?

Once recruitment is unlocked, you can weaken enemies, deter them, and recruit eligible targets into your tribe.

### What are the best first jobs for Tribesmen?

The best first jobs are usually **linen, planks, gathering, and simple support crafting**.

### Why is my Tribesman not working?

Usually because they are missing **materials, tools, or a proper job setup**, not because the entire system is broken.

### Can one Tribesman run the farm?

Yes. A dedicated farm manager can handle **planting, watering, fertilizing, and harvesting** if the farm loop is set up properly.

### Can Tribesmen automate breeding and slaughter?

Yes. With the right setup, a worker can manage breeding and slaughter to produce repeatable animal resources.

### Are profession bonuses worth taking?

Yes. Output bonuses can become very valuable once a worker has spent enough time on one discipline.

### How many Tribesmen can fight with you at once?

You can deploy up to **3 tribesmen** to fight alongside you.

### Are deployed Tribesmen good in boss fights?

Yes, mainly because they provide **distraction, extra damage, and pressure relief**, even if their AI is not always clean.

### Does this guide fully solve Training Ground progression?

Not yet. This version is built around the strongest confirmed tribesmen material in the current run, which is the **role, assignment, production, and follower** side.
