<h2 id="is-shifting-sands-good-for-new-players">
  Is Shifting Sands good for new players?
</h2>

Yes, but only if you approach it with the right mindset.

The biggest beginner mistake is assuming Shifting Sands should be played like a straight combat rush.

That usually creates three problems:

- you fight too much before your gear stabilizes
- you delay tribe and base systems too long
- you treat the first boss wall like a mandatory early stat check

The cleaner way to play is:

**use the first 2 hours to stabilize mobility, storage, workers, ore income, and your first real base location.**

<GuideCallout title="Best beginner mindset">
In Shifting Sands, the first 2 hours are not about proving you can kill everything.

They are about making sure the game stops pushing you around.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-you-should-do-in-the-first-20-minutes">
  What you should do in the first 20 minutes
</h2>

Your real start is not “wander and see what happens.”

It is a short sequence of early stabilizers.

### 1. Repair the Celestial Wings

This is the first big power spike.

The wings immediately change how the opening feels because they give you a mobility tool before you really understand the map.

### 2. Loot the starter gear and use the starter food

Very early on, the run picks up strong starter armor and broth from early chests.

That huge stat jump matters.

It means the opening stops being a naked scramble and starts being a real route.

### 3. Claim the starter ship

Once the early chest sequence gives ship materials and workbenches, the nearby hull effectively becomes your ship.

That is your first major anchor point.

### 4. Do not overbuild yet

At this stage, the ship is best used for:

- storage
- safety
- bench placement
- route control

Not for trying to make your forever base immediately.

<h2 id="what-you-should-do-before-the-first-hour-ends">
  What you should do before the first hour ends
</h2>

Once the ship is under control, the next job is to stop being alone.

### Unlock recruitment early

As soon as tribesmen recruitment is available, it becomes one of the best early upgrades you can get.

That is because your first worker is not just a body.

They are your first real labor multiplier.

### Recruit at least one useful tribesman

Your first recruit helps smooth out the opening by taking on simple tasks like:

- linen
- planks
- gathering
- future support work

That matters because early Shifting Sands overwhelms you with too many small tasks at once.

### Interrogate a scout and open the map

This is another huge beginner checkpoint.

Once you interrogate a scout, the map opens up and nearby points of interest become much easier to route cleanly.

That is a much better use of time than random blind wandering.

<h2 id="what-to-prioritize-in-your-first-real-resource-loop">
  What to prioritize in your first real resource loop
</h2>

The most important early beginner lesson is that **ore matters more than ego**.

### Copper and tin are priority resources

Once the early route opens up, copper and tin become much more important than trying to win every camp fight.

These resources help push:

- tools
- awareness progression
- later smelting and tribe growth
- your ability to stop improvising

### Why mining is so strong early

Mining is valuable not just because of materials.

It also pushes experience and account progress efficiently.

That means it helps both your gear plan and your overall growth.

### What beginners usually get wrong here

A lot of players spend too long on bad fights when they should be doing one of these instead:

- securing ore
- replacing broken tools
- building beast bone tools
- opening map routes
- bringing materials back to the ship

<GuideCallout title="Best early priority rule">
If you are choosing between a messy fight and a clean ore run, the clean ore run is usually better for your first 2 hours.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="when-you-should-try-sobec">
  When you should try Sobec
</h2>

You **can** try Sobec early.

You just should not treat the first attempt like a pass/fail test.

### What the early Sobec attempt teaches you

The first attempt is useful mainly because it exposes your weaknesses.

The run makes that painfully clear:

- the damage feels overwhelming
- the setup is still too thin
- there are not enough food buffs
- there are not enough allies helping
- the route is not stable enough yet

That is exactly why the first try is valuable.

It tells you what is missing.

### Best beginner rule for Sobec

Do **not** interpret an early Sobec failure as “my route is dead.”

Interpret it as:

**I have reached the point where the game is asking me to stabilize first.**

### What to improve before the real attempt

Before committing harder, beginners should try to improve:

- food buffs
- basic gear quality
- support from tribesmen
- confidence in ranged damage or safe damage
- overall survivability

<h2 id="when-to-stop-using-the-ship-as-your-main-base">
  When to stop using the ship as your main base
</h2>

This is one of the most important beginner decisions in the whole DLC.

### Early on, the ship is enough

At first, the ship works well as:

- mobile storage
- bench space
- cooking point
- respawn support
- moving platform

That is exactly why the early route feels manageable.

### Then the ship stops being enough

Once the game starts asking for things like:

- bonfire progression
- foundations
- farmland
- more workstations
- tribe-level requirements

the ship turns into a cramped compromise.

That is the moment beginners should stop pretending the boat clutter is a permanent solution.

### When to place the bonfire base

The clean answer is:

**place a real bonfire base once your tribe progression and farm requirements clearly exceed what the ship can comfortably hold.**

That is the point where the route becomes much smoother.

<h2 id="what-your-beginner-base-should-actually-do">
  What your beginner base should actually do
</h2>

A good beginner base is not supposed to be beautiful.

It is supposed to solve pressure.

### Your first real base should handle these jobs

- basic storage
- tribe progression requirements
- farmland
- grainery support
- water access
- simple worker loops
- room for future workstations

### Why this matters

The moment farming and worker loops start working, your run changes.

Now you are no longer restarting every problem from zero.

Instead, you are building momentum.

That is the real goal of a good beginner route.

<GuideCallout title="Beginner base rule">
If your base reduces panic, it is a good beginner base.

It does not need to look good yet.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="common-early-mistakes">
  Common early mistakes
</h2>

These are the biggest mistakes beginners make in Shifting Sands:

### 1. Treating the first hour like a pure combat rush

That usually just burns durability, healing, and time.

### 2. Leaving recruitment too late

A worker early is stronger than solo busywork.

### 3. Wandering without opening the map properly

Scout information is worth more than random running.

### 4. Forcing Sobec too soon

That turns a useful skill check into a frustrating wall.

### 5. Staying on the ship too long

The starter ship is excellent early, but it is not your whole game plan.

### 6. Building too pretty, too early

In the first 2 hours, function matters far more than looks.

<GuideCallout title="Best anti-tilt rule">
If the route starts feeling messy, the answer is usually not “fight harder.”

It is “stabilize one system at a time.”
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should you do first in Soulmask: Shifting Sands?

Repair the **Celestial Wings**, loot the **starter gear**, and claim the **starter ship**.

### Should you recruit tribesmen early in Shifting Sands?

Yes. Early recruitment is one of the best first-hour upgrades because it removes a lot of solo friction.

### What resources matter most in the first 2 hours?

Basic materials matter, but **copper and tin** are the first major progression resources you should care about.

### Should you fight Sobec early?

You can test the fight early, but beginners should not treat the first attempt like a hard requirement to clear immediately.

### When should you place your first real base?

Once the ship is no longer comfortably handling your storage, tribe progression, farmland, and workstation needs.

### Is the starter ship your permanent base?

Not usually. It is best treated as an early **mobile base** and transition platform.

### What should your first real base include?

At minimum: **storage, bonfire progression, farmland, grainery support, water access, and room for worker loops**.

### What is the biggest beginner mistake in Shifting Sands?

Trying to brute-force combat progression before stabilizing your workers, ore income, and first proper base.
