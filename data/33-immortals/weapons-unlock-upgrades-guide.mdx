<h2 id="bow-of-hope">
  Bow of Hope: safest ranged start
</h2>

Bow is strong because its loop is repeatable: shoot, reposition, recall. The recall is not just an ammo trick. It can damage and stun enemies on the return path, so your positioning before recall decides how much value you get.

The main beginner mistake is recalling while standing in danger. The recall window can slow your movement, so treat recall like a planned damage line, not a panic button.

<h2 id="glaive-of-temperance">
  Glaive of Temperance: high ceiling, spacing required
</h2>

Glaive is strong because Temperance rewards clean chaining and spacing. Light and heavy attacks should flow together, and your damage rises sharply once the Temperance meter is above half.

The real trick is distance. If you stand too close, you may lose the best edge-hit value. If you stand too far, you whiff or lose pressure. Glaive is S-tier because the ceiling is high, not because it plays itself.

<h2 id="crossbows-of-pride">
  Crossbows of Pride: brand first, spend Pride later
</h2>

Crossbows are one of the cleanest strong weapons for new players because the loop is easy to remember: **brand first, build Pride, then spend it with Heavy Bolt**.

The mistake is swapping targets too early. If you brand an enemy and then keep shooting something else, your damage plan falls apart. Against bosses and elites, brand the priority target and stay disciplined.

<h2 id="sword-of-justice">
  Sword of Justice: spend Justice on offense or safety
</h2>

Sword is built around **Justice**. Basic attacks generate Justice, and you spend it on Heavy Slash or Guard.

That makes Sword a strong learning weapon if you want to understand timing. Spend Justice on damage when the fight is safe. Save Justice for Guard when the boss or elite is about to punish the group. Bad Sword play spends all Justice before the defensive moment arrives.

<h2 id="staff-of-sloth">
  Staff of Sloth: control works best near allies
</h2>

Staff is not only about personal damage. Its Sloth loop gives you control, and Torpor becomes valuable when teammates can hit slowed enemies.

That means Staff is stronger when you play near the group. If you slow a pack and nobody is nearby to punish it, the weapon feels worse than it really is.

<h2 id="hooks-of-gluttony">
  Hooks of Gluttony: pull, parry, then punish
</h2>

Hooks sit between mobility and brawling. The hook lets you pull into enemies, while Parry can convert enemy attacks into your own pressure.

The mistake is using Hook as a blind engage. Pulling into a dangerous pack without a parry plan is how Hooks players die early.

<h2 id="daggers-of-greed">
  Daggers of Greed: high risk melee commitment
</h2>

Daggers build Greed and spend it on Takedown. That sounds simple, but Takedown is commitment. If you lunge at the wrong moment, the weapon can move you into danger.

Daggers are not bad because they lack damage. They are risky because the weapon asks you to already understand enemy timing, boss openings, and escape routes.

<h2 id="scepter-of-charity">
  Scepter of Charity: 1.0 support-specialist weapon
</h2>

Scepter of Charity is a 1.0 weapon built around mid-to-long range support and **Blind**. Blinded enemies run away, take more damage from attacks, and take damage over time for a short window.

That makes Scepter valuable in messy groups and boss routes where weakening enemies matters. Its top-end tier is still being assessed, so treat it as a support-specialist weapon first and a solved DPS tier pick second.

The key number is **48%**. Each mastered Weapon Upgrade gives **1% damage**, and mastering all **48 Weapon Upgrades** gives **48% damage**. That does not mean you should grind all 48 immediately. It means every serious main weapon should eventually be part of a mastery plan.

Known mastery stat signals can also affect your long-term main choice. Commonly listed examples include **Bow of Hope** leaning into **Attack**, **Sword of Justice** leaning into **Vitality**, **Daggers of Greed** leaning into **Critical Damage**, and **Staff of Sloth** leaning into **Empathy**. For **Glaive**, **Crossbows**, **Hooks**, and **Scepter**, confirm the current stat signal in the Weapon Shrine or Compendium before making a long-term investment.

<h2 id="upgrade-priority">
  What should you upgrade first?
</h2>

Upgrade the weapon you can already play under pressure.

A tier-list weapon that you cannot execute is not a good first upgrade target. Your first serious upgrade path should help you clear Chambers, survive bosses, and keep the same weapon equipped long enough to build mastery.

<h2 id="faq">
  33 Immortals weapons FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best weapon in 33 Immortals 1.0?

The clearest current high-ceiling signal is Bow of Hope, Glaive of Temperance, and Crossbows of Pride. See the <a href="#weapons-guide">tier signal table</a>.

### What weapon should I unlock first?

Most new players should start with Bow of Hope or Crossbows of Pride for safe ranged learning, then test Sword, Staff, Scepter, Glaive, Hooks, or Daggers based on role.

### Is Bow of Hope good?

Yes. Bow is one of the safest strong picks because recall gives ranged damage, stun value, and a repeatable learning loop.

### Is Glaive of Temperance worth learning?

Yes, if you want a high-ceiling melee weapon. It needs Temperance meter control and spacing, so it is stronger after practice.

### Are Crossbows of Pride beginner-friendly?

Yes. Crossbows are strong because the loop is clear: brand first, build Pride, then spend Pride with Heavy Bolt.

### Is Sword of Justice good for beginners?

Sword is good if you want frontline control. Learn to spend Justice on Guard when safety matters and Heavy Slash when the fight is safe.

### Is Scepter of Charity good?

Scepter is a 1.0 support-specialist weapon built around Blind and safer mid-to-long range team value. Its final tier ranking is still being assessed.

### How much do weapons cost to unlock?

As of 1.0, all weapons cost 50 Eternal Shards to unlock. Scepter of Charity is unlocked at its Weapon Shrine in Dark Woods for 50 Eternal Shards.

### How much does the second weapon upgrade slot cost?

The first upgrade slot is free. The second weapon upgrade slot costs 1000 Eternal Shards and 5 Paragons.

### How does Weapon Mastery work?

Weapon upgrades have a Master Gauge. Each mastered upgrade gives 1% damage, and mastering all 48 Weapon Upgrades gives 48% damage.

### Should I follow a weapon tier list?

Use tier lists as a signal, not as a command. A top-tier weapon only helps if you understand its resource loop.

Read the <Link href="/33-immortals/relics-paths-wishing-guide">Relics, Paths and Wishing Guide</Link> to build around your weapon, or the <Link href="/33-immortals/bosses-guide">Boss Mechanics Guide</Link> if your weapon feels fine but boss fights still collapse.
