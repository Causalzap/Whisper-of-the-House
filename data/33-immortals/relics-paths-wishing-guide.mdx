<h2 id="relics-guide">
  33 Immortals relics guide
</h2>

Relics are one of the biggest build systems in **33 Immortals 1.0**.

The important shift is that Relics are no longer just random bonuses you grab because the rarity color looks good. Relics now belong to Paths, use different trigger types, can appear at different rarity tiers, can be upgraded with Bones, can be dismantled, and can be targeted through Relic Wishing.

That means the better question is not only “which relic is strongest?”

The better question is:

<strong>Can my weapon and role trigger this relic often enough to make it worth building around?</strong>

For first-run basics, read the <Link href="/33-immortals/beginner-progression-guide">Beginner Progression Guide</Link>. If you need to choose a weapon before planning relics, read the <Link href="/33-immortals/weapons-unlock-upgrades-guide">Weapons Unlock and Upgrades Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="how-relics-work">
  How Relics work after the 1.0 update
</h2>

The 1.0 relic system is built around choices, Paths, and long-term discovery.

At the start of a run, Relic choices give you an early direction. You can spend Bones to reroll on the selection screen. Relics can appear at different rarity tiers. <h2 id="path-of-honor">
  Best starting Path: why Honor is still safe
</h2>

If you only want one safe starting recommendation, start by testing **Path of Honor**.

Honor is still a good foundation because it supports safer damage scaling and works well when your weapon can trigger its Path effects consistently. The important point is not “force Honor every run.” The important point is to recognize when your current weapon, stats, and team position can actually use it.

Use Honor when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>your weapon can trigger its relic effects often,</li>
  <li>you need safer damage instead of pure greed,</li>
  <li>you are playing near enough to allies for team value to matter,</li>
  <li>or your run already has Co-Strike, stat conversion, or support effects that fit the plan.</li>
</ul>

Do not force Honor only because it is a strong starting idea. A weaker trigger is still a weaker build.

<h2 id="relics-by-weapon">

</h2>

If your relic asks for Light Attacks, a fast weapon may love it. If it asks for Heavy Attacks, a weapon that rarely uses heavy attacks may not get enough value. If it asks for Co-op Power play, it is much stronger when you stay near allies and actually use the power.

<h2 id="duo-relic-targets">
  Duo Relics: chase them only after your base build works
</h2>

Duo Relics are still worth caring about, but do not chase named Duo targets until you can confirm their Path pair and effect in your current relic pool.

For this guide, treat Duo Relics as a decision rule rather than a database:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Start with one working Path first.</strong> A scattered build with two weak Path pieces is worse than one stable Path.</li>
  <li><strong>Add a second Path only when it naturally helps your weapon.</strong> Do not take a second Path just because a Duo Relic might appear later.</li>
  <li><strong>Check the effect before upgrading.</strong> If the Duo Relic does not improve what your build already does, save your Bones.</li>
</ul>

A good Duo Relic connects two things your run is already doing. A bad Duo chase makes you spend the whole run waiting for a payoff that never becomes stable.

<h2 id="faq">
  33 Immortals relics FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best Relic Path in 33 Immortals?

For most players in 1.0, Path of Honor is the safest starting foundation when your weapon can trigger it consistently. Unity is also very strong for Co-op Power and team-focused builds.

### Why is Path of Honor good?

Honor is good because Inner Fire supports safer damage scaling. It is strongest when your weapon can trigger the Path often and your run already benefits from steady uptime.

### Should I always pick the highest rarity relic?

No. A lower rarity relic that supports your weapon, trigger, Path, or survival plan can be better than a higher rarity relic that does not fit the run.

### What does Bloodstained Stole do?

Bloodstained Stole makes your Co-op Power generate much faster. It is strong for support roles, Scepter, Staff, and builds that want frequent Co-op Power use.

### What does Seraphic Tear do?

Seraphic Tear makes part of your Vitality also count as Empathy. It is useful when your build already leans into survivability but still wants more team-oriented value.

### What does Emerald of Princes do?

Emerald of Princes makes your Co-Strike counter start at 20. It is useful when your build cares about reaching Co-Strike thresholds quickly.

### What does Tusk Of Ciriatto do?

Older tooltips describe Tusk Of Ciriatto as a payoff relic for enemies affected by Stunned, Weak, Exposed, or Marked. In 1.0, verify the current tooltip before upgrading around it.

### When should I reroll Relics?

Reroll when both choices are bad for your build and you still have enough Bones for healing, keys, upgrades, or the next objective.

### When should I dismantle a Relic?

Consider dismantling when the relic is clearly off-plan, does not trigger with your weapon, and is not worth upgrading or keeping for the next fight.

### How do Duo Relics work?

Duo Relics combine two Path directions. They are strongest after your first Path already works, so do not chase a Duo Relic before your base build is stable.

### How does Relic Wishing work?

As of 1.0, Relic Wishing lets you Wish for one Action Relic from a specific Path. It costs 10 Eternal Shards, and the same Relic can be Wished up to 3 times.

### How do I complete Prayer Answered?

Wish for a relic, then find and equip that relic in the Beyond. Choose a Wish target you can recognize during the run.

### What are good boss Relics?

Good boss Relics depend on your team. Shrunken Papal Heart helps unstable teams enter the boss fully healed, Coffer of the Devoted helps maintain Co-Strike longer after the 1.0 rework, and Polished Monstrance helps long fights with Co-op Power recovery.
