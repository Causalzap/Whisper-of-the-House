For your first runs in **33 Immortals**, chase **question marks, chests, jars, bones, and useful fights** instead of clearing random enemies. Spend your purple level-up early, use **Bone Shrines** before hard rooms, avoid soloing **Unique Elites**, equip a revive or survival-focused **Perk** when your first slot opens, and enter **Torture Chambers** with health, players, and a key when possible.

The simple rule is: **build power before Chambers, communicate before splitting, and stop farming before Ascension**.

<h2 id="beginner-progression-guide">
  33 Immortals beginner progression guide
</h2>

The hardest part of learning **33 Immortals** is not memorizing every weapon, relic, or boss. It is learning the rhythm of a run.

Most new-player deaths do not come from one impossible boss attack. They come from small mistakes stacking together:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>fighting away from rewards,</li>
  <li>holding unspent level-up power,</li>
  <li>entering Chambers low,</li>
  <li>trying to solo dangerous elites,</li>
  <li>using shrines too late,</li>
  <li>or arriving at Ascension after the group has already moved.</li>
</ul>

This guide is built around that timeline. First you learn how to turn the opening minutes into power. Then you learn when to spend resources, when to follow the group, when to avoid bad fights, and how to diagnose why a run failed.

For the main hub, read the <Link href="/33-immortals">33 Immortals Guide Hub</Link>. If you are already reaching bosses but wiping to mechanics, use the <Link href="/33-immortals/bosses-guide">Boss Mechanics Guide</Link> after this.

<h2 id="first-10-minutes">
  What to do in the first 10 minutes
</h2>

Your first job is not to clear the entire map. Your first job is to turn map time into useful power.

Look for:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>question marks</strong> for events and rewards,</li>
  <li><strong>chests and jars</strong> for bones and supplies,</li>
  <li><strong>nearby enemies</strong> that guard something useful,</li>
  <li><strong>purple level-up choices</strong> when your upgrade is ready,</li>
  <li><strong>shrines</strong> before risky rooms,</li>
  <li><strong>keys</strong> before important Chambers.</li>
</ul>

Do not chase every enemy across empty space. Enemies are everywhere. Rewards are not.

A good beginner route keeps you close enough to the team, close enough to objectives, and rich enough in bones to recover before the run turns dangerous.

<h2 id="level-up-stat-choice">
  Spend your purple level-up before the next hard room
</h2>

The purple level-up choice is one of the easiest forms of power to waste. If you keep fighting while an upgrade is ready, you are making the next fight harder for no reason.

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

Pick the stat that fixes your current problem.

If you keep dying before Chambers, choose health or sustain. If you are surviving but fights are slow, choose damage. If you are playing near the team and reviving often, team sustain can be more valuable than it looks.

<h2 id="beginner-perks">
  Best beginner Perks: start with revive or survival
</h2>

Perks are easy to ignore when you are new because the game already asks you to learn weapons, relics, shrines, Chambers, and bosses. Do not ignore them.

As soon as your first Perk slot opens, equip something that fixes the most common beginner problem: deaths snowballing.

<GuideTable
caption="Beginner Perk priority."
columns={perkColumns}
rows={perkRows}
/>

A revive-focused Perk is the safest early recommendation because public runs often collapse from missed revives and gray health, not from low damage alone. Once your runs feel stable, you can shift into damage, weapon-specific, or Co-op Power-focused Perks.

Do not leave a Perk slot empty while waiting for the perfect build. A practical Perk now is better than an ideal Perk you never equip.

<h2 id="bones-shrines-and-teleport">
  Bones, Shrines, keys, and teleport access
</h2>

Bones are not just money. They are your run safety budget.

You use them to heal, buy keys, gain teleport access, reroll or upgrade important choices, and recover before major fights. If you spend every bone greedily, you may reach the next Chamber with no way to fix the run.

<GuideTable
caption="Key beginner resources and shrine timing."
columns={resourceColumns}
rows={resourceRows}
/>

The best shrine habit is simple: **use shrine value before danger, not after the run has already collapsed**.

A heal is strongest before the Chamber. A key matters before the locked chest. Teleport access matters before the team rotates. A temporary shrine effect matters before a fight that will actually use it.

<h2 id="torture-chambers">
  How to approach Torture Chambers
</h2>

Torture Chambers are where a messy run starts to show its problems.

Before entering, check four things:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Do you have enough health?</li>
  <li>Did you spend your level-up?</li>
  <li>Do you have a key or enough bones to recover?</li>
  <li>Are enough players entering the right Chamber?</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="unique-elites">
  Unique Elites: do not solo them early
</h2>

Unique Elites are one of the easiest ways for a new player to throw away a good opening.

They look like a challenge you should take immediately. In reality, they are often group targets. If you are alone, under-upgraded, or already low, walking away is the correct play.

<GuideTable
caption="How to handle Unique Elites as a beginner."
columns={uniqueEliteColumns}
rows={uniqueEliteRows}
/>

If you see a Unique Elite near a reward, use the map, emote, or ping system to pull attention. Do not turn an optional elite into a solo death just because it is on your screen.

<h2 id="secret-chambers">
  Secret Chambers are high risk, high reward
</h2>

Secret Chambers are not just harder Torture Chambers. They are a risk decision.

A successful Secret Chamber can reward surviving players with a **Legendary Relic**, which is why strong players may rush toward one when the run is healthy. That reward is real, but so is the danger.

Use this rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Enter if you are healthy, upgraded, and grouped.</li>
  <li>Enter if the team can spare the players.</li>
  <li>Do not enter if you are already low, alone, or unsure of the room.</li>
  <li>Do not follow blindly just because the door is open.</li>
</ul>

The wrong lesson is “never enter Secret Chambers.” The right lesson is “do not enter them like they are free.”

<h2 id="ascension-starts">
  When Ascension starts, stop farming
</h2>

Ascension changes the run.

Once the team begins moving toward Ascension, the value of side farming drops sharply. This is where many new players lose the run without realizing it. They stay behind for one more reward, arrive late, lose health, or force the team to fight underfilled.

<figure className="my-8">

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    When the run shifts toward Ascension, stop playing alone. Check which group needs help and regroup before the team runs out of players.
  </figcaption>

Your priority becomes:

Do not spend your last bones on a small upgrade right before the boss route unless that upgrade directly fixes the run. A safer beginner rule is to enter Ascension with enough bones for one emergency decision. If you cannot afford that, stop taking greedy side fights and regroup earlier.

<h2 id="random-lobby-communication">
  Random lobby communication: emotes, pings, and map awareness
</h2>

Random lobbies are not hopeless. They just need simple communication.

You do not need to type an essay or lead 32 other players. You need to read the basic signals:

<GuideTable
caption="How to read random lobby signals."
columns={lobbyColumns}
rows={lobbyRows}
/>

The biggest random-lobby upgrade is learning when to follow and when to fill gaps.

If three players ping a gate, they probably need bodies. If everyone runs to one side and the map shows another Chamber underfilled, rotate. If a player clearly understands the route, copy their movement until you know the pattern yourself.

The worst random-lobby habit is silent solo play. You do not have to command the lobby, but you do need to respond to it.

A failed run is useful if you know what killed it.

Do not just say “bad team” or “bad build.” Ask where the run broke.

This is the main difference between a beginner who improves and a beginner who repeats the same wipe. Your next run should fix the exact stage that killed the last one.

<h2 id="what-to-upgrade-first">
  What should you upgrade first?
</h2>

In your first runs, do not try to build a perfect endgame setup. Upgrade what helps you survive the next phase.

Good early priorities are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Revive or survival Perk:</strong> first priority when your slot opens and public runs are messy.</li>
  <li><strong>Health or sustain stat:</strong> if you keep dying before Chambers or bosses.</li>
  <li><strong>Damage stat:</strong> if you are already surviving but fights take too long.</li>
  <li><strong>Relic upgrades:</strong> when the relic supports your current weapon, Path, or team role.</li>
  <li><strong>Weapon progression:</strong> after you know which weapon you actually enjoy using.</li>
</ul>

Do not spread resources just because every menu has something to click. Your first goal is a stable run, not a perfect account.

<h2 id="faq">
  33 Immortals beginner progression FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in 33 Immortals?

Start with the <a href="#first-10-minutes">first 10 minutes route</a>: move toward question marks, chests, jars, bones, and useful reward fights.

### Should I save my purple level-up for later?

No. Spend it when a useful choice appears. See <a href="#level-up-stat-choice">level-up stat choices</a>.

### What Perk should beginners use first?

A revive-focused or survival Perk is the safest early pick, especially in random lobbies. See <a href="#beginner-perks">beginner Perks</a>.

### Should I fight Unique Elites alone?

No. Unique Elites are group targets early. Back off, ping, and return with players. See <a href="#unique-elites">Unique Elites</a>.

### Are Secret Chambers worth entering?

Yes, when your group is ready. They can reward surviving players with a Legendary Relic, but they are risky. See <a href="#secret-chambers">Secret Chambers</a>.

### When should I use Shrines?

Use shrine value before dangerous rooms, Ascension, or boss routes. Waiting too long makes the shrine less useful.

### What should I do when Ascension begins?

Stop farming, check the map, regroup, and use teleport access if walking would make you late. See <a href="#ascension-starts">Ascension timing</a>.

### How do I play better in random lobbies?

Watch emotes, pings, map movement, and group behavior. Follow coordinated players or fill underfilled objectives. See <a href="#random-lobby-communication">random lobby communication</a>.

### Why do my runs fall apart before the boss?

Check where you died: before Chamber, inside Chamber, during Secret Chamber, while rotating, or at the boss with no resources. Use the <a href="#failed-run-diagnosis">failed run diagnosis</a> table.

### What should I read next?

Read the <Link href="/33-immortals/bosses-guide">Boss Mechanics Guide</Link> if your team reaches Ascension but wipes to mechanics, or the <Link href="/33-immortals/weapons-unlock-upgrades-guide">Weapons Guide</Link> if you need a weapon plan.
