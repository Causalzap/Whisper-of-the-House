<h2 id="where-to-find-little-my">
  Where to find Little My
</h2>

You first find Little My near Moominhouse after the route to Fillyjonk is blocked by deep snow.

The game points you toward Moominmamma's garden because Moomintroll needs the shovel. Little My has taken it, and she will only give it back if you beat her in a snowball fight.

Later, after you have progressed through the house guest chain and upgraded your mittens, Too-ticky points you toward the pond to find Little My again.

The first fight is the easiest Little My fight. It is short and usually takes about three clean hits.

This fight is not about speed. It is about not wasting shots.

<h2 id="later-little-my-rematches">
  Later Little My rematches
</h2>

Little My returns later near the pond / slide route.

These rematches are not just repeats of the first fight. The pond arena is more open, cover matters less, and slide / ice movement makes it easier to lose your angle.

The key adjustment is to stop following Little My through every movement. Hold better positions and let her cross open lanes.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I beat Little My in Moomintroll Winter's Warmth?

Wait for Little My to move into open space, throw one snowball, then reposition. Do not chase her nonstop or hold your aim for too long.

### Where is Little My?

You first meet Little My near Moominhouse after the route to Fillyjonk is blocked by deep snow. Later, you find her again around the pond / slide route.

### How do I get the shovel?

Beat Little My in the first snowball fight. She gives back Moominmamma's shovel after you win.

### How many times do I need to hit Little My?

The first fight takes about three clean hits; later pond fights take more, with the final round taking the longest.

### Why do I need the shovel?

The shovel clears deep snow, opens the route to Fillyjonk, reveals firewood, and helps with collectible cleanup.

### Is the first Little My fight hard?

No. It is mostly a timing fight. Use the snowmen for cover and throw only when Little My is in the open.

### Why is the pond fight harder?

The pond arena is more open, has fewer cover spots, and includes slide / ice movement. It also requires more hits than the first shovel fight.

### Do I have to beat the later Little My fights?

Yes, if you want to progress her Great Winter Bonfire involvement. The later fights are part of convincing Little My to help bring spring back.

### What should I do if I keep missing?

Hold a stable position, wait for Little My to cross open space, then throw slightly ahead of her path.

### Can I use the shovel immediately after the fight?

Yes. After winning the first fight, you can use the shovel to clear deep snow and continue toward Fillyjonk.

### Does Little My give a vase piece?

The main reward from the first fight is the shovel. Some vase pieces require the shovel later, so use the <Link href="/moomintroll/vase-piece-locations">All 25 Vase Piece Locations guide</Link> for cleanup.

### Is the bonfire snowball moment another fight?

No. The bonfire snowball moment is a short character interaction after the Great Winter Bonfire, not a required progression fight.
