<h2 id="walkthrough-route">
  Moomintroll Winter's Warmth walkthrough route
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Moomintroll Winter's Warmth walkthrough route from the opening to the spring ending."
  columns={walkthroughColumns}
  rows={walkthroughRows}
/>

This complete Moomintroll Winter's Warmth walkthrough follows the main route from the first night in Moominhouse to the final return of spring.

The game looks simple at first, but the structure is closer to a small quest web than a straight line. A lot of progress depends on getting the right tool, then returning to routes that were blocked earlier by snow, icicles, dead trees, ice walls, or darkness.

The main rule is simple:

**If you cannot reach something yet, you probably need a tool upgrade, a new ability, or a later quest step.**

Use this hub for the main route. For detailed collectible routing, use the focused guides linked below.

<GuideCallout title="Walkthrough rule">
Do not try to fully clear every area the first time you visit it. Many routes are tool-gated. Mark the blocked path in your head, continue the main quest, then return after getting the shovel, reinforced mittens, axe, sharp axe, or upgraded shovel.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-to-do-first">
  What to do first in Moomintroll Winter's Warmth
</h2>

At the start, your goal is not to explore the full valley. Your goal is to make Moominhouse safe enough to leave.

Start by doing the opening tasks in order:

1. open the curtains in the bedroom
2. pick up the matches
3. find the missing doorknob
4. leave the bedroom
5. light lamps as you move through the house
6. close the open windows
7. check on Moominmamma and Moominpappa
8. inspect the broken vase
9. go to the basement
10. collect five firewood pieces
11. restart the furnace
12. follow the attic noise
13. follow the squirrel outside

This opening teaches the core rhythm of the game: light dark spaces, interact with small objects, and return to blocked paths after finding the right tool.

The broken vase also starts one of the game's most important optional goals. There are **25 vase pieces** in total, and some are not available until much later.

<h2 id="opening-moominhouse">
  Opening: Moominhouse, matches, vase, and furnace
</h2>

The first area is Moominhouse. It works like a small tutorial for darkness, wind, interactable objects, and item pickup.

The important opening items are:

- **Matches** in the bedroom
- **Doorknob** near the bedroom door
- **First vase pieces** after the vase breaks
- **Five firewood pieces** in the basement
- **Attic route** where the squirrel escapes

Use matches to light lamps whenever a room is too dark. After you close the windows and restart the furnace, the house becomes safer, but the story pushes you outside after the squirrel.

Do not worry if you cannot collect every vase piece immediately. The game scatters them across the whole adventure.

<h2 id="mittens-and-snowballs">
  How to get the mittens and throw snowballs
</h2>

After the squirrel sequence, Too-ticky brings you to the Bathing House and gives you the scarf. This lets you survive outside more safely, but you still need the mittens before you can deal with icicles.

To get the mittens:

1. search the Bathing House
2. open the wardrobe
3. follow the ancestor after it runs away with the mittens
4. recover the mittens when it drops them
5. return to Too-ticky
6. practice throwing snowballs at icicles

Snowballs are one of the most important tools in the game. They let you:

- knock down icicles
- create small bridges
- hit birds carrying items
- fight Little My
- solve several puzzle routes

If a route is blocked by icicles, switch to the mittens and look for a snowball throwing angle.

<h2 id="shovel-and-little-my">
  How to get the shovel from Little My
</h2>

You need the shovel to reach Fillyjonk's house. When the snow blocks your path, the game points you toward Moominmamma's garden, but Little My has already taken the shovel.

To get it back, beat Little My in a snowball fight.

The fight is simple:

1. stay moving
2. use the snowmen as cover
3. throw when Little My pops out
4. break snowmen if she keeps hiding
5. hit her enough times to end the fight

Once you win, you get the shovel and can clear deep snow piles.

The most important chains are:

- **Fillyjonk** and her children

  All tools and upgrades
</h2>

<GuideTable

Most progress in Moomintroll Winter's Warmth comes from tools. When you get stuck, check what kind of obstacle is blocking you:

- darkness usually needs matches
- icicles usually need snowballs
- deep snow needs the shovel
- large snowball puzzles need reinforced mittens
- snow clumps need dash
- dead trees need the axe
- stronger ice needs the sharp axe

Too-ticky is effectively your upgrade helper. If you find an odd item that sounds useful, bring it to her.

Important upgrade items include:

- **Lamp oil** for longer-lasting matches
- **Sturdy yarn** for reinforced mittens
- **Whetstone** for the sharp axe
- **Scrap metal** for the upgraded shovel

<h2 id="vase-pieces">
  All vase pieces overview
</h2>

There are **25 vase pieces** to collect for Moominmamma's broken vase.

Do not treat the vase pieces as one simple house collectible. They appear across the whole game and can come from several types of sources:

- early Moominhouse pickups
- outdoor paths
- birds carrying pieces
- cave routes
- snow piles
- areas unlocked by tools
- winter being side quest rewards
- late cleanup paths

Because of that, the cleanest way to collect them is to follow the main story first, then use a checklist before lighting the Great Winter Bonfire.

<GuideCallout title="Vase piece warning">
Some vase pieces are tied to side quest rewards or later tool-gated paths. If you are trying to collect all 25, do not only search the ground. Help winter beings, revisit caves, check bird routes, and return to blocked paths after upgrades.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="side-quests">
  Side quests and winter beings
</h2>

Side quests are worth doing because they often give firewood, vase pieces, or progress toward the Great Winter Bonfire.

Common side quest patterns include:

- finding a lost item
- lighting torches
- helping a winter being warm up
- completing a snow fort
- removing snowmen
- recovering an item from a bird
- clearing blocked paths after tool upgrades

The winter beings speak in short phrases, but their requests usually map to a nearby puzzle. If one mentions darkness, look for torches. If one mentions cold, look for a campfire. If one mentions a bird, search nearby trees or high routes.

A good cleanup habit is:

1. finish a major tool unlock
2. return to previous areas
3. clear snow piles and dead trees
4. help any winter beings you skipped
5. check for vase piece rewards
6. collect leftover firewood

For a full optional quest list, use the <Link href="/moomintroll/side-quests">All Side Quests Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="ending-explained">
  Ending explained
</h2>

The ending follows the emotional thread that starts with the squirrel and the Lady of the Cold.

At first, winter feels hostile: the house is dark, the windows are open, the squirrel freezes, and Moomintroll sees the Lady of the Cold as something frightening. By the end, the story reframes winter as something that is not simply cruel. It is part of the natural cycle, and it becomes survivable because Moomintroll is no longer alone.

The final ice sequence brings that theme together:

- Too-ticky and the squirrel are in danger
- Moomintroll chooses to rescue them
- the ice cracks
- a final small fire keeps everyone together
- spring returns afterward
- Moominmamma and Moominpappa wake up
- Snufkin returns

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Is this a complete Moomintroll Winter's Warmth walkthrough?

Yes. This walkthrough covers the main story route from the Moominhouse opening to the Great Winter Bonfire, final ice sequence, spring ending, and Snufkin's return.

### What should I do first in Moomintroll Winter's Warmth?

Start by finding the matches, lighting lamps, closing the open windows, checking the broken vase, restarting the furnace, and following the squirrel outside.

### How do I get the mittens?

You get the mittens in the Bathing House. The ancestor runs away with them, and you need to follow it until it drops them. After that, Too-ticky teaches you how to throw snowballs.

### How do I get the shovel?

Beat Little My in the first snowball fight near Moominhouse. After you win, you can use Moominmamma's shovel to clear deep snow.

### How many vase pieces are there?

There are 25 vase pieces in Moomintroll Winter's Warmth. Some are found during the main route, while others require side quests or later tools.

### Should I collect all vase pieces before the ending?

Yes, that is the safest route. Before lighting the Great Winter Bonfire, check your vase piece count and finish any obvious cleanup.

### How do I get the axe?

You get the axe during the musician quest after recovering the lunchbox near the Bathing House. A fallen tree blocks the route, and the musician gives you Moominpappa's axe.

### How do I sharpen the axe?

Get the whetstone from Fillyjonk's children, then bring it to Too-ticky. The sharp axe lets you break stronger ice obstacles.

### What does the Great Winter Bonfire require?

The main bonfire preparation asks you to gather firewood, invite guests, find a musician, and help winter beings around Moominvalley.

### Can I play Moomintroll Winter's Warmth without playing Snufkin first?

Yes. The story references Snufkin and Moominvalley, but this is a standalone Moomintroll adventure. Playing Snufkin first helps with character context, but it is not required.
