<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The most common Necropolis mistakes in Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era are delaying Skeleton Archers, taking too many early losses, rushing Vampires or Liches before your engine is stable, ignoring exact resource bottlenecks, and treating Necromancy like a refund button.**

Necropolis wins by preserving and multiplying value. Your goal is not to trade evenly. Your goal is to build a stack, protect it, convert victories into more Undead value, and enter Week 2 with an army that is easier to snowball than it is to rebuild.

If your Necropolis game feels weak, check this first:

1. Did your hero choice match your opening route?
2. Did Skeleton Archers arrive early enough to reduce losses?
3. Did you take one expensive fight that slowed everything down?
4. Did you rush Vampires or Liches before your economy and army were ready?
5. Did Arcane Dust or another exact resource block your timing?
6. Did you spend Law Points or Focus without a clear purpose?
7. Did you expect Necromancy to make up for bad trades?
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="hero-without-plan">
  Mistake 1: Picking a hero without a route
</h2>

Necropolis heroes are not interchangeable. A Skeleton specialist, a control hero, a spell hero, a Necromancy scaler, and a Diplomacy converter all create different first-week priorities.

The mistake is picking a hero and then playing a generic build order.

<GuideCallout title="Hero planning rule">
Before Day 1 ends, decide whether your Necropolis game is built around Skeleton mass, spell control, Necromancy scaling, or Diplomacy conversion.
</GuideCallout>

Better route examples:

- **Onkos** for a direct Skeleton Archer snowball
- **Marl** for safer Web tempo and map movement
- **Mag** for a spell-heavy route
- **Funerella** for earlier Necromancy scaling
- **Shadespinner Oona** for control through Sleep and Nightshade Magic
- **King-of-Kings** for Diplomacy and Undead Transformer conversion

For more detail, use <Link href="/olden-era/necropolis-guide#best-necropolis-heroes">Best Necropolis heroes</Link>.

Skeletons are cheap, but early melee trades still hurt. If your main stack loses bodies before the snowball begins, Necromancy has less to protect and your Week 2 army becomes smaller.

Upgrade when ranged damage saves units across several fights. Delay only when the gold blocks your next key building.

<GuideCallout title="Skeleton Archer rule">
Do not ask only “can I afford the upgrade?” Ask “will this upgrade save enough units over the next few fights?”
</GuideCallout>

Necropolis wants to fight often, but not carelessly. A nearby fight is not automatically a good fight, and a winnable fight can still be bad if it damages the stack that your whole route depends on.

A good Necropolis fight gives a useful reward while preserving your army. A bad Necropolis fight gives you a short-term reward but breaks your next three turns.

<GuideCallout title="Fight selection rule">
Necropolis does not need to prove it can win one hard battle. It needs enough army left to win the next ten battles.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="focus-mistake">
  Mistake 8: Spending Focus too early
</h2>

Focus is easy to waste because the button feels good to press. For Necropolis, that can be costly because Focus often matters most when it prevents a real loss.

If a Lich revive, Graverobber summon, or tactical block would save your main stack later in the fight, early low-impact Focus spending is usually a mistake.

<GuideCallout title="Focus rule">
For Necropolis, Focus is strongest when it preserves the snowball. A revive, summon, or tactical block is often better than early small value.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="necromancy-mistake">
  Mistake 9: Expecting Necromancy to fix bad trades
</h2>

Necromancy is a snowball tool, not a refund button.

The important detail is that Necromancy scales from defeated enemy HP, not from how many units were on the screen. A fight with many fragile enemies may give less value than expected, and a fight that kills too many of your own key units can still be a bad trade.

<GuideCallout title="Necromancy rule">
The best Necromancy fight is not the fight with the most enemies. It is the fight where enemy HP becomes your value while your own key stacks stay alive.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the biggest Necropolis mistake in Heroes Olden Era?

The biggest mistake is taking too many early losses. Necropolis needs bodies to multiply through Skeleton Archers, Necromancy, Focus tools, and conversion mechanics. If your army is damaged before the snowball starts, the faction feels much weaker.

### Why does my Necropolis start feel weak?

Usually because one part of the loop broke early. You may have delayed Skeleton Archers, taken a bad fight, missed a resource bottleneck, rushed high-tier units too early, or spent Focus and Law Points without supporting your route.

### Should I upgrade Skeletons into Skeleton Archers early?

Yes, if the upgrade makes your next fights safer. Skeleton Archers are usually worth it when ranged damage prevents several melee trades. Do not upgrade blindly if the gold blocks a more important immediate building.

### Should Necropolis rush Vampires or Liches?

Only when your early engine is stable. Vampires and Liches are strong, but they should support your Skeleton Archer, Necromancy, and conversion plan instead of replacing it too early.

### Is Marketplace always good for Necropolis?

No. Marketplace is good when it fixes a real bottleneck. Build it when trading resources unlocks Skeleton upgrades, Mage Guild, Vampires, Liches, or another important timing.

### Why does Necropolis still lose fights after taking Necromancy?

Because Necromancy does not erase bad trades. It gives value after victories, but if you lose too many important units, your army can still shrink in practice.

### How should Necropolis use Focus?

Save Focus for meaningful turns. Lich revive, Graverobber summons, and tactical body blocking are usually more important than spending Focus immediately for small value.

### Are lethal battles worth taking as Necropolis?

Usually no, unless the reward unlocks a major timing and you can win without crippling your main stack. Necropolis wants repeated clean wins, not one expensive victory.

### What should I check when my Necropolis build is blocked?

Check exact resource costs first: gold, Arcane Dust, wood, ore, mercury, gems, crystals, and building prerequisites. Then decide whether Marketplace, a safer fight, or a resource pickup solves the problem fastest.
