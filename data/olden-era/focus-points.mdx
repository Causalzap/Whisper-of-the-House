<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Focus Points in Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era are combat points that build into Focus Charges. Every 6 Focus Points become 1 Focus Charge, and your Focus bar can hold up to 3 Charges.**

Focus Points are generated during battle. A melee attack gives **2 Focus Points**, a ranged attack gives **1 Focus Point**, and being attacked gives **1 Focus Point**. Focus is awarded per strike, so multi-strike units can build Focus faster.

Focus Charges are spent on creature and hero abilities. Creature abilities usually cost **1–3 Focus Charges**, and most Focus abilities end the unit’s turn unless their description says otherwise.

The simple rule is:

**do not spend Focus because a button is available. Spend Focus when it prevents losses, creates a blocker, revives a key stack, disables a dangerous enemy, avoids wasting the 3-Charge cap, or changes the next turn.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="what-are-focus-points">
  What are Focus Points?
</h2>

Focus has two layers: **Focus Points** and **Focus Charges**.

Focus Points are the small points you build during battle. Once you collect 6 Focus Points, they convert into 1 Focus Charge. Focus Charges are the spendable resource used by many creature and hero abilities.

They are not town resources like gold, Law Points, or Astrology Points. You use Focus during battle.

<h2 id="how-focus-is-generated">
  How Focus is generated
</h2>

Focus is generated through combat actions and can be improved by skills and effects.

The key point is that Focus is awarded **per strike**, not just per unit. That means a multi-strike unit, a split ranged stack, or a unit supported by a Focus-generating subskill can build Charges faster than a single large stack with fewer actions.

<h2 id="when-to-spend-focus">
  When to spend Focus
</h2>

The best Focus use is usually the one that changes the board.

A Focus ability is worth spending when it does at least one of these things:

- saves an important stack
- revives fallen units
- summons a blocker
- denies an enemy ability
- prevents incoming damage
- restores mana at a meaningful timing
- avoids wasting Focus at the 3-Charge cap
- lets your main stack survive and keep clearing
- wins the fight before the enemy can punish you

/>

<GuideCallout title="Spend rule">
Spend Focus when it changes what happens next. Save Focus when the current use is only small value and a stronger timing is coming.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="turn-order">
  Check turn order before using Focus
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

    width={1600}

    Focus decisions are tied to turn order. A perfect ability used one turn late may not save the stack you wanted to protect.
  </figcaption>

Focus is strongest when it is timed before the enemy’s important action.

Before spending Focus, ask:

1. Which enemy stack acts next?
2. Which friendly stack is in danger?
3. Can Focus prevent that damage?
4. Can Focus create a body block?
5. Can Focus revive or preserve a key stack?
6. Will waiting give me a stronger use?
7. Will waiting get the Focus user killed?

If the enemy acts before your Focus user, you may need to spend earlier. If your important Focus user acts before the danger appears, you may need to save.

<GuideCallout title="Turn order rule">
Focus is not only about what the ability does. It is about whether the ability happens before the enemy’s important turn.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="focus-vs-mana">
  Focus vs mana
</h2>

Focus and mana are different systems.

**Mana** belongs to the hero and fuels spells.

**Focus** belongs to the battle state and fuels creature abilities or Focus-based effects.

Sometimes they interact. Some effects can spend all Focus Charges to restore hero mana, which means a Focus decision can affect your spell plan after the unit’s action.

<h2 id="final-focus-check">
  Final Focus check
</h2>

Before spending Focus, make these final three checks:

1. **Will waiting get the Focus user killed?**
2. **Will I waste Focus by staying at the 3-Charge cap?**
3. **Will this ability end the unit’s turn when I still need that unit to move, attack, or block?**

If none of those are urgent, save Focus unless the current use clearly wins tempo.

<GuideCallout title="Final Focus rule">
Focus is best spent with a purpose. If you cannot explain what the Focus use changes, it is probably better to wait.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What are Focus Points in Heroes Olden Era?

Focus Points are combat points that build into Focus Charges. Every 6 Focus Points become 1 Focus Charge, and Focus Charges are spent on creature and hero abilities.

### What is the difference between Focus Points and Focus Charges?

Focus Points are the buildup. Focus Charges are the spendable resource. You gain Points through combat actions, then convert 6 Points into 1 Charge.

### How many Focus Charges can you hold?

Your Focus bar can hold up to 3 Focus Charges.

### How many Focus Charges do abilities cost?

Creature abilities can require 1–3 Focus Charges. Hero abilities can also use Focus Charges.

### How do you generate Focus Points?

Melee attacks generate 2 Focus Points, ranged attacks generate 1 Focus Point, and being attacked generates 1 Focus Point. Focus is awarded per strike, so multi-strike units can generate more Focus.

### Does Offence help generate Focus?

Yes. The Battle March subskill from Offence makes friendly units generate +1 Focus Point when they attack.

### Should I spend Focus as soon as I can?

No. Spend Focus when it changes the fight. Save it if a later revive, summon, disable, block, or preservation effect will matter more.

### What is the best use of Focus?

The best Focus use is usually the one that prevents real losses or changes board state. Reviving a key stack, summoning a blocker, disabling a dangerous enemy, or denying an ability is often better than small damage.

### Is Focus the same as mana?

No. Mana fuels hero spells, while Focus fuels creature abilities and battle effects. Some Focus abilities can interact with mana, but they are separate systems.

### Why does Necropolis care about Focus?

Necropolis cares about Focus because Liches can revive or restore units and Graverobbers can summon Skeletons. These abilities help preserve the army and support the undead snowball.

### Why does Schism care about Focus?

Schism cares about Focus because many of its strongest turns involve summons, control, ability denial, and board presence. Focus can help Schism create or deny actions at the right timing.

### Can enemies use Focus too?

Yes. Enemy units can also benefit from Focus or active abilities. Some effects can deny Focus generation or prevent enemies from using active abilities, which can be very strong against dangerous stacks.

### What is the biggest Focus mistake?

The biggest mistake is spending Focus just because the ability is available. Focus should be saved for the turn where it prevents losses, creates a blocker, revives units, denies an enemy, avoids wasting the 3-Charge cap, or wins the fight.
