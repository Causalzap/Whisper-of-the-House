<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Subclasses, also called Advanced Classes, are powerful late-game hero upgrades in Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era. To unlock one, your hero needs five specific secondary skills, and all five must be upgraded to Expert.**

The important rules are:

1. **Each Advanced Class needs exactly five required secondary skills.**
2. **All five required skills must be Expert**, not Basic or Advanced.
3. **Each hero can learn only eight unique secondary skills**, so wrong early choices can lock you out.
4. **Skill roll probability is the real gate.** Some route skills can be around 2% or even below 1% in sample data.
5. **At Level 8–10, stop forcing a route if you do not have at least three required skills.**
6. **Do not plan around ultra-rare skills.** Treat them as lucky confirmation rolls.

The best rule is simple: **plan a subclass early, but only chase it when the skill rolls prove the route is real.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="how-subclasses-work">
  How subclasses work
</h2>

Subclasses are not picked directly from a menu. They are unlocked by building your hero into a specific skill formula.

The formula is always:

**five specific secondary skills → all five at Expert → Advanced Class unlock**

That means four things matter more than the passive itself:

- which skills the route requires
- whether your class can realistically roll those skills
- whether your hero still has enough open skill slots
- whether the rarest skill appeared early enough to justify the chase

<h2 id="skill-roll-probability">
  Skill roll probability: why routes fail
</h2>

The hidden difficulty of subclasses is not the requirement list. It is the probability of seeing the required skills at all.

The level-up system gives you three skill choices, but those choices are not equally random. They depend on your class, existing skills, open slots, and the game's skill offering logic. That means a required skill can be theoretically possible but practically unreliable.

<GuideTable
  caption="Knight to Swashbuckler sample skill roll probabilities."
  columns={probabilityColumns}
  rows={probabilityRows}
/>

The important lesson is not only that Swashbuckler is hard. The lesson is that **one ultra-rare required skill can ruin the entire route**.

If a subclass needs a skill with a roll chance around 1–2%, you should not build your whole game around seeing it later. You should chase that route only if the rare skill appears early.

<GuideCallout title="Probability rule">
A rare skill is not something you “plan to get later.” It is a signal. If the rare skill appears early, the route becomes real. If it does not, play a normal strong hero instead.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="why-expert-skills-matter">
  Why five Expert skills matter
</h2>

The most common subclass mistake is thinking that learning the correct skills is enough.

It is not.

Each required skill must reach **Expert**, which means you need the base skill and then enough future level-up choices to upgrade it. A route with five required skills is really a long-term investment, not a quick bonus.

For example:

- learning Logistics is only the first step
- upgrading Logistics to Advanced is still not enough
- the route needs Expert Logistics
- the same is true for the other four required skills

<h2 id="eight-skill-limit">
  The 8-skill limit
</h2>

Every hero can learn only **eight unique secondary skills**.

This is the number that makes subclass planning dangerous. Since a subclass requires five exact skills, your remaining margin is small.

If you take too many off-route skills, you may mathematically block the subclass.

Example:

- your target route needs 5 specific skills
- your hero can hold 8 total secondary skills
- you already picked 4 off-route skills
- 4 + 5 = 9
- the route is impossible

<GuideCallout title="Skill slot rule">
Before you click a tempting off-route skill, count your remaining open slots. The strongest subclass passive does not matter if your skill bar can no longer fit the formula.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="unlock-levels">
  Earliest unlock level vs realistic unlock level
</h2>

The theoretical earliest unlock is around **Level 15**. Some databases describe an even earlier ideal case around Level 14 if the hero starts with a required skill and every following roll is perfect.

That is not a practical plan.

In real games, subclass unlocks are usually later. A more realistic timing is **Level 20–25+**, and many games should not force a subclass at all.

For Necromancer, **Soulweaver is usually the more immediately threatening route** because summoning temporary Wights after kills affects the current battle. Chronomancer can be powerful, but Economy is the route bottleneck and may be difficult to roll naturally.

<GuideCallout title="Necromancer rule">
If you are chasing Chronomancer, Economy is the key checkpoint. If you are chasing Soulweaver, Logistics is the key checkpoint. Missing the bottleneck skill is usually a stronger reason to abandon the route than missing a common combat skill.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="when-to-abandon">
  When to abandon a subclass route
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="When to keep or abandon a subclass route."
  columns={abandonColumns}
  rows={abandonRows}
/>

The cleanest rule is:

**At Level 8–10, you should usually have at least three required route skills, including the rarest bottleneck skill if the route has one.**

If you do not, the subclass is probably becoming a trap. Your hero may still become strong, but it should stop chasing the advanced class and start taking the best available combat, economy, mobility, or magic skills.

<GuideCallout title="Abandon rule">
If your target route needs five skills and you have fewer than three of them by Level 8–10, stop forcing it unless you have a reliable way to learn the missing skills.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Common subclass mistakes
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Common Heroes Olden Era subclass mistakes."
  columns={mistakeColumns}
  rows={mistakeRows}
/>

Most failed subclass routes are not caused by one bad level-up. They are caused by three small mistakes:

1. ignoring the probability of the rarest required skill
2. picking off-route skills early
3. refusing to abandon the route after the skill pool is already ruined

A subclass is a reward for disciplined planning and good rolls. It should not become an excuse to play a weak mid game.

<h2 id="how-to-use-this-guide">
  How to use this guide while playing
</h2>

Before you start a map, choose one target route and one backup plan.

During the game, check these questions:

1. Did my starting hero already have a route skill?
2. Did I roll the rarest bottleneck skill early?
3. Do I have at least two route skills by the early levels?
4. Do I have at least three route skills by Level 8–10?
5. Do I still have enough open skill slots?
6. Will the passive matter before the decisive fight?
7. Am I sacrificing too much map tempo to force this?

If the answer turns bad, pivot. A strong normal hero is better than a failed subclass project.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you unlock subclasses in Heroes Olden Era?

You unlock a subclass by getting five specific secondary skills and upgrading all five to Expert. See <Link href="#advanced-class-requirements">Advanced Class requirements table</Link>.

### Do subclass skills need to be Expert?

Yes. Basic or Advanced is not enough. Every required skill must be Expert.

### What is the hardest part of unlocking a subclass?

The hardest part is skill roll probability. Some required skills can have extremely low roll chances, so the route may fail even if you know the correct formula. See <Link href="#skill-roll-probability">Skill roll probability</Link>.

### What is the earliest level to unlock a subclass?

The theoretical timing is around Level 15, with some perfect cases listed around Level 14. Most real games unlock much later, usually Level 20–25+ or not at all.

### How many secondary skills can a hero learn?

A hero can learn up to eight unique secondary skills. This makes early off-route picks dangerous.

### When should I stop forcing a subclass?

If you reach Level 8–10 without at least three required route skills, especially without the rare bottleneck skill, you should usually stop forcing the route. See <Link href="#when-to-abandon">When to abandon a subclass route</Link>.

### Which Necromancer subclass is better?

Soulweaver is usually better for immediate battlefield pressure, while Chronomancer depends heavily on whether you can roll Economy. See <Link href="#necromancer-routes">Necromancer: Chronomancer vs Soulweaver</Link>.

### Why can’t I unlock my subclass?

You are probably missing a required skill, one skill is not Expert, your hero has no open skill slots, the rare skill never appeared, or the route data changed during Early Access.

### What is the biggest subclass mistake?

The biggest mistake is treating a rare route as guaranteed. If a key skill has a very low roll chance, you should wait for it to appear early before committing.
