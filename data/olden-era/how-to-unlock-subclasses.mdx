<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**To unlock a subclass in Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era, your hero must finish the exact Advanced Class formula: five specific secondary skills, all upgraded to Expert.**

In-game, check four things:

1. **Your hero's base class**, not only the faction.
2. **Your starting skills**, because a route skill at the start makes the path easier.
3. **Your level-up choices**, especially rare bottleneck skills.
4. **Your skill levels**, because Basic and Advanced do not count as finished.

The practical rule is simple: **do not force a subclass until the game shows you the route is alive.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="what-you-need">
  What you need before a subclass can unlock
</h2>

The unlock condition is short:

**five exact secondary skills, all at Expert.**

But in-game, that means you need to confirm several things:

- the route belongs to your hero's base class
- all five skill names match the Advanced Class formula
- each required skill is learned
- each required skill is upgraded to Expert
- your hero still has enough skill slots to finish the formula

<GuideCallout title="Unlock rule">
Hero level alone does not unlock a subclass. The game checks the completed skill formula.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="route-skill-marker">
  How to tell if a skill belongs to your route
</h2>

When a skill appears during a level-up, ask three questions:

1. Does this skill appear in the exact Advanced Class formula?
2. Is this skill one of the rare bottlenecks for the route?
3. Does the UI show a route marker or icon that suggests subclass relevance?

A route marker is useful, but it should not replace the formula. The formula is what matters.

<h2 id="skill-slots">
  How to check if your skill slots are still open
</h2>

Your hero can learn only a limited number of unique secondary skills. That means a route can die even before you notice it.

The warning signs are:

- your hero has several off-route skills
- the route still needs multiple new skills
- the missing required skill no longer appears
- your level-ups are only offering upgrades or unrelated options

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you unlock a subclass in Heroes Olden Era?

Finish the exact Advanced Class formula: five required secondary skills, all upgraded to Expert.

### Where do I check subclass progress?

Check your hero class, starting skills, level-up choices, skill levels, and open secondary skill slots. See <Link href="#where-to-check-route">Where to check your route in-game</Link>.

### Does Basic or Advanced count for subclass unlocks?

No. Only Expert counts as a completed requirement. See <Link href="#basic-advanced-expert">Basic vs Advanced vs Expert</Link>.

### Why is my subclass not unlocking?

Usually one required skill is missing, one skill is not Expert, your hero is following the wrong base class route, your slots are full, or Early Access data changed. See <Link href="#quick-debug">Quick debug</Link>.

### How do I know if a route is still possible?

A route is still possible when the required skills are appearing, your skill slots are open, and the missing bottleneck skill has appeared early enough. See <Link href="#route-confirmation-checklist">Route confirmation checklist</Link>.

### How rare can subclass skills be?

Some required skills can be extremely rare for specific classes. For example, Knight to Swashbuckler sample data puts Nightshade Magic at 0.91%. See <Link href="#sample-roll-chances">Sample roll chances</Link>.

### Should I force a subclass every game?

No. Only force a route when the level-up choices confirm it. Most games are better with a strong normal hero than a failed subclass chase.

### What is the difference between this page and the Subclass Guide?

This page explains how to recognize and complete a route in-game. The <Link href="/olden-era/subclass-guide">Subclass Guide</Link> explains requirements, route value, roll risks, and which Advanced Classes are worth chasing.
