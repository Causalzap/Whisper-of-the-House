<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era is a strategy game about exploring the map, building your city, recruiting and upgrading units, managing resources, winning tactical battles, and planning long-term hero growth.**

For your first few hours, focus on five things:

1. **Build economy first** so your town keeps producing value every day.
2. **Take low-loss fights** instead of bleeding your important stacks.
3. **Upgrade key early units** that help you clear the map faster.
4. **Learn Law Points, Astrology Points, and Focus** because they are core Olden Era systems.
5. **Do not blindly force subclasses** unless your early skill rolls support the route.

The best starting guide order is:

**Subclass Guide → How to Unlock Subclasses → Necropolis Guide → Beginner Economy Guide → Focus Points Explained → Schism Guide → Temple Guide**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="what-to-do-first">
  What to do first in Heroes Olden Era
</h2>

Start with this route:

1. inspect your starting hero and starting skills
2. recruit your starting army
3. build an economy or growth upgrade in your town
4. choose whether your opening economy favors gold, Law Points, or Astrology Points
5. pick up nearby resources and safe treasure chests
6. fight easy battles that do not cost important stacks
7. upgrade a key early unit if it improves your map clearing
8. return before the weekly creature refresh if you need to recruit
9. decide whether your skill rolls support a subclass route
10. avoid lethal fights unless the reward is worth the losses

<GuideCallout title="Beginner rule">
Do not try to win every fight just because it is available. If a fight costs too many core units, skip it, come back stronger, or let the enemy run.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="subclasses">
  Subclasses and Advanced Classes
</h2>

Subclasses are one of the most important long-term systems in Heroes Olden Era.

The simple version is:

**To unlock a subclass, your hero needs five specific secondary skills, and all five must be upgraded to Expert.**

That sounds simple, but in practice it creates several problems:

- you can pick the wrong skills early
- you can fill too many of your limited skill slots
- you can have the right skill but only at Basic or Advanced
- your class may not easily roll one of the required skills
- chasing the route can weaken your early map clear
- the reward may come too late to matter in a fast game

<GuideCallout title="Subclass rule">
Do not force a subclass just because the passive looks powerful. If you do not have at least two or three route skills by the mid-early levels, it may be better to abandon the route and build for immediate strength.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What guide should I read first?

New players should start with the <Link href="/olden-era/beginner-economy-guide">Beginner Economy Guide</Link>. For hero builds, start with the <Link href="/olden-era/subclass-guide">Subclass Guide</Link>.

### How do subclasses work?

A subclass needs five specific secondary skills upgraded to Expert. Use <Link href="/olden-era/how-to-unlock-subclasses">How to Unlock Subclasses</Link> for the full checklist.

### Why can’t I unlock my subclass?

Usually you are missing a required skill, one skill is not Expert, your skill slots are full, or you are following outdated data. See <Link href="/olden-era/cant-unlock-subclass">Why You Can’t Unlock a Subclass</Link>.

### Should I force a subclass every game?

No. Force a route only when your early skill rolls support it. Otherwise, build for immediate strength.

### Is Necropolis good for beginners?

Necropolis is powerful but punishing. Start with the <Link href="/olden-era/necropolis-guide">Necropolis Guide</Link> if you want a safer early route.

### What should I build first?

Prioritize compounding value: income, creature growth, Law Points, Astrology Points, Mage Guild access, or key unit upgrades. See <Link href="/olden-era/best-buildings-first">Best Buildings to Build First</Link>.

### What are Law Points and Astrology Points?

Law Points unlock faction laws. Astrology Points connect to spell and map spell progression. Read <Link href="/olden-era/law-points-astrology-points">Law Points and Astrology Points Explained</Link>.

### What are Focus Points?

Focus Points are a battle resource used by many active creature abilities. See <Link href="/olden-era/focus-points">Focus Points Explained</Link>.

### Which faction should I start with?

Temple is the easiest beginner angle, Necropolis is the strongest early guide target, and Schism is the best hard-core mechanics faction.

### Is this guide final?

No. Olden Era is in Early Access, so this guide will be updated as balance, costs, subclass data, and faction mechanics change.
