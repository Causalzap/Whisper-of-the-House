<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Soulweaver is usually the better Necromancer subclass route in Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era if Logistics appears early. Chronomancer is worth chasing only when Economy appears early and the game gives you enough time to use the broader Necromancy payoff.**

Use this rule:

1. **Pick Soulweaver** if Logistics appears early and you want immediate battlefield pressure from temporary Wight summons.
2. **Pick Chronomancer** if Economy appears early and your build can turn broader Necromancy value into long-term scaling.
3. **Pick neither** if neither bottleneck appears early.
4. **Do not chase both routes** because they share no required skills.

For most real games, **Soulweaver is the safer Necromancer chase.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="quick-comparison">
  Chronomancer vs Soulweaver comparison
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Chronomancer vs Soulweaver route comparison."
  columns={comparisonColumns}
  rows={comparisonRows}
/>

The comparison is simple:

**Chronomancer is the broader Necromancy-scaling route. Soulweaver is the immediate battlefield-pressure route.**

That means Soulweaver is usually easier to justify in real games. It gives a visible tactical payoff when enemy stacks die. Chronomancer can be strong, but only when the Economy roll appears early and the game lasts long enough for the expanded Necromancy value to matter.

<h2 id="best-heroes">
  Best heroes for Chronomancer and Soulweaver
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Best Necromancer heroes for Chronomancer and Soulweaver routes."
  columns={heroColumns}
  rows={heroRows}
/>

The best route is not only about the passive. It also depends on your starting hero.

**Marl** is the cleanest Soulweaver candidate because Logistics is the Soulweaver bottleneck. If Marl starts close to that route and your early level-ups support the rest of the formula, Soulweaver becomes much more realistic.

**Funerella** is strong for Necromancer scaling because she starts ahead on the faction's core Necromancy identity. She is not automatically locked into either route, but she benefits more than most heroes when the subclass path forms naturally.

**Mag** is the better spell-leaning option when your run naturally points toward magic skills. **Oona** and **Onkos** can still become strong normal Necromancers if neither Advanced Class route comes together.

<GuideCallout title="Hero rule">
Pick the route that your hero and early rolls support. Marl makes Soulweaver easier to justify, but Logistics alone does not complete the route.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="which-should-you-pick">
  Which Necromancer subclass should you pick?
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="When to pick Chronomancer, Soulweaver, or neither."
  columns={decisionColumns}
  rows={decisionRows}
/>

The biggest mistake is choosing by passive text alone.

Choose based on the route the game gives you. If Logistics appears early, Soulweaver becomes realistic. If Economy appears early, Chronomancer becomes possible. If neither appears, build a strong normal Necromancer instead.

<GuideCallout title="Decision rule">
Do not decide Chronomancer vs Soulweaver before the bottleneck skill appears. Economy confirms Chronomancer. Logistics confirms Soulweaver.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="do-not-chase-both">
  Do not chase both routes
</h2>

Chronomancer and Soulweaver are two different five-skill formulas, and they share no required skills.

Trying to chase both usually means you finish neither. Once Economy or Logistics appears, let that bottleneck decide the route instead of splitting your skill slots.

<h2 id="opportunity-cost">
  Opportunity cost
</h2>

The cost of chasing Chronomancer or Soulweaver is not only the five required skills.

It is also what you give up:

- immediate combat upgrades
- safer map clearing
- extra movement
- economy and town tempo
- spell schools that fit your current map
- upgrades to skills you already have

<h2 id="final-verdict">
  Final verdict
</h2>

For most Necromancer games:

**Soulweaver is the better route if Logistics appears early. Chronomancer is a conditional route that needs early Economy. If neither bottleneck appears, chase neither.**

That verdict is not because Chronomancer is weak. It is because Soulweaver's payoff is more immediate, while Chronomancer depends more heavily on timing and route confirmation.


<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Is Chronomancer or Soulweaver better?

Soulweaver is usually better if Logistics appears early because its Wight summons affect battles immediately. Chronomancer is worth chasing only when Economy appears early.

### Which hero is best for Soulweaver?

Marl is the cleanest Soulweaver candidate because Logistics is the Soulweaver bottleneck. Still, he needs all five Soulweaver skills at Expert.

### Which hero is best for Chronomancer?

Chronomancer is less about a fixed hero and more about the Economy roll. Chase it only when Economy appears early and the rest of the route supports your game state.

### What skills does Chronomancer need?

Chronomancer needs Daylight Magic, Tactics, Battle Magic, Offence, and Economy, all upgraded to Expert.

### What skills does Soulweaver need?

Soulweaver needs Summon Avatar, Arcane Magic, Battlecraft, Insight, and Logistics, all upgraded to Expert.

### What is the biggest Chronomancer bottleneck?

Economy. If Economy does not appear early, Chronomancer becomes risky to force.

### What is the biggest Soulweaver bottleneck?

Logistics. If Logistics does not appear early, Soulweaver becomes risky to force.

### Should I chase both Chronomancer and Soulweaver?

No. They share no required skills. Trying to chase both can leave you with neither route completed.

### What should I do if neither route works?

Build a strong normal Necromancer around your current skills: combat, magic, economy, or Logistics-based map tempo.
