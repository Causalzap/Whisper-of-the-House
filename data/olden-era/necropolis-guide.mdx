<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Necropolis is a snowball faction in Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era. The safe plan is to turn Skeleton growth, Necromancy, Skeleton Archers, Diplomacy, and Undead Transformer value into a bigger army every week.**

For most starts:

1. **Pick a hero with a clear plan**: Onkos for Skeletons, Marl for Web tempo, Mag for Arcane Magic, Funerella for Necromancy scaling, Oona for Sleep control, or King-of-Kings for Diplomacy and conversion.
2. **Upgrade Skeletons into Skeleton Archers early** when the gold cost improves your next fights.
3. **Level Necromancy**, but remember that Basic / Advanced / Expert converts **4% / 6% / 8% of defeated enemy HP**, not enemy unit count.
4. **Use the Undead Transformer aggressively** because Diplomacy and creature hut pickups can become Necropolis army value faster than battle-only Necromancy.
5. **Save Focus for Necropolis-specific abilities**, especially Lich revive and Graverobber summons.
6. **Avoid bad early losses** because Necropolis wins by multiplying army value, not by trading evenly.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="is-necropolis-good">
  Is Necropolis good for beginners?
</h2>

Necropolis is strong, but it is not a careless beginner faction. It rewards planning because your early choices decide whether your army starts multiplying or collapses after a few expensive fights.

The faction's core idea is simple:

**win fights with low losses, convert battle value through Necromancy, upgrade Skeletons into ranged damage, and use conversion mechanics to keep growing.**

That means Necropolis is beginner-playable if you follow a structure. It is much harder if you click every fight, delay Skeleton Archer upgrades, misunderstand Necromancy, or never use the Undead Transformer.

<GuideCallout title="Beginner verdict">
Necropolis is best for players who like snowballing armies. Start with a hero plan, upgrade Skeletons into Skeleton Archers, level Necromancy, use conversion mechanics, and avoid fights that damage your main stack before the snowball starts.
</GuideCallout>

Use the full <Link href="/olden-era/beginner-economy-guide">Beginner Economy Guide</Link> if you are still learning buildings, resources, Law Points, Astrology Points, Focus, and first-week priorities.

<h2 id="best-necropolis-heroes">
  Best Necropolis heroes
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Best Heroes Olden Era Necropolis heroes by opening plan."
  columns={heroColumns}
  rows={heroRows}
/>

The best Necropolis hero depends on what kind of snowball you want.

**Onkos** is the easiest hero to recommend for a Skeleton-focused opening. He directly supports the unit that most Necropolis players want to scale first, and he makes the Skeleton Archer plan easier to execute.

**Marl** is the best tempo pick if you want safer clearing. Web gives you more control over enemy movement, and Logistics makes your map route smoother.

**Mag** is the magic route. Pick Mag when you want Necropolis to feel more like a spellcasting faction instead of a pure Skeleton engine.

**Funerella** is the Necromancy scaling pick. Advanced Necromancy gives her a head start on the faction's main snowball system.

**Shadespinner Oona** is a control pick. Sleep and Nightshade Magic help you disable dangerous stacks and reduce early losses.

**King-of-Kings** is the conversion economy pick. Diplomacy pairs naturally with creature huts, neutral creature recruitment, and the Undead Transformer. In the current Early Access version, this route can be stronger than relying only on post-battle Necromancy.

<GuideCallout title="Hero rule">
Do not choose a Necropolis hero only by raw strength. Choose the hero that matches your first-week plan: Skeleton mass, Web tempo, spell control, Necromancy scaling, or Diplomacy conversion.
</GuideCallout>

<GuideCallout title="Early Access balance warning">
This Necropolis guide is based on the April 30, 2026 Early Access version. King-of-Kings, Diplomacy recruitment, Undead Transformer conversion, and Necromancy HP conversion are high-impact systems and should be rechecked after balance patches.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="key-numbers">
  Key Necropolis numbers
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Important Necropolis numbers for early decisions."
  columns={numberColumns}
  rows={numberRows}
/>

The most important opening number is the **Skeleton upgrade cost**.

Skeletons upgrade for **10 gold per unit**, so the real cost depends on your stack size:

- 50 Skeletons → 500 gold
- 100 Skeletons → 1000 gold
- 150 Skeletons → 1500 gold
- 200 Skeletons → 2000 gold

This is why “upgrade when affordable” is too vague. Count your Skeletons, check your gold, and ask whether the upgrade will save more units than the same gold would buy elsewhere.

<GuideCallout title="Skeleton Archer rule">
If upgrading Skeletons into Skeleton Archers lets you take several fights with fewer losses, the upgrade is usually worth the gold.
</GuideCallout>

</h2>

Skeletons are cheap and grow quickly, but basic melee Skeletons still take damage when they trade. The upgrade into **Skeleton Archers** changes the opening because it lets your largest early stack contribute damage without walking into every threat.

This matters most on heroes like **Onkos**, because his Skeleton-focused identity makes every preserved Skeleton more valuable.

Prioritize the upgrade when:

- you have enough gold for the stack size
- your next fights are easier with ranged damage
- you are trying to preserve Wights, Undead Pets, or higher-tier units
- you already have enough economy to keep building
- your route wants 100–200 Skeleton Archers by Week 2

Delay it when:

- the gold blocks a key building
- you have too few Skeletons for the upgrade to matter
- you need Marketplace or Mage Guild first
- a different immediate timing is more important

<h2 id="undead-transformer">
  How the Undead Transformer works
</h2>

The **Undead Transformer** is one of Necropolis' strongest Early Access mechanics because it turns outside creature value into Necropolis-compatible army value.

The important idea is:

**Necromancy grows from battle victories, but the Undead Transformer grows from map conversion.**

That difference matters. Necromancy is limited by defeated enemy HP and by how cleanly you win fights. The Undead Transformer can instead turn recruited neutral creatures, creature hut pickups, and off-faction stacks into undead army value.

This is why **King-of-Kings** is so important in the current Necropolis meta. His Diplomacy start makes it easier to turn neutral creature value into a conversion economy instead of relying only on post-battle Necromancy.

A strong conversion plan looks like this:

1. your main hero clears safe fights
2. a second hero collects creature huts, loose units, and map resources
3. Diplomacy helps recruit or preserve neutral creature value
4. unneeded living creatures go into the Undead Transformer
5. your main army receives more Necropolis-compatible value

<GuideCallout title="Transformer rule">
Do not treat neutral creatures as random extras. In Necropolis, they can become undead economy fuel. This is especially important for King-of-Kings and second-hero routes.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="multi-hero-strategy">
  Multi-hero Necropolis strategy
</h2>

Necropolis benefits heavily from multi-hero play because the faction has more to collect than one hero can efficiently handle.

Use your **main hero** for:

- safe fights
- Necromancy growth
- key objectives
- high-value combat rewards
- Skeleton Archer snowballing
- major army timing

Use your **second hero** for:

- creature huts
- loose resources
- army delivery
- scouting
- town pickup
- Undead Transformer setup
- neutral creature collection

This is especially useful with a King-of-Kings or Diplomacy plan. Your second hero can collect units while your main hero keeps fighting, so the faction grows from both combat and conversion at the same time.

<h2 id="focus-points">
  Focus Points for Necropolis
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Necropolis-specific Focus uses."
  columns={focusColumns}
  rows={focusRows}
/>

Focus is not just a generic battle resource for Necropolis. It directly affects whether some of your most important tactical tools are available when you need them.

The two most important early examples are:

**Liches** can use Rewind Death to restore HP and revive fallen units. This is valuable when an important stack is about to be permanently reduced.

**Graverobbers** can use Dig Up Bones to summon Skeletons. This can create blockers, add bodies, and support the Skeleton mass plan.

<GuideCallout title="Focus rule">
Do not spend Focus just because an ability is available. Save it for the turn where a revive, summon, or body block prevents real losses.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="when-to-fight">
  When to fight and when to let enemies run
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/olden-era/olden-era-necropolis-lethal-battle.webp"
    alt="Necropolis facing a lethal battle warning in Heroes Olden Era."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    A lethal battle can be technically winnable but still wrong if it destroys the army that your Necromancy snowball needs.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Necropolis wants to fight often, but not recklessly.

Take a fight when:

- it is easy or normal
- your Skeleton Archers can deal damage safely
- the reward unlocks your next building or upgrade
- Necromancy value should outweigh losses
- Lich, Graverobber, or spell tools can prevent damage

Skip or delay a fight when:

- it is lethal or impossible
- it threatens your main Skeleton Archer stack
- you need weekly growth first
- the reward does not solve a bottleneck
- your Focus tools are not ready

The best Necropolis play is not proving you can win one hard fight. It is preserving enough army to win the next ten fights.

The biggest Necropolis mistake is treating it like a normal faction.

Necropolis does not only build units. It multiplies them through Skeleton growth, Necromancy, conversion, creature huts, and low-loss fights. If your decisions do not support that engine, your faction advantage becomes much weaker.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Who is the best Necropolis hero?

For a simple Skeleton Archer snowball, start with Onkos. For safe clearing, Marl is excellent. For conversion economy, King-of-Kings is the key Diplomacy pick. See <Link href="#best-necropolis-heroes">Best Necropolis heroes</Link>.

### What is the best Necropolis build order?

A safe Week 1 route is economy or Skeleton Archer access first, then Skeleton Archer upgrade, Marketplace or Mage Guild if blocked, then Graverobber, Lich, Vampire, or conversion setup depending on hero. See <Link href="#week-one-build-order">Necropolis Week 1 build order</Link>.

### How much does it cost to upgrade Skeletons into Skeleton Archers?

The upgrade is 10 gold per Skeleton. For example, 100 Skeletons cost 1000 gold to upgrade. See <Link href="#key-numbers">Key Necropolis numbers</Link>.

### How many Skeleton Archers should I have by Week 2?

A strong start can move toward 100–200 Skeleton Archers by Week 2, depending on hero, fights, laws, conversion, and losses. See <Link href="#week-one-timeline">Necropolis Week 1 timeline</Link>.

### How does Necromancy work?

Necromancy converts defeated enemy HP into allied Undead HP after battle victories. Basic, Advanced, and Expert Necromancy convert 4%, 6%, and 8%. See <Link href="#necromancy">How Necromancy works</Link>.

### Is Necromancy based on unit count or HP?

Necromancy is based on defeated enemy HP, not unit count. A large number of fragile enemies may give less reanimation value than a smaller group of high-HP enemies. See <Link href="#necromancy">How Necromancy works</Link>.

### What does the Undead Transformer do?

It turns collected or recruited creature value into Necropolis-compatible army value, especially with Diplomacy and creature hut routes. See <Link href="#undead-transformer">How the Undead Transformer works</Link>.

### Is King-of-Kings good?

Yes. In the current Early Access version, King-of-Kings is one of the strongest Necropolis picks if you want a Diplomacy and Undead Transformer strategy. See <Link href="#best-necropolis-heroes">Best Necropolis heroes</Link>.

### How should Necropolis use Focus?

Save Focus for meaningful Necropolis abilities, especially Lich revive and Graverobber Skeleton summons. See <Link href="#focus-points">Focus Points for Necropolis</Link>.

### Should I rush Vampires or Liches?

Only if your Skeleton Archer, Necromancy, and conversion engine is already stable. Otherwise, follow the Week 1 build order first. See <Link href="#week-one-build-order">Necropolis Week 1 build order</Link>.
