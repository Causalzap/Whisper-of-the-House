<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best first buildings in Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era are the buildings that improve your next few turns, not the buildings that look strongest on the town screen.**

In most games, your first building should be one of these:

1. **A key unit dwelling or upgrade** if your army needs safer fights.
2. **A compounding economy building** if your army can already clear.
3. **Gold, Law Points, or Astrology Points** depending on what your route needs.
4. **Mage Guild** if spells immediately improve combat, buffs, movement, or map routing.
5. **Marketplace** only if it fixes a real resource bottleneck.
6. **Creature growth before week end** if the weekly refresh is close.

The simple rule is:

**build for the next fight, the next building, and the next weekly growth cycle.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="best-first-building">
  What should you build first?
</h2>

The best first building is not the same for every faction, hero, or map. Olden Era gives you several early directions: economy, military buildings, Mage Guild, Marketplace, Law Points, Astrology Points, and faction-specific growth.

The mistake is thinking of the town screen as a fixed build order.

Instead, ask three questions:

1. **What lets me win the next fight with fewer losses?**
2. **What unlocks my next important building or upgrade?**
3. **What gives value before the end of the week?**

<GuideCallout title="First building rule">
If a building does not improve your next fight, next building, next route, or next weekly growth cycle, it is probably not your best first build.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="gold-law-astrology">
  Gold vs Law Points vs Astrology Points
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/olden-era/olden-era-best-buildings-first-gold-law-astrology.webp"
    alt="Olden Era town upgrade choice between gold, Law Points, and Astrology Points."
    width={300}

    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Gold, Law Points, and Astrology Points are not equal in every opening. Pick the one that supports your route fastest.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Many early town upgrades ask you to choose between direct income and progression resources. The safest beginner choice is often **gold**, because gold recruits units, upgrades stacks, and keeps buildings flowing.

But gold is not always best.

Choose **gold** when:

- you need to recruit immediately
- you need to upgrade a key unit
- your next building is gold-blocked
- you are recovering from losses
- your faction does not need a specific early law yet

Choose **Law Points** when:

- your faction has a strong early law
- the law improves unit growth or combat
- the law supports your hero route
- saving for a higher-tier law is part of your plan

Choose **Astrology Points** when:

- map spells matter soon
- Town Portal or mobility planning matters
- your route depends on global magic
- you already have enough army and gold to keep clearing

<GuideCallout title="Gold vs Law vs Astrology rule">
Gold is the safest default. Law Points are best when you know which law you are chasing. Astrology Points are best when map magic or mobility will change your route soon.
</GuideCallout>

</h2>

<figure className="my-8">

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

Mage Guild is not automatically early or late. It depends on whether spells solve a real problem.

Build Mage Guild early when:

- you need damage spells to clear safely
- you need buffs like initiative, speed, protection, or ranged support
- you have mana and a hero that can use spells well
- the Mage Guild unlocks the next spell tier or constellation path
- your route needs Town Portal or map magic support

Delay Mage Guild when:

- you cannot afford the spell path
- your hero has poor spell usage
- your next fights are already safe
- a unit dwelling or growth building would help more
- a missing resource blocks a more important timing

<GuideCallout title="Mage Guild rule">
Do not build Mage Guild because “spells are good.” Build it when the specific spells you unlock will change the next fight or the next route.
</GuideCallout>

  Build creature growth before week end
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Creature growth buildings become much more urgent when the weekly refresh is close.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Weekly creature growth changes building priority.

A dwelling built before the weekly refresh may give you more units immediately or preserve a full week of growth. The same building built after the refresh may still be useful, but it misses an important timing window.

Prioritize creature growth before week end when:

- your army is damaged
- your next route needs more bodies
- the building unlocks a key ranged or tank unit
- the faction depends on unit count
- you can still afford recruitment afterward

Delay creature growth when:

- you cannot recruit the units anyway
- a resource building unlocks more important progress
- your hero is already strong enough to clear
- the building does not affect the upcoming week

<GuideCallout title="Weekly growth rule">
If the week is about to roll over, ask whether a dwelling or growth building will produce value before the refresh. Timing can matter as much as the building itself.
</GuideCallout>

Gold income buildings are not exciting, but they can be correct because they start paying every day. The earlier you build them, the more turns they have to matter.

Build income when:

- your army can already clear easy fights
- you need gold for recruitment
- you are early enough for the income to compound
- the building does not delay a key dwelling
- your next power spike is gold-heavy

Delay income when:

- your army cannot take safe fights
- the next week’s growth is about to be missed
- the building blocks Mage Guild or a key unit
- you need a specific non-gold resource instead

<GuideCallout title="Income rule">
Gold income is strongest when it lets you keep building and recruiting every turn. It is weakest when it delays the combat power you need to keep clearing.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best first building in Heroes Olden Era?

The best first building is usually the one that improves your next few turns. Build a unit dwelling or upgrade if your army needs safer fights, build economy if your army can already clear, build Mage Guild if spells immediately help, and build Marketplace only when it fixes a real bottleneck.

### Should I build gold income first?

Gold income is a safe early choice when your army can already clear and you need money for recruitment, upgrades, and buildings. Do not choose gold automatically if you are blocked by wood, ore, Alchemical Dust, Law Points, or unit growth.

### Should I choose Law Points or gold first?

Choose gold when you need immediate recruitment or buildings. Choose Law Points when you know the law you are chasing and it supports your faction route. If you are unsure, gold is usually safer.

### When should I choose Astrology Points?

Choose Astrology Points when map spells, Town Portal, or global magic will matter soon. Do not choose Astrology only because it is available if your army still needs gold, units, or resources.

### When should I build Mage Guild?

Build Mage Guild when the spells immediately improve your fights, mobility, buffs, damage, or survival. Delay it if you do not have mana, spell scaling, or a fight where the spells matter.

### Is Marketplace worth building early?

Marketplace is worth building early only if it solves a bottleneck. If trading resources unlocks a key building, unit upgrade, Mage Guild, or recruitment timing, it is good. If not, it may just delay stronger buildings.

### Should I build creature dwellings before the end of the week?

Usually yes if the dwelling affects weekly growth or gives units you can use immediately. A dwelling built before the weekly refresh can be much stronger than the same dwelling built after it.

### What should I do if I cannot build the thing I want?

Check the exact missing cost first. You may need a mine, resource pickup, Marketplace trade, Alchemical Dust source, or cheaper tempo building before forcing the original plan.

### Is there one fixed build order for every faction?

No. Each faction and hero changes the answer. Use the same rule for all factions: build for your next fight, next bottleneck, and next weekly growth cycle.

### Which faction has the clearest early building route?

Necropolis and Temple currently have the clearest early routes from available footage. Necropolis usually checks Skeleton Archers, Marketplace, Mage Guild, Vampires, and Liches. Temple often checks Solar Temple, Muse Level 2, ranged growth, Mage Guild, and economy.
