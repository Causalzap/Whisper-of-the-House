<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Skeleton Archers are the safest early Necropolis engine in Heroes of Might and Magic: Olden Era. They matter because they turn Skeleton growth into ranged damage, but they must be protected from melee contact and bad ranged trades.**

Key rules:

1. **Upgrade cost:** 10 gold per Skeleton.
2. **100 Skeletons → 1000 gold upgrade cost.**
3. **Skeleton weekly growth:** 30 per week in current Early Access data.
4. **Strong Week 2 target:** roughly 100–200 Skeleton Archers if the opening is clean.
5. **Ranged mechanics matter:** far shots lose damage, and adjacent enemies force weaker melee attacks.
6. **Main rule:** protect the Archer stack. A bad fight can ruin the whole Necropolis snowball.

Skeleton Archers are not just a cheap upgrade. They are the unit that lets Necropolis clear early fights without bleeding its core stack.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="why-skeleton-archers-matter">
  Why Skeleton Archers matter
</h2>

Necropolis wants to snowball from repeated low-loss fights. Skeleton Archers are the safest early way to do that because they let your main stack deal damage before melee enemies can trade back.

That matters for three reasons:

- fewer losses means stronger future fights
- a larger stack makes each upgrade more valuable
- clean victories support Necromancy and Week 2 pressure

<GuideCallout title="Core rule">
Skeleton Archers are valuable because they reduce losses. If a fight still damages your main stack heavily, the upgrade is not doing its job.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="ranged-mechanics">
  Ranged attack mechanics
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Skeleton Archer ranged attack mechanics."
  columns={mechanicColumns}
  rows={mechanicRows}
/>

Skeleton Archers can attack from anywhere, but “any range” does not mean “full damage from anywhere.”

The two positioning rules are:

- do not let enemies become adjacent
- avoid unnecessary max-range shots when a safer closer shot is possible

<GuideCallout title="Range rule">
The safest Skeleton Archer fight is not always the farthest fight. It is the fight where enemies cannot touch the Archer stack, while your shots still deal efficient damage.
</GuideCallout>

</h2>

<GuideTable

<h2 id="necromancy-and-transformer">
  Necromancy and Undead Transformer value
</h2>

Skeleton Archers support Necromancy because they make low-loss victories easier.

They also give the rest of your Necropolis plan time to come online:

- Necromancy adds value after clean fights
- Undead Transformer rewards creature collection and conversion planning
- second heroes can collect huts and resources while the main Archer stack clears
- Diplomacy routes can add conversion value without forcing bad battles

<GuideCallout title="Engine rule">
Skeleton Archers are not the whole Necropolis plan. They are the safe engine that gives Necromancy, conversion, Vampires, and Liches time to work.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-if-you-took-losses">
  What if you lose too many Skeleton Archers?
</h2>

If your Archer stack gets damaged early, stop playing as if the snowball is still perfect.

Recover by:

- skipping hard fights
- recruiting before pushing
- delaying Vampires or Liches
- using a second hero for delivery
- taking gold or resource rewards
- rebuilding the stack before the next big objective

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Are Skeleton Archers good in Heroes Olden Era?

Yes. Skeleton Archers are one of the safest early Necropolis engines because they turn Skeleton growth into ranged damage and reduce melee losses.

### What are Skeleton Archer stats?

Skeleton Archers are Tier 1 Necropolis ranged Undead with 6 HP, 4 Attack, 2 Defense, 1–3 Damage, 3 Speed, 4 Initiative, 30 weekly growth, and 50 gold cost in current Early Access data.

### How does Skeleton Archer ranged attack work?

Skeleton Archers can attack at any range, but attacks at 3 or more hexes lose damage by distance, up to -50%. If an enemy is adjacent, the attack becomes a weaker melee attack.

### How much does it cost to upgrade Skeletons into Skeleton Archers?

The current Early Access cost is 10 gold per Skeleton. For example, 100 Skeletons cost 1000 gold to upgrade.

### When should I upgrade Skeletons?

Upgrade when the gold cost lets you clear several nearby fights with fewer losses. Delay if the stack is too small or the gold blocks a key building.

### How many Skeleton Archers should I have by Week 2?

A strong start can move toward 100–200 Skeleton Archers by Week 2, depending on hero, losses, map rewards, laws, and conversion value.

### Who is the best hero for Skeleton Archers?

Onkos is the cleanest pure Skeleton Archer snowball hero. Marl is excellent for safer control, while Funerella supports Necromancy scaling.

### Should I rush Vampires instead of Skeleton Archers?

Usually no. Vampires are strong, but they should not delay your early Skeleton Archer engine unless your economy already supports the rush.

### Should I take every fight with Skeleton Archers?

No. Take fights that your Archers can win safely. Skip lethal fights or fights that threaten your main Archer stack.

### What should I do if I lose too many Skeleton Archers?

Stop forcing fights, recruit, use a second hero for delivery, and rebuild the main stack before chasing greedy buildings.
