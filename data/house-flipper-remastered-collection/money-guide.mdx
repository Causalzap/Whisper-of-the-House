<h2 id="what-raises-sale-price">
  What raises a house sale price?
</h2>

A high sale price usually comes from a combination of clean condition, functional rooms, buyer fit, and controlled spending. Do not assume that expensive furniture always creates profit.

<GuideTable
  caption="Main price drivers in House Flipper Remastered Collection."
  columns={priceDriverColumns}
  rows={priceDriverRows}
/>

The most important early lesson is this: **a useful room is stronger than an expensive room**. A cheap bedroom with a real bed helps more than a beautiful empty room that the game does not understand.

<h2 id="room-requirements">
  Room labels and basic room requirements
</h2>

House Flipper uses room logic. That means a room needs the right objects before the game and buyers treat it as a bedroom, bathroom, office, kitchen, or other useful space.

<GuideTable
  caption="Basic room requirements and money value."
  columns={roomLabelColumns}
  rows={roomLabelRows}
/>

These requirements are why “make rooms functional” is not just generic advice. If a buyer wants a family house, they need real bedrooms. If a buyer values practical essentials, they need rooms that clearly serve a purpose.

<h2 id="sauna-rooms">
  Are sauna rooms good for profit?
</h2>

Sauna rooms were a known high-value idea in the original House Flipper, but in Remastered Collection you should treat sauna as a test route, not a guaranteed money trick.

If sauna items are available in your build, use them only on a house where the extra cost makes sense, then confirm the room recognition and buyer feedback before relying on it for profit.

<h2 id="how-to-use-negotiation">
  How to use negotiation
</h2>

Negotiation is most valuable when the base offer is already strong. Do not use it as a bandage for a badly targeted house.

Use this checklist before negotiating:

<GuideTable
  caption="When negotiation is worth using."
  columns={negotiatorColumns}
  rows={negotiatorRows}

A practical rule: negotiate when the top buyer is clearly ahead, the feedback is mostly positive, and the house value is high enough that a bigger increase matters. If the top buyer only barely leads or still complains about core problems, fix the house first.

For the **Negotiator** achievement, use a valuable house and push the buyer offer as high as you can before negotiating. A cheap starter house is usually the wrong place to chase a large negotiation gain.

<h2 id="which-house-should-you-flip-first">
  Which house should you flip first?
</h2>

Your first flip should not be your dream renovation. It should be a learning property.

If you are not sure which house to buy first, sort the property list by price and start with one of the cheapest small houses. Your goal is to practice cleaning, selling old furniture, fixing obvious problems, reading room labels, and checking buyer feedback without risking too much money.

Pick a first house that is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>cheap enough that mistakes do not ruin your save,</li>
  <li>small enough to clean quickly,</li>
  <li>simple enough to understand room labels,</li>
  <li>big enough to test one buyer direction,</li>
  <li>and not so large that furniture costs spiral out of control.</li>
</ul>

Do not start with a high-value house like Oceanside Villa unless you already understand buyer feedback and renovation cost control. Big properties can make more money later, but they also make bad spending decisions more expensive.

<h2 id="early-money-vs-late-money">
  Early money vs late money
</h2>

Early money comes from jobs. Late money comes from house sales.

In the first hour, jobs are better because they unlock tools and teach you the systems. Later, house sales become stronger because one good flip can beat many small jobs.

Use this split:

  <li><strong>Middle:</strong> buy cheap houses, clean heavily, control furniture cost.</li>

<h2 id="faq">
  House Flipper Remastered Collection Money FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best way to make money early?

Do jobs first. Early jobs pay you while unlocking cleaning, mounting, painting, and task-list skills that make house sales easier later.

### Should I buy a house right away?

No. Buy a house after the basic toolkit feels comfortable. If cleaning, mounting, painting, and item placement still feel slow, keep doing jobs first.

### What raises house sale price the most?

Clean condition, functional room labels, buyer-targeted renovation, and controlled furniture spending matter more than random expensive decoration.

### What are room labels?

Room labels are the game logic that makes a room count as a bedroom, bathroom, office, kitchen, or another useful room. A bedroom usually needs a bed. A bathroom needs core bathroom fixtures. An office needs office-style furniture.

### Are sauna rooms good for money?

Sauna rooms were a strong profit tactic in the original House Flipper. In Remastered, test sauna items, room recognition, and buyer feedback in your build before relying on it as a guaranteed money method.

### Which buyer preferences are confirmed in this guide?

The confirmed feedback in this guide comes from the Oceanside Villa sale example: Dolan Trusk, Jack Tarinton, Smoth Family, and Jantart Family. Other buyer rules are treated as legacy House Flipper logic that should be verified in Remastered.

### Can I use old House Flipper buyer guides?

Yes, but use them as starting points, not guaranteed Remastered rules. Confirm buyer reactions with the live buyer feedback panel before spending heavily.

### Who is a good buyer for practical profit?

Dolan Trusk and Jack Tarinton are good examples of practical-value buyers in the Oceanside Villa example. They respond better to useful layouts than emotional family comfort.

### How do I unlock the Negotiator achievement?

Use a valuable property, make the top buyer clearly like the house, then negotiate from a strong offer. Do not chase Negotiator on a tiny starter house.

### When should I negotiate?

Negotiate when the highest offer is clearly ahead, the buyer feedback is mostly satisfied, and the house value is high enough for a large gain to matter.

### Why did my profit stay low?

You probably overspent on furniture, decorated without a buyer target, ignored room labels, or sold before cleaning and fixing obvious problems.

### Is Free Play good for money testing?

Yes. Free Play is useful for testing room layouts, buyer reactions, and sale setups without committing your normal progression save.
