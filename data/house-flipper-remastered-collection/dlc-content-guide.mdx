<h2 id="dlc-content-guide">
  House Flipper Remastered Collection DLC content guide
</h2>

House Flipper Remastered Collection is not just the old base game with sharper lighting. It bundles the main House Flipper route, the major DLC-style regions, and new Remastered content into one map. That is why the game can feel huge once more areas appear.

For new players, the safest approach is simple: start with the main jobs, then branch outward. For returning players, the better route depends on what you already know. If you played the original and all major DLCs, you can jump much faster into your favorite region or go straight toward Heart Valley to see the new story content.

North Levyville is the 20-mission main route. Regions after that are part of the 93 additional DLC missions, so use the table as a route map instead of trying to clear everything at once.

<h2 id="new-vs-returning-players">
  New players vs returning players
</h2>

The Remastered map can be confusing because it gives different players very different problems. New players need structure. Returning players often want access.

  caption="Best DLC route by player type."

The key is to avoid treating the whole collection like a wiki checklist. A new player should not rush Flipperberg or Heart Valley just because they appear on the map. A returning player can move faster, but should still learn the new Remastered tools before judging how a region feels.

<h2 id="new-remastered-tools">
  New Remastered tools and features to know
</h2>

The new tools matter because DLC content often means bigger spaces, more objects, and more repeated work. Area Cleanup, Area Demolition, Favorites, and the Tool Selection Wheel make more sense once you stop doing tiny starter jobs and begin working on large properties.

<h2 id="flipperberg">
  Flipperberg: restaurant and Dine Out content
</h2>

Flipperberg is the restaurant route. Expect dining layouts, restaurant furniture, food-service objectives, restaurant management-style tasks, and Dine Out achievement progress.

The big difference from normal house jobs is that restaurant rooms need a functional layout, not just nice decoration. Think in terms of dining tables, enough chairs, usable kitchen or service areas, and clear movement space. If the job asks for specific restaurant furniture, search the exact item name instead of placing a similar-looking table or chair.

This region is easier after you already understand room identity from normal jobs. A bedroom needs a bed; an office needs office furniture; a restaurant needs a dining setup that actually reads as a restaurant.

<h2 id="moonrise-bay">
  Moonrise Bay: luxury and high-end homes
</h2>

Moonrise Bay is the luxury route. Expect expensive homes, high-end interiors, luxury renovation choices, and premium room design.

Do not confuse Moonrise Bay with Maple Bloom Village. Maple Bloom Village is the farm route; Moonrise Bay is about luxury and high-end property work.

Before spending heavily on luxury rooms, read the <Link href="/house-flipper-remastered-collection/money-guide">Money Guide</Link> so you understand furniture cost, buyer feedback, and final sale value.

  Winter Hills: winter homes and cold-region interiors
</h2>

Winter Hills is the winter-themed route. Expect cold-region houses, winter interiors, snowy property work, and **Winter Flipper** progress.

This region is more useful once you already understand the standard tool loop, because the main value is the themed property style rather than beginner onboarding. Winter homes are also a good place to practice making interiors feel warm and readable: lighting, wall choices, furniture contrast, and room identity matter more when the whole region leans cold and snowy.

<h2 id="faq">
  House Flipper Remastered Collection DLC FAQ
</h2>

### Are the 20 main missions enough before starting DLC regions?

Yes for most new players. The 20 North Levyville main missions teach the core loop: cleaning, trash removal, mounting, painting, windows, task lists, and early tool use. You do not need to finish every possible side activity before entering DLC regions, but you should feel comfortable with the basic tools first.

### How many additional DLC missions are included?

The major DLC regions add 93 additional missions. Handle them by region instead of trying to clear every area at once.

### What region should I start with?

Start with North Levyville. It teaches the main job loop, tools, task lists, and basic renovation before the DLC map gets too crowded.

### What is the best DLC order?

A safe order is North Levyville, Cozy Village, South Levyville, Maple Bloom Village, Flipperberg, Sunset Bay, Moonrise Bay, Winter Hills, then Heart Valley.

### What does Heart Valley include?

Heart Valley includes 6 new story-driven Lovely Jobs and is the clearest new Remastered content area. New players should save it for later.

### What does Maple Bloom Village include?

Maple Bloom Village is the farm route. Expect animals, barns, fields, countryside jobs, house-extension mechanics, and farm achievements.

### What does Flipperberg include?

Flipperberg is the restaurant route. Expect dining layouts, restaurant furniture, table setup, food-service objectives, and Dine Out achievement progress.

### Is Moonrise Bay farm content?

No. Treat Moonrise Bay as luxury / high-end home content. Use Maple Bloom Village for farm content.

### What region is for beach houses?

Sunset Bay is the beach and coastal-home region.

### Should returning players start from North Levyville?

Returning players can move faster. If you already know the original and its DLC systems, use North Levyville as a refresher or jump toward the region you care about most.

### Does Remastered support Steam Workshop content?

Remastered supports the original House Flipper ecosystem, including Workshop-style custom furniture and decoration support. If you already subscribed to Workshop items, check them in-game before building a full renovation around them, because item behavior and availability should be tested inside Remastered.

### Do original House Flipper owners get a discount?

Veteran players should check Steam directly before buying. Owner-based loyalty discounts can reduce the final price depending on which original House Flipper content and DLC packs you already own.

### What new Remastered tools matter for DLC?

Area Cleanup, Area Demolition, Favorites, Tool Selection Wheel, and House Import / Export are the main quality-of-life features to learn before tackling bigger DLC properties.
