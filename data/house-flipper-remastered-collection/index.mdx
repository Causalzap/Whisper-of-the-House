<h2 id="house-flipper-remastered-collection-guide">
  House Flipper Remastered Collection guide hub
</h2>

House Flipper Remastered Collection is easiest to understand if you treat it as a set of connected routes, not one giant checklist. The early jobs teach the core tools. Those tools make renovation faster. Renovation leads into house sales, buyer feedback, profit, achievements, and DLC regions.

If you are new, start with jobs. If you are returning from the original House Flipper, do not assume every old habit works exactly the same. Remastered keeps the familiar cleaning, painting, mounting, decorating, buying, and selling loop, but it adds new jobs, new houses, new items, quality-of-life changes, new tools, and a broader region map.

The cleanest path is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>start in Classic Mode if you want normal progression,</li>
  <li>clean the office and use the laptop,</li>
  <li>finish the first jobs to unlock tools and perks,</li>
  <li>learn room labels, cleaning, furniture, buyer feedback, and sale value,</li>
  <li>then branch into money, DLC regions, and achievements.</li>

<h2 id="what-to-do-first">
  What to do first in Classic Mode
</h2>

Your first goal is not to buy a house. Your first goal is to unlock enough tools that houses become worth buying.

Start by cleaning your office enough to use it comfortably. Then use the laptop and follow the first job route. The early jobs teach cleaning, moving objects, buying items, mounting fixtures, painting walls, repairing outlets, and reading room task lists.

The first tools and systems to prioritize are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>cleaning tools</strong>, because dirt and trash appear constantly,</li>
  <li><strong>mounting tools</strong>, because radiators, sinks, and towel radiators appear early,</li>
  <li><strong>painting tools</strong>, because room changes quickly require wall work,</li>
  <li><strong>selling and object control</strong>, because furniture management affects renovation value,</li>
  <li><strong>perks for repeated actions</strong>, because cleaning, mounting, and painting upgrades save time over many jobs.</li>
</ul>

Do not worry if the map looks large. In Classic Mode, locked regions and locked tools are part of the learning route.

The first job, **Ex-Boyfriend Stole the Radiator**, is the best example of how the early game works. You clean trash, remove stains, move small objects, buy a radiator, mount it, and check the task list for anything missed.

After that, **Cleaning the Garage** teaches deeper cleaning, windows, hidden stains, and UV light. **A Heated Argument** pushes you into radiators, towel radiators, and sink installation. **Art in Every Corner** starts the painting route and electrical outlet repair. **The Princess on the Way** introduces a larger early room setup with nursery furniture.

If you are stuck, do not guess. Check the room task list, look behind furniture, move small objects, verify exact item names, and use UV light when dirt blends into the room.

<h2 id="money-selling">
  Money, selling, buyers, and profit
</h2>

Early money comes from jobs. Bigger money comes from house sales.

In the beginning, jobs are useful because they pay you while unlocking tools. Later, the real money comes from buying a property, improving it, reading buyer feedback, and selling it for a stronger final offer.

The <Link href="/house-flipper-remastered-collection/money-guide">Money Guide</Link> covers room labels, confirmed buyer feedback, Oceanside Villa, Dolan Trusk, negotiation timing, renovation cost control, and what to do when profit feels too low.

Read it before selling a valuable house.

<h2 id="achievements">
  Achievements and 100% direction
</h2>

House Flipper Remastered Collection currently has **56 Steam achievements**. They are not all the same type. Some unlock naturally from jobs, some come from secret actions, some require buyer sales, and many are tied to DLC routes like pets, farm work, Dine Out, winter homes, luxury content, and coastal properties.

A safe achievement route is:

  <li>learn cleaning, painting, mounting, and room objectives,</li>

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Common mistakes to avoid
</h2>

Most early mistakes come from trying to play the whole collection at once. Start with jobs, unlock tools, and let each route teach one system before moving to the next.

If a job is stuck, use the First Jobs Walkthrough. If profit is low, use the Money Guide. If the map feels too large, use the DLC Content Guide. Each spoke guide has a more specific mistake table for that problem.

Do not trust old House Flipper buyer rules blindly. Legacy buyer logic can help, but confirm with the live Remastered buyer panel before spending heavily.

<h2 id="faq">
  House Flipper Remastered Collection FAQ
</h2>

### Is House Flipper Remastered Collection good for new players?

Yes. Start in Classic Mode if you want the normal learning route. The early jobs teach cleaning, mounting, painting, tool unlocks, room tasks, and basic house work before you need to worry about big sales.

### Should I choose Classic Mode or Free Play Mode?

Choose Classic Mode for progression. Choose Free Play Mode if you want to renovate freely, test a layout, rebuild a property, or repeat a sale setup without grinding jobs again.

### What should I do first?

Clean your office, use the laptop, take the first available jobs, and unlock basic tools. Do not rush into buying houses until you can clean, paint, mount fixtures, and control furniture properly.

### Does House Flipper 1 save data carry over?

No. Treat Remastered Collection as a fresh start. Use Classic Mode for normal progression or Free Play Mode if you want faster access to tools and content.

### Does the Remastered Collection include all original DLC?

It is built as an all-in-one Remastered collection with major DLC-style regions such as garden, pets, farm, restaurants, beach homes, luxury homes, winter content, and Heart Valley. Use the DLC Content Guide for the region route.

### How many main missions and DLC missions are there?

North Levyville contains the 20 main missions. The major DLC regions add 93 additional missions. Heart Valley appears as a 0/6 late route.

### Which region should I start with?

Start with North Levyville. It teaches the main job loop, tools, task lists, and basic renovation before the region map gets too crowded.

### When should I do Heart Valley?

Save Heart Valley for later if you are new. It is framed as a late test and may expect tools or skills you unlock elsewhere.

### Is House Flipper Remastered Collection different from House Flipper 2?

Yes. House Flipper Remastered Collection is a refreshed version of the original House Flipper experience with DLC-style content, updated visuals, quality-of-life changes, and the original-style job / buyer / renovation loop. House Flipper 2 is a separate sequel with its own systems.


### Is there multiplayer or co-op?

No. House Flipper Remastered Collection is a single-player renovation game.

### Does it support PS5 and Xbox?

Yes. House Flipper Remastered Collection launched on June 4, 2026 for Steam, PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X/S.

### Does it support Chinese?

Check the current Steam or platform store language list before buying. Language support can vary by platform and version.

### How do I make money early?

Do jobs first. They pay you while unlocking tools. Once you can renovate properly, start learning house sales, buyer feedback, room labels, and negotiation through the Money Guide.

### When should I start achievement hunting?

Start after you understand early jobs, tools, and selling. Many achievements are easier when you naturally progress through jobs, DLC regions, money goals, and house sales.
