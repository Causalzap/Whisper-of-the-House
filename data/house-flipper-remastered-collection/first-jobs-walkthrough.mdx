<h2 id="ex-boyfriend-stole-the-radiator">
  Ex-Boyfriend Stole the Radiator
</h2>

This is your first real job and the best place to learn the basics.

Your goal is to clean the mess, check for missing objectives, buy a radiator, mount it, and finish the job. The client message hints that the radiator is not the only thing to pay attention to. If the task list still has a second objective after cleaning and radiator work, check for another missing item or hidden room task instead of assuming the job is done.

Do this first:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Enter the house and remove visible trash.</li>
  <li>Clean dirt from counters, floors, furniture, bathroom surfaces, and bedroom items.</li>
  <li>Move small objects if they block stains, trash, or task items.</li>
  <li>Open the task list when the job stops progressing.</li>
  <li>Use the tablet store and search for a radiator.</li>
  <li>Place the radiator on the correct wall mount.</li>
  <li>Assemble the radiator instead of only placing it.</li>
  <li>Check each room before completing the job.</li>
</ol>

This job is also your first practical lesson in tool progression. Cleaning, object handling, tablet buying, and radiator mounting all matter later, so do not treat it as a throwaway job.

A Heated Argument is mostly about heating and bathroom fixtures. Compared with the first radiator job, this one asks you to install more items and pay closer attention to the exact fixture type.

You will usually need to handle normal radiators, a towel radiator, a sink, pipe covers or mount points, and assembly steps that require clicking the highlighted parts.

  <li>Use the store search if you cannot find the correct radiator type.</li>

Art in Every Corner is the first job where painting becomes the main focus. It can feel slow if you have no painting upgrades yet, but it teaches how paint color, wall counts, and room objectives work.

Start with the task list. The job may ask for a specific paint color and may also include electrical outlet repair.

Use this order:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Read the room objective before buying paint.</li>
  <li>Buy the exact paint color the task asks for.</li>
  <li>Move furniture if it blocks outlets or wall sections.</li>
  <li>Repair electrical outlets before or during painting.</li>
  <li>Paint the required walls carefully.</li>
  <li>Refill the paint roller when needed.</li>
  <li>Sell leftover paint if you want to recover a little money.</li>

<h2 id="why-your-job-is-not-complete">
  Why your job is not complete
</h2>

If an early job will not finish, do not assume something is broken. Most of the time, one small objective is missing.

<GuideTable
  caption="Why first jobs get stuck below 100%."
  columns={hiddenColumns}
  rows={hiddenRows}
/>

The fastest check is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Open the room task list.</li>
  <li>Find the room that is not complete.</li>
  <li>Look for missing trash, dirt, fixture, paint, window, outlet, or furniture.</li>
  <li>Move objects if the room looks clean but the objective is not done.</li>
  <li>Use UV light or dirt visibility perks when stains are hard to spot.</li>
</ol>

Do not randomly buy items before reading the task. The task wording is usually the answer.

<h2 id="should-you-finish-100-percent">
  Should you finish jobs at 100%?
</h2>

When you are learning, yes, try to finish jobs as cleanly as possible.

You can often leave once the job allows completion, but stopping early teaches bad habits. The early route is where you learn how the game hides tasks. If you practice checking each room now, later jobs become much faster.

Finish at 100% when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you are still learning,</li>
  <li>you want more practice with the tools,</li>
  <li>you want cleaner job habits,</li>
  <li>you are trying to understand room objectives,</li>
  <li>or you are preparing for achievement and completion routes.</li>
</ul>

Leaving early is fine later when you know exactly what you are skipping. In the first jobs, full completion is better training.

<h2 id="can-you-repeat-jobs">
  Can you repeat early jobs?
</h2>

In the original House Flipper, completed jobs could be repeated from the job archive. For House Flipper Remastered Collection, confirm the replay option through the laptop job list or job archive after you complete a job.

If you see a replay option, use it to practice faster clears, clean up missed completion, or test a better route. Repeating early jobs is most useful when you are trying to understand hidden task logic, improve completion speed, or prepare for completion-focused achievements like **Perfectionist**.

<h2 id="faq">
  House Flipper Remastered Collection First Jobs FAQ
</h2>

### What is the first job in House Flipper Remastered Collection?

The first job is Ex-Boyfriend Stole the Radiator. It teaches cleaning, trash removal, tablet buying, radiator placement, and fixture mounting.

### Why can’t I finish Ex-Boyfriend Stole the Radiator?

You are probably missing a small piece of trash, a stain, the radiator assembly step, or a second hidden objective from the task list. Check each room before completing the job.

### How do I unlock the tablet?

Complete **Ex-Boyfriend Stole the Radiator**. The job introduces the tablet when you need to buy the missing radiator.

### How do I complete Cleaning the Garage?

Remove trash, clean dirt and grease, clean the window, and use the UV light if stains are hard to see. Do not waste time redesigning the garage.

### What does the UV light do?

The UV light helps expose stains that are hard to spot. Use it when a room looks clean but the dirt task is not complete.

### What do I install in A Heated Argument?

A Heated Argument focuses on radiators, towel radiator, and sink installation. Buy mountable fixtures and finish the assembly steps.

### What if I bought the wrong sink?

Use the sell tool to remove the wrong item, then search the store again by the exact fixture type from the task list. Look for a mountable sink, not a decorative cabinet sink.

### How do I repair electrical outlets?

Move furniture if it blocks the outlet, interact with the broken outlet, remove damaged parts, connect the wires, and finish the screws or cover steps.

### How do I complete Art in Every Corner?

Buy the required paint, use the paint roller on the correct walls, repair any electrical outlets the task asks for, and check the task list before leaving.

### What furniture do I need in The Princess on the Way?

The job asks for nursery furniture such as a baby cot, bookcase, cabinet with changing table, and a poof or nursery seat. Match the Remastered task list wording as closely as possible.

### Should I finish early jobs at 100%?

Yes, especially while learning. Full completion teaches the task-list habits you need for later jobs, achievements, and house sales.

### Can I repeat early jobs?

In the original House Flipper, completed jobs could be repeated from the job archive. In Remastered Collection, check the laptop job list or job archive after completing a job to confirm whether replay is available.
