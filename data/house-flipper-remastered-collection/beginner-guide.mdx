<h2 id="classic-or-free-play">
  Classic Mode vs Free Play: what is the actual difference?
</h2>

Pick **Classic Mode** when you want the game to teach you the full loop: jobs, tool unlocks, skill points, houses, and money. Pick **Free Play** when you already know what you want to build or test.

The important difference is not only “slow progression vs freedom.” It changes how you approach the game:

<GuideTable
  caption="Classic Mode vs Free Play for beginners."
  columns={modeColumns}
  rows={modeRows}
/>

In Classic Mode, the early jobs matter because they unlock the tools you need. In Free Play, you can move much faster into renovation and testing, but you also lose some of the natural teaching curve.

Use **Classic Mode** for your first normal save. Use **Free Play** for experiments, repeated sale setups, or rebuilding a property you already sold.

In this job, focus on the basic habits:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>pick up visible trash,</li>
  <li>clean dirt from furniture, counters, floors, and bathroom surfaces,</li>
  <li>move small objects if they hide stains or trash,</li>
  <li>open the task list when progress stops,</li>
  <li>buy the radiator from the tablet,</li>
  <li>mount the radiator instead of only placing it,</li>
  <li>and check each room before completing the job.</li>
</ul>

Most early “stuck” moments are not bugs. Usually one small trash item, stain, fixture, or room task is still missing.

  <li>and do not move or sell client items unless the job asks for it.</li>

This is why cleaning upgrades are so strong early. They do not only save time; they reduce frustration.

<h2 id="dont-rush-buying-houses">
  When should you buy your first house?
</h2>

Do not buy a serious house until the basic toolkit feels comfortable.

In the first hour, jobs are safer because they pay you while unlocking the tools that make later house sales easier. Once cleaning, mounting, painting, and furniture control stop feeling slow, buying a house becomes much less risky.

If you are ready to learn selling, buyers, and profit, use the <Link href="/house-flipper-remastered-collection/money-guide">Money Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  House Flipper Remastered Collection Beginner FAQ
</h2>

### Should I start with Classic Mode or Free Play?

Start with Classic Mode if this is your first normal run. It teaches jobs, tools, skills, and progression. Use Free Play when you want to renovate freely, test layouts, or repeat a house setup.

### What is different about Free Play?

Free Play gives you broad access to tools, skills, jobs, houses, and extra cash much earlier. It is better for testing and renovating than learning the normal progression curve.

### What should I do first?

Clean the office enough to use the laptop, then take Ex-Boyfriend Stole the Radiator. That job teaches cleaning, trash removal, buying a radiator, and mounting fixtures.

### Should I buy a house right away?

No. Do jobs first until cleaning, mounting, painting, and furniture control feel comfortable. Houses are easier to profit from once your tools are ready.

### What are the best first perks?

Take cleaning upgrades first, then dirt visibility, mounting speed, and painting perks. Skip negotiation perks until you start selling houses regularly.

### Why does the map feel overwhelming?

Remastered Collection can show many DLC-style regions early. Start with North Levyville, then branch into Cozy Village or South Levyville. Save Heart Valley for much later.

### What is Area Cleanup?

Area Cleanup is a Remastered quality-of-life feature that helps clean or organize a defined area faster when available. Use it, then still check the task list before leaving.

### What is the Favorites Tab for?

Use the Favorites Tab to save items you buy often, such as common fixtures, lights, chairs, bookcases, or decoration pieces.

### What is the Tool Selection Wheel?

The Tool Selection Wheel helps you switch tools faster once you have cleaning, selling, mounting, painting, and other tools unlocked.

### Is Photo Mode useful?

Yes, but mostly for tracking finished rooms or before / after results. It is not required for early progression.
