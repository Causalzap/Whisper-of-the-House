<h2 id="why-craps-is-good">
  Why Street Craps is worth learning
</h2>

Street Craps is not the safest beginner game, but it is one of the most valuable games to learn because it rewards team control.

Roulette is easy. Duck Race is simple. Slots are fast. But Street Craps gives you something those games do not: **a table where coordination and dice handling can matter.**

Craps is good when:

- one player understands the target
- one player controls the dice
- the team stays out of the way
- items are used before the big bet
- the group knows when to stop

Craps is bad when:

- everyone crowds the table
- the roller throws before reading
- players yell different numbers
- the team max bets before learning the flow
- someone tries to recover every loss with one more throw

If your group is chaotic, learn Roulette first. If your group can actually listen, Craps can carry runs.

<GuideCallout title="Physics note">
Gamble With Your Friends is built around physics-heavy chaos, and Street Craps is one of the tables where dice handling visibly matters. That does not mean every die face can be perfectly controlled. Start with minimum bets, try small adjustments, and only scale up once your team can see that controlled movement is helping the result.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="basic-flow">
  Street Craps basic flow
</h2>

Street Craps is easiest when you slow the table down.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to play Street Craps without throwing the run."
  columns={crapsFlowColumns}
  rows={crapsFlowRows}

The basic flow is:

1. choose the roller
2. choose the caller
3. place a controlled bet
4. read the target
5. move the dice carefully
6. re-check the table after every good or bad hit
7. stop after the planned win or loss limit

New players lose on Craps because they treat every throw like a reaction test. It is not. Read first, throw second.

<h2 id="cheese-strategy">
  The dice cheese strategy
</h2>

The reason players call this a cheese table is simple: you can influence the dice more than you can influence a normal pure RNG table.

That does not mean every throw is free money. It means the team should stop full-sending dice blindly.

The most important habit is this:

**Do not move both dice if one die is already good.**

If the table wants a target and one die is already helping, protect that die. Work the other die. Craps gets much easier when the roller stops resetting both dice every time.

<h2 id="one-die-at-a-time">
  One die at a time
</h2>

This is the single best Craps habit.

When both dice are wrong, a full throw can make sense. But when one die is close or already useful, do not ruin it. Move the other die carefully.

A practical one-die adjustment looks like this:

1. **Caller reads the target.**
2. **Caller says which die is already useful.**
3. **Roller avoids touching the useful die.**
4. **Roller gives the other die a short tap, small push, or low-power throw.**
5. **Team re-checks the table before touching either die again.**

Use one-die control when:

- the caller says one die is already good
- the table needs a small adjustment
- the dice are near the target
- the team is on a high bet
- the roller is getting nervous

Do not use one-die control when:

- the dice are completely scattered
- nobody can read the table
- the timer is too low for careful setup
- the team is only minimum betting for practice

<h2 id="drink-insurance-craps">
  Drink + Insurance + Craps
</h2>

This is the risky old-player setup.

**Drink** increases profit while drunk. **Insurance** decreases loss amount. Together, they make sense on Craps because Craps can produce a big payout but can also punish a bad throw.

Use this setup when:

- the roller already knows Craps
- the team accepts the risk
- the bet is large enough to matter
- Insurance is active or ready
- the Drink player can still control the table

Avoid this setup when:

- the roller panics
- the team has no caller
- the player cannot aim while drunk
- the table is being played blind
- the team is already safe and does not need risk

<h2 id="recovery-craps">
  Recovering after a bad Craps throw
</h2>

The best Craps players do not panic after one bad throw.

Use this recovery order:

1. stop touching the dice
2. read the table again
3. check whether one die is still useful
4. call the next target clearly
5. use one-die control if possible
6. only add items if the bet is worth saving
7. leave the table if the team is spiraling

<h2 id="recommended-craps-route">
  Recommended Craps route
</h2>

Use this route when your team wants to make Street Craps a real strategy:

1. **Practice with a small bet.**
2. **Assign one roller and one caller.**
3. **Learn how the table displays the target.**
4. **Use small adjustments and one-die control.**
5. **Move to medium bets once the caller can read the table.**
6. **Use Drink + Insurance only with a stable roller.**
7. **Use Taser + Golden Chip only after the team knows the table.**
8. **Leave after the planned payout or stop point.**
9. **Use Quota Gun only if Craps fails and quota is still short.**

This is how you turn Craps from a funny table into a run-winning table.


<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Is Street Craps good in Gamble With Your Friends?

Yes. Street Craps is one of the best games if your team can read the target, control the dice, and stop after the planned win.

### Is Street Craps good for beginners?

Not as a first serious table. Beginners should practice with small bets first, then increase bet size once one player can call the target clearly.

### How do you cheese Street Craps?

Assign one caller, read the target, use small controlled adjustments, and move one die at a time when possible. Do not let everyone crowd the table.

### Does the dice cheese always work?

No. Treat it as a physics-based control tactic, not a guaranteed result. Test with minimum bets first and scale up only when your team can see that small adjustments are helping.

### What is the biggest Street Craps mistake?

The biggest mistake is throwing before reading the target. The caller should read first, then the roller should move the dice.

### Should I use Taser and Golden Chip on Craps?

Yes, but only after your team understands the table. Use Taser first to increase maximum bet, then Golden Chip for the free all-in.

### Is Drink good with Craps?

Drink is good if the roller can still control the table while drunk. It is bad if the player becomes too chaotic to aim.

### Is Insurance good with Craps?

Insurance is useful when you want to take a risky Craps push but reduce the downside of a failed bet.

### Should I use Time Machine on Craps?

Use Time Machine only after a meaningful failed Craps play. Do not waste it on small practice bets.

### What should I do after a bad Craps throw?

Stop, read the table again, check whether one die is still useful, and recover with controlled movement instead of throwing faster.

### When should I leave the Craps table?

Leave after the planned payout, after the item window ends, or when the team starts panic throwing without reading the table.
