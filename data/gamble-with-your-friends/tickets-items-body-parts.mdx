**Buy the item for a specific plan, not because it looks funny.**

<h2 id="high-priority-items">
  High-priority items explained
</h2>

These are the items worth explaining beyond a table row because they change how the team plays the next casino day.

<h3 id="holy-statue">
  Holy Statue
</h3>

Holy Statue costs **7 tickets** and says:

**While active, prevent all loss nearby.**

This is one of the best beginner items because it gives the team a simple plan:

1. pick the table first
2. group near the item holder
3. activate the item
4. bet only during the protected window
5. stop when the protection ends

The common mistake is continuing to gamble after the protection window is gone.

<h3 id="golden-chip-and-taser">
  Golden Chip and Taser
</h3>

Golden Chip costs **4 tickets** and gives a single free all-in. Taser costs **4 tickets** and increases maximum bet when used on a keypad.

Together, they form the cleanest early combo:

1. use Taser on the keypad
2. raise the maximum bet
3. spend Golden Chip for a larger free all-in
4. avoid extra unprotected bets afterward

This is only a preview. Full routing belongs in the <Link href="/gamble-with-your-friends/best-item-combos">Best Item Combos and Strategies</Link> guide.

<h3 id="time-machine">
  Time Machine
</h3>

Time Machine says:

**Rolls back time.**

Its price was not captured, but the effect is strong enough to track. It is best after a meaningful failed play, not as a random panic button.

Use it after:

- a planned large bet fails
- a protected strategy goes wrong
- the team burns money too quickly
- the team can repeat the play with better information

Do not use it after tiny losses or when nobody knows what should change.

<h3 id="profit-items">
  Profit items
</h3>

Several items increase profit in different ways:

- **Drink** — 5 tickets, increase profit while drunk.
- **Microphone** — 6 tickets, while active, increase profit nearby by singing.
- **Camera** — 6 tickets, capture a winning player for extra profit.
- **Bonus Draw** — 8 tickets, get ticket on profit.
- **Stake Holder** — 6 tickets, increases the power of each item. Treat it as an item amplifier, not a first-buy core plan.

These are strongest when the team already has a realistic win attempt. They are weak when everyone is scattered across random tiny bets.

<h3 id="risk-reward-reels">
  Angel’s Reel and Devil’s Reel
</h3>

Angel’s Reel and Devil’s Reel both cost **3 tickets**, but they serve opposite moods.

**Angel’s Reel** gives a chance to revert your last loss. Use it after a meaningful loss.

**Devil’s Reel** can triple or lose your last win. Use it only when the team accepts the risk and the previous win is large enough to matter.

<h2 id="gun-and-body-part-value">
  Quota Gun and body part value
</h2>

Quota Gun costs **5 tickets** and says:

**Pays 33% of quota for each body part it shoots off.**

That makes it one of the clearest emergency economy tools in the game. It does not depend on winning a table, and it can convert body parts into immediate quota progress.

Use Quota Gun when:

- the team is short on quota
- the timer is low
- normal bets are too slow
- the group agrees who is losing the part
- the lost part will not ruin the next day

Do not use Quota Gun when:

- the team is stable
- the target player is carrying an important item
- the player needs voice, vision, or movement
- the group is only doing it for comedy

<GuideCallout title="Body part rule">
A body part is not free money. It is borrowed power with a penalty attached.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="recommended-shopping-rules">
  Recommended shopping rules
</h2>

Use these rules before each casino day:

1. **One person calls ticket spending.**
2. **No Mystery Box until the main item is covered.**
3. **No body-part sale unless it saves the run or buys a key item.**
4. **No Golden Chip before checking whether Taser can raise the max bet.**
5. **No Time Machine unless the team knows what mistake it is fixing.**
6. **No item activation until the table and players are ready.**

These rules keep the chaos fun without burning all your tickets before the run starts.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you get tickets in Gamble With Your Friends?

You can earn tickets by hitting quota, completing challenges, overshooting goals, and selling or losing body parts. Bonus Draw can also get ticket on profit.

### What should I buy first with tickets?

For most beginner groups, the best first buys are Holy Statue, Golden Chip, Taser, Microphone, Drink, Insurance, or Quota Gun.

### How much do items cost?

Confirmed prices include Angel’s Reel for 3 tickets, Devil’s Reel for 3 tickets, Mystery Box for 4, Golden Chip for 4, Taser for 4, Quota Gun for 5, Drink for 5, Camera for 6, Stake Holder for 6, Microphone for 6, Holy Statue for 7, Insurance for 7, and Bonus Draw for 8. Time Machine’s price was not captured.

### Are cosmetics worth buying?

Not early. Cosmetics are fun, but your first tickets should usually go toward items that help the team survive the next casino day.

### Are mystery boxes worth it?

Sometimes, but not as a first priority. Mystery Box costs 4 tickets and gives a random item, so buy it only after your team already has a useful item plan or extra tickets.

### How do you pick up items?

Shop screenshots show an **E Pick Up** prompt. After buying, make sure one player actually picks up the item before entering the casino.

### How do you use items after buying them?

Pick up the item first, carry it to the right place, then follow the held item’s on-screen prompt. Some items affect nearby players, while others must be used on a specific target, such as Taser on a keypad or Quota Gun on a body part.

### Why is my item not working?

The item may not have been picked up, the wrong player may be holding it, the holder may be too far from the target, the item may need a specific table/keypad/body part, or the team may be using it outside its active window.

### What does Holy Statue do?

Holy Statue costs 7 tickets and prevents all loss nearby while active.

### What does Golden Chip do?

Golden Chip costs 4 tickets and gives a single free all-in.

### What does Taser do?

Taser costs 4 tickets and is used on a keypad to increase maximum bet.

### What does Time Machine do?

Time Machine rolls back time. Its effect is confirmed, but its ticket price was not captured.

### What does Quota Gun do?

Quota Gun costs 5 tickets and pays 33% of quota for each body part it shoots off.

### Should you sell or shoot off body parts?

Only when the value saves the run or enables a key purchase. Do not trade body parts casually, because the penalty can affect voice, vision, movement, or interaction.

### Which body part is safest to lose?

There is no universally safe body part. Eye loss can hurt vision, mouth loss can hurt communication, and body loss can hurt movement. Choose based on the player’s role.

### What is the biggest ticket mistake?

The biggest mistake is spending tickets before the team agrees on a plan. A random purchase can block a stronger item or body recovery.
