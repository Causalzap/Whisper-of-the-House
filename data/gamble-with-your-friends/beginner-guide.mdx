<h2 id="first-run-plan">
  First run plan
</h2>

Your first goal is not to see every table. Your first goal is to understand the loop without letting the shared bank collapse.

<GuideTable
  caption="Best first-run route in Gamble With Your Friends."
  columns={firstRunColumns}
  rows={firstRunRows}
/>

A practical first run looks like this:

1. check tickets and body parts
2. buy one planned item if you can afford it
3. retrieve the item before entering the limo
4. choose one first table as a group
5. use small bets to learn controls
6. move to medium bets once the table is understood
7. use one item-backed push if the team needs a bigger swing
8. review the graph before spending tickets for the next day

<GuideCallout title="Beginner coordination rule">
Do not let every player freestyle at the same time. In a shared-bank game, six small bad decisions can be worse than one obvious all-in mistake.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="shared-bank">
  The shared bank is the real enemy
</h2>

Gamble With Your Friends is a co-op game, but the most dangerous opponent is usually your own team.

The bank is shared. If one player wins a big Roulette bet and another player immediately spends it on Duck Race, the team has not really stabilized. The money only passed through your hands before returning to the casino.

A good team setup is:

- one player calls the current target
- one player carries the strongest item
- one player handles complicated tables
- one player watches the timer
- everyone else uses low-risk bets or waits during large plays

For 4-6 player groups, make one rule before the floor starts:

**No large bets unless the team calls it.**

<h2 id="tickets-items">
  Tickets and items: what to buy first
</h2>

Tickets are one of the most important resources in Gamble With Your Friends. You can earn them by hitting quota, completing challenges, overshooting goals, and trading body parts.

For beginners, the best item priorities are:

1. **Prevent Loss Nearby**
2. **Free All-In Coin**
3. **Time Machine / Rewind**
4. **Increase Max Bet**
5. **Increase Profit**
6. **Loss Reduction / Insurance**
7. **Ticket-on-Profit tools**
8. **Mystery Box only when the team can afford chaos**

  caption="Observed Gamble With Your Friends item effects and best beginner uses."

### Buying and retrieving items

Use this checklist before entering the limo:

1. pick the item
2. place or confirm it in the buying area
3. press the buy button
4. check whether the item moved to the retrieve area
5. have one player physically carry the item
6. say when the item should be used

<GuideCallout title="Item-buying rule">
Do not buy an item unless the team can answer three questions: who carries it, which table it supports, and when it gets used.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="body-parts">
  Body parts are emergency economy
</h2>

Body parts are funny, but they are also part of the run economy.

You can lose, sell, or trade body parts depending on the run state. In day-one footage, losing a mouth made voice harder to understand, losing an eye affected vision, and losing the body affected movement badly enough that other players had to carry the person.

One early observed value:

**A gun/body-part interaction can pay about 33% of the quota for shooting off a body part.**

Use body parts when:

- the team is under heavy pressure
- normal gambling options are failing
- a key ticket purchase would change the next day
- the player agrees to the trade
- the run would likely fail without it

Do not use body parts when:

- the group is only doing it for comedy
- the player needs vision, voice, or movement for the next floor
- the team has not agreed who is losing what
- you have a safer item-based recovery option

<h2 id="floors">
  Floor clues and progression

<h2 id="when-to-stop">
  When to stop gambling
</h2>

Stopping is the strongest beginner skill.

Stop gambling when:

- the team is over the required target
- the timer is almost over
- multiple people are betting at once
- someone just won a large amount
- someone is chasing losses
- the team has no item backup
- a player is max betting a game they do not understand

Keep gambling only when:

- you are still below the target
- you have a protected item combo
- you are completing a low-risk challenge
- the team agrees to one final push
- the bet size cannot kill the run

<GuideCallout title="The best beginner strategy">
**PRO TIP: THE SURVIVAL RULE**
After hitting quota, the default move is not "one more spin." The default move is "**protect the money.**" The casino wins when you turn a safe win into a wipe.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the first quota in Gamble With Your Friends?

One day-one footage run shows about **$1,000** starting money and a **$1.2K** target. Treat this as an observed launch-version example, not a permanent formula.

### How long is each casino day?

Each casino day lasts **5 minutes**.

### What happens if you miss quota?

Missing quota can trigger a punishment scene and may cost the group momentum, body parts, or the run state depending on the situation.

### Is money shared?

Yes. The group shares one bank account and one debt. A reckless player can spend money that another player just earned.

### Can you play solo?

Yes. Solo play is possible, but the Steam page says it is not advised unless you are feeling lucky.

### What should I buy first with tickets?

Start with reliable run-changing tools: **Prevent Loss Nearby**, **Free All-In**, **Time Machine / Rewind**, **Increase Max Bet**, or **Increase Profit**. Avoid mystery boxes until your group can afford randomness.

### Are mystery boxes worth buying?

They can be, but they are not reliable first purchases. Buy them after the team has handled important items, body recovery, or a clear next-floor plan.

### What does the gun do?

In early community footage, the gun/body-part interaction can remove body parts and convert them into quota value. One observed value was about **33% of quota**.

### Duck Race or Roulette: which is better for a fast quota push?

Roulette is cleaner for a planned fast push **when the team commits to 1:1 bets (Red/Black) and ignores high-risk numbers.** While Duck Race is easier to spam, its randomness makes it more dangerous for undisciplined groups.

### Which Floor 1 game should beginners learn first?

Roulette is the easiest to understand. Blackjack is better for players who know basic card decisions. Street Craps may become strong, but it needs its own guide.

### How do you unlock Floor 2?

The Steam page says higher floors unlock as you get richer and bolder. Exact launch-version thresholds still need cleaner testing.

### How many floors are there?

There are **4 themed casino floors**.

### How many games are there?

There are **17 games of chance**.

### How many endings are there?

There are **3 endings**.

### How many achievements are there?

Steam currently lists **55 achievements**.

### What is the biggest beginner mistake?

The biggest mistake is treating the shared bank like personal spending money. In this game, one player’s bad bet can become everyone’s problem.
