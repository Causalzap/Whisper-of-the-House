<h2 id="challenge-active-rule">
  Challenge must be active first
</h2>

This is the most important rule for achievements that are not in the bugged nine.

Many game-specific achievements are tied to Lone Shark challenges. If you complete the condition without the matching challenge active, the achievement may not unlock.

That means a random lucky win is not always enough.

A clean achievement attempt starts before the bet:

- answer the phone
- check the challenge
- confirm the floor
- confirm the game
- then complete the condition

<h2 id="not-bugged">
  Not unlocking does not always mean bugged
</h2>

The nine achievements above are the ones to skip. For most other achievements, a failed unlock usually means the test was messy.

<GuideTable
  caption="Common not-unlocking causes that are not confirmed bugs."
  columns={notBuggedColumns}
  rows={notBuggedRows}

The useful split is:

**Known bugged:** the nine Floor 2–4 Big Spender / High Roller / Lucky Streak achievements.  
**Unclear:** hidden endings, vague thresholds, co-op state, wrong floor, inactive challenge, body part routes, item routes, push/refund outcomes.  
**Working route issue:** you did the right-looking thing, but not in the exact state the game wanted.

For ending tests, preserve earlier route states and avoid relying only on a final-moment reload. Use <Link href="/gamble-with-your-friends/save-scum-guide">Save Scum Guide</Link> if you are testing ending states.

<h2 id="push-refund-results">
  Push, refund, and win confusion
</h2>

A push or refund can protect your bank, but it may not count as a win.

This matters for streak achievements and “win X games” challenges. If the achievement text says **win**, do not count these unless the achievement actually fires:

- Blackjack pushes
- refunded bets
- returned end-of-day table bets
- partial protection from items
- “we did not lose money” outcomes

If the game gives your money back, that is useful. It is not automatically a win.

<h2 id="host-solo-coop">
  Host, solo, and co-op unlock problems
</h2>

Co-op makes achievement testing harder because the game may care about:

- who hosted
- who accepted the challenge
- who placed the bet
- who completed the condition
- whether another player changed the shared bank state

If a non-bugged achievement fails in co-op, use this retest order.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to retest a non-bugged achievement."
  columns={retestColumns}
  rows={retestRows}
/>

The cleanest co-op test is:

**one active player, one caller, everyone else waits.**

If four players are gambling at the same time, the result may be fun, but it is not a clean achievement test.

</h2>

If the achievement is one of the nine bugged achievements, the best move is usually to skip it and wait for a patch.

If another achievement appears broken, capture enough information for someone else to reproduce it.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Which achievements are bugged in Gamble With Your Friends?

The currently reported bugged achievements are Big Spender 2, Big Spender 3, Big Spender 4, High Roller 2, High Roller 3, High Roller 4, Lucky Streak 2, Lucky Streak 3, and Lucky Streak 4.

### How many achievements are bugged?

Current launch-window reports point to nine bugged achievements.

### Are the Floor 1 versions bugged?

No. The Floor 1 versions — Big Spender, High Roller, and Lucky Streak — are the versions you should complete normally.

### Should I skip the Floor 2–4 Big Spender, High Roller, and Lucky Streak achievements?

Yes. Skip all nine Floor 2–4 Big Spender, High Roller, and Lucky Streak variants until a patch confirms they work.

### Why did my achievement not unlock even though I did the action?

The matching Lone Shark challenge may not have been active, the wrong player may have completed the condition, the result may have been a push or refund, or the attempt may have happened on the wrong floor.

### Do I need the Lone Shark challenge active?

For many game-specific achievements, yes. If the matching challenge is not active first, the action may not count.

### Do pushes or refunds count as wins?

Do not assume they count. If an achievement says win, treat pushes and refunds as unconfirmed unless the achievement actually unlocks.

### Should I test achievements solo or co-op?

If an achievement fails in co-op, retest as host or solo. Co-op adds shared-bank noise, timing changes, and possible actor/host issues.

### Are hidden ending achievements bugged?

Not automatically. Hidden ending achievements need confirmed routes. A failed guessed route does not prove the achievement is broken.

### Can body parts, tickets, or items affect achievement testing?

Yes. Selling body parts, buying items, recovering parts, or spending tickets can change the route context. If the unlock fails, record what changed before calling it bugged.

### Where should I go for the full achievement route?

Use the main <Link href="/gamble-with-your-friends/achievements">Achievements Guide</Link> for the full achievement list and normal route.
