<h2 id="quota-day-plan">
  The quota day plan
</h2>

Quota runs are not won by gambling harder. They are won by changing your decision based on the timer, the remaining gap, and the state of the shared bank.

Before anyone touches a table, the team should answer three questions:

1. **How much do we still need?**
2. **How much time is left?**
3. **Who is allowed to take risk today?**

If nobody can answer those questions, nobody should be placing the first bet.

  caption="Gamble With Your Friends quota day phases."

This table is the whole guide in one place. If your team only follows one rule, follow this:

**Do not play the same way at 4:30, 1:00, and after quota is already safe.**

<h2 id="when-to-switch">
  When to switch from safe play to emergency play
</h2>

The biggest practical mistake is switching to emergency games too early. Emergency games are useful, but only when the clock and the remaining gap justify them.

<GuideTable
  caption="When to play safe, push, or leave."
  columns={decisionColumns}
  rows={decisionRows}
/>

As a simple rule:

**If you are still missing more than 30% of quota with about 60 seconds left, stop pretending this is a normal day. Pick one emergency route.**

But if the gap is small, do not panic. The team does not need a heroic 35:1 hit to cover a tiny shortfall. Use a familiar table, finish the quota, and leave.

<h2 id="over-quota-penalty">
  Why going far over quota can be bad
</h2>

This is the part most beginner guides skip.

Many players report the same pattern: if the team finishes far above the daily quota, especially around **200%+ of the target**, the next day can feel brutally inflated. The game does not always let a huge lucky day become a permanent advantage. In practice, large over-quota finishes often feel like they push the next quota into a much harsher bracket.

The exact formula is still not confirmed. Do not treat any single multiplier as final. But the practical rule is clear:

**Huge over-quota profit is not always safety. Sometimes it is tomorrow’s debt.**

<h3 id="time-after-quota">
  Time after quota is also risk
</h3>

The danger is not only how much extra money you earn. It is how long the team stays in the casino after the day is already safe.

If quota is reached with a lot of time left, the shared bank is exposed to more unnecessary decisions:

- one more Roulette spin
- one more Blackjack hand
- one friend wandering into Slots
- one player trying to double the “extra” money
- one emergency item wasted on a non-emergency

A useful rule:

**If quota is safe and there is no planned route, every extra 30 seconds in the casino is another chance for the team to lose its plan.**

That is why leaving early is not passive. It is the strongest quota decision.

<h2 id="when-to-stop">
  When to stop gambling after hitting quota
</h2>

Once quota is safe, the default move is to leave.

Not “one more spin.”  
Not “one more Blackjack hand.”  
Not “let me double it and then we go.”

Leave.

The only good reasons to keep gambling after quota are specific ones:

- you are routing a money milestone
- you are chasing one planned achievement
- you need a higher-floor unlock or late-game setup
- you have an item-supported strategy that requires extra cash
- the team agreed before the day started that this was a push day

If none of those are true, more gambling is usually just a way to turn success into organ debt.

<h2 id="recovery-day-plan">
  How to play the day after missing quota
</h2>

A missed quota day should change the next day’s plan. Do not start the next run like nothing happened.

If the team lost money, tickets, body parts, or player mobility, the next day is a **recovery day**, not a normal greed day.

Use this recovery plan:

1. **Lower the risk ceiling.** Nobody opens with a max bet.
2. **Assign a caller immediately.** The team already proved it needs one.
3. **Use one safe earner first.** Build a base before approving any miracle play.
4. **Spend tickets on survival value.** Items that protect, multiply, or stabilize a bet matter more than cosmetics.
5. **Do not chase achievements.** A recovery day has one job: survive.
6. **Leave as soon as quota is safe.** Do not try to “make back” the failed day.

The biggest mistake after missing quota is emotional recovery gambling. The team tries to win back pride, not just money, and that usually creates another failed day.

<h2 id="quota-vs-achievements">
  Quota vs achievements
</h2>

Do not chase five goals in one casino day.

If today is a quota recovery day, recover quota.  
If today is an achievement day, pick one achievement.  
If today is a money milestone day, accept that the quota plan will be riskier.

The clean rule:

**One main goal per day.**

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is quota in Gamble With Your Friends?

Quota is the amount of money your team must bring back before the casino day ends. It is the main survival target for each workday.

### Does quota scale every day?

Yes. Quota gets harder as the run continues. Player reports also suggest that finishing far above quota can make the next day feel much harsher.

### Is there a confirmed quota formula?

Not yet. Treat any exact multiplier as unconfirmed unless you have controlled test data. The practical rule is to avoid unnecessary over-quota gambling.

### Is going over quota bad?

A little extra money can be useful. Going far over quota for no reason is dangerous because it may increase next-day pressure and also gives the team more chances to lose the bank.

### When should I switch to emergency games?

A useful rule is: if you are still missing more than 30% of quota with around 60 seconds left, choose one emergency route. If the gap is small, keep playing controlled bets.

### What should I do once quota is reached?

Leave by default. Keep gambling only if the team has a specific plan for achievements, money milestones, floor progression, or item-supported late-game routing.

### What is the safest game for quota?

Blackjack is one of the safest options if one player understands the table. Familiar low-risk games are better than theoretically strong games nobody knows how to play.

### What is the best emergency quota game?

Slots, single-number Roulette, or one item-supported big bet can work as emergency options. They are not normal quota routes.

### How do I stop teammates from wasting money?

Assign a caller, ban silent all-ins, and make the leave decision before the day starts. The shared bank needs rules.

### Should I use tickets to recover quota?

Yes, if the run needs help. Tickets and items should create survival value during serious quota attempts.

### Should I sell body parts for tickets?

Only as an emergency or punishment route. If body parts are part of the plan, something already went wrong.

### Should I chase achievements while doing quota?

Only if the achievement fits the day. If quota is unsafe, stop achievement hunting and survive first.
