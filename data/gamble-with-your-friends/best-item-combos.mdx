<h2 id="taser-golden-chip">
  Taser + Golden Chip
</h2>

This is the cleanest item strategy because both items have obvious jobs.

- **Taser** is used on a keypad to increase the maximum bet.
- **Golden Chip** gives a single free all-in.

Use Taser first. Use Golden Chip second.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to use Taser + Golden Chip."
  columns={executionColumns}
  rows={taserChipRows}
/>

Use this strategy on:

- Roulette
- Blackjack
- Street Craps
- Wheel games
- any table where a higher max bet makes the free all-in worth more

Avoid it when:

- nobody understands the table
- the team has not picked the bet
- the table does not support a meaningful max-bet increase
- people plan to keep gambling normally after the item play

<GuideCallout title="Order matters">
Taser first. Golden Chip second. If you use Golden Chip before raising the max bet, you waste the strongest part of the combo.
</GuideCallout>

This is not a normal gambling combo. It is a quota tool.

Use Quota Gun when:

- the team is short on quota

- the target player is holding an important item

<h2 id="reel-combos">
  Angel’s Reel and Devil’s Reel
</h2>

Angel’s Reel and Devil’s Reel are both 3-ticket result tools.

- **Angel’s Reel** gives a chance to revert your last loss.
- **Devil’s Reel** can triple or lose your last win.

Use **Angel’s Reel** after:

- a meaningful loss
- a failed medium or large bet
- a mistake that is worth trying to undo

Use **Devil’s Reel** after:

- a win that is large enough to matter
- the team agrees to risk the last win
- the next quota needs a snowball

Avoid both when:

- the last result was tiny
- the team is already safe
- the player is tilted
- nobody agrees on whether the last result is worth risking

<GuideCallout title="Reel rule">
Angel’s Reel is for trying to undo a bad result. Devil’s Reel is for gambling a good result. Do not use them the same way.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="stake-holder">
  Stake Holder + item-heavy day
</h2>

Stake Holder costs 6 tickets and increases the power of each item.

Do not buy it as your only plan. Buy it when the team already has useful items and wants to make one casino day revolve around item power.

Good Stake Holder days look like this:

- Stake Holder + Holy Statue + fast-repeat table
- Stake Holder + Microphone + grouped profit play
- Stake Holder + Taser + Golden Chip
- Stake Holder + reels when the team accepts risk

Bad Stake Holder days look like this:

- Stake Holder as the only item
- no chosen table
- no item holder roles
- everyone improvising after entering the casino

<GuideCallout title="Stake Holder rule">
Stake Holder is an amplifier. It makes a real item plan better; it does not create a plan by itself.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="recommended-combo-route">
  In-casino item route
</h2>

Once the casino day starts, use this route:

1. **Call the target table.** Do not activate items while people are still choosing games.
2. **Move the item holder into position.** Taser goes to the keypad, Holy Statue goes to the group, Microphone goes near the bettors.
3. **Confirm the main bettor.** Do not let three players place serious bets at once.
4. **Use setup items before payoff items.** Taser before Golden Chip, grouping before Holy Statue, table choice before Drink.
5. **Make the planned play.** One clean combo is better than five random item activations.
6. **Stop when the item window ends.** Do not turn a successful item play into normal gambling.
7. **Save emergency tools for emergencies.** Time Machine and Quota Gun should fix real problems, not tiny mistakes.

This keeps items from becoming random ticket spam.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best item combo in Gamble With Your Friends?

The best clean combo is **Taser + Golden Chip**. Use Taser first to increase maximum bet, then use Golden Chip for a larger free all-in.

### What should I pair Golden Chip with?

Pair Golden Chip with Taser, profit items, or a table where a free all-in has meaningful value.

### What should I pair Holy Statue with?

Pair Holy Statue with fast-repeat betting setups such as slots, wheel games, roulette, or clustered table plays.

### Can you buy Time Machine?

In the available run material, Time Machine appears as a random / obtained item rather than a confirmed normal shop purchase. Do not plan your ticket spending around it unless you actually get one.

### Is Time Machine good?

Yes. Time Machine is best when it protects a meaningful mistake, such as a failed big bet, bad cash-out, or late-floor table mistake.

### What is the best profit strategy?

Use Drink on the main bettor, Microphone when players are grouped, Camera when one player is expected to win, and Bonus Draw when the team wants ticket value from profit.

### Is Drink + Insurance + Street Craps good?

Yes, if the roller understands Street Craps. Drink raises the upside, and Insurance softens the downside, but the combo is risky if the player loses control.

### Is Quota Gun a combo item?

Quota Gun is an emergency quota strategy. It pays 33% of quota for each body part it shoots off, but the penalty can affect the next day.

### Are Angel’s Reel and Devil’s Reel worth buying?

They are cheap and useful when the last result matters. Angel’s Reel is for a meaningful loss. Devil’s Reel is for a meaningful win that the team agrees to risk.

### Should I buy Stake Holder first?

Usually no. Stake Holder is best when the team already has multiple useful items and a clear item-heavy plan.

### What is the biggest item combo mistake?

The biggest mistake is using items out of order, especially spending Golden Chip before Taser or activating Holy Statue before players are grouped.
