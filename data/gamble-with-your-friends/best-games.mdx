<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best beginner games in Gamble With Your Friends are Roulette, Blackjack, Slot Machine with item support, Street Craps if your group understands it, and Duck Race only if the team can stop spamming it.**

For most groups:

1. **Roulette** is the cleanest fast quota game.
2. **Blackjack** is the best controlled game if one player knows basic decisions.
3. **Slot Machine** becomes much better with Holy Statue, Bonus Draw, or small-bet jackpot goals.
4. **Street Craps** has strong cheese potential, but it needs its own guide.
5. **Duck Race** is easy to understand but easy to overplay.
6. **Wheel of Fortune / Money Wheel** is for emergency multiplier attempts, not stable bankroll building.
7. **Crash and HiLo** are later-floor risk games: learn the cash-out or risk-ladder rules before betting seriously.
8. **Dragon Tower, 1P Poker, and Mine Sweeper** should not be blind max-bet games.

The most important rule:

**Do not max bet a game you do not understand yet.**

<h2 id="roulette">
  Roulette
</h2>

Roulette is the cleanest beginner game because the decision is simple: pick a color, number, or betting zone, then accept the result.

Use Roulette when:

- the team needs a fast quota push
- you have Golden Chip ready
- you are using Taser to increase the max bet
- the group agrees on a single planned swing
- you need a simple table for a new player

Avoid Roulette when:

- someone is using Martingale-style doubling without a hard stop
- the group is chasing losses
- several people are placing conflicting bets with shared money

<h2 id="duck-race">
  Duck Race
</h2>

Duck Race is one of the easiest games to understand. Pick a duck, watch it race, and hope your duck wins.

That makes it a good teaching game, but a risky bankroll game.

Use Duck Race when:

- the group is learning shared-bank pressure
- the bet size is small
- the team sets a hard attempt limit
- a challenge or achievement points you there

Avoid Duck Race when:

- players are emotionally attached to one duck
- the team keeps saying “one more”
- the bankroll is already low
- someone is using shared money to chase personal losses

<h2 id="crash">
  Crash
</h2>

Crash is a later-floor risk game built around **cash-out timing**. The basic idea is usually simple: the value rises, but waiting too long can erase the bet. The skill is not “always wait for the biggest multiplier.” The skill is deciding your cash-out target before greed takes over.

Use Crash when:

- the player can cash out early
- the team agrees on a target multiplier
- you are not relying on one huge late cash-out
- the bankroll can survive a failed attempt

Avoid Crash when:

- the team is already tilted
- the player keeps saying “just a little longer”
- the quota needs reliable progress
- no one understands when the round ends

---

<h2 id="hilo">
  HiLo
</h2>

HiLo is a risk-ladder game. You are usually deciding whether the next result will be higher or lower, then choosing whether to keep going or stop with the current value. The danger is that every correct step makes the next step feel tempting. A good HiLo player stops after the planned profit window instead of treating every win as permission to push again.

Use HiLo when:

- the bet size is controlled
- the player understands the risk ladder
- the team has a clear stop point
- you are testing later-floor game rules

Avoid HiLo when:

- you are trying to force one more correct call
- the team needs stable quota progress
- the player is guessing without a plan
- the bankroll cannot handle a full loss

<h2 id="games-to-avoid-blind">
  Games to avoid blind max betting
</h2>

Avoid serious blind bets on:

- **Crash**
- **HiLo**
- **Plinko**
- **Dragon Tower**
- **1P Poker**
- **Mine Sweeper**
- **Keno**

This does not mean they are bad. It means they are bad **first-contact games**.

A first-contact game is any table where the player is still asking:

- what do I press?
- what does this number mean?
- how do I cash out?
- what counts as a win?
- why did I just lose?

If those questions are still happening, the bet should be tiny.

<h2 id="recommended-route">
  Recommended decision route
</h2>

Use this route to decide what to play, not just where to click first.

First, classify the available pool:

- **Simple pool:** mostly Floor 1-style games such as Roulette, Blackjack, Duck Race, Slot Machine, Street Craps, or Wheel.
- **High-variance pool:** games with fast multipliers, cash-out pressure, ladder risk, or late-floor payouts, such as Crash, HiLo, Plinko, Dragon Tower, Money Wheel, or Penguin Cross.
- **Unknown pool:** any table where the player still does not know how to win, cash out, stop, or lose.

Use this route:

1. **Before the floor starts:** check whether you have Golden Chip, Taser, Holy Statue, profit items, or Time Machine.
2. **When you enter:** compare the available games with the <a href="#floor-game-map">floor game map</a>.
3. **If the pool is simple:** use Roulette, Blackjack, or another controlled Floor 1-style table.
4. **If the pool is high-variance:** use one planned item-backed push instead of repeated blind bets.
5. **If the pool is unknown:** minimum bet once and learn the fail condition.
6. **If the team is under time pressure:** choose a fast game only if everyone agrees on the risk.
7. **After unlocking a new floor:** treat every new game as untested until the table rules are clear.

This route keeps the team from learning the most expensive tables with the biggest possible bet.

### What is the best game in Gamble With Your Friends?

For beginners, Roulette is the cleanest fast game, Blackjack is the best controlled game if someone knows basic decisions, and Slot Machine becomes better with item support. Street Craps may be stronger for skilled groups, but it needs more setup.

### What is the safest Floor 1 game?

Roulette is the easiest to understand. Blackjack can be safer if one player knows how to play. Duck Race is simple, but easy to spam.

### Is Duck Race good?

Duck Race is good for learning and simple bets, but it is dangerous for undisciplined groups because players can repeat bets quickly and drain shared money.

### Is Blackjack good?

Yes, if one player knows basic card decisions. It becomes risky when the team forces streak challenges or panics under the timer.

### Is Street Craps worth playing?

Yes, but not as a blind beginner table. Street Craps has strong strategy and cheese potential, but the group needs to understand the roll flow.

### Is Crash good?

Crash can be good if the player can cash out early and follow a target multiplier. It is bad when the player waits too long because they want one bigger payout.

### Is HiLo good?

HiLo can be useful for controlled later-floor testing, but it becomes dangerous when players keep climbing the risk ladder after they already have a good result.

### Is Roulette better than Duck Race?

Roulette is usually better for a planned quota push because the bet structure is clearer. Duck Race is easier to spam, which makes it riskier for chaotic groups.

### What game should I use Golden Chip on?

Use Golden Chip on a table with a meaningful all-in opportunity, especially after checking whether Taser can increase the maximum bet.

### What game works best with Holy Statue?

Holy Statue works best near fast-repeat betting setups, such as slots or clustered table plays, because it prevents nearby loss while active.

### What games should beginners avoid blind max betting?

Beginners should avoid serious blind bets on Crash, HiLo, Plinko, Dragon Tower, 1P Poker, Mine Sweeper, and Keno until the table rules are clear.
