To care for animals in **Solarpunk**, build the matching shelter, place a **Feeding Trough** and **Water Trough**, add feed and water, then lure the animal home with its preferred crop.

**Chickens** follow **Wheat** and produce **Eggs**. **Pigs** follow **Carrot** and produce **Truffles**. **Sheep** follow **Corn** and produce **Sheep's Milk**. All three animal types grow up in **10 minutes**.

<h2 id="solarpunk-animals-guide">
  Solarpunk animals guide
</h2>

Animals in **Solarpunk** are not just decoration. They become part of your progression once you can feed them, water them, give them shelter, and collect their products.

The basic loop is simple:

<strong>Unlock the animal system, build the right shelter, place troughs, lure the animal home, then keep feed and water stable.</strong>

If your crops are still unstable, fix that first. Animals consume crop-based feed and lure items, so a weak farm turns into a weak animal setup. For crop planning, Wheat, seeds, and food priorities, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/farming-guide">Solarpunk Farming Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="pigs-truffles">
  Pigs and truffles
</h2>

Pigs use **Carrot** as their lure crop and produce **Truffles** at the Pig Shelter.

Do not rush pigs before you have a stable crop base. Carrots are also part of later farming and food planning, so keep enough seeds before using Carrots only as lure items.

A good pig setup needs:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Pig Shelter,</li>
  <li>Feeding Trough,</li>
  <li>Water Trough,</li>
  <li>stable Animal Feed,</li>
  <li>and Carrots saved for luring or future pig routes.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="sheep-milk">
  Sheep and Sheep's Milk
</h2>

Sheep use **Corn** as their lure crop and produce **Sheep's Milk** at the Sheep Shelter.

Sheep are easier to handle once your farm can support Corn without blocking other recipes. The Sheep Shelter also uses **Wheat x5**, so do not spend every Wheat stockpile before building it.

Before adding sheep, check:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you have Corn for luring,</li>
  <li>you have Wheat for the Sheep Shelter recipe,</li>
  <li>your feed supply is stable,</li>
  <li>and you have enough space for another animal type.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="powered-animal-care">
  Powered animal care and Chicken Incubator
</h2>

Powered animal care is useful once your energy network is ready. Do not add powered animal buildings to a weak grid just because the recipes are unlocked.

<GuideTable
  caption="Solarpunk powered animal care buildings."
  columns={automationColumns}
  rows={automationRows}
/>

The **Automatic Feeder** and **Water Trough (Powered)** each use **50 power**. The **Chicken Incubator** uses **200 power** and hatches hatchable eggs in **1 minute**.

If your drills, dock, sprinklers, and animal machines all run from the same grid, check your network before adding more powered buildings. For power setup help, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/energy-system-guide">Energy System Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="animal-transport">
  Animal Transportation and Animal Receiving Station
</h2>

Animal transport matters once you start finding animals away from your main base or across islands.

<GuideTable
  caption="Solarpunk animal transport tools."
  columns={transportColumns}
  rows={transportRows}
/>

Use **Animal Transportation** to send animals between islands, and set up an **Animal Receiving Station** where you want them to arrive.

Do not use transport as a replacement for planning. You still need shelter, feed, water, and space at the destination.

<h2 id="common-animal-problems">
  Common animal problems
</h2>

Most animal problems come from one missing part of the care loop: wrong lure crop, missing shelter, empty trough, no water, poor spacing, or not enough crop production for feed.

<GuideTable
  caption="Common animal problems and fixes in Solarpunk."

When in doubt, check the basics in this order:

  <li>Is the animal close enough to use it?</li>
  <li>Is the Feeding Trough filled?</li>
  <li>Is the Water Trough filled?</li>
  <li>Are you using the correct lure crop?</li>
  <li>Do you have enough crops to keep making feed?</li>
  <li>Is your energy network stable if you are using powered animal buildings?</li>
</ol>

Use this route if you want to avoid wasting crops or building the wrong animal setup.

<h2 id="faq">
  Solarpunk animals FAQ
</h2>

### How do animals work in Solarpunk?

Build the matching shelter, place a Feeding Trough and Water Trough nearby, add feed and water, then lure the animal home with its preferred crop. Once the animal is cared for and grown, it can produce its animal product.

### What animals are in Solarpunk?

The main animal types covered here are chickens, pigs, and sheep. Chickens produce Eggs, pigs produce Truffles, and sheep produce Sheep's Milk.

### What do chickens follow?

Chickens follow Wheat. Wheat is also used for basic Animal Feed, so keep your Wheat farm stable before scaling chickens.

### What do pigs follow?

Pigs follow Carrot and use the Pig Shelter. They produce Truffles.

### What do sheep follow?

Sheep follow Corn and use the Sheep Shelter. They produce Sheep's Milk.

### How long do animals take to grow up?

Chickens, pigs, and sheep each grow up in 10 minutes.

### Why can I not interact with animals?

You may not have the right animal unlock yet. Start with Animal Basics Pack, then unlock the matching animal shelter and bring the correct lure crop.

### Why is my animal not producing?

Check shelter, feed, water, spacing, and crop supply. A shelter alone is not enough if the Feeding Trough or Water Trough is empty.

### How much water does the Water Trough hold?

The basic Water Trough holds 100 water. It can fill from rain or be filled with a watercan.

### What does Animal Feed cost?

Basic Animal Feed uses Wheat x2 at the Crafting Table. Better Animal Feed recipes produce Animal Feed x3 with Sunflower plus another crop.

### Are powered animal buildings worth it?

Yes, once your energy grid is stable. Automatic Feeder and Water Trough (Powered) each use 50 power, while the Chicken Incubator uses 200 power.

### What does the Chicken Incubator do?

The Chicken Incubator hatches hatchable eggs in 1 minute, but it uses 200 power, so do not add it before your energy setup can handle the load.

### How do I move animals between islands?

Use Animal Transportation and the Animal Receiving Station after unlocking Animal Transport. Make sure the destination has shelter, feed, and water ready.

### Should I add pigs and sheep right after chickens?

Not immediately. Add pigs once Carrots are stable, and add sheep once Corn and Wheat are stable. Expanding animals too early can drain feed and crops.
