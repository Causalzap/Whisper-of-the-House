<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Solarpunk**, your first island goal is to secure **food**, **clean water**, **cloth**, **storage**, **sleep**, and **research** before chasing the airship. Start with berries and cotton, craft basic tools, plant your first crops, craft the **Survival Guide** from **Quick Crafting**, build a **Research Table**, make a **Rain Collector**, place a **Bed**, then work toward **Beehives** and the **Airship Dock**.

A good first-session target is: **Research Table + Rain Collector + Bed + cotton growing**. Before your first flight, add storage, beehives, saved iron, cloth, and the **Crashed Airship Component**. Do not rush iron tools or huge buildings before the first island is stable.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="solarpunk-beginner-guide">
  Solarpunk beginner guide
</h2>

**Solarpunk** looks relaxed, but your first island still has a clear order of operations. If you build randomly, you can end up short on water, cloth, storage, iron, or food right when the game asks you to push toward the airship.

The safest beginner route is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Choose Standard or Soft.</li>
  <li>Collect berries, sticks, stones, wood, and cotton.</li>
  <li>Craft your first tools.</li>
  <li>Plant berries and cotton.</li>
  <li>Craft and read the Survival Guide.</li>
  <li>Build the Research Table.</li>
  <li>Make clean water, storage, and a bed.</li>
  <li>Add beehives and save iron for the airship route.</li>
</ol>

That route gives you a stable base before the game starts asking for Traderbot trades, copper, wheat, silicon, eggs, and energy management.

If you already built your airship, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/airship-guide">Airship Guide</Link>. If your machines keep turning off, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/energy-system-guide">Energy System Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="standard-vs-soft-mode">
  Standard vs Soft mode
</h2>

Before you land on your island, choose the mode that matches how you want to play.

<GuideTable
  caption="Solarpunk Standard vs Soft mode."
  columns={modeColumns}
  rows={modeRows}
/>

Standard is the better pick if you want the full survival loop. Soft is better if your main goal is to build, decorate, farm, and explore without being punished as much for early mistakes.

If you are playing mainly for cozy building, there is no shame in Soft. If you want your choices to matter more, pick Standard.

Instead of trying to finish everything at once, use three simple checkpoints.

This gives you a realistic pace. Your first session does not need to end with a fully upgraded airship. It should end with a starter island that can support the next step.

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">

<h3 id="thunderstorm-safety">
  Thunderstorm safety
</h3>

Thunderstorms matter more than normal rain. On Standard mode, lightning can hurt you. Fully grown plants can also be damaged if they are exposed when a storm hits.

Early on, the simple answer is to harvest mature crops before bad weather and take shelter when lightning starts. Later, use greenhouse-style protection for crops that matter to progression, especially wheat, carrots, and sunflowers.

For the full crop protection route, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/farming-guide">Farming Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  Solarpunk beginner FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Solarpunk?

Collect berries, sticks, stones, wood, and cotton. Then craft basic tools, plant berries and cotton, craft the Survival Guide from Quick Crafting, build a Research Table, make a Rain Collector, and set up a bed.

### Should I pick Standard or Soft mode?

Pick Standard if you want the intended survival rules. Pick Soft if you want a more relaxed building and farming experience with lower hunger / thirst pressure, no inventory drop on death, and no lightning damage.

### What should I finish in my first session?

A good first-session goal is Research Table, Rain Collector, Bed, basic storage, and cotton growing. That is enough to make the starter island feel stable.

### Where do I get the Survival Guide?

Craft the Survival Guide from the Quick Crafting menu after collecting basic starter materials. Read it early because it explains clean water, thunderstorms, crop safety, stairs, and airship basics.

### What is the best first crop?

Berries are the safest first food crop, but cotton is the most important early progression crop because it becomes cloth.

### Why is cotton important?

Cotton becomes cloth. Cloth is needed for early research, crafting, bed progress, trades, and later progression steps.

### How do I get clean water early?

Build a Rain Collector. Rainwater is the safest early clean water source, and it also helps with refilling water tools.

### Should I make iron tools early?

Usually no. Basic stone tools are cheaper, and early iron is better saved for the first airship, repairs, dock progress, machines, and energy systems.

### I can't find enough iron. Do I need to explore?

Not always. Look for large ore patches embedded in the ground. These patches are more important than loose rocks and can help before your first airship.

### What should I research first?

Prioritize practical unlocks: Rain Collector, Chest, Bed, Beehive, and the Airship Dock path. Delay decorative unlocks until your base is stable.

### Do thunderstorms damage crops?

Fully grown plants can be damaged by thunderstorms if they are exposed. Harvest mature crops before storms when possible, and use greenhouse protection later for important crops.

### When should I build the Airship Dock?

Build the Airship Dock after your first island has food, water, storage, cloth production, saved iron, and the Crashed Airship Component. Do not fly out underprepared.

### Are beehives worth building early?

Yes. Beehives produce beeswax, which supports Traderbot progression and wax briquettes later.
