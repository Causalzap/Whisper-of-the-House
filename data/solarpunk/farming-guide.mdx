<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Solarpunk**, start farming with **berries** for food and **cotton** for cloth. After the first airship range upgrade, fly to the **east and west islands in the second ring** to find **Wheat**, then bring both Wheat and seeds home.

Do not spend all Wheat on animal feed right after unlocking **Animal Basics**. First cover the major wheat gates: **5 Wheat for Animal Basics**, **10 Wheat for Traderbot**, and **30 Wheat for 10 Bread**. After those gates are safe, feed chickens seriously, keep feed / water / shelter stable, and save your first **6 Eggs** for the next airship upgrade.

<h2 id="solarpunk-farming-guide">
  Solarpunk farming guide
</h2>

Farming in **Solarpunk** is not just a food system. It controls cloth, animal feed, eggs, airship upgrades, Traderbot trades, cooking, and later animal progression. If you treat every crop as food, you can accidentally eat or cook the exact item that blocks your next unlock.

The most important beginner farming rule is simple:

<strong>Grow for progression first, eat for comfort second.</strong>

That means berries and watermelons can keep you alive, but cotton, wheat, eggs, carrots, and sunflowers should be protected until you know what the next trade or research gate needs.

If you are still building the first island basics, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/beginners-guide">Solarpunk Beginner Guide</Link>. If you have not reached Wheat yet, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/airship-guide">Airship Guide</Link> first.

</h2>

Your first farm should be simple. Do not overbuild a giant field before you have water, storage, and a clear resource route.

  </figcaption>

A good starter farm is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Plant berry seeds for food.</li>
  <li>Plant cotton seeds for cloth.</li>
  <li>Water them manually at first.</li>
  <li>Replant every seed until you have a surplus.</li>
  <li>Build storage so food, seeds, and cloth do not get mixed together.</li>
</ol>

Cotton deserves special attention. Cloth appears in early research, crafting, bed progress, trades, and later unlocks. Do not stop growing it after one craft.

<h2 id="wheat">
  Where to find Wheat in Solarpunk
</h2>

Wheat becomes available after your first major airship range upgrade. Once the white travel circle expands, check both sides of the second ring.

Wheat appears on islands to the **east and west** after the first range upgrade. Check both sides of the second ring: one side has a group of smaller islands, and the other side has a larger island. Bring home both Wheat and seeds.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/solarpunk/solarpunk-wheat-quartz-islands-after-upgrade.webp"
    alt="Solarpunk second-ring island where wheat can be found after the first airship upgrade"
    width={1600}

    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    After the first airship range upgrade, search both east and west sides of the second ring for Wheat. Do not leave without seeds.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

When you find Wheat:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Harvest the Wheat.</li>
  <li>Pick up every seed you can.</li>
  <li>Check nearby ledges and paths before leaving.</li>
  <li>Return home and plant Wheat immediately.</li>
  <li>Keep expanding Wheat until the Animal Basics and Traderbot gates are covered.</li>
</ol>

Do not turn your first Wheat into animal feed immediately. Wheat has too many progression jobs.

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

If a chicken does not follow perfectly, do not panic. Check whether it is already moving toward the animal area, whether you are still holding Wheat, and whether the route is blocked.

<h2 id="food-and-cooking">
  When it is safe to start cooking
</h2>

Cooking is useful, but it can also quietly destroy your next unlock if you cook the wrong ingredient too early.

The main danger ingredients are **Wheat** and **Eggs**. Wheat has to cover Animal Basics, Traderbot, bread, and feed. Eggs have to cover the airship upgrade. Once those gates are done, cooking becomes much safer.

<h2 id="greenhouse-thunderstorms">
  Greenhouse and thunderstorm crop safety
</h2>

Thunderstorms can damage fully grown exposed plants. That is annoying when the crop is only food, but painful when the crop is Wheat, carrot, sunflower, or another progression item.

Early on:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>harvest mature crops before bad weather when possible,</li>
  <li>keep extra seeds,</li>
  <li>do not leave every rare crop sitting fully grown outside,</li>
  <li>and use protected growing space once your build route allows it.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">
  Solarpunk farming FAQ
</h2>

### What should I grow first in Solarpunk?

Grow berries and cotton first. Berries cover early food, while cotton becomes cloth for crafting, research, bed progress, and trades.

### Where do I find Wheat in Solarpunk?

After the first airship range upgrade, check the east and west islands in the second ring. One side has a group of smaller islands, and the other side has a larger island. Bring home Wheat and seeds.

### How much Wheat do I need before chickens?

A safe early target is 45+ Wheat: 5 Wheat for Animal Basics, 10 Wheat for Traderbot, and 30 Wheat to make the Dough needed for 10 Bread.

### Why are my chickens not producing eggs?

Check progression order first. If chicken hunger refuses to reach 10/10, stop wasting animal feed and finish the 10 Wheat and 10 Bread Traderbot gates. After that, check feed, water, shelter, spacing, and happiness.

### Should I feed chickens right after unlocking Animal Basics?

No. Unlock Animal Basics, but do not spend all your Wheat on animal feed immediately. Finish the 10 Wheat and 10 Bread Traderbot gates first, then start serious chicken feeding.

### How do I lead chickens home?

Hold Wheat in your hand and walk the chicken toward your animal area. Keep Wheat equipped until the chicken reaches the trough and coop area.

### How do I make Bread?

Make Dough with Wheat, then put Dough into a furnace to make Bread. For 10 Bread, plan around 30 Wheat.

### What should I do with my first eggs?

Save the first six eggs for the airship upgrade. Do not cook them until the upgrade trade is complete.

### When is it safe to start cooking Wheat and eggs?

Start cooking Wheat after Animal Basics, the 10 Wheat trade, and the 10 Bread trade are covered. Start cooking eggs after the six-egg airship upgrade is done and your chickens produce reliably.

### Are sprinklers worth it?

Yes, once your farm grows beyond a few manual plots. A compact sprinkler block saves time, but check your power grid because sprinklers can drain energy.

### How many plots can one sprinkler cover?

A compact early layout can cover eight nearby plots. Keep the layout tight before adding more sprinklers.

### Do thunderstorms damage crops?

Fully grown exposed plants can be damaged by thunderstorms. Harvest important mature crops before storms when possible, and protect progression crops later with greenhouse-style growing space.

### How do I get more carrot and sunflower seeds?

Use seed packs after you unlock them. They are best used on rare or later crops such as carrots and sunflowers, especially before cooking or spending those crops.

### Should I use seed packs on Wheat?

Use seed packs on Wheat if animal feed is your bottleneck. Otherwise, save seed packs for rarer crops like carrots and sunflowers.

### When should I add pigs and other animals?

Add more animals only after your chicken setup is stable. If Wheat, feed, water, or shelter is already strained, more animals will make the farm harder to manage.
