<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Solarpunk**, start farming with **berries** for food and **cotton** for cloth. After the first airship range upgrade, fly to the **east and west islands in the second ring** to find **Wheat**, then bring both Wheat and seeds home.

Do not spend all Wheat on **Animal Feed** right after unlocking **Animal Basics**. First cover the major Wheat gates: **5 Wheat for Animal Basics**, **10 Wheat for Traderbot**, and **30 Wheat for 10 Bread**. After those gates are safe, move into animals, keep your crop supply stable, and save your first **6 Eggs** for the next airship upgrade.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="solarpunk-farming-guide">
  Solarpunk farming guide
</h2>

Farming in **Solarpunk** is not just a food system. It controls cloth, Animal Feed, eggs, airship upgrades, Traderbot trades, cooking, and later animal progression. If you treat every crop as food, you can accidentally eat or cook the exact item that blocks your next unlock.

The most important beginner farming rule is simple:

<strong>Grow for progression first, eat for comfort second.</strong>

That means berries and watermelons can keep you alive, but cotton, Wheat, eggs, Carrots, Corn, and Sunflowers should be protected until you know what the next trade, research gate, or animal route needs.

If you are still building the first island basics, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/beginners-guide">Solarpunk Beginner Guide</Link>. If you have not reached Wheat yet, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/airship-guide">Airship Guide</Link> first.

<h2 id="wheat">
  Where to find Wheat in Solarpunk
</h2>

Wheat becomes available after your first major airship range upgrade. Once the white travel circle expands, check both sides of the second ring.

Wheat appears on islands to the **east and west** after the first range upgrade. Check both sides of the second ring: one side has a group of smaller islands, and the other side has a larger island. Bring home both Wheat and seeds.

<h2 id="greenhouse-thunderstorms">
  Greenhouse and thunderstorm crop safety
</h2>

Thunderstorms can damage fully grown exposed plants. That is annoying when the crop is only food, but painful when the crop is Wheat, Carrot, Corn, Sunflower, or another progression item.

Early on:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>harvest mature crops before bad weather when possible,</li>
  <li>keep extra seeds,</li>
  <li>do not leave every rare crop sitting fully grown outside,</li>
  <li>and use protected growing space once your build route allows it.</li>
</ul>

Greenhouse-style protection matters most for crops that block progression. If you only have limited protected space, prioritize Wheat, Carrots, Corn, Sunflowers, and anything you are currently using for animals or Traderbot.

<h2 id="later-animal-crops">
  Save later crops for animals
</h2>

After chickens, later animals start depending on different crops. Save **Carrots** for pigs and **Corn** for sheep before you treat those crops as normal food or cooking ingredients.

This is why seed expansion matters: animals do not only need shelters and troughs, they also need a crop economy behind them. If Carrot, Corn, Wheat, or Sunflower production is weak, Animal Feed and luring become harder than they need to be.

For pig, sheep, shelter, product, and transport details, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/animals-guide">Solarpunk Animals Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  Solarpunk farming FAQ
</h2>

### What should I grow first in Solarpunk?

Grow berries and cotton first. Berries cover early food, while cotton becomes cloth for crafting, research, bed progress, and trades.

### Where do I find Wheat in Solarpunk?

After the first airship range upgrade, check the east and west islands in the second ring. One side has a group of smaller islands, and the other side has a larger island. Bring home Wheat and seeds.

### How much Wheat do I need before animals?

A safe early target is 45+ Wheat: 5 Wheat for Animal Basics, 10 Wheat for Traderbot, and 30 Wheat to make the Dough needed for 10 Bread.

### How do I make Bread?

Make Dough with Wheat, then put Dough into a furnace to make Bread. For 10 Bread, plan around 30 Wheat.

### What should I do with my first eggs?

Save the first six eggs for the airship upgrade. Do not cook them until the upgrade trade is complete.

### When is it safe to start cooking Wheat and eggs?

Start cooking Wheat after Animal Basics, the 10 Wheat trade, and the 10 Bread trade are covered. Start cooking eggs after the six-egg airship upgrade is done and your animal setup produces reliably.

### Are sprinklers worth it?

Yes, once your farm grows beyond a few manual plots. A compact sprinkler block saves time, but check your power grid because sprinklers can drain energy.

### How many plots can one sprinkler cover?

A compact early layout can cover eight nearby plots. Keep the layout tight before adding more sprinklers.

### Which crops should I save for animals?

Save Wheat for chickens and Animal Feed, Carrot for pigs, and Corn for sheep. Do not spend all of these crops on cooking before your animal setup is stable.

### Where can I learn the full animal setup?

Use the <Link href="/solarpunk/animals-guide">Solarpunk Animals Guide</Link> for Chicken Coop, Pig Shelter, Sheep Shelter, Feeding Trough, Water Trough, lure crops, animal products, incubator, and animal transport.

### Do thunderstorms damage crops?

Fully grown exposed plants can be damaged by thunderstorms. Harvest important mature crops before storms when possible, and protect progression crops later with greenhouse-style growing space.

### How do I get more Carrot, Corn, and Sunflower seeds?

Use seed packs after you unlock them. They are best used on rare or later crops such as Carrots, Corn, and Sunflowers, especially before cooking or spending those crops.

### Should I use seed packs on Wheat?

Use seed packs on Wheat if Animal Feed is your bottleneck. Otherwise, save seed packs for rarer crops like Carrots, Corn, and Sunflowers.
