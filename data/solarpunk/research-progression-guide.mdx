<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Solarpunk**, progression is split between **Research Table tiers** and **Traderbot blueprint tiers**. For the Research Table, unlock **Tier 2** with **1 Build-Hammer + 1 Beeswax**, **Tier 3** with **1 Iron Bar**, **Tier 4** with **1 Copper**, **Tier 5** with **1 Wheat**, and **Tier 6** with **1 Sunflower**.

After Tier 2, research and craft the **Map** with **2 Cloth** so you can stop navigating only by eyesight. For Traderbot blueprints, take **Energy Workbench** early, use quartz to make Silicon, then usually pick **Battery before Animal Transport** if your base already uses solar panels, drills, sprinklers, or the Advanced Dock. 
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="solarpunk-research-progression-guide">
  Solarpunk research progression guide
</h2>

**Solarpunk** progression is easy to misread because there are two unlock systems running at the same time:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Research Table tiers</strong>, which unlock base, farming, map, airship, decor, animal, and cooking recipes.</li>
  <li><strong>Traderbot blueprint tiers</strong>, which unlock energy, electronics, transport, wind power, and logistics tools.</li>
</ol>

If you only follow one of them, your progression starts to feel broken. For example, you can unlock the Airship Dock research tier but still lack the Crashed Airship Component. You can unlock blueprint Tier 2 but pick Animal Transport while your power grid is still collapsing. You can find Wheat but spend it before Animal Basics, bread, or chickens are ready.

This guide is meant to answer one question: **what should you unlock next, and what resource is blocking it?**

If you are still setting up food and water, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/beginners-guide">Beginner Guide</Link>. If you need the first airship route, use the <Link href="/solarpunk/airship-guide">Airship Guide</Link>. If power is already your blocker, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/energy-system-guide">Energy System Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="tier-2-unlocks">
  Tier 2: Map, Furnace, and the first real navigation upgrade
</h2>

Tier 2 matters because it unlocks the **Map**. Before this, navigation is rough: you mostly judge nearby islands by sight and airship range.

<GuideTable
caption="Solarpunk Tier 2 unlock priorities."
columns={tier2Columns}
rows={tier2Rows}
/>

To unlock Map:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Unlock Research Table Tier 2 with 1 Build-Hammer and 1 Beeswax.</li>
  <li>Research Map at the Research Table.</li>
  <li>Craft it with 2 Cloth.</li>
  <li>Use it to read your island ring and plan airship trips.</li>
</ol>

This is why cotton matters so much. Cloth is not only for beds and the Airship Dock. It also gates your Map.

<h3 id="cotton-night-tip">
  Cotton tip: look at night
</h3>

If you cannot find enough cotton on your first island, check again at night. Cotton is easier to spot in the dark because it glows, which makes it stand out more than it does during the day.

That does not mean you should wait until night for all cotton gathering. It just gives you a useful backup method if you are missing one or two plants and need more Cloth for Bed, Map, or Airship Dock progress.

  Tier 3: Airship Dock and the island progression break

Tier 3 is where research stops being only about the starter island. It is the tier that pushes you toward the airship.

<h2 id="traderbot-trade-route">
  Traderbot trade route
</h2>

Traderbot trades are not random errands. They are the main route through blueprints, airship range upgrades, and later progression.

<GuideTable

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

  <li>Eggs expand airship range again.</li>
  <li>Cobalt helps push toward Windmill and drone progression.</li>

If you are stuck, check which trade or tier gate is next before flying around randomly.

<h2 id="battery-vs-animal-transport">
  Battery vs Animal Transport
</h2>

If your base already uses solar panels, drills, sprinklers, or the Advanced Dock, **Battery is usually the safer first Tier 2 blueprint pick**. Animal Transport is useful, but it does not fix a collapsing power grid.

For the full breakdown, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/energy-system-guide">Energy System Guide</Link>. For chickens, eggs, and feed order, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/farming-guide">Farming Guide</Link>.

Use this table when two unlocks look equally tempting.

<h2 id="tier-5-animal-basics">
  Tier 5 and Animal Basics
</h2>

Tier 5 asks for Wheat, and Animal Basics asks for more Wheat after that. This is where many players spend the right crop at the wrong time.

After you find Wheat:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Bring home Wheat and seeds.</li>
  <li>Plant Wheat immediately.</li>
  <li>Save enough Wheat to unlock Tier 5.</li>
  <li>Save 5 Wheat for Animal Basics.</li>
  <li>Still keep enough Wheat for Traderbot's 10 Wheat and 10 Bread gates.</li>
</ol>

Animal Basics is not the signal to dump all Wheat into animal feed. It is the signal to build a Wheat surplus before chickens become the egg route.

For the exact chicken feeding order, use the <Link href="/solarpunk/farming-guide">Farming Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="tier-6-growbox-cooking-seedspack">
  Tier 6: Growbox, Cooking, Seedspack, and better feed
</h2>

Tier 6 asks for Sunflower. That means you are no longer in the simple starter loop. You are now managing later crops, animals, cooking, and seed expansion.

Important Tier 6 priorities:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Growbox</strong> helps protect and control important crop production.</li>
  <li><strong>Cooking</strong> is useful after progression ingredients are safe.</li>
  <li><strong>Seedspack</strong> helps expand rare crop lines like carrot and sunflower.</li>
  <li><strong>Better Animal Feed</strong> matters once animals become a real system.</li>
  <li><strong>Pig Shelter</strong> becomes useful after your farm can support more animals.</li>
</ul>

Do not unlock cooking and immediately spend Wheat, eggs, carrots, or sunflowers without checking the next gate. Many of those ingredients still block research or trades.

  Solarpunk research progression FAQ
</h2>

### How do I unlock Tier 2 at the Research Table?

Unlock Tier 2 with 1 Build-Hammer and 1 Beeswax. Build beehives early so Beeswax does not delay your first major research tier.

### How do I unlock Tier 3 at the Research Table?

Unlock Tier 3 with 1 Iron Bar. Mine iron, process it into an Iron Bar, then use it at the Research Table.

### How do I unlock Tier 4, Tier 5, and Tier 6?

Tier 4 needs 1 Copper, Tier 5 needs 1 Wheat, and Tier 6 needs 1 Sunflower. These tier gates follow your exploration route.

### How do I unlock the Map in Solarpunk?

Unlock Research Table Tier 2, research Map, then craft it with 2 Cloth. Keep cotton growing because Cloth is needed for Map, Bed, Airship Dock, and other progression steps.

### Why can't I find cotton on my first island?

Cotton is easier to spot at night because it glows in the dark. Check your starter island again after sunset if you are short on cotton or need more Cloth.

### What should I research first?

Start with Rain Collector, Chest, Beehive, and Bed. These stabilize water, storage, Beeswax, and sleep before you chase Airship Dock or energy systems.

### Should I research Map early?

Yes. Map is one of the best Tier 2 unlocks because it gives you a proper island-ring view instead of relying only on eyesight.

### What should I unlock first with Traderbot blueprints?

Take Energy Workbench early. It opens solar panels, cables, Network Display, drills, switches, and the rest of the power route.

### Should I choose Battery or Animal Transport first?

Choose Battery first if your base already has power problems. Choose Animal Transport first only if your grid is stable and moving animals is your immediate blocker.

### How do I unlock Battery?

Push Traderbot blueprint progression to Tier 2. You need Silicon to open that tier, so find quartz after the first airship range upgrade and smelt it into Silicon.

### How do I unlock Windmill?

Windmill is part of later blueprint progression. Push toward Cobalt and blueprint Tier 3, then prioritize Windmill if your power grid is strained.

### I'm stuck and don't know what to do next in Solarpunk. What should I check?

Check the resource gate table first. Most stuck points are caused by one missing gate: Beeswax, Iron Bar, Copper, Map, Airship Dock, Silicon, Wheat, eggs, Cobalt, or Sunflower.

### When should I unlock Animal Basics?

Unlock Animal Basics after you have Wheat and can afford the Wheat cost without breaking Traderbot's 10 Wheat and 10 Bread route.
