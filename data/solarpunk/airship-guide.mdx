<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
To build your first **airship in Solarpunk**, progress through the starter island until you can collect the **Crashed Airship Component** from the wreck. Then unlock the **Airship Dock** at the Research Table with **5 Wood, 5 Iron, and 5 Cloth**.

After placing the dock, build the first airship from the dock panel with **10 Iron, 3 Cloth, and 1 Crashed Airship Component**. The first major range upgrade comes from Traderbot and needs **32 Copper**. If your map range did not expand after trading, return to your dock and click **Upgrade** — the trade gives you the item, but the dock applies it. 
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="solarpunk-airship-guide">
  Solarpunk airship guide
</h2>

The airship is the main progression gate in **Solarpunk**. It is not just a vehicle. It decides when you can reach Traderbot, copper, wheat, quartz, silicon, eggs, cobalt, pigs, and later Tier 3 unlocks.

The part that trips up many players is that the airship route does not start with copper or Traderbot. It starts with the **Crashed Airship Component** from the wreck. If you skip that component, the dock panel can look confusing even when you have enough iron and cloth.

Use this guide when you are stuck on any of these:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>where to get the Crashed Airship Component,</li>
  <li>how to unlock the Airship Dock,</li>
  <li>what the Airship Dock materials are,</li>
  <li>what the first airship materials are,</li>
  <li>why the airship range did not expand,</li>
  <li>where to go after the 32 copper upgrade,</li>
  <li>how to get wheat, quartz, silicon, and cobalt,</li>
  <li>or how co-op airships work.</li>
</ul>

If you are still setting up food, water, cotton, and the Research Table, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/beginners-guide">Solarpunk Beginner Guide</Link> first. If your Advanced Dock or powered machines keep shutting down, read the <Link href="/solarpunk/energy-system-guide">Energy System Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="find-crashed-airship-component">
  Find the Crashed Airship Component first
</h2>

Before worrying about Traderbot or copper, make sure you have the **Crashed Airship Component**. The game points you toward crashed airship parts during early progression, and the component is what lets the dock panel move from “almost ready” to actually building the first airship.

When you reach the wreck, interact with the airship part and collect the component.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/solarpunk/solarpunk-crashed-airship-component-wreck.webp"

    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    When the wreck is reachable, collect the component before spending time farming more airship materials.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

If the wreck sits above you or behind a height gap, use stairs. The Survival Guide confirms that stairs can be built with the Build Hammer and stacked, which is why they matter for high ledges and wreck routes.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/solarpunk/solarpunk-survival-guide-stairs-tip.webp"
    alt="Solarpunk Survival Guide explaining that stairs can be built with the Build Hammer and stacked"
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Stairs are not just for base building. Use them to reach high ledges, upper paths, and important wreck routes.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Do this before your first airship build:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Progress through the first island until the wreck route becomes relevant.</li>
  <li>Bring the Build Hammer and wood if you need stairs.</li>
  <li>Reach the crashed airship wreck.</li>
  <li>Collect the Crashed Airship Component.</li>
  <li>Return to the Airship Dock and finish the first airship build.</li>
</ol>

  <li><strong>10 Iron</strong></li>
  <li><strong>3 Cloth</strong></li>
  <li><strong>1 Crashed Airship Component</strong></li>
</ul>

The safe preparation rule is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>save iron instead of wasting it on early iron tools,</li>
  <li>keep growing cotton for cloth,</li>
  <li>do not burn all your wood as fuel,</li>
  <li>build beehives so wax briquettes can reduce wood fuel pressure,</li>
  <li>and collect the crashed component before trying to finish the build.</li>
</ul>

If you are missing iron, check for large ore patches embedded in the ground before assuming you must leave the island.

<h2 id="before-first-flight">
  Before your first flight
</h2>

Do not fly away with a full inventory and no tools. Your first airship trip should solve progression, not create more problems.

<GuideTable
caption="What to bring before your first real airship trip."
columns={prepColumns}
rows={prepRows}
/>

For the first serious flight, I would leave base with:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>food,</li>
  <li>clean water,</li>
  <li>two or more cheap pickaxes,</li>
  <li>mostly empty inventory,</li>
  <li>Build Hammer and wood,</li>
  <li>and repair materials if you have already unlocked repair kits.</li>
</ul>

You do not need airship fuel, but you do need inventory space. New islands often have crops, chests, ore, and loose materials that matter later.

A safe docking pattern is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">

  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>

Use this checklist:

<GuideTable
caption="Why your Solarpunk airship range is not expanding."

Your map shows a white range circle around your airship. If an island is outside that circle, you cannot reach it yet. Finish the next Traderbot upgrade milestone, return to your dock, and apply the upgrade.

The upgrade trade gives you the item. The dock applies it.

A good first target is the **northwest island group in the second ring** if it appears in your map layout. Look for pale quartz ore and wheat plants. Island layout can vary, so use the map ring and visible islands as your guide, not a fixed coordinate.

If Tier 2 is stuck, make sure you actually smelted quartz into silicon. Simply finding quartz is not enough.

<h2 id="advanced-dock-boost">
  Advanced Dock and boost
</h2>

The **Advanced Dock** is useful, but it is not a beginner priority. It needs power, so a weak base can become worse after you add it.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/solarpunk/solarpunk-advanced-dock-boost.webp"
    alt="Solarpunk Advanced Dock boost prompt while flying an airship"
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Boost is useful for long return trips, but it is dangerous near your dock because the airship keeps momentum.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Build Advanced Dock when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>your solar and battery setup is stable,</li>
  <li>you understand your Network Display,</li>
  <li>you are flying between islands often,</li>
  <li>and your base can afford another powered device.</li>
</ul>

Use boost in short bursts. Do not boost hard while already close to an island, dock, or cliff.

<h2 id="co-op-airship">
  Co-op airship behavior
</h2>

In co-op, only the player who built an airship can use it. Your partner needs their own.

The safest co-op rule is simple: **each player who wants independent airship travel should build their own Airship Dock and airship.**

Good co-op setup:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>One player rushes food, water, and cotton.</li>
  <li>One player gathers wood, iron, and cloth for dock progress.</li>
  <li>Each explorer builds their own airship when possible.</li>
  <li>Share crop and resource discoveries at the base.</li>
  <li>Do not send every player in a different direction until everyone understands range limits.</li>
</ol>

If you only have enough materials for one airship, let that player handle Traderbot and resource scouting first, then build the second airship as soon as possible.

<h2 id="recommended-airship-route">
  Recommended airship route
</h2>

After collecting the Crashed Airship Component and unlocking the dock route, follow this clean progression path:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Build and place the Airship Dock.</li>
  <li>Build your first airship with 10 Iron, 3 Cloth, and 1 Crashed Airship Component.</li>
  <li>Practice docking before carrying important resources.</li>
  <li>Fly to Traderbot and start the trade chain.</li>
  <li>Mine copper inside the first exploration ring.</li>
  <li>Trade 32 copper for the first airship upgrade.</li>
  <li>Return home and apply the upgrade at your dock.</li>
  <li>Search the next ring for wheat and quartz.</li>
  <li>Smelt quartz into silicon for Tier 2.</li>
  <li>Grow wheat, support chickens, and save six eggs.</li>
  <li>Trade eggs for the next airship upgrade.</li>
  <li>Push outward for carrots, sunflowers, pigs, and cobalt.</li>
  <li>Use cobalt to unlock Tier 3 and move toward Windmill / drones.</li>
</ol>

This route keeps every flight tied to a specific unlock instead of random island hopping.

<h2 id="faq">
  Solarpunk airship FAQ
</h2>

### How do I start the airship route in Solarpunk?

Collect the Crashed Airship Component from the airship wreck, then continue toward the Airship Dock at the Research Table.

### Where do I get the Crashed Airship Component?

You get the Crashed Airship Component from the crashed airship wreck during early progression. Interact with the wreck part, collect the component, then return to your dock route.

### How do I get Airship Parts in Solarpunk?

For the first airship, you need the Crashed Airship Component from the wreck. Collect it from the crashed airship, then build the first airship from the Airship Dock panel.

### What are the Airship Dock materials?

The Airship Dock research step uses 5 Wood, 5 Iron, and 5 Cloth. After researching it, build and place the dock near a clear edge.

### What are the first airship materials?

The first airship needs 10 Iron, 3 Cloth, and 1 Crashed Airship Component. Build it from the Airship Dock panel.

### How do I upgrade the airship?

Complete the required Traderbot trade, return home, dock the airship, open the dock panel, and click Upgrade.

### Why did my airship range not expand?

You probably completed the trade but did not apply the upgrade at your dock. The map circle expands only after the dock upgrade step.

### How do I know if an island is out of range?

Open the map and check the white range circle. If the island is outside the circle, your current airship level cannot reach it yet.

### What does the first airship upgrade need?

The first major range upgrade needs 32 copper through Traderbot. Bring extra copper if possible because energy systems also need it.

### Where should I go after the first airship upgrade?

Search the next ring for wheat and quartz. A northwest island group can be a good target if it appears in your map layout, but use the range circle and visible islands as your guide.

### How do I get silicon?

Mine quartz on second-ring islands, bring it home, and smelt it into silicon.

### How do I get the egg airship upgrade?

Grow wheat, make animal feed, support chickens with food, water, and shelter, then save six eggs for Traderbot.

### Can co-op players use the same airship?

No. In co-op, only the player who built an airship can use it. Your partner needs their own Airship Dock and airship for independent travel.

### Should I fly during thunderstorms?

Avoid it when carrying rare materials. Bad weather makes flying, docking, and recovery riskier. Wait, sleep, or dock safely if possible.

### Is the Advanced Dock worth it?

Yes, but only after your power grid is stable. Advanced Dock uses power, and boost can make you crash if you use it too close to your dock.

### How do I repair the airship?

Craft or carry a repair kit, then use it on the damaged airship. Also remember that the dock can retrieve a lost or crashed airship.
