<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**In Dwarf Eats Mountain, the best artifact is the one that fixes your current bottleneck. Pick runner artifacts when gold is piling up, damage artifacts when the field is clean but the mountain is slow, Mithril or Mountain Soul effects when rare resources block upgrades, and Luck / Jackpot effects once later mountains start making drop value more important.**

For Mithril, unlock **Mithril Forging** once your run can support it. Early Mithril is usually best spent on upgrades that solve a real wall: **Mithril Pickaxes** for miner damage, **Mithril Chainmail** or **Magnetic Gloves** for runners, and Ballista / Forge / ritual upgrades when your build is ready for them.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

Do not treat this as a complete artifact tier list. The game has a large artifact pool, and the best pick depends on your run. This guide focuses on how to choose correctly as a player: collection, damage, Mithril, Luck, Prestige, or boss prep.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-artifacts-work">How artifacts work</h2>

Artifacts are run-shaping rewards. They are not just passive bonuses you collect because they look rare.

Every few mountains, the game gives you artifact choices. These choices can change how your run plays: faster runners, stronger miners, better Luck, more Mithril, more PP, stronger Demo Dwarves, better Ballistas, safer collection, or more value from rare drops.

The most important rule is simple:

**Pick the artifact that fixes the thing slowing you down right now.**

If the ground is full of gold, do not blindly pick more damage.  
If runners are idle and the mountain is slow, damage is fine.  
If Mithril blocks your next unlock, pick rare-resource support.  
If you are near Prestige, PP or run-start value can be stronger than a temporary damage bump.

<h2 id="runner-artifacts">Runner artifacts: pick these when loot is the wall</h2>

Runner artifacts are stronger than they look because collection is the hidden gate behind every damage upgrade.

Pick runner-focused artifacts when:

- gold is piling up,
- runners are crossing the field too slowly,
- rare drops sit on the ground,
- the mountain starts reclaiming ores,
- falling rocks keep stunning runners,
- or Demo / Flamer damage creates more loot than your economy can collect.

A good runner artifact can be better than a damage artifact if your current damage is already producing more value than you can bank.

<h2 id="damage-artifacts">Damage artifacts: choose by build, not just by number</h2>

Damage artifacts are best when your runners are already keeping up.

The mistake is treating all damage artifacts as the same. Miner damage, Demo burst, Flamer burn, Ballista targeting, and Laser scaling solve different problems. Before picking a damage artifact, check which unit line is actually carrying your run.

<h2 id="luck-jackpot-artifacts">Luck, Jackpot, and gem artifacts</h2>

Luck and gem-related artifacts become more important once your run is stable enough to care about drop quality.

Early on, Luck is nice but not always urgent. If your runners cannot collect normal gold, extra rare drops will only make the field messier.

Later, Luck and Jackpot-style scaling become much more valuable because they can improve what your damage produces. This matters especially when later mountains start feeling less generous and you need each hit, each gem, and each rare drop to carry more value. A run can still stall even with enough damage if hit quality and drop value do not scale with it.

- runner collection is stable,
- you are scaling gem value,
- you have upgrades that benefit from Luck,
- you are pushing later mountains,
- or your build needs better drops instead of only more hits.

<h2 id="how-to-get-mithril">How to get Mithril</h2>

Mithril is one of the first resources that changes your route. Gold buys many early upgrades, but Mithril unlocks stronger systems.

  caption="How to get Mithril in Dwarf Eats Mountain."

The important player habit is this:

**If Mithril drops, help it get home.**

Do not let a rare drop sit under the mountain while runners are stunned or overloaded. Push it manually if needed, then upgrade runners so the next rare drop is easier to collect.

<h2 id="prestige-mithril">Prestige upgrades that help Mithril</h2>

Two early Prestige upgrades matter for Mithril planning: **Ancient Cache** and **Mountain's Hidden Veins**. Ancient Cache is better when your route needs Mithril immediately at the start of a run. Mountain's Hidden Veins is better when you are clearing enough mountains for repeated chances to add up.

Both are useful, but neither fixes a broken collection economy. If Mithril or rare drops are sitting on the ground, improve runners before chasing more Mithril sources.

For exact PP timing and Prestige priorities, use the <Link href="/dwarf-eats-mountain/prestige-ascension-guide">Prestige and Ascension Guide</Link>.

<h3 id="mithril-pickaxes">Mithril Pickaxes</h3>

Choose **Mithril Pickaxes** when mountain HP is the wall and your runners can keep up.

This is a damage route. It is best when the field is clean and you need stronger miner output, not when gold is already sitting uncollected.

<h3 id="pickarang">Pickarang</h3>

**Pickarang** is a miner-scaling upgrade that can create extra hits. It becomes more interesting when you have scientists, Luck, or enough resources to support the route.

Pick it when you are intentionally building around miner scaling.

<h3 id="mithril-chainmail">Mithril Chainmail</h3>

**Mithril Chainmail** is a runner safety upgrade.

Choose it when falling rocks, monsters, friendly explosives, or calamities keep stunning runners before they can deliver loot.

This is especially useful if your build uses Demo Dwarves or other chaotic damage.

<h3 id="magnetic-gloves">Magnetic Gloves</h3>

**Magnetic Gloves** improve ore gathering speed.

Choose them when runners reach the pile but pickup speed is slowing them down. This is different from movement speed. It helps at the loot pile itself.

<h2 id="mountain-soul">Mountain Soul: what it is used for</h2>

Mountain Soul is a rare resource tied to powerful upgrades, rituals, and later systems.

You should not spend Mountain Souls casually. Treat them like build-defining resources.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to think about rare resources in Dwarf Eats Mountain."
  columns={soulColumns}
  rows={soulRows}
/>

Use Mountain Souls when the upgrade does one of these:

- unlocks a system your build needs,
- fixes your current wall,
- improves a route you are already committed to,
- or enables a ritual / Forge path that changes future runs.

Do not spend a soul only because an upgrade is available. If the upgrade supports a build you are not playing, it can wait.

<h2 id="artifact-ritual">Artifact Ritual and Forge routes</h2>

Artifact Ritual appears later in the Mithril / Forge path. It is not an early-game priority.

Think of it as part of the transition from “I am buying simple upgrades” to “I am building around artifacts and rituals.”

Move toward Artifact Ritual when:

- artifacts are becoming your main scaling system,
- you have enough Mithril and Mountain Souls to support the route,
- your basic runner economy works,
- and your build benefits from stronger artifact interactions.

If your opening still collapses because gold is not being collected, do not rush rituals. Fix runners first.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### What are the best artifacts in Dwarf Eats Mountain?

The best artifact depends on your bottleneck. Pick runner artifacts if gold is piling up, damage artifacts if the field is clean but the mountain is slow, Mithril artifacts if rare resources block upgrades, and Luck / Jackpot artifacts when drop quality matters.

### Should I always pick the highest-tier artifact?

Not always. A lower-tier artifact that fixes your current problem can be better than a rare artifact that supports a build you are not using.

### Is Bottled Dwarf good?

Yes, Bottled Dwarf is a strong early value pick because it gives a dwarf, Mithril, and PP. That helps the current run and the next Prestige cycle.

### When should I pick runner artifacts?

Pick runner artifacts when gold, gems, Mithril, or rare drops sit on the ground. If loot is not reaching the Great Maw, runner help is usually better than more damage.

### When should I pick damage artifacts?

Pick damage artifacts when the field is clean and mountain HP, dens, veins, or bosses are the main wall. Match the artifact to your build: miner, Flamer, Demo, Ballista, Laser, or global damage.

### How do I get Mithril?

Mithril can come from dens, special targets, random damage drops, artifact rewards, and Prestige support such as Ancient Cache or Mountain's Hidden Veins.

### What should I spend Mithril on first?

Spend Mithril on the upgrade that fixes your blocker. Use Mithril Pickaxes for miner damage, Mithril Chainmail for runner safety, Magnetic Gloves for gathering speed, and Ballista / Forge upgrades when your build supports them.

### What does Mountain Soul do?

Mountain Soul is used for powerful upgrades, rituals, and advanced unlocks. Save it for upgrades that support your current build instead of spending it randomly.

### Is Luck worth it early?

Luck is better once your basic economy works. If runners cannot collect normal drops, extra rare drops can make the field messier instead of helping.

### When should I use Artifact Ritual?

Use Artifact Ritual when your run is already stable and artifacts are becoming a main scaling system. Do not rush it before fixing damage, runners, and Mithril access.
