<h2 id="how-prestige-works">How Prestige works</h2>

Prestige is the first major reset layer in Dwarf Eats Mountain.

When you Prestige, you reset the current run in exchange for permanent upgrades. These upgrades make future runs faster, smoother, or easier to scale. Some help the opening. Some help runners. Some help Mithril. Some help artifacts, bosses, or later systems.

The part that confuses many new players is **how spending works**：**PP is spent between runs, not during the active run.** You do not click Prestige upgrades on the normal gameplay screen.

To spend PP correctly:

<div className="not-prose my-6 rounded-xl border border-slate-700 bg-slate-900/60 p-5">
  <ol className="list-decimal space-y-2 pl-5 text-sm leading-7 text-slate-200">
    <li>Wait until the current run has slowed down enough to justify a reset.</li>
    <li>Press and hold the top <strong>Hold to Prestige</strong> button.</li>
    <li>Use the Prestige setup screen to spend your PP on permanent upgrades.</li>
    <li>Do not instantly press <strong>Begin Game</strong> before checking your PP choices.</li>
    <li>Start the next run only after your upgrades are selected.</li>
  </ol>
</div>

The most important thing to understand is this:

**A good Prestige upgrade should solve the reason your current run slowed down.**

If your first minutes are slow, pick a start bonus.  
If your gold keeps piling up, pick runner support.  
If Mithril blocks your next route, pick Mithril help.  
If you are pushing later mountains, start planning around T6 and Ascension.

<h2 id="swift-start">Swift Start: why starting runners are so strong</h2>

**Swift Start** is one of the safest early Prestige picks because runners solve the first economy problem before it starts.

Starting miners are useful, but miners only create value. They do not bank it. If the Great Maw has no delivery support, early damage can still leave gold sitting on the ground. Starting runners help the next run feel smoother because collection begins earlier, manual pushing matters less, and your first damage upgrades turn into spendable gold faster.

Choose Swift Start when:

- gold piles up in the first few mountains,
- the Great Maw setup feels slow every reset,
- you are manually pushing too much ore early,
- or your first damage upgrades create drops faster than you can collect them.

Choose a miner or damage start instead when the field is already clean and mountain HP is the only thing slowing your opening.

When there are over **350 uncollected ores**, the mountain can start reclaiming them. **Looming Motivation** helps in that exact situation by giving runners temporary carrying capacity when they return to stash.

<h2 id="t2-and-beyond">T2 and beyond: when Prestige starts shaping builds</h2>

Early Prestige upgrades mostly make the opening smoother. Later Prestige upgrades start pushing you into build paths.

You may see upgrades that support:

- artifact gold,
- extra PP chance,
- Cyber Dwarf upgrades,
- runners with mechanical upgrades,
- den artifact drops,
- free Flamers,
- Forge starts,
- free Ballistas,
- Laboratory starts,
- stronger miners,
- better falling rock value,
- ritual power.

At this stage, stop thinking in isolated upgrades. Think in routes.

A runner route wants collection, safety, speed, carry, and ore control.  
A Flamer route wants fire uptime and cooldown support.  
A Ballista route wants targeting and den pressure.  
A Mithril route wants Forge access and rare-resource support.  
An artifact route wants more artifact access and better artifact value.

<h2 id="t6-prestige">T6 Prestige: why focus starts to matter</h2>

T6 upgrades are the bridge between Prestige and Ascension.

By the time you reach T6, the game is no longer asking, "Can you start faster?" It is asking, "Which long-term scaling path are you building around?"

The key mechanism is that **T6 upgrades do not increase in cost with each purchase**. If each purchase still costs the same amount of PP, stacking one T6 type often gives more useful power than spreading points across every option equally.

That does not mean one T6 upgrade is always best. It means your T6 spending should match your build.

If your build fails because loot is not collected, focus collection-related scaling.  
If your build fails because mountain HP is too high, focus damage scaling.  
If your build fails because drops lose value, focus Luck, Jackpot, artifacts, or resource scaling.  
If your build fails at bosses, focus the route that helps you pass the boss check.

<h2 id="when-to-ascend">When should you Ascend?</h2>

Ascend when all three are true:

1. You have reached T6 Prestige upgrades.
2. Your current layer is slowing down hard.
3. Your kept T6 upgrades are strong enough to make the next cycle better.

Do not Ascend just because it unlocks. If your T6 base is weak, the next cycle can feel worse than expected.

Good Ascension timing usually looks like this:

- your normal Prestige upgrades no longer feel like enough,
- you have invested in T6 upgrades that match your build,
- you understand what will be wiped,
- you are not sitting on unspent PP you are about to lose,
- and the Ascension Rank will make your T6 upgrades stronger in future cycles.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### When should I Prestige in Dwarf Eats Mountain?

Prestige when your run clearly slows down and your next permanent upgrade will make the next run faster.

### How do I spend PP?

Hold the top **Hold to Prestige** button, spend PP on the Prestige setup screen, then press **Begin Game**. You cannot spend PP during an active run.

### Why can't I buy Prestige upgrades during a run?

Prestige upgrades are bought between runs. If you are still in active gameplay, you need to hold the Prestige button first and use the setup screen before starting the next run.

### How do you get PP?

You mainly get PP by eating mountains. Some artifacts and bosses can also give PP or help you reach more PP sources.

### What are the best first Prestige upgrades?

For most players, Swift Start is one of the safest early picks because every run needs runners. Guild Apprentices, Housing Batches, Veteran Settlers, Ancient Cache, and Looming Motivation are also strong depending on your bottleneck.

### Should I choose damage or runner Prestige upgrades first?

Choose based on your problem. If gold piles up, choose runner support. If the field is clean but mountains take too long, choose damage or starting miner support.

### Is Ancient Cache worth it?

Ancient Cache is worth it if Mithril is blocking your route. It is less urgent if your main problem is basic gold collection or dwarf capacity.

### Is Heirloom Inheritance good early?

It can be good, but it is less predictable than Swift Start or Ancient Cache. Pick it once you are comfortable adapting around random Tier 1 artifacts.

### What does Looming Motivation do?

Looming Motivation helps when there are over 350 uncollected ores and the mountain starts reclaiming them. It gives runners temporary carrying capacity when they return to stash.

### What does Ascension reset?

Ascension resets earlier Prestige progress and unspent PP, while keeping T6 upgrades. It also gives Ascension Rank, which directly buffs T6 upgrades.

### When should I Ascend?

Ascend after T6 upgrades matter, your current layer has slowed down, and your kept T6 upgrades are strong enough to make the next cycle better.

### Should I spread T6 upgrades evenly?

Usually no. T6 upgrades do not increase in cost with each purchase, so stacking the T6 type that matches your build often performs better than spreading points randomly.
