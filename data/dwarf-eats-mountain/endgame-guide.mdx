<h2 id="does-it-have-an-ending">Does Dwarf Eats Mountain have an ending?</h2>

Yes. Dwarf Eats Mountain points toward World Spire, Mountain 100, True Ending, and Endless Mode.

The ending is not only a bigger health bar. By the time you push that far, the game is testing whether your whole route still scales: damage, collection, rare resources, artifacts, T6 upgrades, and Ascension Rank.

<h2 id="mountain-50-100">Mountain 50-100: why late mountains feel different</h2>

Mountain 50 is where the game starts changing how valuable your hits feel.

After Mountain 50, the chance for a single hit to drop multiple ores is interpolated downward as you move toward Mountain 100. By Mountain 100, single-ore outcomes become much more common, which means simple hit spam can feel weaker even if your damage number keeps rising.

<GuideTable
  caption="Why Mountain 50-100 changes endgame planning."

This is why late-game progress often needs Luck, Jackpot-style value, artifact scaling, Mithril routes, and Spelunker's Guild support. You are not only trying to hit more. You are trying to make each hit and each reward matter more.

<h2 id="world-spire">What is the World Spire?</h2>

World Spire is the long-term mountain goal the tutorial points you toward.

Treat it as the reason your reset and build systems exist. The first few runs teach you how to break mountains. The later game asks whether your build can keep scaling through lower drop quality, stronger targets, boss checks, rare resources, and Ascension cycles.

Before pushing World Spire seriously, you should have:

- one focused damage route,
- a collection plan that survives large loot fields,
- a Mithril and Mountain Soul plan,
- artifacts that support the build instead of random bonuses,
- and a T6 route worth carrying into Ascension.

<h2 id="goblin-king">Goblin King: first boss damage check</h2>

Goblin King is the first real boss check in Dwarf Eats Mountain.

Think of it as a time-limited damage test, not a normal mountain. You need to kill it within the time limit to earn treasure and the specific artifact that opens the path toward later boss progression.

Failing Goblin King does not end your run. You can keep progressing, strengthen your build, and return later. Treat a failed attempt as feedback: your route is not ready to beat the timer yet, but your run is not bricked.

<h2 id="spelunkers-guild">Spelunker's Guild and rare-resource endgame</h2>

Spelunker's Guild is one of the most important formal-release systems to watch in endgame.

It connects to artifact-den-luck, Mithril-luck, spelunking rewards, and rare-resource flow. That means it belongs in routes where the problem is no longer basic gold, but the quality and consistency of the rewards your run produces.

Use Spelunker's Guild when:

- dens and artifacts are becoming central,
- Mithril-luck starts affecting your route,
- rare-resource flow is limiting Forge or T6 progress,
- artifact-den rewards matter more than another generic damage upgrade,
- or your build needs better long-term resource quality instead of only more raw damage.

<h2 id="collection-endgame">Gorilla Arms, Guzzler, and late collection</h2>

Normal runners can carry the early game, but late fields can outgrow them.

That is where **Gorilla Arms** and **Guzzler-style** collection become important. They help when the issue is no longer “do I have enough runners?” but “can my build move huge amounts of loot quickly enough before reclaim, lag, or missed rare drops cost me value?”

Use late collection tools when:

- Mithril or artifacts sit on the field too long,
- normal runner trips cannot keep up,
- large loot piles build up after damage spikes,
- or Mountain 50+ fights create long collection pressure.

For detailed runner fixes, use the <Link href="/dwarf-eats-mountain/runners-guide">Runners Guide</Link>.

<GuideCallout title="Collection note">
Spelunker's Guild can be part of a rare-resource route while you improve collection, but rare drops still need to reach the Great Maw. If Mithril, artifacts, or high-value ores sit on the field too long, add late collection support before scaling reward quality further.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="mithril-and-forge">Mithril, Forge, and Mountain Souls in endgame</h2>

Mithril becomes a route decision in endgame.

Use Mithril and Mountain Souls for the system that moves your build forward: miner scaling, runner safety, Ballistas, Forge Artifact Ritual, runes, or rare-resource routes. If Spelunker's Guild is part of your plan, Mithril-luck and artifact-den-luck can also become part of the same long-term economy.

<h2 id="artifacts-for-endgame">Artifacts for endgame</h2>

Endgame artifact choices should support your route, not just look rare.

Pick artifacts based on the wall:

- damage artifacts for timed checks like Goblin King,
- runner artifacts when loot is not reaching the Great Maw,
- Mithril or Mountain Soul artifacts when rare resources block the route,
- Luck and Jackpot-style artifacts when hit quality matters,
- den or artifact support when Spelunker's Guild routes are active,
- PP or T6 support when the next reset matters more than the current push.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### What happens if I fail Goblin King?

You can keep progressing. Goblin King is a timed damage check, but failing it does not end your run. Come back when your damage route is stronger.

### What does Goblin King drop?

Killing Goblin King within the time limit gives treasure and a specific artifact needed for later boss progression.

### Why does progress feel worse after Mountain 50?

After Mountain 50, multi-ore outcomes become less reliable as the game moves toward Mountain 100. You need better hit quality, Luck, artifacts, Mithril, and collection instead of only more attacks.

### What does Spelunker's Guild do in endgame?

Spelunker's Guild supports artifact-den-luck, Mithril-luck, spelunking rewards, and rare-resource quality. It matters when rare resources and artifacts become more important than simple gold.

### Are Gorilla Arms and Guzzler important?

Yes, when normal runners cannot handle late loot fields. They help with heavy collection pressure, rare drops, and crowded endgame fields.

### What are the new formal-release units to watch?

Gyrocopter, Bulldozer, and Brewmaster are formal-release units / systems to watch as runs move beyond the older Demo-era unit setup.

### When should I start caring about T6 upgrades?

Start caring about T6 when ordinary Prestige upgrades no longer feel like enough and one long-term route is clearly carrying your run.

### What does Ascension Rank do?

Ascension Rank directly buffs the power of your T6 upgrades, making future cycles stronger.

### Should I Ascend as soon as it unlocks?

No. Ascend when your T6 upgrades are worth keeping, the current layer has slowed down, and the next cycle will be stronger because Ascension Rank amplifies your T6 base.

### Is True Ending the end of the game?

No. True Ending is a major milestone. After that, Endless Mode shifts the focus toward deeper scaling and optimization.
