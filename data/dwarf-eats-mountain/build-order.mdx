<h2 id="opening-setup">Opening setup: Miner's Guild, housing, miners, Great Maw</h2>

Start with **Miner's Guild**, then build **5 Underground Housing** and hire **6 Miners**. This gives you enough early damage to start breaking the mountain without depending only on your cursor.

After that, build **the Great Maw** and hire your first **3 Runners**. This is the real start of the game. Before runners, drops are just objects on the ground. After runners, drops become gold you can spend.

<h2 id="manual-help">Manual help and slap timing</h2>

In the opening minutes, manual control is worth using. You can:

- hold on the mountain to add damage,
- hold on loose gold or ore to push it toward the Great Maw,
- slap stunned runners so they stand up faster.

The best time to slap is when a runner is stunned **near a valuable pile** or when the field is starting to back up. A runner lying next to Mithril, diamonds, large gold chunks, or a crowded pile is costing you more than a runner knocked out on an empty field.

Slap timing matters most after:

- rockfalls,
- goblin hits,
- Demo explosions,
- Wind Bomb chaos,
- calamity events,
- or any moment where several runners are down at once.

If you often lose runs because runners stay stunned while ore piles up, **Wakeful Slap Mastery** is a strong early Prestige option. If you mostly idle and do not watch the field, it is less valuable than passive runner speed, carry, or starting-runner upgrades.

<h2 id="housing-and-dwarf-cap">Add more housing when dwarf capacity blocks you</h2>

If you cannot hire the units you want, build more **Underground Housing**. This becomes more important once you add runners, Demo Dwarves, Flamers, Scientists, Spelunkers, Runesmiths, or later support units.

Do not wait until you are completely stuck. If your next building is about to unlock a useful unit line, make sure your dwarf capacity can support it.

This is also why early Prestige upgrades that add dwarves or improve housing feel so strong. They make the opening less cramped and let your next run reach the real decisions faster.

<h2 id="damage-branch">When to build Flameworks or Demolition Shack</h2>

After your basic miner and runner loop works, choose your first stronger damage branch.

**Flameworks** produces Flamers. They are the steadier damage option and are easier for most new players to control.

**Demolition Shack** produces Demo Dwarves. They bring explosive damage, Wind Bomb utility, and den pressure, but they can also scatter loot, raise danger, and make runners' jobs harder.

<h2 id="mid-game-buildings">Ballistics Workshop, Grand Laboratory, Spelunker's Guild, and Great Forge</h2>

Once the early loop is stable, your next building should solve a specific blocker.

**Ballistics Workshop** is for targeting. Build it when dens, veins, or higher targets are slowing the run.

**Grand Laboratory** is for Scientists, Laser Cannons, and unlock-based scaling. Build it once your economy can support a more expensive advanced route.

**Spelunker's Guild** is for artifact, den, Luck, Mithril-luck, and spelunking progression. Build it when rare-resource systems become more important than simple gold.

**Great Forge** is for Mithril, Runesmiths, Forge upgrades, and rituals. Build toward it after you can actually collect Mithril reliably.

<h2 id="first-prestige">When to take your first Prestige</h2>

Prestige when your current run feels slower than a fresh run with permanent upgrades.

Good signs that your first Prestige is close:

- the next mountain takes much longer than the last few,
- you have enough PP for a useful permanent upgrade,
- the first minutes feel slow every run,
- runners keep falling behind,
- or you can buy an upgrade that fixes your worst early bottleneck.

Do not Prestige only because the button exists. Also do not grind a clearly stalled run forever.

For the full reset layer, use the <Link href="/dwarf-eats-mountain/prestige-ascension-guide">Prestige and Ascension Guide</Link>.

<h3 id="how-prestige-changes-openings">How Prestige changes your opening</h3>

Prestige upgrades change the build order by removing early friction.

For example:

- **Swift Start** makes collection start sooner.
- **Guild Apprentices** adds free early miners.
- **Housing Batches** makes housing scale better.
- **Veteran Settlers** gives you more starting dwarf capacity.
- **Wakeful Slap Mastery** helps active players recover stunned runners.
- **Looming Motivation** helps when uncollected ore gets out of control.

That is why Prestige is not only a late-game system. It rewrites the first few minutes of every run.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">Common beginner mistakes</h2>

### Buying damage while loot is already piling up

If gold is not reaching the Great Maw, more damage usually makes the problem worse. Fix runners first.

### Ignoring N/A upgrade requirements

N/A usually means you are missing a prerequisite. Check whether the upgrade belongs to a building, unit line, Mithril route, spelunking system, artifact system, or Prestige layer you have not opened yet.

### Spending your first Mithril randomly

Early Mithril should fix a real wall. Save it for runner protection, miner damage, gathering speed, Ballista support, or Forge progression.

### Adding Demo Dwarves too early

Demo Dwarves are strong, but explosions can create more chaos than your early economy can handle.

### Waiting too long to Prestige

If a run is clearly crawling and you can buy a permanent upgrade, resetting can be faster than forcing one more slow mountain.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### What should I build first in Dwarf Eats Mountain?

Build Miner's Guild first, then 5 Underground Housing, 6 Miners, the Great Maw, and 3 Runners.

### Why are some upgrades marked N/A?

N/A usually means you have not unlocked the prerequisite building, unit, resource, or system. Progress the related route first, such as Ballistics, Laboratory, Spelunker's Guild, Mithril, Forge, artifacts, or Prestige.

### How do I unlock Spelunker's Guild?

Spelunker's Guild belongs to the artifact, den, Luck, Mithril-luck, and spelunking side of progression. If it is not available yet, keep progressing mountains, dens, artifacts, and rare-resource systems while keeping your runner economy stable.

### Is Stoutly March or Carrying Capacity better first?

Stoutly March is better when runners take too long to reach loot. Carrying Capacity is better when they reach loot but cannot clear piles fast enough. Most early runs want both.

### When should I buy miner damage?

Buy miner damage when the field is clean and mountain HP is the main slowdown.

### Should I build Flameworks or Demolition Shack first?

Flameworks is steadier and easier for beginners. Demolition Shack is stronger but more chaotic. If your runners are already behind, delay both.

### What should I spend my first Mithril on?

If runners keep getting stunned, save for Mithril Chainmail. If mountain HP is the wall, consider Mithril Pickaxes. If pickup speed is the problem, look for Magnetic Gloves. If you are unsure, hold the Mithril until the blocker is clear.

### When should I Prestige for the first time?

Prestige when your current run slows down and a permanent upgrade will make the next run faster.

### Is Wakeful Slap Mastery worth it?

Yes, if you actively watch the field and runner stuns often cause loot piles or ore reclaim. If you mostly idle, starting runners, speed, carry, or Mithril support may be better.
