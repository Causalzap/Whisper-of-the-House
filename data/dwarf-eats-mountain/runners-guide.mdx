<h2 id="why-gold-is-not-collected">Why gold is not being collected</h2>

When gold is not being collected, it usually means your **damage is ahead of your delivery system**.

Miners, Flamers, Demo Dwarves, Ballistas, Lasers, and clicks all create more drops. Runners are the part of the colony that converts those drops into usable gold. If damage grows faster than runner speed, carry, safety, and gathering, the ground starts filling up.

That is why a run can feel slower after buying a big damage upgrade. You are breaking more rock, but the value is not reaching the Great Maw.

<h2 id="runner-upgrade-priority">Best runner upgrade priority</h2>

For most early runs, the runner upgrade order is:

1. **Stoutly March**
2. **Carrying Capacity**
3. **Dodgy Profession**
4. **Prospector's Massive Nose**
5. **Wakeful Slap Mastery**
6. **Looming Motivation**
7. **Mithril Chainmail / Magnetic Gloves later**

This is not a fixed rule. It depends on what is actually slowing the run.

<h2 id="stoutly-march">Stoutly March: when speed is the problem</h2>

Buy **Stoutly March** when runners are spending too much time walking between the loot pile and the Great Maw.

This is usually the safest first runner upgrade because every delivery route gets better with speed. Faster runners also recover from bad field layouts more easily because they spend less time exposed to falling rocks and monsters.

Buy speed first when:

- runners are crossing long distances,
- loot is spread across the field,
- you are manually pushing ore because runners are too slow,
- or runners are not overloaded but still take too long to deliver.

<h2 id="carrying-capacity">Carrying Capacity: when piles are too big</h2>

Buy **Carrying Capacity** when runners reach loot but do not clear it fast enough.

This is different from speed. Speed fixes travel time. Carry fixes trip value.

If runners keep walking back and forth while the pile barely shrinks, speed alone will not solve it. They need to carry more per delivery.

Buy carrying capacity when:

- the field is crowded,
- runners are making trips but piles stay large,
- rare drops are heavy,
- or ore reclaim is starting to threaten your income.

<h2 id="dodge-and-stuns">Dodgy Profession: when runners keep getting stunned</h2>

Runners do not only lose time by walking slowly. They also lose time by getting knocked out.

Falling rocks, monsters, goblins, boulders, calamities, Demo explosions, and general field chaos can all interrupt collection. When runners are stunned often, speed and carry stop mattering because the runner is not moving at all.

That is when **Dodgy Profession** becomes valuable.

- runners fall down before reaching the stash,
- rockfalls are common,
- dens or monsters are active,
- Demo Dwarves are creating friendly-fire chaos,

<h2 id="prospectors-nose">Prospector's Massive Nose: when collection is stable</h2>

**Prospector's Massive Nose** gives runners a chance to find random gems when they pick up non-gem ore. It is a value upgrade, not a basic delivery fix.

That means it is good after the core runner economy already works.

Buy it when:

- gold is being collected cleanly,
- runners are active but not overwhelmed,
- you want more gem value,
- or your build has Luck support.

Wait on it when:

- gold is piling up,
- rare drops are being left behind,
- runners are constantly stunned,
- or the mountain is already reclaiming ores.

<h2 id="manual-pushing">Manual pushing: when to help runners yourself</h2>

Manual pushing is strongest in the opening and during rare-drop emergencies.

Use your cursor to push loot when:

- a diamond, Mithril, artifact, or large gold chunk lands far away,
- runners are stunned,
- a pile is close to the Great Maw but not quite moving,
- or you need a quick purchase and the value is already on the ground.

Do not let manual pushing hide a bad build. If you have to push every pile yourself, your runner economy is behind.

<h2 id="ore-reclaim">Ore reclaim: what it means when the mountain takes loot back</h2>

Ore reclaim is the game telling you that the field has become too crowded. When too many unclaimed ores sit on the ground, the mountain can start reclaiming them.

That is a serious warning. Reclaimed ore is value you created but failed to convert.

When reclaim starts:

1. Stop buying damage for a moment.
2. Buy runner speed or carry.
3. Wake stunned runners.
4. Push high-value drops manually.
5. Consider Prestige upgrades that help when the field overflows.
6. Delay chaotic damage like Demo Dwarves if they make the pile worse.

A crowded field can also cause frame drops. If the game slows down, disable heavy visual options such as shaders or particles, clear the field, then wait before adding more damage.

<h2 id="looming-motivation">Looming Motivation: the anti-overflow Prestige upgrade</h2>

**Looming Motivation** is a strong Prestige upgrade for players whose runs often collapse into ore overflow.

It activates when there are over **350 uncollected ores** and the mountain starts reclaiming them. When that happens, runners gain temporary carrying capacity when they return to stash.

This upgrade is best if:

- your damage spikes create huge loot piles,
- you often see reclaim warnings,
- your runners recover too slowly after a bad calamity,
- or you want a safety net while learning stronger builds.

It is less important if your field usually stays clean.

<h2 id="how-many-runners">How many runners do you need?</h2>

There is no perfect fixed number. Use the field as your answer.

You need more runners or better runner upgrades when:

- gold stays on the ground after a few deliveries,
- runners are all busy and the pile keeps growing,
- rare drops sit far from the Great Maw,
- ore reclaim starts,
- or new damage buildings immediately create a backlog.

You probably do not need more runners yet when:

- the field is mostly clear,
- runners are idle,
- runners are not reaching their carry limit,
- or mountain HP is the actual wall.

<h2 id="damage-vs-runners">Should you buy damage or runners next?</h2>

Use this rule:

  <ul className="space-y-2 text-sm leading-7 text-slate-200">

    <li><strong>Messy field, slow delivery?</strong> Buy runners.</li>
    <li><strong>Runners reaching loot but not clearing it?</strong> Buy carrying capacity.</li>
    <li><strong>Runners walking too long?</strong> Buy speed.</li>
    <li><strong>Runners getting knocked out?</strong> Buy dodge, slap efficiency, or stun protection.</li>

  </ul>

This is the simplest way to avoid the biggest beginner trap: buying damage because the run feels slow, when the real problem is that your existing damage is not being collected.

<h3 id="mithril-chainmail">Mithril Chainmail</h3>

Mithril Chainmail helps when runners are losing too much time to danger. It reduces stun problems and adds protection against threats like monsters, falling rocks, friendly dynamite, and Wind Bomb chaos.

Prioritize it when your build uses explosive damage or when calamities keep interrupting deliveries.

<h3 id="magnetic-gloves">Magnetic Gloves</h3>

Magnetic Gloves help with ore gathering speed. They are better when runners are alive and moving, but dense piles take too long to pick up.

Prioritize them when the runners reach the pile but the pickup step itself feels slow.

<h2 id="commit-to-runner-build">When to hard-commit to a runner build</h2>

Do not turn every run into a pure runner build. Most early runs should stay balanced: enough runners to collect, then enough damage to keep clearing mountains.

Hard-commit to runner economy when collection is the thing that keeps ending your runs.

Good signs are:

- every damage upgrade immediately creates a loot backlog,
- the mountain starts reclaiming ores often,
- rare drops sit on the ground long enough to feel risky,
- runners are always busy and never idle,
- Demo or Flamer damage makes the field worse instead of faster,
- or your next Prestige choice is clearly runner-related.

Stay balanced when:

- the field is mostly clean,
- runners have idle time,
- mountain HP is the main wall,
- or dens and veins need more targeting instead of more carry.

The goal is not “buy every runner upgrade.” The goal is to stop losing value before it reaches the Great Maw.

<h2 id="demo-flamer-warning">Why Demo and Flamer builds can break your runner economy</h2>

Flamers and Demo Dwarves are strong, but they increase pressure on collection.

Flamers create steady extra damage, which means more drops. Demo Dwarves add burst and physics chaos, which can throw loot around, wake up danger, or stun runners if your safety is weak.

Before adding a new damage branch, ask:

- Are runners already busy?
- Is the field already messy?
- Do rare drops get left behind?
- Are stunned runners common?
- Is ore reclaim already starting?

If the answer is yes, improve collection before adding more damage.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Why is my gold not being collected in Dwarf Eats Mountain?

Your runners are usually too slow, overloaded, stunned, or outpaced by your damage. Buy Stoutly March, Carrying Capacity, Dodgy Profession, or more runners before adding more damage.

### How many runners do I need?

There is no fixed number. If gold and rare drops sit on the ground, you need more collection. If runners are idle and the field is clean, you can usually buy damage instead.

### Is Stoutly March or Carrying Capacity better?

Speed fixes travel time. Carry fixes pile size. Most early runs need both.

### When should I buy Dodgy Profession?

Buy Dodgy Profession when falling rocks, monsters, goblins, calamities, or explosions keep stunning runners before they can deliver.

### Is Prospector's Massive Nose worth it early?

It is good after basic collection is stable. Do not buy it before speed and carry if gold is already piling up.

### What does Looming Motivation do?

Looming Motivation helps when there are over 350 uncollected ores and the mountain starts reclaiming them. It gives runners temporary carrying capacity when they return to stash.

### How do I stop the mountain from reclaiming ore?

Reduce the pile. Stop buying damage, push valuable drops manually, upgrade runner speed and carry, wake stunned runners, and use Prestige or Mithril upgrades that improve collection.

### Are Mithril runner upgrades worth it?

Yes, once you unlock them. Mithril Chainmail helps with stun and danger. Magnetic Gloves help runners gather ore faster. Both are strong when basic runner upgrades are no longer enough.
