<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**In your first hour of Farever, do not just run forward and grind random mobs. Claim your free mount, fix your controls, follow map question marks, open the Codeex with L, unlock obelisks, upgrade weapon skills, and stop throwing away old gear once you reach the recycler.**

The best beginner route is:

1. Pick a class you can learn comfortably.
2. Check controls for attack, block, dodge, jump, glider, mount, consumables, and autorun.
3. Claim the free mount from the shop.
4. Equip the mount from **Collections**.
5. Open the map and follow gray question marks.
6. Press **L** to check Codeex monster counters.
7. Finish nearby 4-kill and 8-kill milestones.
8. Upgrade weapon skills when the green plus appears.
9. Unlock obelisks as soon as you reach them.
10. Use the Spark Recycler, bank, and small pouches before your bag fills up.

If you feel lost, check **map completion → obelisk → Codeex → weapon skills** in that order.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="class-choice">
  Best starting class for beginners
</h2>

Farever currently starts with four classes: **Warrior**, **Rogue**, **Mage**, and **Cleric/Priest**. For your first character, comfort matters more than perfect meta ranking.

<GuideTable
  caption="Farever starting class recommendations."
  columns={classColumns}
  rows={classRows}
/>

For most players, **Cleric/Priest** is the safest first character because self-healing lets you recover from bad blocks, missed dodges, and messy Codeex pulls. **Rogue** feels better if you enjoy movement and control; early Rogue can use teleport-style movement and later ranged tools such as bows. **Warrior** becomes clearer once you start using heavier weapon skills such as **Berserk Attack** and **Smash Attack**. **Mage** is the better fit if you want ranged magic and care about scepter swaps, because different scepters can change the spell package.

The main thing to remember: do not judge the entire class from the starter weapon. A weapon swap can change your skill bar enough that the same class feels very different later.

<h2 id="controls-combat">
  Controls and combat basics
</h2>

Before you fight for long, open the hotkey menu and make sure the basics feel right. Farever is action combat, and small control problems become annoying quickly.

<GuideTable
  caption="Important Farever beginner controls and menus."
  columns={controlColumns}
  rows={controlRows}
/>

Once the controls feel comfortable, focus on three combat habits.

**First, do not hold block forever.** Blocking is useful when you are learning enemy patterns, but it drains stamina. Use it to survive a mistake, then move, dodge, or counterattack instead of standing still.

**Second, dodge before you panic-heal.** Rogue-style play especially rewards movement: dodge out of pressure, reposition, then attack when the enemy finishes its animation. If you try to face-tank everything, early fights feel much worse.

**Third, check your weapon skills after every weapon swap.** Weapons are not just stat sticks in Farever. A new weapon can change your active skills, your cooldowns, or your safety tools. If a green plus appears on the skill screen, spend the upgrade before pushing into harder fights.

<GuideCallout title="Beginner combat habit">
After every new weapon, do a quick check: skill bar, green upgrade plus, consumable slot, and whether your block/dodge keys still feel comfortable.
</GuideCallout>

If your first few fights feel awkward, fix controls early. Do not wait until the first dungeon to realize your consumable, block, dodge, or mount key is uncomfortable.

<h2 id="free-mount">
  Claim and equip your free mount
</h2>

One of the best first steps is getting your mount. The confusing part is that **claiming** the mount and **equipping** the mount are two different actions.

Use this order:

1. Press **Esc**.
2. Open the shop.
3. Claim the free items if they are available.
4. Press **Y** to open Collections.
5. Select the mount tab at the bottom.
6. Equip the mount.
7. Check your mount/dismount key in **Options → Hotkeys**.
8. Test the mount outside combat.

<h2 id="map-codeex">
  Map completion and Codeex leveling
</h2>

When you are lost, open the map first. Gray question marks on the right side point toward local objectives, and map completion gives strong early progress. The first region can be completed to **13/13**, so do not leave an area just because the main path looks done.

After checking the map, press **L** and open the Codeex. The **Monsters** section shows how many enemies of each type you have killed.

The early pattern:

- map question marks show where to go
- Codeex shows what nearby enemies are still worth killing
- 4-kill and 8-kill milestones can give XP
- 20-kill progress is a reward check, not something to force on every monster
- mini bosses and special enemies can also update progress
- chests and side objectives should be done while you are already nearby

<h2 id="weapon-skills">
  Weapon skills and early upgrades
</h2>

Farever’s weapon system is one of the first systems you should learn. Your class has its own skills, but a large part of your active kit comes from the weapon you equip.

Important early rules:

- different weapons can change your active skills
- one-handed weapons may give fewer skills
- two-handed weapons can give a larger active/passive package
- a green blinking plus means a skill upgrade is available
- skill upgrades can add damage, cooldown value, or synergies
- weapon enhancement stations exist, but low-level stat upgrades can be minor
- save enhancement materials unless the weapon effect is worth keeping

<h2 id="inventory-salvage">
  Inventory, salvage, and early resources
</h2>

Your inventory fills quickly because you pick up gear, materials, lost packages, runes, recipes, and random items before you know what they do.

The most important rule: **do not discard old gear once you can reach the village hub.**

Use these early resource tools:

- use the **Spark Recycler** to turn old gear into useful materials
- buy **small pouches** from the guild merchant
- each small pouch adds **6 inventory slots**
- use the bank for materials, manuals, runes, recipes, and gear you do not need now
- right-click **lost packages** to turn them into completed packages
- hand completed packages to the guild merchant for **100 gold**
- keep runes until you know which skill they enhance
- avoid dumping materials just because you do not know the recipe yet

For potions, **Alchemist** is the most practical first profession because it gives access to minor healing potions. Vials cost **30 gold** from the guild merchant, and minor healing potions can heal up to **100 HP**, which is a lot when your early HP pool is only around the low 100s.

<h2 id="obelisks-fast-travel">
  Obelisks and fast travel
</h2>

Obelisks are not just landmarks. They are part of your route.

When you find one, interact with it. Obelisks can:

- reveal a zone
- show nearby points of interest
- set a respawn point
- unlock automatic travel to another obelisk
- reduce backtracking
- make map completion easier

Fast travel is not instant teleporting. Your character travels automatically, but it is still much faster than running the same path again.

<h2 id="underwater-exploration">
  Underwater exploration
</h2>

Farever does not use normal swimming. When you enter water, you walk underwater instead. This matters because some objectives, enemies, and chests are placed below the surface.

Remember these rules:

- there is no normal swimming movement
- you can walk under the water
- a blue breath bar limits how long you can stay under
- when the bar runs out, you start losing HP
- you can still double jump underwater
- self-healing or potions can help you survive longer if you push too far

<figure className="my-8">

Do not skip underwater paths automatically. Some of them are built for objectives, enemies, or chests.

<h2 id="first-dungeon-prep">
  Before your first dungeon
</h2>

Do not rush into the first dungeon with a full bag and unspent skill upgrades.

Before entering:

- clear inventory space
- recycle old gear if you have reached the recycler
- equip useful gear upgrades
- spend available weapon skill points
- use runes if you have already unlocked any
- bring potions if your build lacks self-healing
- practice gliding and jumping
- remember that mounts may be disabled inside
- look for orbs, side platforms, grapple points, and boss shield timing

The first dungeon has **5 orbs**, and it is easy to miss one if you treat the dungeon like a straight boss hallway.

Read <Link href="/farever/first-dungeon-guide">Farever First Dungeon Guide</Link> for orb locations and boss tips.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Farever?

Claim your free mount, equip it from Collections, check your controls, open the map, follow gray question marks, unlock obelisks, check Codeex with L, and upgrade weapon skills when the green plus appears.

### What is the best beginner class in Farever?

Cleric/Priest is the safest beginner pick because it has self-healing. Rogue is better for mobility, Warrior is the direct melee pick, and Mage is better if you want ranged magic and scepter-based skill swaps.

### How do I equip my mount in Farever?

Claim the free mount from the shop, then equip it from Collections with **Y**. For the full flow, use the <Link href="#free-mount">mount section</Link> above.

### What key opens the Codeex?

Press **L** to open the Codeex by default.

### What should I do if there is no quest log?

Read NPC text, open the map, follow gray question marks, unlock obelisks, and use Codeex/map completion to guide your route.

### How do I level early in Farever?

Use map completion, Codeex milestones, chests, mini bosses, and dungeons. Do not just grind one enemy forever.

### Should I kill every monster 20 times?

No. Treat 20-kill progress as a reward check. For the full logic, read <Link href="/farever/codeex-leveling-guide">Farever Codeex and Leveling Guide</Link>.

### How do weapon skills work?

Weapons provide active skills, and switching weapons can change your skill bar. Upgrade weapon skills when the green plus appears.

### Should I upgrade low-level weapons?

Do not spend too many materials on low-level weapon enhancement unless the weapon effect is worth keeping. Early skill upgrades are usually more important.

### What should I do with old gear?

Keep old gear until you reach the Spark Recycler, then recycle it into useful materials instead of discarding it.

### How do I get more inventory space?

Buy small pouches from the guild merchant and place them into your bag slots. Each small pouch adds 6 inventory slots.

### What are lost packages for?

Right-click lost packages to turn them into completed packages, then hand them to the guild merchant for 100 gold.

### What is the best first profession?

Alchemist is the most practical first profession because it lets you craft minor healing potions, which helps classes or weapon setups without self-healing.

### How do obelisks work?

Obelisks reveal zones, set respawn points, and unlock automatic travel routes. Interact with every obelisk when you reach a new area.

### Can you swim in Farever?

Not normally. You walk underwater, manage a blue breath bar, and start losing HP if the bar runs out. You can still double jump underwater.

### Can I play Farever solo?

Yes, much of the early game can be handled solo, especially with a safe class, potions, or self-healing setup. Farever is still online, so you will see other players and can group for content.
