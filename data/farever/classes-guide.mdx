<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best beginner class in Farever is Cleric/Priest if you want the safest solo start, Rogue if you want fast movement and control, Warrior if you want direct melee pressure, and Mage if you want ranged magic shaped by scepter weapon swaps.**

The important rule is that Farever is not only class-based:

1. Your class gives you class skills.
2. Your weapon gives a large part of your active skill bar.
3. Runes can modify class skills.
4. Arsenal can add one skill from another weapon later.
5. A class can feel very different after a weapon swap.

For a first character, pick comfort over meta. Cleric/Priest is safest, Rogue is fastest, Warrior is simplest for melee pressure, and Mage is best if you want magic weapon play.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="best-starting-class">
  Best starting class
</h2>

For most new players, **Cleric/Priest** is the safest starting class because self-healing makes early mistakes easier to recover from. If you are learning Farever’s block, dodge, Codeex routing, and first dungeon movement, that extra safety matters.

The other three classes are not bad; they simply ask for different habits. Rogue rewards movement and control, Warrior rewards direct melee pressure, and Mage depends heavily on magic weapon packages.

<h2 id="current-and-planned-classes">
  Current and planned classes
</h2>

Farever currently has **four playable starting classes**:

- Cleric / Priest
- Rogue
- Warrior
- Mage

**Monk** and **Druid** are planned for later updates, but they are not part of the current starting class choice. Treat them as roadmap classes, not classes you can build around right now.

<GuideCallout title="Current class choice">
Pick from the four playable classes first. Monk and Druid are worth watching, but they should not affect your first-character decision until they are actually added.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best beginner class in Farever?

Cleric/Priest is the safest beginner class because Judgment can heal and the class gives you more room to recover from mistakes.

### How many classes are in Farever?

Farever currently has four playable starting classes: Cleric/Priest, Rogue, Warrior, and Mage.

### Are Monk and Druid in Farever?

Not yet. Monk and Druid are planned for later updates, but they are not current starting classes.

### Is Rogue good for beginners?

Rogue is good if you like mobility and control. Shadow Step teleports behind the target and stuns for 2 seconds, but Rogue is less forgiving than Cleric/Priest.

### Is Warrior good in Farever?

Warrior is good if you want direct melee pressure. Its early identity becomes clearer once weapon skills such as Berserk Attack and Smash Attack appear on axe setups.

### Is Mage good in Farever?

Mage is good if you want ranged magic. The key is the weapon: fire scepter gives fire abilities, while another scepter can add healing and magic damage.

### What does Cleric/Priest do?

Cleric/Priest is the safest support-style class. Judgment is the key early example because it releases a prayer-based nova and can heal.

### What does Rogue do?

Rogue focuses on mobility, control, and positioning. Read the Rogue section above for Shadow Step and bow notes.

### What weapons can Rogue use?

Early examples show Rogue using dagger-style weapons and bow. Bow is the clearest ranged option tied to Rogue so far.

### What weapons can Warrior use?

Early examples point Warrior toward swords and axes. Axe-style setups can unlock Berserk Attack and Smash Attack.

### What weapons can Mage use?

Mage-style builds lean on magic weapons such as scepters. A fire scepter changes the skill package into fire abilities.

### What weapons can Cleric/Priest use?

Cleric/Priest can use healing or magic weapons, and some setups can push it toward a defensive or paladin-style route. Always check the weapon tooltip.

### Should I pick a class based on a tier list?

Not yet. Farever builds depend on class, weapon, runes, and Arsenal together. Pick the class that fits how you want to learn the game first.

### Which class is best for solo play?

Cleric/Priest is the safest solo pick. Rogue is faster but squishier, Warrior is direct melee, and Mage is ranged but weapon-dependent.

### Which class is best for co-op?

Cleric/Priest is the easiest co-op recommendation because healing and support are always useful. Rogue, Warrior, and Mage all become easier to play when another player helps with pressure or control.
