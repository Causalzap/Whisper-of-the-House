<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Farever builds are not only class-based. A large part of your active kit comes from your weapon, and later your build also depends on skill ranks, runes, and the Arsenal slot.**

The most important rules:

1. Your class unlocks skills as you level, but **weapon skills make up a huge part of your combat kit**.
2. A simple one-handed weapon may give a basic attack plus a cooldown skill.
3. A two-handed weapon can give a larger package, such as **three active skills and one passive**.
4. A **green blinking plus** means a skill rank upgrade is available.
5. Early weapon enhancement stats can be small, so do not spend all materials on low-level weapons.
6. **Runes** unlock class skill bonuses after you right-click them in your inventory.
7. **Arsenal** lets you equip an extra weapon and use one of its skills.
8. If a class feels weak, try another weapon before rerolling.

Start with weapon skill ranks first, use runes when you get them, then use Arsenal to cover what your main weapon is missing.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="how-weapon-skills-work">
  How weapon skills work
</h2>

Farever does something many new players miss: your class is only part of your build. A large part of your active combat kit comes from the weapon you equip.

That means a new weapon can change:

- your basic attack flow
- your cooldown skills
- your AoE pattern
- your healing or shielding options
- your passive effects
- your support value
- your safety while soloing

<h2 id="pet-net">
  Pet net warning
</h2>

There is a pet net used for catching small pets in the world. Do not confuse it with Arsenal.

The pet net:

- is used for catching companions
- is bought from the guild merchant
- takes an active weapon slot
- should not be equipped right before a fight
- is not your normal Arsenal utility tool

Use the net when you are exploring or collecting pets. Swap back to a real weapon before fighting.

<h2 id="build-priority">
  Beginner build priority
</h2>

If you are not sure what to upgrade first, use this order:

1. **Check your active weapon skills** after every weapon swap.
2. **Spend green-plus skill rank upgrades** on skills you are actually using.
3. **Use runes** that affect important class skills.
4. **Equip an Arsenal weapon** that fixes a real weakness.
5. **Save weapon enhancement materials** unless the weapon is worth keeping.
6. **Only chase “best build” ideas** after you know which weapon package you like.

A strong early build is not always the one with the highest weapon stat. It is the one where your active skills, cooldowns, rune bonuses, and Arsenal tool solve the fights you are actually doing.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do weapon skills work in Farever?

Weapons provide active skills, and switching weapons can change your skill bar. Your class has its own skills, but a large part of your active combat kit comes from your equipped weapon.

### Do all weapons give the same number of skills?

No. A simple one-handed weapon may give fewer skills, while a two-handed weapon can give a larger package such as multiple active skills plus a passive.

### What does the green plus mean on a skill?

A green blinking plus means that skill can be upgraded. Open the skill screen, click the skill, and spend the rank point if the upgrade fits your current weapon kit.

### Should I upgrade weapon skills or the weapon itself first?

Upgrade active weapon skills first. Early weapon enhancement can give small stat gains, while skill ranks directly improve the buttons you are pressing in combat.

### Should I enhance low-level weapons?

Only if you plan to keep the weapon or the effect is strong. Early station upgrades can be minor, so save materials for weapons you will actually use.

### What is Arsenal in Farever?

Arsenal is a secondary weapon slot that lets you use one skill from another weapon. It is useful for adding healing, shielding, utility, or extra damage.

### How do I change my Arsenal skill?

Hold **Control** and left-click the Arsenal weapon, then select the skill you want from that weapon.

### How do runes work?

Runes unlock class skill bonuses. Right-click the rune in your inventory first, then choose the unlocked bonus on the related skill screen.

### Are runes for weapon skills or class skills?

Runes are mainly for class skill bonuses. Weapon skills are upgraded separately through skill ranks.

### What is a good early Arsenal choice?

Use Arsenal to cover your weakness. If your main weapon has damage but no safety, add a healing or shield tool. If your main weapon is safe but slow, add a burst or AoE tool.

### Can Rogue use a bow?

Bow appears as a Rogue-friendly ranged option in early examples. Use it if you like Rogue mobility but want more range than dagger-style play.

### What are Berserk Attack and Smash Attack?

They are early axe-style skills seen with a Warrior-style double-axe setup. They make the weapon feel more direct and AoE-focused.

### Is Shadow Step a weapon skill?

Shadow Step is a Rogue-style mobility/control skill that teleports behind the target and stuns. It is a good example of why class skills and weapon skills both matter.

### What weapons can Rogue use in Farever?

Early examples show Rogue using dagger-style weapons and bow. Bow is the clearest ranged option tied to Rogue so far.

### What weapons can Mage use in Farever?

Mage-style builds lean heavily on magic weapons such as scepters. A fire scepter can swap you into fire abilities, while another scepter can add healing and magic damage.
