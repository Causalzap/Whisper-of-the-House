<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best early leveling route in Farever is map completion first, Codeex second, and random mob grinding last. Open the map, clear gray question marks, unlock obelisks, then use the Codeex to finish nearby monster milestones.**

Use this loop:

1. Open the map and check nearby gray question marks.
2. Unlock the local obelisk before going deeper.
3. Press **L** to open the Codeex.
4. Check the **Monsters** section.
5. Finish efficient **4-kill** and **8-kill** milestones on nearby mobs such as crabs, slimes, bees, and fish guys.
6. Kill local mini bosses or sparkling creatures once when they are on your route.
7. Check rewards before pushing any monster counter to **20**.
8. Move on when map completion, Codeex, chests, and mini boss value slow down.

If you are lost, do not stand still farming random mobs. Check **map completion → obelisk → Codeex** in that order.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="map-completion">
  Map completion: what to do when you feel lost
</h2>

When you do not know what to do next, open the map first. Look at the right side for **gray question marks** and local completion objectives. These are not decoration; they are one of the main ways the game tells you where the next useful activity is.

A good early pattern is:

- open the map after every new area
- look for gray question marks
- zoom or pan until you understand nearby objectives
- complete local markers before wandering far away
- check the completion count before leaving

The first region can be completed to **13/13**, so do not assume an area is done just because the main path looks clear.

<h2 id="codeex-basics">
  Codeex basics
</h2>

Press **L** to open the Codeex, then go to the **Monsters** section. This is where you can see how many enemies of each type you have killed.

The important part is the counter. If a monster says something like **2/8**, that means the entry still has progress left. In early areas, this usually starts with simple local enemies: finish nearby **crabs** and **slimes** first, then check **bees**, **fish guys**, sparkling creatures, or mini bosses as the map route pushes you outward.

Use this rule:

- **2/8 or 3/8**: keep going if the enemy is nearby
- **4 kills**: check for an XP milestone
- **7/8**: finish the entry before leaving
- **8/8**: the main early entry is done
- **20 kills**: check the reward before committing
- **Named or sparkling enemy**: tag it once, then check Codeex and map completion

The Codeex is a routing tool. Use it to decide whether the next few kills are still valuable, not as an excuse to overfarm one safe monster forever.

<h2 id="mini-bosses">
  Mini bosses and special entries
</h2>

Not every Codeex entry is a normal mob counter. Some entries are tied to **mini bosses**, elite enemies, or sparkling creatures. If an NPC asks you to show a Codeex with several monsters in it, do not only farm basic crabs or slimes. Check for named enemies in the area too.

Mini bosses are worth checking because they can:

- complete a Codeex entry
- drop gear
- give XP
- update local activity progress
- count toward map completion
- unlock rewards that normal enemies do not

<h2 id="obelisk-route">
  Obelisks, respawn, and fast travel
</h2>

Obelisks are part of leveling because they make the map usable. When you enter a new area, look for the big glowing stone and interact with it.

Obelisks can:

- reveal the local map
- show hidden locations and points of interest
- set a respawn or teleport point
- unlock automatic travel to another obelisk
- reduce backtracking when you move between objectives

Fast travel is not instant. Your character travels automatically through the air, but it is still much faster than running back manually.

<h2 id="when-to-stop-grinding">
  When should you stop grinding a monster?
</h2>

This is the only place the “stop grinding” rule needs a full explanation.

Use this checklist:

- Is the monster entry still before its 4-kill or 8-kill XP milestone?
- Is the monster dense enough that the next milestone will take less than a minute or two?
- Does the 20-kill reward show something useful, like a resource you need?
- Is there a nearby mini boss, chest, or gray question mark that gives better progress?
- Are you underleveled for this enemy group?
- Are you only grinding because you do not know where to go?

If the next reward is not clear, move on. Farever rewards routing through **map objectives + Codeex + chests + elites** more than standing in one spot forever.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the fastest way to level early in Farever?

The fastest early route is to combine map completion, Codeex milestones, chests, mini bosses, and dungeons. Do not just grind one enemy forever.

### What key opens the Codeex in Farever?

Press **L** to open the Codeex by default.

### Where are monster entries in the Codeex?

Open Codeex, then check the **Monsters** section. This is where you can see kill counters and monster progress.

### What does 2/8 mean in Farever?

It means you have killed 2 enemies toward an 8-kill monster entry. If that monster is nearby, it is usually worth finishing the entry before leaving.

### What happens at 4 kills?

Some early Codeex entries give XP around 4 kills. This makes partial entry progress valuable even before the full 8-kill entry is done.

### What happens at 8 kills?

8 kills usually completes the main early monster entry. After that, check whether continuing toward 20 is worth the time.

### Do mini bosses count for Codeex?

Yes. Some Codeex entries are tied to mini bosses, sparkling creatures, or special enemies instead of normal kill counters.

### Does party play share Codeex progress?

Party kills can update your Codeex, but you should stay close and tag mini bosses yourself. If you are too far away, you may miss credit or loot.

### What should I do if I feel lost?

Open the map, check gray question marks, unlock the nearest obelisk, then open Codeex and check unfinished monster entries.

### How do obelisks help leveling?

Obelisks reveal zones, set respawn or teleport points, and unlock automatic travel routes. They reduce backtracking and make map completion easier.

### Should I level by grinding mobs or exploring?

Do both, but do not only grind. The best route is map objective → Codeex entry → chest or mini boss → obelisk → next area.
