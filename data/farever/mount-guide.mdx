<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**To get a mount in Farever, press Esc, open the shop, claim the free items, then press Y to open Collections and equip the mount from the mount tab. Claiming the mount is not enough; it must be equipped before your mount key works.**

The fast fix if your mount does not work:

1. Press **Y** to open Collections.
2. Select the **mount tab** at the bottom.
3. Equip the mount.
4. Open **Options → Hotkeys**.
5. Check your **mount/dismount** key.
6. Test the mount in a safe open-world area.

Do not buy the 1000 gold vendor mount until you have checked whether your free mount is already equipped.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="mount-keybinds">
  Mount keybinds and controls
</h2>

After the mount is equipped, open **Options → Hotkeys** and check your mount/dismount key. This is worth doing early because movement keys become muscle memory fast.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/farever/farever-mount-hotkeys.webp"
    alt="Farever hotkey menu showing mount and dismount keybind options."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Rebind mount and dismount before you get too far into the game. A bad movement key gets annoying quickly.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Good keybind candidates are usually:

- a side mouse button
- a nearby keyboard key you do not use for combat
- the same mount key you use in other MMOs
- a key that does not conflict with dodge, block, consumables, or weapon skills

If you also changed glider, autorun, or consumable keys, test all of them together before leaving the starter area.

A useful beginner habit is to practice this before your first dungeon:

1. Mount up in an open area.
2. Ride toward a safe slope or ledge.
3. Jump.
4. Trigger the glider while airborne.
5. Land, remount, and repeat until it feels natural.

This helps later when routes ask you to combine jumping, gliding, platforms, and side paths.

<h2 id="collections-tabs">
  Mounts, gliders, companions, and appearance tabs
</h2>

Collections can be confusing because several reward types live there. A free claim can include more than one kind of item, and not all of them are mounts.

Common tabs or reward types include:

- mounts
- gliders
- companions
- appearance items
- cosmetic rewards

A companion is not the same as a mount. A companion follows you; a mount carries you. If you equip a companion and still cannot ride, you probably equipped the wrong type of reward.

<h2 id="obelisk-fast-travel">
  Obelisks and fast travel
</h2>

Mounts are for local travel. Obelisks are for bigger route management.

When you find an obelisk, interact with it. Obelisks can:

- reveal the local zone
- show points of interest
- act as respawn points
- set teleportation points
- enable automatic travel to another unlocked obelisk
- reduce backtracking between objectives

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/farever/farever-obelisk-fast-travel.webp"
    alt="Farever obelisk menu showing zone reveal, respawn setup, and automatic travel options."

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>

The clean movement rule is:

- use **mount** for short open-world travel
- use **glider** for drops, gaps, and vertical movement
- use **obelisk travel** for longer backtracking
- unlock every obelisk before leaving a new area

<h2 id="pet-net-warning">
  Pet net warning
</h2>

The pet net is not a mount tool. It is used for catching small pets or companions, and it takes an active weapon slot.

That means:

- do not equip the net before combat
- do not confuse pet catching with mount equipping
- use the net only when you are collecting pets
- swap back to a real weapon before fighting

This matters because a new player can accidentally weaken their combat setup by treating the net like a harmless collection item.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you get a mount in Farever?

Press **Esc**, open the shop, claim the free items, then press **Y** to open Collections. Select the mount tab and equip the mount.

### Why does my mount key not work?

You probably claimed the mount but did not equip it. Open Collections with **Y**, select the mount tab, equip the mount, then test the key again.

### Where is the mount after I claim it?

The mount is in **Collections**, not your normal inventory.

### What key opens Collections?

The default Collections key is **Y**.

### How do I change my mount key?

Open **Options → Hotkeys** and change the mount/dismount key there.

### Do I need to buy the 1000 gold mount?

No, not if you already claimed and equipped the free mount. Treat the 1000 gold vendor mount as optional unless you want that specific mount.

### How much does the vendor glider cost?

One early vendor shows a glider for **500 gold**.

### Can I use the glider and mount together?

Yes. You can use the mount for ground movement, then jump and glide while airborne for smoother travel.

### Is a companion the same as a mount?

No. A companion follows you; a mount is used for riding. Check the correct Collections tab.

### Is the pet net a mount item?

No. The pet net is for catching pets and uses an active weapon slot. Do not equip it before combat.

### How do obelisks work with mounts?

Mounts help with local travel, while obelisks help with longer travel. Unlock obelisks to reveal zones, set respawn points, and use automatic travel routes.
