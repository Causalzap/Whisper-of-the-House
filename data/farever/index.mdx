<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Farever is easiest to understand if you treat the first hour as a systems check: claim your mount, learn the Codeex, follow map completion, use obelisks, manage inventory early, and watch your weapon skills.**

The early rules that matter most:

1. **4 starting classes**: Warrior, Rogue, Mage, and Cleric/Priest.
2. **Level 20** is the current Early Access cap.
3. **25+ weapons** and **100+ skills** make weapon choice a real build system.
4. Codeex entries can reward early milestones like **4 kills** and **8 kills**, while some counters continue toward **20 kills**.
5. The first dungeon has **5 orbs**, and missing one is easy if you rush the route.
6. Do not discard old gear too early. Use the **Spark Recycler** once you reach the village hub.
7. If your bag fills up, buy **small pouches** for **+6 inventory slots** each.
8. Unlock **obelisks** early because they reveal zones, set respawn points, and enable automatic travel.

Start with the Beginner Guide if you are lost, the Codeex Guide if you are trying to level faster, and the Weapon Skills Guide if a new weapon changed your abilities.

<h2 id="codeex-leveling">
  Codeex and leveling
</h2>

The Codeex is where a lot of early progression starts to make sense. Press **L** and open the monster section when you enter a new area. If a monster shows a counter like **2/8**, that entry still has XP waiting.

Some entries reward early milestones such as **4 kills** and **8 kills**, while the counter can continue toward **20 kills** for mastery or extra reward behavior. Some entries are simpler and only ask you to kill a mini boss.

The clean early rule is:

- check the Codeex whenever you enter a new area
- finish nearby 4-kill or 8-kill XP milestones before moving on
- only push to 20 kills when the reward or route makes sense
- use map completion and Codeex together
- do not treat every enemy like an endless XP farm

<h2 id="map-completion">
  Map completion and obelisks
</h2>

When you do not know what to do next, open the map and look at the right side. Gray question marks point toward local objectives, and clearing those objectives gives strong early XP. The first region can be completed to **13/13**, so do not leave only because the main path looks finished.

Obelisks are just as important. They are not only landmarks. They can:

- reveal a new zone
- show a short area preview
- act as respawn points
- enable automatic travel to another unlocked obelisk
- help you route back without running the full distance manually

Fast travel is not instant teleporting. Your character travels automatically, but it still saves time and prevents a lot of backtracking.

<h2 id="classes">
  Classes
</h2>

Farever starts with **four classes**: Warrior, Rogue, Mage, and Cleric/Priest. Your class matters, but it does not lock your whole playstyle because weapons can change a large part of your active kit.

For a first character:

- choose **Cleric/Priest** if you want the most forgiving solo route. Self-healing makes early mistakes easier to recover from, especially before you understand boss tells and Codeex routing.
- choose **Rogue** if you like fast movement, dodging, and control. Rogue starts feeling good once you lean into tools like teleport-style movement, backstab-style attacks, stuns, and later ranged options such as bows.
- choose **Warrior** if you want a direct melee route with heavier damage pressure. Warrior is the better fit if you want to build around raw power, rage-style damage windows, and weapons such as axes that can give AoE-focused skills like **Berserk Attack** and **Smash Attack**.
- choose **Mage** if you want ranged magic with more forgiving aim than a pure shooter. Mage-style play benefits from scepter and staff weapons: a fire scepter can swap you into fire abilities, while other magic weapons can add healing or magic-damage tools.

The important mistake to avoid: do not judge a class only by the starter weapon. A Rogue with daggers, a Rogue with a bow, a Cleric/Priest with sword and shield, or a Mage-style build with a different scepter can feel like a different setup.

<h2 id="weapon-skills">
  Weapon skills, runes, and Arsenal
</h2>

Farever’s weapon system is easy to underestimate. With **25+ weapons** and **100+ skills**, weapons are not just stat upgrades. A new weapon can change your active skills, your healing pattern, your AoE, or your support role.

Your class unlocks its own skills as you level, but a large part of your active kit comes from your equipped weapon. A one-handed weapon may give a basic attack and a cooldown skill. A two-handed weapon can give a larger set, such as multiple actives plus a passive.

The first thing to check after equipping a new weapon is your skill bar. If the abilities changed, that is normal. If a green plus appears on a skill, open the skill screen before continuing; an early skill rank can matter more than a small gear stat bump.

Do not ignore the **Arsenal** slot either. Once extra weapons start dropping, you can place a weapon in the Arsenal slot and use one of that weapon’s skills as an extra tool. If the selected Arsenal skill is not the one you want, hold **Control** and left-click the weapon to choose a different skill.

Runes are another early upgrade layer. After your first dungeon and later chests, you can get runes that unlock bonuses for class skills. Right-click the rune in your inventory first, then pick the unlocked bonus on the skill screen.

<h2 id="mounts-glider">
  Mounts and glider
</h2>

The mount flow is one of the first places players can think the game is bugged. Claiming the mount does not automatically mean it is ready to use.

Use this order:

1. Press **Esc** and open the shop.
2. Claim the free launch items if they are available.
3. Press **Y** to open Collections.
4. Select the mount tab at the bottom.
5. Equip the mount.
6. Change your mount/dismount key in **Options → Hotkeys** if needed.

Later, you may also see movement vendors. A basic mount price of **1000 gold** and a glider price of **500 gold** appear early, so do not waste gold if you already claimed the free mount.

You can also chain movement with the glider. If you jump while moving and then trigger the glider, travel feels much smoother than running everywhere. In dungeons, though, do not expect the mount to work; plan around jumping, gliding, grapple points, and route timing.

<h2 id="inventory-salvage">
  Inventory, salvage, and potions
</h2>

Inventory fills up quickly in Farever because you pick up gear, materials, lost packages, runes, recipes, and crafting items before you know what most of them do. The early mistake is dropping items too soon.

Once you reach the village hub in the second map, start using the resource tools instead of treating everything as junk.

Do this first:

- use the **Spark Recycler** to turn old gear into crafting or upgrade materials
- buy **small pouches** from the guild merchant and place them into your bag slots
- each small pouch adds **6 inventory slots**
- use the guild merchant’s **bank** for training manuals, runes, materials, and gear you do not need right now
- right-click **lost packages** to turn them into completed packages
- hand completed packages to the guild merchant for **100 gold**
- keep early runes until you know which skill they enhance
- avoid spending upgrade materials too aggressively on low-level weapons

For crafting, the first practical job is **Alchemist**. It lets you craft minor healing potions early, which is useful if your class or weapon setup does not have easy self-healing. Vials can be bought from the guild merchant, and early healing potions can cover a large chunk of your starter HP.

This section is not trying to be a full crafting guide. The early goal is simpler: stop wasting gear, make space, store useful items, and get a basic healing option before you start harder fights.

<h2 id="dungeons">
  First dungeon
</h2>

The first dungeon has **5 orbs**, and it is very easy to treat the boss as the only objective. Do not rush straight to the exit after the fight. Check side platforms, vertical routes, and any path that looks like it loops away from the main line.

Before entering:

- clear inventory space
- upgrade available weapon skills
- use any useful runes you already have
- practice gliding before long gaps
- check side platforms for orbs
- watch for grapple points or vertical routes
- learn the boss shield timing before wasting cooldowns

The most common first-dungeon mistake is assuming every orb is on the main path. If the counter is still missing one, look backward from the boss route instead of only pushing forward.

<h2 id="underwater-exploration">
  Underwater exploration
</h2>

Farever does not use normal swimming. When you enter water, you walk underwater instead. That can feel strange at first, but some objectives and chests are built around it.

Remember these rules:

- there is no normal swimming movement
- you can walk under the water
- a blue breath bar limits how long you can stay under
- when the bar runs out, you start losing HP
- you can still double jump underwater
- self-healing can help you survive longer if you push too far

This is not a full exploration guide, but it is worth knowing before you skip underwater paths that may hide objectives or chests.

<h2 id="multiplayer">
  Multiplayer and parties
</h2>

Farever is online and instance-based. You can see random players in the world, but that does not mean every player is in the same copy of the map.

Quick notes:

- parties support **4 players**
- nearby players can be invited
- Steam friends can be invited
- map instances are small
- you may not naturally land in the same instance as a friend
- PvP is planned later, but the early flow is mainly PvE
- friend list, guilds, auction house, player trading, and deeper social tools are planned later rather than fully present now

If you want to play together, use the party menu or Steam invite. Do not rely on meeting randomly in the same world copy.

<h2 id="disconnecting">
  Why does Farever keep disconnecting?
</h2>

If Farever shows **lost connection to server**, **stuck connecting**, or repeated disconnects, do not immediately assume your PC is the problem. Launch-window server instability can look exactly like a local issue.

Try this order:

1. **Check whether your region is down.** If many players report the same region failing, wait before changing local settings.
2. **Restart the game after maintenance or a patch.** A clean reconnect often matters after server changes.
3. **Check Steam for a client update.** If the client is behind the server, reconnecting may fail.
4. **Try another region only as a test.** If another region loads, your original region may be the issue.
5. **Avoid starting dungeons during unstable windows.** A disconnect inside a dungeon can waste the run.
6. **Stop troubleshooting locally if every region is down.** At that point, there is no local fix.

If your character stays connected but skills feel delayed, treat it as latency or FPS troubleshooting instead.

<h2 id="fps-stutter">
  How do you fix FPS drops or stutter in Farever?
</h2>

Open-world areas can feel worse than dungeons because more players and effects are nearby. Test performance in a quiet area before deciding the whole game runs badly.

Try these steps:

1. **Test in a dungeon or quiet area.** If FPS improves, the problem is probably open-world density.
2. **Switch between borderless window and fullscreen.** Use whichever gives smoother frame pacing.
3. **Disable unnecessary overlays.** Turn off Discord overlay, recording overlays, and extra FPS counters while testing.
4. **Cap FPS externally if needed.** A stable 60 FPS can feel better than unstable higher FPS.
5. **Lower settings even if the art style looks simple.** Simple visuals do not guarantee perfect Early Access optimization.
6. **Retest after patches.** Performance may change quickly during Early Access.

If the game feels smooth in dungeons but drops near crowded hubs, avoid crowded areas when testing combat, recording footage, or tuning settings.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Farever?

Claim your free mount if available, equip it from Collections, check map completion, fill nearby Codeex entries, unlock obelisks, upgrade weapon skills, and avoid discarding old gear before you reach the recycler.

### What is the best beginner class in Farever?

Cleric/Priest is the safest early solo pick because it has self-healing. Rogue is better if you like fast movement, dodging, Shadow Step-style mobility, and control. Warrior is better if you want a direct melee route with heavier damage pressure. Mage is better if you want ranged magic and scepter-based skill swaps.

### How many classes are in Farever?

Farever currently starts with four classes: Warrior, Rogue, Mage, and Cleric/Priest.

### What is the level cap in Farever Early Access?

The current Early Access cap is level 20.

### How do you level in Farever?

Use map completion, Codeex milestones, nearby activities, chests, bosses, and dungeons. Codeex entries can reward early milestones such as 4 kills and 8 kills, while some counters continue toward 20.

### What is the Codeex in Farever?

The Codeex tracks discoveries and monster entries. If a monster shows a counter like 2/8, finish that entry before leaving the area unless you have a better route.

### Does Farever have a normal quest log?

Not in the usual MMO way. You should read NPC text, open the map, follow gray question marks, unlock obelisks, and use Codeex/map completion to guide your route.

### How do weapon skills work in Farever?

Weapons can provide active skills, and switching weapons can change your kit. One-handed weapons may give fewer skills, while larger weapons can give a bigger active/passive package. Because Farever has 25+ weapons and 100+ skills, weapon testing matters before final build rankings.

### What is the Arsenal slot in Farever?

The Arsenal slot lets you equip an extra weapon and use one of its skills as an additional tool. Hold Control and left-click the Arsenal weapon if you want to choose a different skill from it.

### How do runes work in Farever?

Runes unlock extra bonuses for class skills. Right-click the rune in your inventory first, then choose the unlocked bonus on the relevant skill screen.

### Should I upgrade low-level weapons?

Do not spend too many materials on low-level weapon enhancement unless the weapon effect is worth keeping. Early weapon skill upgrades are usually more important than tiny stat gains on a weapon you may replace soon.

### How do you get a mount in Farever?

Open the shop, claim the free reward if available, then equip the mount from Collections. If your mount key does nothing, the mount may not be equipped yet.

### Can you use a mount in dungeons?

The first dungeon disables mount use. Use gliding, jumping, grapple points, and dungeon movement tools instead.

### How many orbs are in the first dungeon?

The first dungeon has 5 orbs. If you leave with fewer, check side paths, vertical routes, grapple points, or early platforms.

### How do you get more inventory space in Farever?

Buy small pouches from the guild merchant and place them into your inventory bag slots. Each small pouch adds 6 slots. Use the bank for materials, runes, manuals, recipes, and gear you do not need immediately.

### What should I do with old gear in Farever?

Do not discard old gear once you can reach the village hub. Use the Spark Recycler to turn junk gear into useful crafting or upgrade materials.

### What are lost packages for in Farever?

Right-click lost packages to turn them into completed packages, then hand them to the guild merchant for 100 gold.

### What is the best first profession in Farever?

Alchemist is the safest early recommendation because it lets you craft minor healing potions. It is especially useful for Rogue, Mage, or any weapon setup without reliable self-healing.

### How do you fast travel in Farever?

Unlock obelisks in each area. Obelisks reveal the zone, set respawn points, and let you travel automatically to other unlocked obelisks.

### Can you swim in Farever?

There is no normal swimming. You walk underwater, watch the blue breath bar, and take damage if it runs out. You can still double jump underwater.

### Is Farever free to play?

No. Farever is buy-to-play.

### Is Farever pay to win?

Right now, the shop items you run into are cosmetics such as mounts, pets, gliders, and appearance items. There is no buy-power item in the early route.

### Does Farever have PvP?

PvP is planned later, but the current early flow is mainly PvE-focused.

### Can you play Farever solo?

You can handle much of the early game solo, but Farever is still online. You will see other players and can group for content.

### How do you play with friends in Farever?

Use Steam friend invites or invite nearby players through the party menu. Randomly meeting in the same instance may be unreliable.

### Does Farever have guilds?

A full guild system is not available yet. Friend list, guilds, auction house, and player trading are planned later.

### Why does Farever keep disconnecting?

Check region status, restart after patches, update through Steam, and avoid starting dungeons during unstable windows. If all regions are down, there is no local fix.

### Why is Farever stuttering or dropping FPS?

Open-world areas with more nearby players may run worse than dungeons or quiet areas. Test fullscreen vs borderless, disable overlays, cap FPS externally, and update after performance patches.
