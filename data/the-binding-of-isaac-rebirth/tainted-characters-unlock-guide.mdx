<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
To unlock **Tainted characters** in **The Binding of Isaac: Repentance**, the real prerequisite chain is longer than most players expect: unlock **Blue Womb**, defeat **Hush 3 times** to open the alternate path, get both knife pieces, defeat **Mother** in Corpse II, then use the **Strange Door** route to reach **Home**.

For the Home route, beat **Mom**, take the **Polaroid or Negative**, teleport out with **The Fool** or another teleport, open the **Strange Door**, pick up **Dad's Note**, climb the **Ascent**, then open the hidden closet at **Home**. The first Tainted unlock uses **Red Key** from Mom's bedroom chest. Second and later unlocks use **Cracked Key**, prepared by dropping a trinket in a boss room or treasure room before Dad's Note.

<h2 id="tainted-characters-unlock-guide">
  The Binding of Isaac Tainted characters unlock guide
</h2>

Tainted characters are not just “go to Home and open a door.”

That is the final part. The part most players miss is everything before it.

On a fresh or midgame file, I first need to unlock the route that even lets me reach Mother. Then I need to defeat Mother once. Only after that does the Strange Door route become available for Tainted character unlocks.

So I think of the route as three separate jobs:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Unlock the route:</strong> Blue Womb, Hush, and Repentance alternate path access.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Unlock the Strange Door:</strong> knife pieces, Corpse, and Mother.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Unlock the Tainted character:</strong> Mom, Polaroid or Negative, teleport, Dad's Note, Ascent, Home, and the red closet.
  </li>
</ul>

There are **17 Tainted characters**, and the unlock always matches the regular character I bring to Home. If I reach Home as Isaac, I unlock Tainted Isaac. If I reach Home as Bethany, I unlock Tainted Bethany. If I reach Home as Cain, I unlock Tainted Cain.

That means I choose the character before the run starts. I do not want to spend a long Home route unlocking the wrong Tainted version.

The most important split is this:

<strong>Mother unlocks the Strange Door. Hush unlocks the alternate path that lets me reach Mother.</strong>

If I have not beaten Hush 3 times, I should not be asking why the Mother route feels unavailable. If I have not beaten Mother once, I should not be asking why the Strange Door is missing.

This is why I do not send a new player straight to Dad's Note. The real first goal may be much earlier: clear Mom's Heart enough times, reach Blue Womb before 30 minutes, and start working on Hush kills.

<h2 id="blue-womb-hush">
  Gate 1: Blue Womb and Hush
</h2>

The first big gate is **Hush**.

Before I can route Hush, I need **Blue Womb** access. That means beating **Mom's Heart 10 times** first. After that, the Blue Womb door can appear when I beat **Mom's Heart** or **It Lives** before the **30-minute** timer. After **11 Mom's Heart kills**, Mom's Heart is permanently replaced by **It Lives**, but the timer rule stays the same for the Hush route.

This is already a real skill check. A slow run can miss the Blue Womb door even if the build is strong, so I do not play a Hush attempt like a normal full-clear run.

My Hush route priority is different from a normal clear:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>I take damage and tears upgrades over slow greed.</li>
  <li>I do not full-clear every floor if the timer is the problem.</li>
  <li>I buy key survival items, but I do not waste minutes shopping without a reason.</li>
  <li>I value mapping, speed, flight, and burst damage more than usual.</li>
  <li>I accept that Hush is a route gate, not just an optional boss.</li>
</ul>

After **3 Hush kills**, the Repentance alternate path doors become the next target. Until then, Downpour, Mines, Mausoleum, and Corpse planning is premature.

<h2 id="mother-route">
  Gate 2: alternate path, knife pieces, and Mother
</h2>

After Hush 3 times, I start the **Mother** route.

This is where I need the two knife pieces:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Knife Piece 1:</strong> Downpour II or Dross II, using the mirror-world section.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Knife Piece 2:</strong> Mines II or Ashpit II, using the chase section.
  </li>
</ul>

With both pieces, I continue to **Mausoleum II** or **Gehenna II** and use the completed knife on the red door after Mom. That sends the run toward **Corpse**, ending with **Mother** in Corpse II.

<h2 id="home-route">
  Gate 3: Home route after Mother
</h2>

Once Mother has been defeated once, the Home route becomes available.

The Home route starts in the Mom floor section, not in the alt path. I go to **Depths II**, **Necropolis II**, or **Dank Depths II**, beat Mom, take the **Polaroid** or **Negative**, then teleport out and open the **Strange Door**.

The clean route differs depending on whether this is the first Tainted unlock or a repeat unlock.

This table is the part I follow during the actual run. Everything before it is prerequisite work. Everything after it is Beast-route cleanup.

<h2 id="no-teleport-marked-skull">
  No teleport item? Find the marked skull for The Fool
</h2>

This is the practical detail that saves a lot of failed Home attempts.

To open the Strange Door, I need to leave Mom's boss room after taking the Polaroid or Negative. If I already have a teleport card, I am fine. If I do not, I look for the **marked skull** on Depths II, Necropolis II, or Dank Depths II.

Bombing that skull drops **0 - The Fool**, which lets me teleport out after Mom.

My rule is:

<strong>Before I fight Mom on a Home attempt, I find the marked skull or confirm I already have another teleport.</strong>

If I cannot teleport out, I do not have a clean Strange Door route. I may still continue the run, but I stop treating it as a Tainted unlock attempt.

<h2 id="red-key-vs-cracked-key">
  Red Key vs Cracked Key: first unlock and repeat unlocks
</h2>

This is where I split the route into two cases.

For my **first** Tainted unlock, I do not need to prepare Cracked Key. When I reach Home, I open Mom's bedroom chest and take **Red Key**. Then I use Red Key on the hidden red closet door.

For **second and later** Tainted unlocks, I prepare **Cracked Key** before Dad's Note. I drop a trinket in a boss room or treasure room, then pick up Dad's Note. During Ascent, that dropped trinket can become Cracked Key.

The mistake I avoid is mixing the two cases. If it is my first Home route, I use Red Key. If I already used that first Red Key unlock, I prepare Cracked Key before Dad's Note.

<h2 id="dads-note-ascent">
  Dad's Note and Ascent
</h2>

**Dad's Note** is the point where the route reverses.

Once I pick it up, the run begins **Ascent** and starts climbing back through earlier floors toward Home. At this point, I stop adding random side goals. I am no longer asking whether I can squeeze one more room out of the floor. I am asking whether I can reach Home without throwing away the unlock.

The hidden closet is the actual unlock check. I use **Red Key** or **Cracked Key** on the closet entrance, then touch the Tainted version of my current character.

<h2 id="dogma-beast">
  Dogma and Beast are the follow-up, not the unlock button
</h2>

After the closet, I can continue into **Dogma**, then the Horsemen and **Beast** sequence.

I only continue if the build can handle it. If the run is strong, I want the mark. If the run is barely alive, I at least make sure the Tainted unlock is already secured before I take the final risk.

  <li>If I have a strong defensive card, rune, or active item, I save it for the final chain.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="which-character-first">
  Which Tainted character should I unlock first?
</h2>

For the first Tainted unlock, I pick based on route reliability, not hype.

The unlock matches the regular character I bring to Home, so I choose a regular character I can actually route. If I can consistently reach Home as Isaac, Cain, Azazel, Bethany, Lilith, or another favorite, that is a better first choice than forcing a character I barely understand.

My rule is:

<strong>First unlock: choose the character I can bring to Home safely. Later unlocks: choose the Tainted character I actually want to play.</strong>

That keeps the first clear from becoming a route test, character test, and final boss test all at once.

<h2 id="soul-stones-later">
  Soul Stones are later, not the first Home goal
</h2>

Tainted characters also lead into later unlock planning, especially **Soul Stones**.

I do not make Soul Stones the first Home goal. I first prove I can unlock Tainted characters cleanly. Later, I decide which Tainted characters are worth taking through **Boss Rush** and **Hush** for Soul Stone unlocks.

<h2 id="faq">
  The Binding of Isaac Tainted characters FAQ
</h2>

### Why are the alternate path doors not showing up?

You need to defeat Hush 3 times to unlock the Repentance alternate path doors. If Downpour, Mines, Mausoleum, or Corpse access is missing, check Hush progress first.

### Why is the Strange Door not showing up?

The Strange Door appears only after Mother has been defeated once on the file. Hush unlocks the alternate path; Mother unlocks the Strange Door.

### How do I leave Mom's boss room for the Strange Door?

Use The Fool or another teleport. On Depths II, Necropolis II, or Dank Depths II, find the marked skull before Mom, bomb it, and keep the Fool card for after the fight.

### Do I need Cracked Key for the first Tainted unlock?

No. The first Home route gives Red Key from Mom's bedroom chest. Use that Red Key on the hidden closet door.

### When do I need Cracked Key?

Use Cracked Key for second and later Tainted unlocks. Drop a trinket in a boss room or treasure room before Dad's Note so it can turn into Cracked Key during Ascent.

### Does Dogma unlock Tainted characters?

No. The Tainted unlock comes from opening the hidden closet at Home. Dogma and Beast are for the final route and completion mark.

### Which Tainted character should I unlock first?

Choose the regular character you can bring to Home most consistently. The first unlock should prove the route, not force the hardest target.

### Are Soul Stones part of the first Tainted unlock?

No. Soul Stones are later Tainted character goals tied to Boss Rush and Hush progress. I treat them as later pool planning.
