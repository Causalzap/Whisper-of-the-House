<h2 id="binding-of-isaac-unlock-order">
  The Binding of Isaac Rebirth unlock order guide
</h2>

Isaac has a dangerous kind of progression.

The game keeps unlocking things, but not every unlock helps the next run equally. Some rewards add strong cards, runes, shop upgrades, or boss-route tools. Others add noise, niche items, or late goals that only matter once I am already deep into completion.

So I do not ask:

<strong>What can I unlock next?</strong>

I ask:

<strong>What unlock makes future runs stronger?</strong>

That one question changes the whole route. It keeps me from chasing completion marks before my run quality is good enough, and it keeps me from filling the item, rune, card, and trinket pools with rewards I do not understand yet.

<h2 id="mom-first">
  Step 1: stabilize Mom before chasing everything
</h2>

The first real goal is not Dead God. It is not Tainted characters. It is not perfect Hard marks.

The first real goal is reaching and beating **Mom** often enough that the early floor loop stops feeling random.

Before I worry about advanced unlocks, I want to understand:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>when to open the item room,</li>
  <li>when a shop is worth a key,</li>
  <li>when one bomb should be saved for a tinted rock or secret room,</li>
  <li>when a Devil Deal is too expensive,</li>
  <li>when a boss fight is safer than clearing more rooms,</li>
  <li>and why a run died before the death screen.</li>
</ul>

If that loop still feels unstable, use the <Link href="/the-binding-of-isaac-rebirth/beginner-guide/">Beginner Guide</Link> before forcing a long unlock plan.

<h2 id="challenges">
  Step 2: do high-value challenges early
</h2>

I like early challenges because the right reward changes many future runs.

A challenge reward is not just one mark. It can add a rune, card, item, or trinket that appears later when another character needs help. That is why I prioritize challenge rewards that improve future routing, defense, damage, or resource swings.

<h2 id="donation-machines">
  Step 3: separate the two donation machines
</h2>

This is one of the easiest places to confuse unlock order.

There are two different long-term donation tracks:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Regular shop Donation Machine:</strong> the machine that appears in shops and upgrades normal shop progression.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Greed Donation Machine:</strong> the machine that appears after Ultra Greed in Greed or Greedier mode.
  </li>
</ul>

The numbers do not mean the same thing. When I say “push to 600 first,” I mean the regular shop Donation Machine. When I say “879 for The Lost's Holy Mantle,” I mean the Greed Donation Machine.

<h2 id="boss-route-unlocks">
  Step 4: boss route unlocks and efficient mark stacking
</h2>

Once Mom and the Womb route are stable, I start caring about boss routes.

The important route gates are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Mom's Heart / It Lives:</strong> opens the deeper structure of the file and starts making Womb clears meaningful.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Cathedral and Sheol:</strong> split the route toward Isaac / ??? and Satan / The Lamb progress.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Boss Rush:</strong> appears when Mom is defeated within the 20-minute window.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Hush:</strong> becomes a timed route after Mom's Heart / It Lives and later unlocks more progression.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Delirium:</strong> is reached through The Void, with a guaranteed entrance after Hush.
  </li>
</ul>

The efficiency trick I keep in mind is the strong-run chain:

<strong>Boss Rush → Hush → Delirium.</strong>

If a run is powerful and fast enough, I can beat Mom within 20 minutes for Boss Rush, continue to beat Mom's Heart or It Lives within 30 minutes for Hush, then use the guaranteed Void entrance after Hush to attempt Delirium. I do not force this chain on weak runs, but when a run is clearly broken, this is one of the best ways to stack marks.

That is the difference between casual progression and efficient progression. I do not need every run to be a triple-mark run. I need to recognize the run that can become one.

<h2 id="character-unlocks">
  Step 5: unlock characters that solve problems
</h2>

I do not unlock characters just to fill the select screen.

A new character matters when they solve a real problem:

    <strong>Magdalene:</strong> useful when health mistakes are ending runs too early.

    <strong>Lazarus:</strong> useful when one bad mistake keeps ruining otherwise good starts.

  <li>
    <strong>Azazel:</strong> useful when I need easier clears, boss damage, flight, and route momentum.

  <li>
    <strong>Cain:</strong> useful after I understand coins, keys, shops, and pickup value.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Judas and Eve:</strong> useful later, after low-health play stops feeling like panic.
  </li>

<h2 id="repentance-route">
  Step 6: Repentance route unlocks

Repentance adds excellent long-term goals, but I do not rush them before the base route works.

The big Repentance progression chain eventually includes:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>alternate path access,</li>
  <li>Knife Piece 1 and Knife Piece 2,</li>

  <li>Home, Dogma, and Beast,</li>
  <li>Tainted character unlocks,</li>
  <li>and later Soul Stone / reverse card planning.</li>

This is why I do not send a new player straight to Tainted characters. That route has several gates before the actual Home closet unlock. If that is the current problem, use the <Link href="/the-binding-of-isaac-rebirth/tainted-characters-unlock-guide/">Tainted Characters Unlock Guide</Link> instead of trying to solve it from this page.

<h2 id="soul-stones-reverse-cards">
  Step 7: Soul Stones and reverse cards are selective cleanup
</h2>

Soul Stones and reverse cards can be powerful, but I do not blindly unlock all of them first.

The reason is pool quality. Every new card, rune, trinket, or item changes what can appear later. Some additions are clearly useful. Others are niche, confusing, or only valuable once I understand the character that unlocks them.

My rule is:

<strong>Unlock the Soul Stones and reverse cards I actually want first, then finish the rest when I am closer to full completion.</strong>

<h2 id="what-i-delay">
  Unlocks I delay on purpose
</h2>

Delaying an unlock is not the same as ignoring it.

I delay unlocks when they do one of these things:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>add a reward I do not understand yet,</li>
  <li>require a route I cannot reach consistently,</li>
  <li>push me into a character I am not ready to play,</li>
  <li>make the item, card, rune, or trinket pool noisier before it becomes stronger,</li>
  <li>or turn the game into checklist work before the run itself is fun.</li>
</ul>

This is especially true for late Repentance cleanup. I would rather unlock one useful thing and feel it in future runs than unlock five random things and forget what changed.

<h2 id="faq">
  The Binding of Isaac unlock order FAQ
</h2>

### What should I unlock first in The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth?

Start with stable Mom clears, then high-value challenge rewards, then regular shop Donation Machine progress toward 600. After that, move into boss route unlocks, practical character unlocks, Repentance routes, and selective pool cleanup.

### Should I clear every challenge immediately?

No. I start with high-value challenge rewards like Jera, Perthro, Algiz, Credit Card, Death's Touch, Tech.5, and later Repentance rewards. I skip miserable challenges until the file is stronger.

### Is the regular Donation Machine the same as the Greed Donation Machine?

No. The regular shop Donation Machine and the Greed Donation Machine are separate tracks with different milestones. The regular machine controls shop-related unlocks like 600 and 999, while the Greed machine has milestones like Cain's Paper Clip, The Lost's Holy Mantle, Generosity, and Keeper.

### Should I donate to 600 or 999 first?

I prioritize 600 on the regular shop Donation Machine because it upgrades shops for future runs. I treat 999 as a later goal for Stop Watch.

### What happens if I donate the 1000th coin to the regular Donation Machine?

The regular Donation Machine explodes and resets to 0. I avoid accidental 1000th donations after reaching 999.

### Which Greed Donation Machine milestones matter most?

The important ones I watch are 68 for Cain's Paper Clip, 879 for The Lost's Holy Mantle, 999 for Generosity, and 1000 for Keeper.

### When should I try Boss Rush, Hush, and Delirium together?

Only on a strong and fast run. If I can beat Mom within 20 minutes, continue to Hush within the 30-minute route, and the run is still strong, Hush gives a guaranteed Void entrance for Delirium.

### Should I unlock every Soul Stone and reverse card immediately?

No. I unlock the ones I want first, then clean up the rest later. Selective pool management is better than adding rewards I do not understand yet.

### When should I start Repentance route unlocks?

Start Repentance route planning after the base route feels stable. Hush, Mother, Strange Door, Dad's Note, Home, Beast, and Tainted characters are not first-night goals.

### What is the biggest unlock order mistake?

The biggest mistake is treating the checklist as the strategy. A good unlock route improves future runs first and saves completion cleanup for later.
