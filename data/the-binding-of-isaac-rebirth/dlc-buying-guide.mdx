<h2 id="which-dlc-should-you-buy">
  Which Binding of Isaac DLC should you buy?
</h2>

The confusing part of buying **The Binding of Isaac: Rebirth** is that the DLC names look simple, but the purchase path is not.

A new player sees **Rebirth**, **Afterbirth**, **Afterbirth+**, **Repentance**, and **Repentance+**. Then Steam discussions, old guides, and sale pages all answer the question differently because they are solving different problems.

I split the decision into three questions:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Do I know I like Isaac?</strong> If not, Rebirth alone or a deep bundle sale is safer than a full-price impulse buy.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Is the Complete Bundle cheap right now?</strong> If it is near the $5-$10 sale range, I usually buy the whole thing.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Am I ready for Repentance difficulty?</strong> Repentance is the modern version, but it can be harsher than earlier Isaac.
  </li>
</ul>

This guide is not trying to sell every player the largest package at any price. It is trying to stop the common mistake: buying one middle DLC, realizing another DLC depends on it, then going back to Steam confused.

<h2 id="price-anchor">
  Price anchor: what counts as a good deal?
</h2>

The best buying advice depends on price.

The useful anchor is that the Complete Bundle's individual total is about **$50.96** on Steam. During the 2026 Steam Summer Sale, that same full bundle dropped to about **$5.06-$5.09**, roughly **90% off**.

That changes the decision completely.

My personal rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Under $10:</strong> I would buy the Complete Bundle if I expect to play more than a few runs.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>$10-$25:</strong> I would still consider it if I already know I like Isaac.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Near full price:</strong> I would buy Rebirth first, use a wishlist, or wait for the next major sale.
  </li>
</ul>

The base game alone is cheaper than the full individual bundle price, but the full package can become a much better value during major sales. That is why I do not answer “Rebirth or bundle?” without looking at the current Steam cart.

The bundle is best when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

  <li>the discount is deep,</li>

  <li>I do not want to manage DLC dependency confusion later.</li>

If I already own part of the game, I do not blindly rebuy everything. I open the bundle while logged into Steam, add it to the cart, and check whether Steam is charging only for the missing content. Steam's complete-the-set bundle system is designed for that, but the final cart is what matters.

<h2 id="when-to-wait">
  When I would wait instead of buying all DLC
</h2>

I would not buy every DLC immediately if the problem is not actually a DLC problem.

I wait when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

  <li>I am buying only because Repentance+ online sounds interesting but I do not own the required paid chain.</li>
</ul>

That is the anti-impulse part of my buying advice. DLC adds more game, more routes, more items, more bosses, and more decisions. It does not make Isaac stop being Isaac.

<h2 id="repentance">
  Should you buy Repentance?
</h2>

I would buy **Repentance** long term, especially during a deep sale.

The reason is content depth. Repentance adds **130+ new items**, pushes the game to **700+ total items**, adds a full alternate path, **100+ new enemies**, **25+ new bosses**, **2 new playable characters**, **5 new challenges**, **100+ achievements**, and **5,000+ room designs**.

That is not a small add-on. It is the version most current players and many current guides assume.

But I would not describe Repentance as “just more value” without the warning. It is also harder, and some players genuinely prefer the older Afterbirth+ rhythm. If a new player already feels crushed by basic rooms, Repentance can make the game feel bigger and harsher at the same time.

My balanced recommendation is:

<strong>Buy Repentance during a strong sale if you want the modern full game, but do not use Repentance as a shortcut around learning the base loop.</strong>

<h2 id="repentance-plus">
  What is Repentance+?
</h2>

**Repentance+** is the free online-focused layer.

It adds online-focused features such as Quick Match, Public Match, Friend Match, online Daily Run, online achievements and completion markers, communication tools, and balance changes.

The buying decision is simple:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If I already have the paid chain through Repentance, I install Repentance+.</li>
  <li>If I only own Rebirth, Repentance+ is not the missing purchase that gives me the full game.</li>
  <li>If I want online co-op, I plan for the full chain rather than expecting the free update to stand alone.</li>
</ul>

Repentance+ is useful, but it belongs at the end of the purchase path.

<h2 id="complete-the-set">
  What if I already own Rebirth or some DLC?
</h2>

If I already own part of Isaac on Steam, I do not manually guess the cheapest path.

I open the **Complete Bundle** while logged into the account that owns the game, then check the cart. Steam's newer bundle system can support complete-the-set pricing, where I only pay for the missing items instead of rebuying what I already own.

My checkout rule:

<strong>If the cart looks wrong, stop before paying.</strong>

I check:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>whether Steam recognizes the items I already own,</li>
  <li>whether the cart is charging only for missing content,</li>
  <li>whether Repentance appears as unavailable because I am missing earlier DLC,</li>
  <li>whether the bundle price is actually better than buying the missing DLC steps separately.</li>
</ul>

This is especially important during sales because the best price can depend on what is already in my library.

<h2 id="item-descriptions">
  Do item descriptions change the DLC decision?
</h2>

Item descriptions help, but they do not change the buying decision by themselves.

They solve the “what does this item do?” problem. That matters a lot in a game with hundreds of items. But they do not solve routing, damage, health, shops, Devil Deals, unlock order, or whether I should buy DLC at full price.

My rule is:

<strong>Use descriptions to reduce item confusion. Use the Beginner Guide to learn the decisions descriptions cannot make for you.</strong>

<h2 id="faq">
  The Binding of Isaac DLC buying FAQ
</h2>

### Should I buy The Binding of Isaac Rebirth or the Complete Bundle?

If the Complete Bundle is deeply discounted, I would usually buy the bundle. If the bundle is near full price and you are unsure whether you like Isaac, buy Rebirth first or wait for a sale.

### What price is a good deal for the Complete Bundle?

The individual total is about $50.96, while the 2026 Summer Sale dropped the bundle to about $5.06-$5.09. My personal easy-buy range is under $10 if you expect to play.

### Does the Complete Bundle include Repentance?

Yes. The Complete Bundle includes Rebirth, Afterbirth, Afterbirth+, and Repentance. Repentance+ is the free online update layer after the paid chain.

### Can I buy Repentance alone?

Repentance depends on the earlier paid chain. If Steam does not show a clean standalone purchase for your account, use the Complete Bundle or cart page while logged in and check which missing items Steam is charging for.

### What if I already own Rebirth?

Open the Complete Bundle while logged into Steam and check the cart. Steam complete-the-set bundles can charge only for missing items, but I still verify the final cart before paying.

### Is Repentance worth it?

Yes if you want the modern full Isaac route. It adds a huge amount of content, but it is also harder and more polarizing than earlier versions.

### Should a brand-new player start with Repentance?

I would use Repentance if it came in a cheap bundle, but I would still learn the game in layers. Do not rush alternate paths, Tainted characters, or deep unlock routing on the first night.

### Is Repentance+ free?

Yes, Repentance+ is a free online-focused update layer. It is useful after the paid chain is in place.

### Do I need Repentance+ for online co-op?

Plan around Repentance+ if online play is the goal, but remember it is the online layer on top of the paid DLC chain, not a standalone replacement for the earlier expansions.

### Why does the game still feel confusing after buying all DLC?

Because DLC adds more content, not automatic understanding. Use item descriptions for item effects, then use a beginner route for floor, shop, health, boss, and unlock decisions.

### Should I wait for a sale?

Yes if the bundle is near full price and you are not sure you like Isaac. Major Steam sales can change the value calculation completely.

### Is this DLC guide a full gameplay guide?

No. This page is for buying decisions. Once the version is solved, switch to beginner routing, unlock order, character choice, or Tainted character guides.
