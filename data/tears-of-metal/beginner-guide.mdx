My first Tears of Metal Campaigns failed because I added one route too many,
crossed another Threat threshold, spent the last useful Coins on a Chest, and
reached the next Captain or Gilles without enough health to survive one mistake.

My cleanest Act 1 clear used one Emblem for a repeatable Wallace damage loop, one
detour for health or economy, and then the Great Gates before the next reward became
less valuable than the added risk.

### The stop rules I use

I turn toward the Great Gates when any one of these becomes true:

- health is below half and no healing stop is visible;
- the next fight crosses another Threat marker;
- my main attack already clears Captains during one safe opening;
- important Commanders are dead or repeatedly entering danger;
- the next reward does not repair damage, survival, economy, or army strength.

One Wallace run had already produced roughly **14,000 damage** and a best combo
chain of **75**. I still took another branch, entered the Monastery section with
only **7 HP**, and died to a Pikeman Captain.

That run did not need more damage. It needed a shorter route.

<h2 id="threat-level">
  How Threat Level works and when to stop exploring
</h2>

The Threat tooltip confirms two rules:

- Threat increases after battles and each failed objective.
- Every visible threshold increases battle difficulty.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/tears-of-metal/tears-of-metal-threat-threshold-tooltip.webp"
    alt="Tears of Metal Threat Level tooltip explaining that battles and failed objectives increase difficulty"
    width={1024}
    height={510}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    I use each visible marker as a route checkpoint. The tooltip confirms the
    difficulty increase without displaying an exact enemy-stat percentage.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

I treat the meter as a route signal:

- **Early:** take the Emblem that gives the build a usable trigger.
- **Middle:** add a node only when it repairs a specific weakness.
- **High:** route toward healing or the boss.
- **Maximum:** Lu Bu can appear, so I do not reach it deliberately on a first clear.

In my Early Access launch-build run, defeating Lu Bu reduced Threat by only
**one level**. That result is too small to justify filling the meter just to fight
him. I treat Lu Bu as a recovery opportunity for a strong run that already went
long, not as a first-clear farming plan.

A failed timed objective also raises Threat. I only begin one when it does not
conflict with a Commander rescue, active Beacon, or confirmed healing stop. Merchant,
shrine, and event nodes are not treated as guaranteed increases unless the meter
actually moves.

<h2 id="block-parry-dodge">
  Block, Parry, or Dodge: the defensive routine I use
</h2>

I Block short, readable attack strings and release before the Block bar breaks.
Against a guard-breaking Captain attack, I either time the parry or leave the lane;
I do not force the timing when the camera is crowded.

I save Dodge for:

- large ground attacks;
- charge lanes;
- the moment Block Stamina is close to breaking;
- or a crowded situation where I no longer have safe positioning.

Against ordinary soldiers, constant Dodging wastes the escape I need for the
Captain. Against Gilles, leaving one opening early is safer than finishing a long
combo with no defensive option ready.

<h2 id="target-priority">
  Target priority: emergency rescue first, then structures and Captains
</h2>

A Commander already in immediate danger overrides the normal order. I remove the
one threat blocking the rescue, save the Ally, and return to the objective.

When no emergency rescue is active, I use this order:

1. Beacon or enemy-buff structure;
2. Archer Tower or permanent ranged structure;
3. Captain or shielded elite;
4. active objective;
5. remaining soldiers;
6. Coins, Chicken, and Chests before leaving.

### Destroy the Beacon before spending Specials

Beacons strengthen the fight around them. I move around the main group, destroy the
Beacon, and only then spend Specials and Ally Commands on the Captain.

<h2 id="healing-vs-chest">
  Healing Font vs Chest: what to spend Coins on
</h2>

Health does not refill automatically after an ordinary battle. Before leaving, I
sweep the area for Chicken, Coins, Chests, and unused healing.

The early values I encountered included:

- a basic Chest costing **75 Coins**;
- a Healing Font costing **100 Coins**;
- that Font moving displayed health from **6 HP to 27 HP**;
- a later Chest costing **200 Coins**;
- and a **+30% Strike Damage** reward from that 200-Coin Chest.

### I buy healing when

- health is near one quarter or lower;
- a Captain, Tower, unknown event, or boss is next;
- and one missed attack would end the Campaign.

At **6 HP**, I buy the Font. At moderate health with Chicken nearby and another
recovery option ahead, I keep the Coins.

### I open a Chest when

- the build still lacks an effect on the action I use most;
- the reward improves Captain or boss damage;
- and healing money remains.

The **200-Coin Chest** was worth buying when it added **+30% Strike Damage** to a
Strike-centered run. I skip it when the combo already works and health is the real
weakness.

### I save for a merchant when

- it remains connected to the route;
- I can arrive with enough Coins;
- and the fights before it are safe.

Once the path closes, I spend the reserve on a useful Chest or healing. Unused Coins
do not move into the Village after death.

Chicken is free but small. I use it to repair moderate damage and leave it untouched
at full health. I do not rely on it when one Captain hit can already end the run.

<h2 id="starter-build">
  Simple Wallace starter builds for Act 1
</h2>

For a first clear, I give one action a setup, a payoff, and enough safety to finish
the combo. Advanced hero and Clan combinations stay on the full Builds page.

### Safe Wallace Vortex build

My safer route is:

- **Heavy:** Crusader Vortex Heavy;
- **support:** one Block, healing, or Block Stamina effect;
- **army:** Spearmen for protected targets and Archers for damage while I reposition.

In the launch-build Emblem description, Vortex Heavy deals **15 Smite Damage**, and
its Heavy Finisher creates a Vortex dealing another **15 Smite Damage**. The Vortex
attracts and briefly stuns Fodders, which gives me a cleaner Captain opening.

My sequence is:

1. Block or Dodge the Captain's first attack;
2. send Spearmen toward the protected target;
3. finish Heavy to create the Vortex;
4. let Archers attack while I leave the counterattack;
5. repeat only after the next safe opening.

I use this setup when survival and crowd control matter more than maximum damage.

### Damage-focused Wallace Ravager build

When Block and Dodge timing already feels reliable, I use Ravager as the damage
setup.

In the launch-build description, Heated Strike applies **15 Delayed Heat**, and its
Strike Finisher applies **30 Delayed Heat in a wave**. I then look for a Heavy
explosion or another payoff that triggers the stored damage while the enemies are
grouped.

One working Strike-to-Heavy loop is more valuable than four unrelated effects. I
stop once it damages Captains reliably and has a safe exit; another node must fix a
specific weakness.

<h2 id="great-gates">
  Great Gates and Gilles preparation checklist
</h2>

I enter the Great Gates when the run can survive one mistake, not when every slot
looks perfect.

Before selecting the node, I confirm:

- health is high enough to survive one missed Dodge or broken Block;
- my main attack deals reliable boss damage;
- at least one useful Commander or Companion is alive;
- the current battlefield has been swept for Chicken, Coins, and rewards;
- remaining Coins have a clear purpose;
- and my Special is available or close to ready.

3. use a short Strike or Ally Command to test the opening;

5. let Archers continue damaging him while I reposition;
6. save the Special for his stronger second form when phase one is already stable.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tears of Metal first Campaign FAQ
</h2>

### Does health refill after every battle?

No. I check for Chicken, Coins, Chests, and paid healing before leaving. Once the
countdown begins, I do not assume I can return later.

### Does a failed objective increase Threat?

Yes. The tooltip explicitly includes failed objectives. Because the exact amount
is not displayed, I treat an optional timed event as a real route cost.

### Can Chicken replace a Healing Font?

Not at critical health. Chicken is useful cleanup healing, but I still buy a Font
when one Captain attack can end the run, and I avoid consuming Chicken at full
health.

### Do I need to kill every enemy before leaving?

Not always. On several clear-area stages, defeating the important elites completed
the objective while distant Fodders remained. I follow the objective marker and
completion prompt rather than hunting every soldier automatically.

### What happens at maximum Threat?

Lu Bu can appear. In my launch-build run, beating him reduced Threat by one level
rather than resetting the full meter, so I do not deliberately reach maximum Threat
during a first-clear attempt.

### What carries over after a failed Campaign?

Coins and run-specific Emblems do not become permanent Village stock. Permanent
currencies already awarded and upgrades already purchased remain available. Some
hero, item, and content unlocks still require their own conditions, so I check the
Village menu before assuming a failed run completed them.
