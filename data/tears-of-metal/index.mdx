<h2 id="diagnose-the-failure">
  Diagnose why the Campaign failed
</h2>

<GuideTable

The rule connecting every page is:

> Stop treating every failure as a damage problem.

A run can end because the route added too much Threat, healing Coins were spent too
early, the build lacks control, the army lost key Commanders, or a boss punish
started before recovery.

<h2 id="core-rules">
  Four rules that connect the guides
</h2>

### Route: stop after the build can clear Captains

Plan the line to the Great Gates before selecting the first node. Once one attack
loop works, health is falling, or the next battle crosses another Threat threshold,
stop adding optional nodes.

### Build: use setup, payoff, control, and a safe exit

A strong Emblem does not make a complete build by itself. Wallace and Brienne both
need a repeatable setup, a damage payoff, a way to control pressure, and a way to
leave before the counterattack.

### Village: upgrade the diagnosed bottleneck

A direct hero failure points toward a hero stat. Weak Ally Commands point toward
the Barracks. Poor merchant or Shrine access points toward Market or Cartographer.
Route greed often means buying nothing and changing the next path.

### Co-op and bosses: confirm before committing

Co-op is shared routing with separate survival resources. Check both health bars
and both Coin balances before confirming.

Against Gilles, wait for recovery before the long punish. Against Iseult, confirm
the real target and safe lane before spending burst.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tears of Metal guide FAQ
</h2>

### Where should a new player start?

Start with the Beginner Guide. It covers the Act 1 route, Threat, healing, target
priority, Wallace’s first usable build, and Gilles preparation.

### Does health refill after every battle?

No. Sweep the battlefield for Chicken and unused recovery, then decide whether the
next Healing Font is worth the Coins.

### What carries over after a failed Campaign?

Permanent hero and Barracks upgrades, Village investments, Monikers, and permanent
currencies remain. Coins and the current Emblem build do not carry over.

### Is Tears of Metal co-op?

Yes. The game supports solo play and co-op for up to four players. The detailed
Host, loot, Coin, healing, and revive rules currently documented here come from a
tested two-player Campaign.

### What should I do after my first failed Campaign?

Identify whether the run failed because of health, Block Stamina, army output,
route Threat, missing healing, or boss execution. Then open the matching Beginner,
Village, Build, Co-op, or Boss guide.

### Should I always buy another permanent upgrade after dying?

No. When the build works and the Campaign ends because of route greed, maximum
Threat, or unused healing Coins, the next improvement is a decision change rather
than another stat.
