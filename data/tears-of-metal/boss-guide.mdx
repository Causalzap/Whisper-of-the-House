<h2 id="boss-ready-check">
  My boss-ready check before selecting the node
</h2>

I do not need a perfect build. I need enough health to survive one mistake and one
short damage loop that works against a single target.

Before entering either boss node, I check:

- health can survive one failed Dodge or broken Block;
- Strike or Heavy has one reliable boss-damage payoff;
- at least one useful Commander or Companion is still alive;
- the current battlefield has been swept for Chicken, Coins, and rewards;
- remaining Coins are assigned to healing or a specific upgrade;
- Special and the most useful Ally Command are ready or close to ready.

For Gilles, I want a short test attack plus one strong Finisher.

For Iseult, I want a quick Strike for clone checks, direct boss damage, movement
support, and enough area pressure to remove nearby soldiers without depending on
their deaths for all of my damage.

<h3 id="gilles-first-form">
  Gilles first form: test the opening before committing
</h3>

I begin defensively and watch one complete attack string.

My opening loop is:

1. stay outside the first swing;
2. Block the readable follow-up while Stamina is healthy;
3. leave before the Block bar breaks;
4. wait until Gilles stops moving;
5. use one short Strike or Ally Command to confirm the recovery;
6. complete one Heavy, Finisher, or direct combo;
7. leave before the next string begins.

A high-Threat Gilles sequence removed roughly half of my health in three hits.
That changed the fight from trading damage into taking one confirmed punish at a
time.

### When I Block and when I move

I Block a short normal swing when I can see the entire attack and still have enough
Block Stamina for the follow-up.

I move or Dodge when:

- the swing string is continuing longer than my Block bar can support;
- the weapon is raised for the overhead slam;
- another enemy is attacking from outside the camera;
- or I am already recovering from a slow Heavy animation.

I do not force a Block through a large ground impact. Moving out preserves both
health and Block Stamina for the next direct exchange.

### Do not let lock-on hide the battlefield

I do not hold lock-on so tightly that Gilles fills the entire camera.

When he crosses the arena, I briefly release or adjust the camera, relocate the
nearest ranged threat, and rebuild the angle from open ground.

That matters most when:

- an Archer is firing from behind Gilles;
- a Commander is in danger;
- Chicken or a recovery interaction is near the arena edge;
- or the overhead weapon animation is partially outside the frame.

<h3 id="gilles-overhead-slam">
  Overhead slam: the raised weapon is the warning
</h3>

The raised weapon is not the attack window. It is the signal to leave.

    return for one complete punish.

My response is:

1. stop the current combo;
2. move outside the impact area;
3. keep Gilles visible without circling underneath him;
4. wait for the weapon to land;
5. wait through the recovery;
6. return with one Wallace Heavy, Brienne combo, Special, or Ally Command sequence.

I attempt a second string only when Gilles is clearly staggered or held by control.
A normal slam recovery gives me one reliable punish, not permission to empty every
animation into him.

<h3 id="gilles-army">
  How I use the army against Gilles
</h3>

I preserve Command Energy before entering the Great Gates.

Spearmen pressure protected targets during a confirmed opening. Archers continue
dealing damage while I retreat from the counterattack.

My usual order is:

1. wait for the boss string to finish;
2. send Spearmen;
3. complete one personal damage sequence;
4. send Archers while leaving;
5. rescue a valuable Commander during the next reset.

I do not cross an active slam to rescue a low-value unit.

I rescue an Archer, Companion, or Commander carrying an important Ally Emblem when
I can first move Gilles away from the rescue position.

I use the transformation itself instead of trying to time an exact health

My second-form plan is:

1. stop attacking during the transformation;
2. create distance before the first empowered sequence;
3. keep enough room to leave a larger impact area if the empowered attack expands beyond its first-form range;
4. watch which part of the familiar attack string has changed;
5. send the army when the first recovery begins;
6. use the saved Special or strongest Finisher;
7. leave again before Gilles restarts.

The reliable change is increased punishment and pressure after the transformation.
I do not treat the first second-form attack as safe merely because an earlier
version of the same animation could be Blocked.

If the attack produces a large impact area or empties Block Stamina too quickly, I
move out instead of testing the limit with another trade.

<h3 id="gilles-failures">
  Why my Gilles attempts fail
</h3>

### Heavy starts during a wind-up

I mistake a pause for the end of the attack string.

**Fix:** use one short Strike or Ally Command before committing to Heavy.

### The army dies while I watch the boss

Ranged enemies and nearby Fodders keep pressuring Commanders outside the center of
the camera.

**Fix:** clear the nearest interruption during Gilles’s recovery, then return.

### I rescue through an active attack

The rescue attempt is interrupted or costs most of the health bar.

**Fix:** pull Gilles away from the rescue position and return after the next slam or
long recovery.

### I enter at critical health

The damage build works, but one missed defense ends the Campaign.

**Fix:** skip the final optional node or speculative Chest and buy recovery before
the Great Gates.

<h3 id="iseult-preparation">
  Iseult preparation: direct damage and movement first
</h3>

One successful attempt entered with:

- **50 HP**;
- no healing shrine waiting on the boss node;
- **+30% Strike Damage** purchased immediately before the fight;
- direct Strike and Heavy damage already working.

The Strike purchase was worth it because Strike was already my safe confirmation
attack.

I would choose healing instead when one ground hit can immediately end the run.

My minimum Iseult setup is:

- a short Strike that can test a possible clone safely;
- direct boss damage that does not require a Fodder death;
- Dodge, movement, or another way to leave Erupting Earth;
- enough area pressure to clear the nearby group;

<h3 id="iseult-erupting-earth">
  Erupting Earth: leave first and attack later
</h3>

When the marked ground expands, I abandon the current combo and move toward a clear
lane.

  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    The correct response is movement, not damage. I wait until the route to Iseult
    is clear before rebuilding the combo.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

My sequence is:

1. stop attacking;
2. move out of the marked area;
3. keep the camera wide enough to see Iseult and nearby soldiers;
4. wait for her relocation to finish;
5. confirm the target using the clone method below;
6. complete one direct combo;
7. leave before the next ground cycle.

I do not rely on Block against Erupting Earth. I leave the marked area and preserve
Block Stamina for direct attacks from the boss or nearby soldiers.

I also do not spend a Special while Iseult is moving. Even a strong attack is wasted
when the target relocates before the damage connects.

<h3 id="iseult-clones">
  How I find the real Iseult during the clone phase
</h3>

I use the shortest safe attack as a test.

  <Image
    src="/images/tears-of-metal/tears-of-metal-iseult-clone-phase.webp"
    alt="Tears of Metal Iseult clone phase with multiple possible boss targets"

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    I test one target with a short hit and watch the main boss health bar. Heavy,
    Special, and long Finishers stay unused until the real body is confirmed.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

The check is:

1. approach from open ground;
2. use the shortest Strike or quick hit;
3. watch the main boss health bar;
4. leave immediately when the tested body does not reduce it;
5. test the next reachable target;
6. commit only after the main health bar responds.

Damage numbers can be difficult to read inside the crowd, so the main boss health
bar is my primary confirmation.

I do not treat lock-on behavior, Ally targeting, a single animation, or visual noise
as proof when the health bar does not move.

In two-player co-op, one player tests targets while the other preserves Special and
clears the nearby group.

<h3 id="iseult-adds">
  Why I clear the nearby soldiers
</h3>

The smaller enemies can consume defensive effects, interrupt the clone check, and
force me across unsafe ground.

I clear the group already affecting the current lane when:

- several soldiers are surrounding the hero;
- a defensive effect has limited charges or duration;
- Iseult is relocating;
- the next target check would begin inside the crowd.

I do not hunt every distant Fodder. I remove enough pressure to make the next clone
check and direct combo safe.

Brienne’s Burning and Combust are strong here because the group creates additional
death-burst value. Wallace can use Vortex and Heavy explosions to clear the space
around the real target.

<h3 id="iseult-openings">
  The Iseult punish window I use
</h3>

After the real target is confirmed, I attack only when:

- Erupting Earth no longer blocks the route;
- Iseult has finished relocating;
- nearby soldiers are not interrupting the approach;
- the tested body reduced the main boss health bar;
- one direct combo can finish before she moves again.

I complete one Strike-to-Heavy sequence or another short direct combo, then leave.

I do not force a second string after the movement cue has already begun.

Near the end of the successful fight, I gave up one possible hit and waited for the
next clean cycle. The final safe opening was more valuable than preserving the
combo.

<h3 id="iseult-reward">
  What happens after beating Iseult
</h3>

Confirmed after the clear:

- the Campaign entered the next region;
- the hero recovered health during the transition;
- soldiers that had already died remained unavailable;
- the surviving army condition carried into the next route decision.

I inspect the remaining formation before selecting the first Act 3 node.

The health recovery is not a complete reset. A run with several dead Commanders
still enters the next region with weaker Ally Commands, fewer Emblems, and less
battlefield coverage.

<h2 id="threat-differences">
  Normal vs high Threat boss fights
</h2>

In these runs, high Threat increased punishment and reduced recovery margin while
the core visual cues remained readable.

The clearest example was Gilles: a three-hit sequence removed roughly half of the
health bar during a high-Threat attempt.

High Threat changes my decisions in four ways:

- I trade less health for damage;
- I use one punish instead of forcing a second string;
- I value healing and surviving Commanders more;
- I stop taking optional nodes earlier.

A boss can feel completely different even when the central mechanic is unchanged.
Entering with higher Threat, lower health, fewer Commanders, and an unfinished
single-target loop compounds every mistake.

<h2 id="wallace-or-brienne">
  Wallace or Brienne: minimum builds for each boss
</h2>

### Gilles

I prefer **Wallace** while learning the fight.

Minimum setup:

- one short Strike or Ally Command to test openings;
- one strong Heavy or direct Finisher;
- Block Stamina or reliable Dodge timing;
- at least one Archer or ranged Commander;
- Special saved for the second form.

Brienne works, but I use shorter strings and leave before preserving Direct Hits
becomes greedy.

### Iseult

I prefer **Brienne** when I already understand the movement cycle.

Minimum setup:

- short Strike for clone checks;
- direct Strike or Heavy boss damage;
- Dodge or movement support;
- one area option for nearby soldiers;
- damage that does not depend entirely on enemy deaths.

Wallace remains the safer choice when survival is the main weakness.

Vortex helps clear the group, but his slow Heavy must begin only after the real
target and safe lane are confirmed.

<h2 id="two-player-co-op">
  What changes in two-player co-op boss fights
</h2>

These role changes come from a **two-player Campaign**.

Against Gilles:

- one player keeps the boss centered and tests the recovery;
- the second clears ranged threats and rescues Commanders;
- both attack during the same confirmed punish window;
- the player with the stronger burst preserves it for the second form.

Against Iseult:

- one player checks the possible real body;
- the second clears nearby soldiers and preserves Special;
- both stop attacking when Erupting Earth controls the route;
- the confirmed target is called out before either player commits a long combo.

I do not send both players toward separate clones without communicating. That splits
the army, rescue coverage, and visibility.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tears of Metal Gilles and Iseult FAQ
</h2>

### Should I stay locked on Gilles?

Not constantly. Lock-on helps during the direct exchange, but I release or adjust
the camera when Gilles fills the screen and hides ranged enemies, Commander danger,
or the next ground attack.

### Can I Block Gilles?

I Block short, readable swings while Block Stamina is healthy. I leave an extended
string before the bar breaks and move out of the overhead slam instead of trying to
Block the large impact.

### How do I find the real Iseult?

Use the shortest safe hit and watch whether the main boss health bar decreases.
Preserve Heavy, Special, and long Finishers until that response confirms the target.

### Can I Block Erupting Earth?

I do not rely on Block for Erupting Earth. I leave the marked ground, preserve Block
Stamina, and return after the hazard stops controlling the route.

### Does defeating Iseult revive dead Allies?

No. In the successful run covered here, the hero recovered health, but dead soldiers remained unavailable. The hero recovered during the transition,
but soldiers that had already died remained unavailable in the next region.

### Why is the same boss harder on another run?

Check Threat, current health, surviving Commanders, and the build before assuming
the mechanics changed. High Threat gives mistakes less margin, and a damaged army
makes the same punish window harder to use.

### Is Wallace or Brienne better for Gilles?

Wallace is my safer choice because Block, deliberate Heavy openings, and Archer
support fit the fight. Brienne works when I keep attack strings short and avoid
trading health for Direct Hits.

### Is Wallace or Brienne better for Iseult?

Brienne is strong when movement and short target checks are comfortable. Wallace is
safer when survival is the problem, but Heavy must wait until the real target and
safe lane are confirmed.
