The two early Tears of Metal builds I can reproduce most reliably are
**Wallace Heavy Explosion** and **Brienne Burn and Combust**.

Wallace uses Delayed Heat as the setup, Heavy as the payoff, and Vortex to hold
Fodders inside the blast. Brienne preserves Direct Hits with sideways Dodges,
spreads Burning through Trails, and converts Heavy Finishers into Combust bursts.

<h2 id="how-builds-work">
  How I build around random Emblem rewards
</h2>

I give one action four jobs:

1. **Setup:** apply Delayed Heat, Burning, Fragile, or another useful state.
2. **Payoff:** trigger an explosion, Combust burst, or strong Finisher.
3. **Control:** hold targets with Vortex, stun, army pressure, or positioning.
4. **Safe exit:** leave through Block, Dodge, Resilient, healing, or distance.

Before taking an Emblem, I ask which action activates it and whether I can finish
that action against a Captain. A rare Block effect adds little when I rarely Block;
a smaller Heavy upgrade can be better when Heavy already delivers the payoff.

Once the loop has setup, payoff, control, and a safe exit, I stop adding unrelated
Emblems. Route and Threat decisions belong in the{" "}

<h2 id="wallace-heavy-explosion">
  Wallace Heavy Explosion build
</h2>

Wallace is my safer early hero because I can wait for one clear opening instead of
maintaining constant pressure.

The build needs:

- **Main actions:** Strike into Heavy
- **Primary Clan:** Ravager
- **Secondary Clan:** Crusader
- **Required core:** one Delayed Heat setup and one Heavy payoff
- **Core completion:** Vortex, stun, or another reliable control effect
- **Strong support:** Block Stamina, Spearmen, Archers, or healing
- **Optional upgrade:** Flamberge tackle stun

<div className="my-6 rounded-xl border border-emerald-200 bg-emerald-50 p-5 text-sm leading-7 text-slate-700">
  <strong className="text-emerald-900">Tested result.</strong>{" "}
  Solo. The core was active during Act 1, defeated Gilles and Iseult, and reached
  Act 3. The run ended after an extended high-Threat route with most of the army
  already dead and heavy ranged pressure in the third region.
</div>

### Flamberge is useful, not required

Flamberge gives Dodge Tackles a **33% chance** to stun tackled enemies for
**2 seconds**.

When available, I pair Wallace with **three Spearmen and three Archers**.
Disciplined Battalion provided **+5% Attack Rate** and
**+10% Archer Ally Command Damage** in the tested run.

Spearmen pressure protected targets. Archers continue damaging Captains and bosses
while I leave the counterattack range.

### Step 1: apply Delayed Heat

The most reliable opener is Heated Strike:

- Strike applies **15 Delayed Heat**.
- Strike Finisher applies **30 Delayed Heat in a wave**.

Heated Block instead applies **50 Delayed Heat in a wave**.

  <Image

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    I take Heated Strike for the reliable setup. Heated Block is worth it when Block
    is already part of my Captain routine.
  </figcaption>

Delayed Heat loses up to **40** stored value every **2 seconds** and deals that
amount as damage. Explosive Blast can trigger it immediately.

I apply Heat, leave the Captain's next attack, and collect damage without remaining
inside melee range.

### Step 2: add a Heavy payoff

Once Ravager adds an explosive Heavy Finisher, the loop becomes:

1. apply Delayed Heat with Strike;
2. wait for the Captain's dangerous animation to finish;
3. start Heavy after the opening is confirmed;
4. trigger the Heavy explosion;
5. leave before the counterattack;
6. let Archers continue firing during the reset.

Wallace usually fails here because Heavy begins too early, not because the build
lacks damage. I use a short Strike or Ally Command to test the opening before the
full Heavy sequence.

### Step 3: complete it with Vortex

Vortex Heavy provides:

- Heavy deals **15 Smite Damage**.
- Heavy Finisher creates a Vortex dealing **15 Smite Damage**.
- The Vortex attracts and briefly stuns Fodders.

    Ravager supplies the stored damage and explosion. Crusader keeps nearby Fodders
    inside the payoff.

My full Wallace sequence is:

1. Block or Dodge the first elite attack.
2. Apply Delayed Heat with Strike.
3. Send Spearmen into the protected target.
4. Finish Heavy to create Vortex and the explosion.
5. Send Archers while leaving the counterattack.
6. Repeat after the next safe opening.

Exposing Dodge is a strong optional upgrade. It inflicts Fragile within
**5 metres**; Fragile adds **+20% damage**, and Smite is shown as
**300% more effective** against Fragile targets. I take it when Dodge already fits
my timing.

### Wallace Artifact, Charm, and equipment priorities

I prioritize effects in this order:

1. **Heavy or Finisher damage** to strengthen the payoff already used every fight.
2. **Block Stamina, guard safety, or healing** to protect the Heavy commitment.
3. **Stun or movement control** when Captains leave Vortex before the explosion.
4. **General damage** only after the action-specific pieces are already working.

I skip effects tied to an action I do not use. A strong Dodge bonus does not repair
a Wallace run that still needs a safe Heavy opening.

### Wallace fallback choices

- **Ravager Strike:** best setup.
- **Only Ravager Block:** use it when I actively Block.
- **No Ravager:** take direct Heavy damage, stun, or defensive support.
- **Vortex but no explosion:** use Vortex as control while strengthening Heavy.
- **Explosion but no Vortex:** use stun, Spearmen, or another Ally Command.

I do not restart because the first reward is imperfect. I take the closest effect
that gives the main action a useful job.


<h2 id="brienne-burn-combust">
  Brienne Burn and Combust build
</h2>

Brienne's passive gives the build its identity:

- damage increases with Direct Hits;
- receiving damage resets Direct Hits;
- Dodge does not cancel her combos.

    Dodge without cancelling the active combo.

The build needs:

- **Main actions:** Strike, sideways Dodge, then Heavy
- **Primary Clan:** Alchemist
- **Optional support:** Gambler
- **Required core:** one repeatable Burning or Combust setup
- **Main payoff:** Heavy Combust or strong direct Strike/Heavy damage
- **Strong support:** Balanced Army, Resilient, healing, or movement

I use **two Infantry, two Spearmen, and two Archers** when available. That activates
the Balanced Army formation and gives Brienne a practical mix of frontal pressure,
protected-target control, and ranged coverage.

The full formation bonus was not visible in the captured UI, so I use the
**2/2/2 split for battlefield coverage**, not as a required damage multiplier. When
the exact formation is unavailable, I keep at least one Archer for ranged pressure
and one Spearman for shielded targets, then fill the remaining slots with the
strongest surviving Commanders.

### Step 1: begin with Strike or Dodge

Early Alchemist options included:

- Trailing Dodge leaves a Trail for **3 seconds**.
- Enemies crossing the Trail gain Burning.
- Burning deals **2 damage every 0.5 seconds**.
- Bursting Strike has **Combust 1**.
- Strike Finisher has **Combust 3**.
- Sal Ammoniac makes Block grant Resilient for **1 second**.

I take Bursting Strike for reliable pressure, Trailing Dodge for dense objectives,
and Resilient when incoming hits interrupt the Finisher.

### Step 2: add Heavy Combust

Trailing Heavy provides:

- Heavy has **Combust 2**.
- Heavy Finisher launches **three projectiles** with a Trail.
- The projectiles have **Combust 4**.

My Brienne sequence is:

1. enter from the side;
2. build Direct Hits with Strike;
3. Dodge sideways through the dangerous attack;
4. finish the Strike sequence;
5. use Heavy Finisher through the group;
6. let Trails apply Burning and Combust;
7. leave before the Captain turns.

The combo is expendable when an area attack covers the exit. I leave and rebuild
Direct Hits rather than trading most of the health bar for one extra Finisher.

### Step 3: use Burning deaths for crowd damage

A later Alchemist effect caused an enemy killed by Burning to deal **20 damage**
within a **5-metre radius**.

I spread Burning, focus one weakened Fodder, trigger the death burst, and return to
the Captain after the surrounding group is thinner.

This is excellent in dense objectives but weaker against an isolated boss. For boss
fights, I keep direct Strike and Heavy damage instead of relying only on Burning
deaths.

### When Gambler is worth adding

Gambler can provide:

- **30%** shop discounts;
- a **10% chance** for Dodge to repeat **25%** of the last Strike or Heavy damage;
- additional Coin effects.

The Dodge repeat is useful after Strike or Heavy already deals real damage. The shop
discount is useful only while a merchant remains reachable. When the route closes,
I spend the saved Coins instead of building around a shop I cannot visit.

### Brienne Artifact, Charm, and equipment priorities

I prioritize:

1. **Strike damage** because Strike builds Direct Hits and starts the main loop.
2. **Dodge recovery, movement, or mobility** to preserve the chain through pressure.
3. **Resilient, healing, or damage resistance** when one interruption keeps ending the run.
4. **Heavy or Finisher damage** after the setup is already reliable.

I deprioritize effects that require standing still or being hit. Brienne's best
defense is keeping the chain while leaving the attack, not receiving damage to
activate a bonus.

### Brienne fallback choices

- **Bursting Strike:** most reliable opening.
- **Only Trailing Dodge:** use it on dense maps.
- **Heavy Combust early:** use direct Strike to build the chain, then Heavy as payoff.
- **Burning without Combust:** strengthen Strike and Direct Hits.
- **Combust without a Trail:** apply it through Strike or Heavy and use the army to hold targets.
- **No useful Alchemist:** take direct damage, Resilient, or movement.

Brienne does not need every Burning effect. She needs one repeatable setup, one
payoff, and enough safety to preserve Direct Hits.

<h2 id="solo-co-op">
  Solo and co-op versions
</h2>

**Wallace solo:** prioritize Vortex, Block Stamina, healing, and Archers because he
must provide control, survival, and boss damage.

**Wallace co-op:** hold Captains and create Vortex while another player attacks from
the side. When a teammate already provides control, replace one support choice with
direct Heavy damage.

**Brienne solo:** keep Resilient, healing, or army coverage because she must clear
structures and protect Commanders without dropping Direct Hits.

**Brienne co-op:** attack from the side while Wallace or another player holds the
central elite. Lean harder into Strike and Heavy damage when the teammate already
provides control.

In both cases, I avoid knocking a Captain out of a teammate's Vortex, Burning zone,
or large Finisher.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tears of Metal Wallace and Brienne build FAQ
</h2>

### Should I stay with one Emblem Clan?

Not always. I combine Clans when one supplies the setup and another supplies the
payoff. Ravager and Crusader work because Delayed Heat and the Heavy explosion
benefit from Vortex control.

### Is Wallace Heavy better than Strike?

Heavy is the payoff; Strike is the Delayed Heat setup. I use Strike first, then
Heavy during the confirmed opening.

### Is Flamberge required for Wallace?

No. Its 33% tackle-stun chance helps create Heavy openings, but the core Ravager and
Crusader loop works without it.

### Does Brienne lose her combo when she Dodges?

No. Dodge does not cancel the combo. Receiving damage still resets Direct Hits, so
I Dodge to leave attacks rather than remain inside them.

### Is Burning enough for bosses?

Not alone. Burning and Combust help, but the 5-metre death burst needs an enemy to
die. Against an isolated boss, direct Strike and Heavy damage matter more.

### What should I take when the core Emblem does not appear?

Take the closest effect that gives the same action a useful job. Wallace can use
direct Heavy damage, stun, or Block support. Brienne can use direct Strike damage,
Trailing Dodge, Resilient, or Heavy damage until Alchemist appears.

### How do I evaluate a duplicate or upgraded Emblem?

Compare the new tooltip with the equipped effect and ask whether it strengthens the
same setup, payoff, control, or safe exit. I take the upgrade when the displayed
value improves the current loop; I do not assume every duplicate adds a separate
full-strength stack.
