My early Tears of Metal Village order is based on the last Campaign failure.

For Wallace, I usually:

1. spend **50 Triskeles** to unlock special soldier commands;
2. choose one hero stat that fixes the last death;
3. use Statues on the Ally Command I actually issue;
4. choose Market or Cartographer only after the route problem is clear;
5. save Gems, Scrolls, and Dragonstone Shards without a named target.

<h2 id="first-wallace-upgrades">
  The first Wallace upgrades I buy
</h2>

Wallace’s observed early options included:

- special soldier commands for **50 Triskeles**;
- **Hardy Constitution:** permanently increase Max HP by **20%**;
- a permanent **+25 Max Block Stamina** upgrade;
- **Brute Force:** permanently increase Heavy Damage by **5%**.

I unlock soldier commands first because they add a new tactical tool. Infantry can
pressure the central group, Spearmen can target protected enemies, and Archers can
keep dealing damage while Wallace repositions.

### Hardy Constitution after a survival failure

One Wallace Campaign produced roughly **14,000 damage** and a best combo chain of
**75**. I still extended the route, entered the Monastery section with **7 HP**, and
died to a Pikeman Captain.

The run already had damage. It needed a shorter route, better healing discipline,
and more room for one mistake. The **+20% Max HP** upgrade directly tested that
failure.

### Block Stamina when the guard is the bottleneck

I take **+25 Max Block Stamina** when normal Captain strings repeatedly empty the
Block bar even though I am releasing and repositioning correctly.

I do not buy it to compensate for standing inside a large ground attack that should
have been Dodged.

### Brute Force after execution is stable

I take **+5% Heavy Damage** when Wallace survives, Heavy begins after a safe
opening, and Captains still live through correctly executed Finishers.

It does not repair an early Heavy animation, maximum Threat, or critical health.

<h2 id="hero-or-army">
  Hero or army: which one I upgrade first
</h2>

I upgrade the **hero** when:

- Wallace dies to direct damage;
- Block Stamina breaks before I can leave;
- or a correctly timed boss punish lacks damage.

I upgrade the **army** when:

- Wallace survives but Ally Commands barely affect the target;
- shielded Captains remain active after Spearmen arrive;
- ranged pressure continues because Archers are weak;
- or Commander selection limits the formation.

The 7-HP Monastery failure above was a route and survival problem, not evidence that
the hero or army needed another damage purchase.

<h2 id="marriott-companion">
  Marriott and Tools of the Trade
</h2>

Completing **Tools of the Trade** unlocked **Marriott**, leader of Clan McGregor, as
a Companion for later Campaigns.

Companions are stronger than ordinary Allies and bring powerful Ally Emblems, so
this quest expands future army selection rather than only rewarding the current
run.

I take it when the route can absorb the extra battle without pushing a collapsing
run through another dangerous Threat marker.

<h2 id="first-three-returns">
  My first three Village returns
</h2>

### First return

Unlock Wallace’s commands for **50 Triskeles**, then buy one stat based on the
failure:

- Max HP after a low-health death;
- Block Stamina after repeated guard breaks;
- Heavy Damage only after safe Heavy windows are working.

### Second return

Strengthen the command used most, or take Recruitment Drive or Military Advantage
when army selection is the bottleneck.

### Third return

Choose:

- **Market** when merchants are reachable but weak;
- **Cartographer** when Shrine or encounter availability is the problem;
- or **save resources** when neither has a defined purpose.

After that, I stop following a universal order. Each purchase becomes a test for the
next Campaign.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tears of Metal Village upgrade FAQ
</h2>

### What should I upgrade first?

For Wallace, unlock soldier commands for 50 Triskeles. Then choose Max HP, Block
Stamina, or Heavy Damage based on the previous failure.

### Is Hardy Constitution always the best first stat?

No. It is best after a low-health failure. Block Stamina fits repeated guard breaks;
Heavy Damage fits safe Finishers that still lack damage.

### Should I spread Triskeles across every hero?

Not early. Strengthen the hero used for the next clear. Brienne and Ruadh have
different action loops, so I do not copy Wallace’s Heavy-focused order into their
trees without testing their options.

### Which Ally Command should I upgrade first?

Upgrade the troop type already reaching its intended target: Infantry for groups,
Spearmen for protected enemies, or Archers for ranged pressure.

### Should I build the Market or Cartographer first?

Market for reachable merchants and weak items. Cartographer for poor Shrine and
encounter availability.

### Do my upgrades work when I join another player?

The tested warning stated that the Host’s owned items were active. Choose the Host
based on the stronger relevant progression pool.

### What should I buy with my first Dragonstone Shard?

Save it until a specific weapon, equipment item, skill, hero option, or trial is the
target.

### Are Ally Monikers permanent?

The game describes the selected Moniker as a lasting buff on the chosen Commander.
