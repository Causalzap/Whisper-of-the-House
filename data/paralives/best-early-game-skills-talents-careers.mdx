<h2 id="application-points">
  Application Points: the system that decides better jobs
</h2>

**Application Points** are one of the most important early career systems in Paralives. They decide whether your Para can qualify for higher-rank jobs.

A job's rank tells you how many Application Points you need.

<GuideTable
  caption="Application Points needed by job rank in Paralives."
  columns={applicationPointColumns}
  rows={applicationPointRows}
/>

Rank 1 jobs are easy to enter because they require **0 Application Points**. Higher-rank jobs need more points, which is why many jobs show as **Not Qualified** when you first check the job board.

Application Points come from:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Relevant skill levels</li>
  <li>Relevant work experience</li>
  <li>Charisma stat</li>
  <li>Vibe bonuses, especially Serious for many career applications</li>
</ul>

This is the real reason **Serious** is such a strong early pick for career-focused players. It is not just good because it sounds productive. It can help your Para qualify for better jobs when the rest of your build also supports that career.

<h2 id="application-points-example">
  Example: trying to reach Rank 5 Gameplay Programmer
</h2>

Let’s say you want your Para to apply for a **Rank 5 Gameplay Programmer** job.

That job needs **5 Application Points**.

<GuideTable
  caption="Example sources of Application Points for a Gameplay Programmer route."
  columns={applicationExampleColumns}
  rows={applicationExampleRows}
/>

So instead of asking, “Can I get this job?” ask:

> Do my skills, work history, Vibe, and Charisma add up to enough Application Points for this rank?

If the answer is no, do not force it. Take a lower-rank role, build skills, gain work experience, and come back later.

<h2 id="job-hopping">
  Job hopping can be stronger than staying in one ladder
</h2>

Paralives does not force you to stay in one linear career ladder forever.

If your Para has enough **Application Points**, you can jump into a higher-rank job in another career path instead of starting from Rank 1 every time. This is a major difference from many life sims, where changing careers often feels like resetting your progress.

A strong early strategy is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Take a manageable Rank 1 job.</li>
  <li>Train related skills outside work.</li>
  <li>Build Application Points through skills and experience.</li>
  <li>Check job postings often.</li>
  <li>Jump into a better job when your Application Points qualify.</li>
</ol>

For Sims players, this is important: you do not have to think of careers as locked tracks. In Paralives, your Para's skill profile can open doors across categories.

<h2 id="tech-route">
  Best early route for tech careers
</h2>

The best long-term career route is usually **Technology + Programming**.

<GuideTable
  caption="Best early tech career route in Paralives."
  columns={routeColumns}
  rows={techRouteRows}
/>

A Gameplay Programmer starts as a practical early target, but the tech route gets better as you build skills and qualify for stronger jobs.

In early job data, Gameplay Programmer can start around **₱250/day** at Rank 1. Later software roles can rise much higher. Some tech routes can eventually lead into stronger positions such as **Full-Stack Developer**, **Front-End Developer**, **Technical Director**, and **Cybersecurity Engineer**.

Tech also has a useful milestone: **Work from Home** can become available in the programmer route. That matters because it lets you keep building skills without losing as much time to commuting and daily routine pressure.

A good tech path looks like this:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Start with Mind.</li>
  <li>Pick Technology talent.</li>
  <li>Train Technology first.</li>
  <li>Practice Programming.</li>
  <li>Take a tech job you qualify for.</li>
  <li>Avoid upgrading rank if Programming or Technology is red in the career panel.</li>
  <li>Jump to better tech jobs once your Application Points are high enough.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="skills">
  How Knowledge Skills and parent skills work
</h2>

Paralives splits many skills into **Knowledge Skills** and related practical skills. Knowledge Skills act like parent skills. Raising the parent skill helps the skills underneath it feel better to train.

There are conflicting Early Access reports about the exact skill cap, with different community sources reporting different max levels. Because the game is still changing, this guide does not build recommendations around a fixed cap number. The useful early-game rule is the same either way: train the Knowledge Skill that supports your route before grinding the smaller skill underneath it.

<h2 id="inspired-training">
  Use Inspired as a small training bonus
</h2>

**Inspired** is useful, but it is not a full build by itself. Treat it as a small training bonus that is best used when it already lines up with your route.

When your Para becomes Inspired, use that time on the skill you were already planning to train. Do not stop your whole routine just to chase the mood.

<GuideTable
  caption="Good ways to use Inspired mood for early skill training."
  columns={inspiredColumns}
  rows={inspiredRows}
/>

The best use of Inspired is to pair it with a matching **Knowledge Skill**, a matching **Talent**, and any temporary learning buff you already have.

For now, use Inspired when it appears, but do not build your entire early-game plan around forcing it. Your route, skills, Application Points, and career choices matter more.

  <li>Add work flexibility</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="mistakes">
  Two mistakes that actually matter
</h2>

### Upgrading rank before your skills are ready

This is the big one. A better rank can mean better pay, but it can also make your job harder. If your career panel shows red skill icons, train first.

### Ignoring Application Points

If you only look at salary, the job board can be misleading. A higher-rank job is not a real option until your Application Points qualify. Build the skills, experience, Charisma, and Vibe bonuses that help you reach the rank you want.

### Can I respec my Para later?

You can change some parts of your Para later with commands such as `editcharacter` or personality reset options, but you should still treat your starting choices seriously. Save before making major changes.

### Does a Talent affect every skill?

No. Most Talents are route-specific. Art helps creative routes, Technology helps tech routes, Food helps cooking, Music helps instruments, and Fitness helps active play. Jack-of-All-Trades is the flexible option.

### What happens if I ignore Knowledge Skills?

Your sub-skills may feel slower to train. You can still improve them, but you are missing the parent skill support that makes related skills grow more efficiently.

### Is Serious the best Vibe?

Serious is one of the strongest early picks for career-focused players because it can help with job applications and productive work. It is not always the best for social or relaxed play.

### Should I pick the highest-paying job?

Not automatically. Pick the job your Para can qualify for and perform well in. A lower-paying job with stable performance is often better than a higher-rank job that gives you strikes.

### What is the skill level cap?

Early Access sources currently conflict on the exact skill cap, so this guide avoids making build decisions around a fixed number. For early-game planning, the more important point is to train the right Knowledge Skill before grinding the related skill.

### Should I pick Upgrade Job Rank or a perk?

If your skills and performance are ready, pick Rank. If your Para is struggling, pick a perk that supports performance, stability, schedule, or training first.
