<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
If **job performance is not improving in Paralives**, open the career panel and check four things first: **needs, mood, required skills, and strikes**. If the job skill icons are red, train those skills before pushing rank again. If your Para has **2/3 strikes**, you are not doomed: strikes can expire after 14 in-game days, and the **Moon Book** Story Card can remove a strike when it appears.

Do not only spam workdays. A stuck career usually needs one of three fixes: better needs before work, stronger job skills, or a safer career/Story Card choice.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="read-career-panel">
  How to read the job performance panel
</h2>

The career panel is the first thing you should check when performance feels stuck.

<GuideTable
  caption="What each part of the Paralives career panel tells you."
  columns={panelColumns}
  rows={panelRows}
/>

In the example above, the Para is a **Gameplay Programmer, Rank 3**, earning **₱300 per day**, working **9:00 to 16:00**, with **65% performance** and **2/3 strikes**. Mood is helping, but **Programming** and **Technology** are hurting performance.

That is the important lesson: if one part of the panel is green, another part can still be holding you back.

<h2 id="needs-before-work">
  Check needs and timing before work
</h2>

Low needs can make work days much worse. If your Para is tired, hungry, uncomfortable, stressed, or desperate for the bathroom before work, do not expect a clean performance gain.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/paralives/paralives-needs-before-work.webp"
    alt="Paralives Para getting ready for work with low energy and needs."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    A bad morning can turn into a bad workday. Fix the worst needs before the shift starts.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Before work, fix the biggest need first, then send your Para early enough that they are not starting the work trip at the last second. You do not need a perfect morning, but you do need to avoid showing up exhausted, starving, late, or in a bad mood.

If your current shift always creates bad mornings, change the schedule when the job allows it. Even moving a shift from **9:00-16:00** to **10:00-17:00** can give your Para enough time to sleep, eat, and arrive in better shape.

<h2 id="skills-match-job">
  Make sure your skills match the job
</h2>

One of the most common reasons job performance gets stuck is that your Para is under-skilled for the position.

Some jobs are tied to specific skills. If your Para does not meet the expected skill level or practice level, the job can be harder to perform well. In the job list, some positions may even show as unavailable or not qualified when your Para lacks the right background.

<h2 id="dont-rush-rank">
  Do not raise job rank too fast
</h2>

A promotion feels good, but it can also make your job harder.

If your Para ranks up before their skills are ready, the new position may expect more from them. That can create a loop where the salary is better, but performance becomes harder to maintain.

Signs your job rank is too high:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Your performance meter barely moves.</li>
  <li>Skill icons in the career panel are red.</li>
  <li>You start getting strikes.</li>
  <li>Your Para comes home in bad shape.</li>
  <li>You feel forced to use bonuses just to stay stable.</li>
</ul>

If that happens, stop chasing the next rank for a few days. Use the time outside work to train the weak skills, fix needs, and build a safer routine.

<h2 id="strikes">
  How strikes actually work
</h2>

If your Para already has **2/3 strikes**, you are not automatically finished. You need to avoid triggering a third strike long enough for the existing strikes to clear.

The key details:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Strikes can expire automatically after <strong>14 in-game days</strong>.</li>
  <li>If you have 2/3 strikes, your goal is to survive the next two weeks without getting the third one.</li>
  <li>The <strong>Moon Book</strong> Story Card can remove one strike when it appears.</li>
  <li>If you need a safer day but have no vacation days, the console command <code>ADDVACATIONDAY</code> can add one.</li>
  <li>If you want an immediate command-based reset, <code>CLEARSTRIKES</code> can clear strikes.</li>
  <li>Storyteller Settings can make strikes less punishing if the career system feels too strict.</li>
</ul>

<GuideTable
  caption="What to do when your Para has strikes."
  columns={strikeColumns}
  rows={strikeRows}
/>

If you are at **2/3 strikes**, do not panic-delete the save and do not quit the job immediately. Play safely until the strikes expire, take **Moon Book** if it appears, and use a vacation day if the next workday looks dangerous.

The safest strike recovery plan is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Do not miss work.</li>
  <li>Do not arrive in terrible condition.</li>
  <li>Do not upgrade job rank while skills are still red.</li>
  <li>Use a vacation day if your Para needs time to recover or train.</li>
  <li>Use <code>ADDVACATIONDAY</code> if you want a console-based safety day.</li>
  <li>Take Moon Book if it appears.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="still-stuck">
  What to do if performance is still stuck
</h2>

If job performance is still stuck, compare the last workday against the career panel instead of only looking at the final result screen. The useful question is not “did the number go up?” but **which system blocked it?**

Before assuming it is a bug, try this:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If skill icons are red, train the required skill before upgrading rank again.</li>
  <li>If needs were low before work, fix the morning routine first.</li>
  <li>If a Story Card or temporary bonus expired, do not expect the next day to match the boosted day.</li>
  <li>If strikes are active, play safely until they expire or use Moon Book / vacation days.</li>
  <li>If the job rank is ahead of your skills, stop pushing rank and stabilize the role.</li>
  <li>If nothing changes after several clean workdays, save and reload or wait for an Early Access patch.</li>
</ul>

If the job still feels broken after several clean workdays, it may be safer to switch jobs, take a lower rank, adjust Storyteller Settings, or use a console command if you are comfortable with that.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Why is my job performance not improving in Paralives?

Usually because your Para’s needs are low, mood is bad, job-related skills are too weak, the schedule is awkward, or the job rank is ahead of your current skill level.

### What do the red skill icons mean?

They usually mean that skill is hurting your work performance. Train that skill or its related Knowledge Skill before pushing for more promotions.

### Can low needs affect job performance?

Yes. A tired, hungry, uncomfortable, or stressed Para can have a worse work day. Fix the worst needs before the shift whenever possible.

### Should I always take a promotion?

Not always. Higher rank usually means better pay, but it can also raise expectations. If your skills are already behind, train first.

### What should I do if I have two strikes?

You are not doomed. Strikes can expire after 14 in-game days, so your goal is to avoid getting a third strike until they clear. You can also take the Moon Book Story Card if it appears, use a vacation day, add a vacation day with `ADDVACATIONDAY`, adjust Storyteller Settings, or use `CLEARSTRIKES` if you want a console fix.

### What is Moon Book?

Moon Book is a Story Card option that can remove a strike when your Para already has one. If you are at 2/3 strikes and it appears, it is usually worth taking.

### Can Story Cards improve job performance?

Yes. Some Story Cards can give work-related skill XP, rewards, job perks, small pay boosts, performance bonuses, or strike-related help. Check Story Cards before repeating another risky workday.

### Is job performance bugged?

It can be hard to tell during Early Access. Check needs, skills, schedule, strikes, Story Cards, and work results first. If several clean work days still do not change anything, it may be a bug or tuning issue.

### What does ADDVACATIONDAY do?

`ADDVACATIONDAY` adds a vacation day through the console. It is useful if your Para is close to getting fired, has no time to recover, or needs a day to train weak job skills before returning to work.
