<h2 id="progression-loop">
  The early-game progression loop
</h2>

The main Paralives loop is simple, but easy to break:

> route → skills → job → performance → upgrades → better route

If one part falls behind, the whole save starts to feel worse.

<GuideTable
  caption="How early progression systems connect in Paralives."
  columns={loopColumns}
  rows={loopRows}
/>

This is the Hub page’s main idea: **most early problems are not isolated**. A stuck job may actually be a skill problem. A slow route may actually be a stat or talent problem. A wasted upgrade may actually be a career stability problem.

<h2 id="minimum-framework">
  A simple first-save framework
</h2>

For a first save, keep the plan small. You do not need a perfect Para. You need a Para whose choices make sense together.

Use this framework:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Pick one goal: money, tech, social, creative, or easier daily life.</li>
  <li>Choose a stat and talent that support that goal.</li>
  <li>Train the matching Knowledge Skill before over-grinding the practical skill.</li>
  <li>Take a job your Para can actually qualify for and perform well in.</li>
  <li>Spend upgrades on the current bottleneck, not the flashiest option.</li>
</ol>

That is enough to avoid most early mistakes. The detailed routes are covered in the sub-guides.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### I am completely new. Which Paralives guide should I read first?

Start with <Link href="/paralives/best-early-game-skills-talents-careers">Best Early Game Skills, Talents, and Careers</Link>. It helps you choose a route before you spend too much time training random skills or taking a mismatched job.

### What order should I read these guides in?

For most players: early-game route guide first, skill leveling second, job performance third, and upgrade points fourth. If you are already stuck in a job, start with the job performance guide instead.

### Why are these systems connected?

Because skills affect job performance, job performance affects Career Points, Career Points affect rank and perks, and those choices affect whether your route keeps moving or stalls.

### Is this page a full wiki?

No. This page is a hub for player decisions. It points you to focused guides instead of listing every career, skill, item, or mechanic.
