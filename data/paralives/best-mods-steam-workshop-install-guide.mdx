<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
The safest way to use **Paralives mods** is to start from the **Main Menu → Mods** screen. Use Steam Workshop for normal mods, custom content, households, and lots. Use the blue toggle on the Mods page to enable or disable items before removing anything.

For a first save, I would start with comfort mods: **Sensible Camera Control**, **Display Townies on Map**, **Invite to Current Lot**, **Crystal Clear Map View**, and **Zoom Out More**. Add script mods last, because official in-game modding tools are focused on content editing and do not currently support scripting as a normal built-in feature.

If something breaks, back up your save, turn off the most recent mod, check the Console with **Ctrl+F1**, and use the Control Panel with **Ctrl+F2** on Windows or **Cmd+F2** on Mac when you need deeper inspection.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="install-first">
  What I would install first
</h2>

I would not install ten mods on a save I care about. Paralives is still in Early Access, and the faster you stack mods, the harder it becomes to know what actually caused a problem.

My safe first pass is simple. First, install one camera or map mod. Then load the save and play one normal in-game day. After that, install one town-finding or social helper mod, then test travel, conversations, saving, and reloading. Only after that would I add larger custom content packs or script mods.

That order is slower, but it saves time later. If something breaks, I know which mod changed the save.

<h2 id="best-mods">
  Best Paralives mods to use first
</h2>

The first mods I care about are not the flashiest. They are the ones that remove friction from normal play.

**Sensible Camera Control** is the one I would test first if the camera feels awkward while building, rotating, or following a Para. It is better to fix camera control before adding a pile of cosmetic content, because every other task feels worse when the camera is fighting you.

**Display Townies on Map** is the best early helper when I am trying to finish town goals or meet a specific Para. Instead of wandering the map and hoping the right person appears, I can scan the map and move with a plan.

**Invite to Current Lot** is the other half of that problem. Use map visibility when you want to find people yourself. Use invite tools when you already know who you need and do not want to lose an evening chasing them across town.

**Crystal Clear Map View** and **Zoom Out More** are small quality-of-life picks. They do not change your save strategy, but they make the town easier to read.

**No Learn Recipe Cooldown** is only worth installing early if you are building around cooking. If your Para barely cooks, skip it. If you are trying to unlock recipes quickly, it saves repeated trips and waiting.

<h2 id="steam-workshop-install">
  How to install normal Steam Workshop mods
</h2>

For normal Workshop items, I start from the main menu. Open **Mods**, choose **Browse for Mods**, find the item on Steam Workshop, then click the plus icon or **Subscribe**. After that, return to the game and check the Mods page before loading an important save.

I do not load an important save immediately after subscribing. I go back to the Mods page first and make sure the item is actually listed and enabled.

<h2 id="content-types">
  Mods, households, lots, and script mods are different
</h2>

This is the part many players miss. Not every downloaded item appears in the same place.

<GuideTable
  caption="Where different Paralives mod and Workshop content types appear."
  columns={contentTypeColumns}
  rows={contentTypeRows}
/>

The most important rule is simple: check the content type before you decide it is broken.

A hair item belongs in Paramaker. A furniture item belongs in the catalog. A household belongs in the household library. A house belongs in the lot library. A script mod may need local files.

<h2 id="enable-disable-delete">
  How to enable, disable, or remove a mod
</h2>

When a mod causes problems, use the blue toggle on the **Mods** page before you unsubscribe or delete anything. That toggle is the first test because it lets you confirm whether the item is actually causing the issue.

My order is: turn off the newest mod, restart if needed, load a test save, and only remove the mod after the problem is proven. I do not delete five mods at once because that makes the real cause harder to identify.

<h2 id="backup-save">
  Back up your save before troubleshooting
</h2>

Before I test script mods, big custom content packs, or lot downloads, I back up the save folder.

On Windows, the save folder can be checked here: `%USERPROFILE%\AppData\LocalLow\Alex Masse\Paralives\Saves`.

Copy the whole `Saves` folder somewhere safe before you start disabling, deleting, replacing, or updating a large mod setup.

This matters most when you are testing script mods, downloaded households, downloaded lots, custom content packs, or mods after a game update.

If the save loads correctly after testing, you can keep playing. If something goes wrong, you have a clean copy to return to.

<h2 id="households">
  How to download and use Workshop households
</h2>

Households are library content, not normal gameplay mods.

The path I use is Paramaker, then the household library, then Steam Workshop households. After subscribing, I return to the household library and look for the cloud icon. From there, the important choice is **Merge** or **Replace**.

<h2 id="lots">
  How to place downloaded Workshop lots
</h2>

Downloaded lots are the easiest Workshop content to misunderstand.

The route is to open your save, zoom out to the town map, choose a lot you own or buy an empty one, bulldoze the lot first, then open **Place a Lot from the Library**. Select the downloaded lot and confirm with the place button.

<h2 id="floating-lot">
  What to do if a Workshop lot floats above the ground
</h2>

A downloaded lot can float if it was built somewhere else or if the terrain does not match the lot you picked.

The first thing I check is whether the target lot is large enough and empty. After that, I hold **Shift** and try dragging the home down to ground level. If the result still looks wrong, I cancel and use a different lot instead of forcing a broken placement.

<h2 id="script-mods">
  Script mods need a different install route
</h2>

I treat script mods differently from normal Workshop items because the official in-game modding tools are not the same thing as full scripting support. In practical terms, a script mod is usually a third-party setup that may depend on a loader, local files, or a plugins folder.

A normal Workshop item usually follows this pattern: Subscribe, enable it on the Mods page, then load the save.

A script mod may require creator instructions, local files, a required loader, a first launch to generate folders, and then the correct plugins folder.

  </figcaption>
</figure>

The mistake I avoid is dropping files into the first folder that looks right. If the instructions say to use a plugins folder, I use that exact folder. If the instructions say to launch the game once first, I do that before moving the file.

<h2 id="patch-maintenance">
  What to do after a Paralives patch
</h2>

Game updates are where a stable mod setup can suddenly become unstable. I do not assume every mod is broken after a patch, but I also do not load my main save and keep playing without checking.

<GuideTable
  caption="How I maintain Paralives mods after a patch."
  columns={patchMaintenanceColumns}
  rows={patchMaintenanceRows}
/>

The most important rule is to separate normal custom content from gameplay-changing or script-based mods. A hairstyle being outdated is annoying. A script mod being outdated can affect the save more deeply.

After a patch, I check the creator notes first. If the creator has not confirmed compatibility, I leave the risky mod off and test the save without it.

<h2 id="control-panel-console">
  Use the Console and Control Panel when a mod breaks
</h2>

Paralives has built-in tools that are useful when a mod starts causing problems.

Use **Ctrl+F1** for the Console on Windows. Use **Ctrl+F2** for the Control Panel on Windows. Use **Cmd+F2** for the Control Panel on Mac.

The Console is where I look for red errors after enabling a mod. If the save starts acting wrong and the Console fills with errors right after one mod was enabled, that mod becomes the first suspect.

The Control Panel is more advanced. I use it when I want to inspect how game content is set up or when I am trying to understand whether a mod changed something deeper than a normal Workshop object.

For help beyond a basic install problem, I would also check the official Paralives Discord modding channel. That is a better place for creator-specific or tool-specific issues than guessing inside a save.

<h2 id="curseforge">
  Steam Workshop vs CurseForge
</h2>

For most players, I would start with **Steam Workshop** because it is integrated into the game menu. It is the cleanest route for browsing, subscribing, enabling, disabling, and finding downloaded content.

CurseForge also has a Paralives section, but I would not mix download sources casually. If I install a Workshop version and a CurseForge version of the same idea, I create a harder troubleshooting problem for myself.

My rule is to use Steam Workshop first for normal content, use another source only when the creator clearly tells me to, keep a note of where each mod came from, and avoid duplicate versions of the same mod.

<h2 id="create-mod">
  Should you create your own mod from here?
</h2>

Paralives has in-game modding tools, but I would not mix installing mods and creating mods in the same session.

If you want to create your own mod, start from the main menu and look for **Modding Tools** or **Create New Mod**. That route is for changing or packaging content yourself, not just downloading another player's work.

For a normal player, I would learn the safer order first: install and enable a simple Workshop item, disable it and confirm the save still works, install a household or lot, use the Console to check for errors, and only then experiment with creating or editing a mod.

Creating your own mod is worth a separate guide because the decisions are different: naming the mod, editing content, testing changes, setting a thumbnail, and uploading to Steam Workshop.

<h2 id="safe-order">
  My safe mod order for a fresh save
</h2>

On a normal save, I back up the save folder first. Then I add one camera, map, or UI comfort mod and play one day before reloading the save. After that, I add custom content like hair, furniture, or tattoos, then test Paramaker and the catalog.

I only add households or lots after checking where they belong, and I save script mods for last. If anything behaves strangely, I open the Console before removing anything.

This is slower than subscribing to everything at once, but it gives me a clean rollback point. In Early Access, that matters more than having every mod installed on the first day.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Where do I install Paralives mods?

Start from **Main Menu → Mods → Browse for Mods** for normal Steam Workshop items. Use local files only when the creator specifically tells you to.

### How do I enable or disable a mod?

Use the toggle on the Mods page. If you are troubleshooting, follow the test order in the enable and disable section above.

### How do I remove a Steam Workshop mod?

Use the unsubscribe or remove option from the Workshop item or the Mods page. If the save is acting strangely, prove the cause before removing several items at once.

### Where are Paralives saves on Windows?

Check this folder: `%USERPROFILE%\AppData\LocalLow\Alex Masse\Paralives\Saves`.

Copy the full `Saves` folder before testing risky mods.

### Why is my subscribed mod not showing?

Check the content type. A household appears in the household library, a lot appears in the lot library, and a normal mod appears on the Mods page. Script mods may require local installation.

### Why is my downloaded lot floating?

Use the floating-lot section above. The short version is: use an owned empty lot, check the lot size, try Shift-dragging once, then switch lots if the placement still looks wrong.

### What is the best first mod?

I would start with **Sensible Camera Control** or a map helper. Those improve daily play without changing your whole save.

### Should I use CurseForge or Steam Workshop?

For most players, start with Steam Workshop because it is built into the Paralives menu. Use CurseForge or another source only when a mod creator directs you there.

### What should I do after a game patch?

Check creator notes, keep risky script or gameplay mods disabled until they are confirmed compatible, then test on a backup or test save first.

### What are Ctrl+F1 and Ctrl+F2 for?

Use **Ctrl+F1** to check the Console for red errors. Use **Ctrl+F2** on Windows or **Cmd+F2** on Mac to open the Control Panel when you need deeper content inspection.
