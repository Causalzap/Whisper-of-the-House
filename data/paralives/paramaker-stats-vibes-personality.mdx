In **Paralives**, Paramaker choices are not just cosmetic. Your **Stats**, **Vibe**, **Talent**, and **Social Perk** decide what your Para is naturally good at, which skills feel smoother, and which early routes make sense.

For a smooth first save, do not leave a key stat empty, do not pick a Talent that fights your route, and do not choose a Vibe only because it sounds fun. Build the Para around what you actually plan to do: painting, programming, social play, fitness, cooking, or a flexible first save.

<h2 id="paramaker-overview">
  What Paramaker choices actually affect
</h2>

Paramaker is where you set the foundation for your Para. Appearance matters, but the gameplay choices matter too.

The main gameplay choices are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Stats</strong>, such as Physique, Mind, Creativity, and Charisma.</li>
  <li><strong>Vibe</strong>, which affects your Para’s emotional and lifestyle loop.</li>
  <li><strong>Talent</strong>, which supports specific skill routes.</li>
  <li><strong>Social Perk</strong>, which shapes how your Para handles social situations.</li>
  <li><strong>Personality growth</strong>, which continues after creation through wants and level-ups.</li>
</ul>

The mistake is treating these as flavor-only choices. A painting Para, tech Para, social Para, and low-maintenance Para should not start the same way.

<h2 id="stat-points">
  Stats explained: Physique, Mind, Creativity, and Charisma
</h2>

Stats are your Para’s foundation. They support the routes your Para will naturally feel better at.

<GuideTable
  caption="Paralives stats and the routes they support."
  columns={statColumns}
  rows={statRows}
/>

The key rule is simple: **your highest stat should support your main route**.

If you want to paint for money, Creativity matters. If you want Programming or tech jobs, Mind matters. If you want social careers or better applications, Charisma matters. If you want an active or low-maintenance routine, Physique matters.

<h2 id="zero-stat-warning">
  Do not leave an important stat empty
</h2>

One of the easiest Paramaker mistakes is leaving a stat empty because you want to push another stat higher.

<GuideTable
  caption="Why zero or weak stats can hurt a Paralives build."
  columns={zeroStatColumns}
  rows={zeroStatRows}
/>

For a normal first save, keep every stat usable. You can still specialize, but do not make your Para helpless in an area that the route will need later.

<h2 id="vibes">
  Vibes explained
</h2>

A **Vibe** is your Para’s core emotional style. It can affect how they handle work, social life, moods, romance, and daily routine.

<GuideTable
  caption="Paralives Vibes and when to pick them."
  columns={vibeColumns}
  rows={vibeRows}
/>

For efficiency, **Serious** is one of the safest career-focused picks. For easier daily life, **Energetic** is often more comfortable. For social or relationship-heavy saves, **Overjoyed** can fit better.

Do not pick Vibe only for the name. Pick it for the kind of day-to-day gameplay you want.

<h3 id="vibe-compatibility">
  Vibe compatibility can affect romance
</h3>

Vibes are not only solo personality labels. They can also affect how two Paras feel together.

<GuideTable
  caption="Vibe compatibility notes for romance and social play."

/>

The important example is **Serious + Jester**. If you want a romance-focused save, this pairing can be more difficult than it looks because their emotional styles clash. It is not impossible, but you should expect to work harder than with a smoother pairing.

<h2 id="social-perks">
  Social Perks explained
</h2>

Social Perks shape how your Para handles people. They matter most when your route includes relationships, group activities, solo work, flirting, friendship, or caring for other Paras.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to choose Social Perks in Paralives."
  columns={socialPerkColumns}
  rows={socialPerkRows}
/>

Do not treat Social Perks as one generic “social bonus.” They can point in different directions: being better alone, cooperating with others, flirting, making friends faster, or helping sad Paras.

For example, **Good at Being Alone** makes more sense for a solo painter or programmer than for a Para who spends all day building relationships. A flirting or friendship perk makes more sense for a romance or town-life save.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How many Stat Points do you get in Paramaker?

You start with a limited pool of Stat Points to divide between Physique, Mind, Creativity, and Charisma. Plan them around your route instead of spreading them randomly.

### What happens if I leave a stat at zero?

Leaving a stat empty can create a permanent weakness or negative effect. For a normal first save, keep every stat usable unless you are intentionally making a challenge Para.

### What is the best Talent in Paralives?

The best Talent depends on your route. Art is best for painting, Technology is best for programming, Food is best for cooking, Music is best for instruments, Fitness is best for active play, and Jack-of-All-Trades is best for flexibility.

### Can I change my Talent after creating a Para?

Do not assume you can freely change the starting Talent after creation. Treat it as an important build choice. If you are not sure what to pick, Jack-of-All-Trades is the safer flexible option.

### What does Personality Level unlock?

Personality Level can unlock new traits, bonuses, or personality-related choices. The exact options can depend on your Para’s path, so complete wants that reinforce the route you want.

### Is this the same as Career Upgrade Points?

No. Paramaker choices shape your Para before work starts. Career Upgrade Points come later through jobs and are used for rank, perks, vacation days, and work stability.

### Does Vibe compatibility affect romance?

Yes, some Vibe pairings can make romance harder. The most important example is Serious + Jester, which can create a romance compatibility penalty. You can still build the relationship, but expect slower progress or more failed romance attempts.

### Which Social Perk should I pick?

Pick the Social Perk that matches how your Para spends time with people. Good at Being Alone is better for solo workers, cooperation perks fit group play, flirting perks fit romance saves, friendship perks help social townies, and care-focused perks help supportive family or relationship play.
