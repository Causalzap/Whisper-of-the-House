<h2 id="cagliostro-ai-controversy">
  Cagliostro AI: why I still use her, and what I do not expect
</h2>

Cagliostro is the slot that needs the clearest explanation.

The complaint that **Cagliostro AI does not play her charged Unique Attack / Collapse++ route like a manual player** is fair. I do not use her because I expect perfect Collapse++ combo finisher timing. I use her because she gives the party support safety while I am learning Chaos.

That changes the build.

I value:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Phantasmagoria</strong> for team value,
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Reinforce</strong> for defensive stability,
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Rhizomata</strong> for revive safety,
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Potion Hoarder</strong> and <strong>Auto Revive</strong> while clears are unstable,
  </li>
  <li>
    then cap conversion and Skill DMG Cap after the team stops dying.
  </li>
</ul>

I treat <strong>Pain Train</strong> and deeper Crippling Combos damage value as later optimizations, not the reason I bring her to first Chaos clears.

This is the key difference: I use Cagliostro as a **stability slot first**, then convert her into more damage after the route is safe.

<h2 id="master-traits">
  Master Traits for Eugen, Cagliostro, and Zeta AI
</h2>

Master Traits matter, but I do not use them like a full database checklist. For AI companions, I ask one question first:

<strong>Does this trait improve the behavior the AI is already performing well?</strong>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Eugen is the clearest example of how I read AI Master Traits: Flawless Vision for sniper vision value, Grenade Punch for grenade pressure, and Spitting Venom for Skill DMG Cap or DEF Down value.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

For **Eugen**, I start from **Flawless Vision** when I want stable sniper vision value. It gives his ranged AI role a clear direction: sniper vision, Damage Cap, and ranged uptime. I test **Grenade Punch** only when grenade behavior is consistent in the fight. I use **Spitting Venom** when the run needs Skill DMG Cap or extra DEF Down value.

For **Cagliostro**, I read **Crippling Combos** differently for AI than I would for manual play. The flashy Collapse++ and charged Unique Attack route is not my first AI assumption. I care more about debuffs, Truth levels, Skill DMG Cap, cooldown shortening, Damage Cap per Basic Stats-type sigil, and support-safe value.

For **Zeta**, I use **Intense Loops** only when it supports the behavior I actually want from AI: simple loop pressure, normal attacks, and Damage Cap. I do not rely on manual-perfect timing perks unless the AI is clearly landing that behavior in real fights.

Summons follow the same rule. I do not build AI companions around gimmicks they cannot execute. I use summon passives that support the role: ranged damage for Eugen, support stability for Cagliostro, and repeatable damage windows for Zeta.

<h2 id="new-characters-as-ai">
  Should new Endless Ragnarok characters be AI companions?
</h2>

I unlock new characters early, but I do not assume every new character is a good AI companion.

My rule is:

<strong>technical characters get manual testing before AI investment.</strong>

Beatrix, Eustace, Fraux, Fediel, Gallanza, and Maglielle all bring strong playstyles, but the question for this page is different. I am not asking whether they are fun. I am asking whether the AI uses them well enough to replace a finished companion.

I test a new character as AI in lower-risk content first. If they lose uptime, misuse the gauge, or need manual timing to shine, I keep them as a manual-main candidate instead of forcing them into the AI core.

### What is the best AI team for Endless Ragnarok?

My first solo AI team is manual main, Eugen, Cagliostro, and Zeta. Eugen covers ranged pressure, Cagliostro stabilizes early Chaos, and Zeta gives simple AI damage when I strip her skills for auto attacks.

### What skills should Eugen AI use?

I build Eugen around his ranged role, especially Detonator, Armor-Piercing Round, and Sumrak value. I do not force grenade-heavy behavior unless the AI is using it cleanly in the fight.

### Why does Cagliostro AI feel weak sometimes?

Because AI does not play her charged Unique Attack / Collapse++ route like a manual player. I use her as a support-safe slot first, then add damage after Chaos clears are stable.

### Should Zeta AI use skills?

For the most consistent setup, I strip Zeta's skills and let her auto attack. That keeps her behavior simple and pairs well with Intense Loops, normal attack value, and Damage Cap.

### Should I use Backup Character or farm Conflux?

Use Backup Character when one slot is blocking progress right now. Then farm Conflux to catch up your own AI companion and replace the borrowed slot later.
