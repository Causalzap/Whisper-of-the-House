<h2 id="new-characters-unlock-guide">
  Granblue Fantasy Relink Endless Ragnarok New Characters Guide: Who to Unlock First and When to Spend Crewmate Cards
</h2>

Endless Ragnarok adds six characters, but I do not build all six at once. Unlocking a character is cheap compared with preparing them for Conflux, Fatebreaker, or Chaos.

My default pick is **Eustace** if I want the safest first test, **Beatrix** if I want melee, **Fediel / Fraux** only if I accept the 2-card early cost, and **Gallanza / Maglielle** when their Chapter Ø route and exclusive card unlock opens.

The first question I ask is:

<strong>Do I want to test this character, or am I ready to invest in them?</strong>

Those are different decisions. A quick unlock is fine. A full build needs a role, a weapon plan, sigils, and a reason to spend rare materials.

<h2 id="unlock-plan">
  New character unlock plan: cards, route progress, and first pick
</h2>

The most important resource trap is spending normal Crewmate Cards too quickly.

**Beatrix** and **Eustace** are the cleanest early tests because each uses **1 normal Crewmate Card**, and the early Conflux tutorial route can supply those cards. **Fraux** and **Fediel** are different because early unlocks can cost **2 normal Crewmate Cards** each, while waiting for their story route can save normal cards. **Gallanza** and **Maglielle** are not my first generic-card targets; I wait for the Chapter Ø Hard quest / cutscene route that hands over their exclusive Crewmate Cards.

<h2 id="beatrix-eustace">
  Beatrix and Eustace: the safest early tests
</h2>

**Beatrix** and **Eustace** are the easiest characters to test first because they each use **1 normal Crewmate Card** when available, and the early Conflux tutorial path can supply those first cards.

I pick **Eustace** first when I want a safer ranged character. My first 5-minute test is not a training dummy check; I want to see whether I can keep ranged uptime while moving, dodging, and setting up burst windows in a real fight.

I pick **Beatrix** first when I specifically want aggressive melee. My first 5-minute test is whether I can keep **Delta Clock** rhythm stable under pressure, not whether one burst number looks good.

<h2 id="fraux-fediel">
  Fraux and Fediel: spend 2 cards early or wait?
</h2>

**Fraux** and **Fediel** are the characters I do not unlock casually on a low-card account. If I unlock them early, I expect to spend **2 normal Crewmate Cards**. If I wait for their story route, I can save those normal cards for other tests.

That does not mean they are bad early unlocks. It means I need a reason.

I unlock **Fraux** early if I specifically want a technical stance / gauge character and I am willing to learn her before judging her damage. My first 5-minute test is whether stance and gauge changes feel natural, not whether she immediately outdamages an old main.

I unlock **Fediel** early if I want dark pressure, mobility, and a more involved character rhythm. My first 5-minute test is whether her movement, near/far pressure, and resource rhythm stay controlled when the boss starts moving.

<h2 id="gallanza-maglielle">
  Gallanza and Maglielle: Chapter Ø route and exclusive cards
</h2>

I do not treat **Gallanza** and **Maglielle** as first generic-card targets. After the required **Endless Ragnarok Chapter Ø Hard-difficulty quest** and its cutscene, the route can hand over their exclusive Crewmate Cards. If they are not available yet, I keep pushing the DLC route instead of spending normal cards blindly.

**Gallanza** is the heavy commitment test. My first 5-minute check is whether his spin pressure makes me greedy. If I keep eating punishment because I refuse to stop spinning, I wait before spending serious materials.

**Maglielle** is the control and gauge test. Her floating weapon style has obvious novelty appeal, but I still treat that curiosity as a test, not an investment decision. My first 5-minute check is whether veil gauge and floating weapon control feel smooth enough to build around.

<h2 id="faq">
  Granblue Relink Endless Ragnarok new characters FAQ
</h2>

### Should I unlock every new character right away?

No. I unlock the character I want to test first, then save cards and materials until I know who deserves a real build.

### Who is the safest first unlock?

**Eustace** is my safest first test because ranged uptime and mobility are easier to judge without committing to close-range pressure. **Beatrix** is my first pick if I specifically want melee.

### Should I spend 2 normal Crewmate Cards on Fraux or Fediel?

Only if I already want their style. If I am not in a hurry, I wait for the story route because their exclusive card path can save normal cards.

### What if Gallanza or Maglielle is not available yet?

Keep pushing Endless Ragnarok Chapter Ø and the required Hard-difficulty quest route. I do not spend generic cards blindly when their exclusive Crewmate Card path has not opened yet.

### Can I use the new characters as AI immediately?

I test manually first. A character can be strong by hand but still need AI behavior testing before becoming a party slot.

### When should I spend Chaos or late weapon materials?

Only after the role is clear. I use Conflux and safer quests first, then spend rare materials on the character I already trust.
