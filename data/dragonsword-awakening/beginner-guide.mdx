<h2 id="early-system-priority">
  What I do as each early system unlocks
</h2>

The interface does not give a reliable chapter-number answer for every system, so I
do not call this an exact unlock timetable. I use the order below as an action list:
what I do immediately and what I deliberately postpone.

### First Familiar: use it immediately

The basic Familiar opens during the first Guild request chain. I use it for ground
travel. Later dash, climbing, gliding, swimming, and diving abilities are also my
signal to leave movement-gated routes for a return visit.

### Fate's Invitation and Character Exchange: identify the missing team slot

The English item name is **Fate's Invitation**. It is used at the Exchange to
recruit a hero.

Story progression and Hero Logs support the recruitment route. I check a Hero Log's
reward first because it can also provide that hero's Exclusive Karma.

### Blacksmith: complete one useful set tier

I craft for the character who remains active most often. I do not build separate
sets for side heroes while the main damage dealer still lacks a working two-piece
or three-piece effect.

### Regional Missions: run one test, then decide whether to farm

Access is not proof that my team is ready. I try one relevant mission and use the
clear time, incoming damage, and reward to decide whether it belongs in my daily
route.

### World Bosses: target the right reward pool

A World Boss can drop basic Karma, but the result is random. I choose a pool that
contains a condition my core hero uses and do not treat one clear as a guaranteed
drop.

### Master Skills: act at level 30 with the required Grimoire

When the first core hero reaches **level 30** and has the required Grimoire, I use
Treasure Map rewards and Awakening Points on that hero instead of splitting them
across the roster.

For a safer first team, I unlock whichever member of **Cerese, Othello, or

three-character teams is complete.

<h2 id="signal-skills">
  Learn the Signal Skill chain before chasing damage
</h2>

The combat loop is not only two Active Skills followed by waiting for cooldowns.

My basic sequence is:

1. use an Active Skill;
2. continue landing safe attacks so the skill returns faster;
3. create the Break, Airborne, Stun, Bleed, or other condition required by a
   teammate;
4. confirm that the teammate icon is active;
5. use the Signal Skill;
6. switch or return to the main damage dealer after the safe follow-up ends.

Break is the easiest early condition to recognize.

When the follow-up does not activate, I check the condition before changing the
team.

A switch gives only a brief protected window, so I cross one timed hit and move.
If Super Armor prevents the expected launch, I stop the air combo and use shorter
attacks until Break or another safe opening appears.

<h2 id="gear-karma">
  Gear, main stats, and Karma without the early waste
</h2>

At the Blacksmith, I match the set to a condition the team creates reliably. A
Poison, Burn, Stun, or Airborne bonus is not useful merely because its number looks
offensive.

### Use the set tiers that actually exist

Azure Gale gives a concrete example:

- **2 Set:** Airborne Additional Damage **+15%**
- **3 Set:** Attack Power **+10% for 8 seconds** after using an Active Skill

The shown Azure Gale Top craft costs **5,000 Gold**. I check the current recipe
in my own Blacksmith because the material names and costs are the details that
matter if a balance patch changes the recipe.

- three pieces from the main elemental or condition set;
- two pieces from a second set with a reliable bonus.

### Confirm the main stat before crafting

On controller, opening the main-stat list is not the same as applying a selection.
I highlight the intended stat and confirm that it remains visibly selected before
the final craft input. A list or gear preview alone is not enough.

### Treat Forging Mold as a one-use decision

The Forging Mold tooltip says it is used when crafting equipment and is destroyed
after one use. I reserve it for a core piece rather than a story item I am likely to
replace.

### Use basic Karma before forcing an Exclusive Karma

I use matching basic Karma as a bridge and delay expensive enhancement while it
is temporary. I spend Exclusive Karma resources after the first team is fixed and
check the dismantle return before consuming an unwanted one.

<h2 id="dailies">
  When I start Regional Missions and World Bosses
</h2>

I enter one activity as a test. I leave and improve the main team when:

- normal enemies survive several complete rotations;
- one mistake removes most of the active character's health;
- the boss repeatedly reaches a reset boundary or inaccessible edge;
- or the listed reward does not solve my current bottleneck.

The Regional Mission screen displays:

- a daily reset at **9:00 AM**;
- **300 EXP** on each visible mission;
- three skill-material rewards on one mission;
- a higher-rarity crafting material on another;
- and **50,000 Gold** on “Defeat Kuaku the Mad.”

I prioritize the activity that fixes the current limit:

- Gold when crafting or skill upgrades are blocked;
- skill materials when the real rotation is underleveled;
- crafting materials when the main set tier is incomplete;
- basic Karma when the core hero still has no matching option.

Mission medals can also be exchanged for standard materials and skill scrolls. I use
the exchange to finish one upgrade, not to buy stacks of every material in advance.

### My early daily order

1. Regional Mission whose secondary reward fixes the current bottleneck.
2. World Boss with a relevant basic Karma reward pool.
3. Hero Log for a member of the core team.
4. Main-story progression when the team still needs a system or material source.
5. Optional chest and exploration cleanup after the important activities are done.

<h2 id="master-skill">
  Master Skills, Treasure Maps, and Awakening Points
</h2>

The Master Skill route becomes relevant at **level 30** with the required Grimoire.

Treasure Map search areas are not always flat circles with the chest in the center.
When the marker appears correct but I cannot see the chest, I check:

1. a cave entrance near the edge of the marked area;
2. an underwater level;
3. a lower ledge beneath the map marker;
4. bushes or terrain hiding the chest.

A Treasure Map chest can award **one or two Awakening Points**. I keep them on
the hero whose Master Skill route I am completing and return later if the chest
requires a movement option I have not unlocked.

### Can I eventually unlock every character?

The Exchange tooltip confirms that Fate's Invitation recruits a hero, while story
progression and Hero Logs support the recruitment route. I have not confirmed an
account-wide guarantee that every hero can be obtained without a limit, so I do not
spend invitations under that assumption.

### Are there permanent missables?

I have not confirmed a permanent lockout for the movement-gated routes covered
here. Moon Key chests, underwater paths, and Familiar-gated ledges are return-later
objectives, but that does not prove every side quest in every chapter is
non-missable.

### Should I dismantle an Exclusive Karma I do not use?

Only after the first team is settled. I check that it is unequipped, not needed for
an immediate ascension, and that the dismantle preview returns the exchange value I
expect.

### Why is the Treasure Map chest not inside the visible circle?

The objective can be above or below the map level. I check caves, underwater paths,
lower ledges, and bushes before assuming the marker is broken.

### What time do Regional Missions reset?

The English menu displays **9:00 AM**, but that panel does not state a time zone. I
use the in-game countdown or observe one reset before planning a real-world daily
schedule.

### Is Lute worth upgrading early?

I keep Lute strong enough to survive and heal. The main damage dealer's set,
Karma, and frequently used skills receive the first premium investment.
