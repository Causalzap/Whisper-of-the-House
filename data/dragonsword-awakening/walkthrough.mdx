DragonSword Awakening has eight main-story chapters, but the hardest progression
problems are rarely ordinary damage checks. I am more often blocked by a route that
looks closed, an objective that moves to another map level, or a scripted failure
that looks like a broken quest.

This walkthrough follows the Chapter 1–8 main route and focuses on those decisions:
where I go next, which combat state must be finished before the marker advances, and
when an apparent failure is actually the intended story route.

### Route-adjacent loot vs later cleanup

I take visible chests and short detours that sit beside the active route. I leave
underwater paths, Moon Key chests, and ledges that clearly require a later Familiar
or movement ability.

Repeatedly circling a movement-gated object usually wastes more time than returning
after the related system opens.

<h2 id="chapter-1">
  Chapter 1: Reach Orbis, join the Guild, and open the Goblin cave
</h2>

**Starting route:** Follow Castella through the opening escape and reach Orbis.

**Main blocker:** The Goblin treasure cave below Elli's hill looks sealed.

**Chapter completion signal:** The first Guild loop and cave are finished, and the
party returns to Orbis.

### Reach Orbis and join Johnny's mercenary group

The opening path is mostly linear. I follow Castella, clear the enemies blocking the
escape route, and continue toward Orbis.

After the city meetings, Lute joins Johnny, Castella, and Aria. The story group now
contains four people, but the active combat party still uses three heroes.

I continue into the Mercenary Guild route before investing in a second complete
team.

### Accept both early Guild requests before leaving

At the Guild Office, I accept the lunch delivery and lost-sheep requests together.
They share the same outward route.

My order is:

1. accept both requests;
2. unlock and summon the first Familiar;
3. deliver the lunch;
4. continue to Elli's farm above the large-tree hill;
5. speak with Elli and recover the sheep;
6. clear the Goblin camp when the request expands;
7. open the treasure cave below the hill;
8. return to Orbis after the cave encounter.

### Find the sheep before returning to the Guild

After delivering the lunch, I continue to the farm. Elli waits near the upper route
around the large-tree hill.

I speak with her first, follow the nearby search path, and bring the sheep back. The
request then expands into the Goblin camp objective.

Finish that extension while already outside Orbis. Returning immediately after the
sheep only creates another trip through the same area.

### Blow open the Goblin treasure cave

After the camp, Elli points toward the cave below the hill. A large rock covers the
entrance.

The solution is beside the rock:

1. face the blocked entrance;
2. locate the explosive barrels beside it;
3. create enough distance to avoid the blast;
4. shoot the barrels;
5. enter after the rock breaks.

<h2 id="chapter-2">
  Chapter 2: Recover Alboin's gear and activate the ancient device
</h2>

**Starting route:** Meet Alboin beside the partially disassembled device.

**Main blocker:** Returning the large gear does not immediately activate the
mechanism.

**Chapter completion signal:** The ancient device visibly begins operating.

Alboin explains that he dismantled part of the ancient machine while studying it and
that Goblins stole a component shaped like a large gear.

### Follow the Goblins away from the device

The missing part is not hidden around the machine.

My route is:

1. speak with Alboin;
2. follow the direction the Goblins escaped;
3. clear the groups blocking the path;
4. recover the large gear-shaped component;
5. return to Alboin;
6. install the component;
7. remain beside the machine for the remaining interaction.

### Use the additional control after returning the gear

The first restored state can look incomplete. Leaving at this point makes the
objective appear bugged.

Stay in the device area and interact with the additional control. The successful
state is not merely the gear sitting in place; the mechanism itself starts moving.

<h2 id="chapter-3">
  Chapter 3: Enter the Dragon Cult altar and continue after the teleport
</h2>

**Starting route:** Return to the Organa Order and accept the altar retrieval
objective connected to Isera's funeral.

**Main blocker:** The worship object triggers a forced teleport instead of completing
the retrieval.

**Chapter completion signal:** The party escapes the altar and completes the funeral
and faction-letter sequence.

The Order sends the party to a discovered Dragon Cult altar to retrieve a worship
object. The recovered Dragon Dust is intended for the funeral preparations.

### Treat the teleport as part of the altar

The confusing moment occurs when the party reaches the apparent objective. Touching
it triggers a trap and moves everyone deeper into the ruin.

The new area is not a separate open-world destination, and the route has not failed.

After the teleport:

1. confirm the party is still inside the Dragon Cult architecture;
2. use the statues, altar walls, and corridors to orient the route;
3. follow the only path that continues deeper;
4. clear the enemies blocking the interior;
5. finish the real retrieval and exit through the story route.

### Finish the funeral and faction-letter loop

After returning, the Order continues the funeral preparations. The party then
delivers letters explaining the Order's intentions to the other major factions,
including the royal side and Amber merchants.

Complete these deliveries before repeatedly checking the Mercenary Guild for another
normal job. The next progression comes from the faction conversations and story
summons.

<h2 id="chapter-4">
  Chapter 4: Bardok's note, the Barrier Stone, and the northern fortress
</h2>

**Starting route:** Wake in the village and speak with Karina.

**Main blocker:** The Barrier Stone route sounds like a material-gathering problem,
but the preparation is already complete.

**Chapter completion signal:** The restoration is reported to Ewald and the story
assigns the northern fortress route.

### Go to the hill behind the village

Karina explains that a hooded person moved the party to safety and asked them to come
to the hill behind the village.

Follow that instruction immediately after the opening conversations.

At the hill, the party receives:

- Bardok's note;
- the method for restoring the Barrier Stone;
- and confirmation that the required preparation has already been left in place.

This is not a request to farm a new crafting ingredient.

### Complete the prepared Barrier Stone route

The reliable order is:

1. wake and speak with Karina;
2. go to the hill behind the village;
3. receive Bardok's note and restoration instructions;
4. follow the active marker through the prepared route;
5. clear enemies blocking the story interaction;
6. use the available Barrier Stone interaction;
7. return to Ewald when the objective changes.

The story—not an inventory checklist—confirms completion by moving the party back to
the royal report and the northern assignment.

The source material confirms this sequence but does not preserve a reliable English
client label for every interaction. I therefore do not invent an exact quest title
or button prompt.

### Resolve the fortress attack before searching for the contact

The northern visit begins as information gathering, but the fortress comes under
attack as the party arrives.

Finish the active attackers first. Running past the battle toward the earlier marker
leaves the defense state unresolved.

After the immediate threat is cleared, resume the conversations and investigation
that lead into Chapter 5.

<h2 id="chapter-5">
  Chapter 5: Golem trail, fog cliff, Rock Tribe, Reina rescue, and fortress defense
</h2>

**Starting route:** Join the northern investigation after the fortress report.

**Main blocker:** The apparent road is deliberately blocked while the objective
remains on the other side.

**Chapter completion signal:** Reina returns to the fortress and the defense sequence
reaches its immediate aftermath.

### Inspect the attacked cart and follow the golem trail

The party follows tracks through the fog and finds an attacked research or scout
route. A survivor explains that Dragon Cultists are attempting to wake and move a
golem toward the fortress.

The key sequence is:

1. follow the cart or dragged-object tracks;
2. inspect the attacked site;
3. speak with the survivor;
4. secure the survivor as the story directs;
5. pursue the cultist and golem route immediately.

The blocked path that follows is part of the ambush route.

### Climb beside the blocked fog road

At the obstruction, the marker remains beyond the road. The route is vertical.

1. stop at the blocked road;
2. look beside the obstruction;
3. use the climbable cliff;
4. continue across the upper path;
5. drop back toward the cultist route after passing the blockage.

### Continue deeper when the golem has not moved

After catching the cultists, the party checks for movement traces. When the golem has
not yet left, search farther inside rather than returning to the fortress.

The objective shifts toward the original golem area and the Rock Tribe
investigation.

### Infiltrate the Rock Tribe while Reina creates the distraction

The knights draw the Orcs' attention while the party enters to gather information.

Finish the infiltration state before returning:

- clear the groups assigned to the infiltration route;
- inspect the prepared weapons and supplies;
- defeat the high-ranking Rock Tribe warrior blocking the escape;
- finish the conversation confirming what the Orcs are preparing.

Leaving after the first Orc group does not complete the investigation.

### Rescue Reina without searching indefinitely for the Lich

A surviving apprentice reports that Reina's unit was overwhelmed by three sources of
pressure:

- charging Orcs;
- Skeleton ambushes and repeated resurrection;
- and a Lich's magic.

However, in the recorded rescue phase, the Lich is not visible after the party
arrives. Reina later confirms that the caster was no longer present.

My rescue priority is therefore:

1. follow the apprentice to the marked entrance;
2. break through the Orc and Skeleton groups surrounding the route;
3. clear enemies trapping the active character;
4. reach Reina instead of searching side areas for the missing Lich;
5. finish the active rescue combat state;
6. listen for the immediate return order.

### Return when the fortress attack warning appears

After the investigation and rescue, the story warns that the attack may begin within
a day, two days, or immediately.

Once the return objective appears, stop clearing northern side paths.

At the fortress:

1. follow Reina to each active defense point;
2. finish the marked group before moving;
3. keep enemies near the defense area rather than chasing distant stragglers;
4. preserve the main damage dealer for the larger threats after the regular waves.

The defense transitions into the wider True Dragon crisis and the next Six Heroes
route.

<h2 id="chapter-6">
  Chapter 6: Deliver Ewald's letter, meet Ropi, and solve the mural door
</h2>

**Starting route:** Return to Ewald after the fortress operation.

**Main blocker:** Chapter 6 combines a long character-following route with a ruin door
whose key was shown earlier.

**Chapter completion signal:** The Ropi scenes and later mural-door route are
complete, and the story moves into the True Dragon response.

### Deliver the letter to Ropi's old cabin

Ewald believes Ropi is still near Orbis and asks the party to leave a letter where he
previously stayed.

The route is:

1. receive Ewald's Six Heroes request;
2. travel to the old cabin;
3. clear the monsters gathered around it;
4. enter and place the letter in the visible location;
5. inspect the cabin until Ropi appears;
6. listen for his message about the meeting place;
7. report the response to Ewald.

Do not wait beside the letter for a separate pickup. Ropi appears as part of the
cabin scene and supplies the next appointment.

### Follow the meeting route instead of waiting in Orbis

After the report, Ewald leaves for the meeting. Follow the current travel and
dialogue markers.

The route later connects with other Six Heroes information and leads to Ropi's
meeting. Continue through the active conversations rather than repeatedly returning
to the palace or cabin.

Ropi refuses Ewald's request to reunite around Orbis, but he provides information
about the approaching True Dragon.

### Ropi's offer to Lute is scripted

Ropi later asks Lute to leave with him. Lute refuses because he wants to remain close
to people he can help.

This is a story scene, not a route branch:

- Lute remains with the current group;
- there is no alternate map exit to choose;
- and the main objective resumes after the conversation.

### Use the first mural as the later door key

Chapter 6 includes a ruin route with two connected mural moments.

The useful solution is:

1. inspect the first mural when the party identifies it as the clue;
2. keep its arrangement in mind;
3. continue until the later inner door appears inactive;
4. inspect the mural in that room;
5. reproduce the earlier wall arrangement using the room mechanism;
6. continue when the door opens.

> The mural is a visual key supplied earlier in the same route. There is no reason to
> leave the ruin and search outside for a separate lever.

The transcript confirms that copying the earlier wall opens the door, but it does not
preserve a reliable text description of every visual symbol or input. I do not add an
invented symbol order.

<h2 id="chapter-7">
  Chapter 7: Gather True Dragon intelligence and prepare Eona's Legacy
</h2>

**Starting route:** Accept the kingdom-backed large-scale True Dragon request.

**Main blocker:** The party can spend too long searching for Alboin before the palace
meeting.

**Chapter completion signal:** The operation roles, lightning countermeasure, and
departure to the designated hills are confirmed.

Chapter 7 explains why the Chapter 8 plan uses a living Dragon, an egg, a trap, a
lightning-absorbing crystal, and Eona's Legacy.

### Finish the faction information loop

The route gathers information from the royal side, the Organa Order, Amber merchants,
and Alboin.

The useful conclusions are:

- the True Dragon moves too quickly to track continuously;
- it slows down because it hunts and consumes other Dragons;
- the cult has internal factions rather than one perfectly unified command;
- and the ancient records point toward Eona's Legacy.

### Go to the palace when the meeting begins

The party attempts to find Alboin before the announced royal meeting, but he may not
appear on the city search route before the deadline.

When the meeting time arrives:

1. stop searching the city;
2. go to the palace;
3. complete the current faction conversation markers;
4. enter the operation briefing.

Alboin appears there as the expert presenting the ancient research. Missing him in
the city is not a progression failure.

### Learn why Eona's Legacy is the finishing plan

At the briefing, Alboin connects the ancient record to **Eona's Legacy** and explains
that it is an ancient weapon, not merely a research device.

The operation assigns distinct roles:

- royal knights establish and tighten the perimeter;
- the trap team prepares the restraint;
- the Order supports the trap and barrier;
- the party searches for a Dragon nest;
- Kalien draws the adult Dragon away;
- Johnny's group takes the egg to the trap area;
- Lute prepares Eona's Legacy;
- other knights and mercenaries clear monsters drawn into the operation.

The egg is bait. The adult Dragon must remain alive long enough to attract the True
Dragon, and Eona's Legacy is intended to finish the target.

### Understand the lightning-crystal test

The operation also uses a crystal that reacts to lightning.

The test establishes:

- ordinary physical attacks do not easily break it;
- it absorbs nearby lightning energy;
- it has a finite capacity;
- and it explodes after enough lightning is stored.

This explains why the kingdom chooses a location containing the crystal. Ursula's
lightning can become part of the operation rather than only an environmental threat.

### Finish the preparation markers before searching for the nest

At the operation area, complete the assigned preparation objectives before wandering
into the Dragon nest search.

The trap team may request nearby local materials used to keep Worms away long enough
for the operation. Collect the marked items and return before beginning the main nest
route.

<h2 id="chapter-8">
  Chapter 8: Find the nest, lose the Dragon Egg, and finish the Ursula operation
</h2>

**Starting route:** Search the designated hills for a Dragon and an intact egg.

**Main blocker:** Several planned steps fail, but each failure creates the next
objective.

**Chapter completion signal:** Eona's Legacy fires and the royal ceremony and
follow-up conversations finish.

### Follow the large Worm tunnel after finding broken eggs

The first nest search may lead to broken eggs and signs that Worms attacked the area.

When that happens, inspect the route rather than searching the same chamber:

1. look for an unusually large, smooth Worm tunnel;
2. confirm that the traces come from another direction;
3. use the tunnel to cross the mountain route;
4. continue where Dragon and Worm battle marks remain;
5. reach the intact nest.

The large tunnel is the route to the successful nest, not only background scenery.

### Wait for Kalien before taking the egg

At the intact nest:

1. let the operation threaten or distract the adult Dragon;
2. wait for Kalien to draw it away;
3. enter after the Dragon leaves;
4. take the egg;
5. begin the return route.

Fighting the adult Dragon to the death at its nest is not the intended objective.

### Chase the Worm when it steals the egg

A large Worm takes the egg during the return.

Follow it immediately:

1. enter the large Worm tunnel;
2. continue toward its nest;
3. clear only groups blocking the pursuit;
4. search the marked chamber;
5. return toward the surface when the objective changes.

<h2 id="faq">
  DragonSword Awakening walkthrough FAQ
</h2>

### How many main-story chapters are in DragonSword Awakening?

The completed main route covered here contains eight chapters.

### Should I clear every chest before leaving a chapter?

No. I take visible chests and short detours, but leave routes that require underwater
travel, a Moon Key, a later Familiar skill, or another locked movement option.

### When should I stop the story and upgrade?

Stop when normal enemies survive several full rotations, one boss hit removes most
of a hero's health, or the team cannot create its own Signal conditions. A new menu
appearing is not by itself a reason to pause the story.

### Is Ropi's offer a route choice?

Not in the Chapter 6 route covered here. Lute remains with the group and the main
story continues after the scripted conversation.

### Does the Chapter 6 transcript provide the exact mural symbol order?

No. It confirms that the earlier mural is copied to open the later door, but the text
material does not preserve a reliable symbol-by-symbol answer. A screenshot of the
reference mural and mechanism is still the best future addition.

### Can I recover the Dragon Egg and restore the original plan?

The party pursues the Worm, but the operation continues after the objective returns
to the surface. Do not keep searching every chamber after the marker changes.

### Is the final battle over when the combat UI disappears?

No. Continue through the battlefield aftermath, Orbis return, royal ceremony, and
later conversations.

### Does this walkthrough prove that the game has only one ending?

No. It covers the complete scripted Chapter 1–8 route shown in the source material.
That does not prove that no hidden condition, future update, or later expansion can
add another ending.
