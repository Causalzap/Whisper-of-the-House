DragonSword Awakening Chapter 8 is not one stationary final-boss arena. The operation
starts with a Dragon Egg plan, changes when a Worm steals the egg, returns to an
improvised trap, and then becomes a multi-location pursuit against **Lv.70 Ursula**.

The practical rule is simple: follow the current objective even when the original
plan fails. The missing Dragon Egg is scripted, Ursula leaves the battlefield twice,
the second retreat leads to a signal flare, and the operation ends only after Eona's
Legacy fires.

<h2 id="before-final-operation">
  What I prepare before the final operation
</h2>

The boss interface confirms **Lv.70 Ursula**, but that does not mean all three party
members must be exactly Level 70. I care more about whether the team can survive and
complete its actual rotation.

The setup I use has:

- one active hero who can survive a normal boss hit;
- gear and Karma equipped on all three characters;
- at least one condition that reliably activates a teammate Signal Skill;
- a completed two-piece or three-piece set effect on the main damage dealer;
- a secondary hero who can enter without dying immediately;
- and enough healing or survivability to learn one unfamiliar pattern.

The Chapter 8 route includes Level 60 Worm enemies. When those groups survive several
complete rotations, I upgrade the active hero before committing to the final
operation.

I also stop expensive rerolls once the build is functional. A late reforge can cost
**50,000 Gold**, so one acceptable working set is more useful here than draining the
remaining Gold while chasing a perfect substat.

### Which team I use against Ursula

For learning the fight, I prefer **Cerese, Othello, and Dana**. The ranged phase keeps
more of Ursula and the lightning-marked ground visible, while Othello handles the
closer part of the encounter.

I use **Charlotte, Johnny, and Reina** after I can read the patterns reliably.
Charlotte provides faster close pressure, Johnny returns the sequence to the ground,
and Reina restores movement between attack windows.

A functional core plus **Lute** is useful when healing is the only real failure. Lute
does not fix a lineup whose conditions never activate the next Signal input.

### Lightning management

I preserve defensive actions instead of spending every dodge and swap on the first
small hit.

My order is:

1. walk or sprint out of a slow ground warning;
2. dodge the committed impact;
3. use a character switch only when I need to cross one clearly timed hit;
4. move as soon as the incoming hero lands;
5. wait outside a persistent lightning field until the ground clears.

A switch gives only a brief protected entry. It can cross one impact, but it cannot
keep the new character safe inside continuing lightning.

### Super Armor and failed aerial routes

When Ursula remains active through the hits, I end the long aerial route. Continuing
to attack into Super Armor makes the next counterattack harder to see and removes the
movement needed to escape it.

I land, move to the side, use shorter Active Skill windows, and rebuild the next
Break or Signal opportunity with safe hits.

<h2 id="ending-explained">
  DragonSword Awakening ending explained
</h2>

The ending resolves the immediate True Dragon crisis but leaves the larger mysteries
open.

### Why the victory changes Orbis

For roughly 60 years, Orbis has relied on the story of the Six Heroes. That legend
became limiting because people began to treat True Dragons as enemies only the old
heroes could defeat.

The Chapter 8 operation succeeds through cooperation among knights, the Order,
merchants, mercenaries, researchers, and the current party. The result proves that
the continent can organize against a True Dragon instead of waiting for the Six
Heroes to return.

### What “Dragon Sword” means

At the royal ceremony, Kalsion explains that the Six Heroes represented hope during
an earlier dark period, but the present generation now has to create its own history.

“Dragon Sword” is therefore not only the name of one weapon. It becomes a title for
the new generation taking over the protective role once associated with the Six
Heroes.

Johnny accepts the idea enthusiastically. Lute is less comfortable claiming a heroic
identity and treats the continuing protection of Orbis more like another mercenary
request.

### Who Ropi is

Ropi is one of the Six Heroes and Lute's grandfather.

Lute refuses to accept that Ropi simply betrayed his companions. He intends to
discover why Ropi disappeared and what he has been doing.

This leaves Ropi as an ongoing mystery, not a confirmed final villain.

### Who fired Eona's Legacy?

Chapter 8 does not identify the operator.

A person is shown at the ancient device, and the Order later says the figure
resembled a Dragon Cultist. That is suspicion rather than confirmation. The scene
also leaves open a connection to the device or Alboin, but the completed route does
not prove the identity.

### What Lute's Dragon power means

The ending confirms that Kalsion witnessed a powerful Dragon-like force from Lute.
Lute does not fully understand it, and Ropi never explained it to him.

The story therefore confirms the power's existence while leaving its source, limit,
and purpose for later content.

<h2 id="multiple-endings">
  Does DragonSword Awakening have multiple endings?
</h2>

The completed Chapter 1–8 route leads to one scripted main-story ending.

The route contains no:

- final dialogue choice;
- spare-or-kill decision for Ursula;
- branch based on recovering the Dragon Egg;
- choice to accept or reject Lute's power;
- or route selection during the royal ceremony.

That supports describing this as the current main ending. It does not rule out a
future expansion, update, or undiscovered condition adding other content later.

<h2 id="troubleshooting">
  Common Chapter 8 and Ursula problems
</h2>

### The Dragon Egg route looks failed

Follow the Worm into its nest. Return to the operation area when the objective
changes; the mission continues without restoring the original egg plan.

### Ursula disappeared after the fight started

Follow the relocated objective immediately. Ursula moves the battle into another
area instead of returning to the empty arena.

### Ursula retreated again

The second retreat leads to the signal flare and allied regrouping sequence. Continue
with the new objective rather than returning to the previous battlefield.

### A character switch still took damage

The protected entry is brief. Use it to cross one timed impact, then move out of the
continuing attack or lightning field.

### Ursula will not stay in an aerial combo

End the aerial route when she remains active through the hits. Super Armor turns a
long extension into an unsafe counterattack window.

### The battle ended but Chapter 8 did not complete

Continue through the battlefield aftermath, Orbis scenes, royal ceremony, and later
Ropi discussion.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ursula final boss and ending FAQ
</h2>

### What level is Ursula?

The final-boss interface displays **Lv.70 Ursula**. This confirms the boss level, not
a requirement that every party member must be exactly Level 70.

### Should I return to Orbis after losing the Dragon Egg?

No. Follow the Worm, clear the nest route, and return to the operation area when the
objective changes.

### Is Ursula weak to Shock, Poison, Ice, and Air?

Those labels appear during combat, but the interface does not identify all four as
permanent elemental weaknesses. Keep using the condition your team triggers
reliably.

### How many times does Ursula leave the arena?

She leaves after the initial transformed encounter and retreats again after the next
fight. The second retreat leads to the signal flare and regrouping phase.

### Who defeats Ursula?

The party and allied forces keep the operation alive long enough for Eona's Legacy
to deliver the finishing attack. The operator of the ancient device is not identified.

### Does Lute become a Dragon?

Lute displays Dragon-like power, but Chapter 8 does not explain whether he is a
Dragon, where the power comes from, or whether the change is permanent.

### Is Ropi the final villain?

The ending does not establish that. Ropi is Lute's grandfather and one of the Six
Heroes, but his motives and current actions remain unresolved.
