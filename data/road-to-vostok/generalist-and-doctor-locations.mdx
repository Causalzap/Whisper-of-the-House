**The Generalist is the first trader you should learn. Exit your Cabin, turn right, follow the train tracks, and look for the first big warehouse building on the left. He appears on Day 1. 1. **Generalist: Cabin -> right turn -> train tracks -> first big warehouse on the left**
2. **Doctor: Day 2+ only -> School transition -> bridge -> school on the left -> nearest door -> stairs on the right -> top floor -> first door on the right**
3. **Go Generalist first for food, water, ammo, space, and junk conversion**
4. **Go Doctor when you need real medical depth, painkillers, bandages, antiseptic, or Doctor-side tasks**
5. **Treat Gunsmith as later progression context, not your first priority**

<h2 id="where-is-the-generalist">
  Where is the Generalist in Road to Vostok?
</h2>

The **Generalist** is the easiest trader to reach and the most important one for stabilizing your first runs.

Use this exact route:

1. **Exit your Cabin**
2. **Turn right**
3. **Follow the train tracks**
4. **Look for the first large warehouse building on the left**
5. **Enter the building and find the Generalist inside**

That is the version most new players actually need.

“Near the Cabin” is true, but it is not precise enough when you are still learning the Village layout.

The reason this trader matters so much early is not just his location.

It is that he solves the most common early-run problems:

- too much junk
- not enough water
- not enough food
- mismatched ammo
- no spare magazine
- no room left for the next phase

That makes the **Generalist** your first real stabilizer.

<h2 id="what-the-generalist-is-actually-for">
  What the Generalist is actually for
</h2>

Do not think of the **Generalist** as “the junk guy.”

Think of him as the trader that turns a messy first loop into a playable second loop.

He is usually your best answer when:

- your backpack is full of wires, tape, low-tier junk, clothes, or random sellables
- you are low on water or food
- your ammo situation is mismatched
- you need a basic weapon fix rather than a dream loadout
- you need to clear backpack space before deciding whether to keep pushing

He is also where a lot of your early routing discipline begins.

If the Village loop gives you dead weight, the **Generalist** converts that into:

- basic survival supplies
- usable ammo
- more manageable carry space
- better early task momentum

That is why he should usually come **before** the Doctor in your first few runs.

<h2 id="where-is-the-doctor">
  Where is the Doctor in Road to Vostok?
</h2>

The **Doctor** is not part of your default first Village loop.

He only appears on **Day 2**, and reaching him requires a transition into the **School** map.

Once you enter the School area, use this exact navigation:

1. **Go straight ahead**
2. **Cross the bridge**
3. **Look left for the large school building**
4. **Enter through the nearest door**
5. **Find the stairs on your right**
6. **Go to the highest floor**
7. **Enter the corridor**
8. **Go through the first door on the right**

That is the Doctor.

This is much more reliable than just saying “third floor, first room.”

It tells the player how to solve the building, not just where the NPC technically ends up.

<GuideCallout title="Important Day 2 warning">
The Doctor does **not** function as a Day 1 trader.

If you go to School before he appears, you are taking on travel cost and combat risk without getting the medical trader payoff that makes the trip worth it.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="when-the-doctor-trip-is-worth-it">
  When is the Doctor trip actually worth it?
</h2>

The **Doctor** becomes worth the travel when your run has moved beyond basic survival trouble and into real medical risk.

That usually means one of these is true:

- you are short on **bandages**
- you need **painkillers**
- you need **antiseptic**
- you need **rags** for improvised medical crafting
- your next fights are likely to be harder than your current healing can support
- you want to start building **Doctor-side task progression**

In other words:

the Generalist helps you stay alive now, while the Doctor helps you avoid collapsing later.

That is why the Doctor trip is strongest when your bag is not just full of junk, but full of **medical need**.

<h2 id="which-trader-first">
  Which trader should you visit first?
</h2>

Use this decision rule.

### Go to the Generalist first if you need:
- food
- water
- basic ammo
- a spare mag
- more backpack space
- a way to convert junk into immediate survival value

### Go to the Doctor first if you need:
- bandages
- painkillers
- antiseptic
- rags
- stronger medical depth before a harder push
- Doctor-side tasks or classroom progression

### Default rule for most early runs
**Generalist first, Doctor second.**

That is still the best default because most failed early runs die from:

- bad inventory state
- weak hydration
- no practical ammo plan
- too much dead weight

not from a lack of specialized medical barters on minute one.

<h2 id="tasks-matter-more-than-random-trades">
  Why tasks matter more than random trades
</h2>

This is the part many merchant-location pages miss.

The traders are not just there to sell you things.

They shape your **routing**, your **shelter network**, and your **risk tolerance**.

### Generalist task value
One of the best early progression wins is the **Attic Shelter** path.

That matters because the Attic is a **Village-side** upgrade, not a School-side one.

It improves your storage and reset rhythm where you are already doing most of your first farming and task completion.

### Doctor task value
The Doctor matters for more than medicine.

He is tied to the **Classroom Shelter** path, which is the School-side shelter progression you actually care about once School stops being a one-off visit and starts becoming a repeat route.

### Practical takeaway
A trader visit is often worth more because of the **task chain and shelter key path** than because of one single item in stock.

That is why you should record trader tasks instead of treating them like flavor text.

<GuideCallout title="Best early strategic shortcut">
Do not ask only, “What can this trader sell me right now?”

Ask:

**“Does this trader unlock a better shelter loop for the routes I want to run next?”**

That question usually leads to better progression decisions than chasing one lucky barter.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="shelters-and-risk">
  Shelters and risk: what to unlock first
</h2>

For early progression, the shelter logic should be:

1. **Use Cabin as your default safety anchor**
2. **Prioritize the Attic path through Generalist-side progression**
3. **Treat Classroom as your School-side upgrade once Doctor tasks become relevant**
4. **Do not confuse Village-side convenience with School-side safety**

This is important because a lot of players mix these up.

The **Attic** makes your Village loop better.

The **Classroom** makes your School runs less fragile.

Those are related, but they are not the same thing.

<h2 id="do-not-carry-task-items-carelessly">
  Do not carry task items carelessly
</h2>

This is one of the highest-value beginner warnings on the page.

If an item matters for a task, a shelter key path, or a high-value barter, do **not** keep dragging it around longer than necessary.

Why?

Because in normal runs, death still costs **everything you are carrying** on that run.

And if you push into **Vostok**, the punishment is even harsher.

That means your safest habit is:

- complete the immediate task turn-in, or
- stash the item in shelter storage before doing anything risky

Do not treat task items like pocket clutter.

Treat them like progression.

<GuideCallout title="Simple survival rule">
If you would be angry to lose an item, do not take it on a “maybe I’ll check one more building” run.

Bank it first.
</GuideCallout>

### Where is the Generalist in Road to Vostok?

Exit the **Cabin**, turn **right**, follow the **train tracks**, and enter the **first large warehouse building on the left**.

### When does the Generalist appear?

The **Generalist** is available on **Day 1**.
