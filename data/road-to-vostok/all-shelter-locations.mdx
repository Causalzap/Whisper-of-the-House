<GuideCallout title="About this guide">
This page is built for the question shelter searches are really trying to solve in **Road to Vostok**:

**Which shelters actually matter first, how do you find them fast, how do you unlock them, and which ones are worth rushing instead of just knowing where they are?**

Instead of treating shelters like a flat location list, this guide focuses on:

- all current shelter locations in the **current Early Access build**
- the **fastest practical navigation** to each one
- the difference between **good shelters** and **good first shelters**
- why **Attic**, **Tent**, and **Bunker** matter more to progression than their unlock drama might suggest
- why **Classroom** is powerful but often over-prioritized
- why **Apartment Room** belongs to late-game planning, not early-game ambition
</GuideCallout>

<GuideQuickAnswer title="Road to Vostok All Shelter Locations">
**Road to Vostok currently revolves around six practical shelters: the Cabin, Attic, Classroom, Tent, Bunker, and Apartment Room. The best early shelter upgrade is the Attic because it improves the Village loop you are already running. The best first forward shelter is the Tent because it gives you immediate Outpost safety. The best mid-game shelter is the Bunker. The Classroom is useful but much more expensive than it first looks, and the Apartment Room is a late-game luxury inside the permadeath side of the map.**

If you only want the shortest useful version:

1. **Cabin is your starting shelter and first true bank**
2. **Attic is the best early upgrade**
3. **Tent is your first forward shelter and should not be ignored**
4. **Bunker is the best mid-game shelter**
5. **Classroom is strong but dangerous to rush**
6. **Apartment Room is late-game, not early-game**
7. **The best shelter is not the hardest one to unlock**
8. **The best shelter is the one that improves your route network earliest for the least risk**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h3 id="cabin-village-area-05">
  Cabin (Village, Area 05)
</h3>

The <strong>Cabin</strong> is your starting shelter.

You do not need to unlock it, and that matters more than it sounds.

This is not just where the game begins.
It is where your first stable economy begins.

### How to find it quickly
You start here in Standard, so the real navigation lesson is learning how to return to it mentally.
Treat the Cabin as your western anchor: if you can still picture the train tracks, the Generalist barn, and the Village exit lanes relative to the Cabin, your whole Area 05 loop becomes easier to recover.

What it is actually good for:

- your first stash
- your first safe save point
- your Village reset loop
- your early medicine, food, and spare-gear bank

A lot of players mentally downgrade the Cabin because it is “default.”

That is the wrong way to think about it.

The Cabin is the foundation every better shelter depends on.

<h3 id="attic-village-area-05">
  Attic (Village, Area 05)
</h3>

The <strong>Attic</strong> is located directly above the <strong>Generalist</strong> in the Village.

This is the shelter most players should want first.

### How to find it quickly
Exit your Cabin and turn right.
Follow the train tracks until you see the red trader building on the left side of the tracks.
That building is the Generalist shop, and the Attic is directly above it.

### Official unlock route
The cleanest progression route is the <strong>Generalist task path</strong>.
Current task coverage points to his final task chain as the route that awards the <strong>Attic key</strong>.

### Delivery bug note
If the Generalist task interface looks stuck and does not seem to accept the required delivery items, manually press <strong>Start Delivery</strong> in the trader task window before assuming the chain is broken.
That small UI step is easy to miss and can make the Attic route feel more confusing than it really is.

### Community shortcut note
There are also community reports that the key can appear as rare loose loot in the School sports-hall route.
Treat that as a lucky shortcut, not as a route you should build your plan around.

What it is actually good for:

- expanding your safest area
- staying close to the Generalist
- making Village storage less cramped
- improving the loop you are already running instead of forcing a new one

Should you rush it?

<strong>Yes.</strong>

This is the best early upgrade because it gives you more space in the least punishing part of the map.

<h3 id="classroom-school-area-05">
  Classroom (School, Area 05)
</h3>

The <strong>Classroom</strong> sits in the <strong>School</strong> map.

On paper, it looks like one of the strongest shelters in the game because it creates a real School-side anchor.

In practice, it is often over-prioritized.

### How to find it quickly
From the Cabin, turn right and follow the train tracks to the eastern side of Village.
Keep following the map edge until you reach the School transition, then load into School and move straight over the bridge toward the large school building on your left.
Enter through the nearest door, use the staircase on your right, climb to the top floor, and follow the same corridor route used for the Doctor; the Classroom shelter is one of the locked rooms on that upper level.

### Official unlock route
Current task coverage ties the <strong>Classroom key</strong> to the <strong>Doctor</strong> and the oil-sample task chain.

This is the part many short guides under-explain:

- you need <strong>three oil samples</strong>
- the samples are tied to the <strong>Terminal / Vostok permadeath side</strong>
- current guide coverage and player reports consistently describe this as an extremely dangerous errand
- for most players, getting all three in one run is not a realistic expectation

That is why the Classroom is not just a “School shelter.”
It is a shelter whose official path asks you to pay Vostok-level risk before the room becomes yours.

### Community shortcut note
There are community reports of lucky key finds in the School gymnasium route.
That can happen, but it should be treated as a bonus outcome rather than as the default plan.

What it is actually good for:

- turning School into a repeatable base instead of a one-off run
- lowering the fragility of repeated School trips
- supporting Doctor-side progression
- reducing the feeling that every School push is a full there-and-back commitment

Should you rush it?

<strong>Usually no.</strong>

The Classroom is good.
It is just often worse than it looks when measured against unlock cost and risk.

<GuideCallout title="Classroom reality check">
A good shelter is not automatically a good first shelter.

The Classroom becomes far more attractive once your eastern routes are stable and your gear quality can support the unlock pressure.
</GuideCallout>

<h3 id="tent-outpost-border-zone">
  Tent (Outpost, Border Zone)
</h3>

The <strong>Tent</strong> is in the <strong>Outpost</strong> and is the first real forward shelter most players should care about.

It matters the moment you reach it.

### How to find it quickly
From the Cabin, follow the train tracks right to the School transition, then move straight through School by following the main road beyond the bridge.
At the far end of School, use the Outpost transition.
Once you spawn in Outpost, follow the dirt road until it splits and take the left-hand route; the small yurt-style Tent will be on that side as your first Border fallback point.

### Important Tent limitation
The Tent is useful immediately, but it is <strong>not</strong> a Cabin replacement.

Current guide coverage consistently treats it as a limited forward shelter:

- very small storage footprint by default
- no reason to mistake it for a comfortable main base
- something that becomes better only once you expand its practical storage value with shelves and organization

The safe takeaway is simple:

<strong>use the Tent as a forward stash and route anchor first, not as a luxury home.</strong>

What it is actually good for:

- giving you a safe midpoint east of Area 05
- reducing the psychological cost of pushing farther
- making longer Outpost / Border plans feel less all-or-nothing
- functioning as your first real “I do not have to drag everything back to Village immediately” shelter

Should you rush it?

<strong>Yes, as soon as you are actually reaching Outpost.</strong>

It may not be the biggest shelter, but it changes your route network immediately.

<h3 id="bunker-outpost-border-zone">
  Bunker (Outpost, Border Zone)
</h3>

The <strong>Bunker</strong> is the most important mid-game shelter.

If the Attic is the best early upgrade, the Bunker is the best shelter that actually changes your mid-game.

### How to find it quickly
Once you are in Outpost, do not stop at the Tent.
Move deeper into the zone and use the left side of the valley as your reference line.
The practical landmarks are the tank, the sandbags, and the cave-side approach across the water; that cave entrance is the route players use to reach the Gunsmith side and the Bunker shelter.

### Official unlock route
Current trader-task coverage ties the <strong>Bunker key</strong> to the <strong>Gunsmith</strong> and the <strong>Punisher</strong> bounty path.

That means the unlock is not just about walking somewhere.
It is about being strong enough to complete a real combat objective and return with proof.

What it is actually good for:

- making Outpost a true base instead of a stopover
- supporting Border progression
- giving you a much better eastern staging point
- improving long-route logistics far more than a random locked room ever could

Should you rush it?

<strong>Yes, once your Border progression is real.</strong>

This is the shelter that deserves “worth rushing” more than Classroom does.

<h3 id="apartment-room-vostok">
  Apartment Room (Vostok)
</h3>

The <strong>Apartment Room</strong> is the shelter that sounds coolest and matters least for your early priorities.

That does not make it weak.
It makes it late.

It sits in the <strong>Vostok</strong> side of progression, which means every attempt to use or unlock it lives under the harshest risk model in the game.

### How to find it quickly
Treat the Apartment Room like a Vostok objective, not a normal shelter errand.
You only worry about this room after you already know how to cross Border reliably, survive Vostok entries, and read the apartment-side layout under pressure.
If you still think in terms of “my next shelter,” this one is not for your next run.

What it is actually good for:

- late-game staging
- deep-run persistence planning
- turning Vostok operations into something more deliberate
- giving advanced players an eastern anchor inside the most dangerous side of the map

### The warning most players need to hear
Do not think about the Apartment Room in terms of “late shelter value” only.

Think about the price of failure.

The official game page states that dying in <strong>Vostok</strong> means you <strong>lose everything</strong>.
For player-facing strategy writing, the safest assumption is that a failed Vostok commitment can wipe the whole shelter network you have built, not just the backpack on your body.

Should you rush it?

<strong>No.</strong>

This is a late-game luxury objective, not a practical early-game target.

<GuideCallout title="Do not build your early plan around the Apartment Room">
If you are still asking which first shelter to prioritize, the Apartment Room is not the answer.

Your next best shelter is almost always somewhere west of it.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="area-05-vs-border-vs-vostok-shelters">
  Area 05 vs Border vs Vostok shelters
</h2>

The best way to understand shelter value is to compare them by region instead of by unlock flavor.

### Area 05 shelters
- Cabin
- Attic
- Classroom

These shelters are about <strong>stability</strong>.

They improve your early economy, your task loop, and your ability to bank value safely before things get expensive.

### Border Zone shelters
- Tent
- Bunker

These shelters are about <strong>forward logistics</strong>.

They do not just save time.
They change how ambitious your eastern planning can become.

### Vostok shelter
- Apartment Room

This shelter is about <strong>endgame anchoring</strong>.

It matters, but only after you are already strong enough that Vostok is a real part of your routine instead of a terrifying exception.

<GuideCallout title="The Vostok difference">
Area 05 and Border shelters help you recover from bad runs.

Vostok asks whether you are ready to stake the whole network.

The official game page frames Vostok as the zone where a death means <strong>lose everything</strong>, which is why Apartment Room should be read as an endgame commitment, not an early progression reward.
</GuideCallout>

### Decor Mode matters more than it sounds
Shelter customization is not just cosmetic fluff.
Once you understand that F1 opens Decor Mode, the room starts functioning like a real home base instead of a static loot box.

Beds are valuable not only because they let you recover.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Can you customize shelters in Road to Vostok?

Yes. Current guide coverage describes <strong>F1</strong> inside Shelter as the way to open Decor Mode and place trader-bought furniture and props.

### Can you sleep outside without a shelter?

Current public guide coverage says yes, using sleep items such as a sleeping bag, mattress, blanket, pillow, or sleep aids. But sleeping outside is not the same thing as having a shelter because it does not create safe stash.

### Do all shelters share the same stash?

No. Each shelter keeps its own storage.

### Does shelter management pause hunger and thirst?

Do not count on it. Player reports consistently warn that survival stats can keep draining while you spend time organizing.

### Why is the Classroom unlock considered so dangerous?

Because the official route is tied to three oil samples on the Terminal / Vostok permadeath side, and current guide coverage consistently treats that as one of the harshest shelter unlocks in the game.

### What is the best first shelter upgrade?

For most players, the answer is still the <strong>Attic</strong>.

### Is the Tent a real main base?

Not by default. Treat it as a forward shelter first and a serious main base only after you solve its storage limitations.

### What happens if you die in Vostok?

The official game page describes Vostok as the zone where you <strong>lose everything</strong>. For practical strategy writing, that means treating a failed Vostok push as a whole-network disaster, not just a dropped backpack.

### What is the best current shelter order?

For most runs: <strong>Cabin -> Attic -> Tent -> Bunker -> Classroom -> Apartment Room</strong>.
