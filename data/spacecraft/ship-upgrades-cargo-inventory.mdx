<h2 id="best-first-upgrade">
  Best first upgrade: Cargo Hold
</h2>

For most players, the best first ship upgrade is **Cargo Hold**.

The reason is simple: your starter cargo space is the first thing that interrupts the route. You mine Copper, Iron, Quartz, Carbon, or random useful materials, then the ship hits the storage limit and forces you to drop cargo.

A better mining laser does not fix that. A better detector does not fix that either. If the ship cannot bring materials home, every other upgrade feels worse.

  Resource Detector: upgrade when finding resources is the problem

Upgrade detector when:

  <li>you keep seeing common nodes instead of the resource blocking you,</li>
  <li>or planet searching is taking longer than mining.</li>

The **Simple Resource Detector** is an early step up from the crude detector route.

<h2 id="overclocked-mining-laser">
  Should you rush Overclocked Mining Laser?
</h2>

Do not rush **Overclocked Mining Laser** just because the name sounds better.

The shown module has:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>200 SP</strong> system requirement,</li>
  <li><strong>3</strong> patch slots,</li>
  <li><strong>1000</strong> durability,</li>
  <li>and a description focused on vaporizing many materials, though not precisely enough to cut through things.</li>
</ul>

The important hidden detail is **patch slots**. Patch slots are upgrade or tuning slots on the module. They are not instant power by themselves, but a module with more patch slots may scale better later once you have compatible patches.

That makes Overclocked Mining Laser a serious upgrade, but also a bigger commitment. If your ship still has weak cargo or poor detection, the overclocked laser may not solve the problem you actually feel.

A safe rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If you cannot carry enough, upgrade cargo.</li>
  <li>If you cannot find enough, upgrade detector.</li>
  <li>If you can find and carry resources but cannot mine them well, upgrade laser.</li>
  <li>If the module load is getting high, check System Support before adding more.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="system-support-heat">
  System Support, SP, and overheating
</h2>

The most important hidden upgrade limit is **System Support**.

Each module has a **System Requirement**, usually shown in SP. Your ship also has a System Support value. If total system requirements become greater than ship support, the ship has a harder time evacuating heat properly and may overheat faster.

You can check this in the **Shipyard editor**:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>select a module to see its System Requirement,</li>
  <li>look at the Ship Stats / System panel for current load and support,</li>
  <li>compare the new module’s SP cost before confirming the edit.</li>
</ul>

In current screenshots, the ship shows examples such as **1140 / 1620 SP** and **1240 / 1620 SP**. Your own value can differ depending on installed modules and current build balance.

<h2 id="faq">
  SpaceCraft ship upgrades FAQ
</h2>

### What should I upgrade first in SpaceCraft?

For most players, upgrade Cargo Hold first. The starter ship fills quickly, and more storage makes every mining run more efficient.

### How do I increase cargo space?

Craft a Small Cargo Hold, install it through the Shipyard editor, and confirm the ship edit.

### What does Small Cargo Hold require?

In the shown build, Small Cargo Hold uses 1 Small Module Kit, 6 Metal Sheet, and 4 Silicon Gel.

### When should I upgrade the Resource Detector?

Upgrade it when finding resources becomes harder than mining them. This is especially useful for Mining Bureau targets and specific recipe materials.

### What does Simple Resource Detector require?

In the shown build, Simple Resource Detector uses 1 Small Module Kit and 2 Magnetic Coil.

### How do I replace an installed detector?

Open the Shipyard editor, select the old detector, remove it if the editor allows it, place the new detector in the freed slot, then confirm the ship edit.

### When should I upgrade the Mining Laser?

Upgrade your Mining Laser when your current laser cannot mine the materials you need or mining speed becomes the bottleneck.

### What does Simple Mining Laser require?

In the shown build, Simple Mining Laser uses 1 Small Module Kit and 2 Crystalline Focuser.

### Should I rush Overclocked Mining Laser?

Not immediately. Check cargo, detector, Quartz, previous module requirements, System Support, and heat first.

### What are patch slots?

Patch slots are upgrade or tuning slots on a module. They do not give instant power by themselves, but they can make a module scale better later if compatible patches are available.

### When should I upgrade the battery?

Upgrade battery when maximum power storage or charging speed starts limiting longer routes and module-heavy setups.

### What does Flywheel Battery require?

In the shown build, Flywheel Battery uses 1 Small Module Kit, 1 Motor, and 2 Concrete.

### Where do I check System Support?

Open the Shipyard editor and look at the Ship Stats / System panel. Current screenshots show examples such as 1140 / 1620 SP and 1240 / 1620 SP.

### Why is my ship overheating?

Your system requirements may be higher than your ship’s System Support. Reduce module load or improve support before stacking more upgrades.
