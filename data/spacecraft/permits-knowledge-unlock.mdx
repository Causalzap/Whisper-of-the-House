<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **SpaceCraft**, buy permits by blocker, not by curiosity. Start with **Modular Systems** when the Knowledge route offers it, then buy **Electromagnetism** for the first FTL chain. After RakMu, **Cargo Accommodation** is one of the best practical 100-credit unlocks because it opens Small Cargo Hold. Buy **Personal Factory** when you are ready to claim a base, then move into **Solar Panel** and **Automated Factories** after the first Extractor-to-Smelter loop works.

Do not treat every missing recipe as a permit problem. Some routes also need **Laboratory analysis**, **Science level**, **Technology XP**, or **Exploration progression**. If a recipe is missing after buying the right permit, check the progression requirement before spending credits on unrelated nodes. 
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="spacecraft-permits-knowledge-unlock-guide">
  SpaceCraft permits and Knowledge unlock guide
</h2>

The Knowledge menu can look like a normal tech tree, but SpaceCraft plays better when you treat every permit as a practical route unlock.

If you only want the early answer:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Modular Systems</strong> opens the Scanalyzer Alpha and early ship-module route.</li>
  <li><strong>Electromagnetism</strong> supports the first FTL, detector, laser, and Magnetic Coil route.</li>
  <li><strong>Cargo Accommodation</strong> solves the first serious cargo problem.</li>
  <li><strong>Personal Factory</strong> starts base building.</li>
  <li><strong>Solar Panel</strong> and <strong>Automated Factories</strong> make your first base scalable.</li>
  <li><strong>Modular Systems 2</strong> becomes important when basic cargo, lasers, and module parts are no longer enough.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="electromagnetism-ftl">
  Electromagnetism: the first FTL gate
</h2>

**Electromagnetism** is a small 100-credit unlock, but it sits on the critical early route. When the Interstellar Travel objective asks you to craft **FTL Engine - Eco**, it also points you toward unlocking Electromagnetism from the Knowledge section.

This permit is important because it unlocks tools and parts that support early mining, detection, and Magnetic Coil progression.

<h2 id="cargo-accommodation">
  Cargo Accommodation: the first comfort upgrade that actually matters
</h2>

**Cargo Accommodation** is easy to underestimate because it is not as flashy as a new laser or a base unlock. In practice, it is one of the best early 100-credit purchases once mining trips start feeling too short.

Buy it when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>your cargo fills before you finish the resource route,</li>
  <li>you are making repeated station trips for Copper, Iron, Quartz, or upgrade parts,</li>
  <li>Bob's route is pushing ship improvements,</li>
  <li>or corpo requests start asking for materials you cannot comfortably carry.</li>
</ul>

Cargo Accommodation opens **Small Cargo Hold**, which is useful for ship upgrades, base prep, and early corporation deliveries.

  <li><strong>Extractor</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>Smelter</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>Pylon</strong>,</li>

  <li>and an unclaimed resource deposit worth building around.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="automated-factories">
  Automated Factories: buy it after the first loop works
</h2>

**Automated Factories** is the permit that turns a single mining outpost into a real factory system.

It unlocks the buildings that matter after the first loop:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Warehouse</strong> for storage,</li>
  <li><strong>Assembler</strong> for component production,</li>
  <li><strong>Drone Dispatcher</strong> and <strong>Drone</strong> for routing,</li>
  <li><strong>Landing Pad</strong> for connecting ship logistics to the base,</li>
  <li>and industrial materials such as Steel Ingot, Structural Beam, and Microchip.</li>
</ul>

Do not rush it before one Extractor-to-Smelter loop works. The correct timing is when your base can already produce something, but the next blocker is storage, movement, or scaling.

<h2 id="modular-systems-2">
  Modular Systems 2: why the 2000-credit unlock matters
</h2>

**Modular Systems 2** is the first permit in this guide that feels expensive compared with the opening route, but it is not a cosmetic delay node.

It unlocks the next practical ship-upgrade layer:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Cargo Hold</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>Hi-Pi Laser</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>Overclocked Hi-Pi Laser</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>Module Kit</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>Strong Metal Sheet</strong>.</li>
</ul>

Buy it when your early ship setup is no longer enough:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Small Cargo Hold no longer carries enough for longer routes,</li>
  <li>early mining lasers feel too weak,</li>
  <li>Module Kit becomes a repeated crafting blocker,</li>
  <li>or Strong Metal Sheet starts appearing in ship/base upgrade chains.</li>
</ul>

Do not buy it before your first FTL, Cargo Accommodation, and base-start route are stable. Once those are done, Modular Systems 2 becomes one of the most important mid-game Knowledge unlocks.

<h2 id="interplanetary-logistics-plumbing-liquid-logistics">
  Interplanetary Logistics, Plumbing, and Liquid Logistics
</h2>

**Interplanetary Logistics** is the next base layer after your first local production loop. The route explains that Interplanetary Logistics will let your base connect to stations and later to other bases. Before that, the game asks you to master several intermediate steps.

The confirmed blocker chain is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>get <strong>Carbon</strong>,</li>
  <li>unlock the <strong>Plumbing</strong> permit,</li>
  <li>earn <strong>Technology points</strong> from your base,</li>
  <li>develop the base station,</li>
  <li>and deploy / assemble a <strong>Solar Power Plant</strong>.</li>
</ul>

That means Plumbing is not just a random fluid node. It becomes relevant when the base route starts moving from a local mining loop toward station-connected logistics.

Use this timing:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Still building the first Extractor-to-Smelter loop?</strong>{" "}
    Delay Plumbing and Liquid Logistics.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Base works, but power and expansion are limiting you?</strong>{" "}
    Prioritize Solar Panel and the base development tasks first.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>The objective starts asking for pumps, tanks, bottles, or fluid movement?</strong>{" "}
    Move into Liquid Logistics and Plumbing.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>The route mentions station connection or future base connection?</strong>{" "}
    You are entering the Interplanetary Logistics layer.
  </li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">
  SpaceCraft permits and Knowledge FAQ
</h2>

### What permit should I unlock first in SpaceCraft?

Unlock Modular Systems when the opening route points you to the Knowledge menu. It starts the early ship-module and Scanalyzer Alpha route.

### Is Electromagnetism required for FTL?

Yes. Buy Electromagnetism when the FTL route asks for it, then check the FTL Engine - Eco ingredient chain.

### Is Cargo Accommodation worth it?

Yes, once cargo space becomes the bottleneck. It opens Small Cargo Hold and makes mining trips, upgrade runs, and request deliveries easier.

### When should I buy Personal Factory?

Buy Personal Factory when you are ready to claim a base and build the first production loop. Do not buy it just to stare at base recipes without parts, credits, or a good deposit.

### When should I buy Automated Factories?

Buy Automated Factories after your first Extractor-to-Smelter loop works. It is strongest when storage, drone routing, landing, and production scaling are the next bottlenecks.

### Is Modular Systems 2 worth 2000 credits?

Yes, once the first FTL, Cargo Accommodation, and base-start routes are stable. Modular Systems 2 opens Cargo Hold, Hi-Pi Laser, Overclocked Hi-Pi Laser, Module Kit, and Strong Metal Sheet.

### Are Corpo Permits the same as Knowledge permits?

No. Knowledge permits are personal unlocks. Corpo permits are corporation-side upgrades that matter after Noviciate and the first corpo request.

### Should I buy decoration permits early?

Usually no. Ship Coating and Brand Management are cheap, but they do not solve FTL, cargo, base, power, or automation blockers.

### How do I get Science XP in SpaceCraft?

Study minerals and organic resources at the Laboratory. Resource analysis can give Science XP and may also unlock recipe or material knowledge.

### How do I get Technology XP in SpaceCraft?

Crafting items in automated Smelters and Factories inside your space base generates Technology XP. Deploying a new base building for the first time also gives Technology XP.

### Why does a recipe say Requires Science level 2?

That recipe is gated by Science progression, not only by a Knowledge permit. Analyze resources at the Laboratory until the required Science level is reached.

### Why do I have materials but no recipe?

Check Knowledge, Laboratory analysis, and progression levels. Some recipes stay unavailable until the relevant permit, resource analysis, blueprint access, or Science / Technology / Exploration requirement is unlocked.

### What is the difference between permits and Science / Technology levels?

Permits are bought unlocks in Knowledge. Science and Technology levels can reveal additional tools, recipes, bases, structures, and higher-tier options through progression.

### Should I follow the full permit tree in order?

No. Follow your active blocker. Buy the permit that solves your route now, then come back for later systems when they become relevant.
