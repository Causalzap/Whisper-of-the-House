After RakMu, use the **Mining Bureau** for harder targets like **B-Carbon, Silicate, and Pyrite**. If a node is visible but will not mine properly, check your **Mining Laser tier**, Resource Detector, or required extraction tool.

Use this route logic: **mine early metals manually, use Bureau coordinates for specific targets, scout true deposits for base automation, and check active objectives before trading resources away**.

<h2 id="resources-locations-guide">
  SpaceCraft resources and locations guide
</h2>

Resources in SpaceCraft are not just “pick up everything.” The first hours punish bad hauling, bad selling, weak tools, and blind searching.

You need different resource habits at different stages:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Copper and Iron</strong> get your first parts moving.</li>
  <li><strong>Gray Quartz</strong> pushes the Scanalyzer and FTL route forward.</li>
  <li><strong>Gravite</strong> teaches you that some targets need visual search, not a perfect marker.</li>
  <li><strong>Silicon Gel</strong> comes from the Company Exchanger and feeds FTL crafting.</li>
  <li><strong>B-Carbon, Silicate, and Pyrite</strong> become important once the Mining Bureau starts giving sample work.</li>
  <li><strong>Deposits</strong> matter later because Extractors need them for automation.</li>
</ul>

If you are still in the opening route, read the <Link href="/spacecraft/beginner-guide">SpaceCraft Beginner Guide</Link>. If your issue is specifically FTL, use the <Link href="/spacecraft/scanlyzer-alpha-ftl-engine">Scanalyzer Alpha and FTL guide</Link>.

<h2 id="copper-iron-location">
  Copper and Iron location: Solar Alpha 1
</h2>

Your first real resource stop is **Solar Alpha 1**.

The onboarding route asks you to gather **Iron Ore** and **Copper Ore**. Use radar near the surface, then mine the visible resource nodes. You should treat Copper and Iron as progression materials, not just things to sell.

Copper is especially important because it becomes **Wire**, and Wire appears repeatedly in early module chains. Iron becomes basic parts such as **Nuts and Bolts** and supports the Small Module Kit route.

<h2 id="cargo-limit">
  Do not overfill your starter cargo hold
</h2>

The first resource trap is cargo weight. It is tempting to mine every stone because every material feels useful, but the starter ship fills fast.

If you exceed storage, the game forces you to drop cargo. That can waste the exact material you were trying to keep.

Early priority:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Finish the required Copper and Iron objective.</li>
  <li>Keep extra Copper if you have room.</li>
  <li>Do not load random stones if the cargo warning is close.</li>
  <li>Return to Babylon before your hold becomes a problem.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="gray-quartz-location">
  Gray Quartz location: Solar Alpha 2
</h2>

After you craft and install **Scanalyzer Alpha**, the next important resource is **Gray Quartz** on **Solar Alpha 2**.

You need to scan the resource before mining it. This is where Scanalyzer Alpha starts paying off. If you fly to Solar Alpha 2 without the module installed, the route becomes confusing quickly.

Gray Quartz matters because it connects to the **Crystalline Focuser**, and the Crystalline Focuser connects to **FTL Engine - Eco**. It can also matter again later when mining laser upgrades pull you back into Quartz and focuser-style recipes.

<h2 id="gravite-location">
  How to find Gravite
</h2>

Gravite is not a normal resource search.

The radar gives you an approximate direction because Gravite interferes with precise tracking. That means you should not chase the marker like Copper or Iron.

Use this method:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Use radar and watch the signal direction.</li>
  <li>Fly more slowly once the signal gets stronger.</li>
  <li>Look across the terrain for the deposit.</li>
  <li>Approach and scan it.</li>
</ol>

This step is about scanning and progression. Do not waste time expecting a perfect pinpoint marker.

<h2 id="silicon-gel">
  Where to get Silicon Gel
</h2>

**Silicon Gel** is not something you need to hunt manually during the first FTL route. The early trading objective sends you to the **Company Exchanger** to buy 10 Silicon Gel after selling enough resources.

Before trading, check two things: your active objective and your next recipe. Sell only enough value to complete the trade step, then confirm you still have the materials needed for Crystalline Focuser, FTL Engine - Eco, and Magnetic Plasma travel.

A safe pre-FTL check is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you have the Silicon Gel required by the FTL route,</li>
  <li>you still have enough Copper or Wire for module parts,</li>
  <li>you still have enough Gray Quartz for Crystalline Focuser,</li>
  <li>and you still have credits for Magnetic Plasma if the next jump is coming.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="mining-laser-tool-tier">
  Why some resources cannot be mined yet
</h2>

Sometimes the problem is not the location. You found the resource, but your ship still cannot mine it properly.

That usually means one of three things:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>your **Mining Laser** is too weak for the node,</li>
  <li>your **Resource Detector** is too weak to find the right material consistently,</li>
  <li>or the resource is an advanced type that needs a different extraction tool or interaction.</li>
</ul>

Bob’s upgrade route makes this clear: as you try to extract tougher materials, not every laser can cut through them. If a node refuses to cooperate, stop forcing it and check your tool tier.

<GuideTable
  caption="Resource tool and mining laser problems."
  columns={toolGateColumns}
  rows={toolGateRows}
/>

The safe upgrade order is not always “more laser.” If you cannot find the resource, improve detection first. If you can find it but cannot cut it, improve the Mining Laser. If the node is not a normal solid mineral, check the objective text before assuming a laser upgrade is enough.

<h2 id="mining-bureau">
  How the Mining Bureau helps you find resources
</h2>

After you reach RakMu, the **Mining Bureau** becomes one of the most useful resource systems.

The Mining Bureau has two jobs:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>It can sell information when you need a specific resource in the sector.</li>
  <li>It can pay you when you bring requested resource samples or useful planet data.</li>
</ul>

This matters because blind searching becomes expensive in time. If you need B-Carbon, Silicate, or Pyrite and cannot find them naturally, the Bureau gives you a more directed path.

<h2 id="mining-bureau-planet-information">
  How selling planet information works
</h2>

The Mining Bureau can also buy useful planet information, but it is not the same as grabbing one rock and leaving.

When the Bureau talks about planet data, think in terms of a full survey:

<GuideTable
  caption="How to prepare planet information for the Mining Bureau."
  columns={bureauPlanetDataColumns}
  rows={bureauPlanetDataRows}
/>

A good planet survey should identify the resources, deposits, and points of interest the objective cares about. In practice, that means flying lower, scanning more patiently, and checking whether the objective has actually updated before leaving.

This is also why **deposit vs outcrop vs stone** matters. A planet with loose rocks can help your inventory today, but a planet with true deposits can help your future base.

<h2 id="carbon-silicate-pyrite">
  Carbon, Silicate, and Pyrite
</h2>

<h2 id="faq">
  SpaceCraft resources FAQ
</h2>

### Where do I find Copper in SpaceCraft?

Copper appears early on Solar Alpha 1. Use radar near the surface and look for Copper or Cupri-style nodes during the opening mining objective. Copper Deposits can also appear on later planets and make strong first-base targets.

### Where do I find Iron?

Iron also appears on Solar Alpha 1 during the opening route. Mine it with Copper because both feed early module crafting. For base automation, look for actual Iron Deposits rather than only loose stones.

### Where do I find Gray Quartz?

Gray Quartz appears on Solar Alpha 2 after Scanalyzer Alpha is installed. Scan it first, then mine it. Keep enough for Crystalline Focuser and later upgrade paths.

### Why can’t I find Gravite?

Gravite does not give a perfect radar marker. Follow the approximate direction, slow down, and search visually.

### Where do I get Silicon Gel?

Buy Silicon Gel from the Company Exchanger during the trading objective. The early route asks you to buy 10, and later recipes can ask for more.

### Where do I find Carbon or B-Carbon?

After RakMu, use the Mining Bureau. The Integration Protocol can ask for B-Carbon, and paid coordinates can point you to a target system in the current build.

### Where do I find Silicate?

Use the Mining Bureau target list or buy coordinates if you do not already know a source. Silicate can become an early Bureau blocker after RakMu.

### Where do I find Pyrite?

Pyrite appears as a Mining Bureau target after RakMu. Use the Bureau if visual searching is taking too long, and confirm the resource label before assuming a gold-looking node is Pyrite.

### What is the Mining Bureau for?

The Mining Bureau helps you locate specific resources, buy coordinates, bring resource samples, and sell useful planet information.

### How do I sell planet information to the Mining Bureau?

Fully survey the planet instead of leaving after one scan. Identify resource types, deposits, and points of interest the objective cares about, then return to the Bureau when the exploration data is useful.

### Why can’t I mine a resource I found?

Your Mining Laser, Resource Detector, or extraction tool may be too weak for that resource. Upgrade your tools before forcing higher-tier nodes.

### Should I refine ore before selling?

Refine when a recipe needs ingots or processed parts. Sell raw or spare materials only when you need credits and the material is not part of your next recipe chain.

### What resources should I keep early?

Keep Copper, Iron, Gray Quartz, Silicon Gel, and active Bureau samples until you know your next recipe or objective does not need them.

### What matters for a first mining base?

Look for actual deposits, not only loose stones or outcrops. Extractors need valid resource deposits to automate mining.
