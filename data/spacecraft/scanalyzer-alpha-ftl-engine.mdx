<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **SpaceCraft**, the FTL route is: unlock **Modular Systems** when Knowledge offers it, craft and install **Scanalyzer Alpha**, scan **Gray Quartz** on Solar Alpha 2, scan **Gravite**, buy **10 Silicon Gel**, unlock **Electromagnetism**, craft and install **FTL Engine - Eco**, then fill **Magnetic Plasma** at the station before jumping.

Most players get stuck because a module was crafted but not installed, Crystalline Focuser still needs Wire after Quartz, or Magnetic Plasma was never filled.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="scanlyzer-alpha-ftl-engine-guide">
  SpaceCraft Scanalyzer Alpha and FTL Engine guide
</h2>

The first FTL route in SpaceCraft is not one craft. It is a chain of permits, modules, scanning, trading, and ship editing.

The clean route is:

  caption="SpaceCraft Scanalyzer Alpha and FTL Engine - Eco unlock route."

/>

If you are still learning the opening mining route, read the <Link href="/spacecraft/beginner-guide">SpaceCraft Beginner Guide</Link> first. This page focuses only on the Scanalyzer Alpha, Gray Quartz, Gravite, Electromagnetism, FTL Engine - Eco, and Magnetic Plasma chain.

<h2 id="modular-systems-permit">
  Step 0: check Modular Systems permit
</h2>

Before treating the FTL route as a normal crafting problem, check the **Knowledge** menu.

The early module route points you toward **Modular Systems**. This permit matters because it is tied to the Shipyard and blueprint path that makes early module progression work.

Use this rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If Knowledge offers **Modular Systems**, buy it as early as you can.</li>
  <li>If the permit is not available yet, follow the current onboarding objective until it appears.</li>
  <li>If the Workshop or Shipyard route feels empty, check Knowledge before assuming you are missing ore.</li>
</ul>

This prevents the most confusing early problem: having materials but not having the correct permit or blueprint path unlocked.

<h2 id="craft-scanalyzer-alpha">
  Craft Scanalyzer Alpha
</h2>

After your first Copper and Iron trip, return to Babylon and use the station machines. The chain usually goes through Copper into Wire, Iron into basic parts, and then into the Small Module Kit path.

If Scanalyzer Alpha is not craftable, do not leave the station immediately. Check the subcomponents first:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Do you have enough Copper processed into Wire?</li>
  <li>Do you have enough Iron processed into Nuts and Bolts or basic parts?</li>
  <li>Do you have the Small Module Kit ready?</li>
  <li>Did you unlock the required permit or blueprint path in Knowledge?</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="install-scanalyzer-alpha">
  Install Scanalyzer Alpha in the Shipyard
</h2>

Crafting Scanalyzer Alpha is only half the step.

Open the Shipyard, load your ship, edit the ship, place Scanalyzer Alpha onto a valid hull slot, and confirm the change. If the module remains in your inventory, Solar Alpha 2 will still feel broken because the ship does not actually have the scanner equipped.

  </figcaption>
</figure>

<h2 id="gray-quartz">
  Find Gray Quartz on Solar Alpha 2
</h2>

After Scanalyzer Alpha is installed, fly to **Solar Alpha 2** and look for the **High Peak** route. Gray Quartz appears as a dark, crystal-like resource near the high landmark area.

The sequence is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Use radar near Solar Alpha 2.</li>
  <li>Approach the High Peak area.</li>
  <li>Find the dark crystal-like Gray Quartz node.</li>
  <li>Scan it with Scanalyzer Alpha.</li>
  <li>Mine enough Gray Quartz for the Crystalline Focuser and FTL route.</li>
</ol>

Do not sell all of it after the trade step. Quartz can come back again through Crystalline Focuser and mining laser upgrade paths.

<h2 id="crystalline-focuser">
  Craft Crystalline Focuser
</h2>

The **Crystalline Focuser** is the most common hidden blocker in the FTL route.

In current build examples, each Crystalline Focuser uses **1 Gray Quartz plus Wire**. The Quartz part is easy to notice because it sends you back to Solar Alpha 2. The Wire part is easier to miss because it sends you back into Copper processing.

Before leaving Babylon again, check the Workshop recipe and make sure you have:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>at least 1 Gray Quartz for the Focuser,</li>
  <li>enough Copper processed into Wire,</li>
  <li>and enough spare Wire left for FTL Engine - Eco itself.</li>
</ul>

If the Focuser is still blocked after you brought Quartz back, the missing part is usually Wire. Mine more Copper, process it, and return to the Workshop.

<h2 id="find-gravite">
  Find and scan Gravite
</h2>

Gravite does not behave like Copper, Iron, or Gray Quartz.

Its radio properties interfere with radar, so the ship gives you an approximate direction instead of a perfect marker. That means the correct method is to follow the direction, slow down, and search the terrain visually.

Use this pattern:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Track the Gravite objective.</li>
  <li>Use radar for the general direction.</li>
  <li>Slow down once the signal gets stronger.</li>
  <li>Look for the target visually.</li>
  <li>Scan it to complete the objective.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="buy-silicon-gel">
  Buy Silicon Gel from the Company Exchanger
</h2>

After the Solar Alpha 2 objectives, return to Babylon and use the **Company Exchanger**. The early trading step asks you to sell enough value, then buy **10 Silicon Gel**.

Before selling, check the active objective and the next Workshop recipe. You need credits, but you also need enough materials left to finish Crystalline Focuser, Wire, Small Module Kit, and FTL Engine - Eco.

<h2 id="unlock-electromagnetism">
  Unlock Electromagnetism
</h2>

After buying Silicon Gel, open Knowledge and unlock **Electromagnetism** when the route asks for it. This is the permit gate before **FTL Engine - Eco**.

If Electromagnetism is visible but you cannot buy it, the problem is usually credits. Avoid spending all your money on repairs or optional purchases before the FTL craft is complete.

<h2 id="craft-ftl-engine-eco">
  Craft FTL Engine - Eco
</h2>

Once Electromagnetism is unlocked, go back to the Workshop and craft **FTL Engine - Eco**.

In the current build example, the FTL Engine - Eco recipe shows:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>1 Small Module Kit</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>1 Crystalline Focuser</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>Wire</strong>,</li>
  <li><strong>Silicon Gel</strong>.</li>
</ul>

The common trap is thinking Quartz alone solves the recipe. It does not. Quartz becomes the Focuser, but the engine still needs Wire and other module parts.

  <Image

    FTL Engine - Eco combines the whole route: module parts, Crystalline Focuser, Wire, and Silicon Gel.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

<h2 id="install-ftl-engine">
  Install FTL Engine - Eco
</h2>

Crafting the engine does not make the ship jump-ready. Install it through the Shipyard.

The install path is the same logic as Scanalyzer Alpha:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Open the Shipyard.</li>
  <li>Load your ship.</li>
  <li>Edit the ship.</li>
  <li>Place FTL Engine - Eco into a valid slot.</li>
  <li>Confirm the ship edit.</li>
</ol>

If you craft the engine and leave it in inventory, the next travel step will still fail.

<h2 id="refill-magnetic-plasma">
  Refill Magnetic Plasma before jumping
</h2>

After FTL Engine - Eco is installed, you still need **Magnetic Plasma**.

Use the station ship or dock prompt before taking off. In current build examples, the game asks you to fill Magnetic Plasma before departure, and the fill prompt can cost **40 credits**. If you cannot afford it, sell a small amount of spare processed material, return to the dock, and fill the plasma.

This is the last common FTL blocker:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>FTL Engine - Eco crafted? Good, but not enough.</li>
  <li>FTL Engine - Eco installed? Good, but still not enough.</li>
  <li>Magnetic Plasma filled? Now the ship can actually make the jump.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="what-happens-after-ftl">
  What happens after the first FTL jump?
</h2>

<h2 id="faq">
  SpaceCraft Scanalyzer Alpha and FTL FAQ
</h2>

### How do I unlock Scanalyzer Alpha in SpaceCraft?

Follow the early Copper and Iron route, check the Knowledge menu for Modular Systems when the objective offers it, then craft Scanalyzer Alpha in the Workshop at Babylon.

### What does Modular Systems permit do?

Modular Systems is tied to the early ship-module and Shipyard progression path. Buy it as soon as Knowledge offers it so the first module route does not stall.

### Why can’t I find the Scanalyzer Alpha recipe?

Check the active objective and Knowledge menu. If Modular Systems or the current permit path is not handled yet, the Workshop/Shipyard route may not show what you expect.

### Why is Scanalyzer Alpha not working?

It is probably crafted but not installed. Return to the Shipyard, place Scanalyzer Alpha on the ship, and confirm the edit.

### Where do I find Gray Quartz?

Go to Solar Alpha 2 after installing Scanalyzer Alpha. Use radar near the High Peak route, scan the dark crystal-like Gray Quartz, then mine it.

### What does Crystalline Focuser require?

In current build examples, each Crystalline Focuser requires 1 Gray Quartz plus Wire. If you have Quartz but the craft is still blocked, process more Copper into Wire and check the Workshop count.

### Why can’t I craft FTL Engine - Eco?

Check four things: Electromagnetism must be unlocked, and the recipe needs Small Module Kit, Crystalline Focuser, Wire, and Silicon Gel.

### Where do I buy Silicon Gel?

Buy Silicon Gel from the Company Exchanger during the trade step. The early route asks you to buy 10.

### How do I unlock Electromagnetism?

Open Knowledge after the Silicon Gel step and buy Electromagnetism when the FTL route asks for it.

### Why can’t I find Gravite?

Gravite gives an approximate radar direction, not a perfect marker. Follow the direction, slow down, and search visually.

### Do I need to mine Gravite?

For the early route, treat Gravite as a scan/progression target. Follow the objective text instead of treating it like normal ore.

### Why can’t I jump after installing FTL Engine - Eco?

The engine must be installed and Magnetic Plasma must be filled. Use the station ship/dock prompt to fill Magnetic Plasma before taking off.

### Where do I refill Magnetic Plasma?

Use the station ship or dock prompt before departure. In the current build example, filling Magnetic Plasma costs 40 credits.

### What should I do after the first FTL jump?

Follow the route toward RakMu. Talk to the clerk, then use Bob for ship upgrades, Ingrid for Noviciate, and the Mining Bureau for resource work.
