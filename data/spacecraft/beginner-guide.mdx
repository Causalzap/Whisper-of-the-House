<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **SpaceCraft**, your first-hour route is: fly carefully to **Solar Alpha 1**, mine **Copper** and **Iron**, return to **Babylon**, craft and install **Scanalyzer Alpha**, go to **Solar Alpha 2**, find **High Peak**, scan and mine **Gray Quartz**, locate **Gravite**, buy **Silicon Gel**, craft and install **FTL Engine - Eco**, refill **Magnetic Plasma**, then jump toward **RakMu**.

The biggest beginner traps are crashing the ship, overfilling cargo, crafting modules without installing them, selling too much Quartz, and forgetting Magnetic Plasma before the first FTL jump.

<h2 id="spacecraft-beginner-guide">
  SpaceCraft beginner guide
</h2>

SpaceCraft starts as a mining and ship-editing tutorial, but the first hour has several easy failure points. The game asks you to mine early resources, process them at Babylon, install your first scanning module, unlock FTL, and reach RakMu.

The important part is not doing everything. The important part is doing the first route cleanly:

<h2 id="first-flight-controls">
  First flight tips: slow down, manage battery, avoid repair costs
</h2>

Before mining anything, learn the first flight rule: **boost is for distance, not for landing**.

When you approach a planet, landmark, or station, slow down early. Hitting terrain or bumping into objects can damage the ship, and repairs cost credits. That matters because the first hour also asks you to buy Silicon Gel, refill Magnetic Plasma, and keep enough money for basic crafting.

<GuideTable
  caption="First flight and ship control mistakes to avoid."
  columns={flightColumns}
  rows={flightRows}
/>

If the ship battery drains while boosting, stop boosting and give it time to recharge. Do not panic just because the battery drops during travel; the problem is continuing to boost when you should be scanning, landing, or docking.

<h2 id="solar-alpha-1-copper-iron">
  Solar Alpha 1: mine Copper and Iron
</h2>

Your first resource stop is **Solar Alpha 1**. The goal is to mine the Copper and Iron needed for early station crafting.

Look for resource labels and node types such as:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Copper / Cupric stones</strong> for Copper Ore.</li>
  <li><strong>Iron / Ferric stones</strong> for Iron Ore.</li>
  <li><strong>Brassy or Ferrous-style outcrops</strong> later when the same resource family appears in larger surface nodes.</li>
</ul>

Copper and Iron are not just sell items. Copper becomes Wire, and Iron becomes parts such as Nuts and Bolts. Both feed the Scanalyzer Alpha route.

<h2 id="return-to-babylon">
  Return to Babylon and use the station machines
</h2>

After the first mining trip, return to Babylon and dock. The early station has the machines you need to process ore and craft ship modules.

The important machines are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Company Exchanger</strong>, used for buying and selling resources.</li>
  <li><strong>Smelter</strong>, used to process ore into usable materials.</li>
  <li><strong>Workshop</strong>, used to craft parts and modules.</li>
  <li><strong>Shipyard</strong>, used to install modules on your ship.</li>
</ul>

This is where the beginner route becomes a crafting chain instead of a simple mining objective.

<h2 id="craft-scanalyzer-alpha">
  Craft Scanalyzer Alpha
</h2>

Your first major ship module is **Scanalyzer Alpha**.

To craft it, process your Copper and Iron into the required parts. The main blockers are usually Wire, Nuts and Bolts, and the Small Module Kit. If you cannot craft Scanalyzer Alpha yet, check the subcomponents instead of leaving the station immediately.

<h2 id="install-scanalyzer-alpha">
  Install Scanalyzer Alpha in the Shipyard
</h2>

Crafting Scanalyzer Alpha is not enough. You must install it.

Open the Shipyard, load your ship, enter the editor, drag Scanalyzer Alpha from inventory onto the hull, and confirm the edit. If the module stays in your inventory, Solar Alpha 2 will not behave the way the objective expects.

<h2 id="solar-alpha-2-high-peak-gray-quartz">
  Solar Alpha 2: find High Peak and mine Gray Quartz
</h2>

After Scanalyzer Alpha is installed, go to **Solar Alpha 2**. Your next goal is to find the **High Peak** landmark and scan **Gray Quartz**.

Use radar near the surface and watch for the High Peak route. Gray Quartz appears as a dark, crystal-like resource near the high landmark area. You may find the node before you can mine it, but you still need to scan it with the installed module first.

The common mistake is flying too fast past the landmark or trying to mine the unknown node before scanning it.

<h2 id="find-gravite">
  Find Gravite with radar direction and visual search
</h2>

Gravite is different from normal Copper or Iron. Its radio properties interfere with precise tracking, so radar gives you an approximate direction rather than a perfect location.

Use this pattern:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Track the Gravite objective.</li>
  <li>Use radar to get the general direction.</li>
  <li>Slow down as the signal improves.</li>
  <li>Search visually instead of waiting for a perfect marker.</li>
  <li>Scan the Gravite once you find it.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="company-exchanger-silicon-gel">
  Use the Company Exchanger to buy Silicon Gel
</h2>

The FTL route needs **Silicon Gel**, and the early trading objective sends you to the **Company Exchanger**.

The safe move is to sell only enough value to complete the trade step, then buy the required Silicon Gel. Do not dump every piece of Copper, Iron, or Gray Quartz unless you already know the next recipe is safe.

<h2 id="unlock-electromagnetism-and-craft-ftl">
  Unlock Electromagnetism and craft FTL Engine - Eco
</h2>

After Gray Quartz, Gravite, and the Silicon Gel step, unlock **Electromagnetism**. This opens the route into **FTL Engine - Eco**.

The usual blockers are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>not enough Wire,</li>
  <li>missing Silicon Gel,</li>
  <li>missing Crystalline Focuser,</li>
  <li>or not enough basic module parts.</li>
</ul>

If you are short on a part, check whether the missing item comes from Copper, Iron, or Gray Quartz before flying away.

<h2 id="install-ftl-and-refill-magnetic-plasma">
  Install FTL Engine - Eco and refill Magnetic Plasma
</h2>

After crafting **FTL Engine - Eco**, return to the Shipyard and install it on your ship. Confirm the edit before leaving the station.

Then refill **Magnetic Plasma**. The first FTL route can fail here because the engine is installed, but the ship still needs plasma for the jump. In the current build shown, the station ship/dock prompt offers a Magnetic Plasma fill for **40 credits**. If you are short on money, sell a small amount of spare processed material and come back to the dock.

Use this checklist before taking off:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>FTL Engine - Eco is installed, not just crafted.</li>
  <li>Magnetic Plasma is refilled at the station.</li>
  <li>You still have enough credits for possible repair or early travel mistakes.</li>
  <li>The galaxy map route points toward the next system on the RakMu path.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="reach-rakmu">
  Reach RakMu
</h2>

After Magnetic Plasma is refilled, use the galaxy map to follow the jump route toward **RakMu**. You may need more than one jump, so use the map instead of guessing by sight.

When you reach the station, dock carefully and talk to the clerk. RakMu is not the end of the beginner route. It is the point where the game opens into ship upgrades, Mining Bureau resource work, Noviciate progression, and eventually base building.

<h2 id="faq">
  SpaceCraft beginner FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in SpaceCraft?

Fly to Solar Alpha 1, mine Copper and Iron, return to Babylon, craft and install Scanalyzer Alpha, then go to Solar Alpha 2 for Gray Quartz and Gravite.

### How do I avoid crashing my ship early?

Slow down before approaching planets, terrain, landmarks, or docking ports. Use boost for travel, then stop boosting when you need control.

### Does boosting drain battery?

Yes. Boosting drains ship battery, and stopping boost lets it recharge over time. Do not keep boosting when you are trying to scan or land.

### What do Copper and Iron look like?

Early Copper is tied to Copper or Cupric-style stones, while Iron is tied to Iron, Ferric, or Ferrous-style stones and outcrops. Check the resource label when you scan or target the node.

### Where do I find Gray Quartz?

Go to Solar Alpha 2 after installing Scanalyzer Alpha. Use radar to find the High Peak landmark, then look for the dark crystal-like Gray Quartz nodes, scan them, and mine them.

### Why can’t I mine Gray Quartz?

You probably have not scanned it yet, or Scanalyzer Alpha is crafted but not installed. Install the module in the Shipyard before returning to Solar Alpha 2.

### How do I find Gravite?

Use radar for the approximate direction, then search visually. Gravite interferes with precise radar tracking, so it does not behave like a normal ore marker.

### Where do I get Silicon Gel?

Buy Silicon Gel from the Company Exchanger during the trading objective. Sell only enough resources to afford the purchase unless you know the next recipe is safe.

### How do I refill Magnetic Plasma?

After installing FTL Engine - Eco, return to a station, board or access the ship/dock prompt, and refill Magnetic Plasma before jumping. In the current build shown, the refill prompt can cost 40 credits.

### Why can’t I jump after installing FTL Engine - Eco?

Check two things: the engine must be installed in the Shipyard, and Magnetic Plasma must be refilled before the jump.

### Who should I talk to first at RakMu?

Talk to the RakMu Clerk first. The clerk introduces the next routes: Bob for ship upgrades, Ingrid for Noviciate, and the Mining Bureau for resource work.

### What does Bob do at RakMu?

Bob starts the ship improvement route. He points you toward cargo space, resource detectors, mining lasers, and battery upgrades.

### What does Ingrid do at RakMu?

Ingrid starts the Noviciate test route. Noviciate rank matters later if you want corporation access.

### What does the Mining Bureau do?

The Mining Bureau helps with targeted resource work. It can give sample tasks, sell coordinates for resources, and pay for useful exploration information.

### Should I build a base right after RakMu?

Not immediately unless you already have the parts and a clear deposit target. Most players should first improve cargo, detector, mining laser, and resource knowledge before committing to the first base.
