<h2 id="supermarket-chaos-section-map-item-locations">
  Supermarket Chaos section map and item locations
</h2>

Supermarket Chaos says the store has **4,668 products** spread across **16 sections**. That sounds like a normal supermarket map, but the actual sorting logic is stranger than that.

The mistake I avoid is using real-life supermarket logic too confidently. Some signs behave exactly how I expect: pizza goes Frozen, routers go iT / Electronics, and vases go Home Decor. Other signs are less intuitive. The sweet and pantry areas are the biggest example: **Breakfasts / Sweets**, **Tea / Candies**, and **Pasta / Condiments** can all catch items that look similar at first.

So this page does not try to become a 4,668-item database. It gives me a route map: where I check first, what I test next, and when I should stop guessing.

<h2 id="faq">
  Supermarket Chaos section map FAQ
</h2>

### How many sections are in Supermarket Chaos?

The game lists **16 sections**, but several signs are paired or visually close, so I route by section families instead of trying to memorize a perfect list immediately.

### Does sugar go in Breakfasts / Sweets or Tea / Candies?

I check **Tea / Candies** first for sugar. Sugar is one of the best examples of why I do not rely on real supermarket logic.

### Do all candy items go in Tea / Candies?

No. Candy and sweet items can split between **Breakfasts / Sweets** and **Tea / Candies** depending on the exact product. I use shelf feedback instead of moving every candy-labeled item to one sign.

### Where do chocolate chips and cakes go?

I check **Breakfasts / Sweets** first for cake-style and chocolate-chip items.

### Where do pasta and condiments go?

Use **Pasta / Condiments** for pasta, sauces, canned pantry items, salt, pepper, mayonnaise, chips, and other shelf-stable goods that do not fit a more specific sign.

### Where do sushi and instant ramen go?

I check **Bento** for sushi and prepared boxed food. I check **Ramen** for instant ramen, udon cups, and noodle bowls.

### Where do shampoo and toothpaste go?

I check **Toilet / Personal Care** first. Cleaning products like bleach, dish soap, and multi cleaner belong in the Cleaning route instead.

### Where do routers, headphones, and gamepads go?

I check **iT / Electronics** first, but I still read the item name. Some technical-sounding products may actually be books.

### Should I use Shelf Finder for every confusing item?
