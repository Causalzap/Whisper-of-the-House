The important rule is simple: **skills solve speed problems, not category problems**. If I know what an item is and where it belongs, upgrades save time. If I am guessing whether sugar, shampoo, wine, bento, ramen, or frozen food belongs somewhere, I need section knowledge first.

<h2 id="supermarket-chaos-best-upgrades-skills">
  Supermarket Chaos best upgrades and skills
</h2>

Supermarket Chaos gets slow for one reason: the store is huge. Once the first shelves are done, the real cost is walking, searching, checking shelf signs, and carrying too few products per trip.

That is why I do not buy upgrades randomly. I buy the upgrade that fixes my current bottleneck.

My default order is:

<strong>Extra Carry → Match Finder → Shelf Finder → more Extra Carry → Match Finder+ → optional movement speed.</strong>

This page is only about upgrade decisions. If a product keeps going to the wrong shelf, use the <Link href="/supermarket-chaos/section-map-item-locations">section map and tricky placements guide</Link>. If you are chasing Too Heavy, One More, or Complete, use the <Link href="/supermarket-chaos/achievements-guide">achievements guide</Link>.

<h2 id="extra-carry-first">
  Why I take Extra Carry first
</h2>

**Extra Carry** is the safest first upgrade because it helps everywhere.

At the start, a small inventory means I keep interrupting myself. I spot several products from the same area, but I cannot take enough of them. Once carry capacity goes up, each trip becomes more useful, and I can clear more of a section before walking away.

Extra Carry also matters for the achievement route, but I do not repeat the full Too Heavy route here. The important upgrade decision is simple: if I am walking back and forth with too few items, carry capacity fixes that problem immediately.

<h2 id="matchfinder">
  How to use Match Finder
</h2>

**Match Finder** is useful when I already know what product I am holding and want more copies of it.

I do not use it as a replacement for section knowledge. If I am holding a confusing product and I do not know whether it belongs to bento, frozen, sweets, or another section, finding more copies only gives me a bigger pile of the same problem.

  <li>products with several visible copies nearby,</li>

<h2 id="shelf-finder">
  When Shelf Finder is better
</h2>

**Shelf Finder** solves a different problem. It helps when I know what the product is, but I do not remember where the shelf is.

That happens often after the store starts opening up. I may know that an item belongs to books, toilet, cleaning, dairy, drinks, or home decoration, but the exact shelf is not in my head anymore. Shelf Finder turns that uncertainty into a route.

<h2 id="matchfinder-plus">
  Match Finder+ and late cleanup
</h2>

**Match Finder+** is a stronger late-run tool because it improves how Match Finder behaves after the store becomes easier to read.

While **Match Finder** is active, highlighted products are picked up automatically. That matters most after I have already removed a lot of floor clutter. If I use it too early, the store is still visually noisy, and I may not follow the highlights cleanly.

Shelf feedback also tells me what kind of problem I have. If a product is wrong, I probably have a category issue. If a shelf is incomplete, I may need more copies of the same product. If a shelf is complete, I can move on instead of overworking that area.

<h2 id="faq">
  Supermarket Chaos best upgrades FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best first upgrade in Supermarket Chaos?

I take **Extra Carry** first because it makes every trip more efficient and helps the whole route, not just one section.

### What does Extra Carry cost?

At the start of the skill track, **Extra Carry** shows **Lv 0/10** with a **$2** current upgrade cost. That makes it the easiest early purchase in my route.

### How many levels does Extra Carry have?

Extra Carry shows **Lv 0/10** on the skill screen. I do not need to max it immediately, but I keep adding levels when short inventory trips become the main slowdown.

### What level of Extra Carry do I need for Too Heavy?

Too Heavy needs me to hold **10 or more products at once**. I keep upgrading until the inventory can reach 10 held products, then use the achievements guide for the actual 10/10 setup.

### What does Match Finder do?

Match Finder shows dropped products that match the product I am holding. It is strongest after I already know where that product belongs.

### What does Shelf Finder do?

Shelf Finder shows the nearest shelf for the product I am holding. I use it when I know the product family but forgot the exact shelf.

### Is Match Finder+ worth it?

Yes, but I like it more in the mid-to-late run. It costs **$15** in my visible screenshot and is best after the store is less cluttered.

### Should I buy movement speed first?

I usually wait. Movement speed is useful, but carry capacity and finder tools solve bigger early problems.

### How do I get more money for upgrades?

Place products correctly. Correct stocking earns money, and that money funds the skill route.

### What should I do if a product still will not fit?

That is usually a category problem, not an upgrade problem. Use the section map and tricky placements guide instead of brute forcing shelves.

### Do I need every upgrade before finishing the game?

No. A good route needs enough carry and finder help to avoid wasting time. You do not need to max everything before making real progress.
