<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Romestead**, gods are your progression map. Use the **Altar** to read god objectives, offer the required item, claim the reward, then return to the **Workbench** to check new buildings.

Do not dump rare loot into the altar blindly. **Guardian's Eye**, **40 wheat**, and **The Moonstring** are all examples of items that unlock real systems: Minerva progression, Farmstead, and Well. Favor is different again: spend it on practical buffs that solve your current bottleneck.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="profane-artifice">
  What is Profane Artifice in Romestead?
</h2>

**Profane Artifice** is the kind of altar-related term you should treat as progression, not flavor text. If it appears in your god, artifact, altar, or crafting route, pause and read the objective carefully before spending rare materials elsewhere.

The safe way to handle it is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Check which god or altar objective mentions Profane Artifice.</li>
  <li>Look for the required artifact, crafted item, or dungeon-related step.</li>
  <li>Do not sacrifice rare materials until you know whether they are needed for this route.</li>
  <li>After completing the objective, claim the reward and return to the Workbench to check new construction options.</li>
</ul>

If nothing changes after the step, treat it like other altar rewards: confirm that the item was accepted, claim the reward, and check whether the unlock appears at the Workbench instead of the altar screen.

<h2 id="temple-of-vulcan">

</h2>

If your next altar route points toward **Vulcan** or the **Temple of Vulcan**, treat it as a crafting and progression check. Vulcan-related objectives are usually not solved by random offering spam. They often ask you to understand which artifact, crafted item, building unlock, or Workbench option the altar is pointing toward.

Before spending materials, check:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

  <li>whether the required item is a dungeon artifact or crafted object,</li>
  <li>whether the reward needs to be claimed before it appears,</li>
  <li>whether the next step appears in Construction Mode at the Workbench.</li>

If you are also stuck on dungeon artifacts, use the dungeon recovery guide before carrying rare items into another run.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I restore gods in Romestead?

Use the Altar. Read the god objective, offer the required item or resource, claim the reward, then check the Workbench for new construction options.

### Which gods appear early in Romestead?

Early routes show or reference gods and god clues such as Minerva, Mars, Mercury, Diana, Venus and Vulcan. Minerva is tied to the first major Guardian's Eye route.

### What is the difference between offerings and sacrifices?

Offerings are usually specific items or resources given to a god objective. Sacrifices are broader or more costly altar commitments. In both cases, read the altar text before spending rare items.

### What does favor do?

Favor unlocks god-related buffs or bonuses. Pick favor based on your current bottleneck: wood, mining, building, healing, movement, defense, trade, food, or production.

### What does the Guardian's Eye do?

Offer the Guardian's Eye at the altar after beating the Giant Owl. It pushes Minerva progression and unlocks major early construction progress after you claim the reward.

### How do I unlock Farmstead?

Complete Honoring the Soil by offering 40 wheat. This unlocks Farmstead and starts the farming automation route.

### How do I unlock Well?

Follow The Moonstring objective. It points you toward a dungeon artifact and unlocks Well, which matters for Farmstead watering.

### Why did I complete an offering but nothing changed?

You may not have claimed the reward, or you may not have checked the Workbench afterward. Some altar rewards show up as new construction options.

### Should I offer bones and monster loot?

Check the altar first. Bones and monster loot can matter for gods like Mars or other objectives, but you should not dump valuable stacks blindly.

### Is there a best god to focus first?

Early on, focus on the god route that solves your current bottleneck. Minerva pushes the first boss unlock route, Farmstead needs wheat, Moonstring unlocks Well, Mars helps defense/combat pressure, and Mercury points toward speed, trade, and economy-style bonuses.

### What is Profane Artifice in Romestead?

Treat Profane Artifice as an altar or progression clue, not random flavor text. Check which god objective mentions it, what item or artifact it requires, and whether the reward needs to be claimed before new Workbench options appear.

### What is the Temple of Vulcan in Romestead?

The Temple of Vulcan is connected to Vulcan-style crafting, artifact, or progression checks. If your altar route points there, read the objective text carefully before sacrificing rare items or assuming the unlock appears automatically.

### Why is Moonstring important for Farmstead?

The Moonstring route unlocks Well. That matters because Well supports Farmstead watering, so a farming problem can actually be a god or dungeon artifact progression problem.
