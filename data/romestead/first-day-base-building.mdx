<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
For your **first day in Romestead**, rush a working settlement, not a giant base. Get sticks and flint shards, craft tools, place a **Workbench**, build a **Town Core**, then add an **Altar**, housing, **Food Storage**, a **Leatherworker**, and one assigned worker.

Do not recruit aggressively or rush the Giant Owl until your town can feed, house, employ, and defend people. The detailed readiness check is in the exploration section below.

<h2 id="death-setting">
  Choose the death setting before you build
</h2>

Romestead can punish early wandering if you die while carrying too much. Before you start treating every ruin and cave as free loot, check whether your current death setting drops carried inventory.

If you are still learning the map, Softcore is easier. If you want full survival pressure, keep the normal setting, but play around it: store rare items, return home before night spirals out of control, and do not carry boss or altar items into random danger.

For dungeon-specific recovery, use <Link href="/romestead/dungeon-traps-death-recovery/">Romestead Dungeon Traps and Death Recovery</Link>.

<h2 id="flint-tools">
  Get flint shards, sticks and your first tools
</h2>

Your first resource goal is simple: collect sticks and make **flint shards**. If you pick up a full Flint piece, throw the Flint onto a Rock to break it into shards. That gives you the early material you need for your first tools.

If a later Early Access patch changes the exact interaction, follow the current tutorial prompt, but the lesson stays the same: do not wander around waiting for perfect shard pickups when the game is teaching you how to break raw flint into usable pieces.

Once you have enough early materials, craft your first tools and start processing wood. Logs are not just inventory icons. You may need to chop, move, and process them into usable building materials before the first town comes together.

<h2 id="base-layout">
  Best early base layout in Romestead
</h2>

Your first Romestead base does not need to look perfect. It needs to be compact, easy to defend, and simple for citizens to use. Put the **Town Core**, first housing, **Food Storage**, **Leatherworker**, and your first workplace close enough that you can protect them during raids and check them quickly when something stops working.

A good early layout is a small support cluster:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Town Core</strong> near the center, because worker assignment and town checks happen there.</li>
  <li><strong>Food Storage</strong> close to housing and workplaces, so citizens are not separated from accessible food.</li>
  <li><strong>Housing / Insula</strong> close to the core instead of scattered around the map.</li>
  <li><strong>Leatherworker</strong> near storage, because it supports early armor, Bedroll, and worker progression.</li>
  <li><strong>Workbench</strong> in a place you can reach often while expanding the first settlement.</li>
  <li><strong>Walls, traps, battlements, or early defense</strong> around the cluster before you spread outward.</li>
</ul>

Do not place your first buildings in a long line or far apart just because the land looks open. A spread-out town is harder to defend, harder to troubleshoot, and easier to lose control of when raids arrive.

If you are not sure where to expand next, leave open space near the Town Core for storage, production, and later unlocks instead of boxing yourself in with early houses.

<h2 id="food-storage">
  Food Storage is not optional
</h2>

Food in your backpack helps you. Food in **Food Storage** helps the town.

That distinction matters because citizens need accessible food, not just the fact that you personally own some olives, meat, or crops. If your first citizen arrives and the food system is not ready, your town can become unstable before the rest of your base is even finished.

For food and Farmstead routing after the first day, use <Link href="/romestead/farmstead-workers-food-logistics">Romestead Farmstead, Workers, Food and Logistics Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="material-storage-note">
  Do not plan around Material Storage on day one
</h2>

Early storage is awkward, but do not assume **Material Storage** is a first-minute fix. It comes later through boss and god progression. That means your first-day plan should use simple storage discipline instead of waiting for the perfect storage building.

Use chests, Food Storage, and careful backpack habits until the first major unlocks start opening better options.

For the first boss route that leads into stronger building unlocks, read <Link href="/romestead/giant-owl-guardian-of-minerva">Romestead Giant Owl Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Romestead?

Collect sticks and flint shards, craft tools, place a Workbench, build a Town Core, then add an Altar, housing, Food Storage, a Leatherworker, and one assigned worker.

### How do I get flint shards?

Look for flint and interact with the environment. Earlier builds show flint being broken into shards by throwing or striking it against rock. Follow your current tutorial prompt if the exact interaction changes.

### Is Altar required on the first day?

Yes, you should build it early. The Altar connects your first town to god objectives, offerings, the Guardian's Eye route, and later unlocks.

### What are the best first buildings?

Workbench, Town Core, Altar, housing, Food Storage, and Leatherworker are the most important early buildings. After that, add worker support, hauling, roads, and basic defense.

### How do I sleep in Romestead?

Craft a Leather Bedroll at the Leatherworker with 2 Leather, 2 Wool, and 1 Wheat, place it inside a house, then interact with it to sleep through the night. This also sets your respawn point.

### Does Bedroll set a respawn point?

Treat Bedroll as part of your sleep and safety setup. It is especially useful before night, raids, boss attempts, and risky dungeon trips.

### Why are my citizens not working?

Most work buildings need a citizen assigned. Open the Town Core and assign the citizen to the correct workplace.

### Why do I need Food Storage?

Citizens need accessible food. Food in your personal inventory does not solve the town's food problem.

### What is Defense Value?

Defense Value reflects how prepared your town is to handle threats. If you build walls, battlements, traps, or defensive structures, check whether the town defense value improves.

### What are Town Raids?

Town Raids are attacks on your settlement. If a raid is approaching, return home, prepare food and healing, and fight near your defenses instead of staying far away.

### Can citizens die during raids?

Your citizens can be put at risk if the town is under-defended. Avoid over-recruiting before you have housing, food, jobs, and defense.

### Should I fight the Giant Owl on the first day?

Usually no. Build a stable town, craft armor or better gear, prepare food, understand sleep/death recovery, and then fight the Giant Owl when you can safely claim the Guardian's Eye reward.

### When do I unlock Material Storage?

Material Storage is tied to early boss and god progression rather than your first few minutes. Do not rely on it to solve day-one storage.

### Can you move buildings in Romestead?

If a building placement feels wrong, first check the building or construction UI for move, cancel, dismantle, or rebuild options. During the first day, the safer habit is to place the Town Core, housing, Food Storage, and workplaces with extra room around them so you do not have to rebuild your whole town later.

### Can you remove a Workbench, chest, or farmland in Romestead?

Check the object or building interaction menu for any remove, dismantle, cancel, or destroy option. If the game does not offer a clean removal option for that object in your current build, work around it by expanding your layout instead of trapping your important buildings in a cramped starter area.
