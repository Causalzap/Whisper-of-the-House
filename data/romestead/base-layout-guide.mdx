<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
The best **Romestead base layout** is a compact town near water, clay, stone, wood, coal, and open space, preferably close to the center of your early travel routes. Do not place Town Core at spawn without scouting.

Your build priority is simple: **feed citizens first, assign workers, stabilize production, expand carefully, then defend the core**. Keep Blacksmith, Farmstead, Food Storage, housing, Workbench, Material Storage, and roads close enough that workers can function and raids cannot punish a huge scattered town.

<h2 id="base-location">
  Best base location: central, resource-rich and expandable
</h2>

A good Romestead base location is not just “near some trees.” It should shorten your early travel loop and still make sense after new buildings unlock.

Look for a spot that is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>near the center of your early map routes</strong>, so travel to resources, dungeons, and later biome paths is not painful,</li>
  <li><strong>close to water</strong>, because manual watering and Watermill placement both depend on it,</li>
  <li><strong>close to clay</strong>, because clay-heavy construction becomes a real bottleneck,</li>
  <li><strong>close to stone and wood</strong>, because early buildings, roads, walls, and gates consume them quickly,</li>
  <li><strong>not too far from coal or stronger resource routes</strong>, because mid-game crafting eventually matters,</li>
  <li><strong>open enough for Farmstead, Well, Watermill, Material Storage, roads, housing, and defenses</strong>.</li>
</ul>

If you already placed the Town Core badly, do not panic. Use roads, carts, storage, and a compact expansion plan to reduce the damage. But if you have not committed yet, spend a few minutes scouting first.

<h2 id="town-core-placement">
  Place Workbench and Town Core deliberately
</h2>

The **Workbench** opens Construction Mode, and the **Town Core** defines your settlement loop. Treat both as long-term anchors, not temporary tutorial objects.

A good first Town Core position should be:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>close enough to Food Storage and housing,</li>
  <li>not blocking the space you want for roads or Farmstead later,</li>
  <li>near the daily path between Workbench, storage, and workplaces,</li>
  <li>inside the area you can defend with torches and short walls,</li>
  <li>close enough to resources that the first construction push is not painful.</li>

<h2 id="compact-core">
  Build a compact production core
</h2>

Your first town should be a working support cluster, not a huge city. Keep the buildings you check every day close enough that you can feed citizens, assign workers, move materials, and respond to raids without running across the map.

  Farmstead, fertile land, Well and Watermill layout
</h2>

Farmstead is where many early layouts start to fail. It is not enough to place the building wherever you see empty land. Farming depends on space, water, food storage, and processing.

The important rule is that **Watermill is not a normal free-placement production building**. Plan it around water access, then connect it back to Farmstead, Food Storage, and the road loop. If you place farms far from water and later realize the Watermill route does not fit, your food chain becomes harder than it needs to be.

<h2 id="material-storage-logistics">
  Material Storage and Logistics Tent change the layout
</h2>

Material Storage is powerful, but placement still matters. Before your logistics systems improve, every distant building becomes a manual hauling route.

Place Material Storage where it shortens the path between:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Lumberyard and wood-heavy construction,</li>
  <li>Quarry / stone output and building projects,</li>
  <li>Clay Pit or clay gathering routes,</li>
  <li>Blacksmith and crafting needs,</li>
  <li>Carpenter and building upgrades,</li>
  <li>the roads you actually use while expanding.</li>
</ul>

The **Logistics Tent** changes the town later because it lets buildings pass resources through a more automated route. Once that unlock is available, your layout can spread slightly more, but it should still be readable: production buildings should connect to storage and crafting instead of becoming isolated islands.

<h2 id="raid-defense-layout">
  Layout for raids: compact core, choke points and kill boxes
</h2>

Raid defense starts with layout. If your important buildings are scattered, every night raid costs more wood, more torches, more repair time, and more attention.

The basic pattern is:

caption="Practical defensive layout patterns in Romestead."

A simple early **kill-box courtyard** is not complicated. Leave one obvious approach into the protected core, light it with torches, place walls or natural terrain on the sides, and keep yourself positioned where you can fight enemies before they reach Blacksmith, Farmstead, Food Storage, or housing.

Do not overbuild the kill box before your food and workers are stable. A small controlled entrance is enough early. Later, you can add a second wall layer, traps, better weapons, or automated defense.

<h2 id="natural-barriers">
  Use natural barriers and choke points
</h2>

Natural terrain can reduce your wall cost. If cliffs, water, quarry edges, land bridges, or dense terrain already create a narrow approach, use that shape instead of drawing a huge square around the town.

Good choke points are useful because they:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>reduce how many wall pieces you need,</li>
  <li>make torch placement more efficient,</li>
  <li>give enemies fewer paths into town,</li>
  <li>make traps and ranged attacks easier to use,</li>
  <li>turn a messy raid into a predictable fight.</li>
</ul>

Do not pick a bad base location only because it has one narrow entrance. The best layout combines resources, open building space, and defensible routes.

<h2 id="move-remove-buildings">
  Can you move or remove buildings?
</h2>

During Early Access, always check the current building UI before assuming a structure can be moved cleanly. Some objects may have remove, dismantle, cancel, or rebuild options, while other placement mistakes may be easier to work around.

The best fix is prevention: scout first, leave space around Farmstead, and avoid trapping Town Core, Food Storage, or Material Storage behind roads and buildings you may regret.

If you already placed something badly, decide whether rebuilding is worth the material cost. Moving a whole settlement can waste time, especially if clay-heavy structures are involved.

<h2 id="construction-tools">
  Use construction tools to plan faster
</h2>

Construction Mode is easier when you use the tools the game gives you. If you are placing roads, walls, or repeated structures one tile at a time, you may be making layout work harder than it needs to be.

Use line and rectangle placement for roads and walls when possible. Rotate buildings before final placement, and cancel bad construction sites before you spend materials.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Where should I build my first base in Romestead?

Build near the center of your early travel routes, with water, clay, stone, wood, coal access, and enough open space for Farmstead, Well, Watermill, Material Storage, roads and defenses.

### What is the best town layout in Romestead?

The best early layout is a compact protected core with Town Core, housing, Food Storage, Workbench, Blacksmith, Farmstead route, Material Storage, and roads close enough to defend and manage.

### Should I make a compact base or a large city?

Start compact. A large city is harder to light, wall, repair, and defend during raids. Expand once food, workers, storage, logistics and defense are stable.

### Where should I place Farmstead?

Place Farmstead where it has fertile space, access to water or future Well support, nearby Food Storage, and a clean route to processing buildings such as Mill or Watermill.

### Where should I put the Watermill?

Plan Watermill around water access. Do not treat it like a normal building that can go anywhere in your production core.

### How does Logistics Tent change base layout?

Before Logistics Tent, keep production and storage close because you move many items by hand. After Logistics Tent unlocks, you can connect buildings more efficiently, but you still need a layout that is easy to defend and understand.

### Should I wall the entire town?

Not at first. Protect the important core first: Blacksmith, Farmstead, Food Storage, housing, Town Core, and main roads. A huge wall around a scattered town is expensive and slow to repair.

### Can I move buildings in Romestead?

Check the current building UI for remove, dismantle, cancel, or rebuild options. If moving is expensive or awkward, work around the mistake and plan the next expansion more carefully.
