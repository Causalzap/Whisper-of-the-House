For **Romestead night raids**, defend the buildings the Fallen actually care about. Protect the **Blacksmith** first, then the **Farmstead** and the food/worker core around it.

Place **torches before full walls**. Light helps deter the Fallen, while walls mainly buy time and fill approach gaps. After each raid, check **Defense Value** at the Workbench and read the **Town Report** so you know whether citizens were injured, loyalty changed, or your defense line failed.

<h2 id="when-raids-start">
  When should you prepare for raids?
</h2>

Start preparing before your town feels “big.” The mistake is waiting until the first serious raid warning, then realizing your buildings are too spread out, your citizens are exposed, and your defense line is too expensive to finish.

Your first goal is not a perfect fortress. It is a settlement that can survive pressure:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Blacksmith and Farmstead are close enough to protect.</li>
  <li>Town Core, housing, Food Storage, and early workplaces are not scattered across the map.</li>
  <li>Citizens have places to sleep and food they can actually access.</li>
  <li>Roads and entrances are lit.</li>
  <li>Walls or fences cover the most important approach paths first.</li>
  <li>You know where to fight when enemies arrive.</li>
</ul>

If you are still working on the opening setup, start with <Link href="/romestead/first-day-base-building/">Romestead First Day Build Order</Link> before turning the town into a large defended city.

</h2>

The priority order is what makes night raid defense different from normal base building:

The practical rule is simple: map the Blacksmith and Farmstead first, then build your defense around them. A short, lit route around the buildings the Fallen want is stronger than an unfinished wall around a scattered town.

<h2 id="light-torches">
  Torches before walls: where to place light first
</h2>

Do not treat torches as cosmetic decoration. In Romestead, light helps deter the Fallen, which makes torches one of your cheapest early defense tools.

The order matters. Do not start by lighting random roads or decorating your whole town. Light the production buildings that raids threaten first.

<GuideTable
caption="Torch placement priority for early night raids."

rows={torchPriorityRows}
/>

After that, use walls to close the dark approach gaps between lit zones. This is cheaper and safer than trying to build a full four-sided perimeter before your town can afford it.

<h2 id="compact-layout">
  Keep the first town compact enough to defend
</h2>

A spread-out town makes every raid more expensive. You need more torches, more walls, more repair time, and more running between targets.

Your first protected core should keep these close together:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Blacksmith</strong>, because it is the key production target to defend.</li>
  <li><strong>Farmstead</strong>, because food automation becomes a raid recovery issue.</li>
  <li><strong>Town Core</strong>, housing, and Food Storage.</li>
  <li><strong>Workbench</strong> and early production buildings you use after attacks.</li>
</ul>

You do not need a perfect square city. You need a short defense line around the buildings that matter.

For a deeper building plan, use <Link href="/romestead/base-layout-guide/">Romestead Town Layout Guide</Link> once that guide is available.

<h2 id="natural-barriers">
  Use terrain and natural barriers
</h2>

Natural barriers can reduce the amount of wall you need. If the land already creates a narrow path, a quarry gap, a water edge, a cliff edge, or a small entrance between obstacles, use that shape instead of forcing a giant square wall.

This matters most early, when wood, stone, and worker time are still limited. A shorter wall line is easier to finish before raids become a bigger problem.

<h2 id="walls-traps">
  Walls fill gaps; they do not replace light
</h2>

Walls are useful, but they are not the first layer of defense. Use them to slow enemies on the direct paths toward your Blacksmith, Farmstead, and citizen core.

Build first wall sections where they change enemy movement:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>between dark approach routes and the Blacksmith,</li>
  <li>beside the Farmstead if it sits near the edge of town,</li>
  <li>at natural choke points where a short wall closes a long path,</li>
  <li>around housing and Food Storage if citizens are exposed.</li>
</ul>

Traps are strongest when the route is predictable. Do not scatter them randomly. Put them where enemies are already being funneled by walls, terrain, and torch placement.

<h2 id="citizens-loyalty">
  Protect citizens, loyalty and worker progress
</h2>

Walls are repairable. Citizen damage can cost you more.

If citizens are injured, killed, or lose loyalty because the raid went badly, the town can be set back even if the buildings still stand. This is why your defense plan should protect people, not only your personal house.

After each raid, check:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>whether any citizens were hurt,</li>
  <li>whether loyalty changed,</li>
  <li>whether anyone lost progress,</li>
  <li>whether housing is enough for the current population,</li>
  <li>whether important workers were caught outside the defended area.</li>
</ul>

Use the Town Report and the town management screen as your post-raid diagnosis tools.

<h2 id="defense-value">
  Check Defense Value at the Workbench
</h2>

Do not guess whether the town is defended. Check **Defense Value** at the Workbench and use it as your warning signal before the next raid.

If Defense Value is low, diagnose the town in this order:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Key buildings:</strong> Are Blacksmith, Farmstead, Food Storage, and housing actually protected?</li>
  <li><strong>Light coverage:</strong> Are torches covering the buildings the Fallen target, or only random roads?</li>
  <li><strong>Approach gaps:</strong> Are there dark paths that lead straight to production buildings?</li>
  <li><strong>Wall placement:</strong> Are walls blocking real enemy routes, or just decorating the outside edge?</li>
  <li><strong>Town spread:</strong> Is your settlement too wide for the amount of defense you can maintain?</li>
  <li><strong>Population risk:</strong> Do citizens have housing and food inside the defended area?</li>
</ul>

Defense Value is not a full strategy by itself, but it is the best quick check for whether your defense upgrades are actually being recognized.

<h2 id="raid-scaling">
  How raids scale as your town grows
</h2>

The defense that works for your first small settlement will not hold forever. As your town expands, raids become harder because there are more buildings to protect, more dark approach gaps, and more citizens who can be affected by a bad night.

<GuideTable
caption="How to scale raid defense as Romestead progression opens up."
columns={scalingColumns}
rows={scalingRows}
/>

The key is to expand defense with your production core, not only with your outer border. When you add a new important building, ask whether it needs light, a wall gap closed, a safer road, or a better fallback path.

If raids are your main bottleneck, check the Mars route in the god system. Scorpios and other automated defense tools can reduce how much of the fight you need to handle manually.

<h2 id="after-raid">
  What to do after a raid
</h2>

A good raid defense plan includes the morning after. Do not immediately run into the wilderness with damaged walls, injured citizens, and empty food stores.

After a raid:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Clear any remaining enemies.</li>
  <li>Read the Town Report.</li>
  <li>Check citizen injuries, loyalty, and worker progress.</li>
  <li>Repair walls, traps, and broken defensive paths.</li>
  <li>Restock Food Storage.</li>
  <li>Add torches or walls where enemies reached the town too easily.</li>
  <li>Shorten your defensive line if the town is too spread out.</li>
</ol>

If the same weak point fails twice, do not just repair it. Redesign that entrance, light it better, or turn it into a controlled choke point.

<h2 id="coop-defense">
  Co-op raid defense roles
</h2>

In co-op, raids become much easier when players stop doing the same job. Do not have everyone chase the same Fallen while torches go out, walls break, or citizens are exposed.

For two players, the cleanest split is one fighter and one builder/repair player. For larger groups, assign someone to watch production buildings specifically, especially the Blacksmith and Farmstead.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What do the Fallen target in Romestead raids?

The Fallen pressure production buildings. Protect the Blacksmith first, then Farmstead, Food Storage, housing, and the citizen core around them.

### Do torches help against the Fallen?

Yes. Light helps deter the Fallen, so torches should go around Blacksmith, Farmstead, entrances, roads, and choke points before you spend everything on a full wall.

### Should I build torches or walls first?

Build torches first around key buildings, then use walls to fill the dark approach gaps. Walls are still useful, but they work best after you know what you are protecting.

### How do I defend against raids in Romestead?

Protect Blacksmith and Farmstead, keep your town compact, light the main routes, build walls on direct approach paths, check Defense Value, and fight near your prepared defenses.

### What is Defense Value?

Defense Value is a town defense signal you can check at the Workbench. Use it to confirm whether your torches, walls, traps, and defensive structures are actually improving the settlement.

### Why did my citizens lose loyalty after a raid?

A bad raid can affect citizen status, injuries, or loyalty. Read the Town Report after the attack and fix the weak point before the next night.

### Do raids get harder later?

Yes. As your town grows, you need wider torch coverage, stronger walls on real approach routes, and eventually better defensive tools or god progression support.

### What does Mars do for raid defense?

Mars progression can lead toward automated defense tools such as Scorpios. If raids are your main bottleneck, check the Mars route instead of only adding more manual walls.

### What should I check after a raid?

Clear remaining enemies, read the Town Report, check citizen injuries and loyalty, repair broken defenses, restock Food Storage, and improve the route where enemies reached your town.
