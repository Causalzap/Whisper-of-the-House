<h2 id="steam-deck-controls-settings">
  Steam Deck, controls, comfort settings, and Zen Mode
</h2>

Moldwasher is Steam Deck Verified, so I keep the Hub advice simple: focus on readable controls, quick tool switching, and comfort settings rather than making a separate thin Steam Deck page.

The practical control notes are:

- use wide spray for open mold, floors, and broad surfaces
- use precise stream for stickers, tight corners, and stubborn leftovers
- use Tab when the last tiny dirty spot will not show clearly
- switch tools often in late missions instead of forcing one tool
- use the blower for rice, rubbish bags, and loose-object objectives
- use fire only when the object reacts to burning
- use Zen Mode if normal mold growth feels too stressful
- use auto-fire / auto-clean if constant button holding gets tiring
- do not leave a mission before checking the result screen

For comfort, prioritize clear visibility and quick tool switching over trying to play every mission with one tool.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is Moldwasher?

Moldwasher is a tiny-sushi cleaning game where you clear mold, rice, bugs, mushrooms, hard chunks, toys, and kitchen messes with tools like a pressure washer, leaf blower, pickaxe / axe, and flamethrower.

### What should I do first in Moldwasher?

Clean the opening room, pick up the first coin and collectible, then start the fridge missions. Check the clear screen after each job so you do not miss hidden drops.

### What is the difference between wide spray and precise stream?

Wide spray is for open mold and large surfaces. Precise stream is for stickers, tight corners, edge pixels, and stubborn leftovers.

### What is the best early upgrade?

I like early pressure and radius on the starter washer, then I save for the next required tool. Buying the next tool usually opens more progress than maxing one tool too early.

### How do I get more FreshCash?

Replay short jobs and return to garage toy batches. In my run, garage batches paid about $200 FreshCash and also helped with coins and toy cleanup.

### What is gachapon used for?

Gachapon uses coins to buy rewards and can matter for final decorations, stickers, toys, and collectible cleanup. Use the collectibles guide when a reward-pool item seems missing.

### How do I clean rice or rubbish bags?

Use the leaf blower. Push rice or bags toward the target area instead of trying to wash them like mold.

### Why does the mold keep growing back?

Small leftovers can spread again. Clean the edge of the spread first, then sweep inward and use precise stream on stubborn patches.

### How do I find hidden drops?

Read the mission clear screen. If it shows 0/1 Hidden Drops, replay that mission and look for optional objects, covered spots, hidden pickups, or areas blocked by props.

### How many achievements does Moldwasher have?

Moldwasher has 27 achievements. Use the achievements guide for the full checklist and route.

### What is the hardest achievement?

Gamer Brain® is usually the hardest because it requires every collectible. All stickers, all toys, Sushi Set, and Finally Inner Peace are also common final blockers.

### What should I do after finishing the story?

Use the Hidden Objects & Collectibles Guide for Gamer Brain® and missing collectibles, then use the Achievements Guide for the final 27-achievement cleanup.
