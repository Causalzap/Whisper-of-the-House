<h2 id="servo-skull-inputs">
  Servo Skull inputs: double-tap Tag vs Blitz input
</h2>

Most Servo Skull confusion comes from one mistake: treating every skull command like the same button.

The base Servo-Skull is controlled with **double-tap Tag**. That is the command I use when I want the skull to shoot an enemy or handle a Data Interrogator.

Medicae Servo-Skull and Purgator Servo-Skull are different. Those use the **Blitz / grenade input**. Medicae needs a valid ally target. Purgator needs a valid area target.

The simple rule is:

* **enemy or Data Interrogator:** double-tap Tag;
* **downed, hogtied, or netted ally:** Blitz / grenade input with Medicae Servo-Skull;
* **flamer skull placement:** Blitz / grenade input with Purgator Servo-Skull;
* **nothing happens:** wrong input, wrong target, no line of sight, or no shared Charges left.

<GuideCallout title="Fast troubleshooting rule">
If the skull is not responding, stop spamming the same button. Check whether the action wants double-tap Tag or Blitz input first.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="base-servo-skull">
  Base Servo-Skull and Improved Servo-Skull
</h2>

The base Servo-Skull is not just cosmetic. It follows Skitarii, shoots with a Laspistol, and can be ordered with **double-tap Tag**.

Use double-tap Tag for two main jobs:

1. send the skull to shoot nearby enemies;
2. send the skull to solve a Data Interrogator.

The base skull can also be empowered with Blitz input. During the 8-second empower window, it gains **+100% Fire Rate**, **+25% Damage**, applies **1 Burn stack per hit up to 8**, and makes the target take **+15% more damage for 5 seconds**. The empower has a **16-second cooldown**.

That means the base skull is stronger than it first looks, especially when I use it on a target the team is already focusing.

The important upgrade is **Improved / Artificer Servo-Skull**. This makes the previously temporary base skull bonuses permanent. If I want a build where the basic skull is always doing meaningful damage and debuff work, this is the upgrade I care about.

### Noospheric Command

**Noospheric Command** is the base Servo-Skull attack modifier I would take only if I am actually using the skull as part of my damage loop.

When I order the Servo-Skull to attack an enemy, Noospheric Command greatly increases its Fire Rate for **2 seconds** and costs **25% Capacitance**. That makes it useful when I have enough Capacitance generation and want the skull to help burn down priority targets.

I would take Noospheric Command when:

* my build already generates Capacitance reliably;
* I often double-tap Tag enemies for the base skull attack;
* I want the basic skull to contribute damage instead of only utility;
* I already understand the difference between base skull, Medicae, and Purgator commands.

I would skip or delay it when:

* Capacitance still feels rare;
* I mainly use Servo Skull for Data Interrogator and rescues;
* I need points for Medicae Servo-Skull, Purgator Servo-Skull, or survival first.

The short version: **Noospheric Command is worth it for an active base-skull attack build, but it is not the first support node I would take if my main problem is rescue or objective safety.**

<h2 id="data-interrogator">
  How to use Servo Skull on Data Interrogator
</h2>

Data Interrogator is one of the best reasons to bring Servo Skull utility.

Normally, someone has to stop fighting and interact with the objective. With Skitarii, I can double-tap Tag on the Data Interrogator and let the skull handle the interaction while I keep fighting.

<h2 id="why-servo-skull-not-working">
  Why Servo Skull is not working
</h2>

If Servo Skull does nothing, I check these problems in order.

### You are using the wrong input

Base skull and Data Interrogator use **double-tap Tag**. Medicae and Purgator use **Blitz / grenade input**. This is the most common reason the skull feels broken.

### You are targeting the wrong thing

Medicae needs a knocked down, hogtied, or netted ally. Purgator needs an area. Data Interrogator needs the objective prompt. If the target is not valid, the skull will not behave the way you expect.

### You do not have line of sight

Medicae rescue is strongest when the ally is visible. If walls, corners, enemies, or awkward elevation block the target, move first.

### Medicae and Purgator Charges are gone

Medicae and Purgator share **4 Charges**. If I spend charges on flamer placement, I may not have Medicae ready for a rescue. If I spend charges on rescue, I may not have Purgator ready for a horde.

### The skull is following a bad position

If I hide behind poor cover or break the angle, the skull may not have the line it needs. Reposition before assuming the talent is bad.

<h2 id="medicae-servo-skull">
  Medicae Servo-Skull: rescue numbers and use
</h2>

Medicae Servo-Skull is the support skull I care about most in unstable groups.

It can target a **knocked down**, **hogtied**, or **netted** ally. The injection revives or rescues them, then grants **+75% Toughness Damage Reduction** and **20% Toughness per second** for **5 seconds**.

That post-rescue protection matters. It gives the rescued ally a short window to stand up, move, dodge, block, or get back into coherency instead of being instantly deleted again.

To use it:

1. look at the ally;
2. use Blitz / grenade input;
3. wait for the valid target highlight or prompt;
4. release to send the skull;
5. cover the rescue with shooting, blocking, or crowd control.

Medicae shares **4 Charges** with Purgator. I do not spend those charges casually if the team is already unstable. One saved charge can be the difference between rescuing a netted teammate and watching the run collapse.

<h2 id="purgator-servo-skull">
  Purgator Servo-Skull: flamer placement
</h2>

Purgator Servo-Skull gives you an additional skull equipped with a flamer. It uses Blitz / grenade input to target an area.

Once deployed, use Primary Action to switch between **Dispersed** and **Focused** Fire Mode.

I use **Focused Fire** when enemies are coming through one lane, doorway, stair, bridge, or tight choke.

<h2 id="objective-vs-manual">
  When Servo Skull is better than doing the objective yourself
</h2>

Servo Skull is better when touching the objective would put the team at risk.

I send the skull when:

* a wave is spawning;
* a Data Interrogator needs repeated attention;
* specials are alive;
* the team is split under pressure;
* enemies are already pushing the objective;
* I can contribute more by fighting than by standing still.

I do the objective manually when:

* the area is safe;
* the skull feels slower than the situation allows;
* I need to save skull resources;
* the prompt is awkward;
* the team is already covering me well.

The skull is not always faster. It is safer when it lets me keep control of the fight.

<h2 id="servo-skull-build-priority">
  Servo Skull build priority
</h2>

For a Servo Skull-focused route, I use this priority:

1. Learn double-tap Tag for base skull and Data Interrogator.
2. Add **Improved / Artificer Servo-Skull** if I want the base skull’s empowered bonuses active all the time.
3. Add **Medicae Servo-Skull** if public groups keep failing rescues.
4. Add **Purgator Servo-Skull** if the team needs area denial.
5. Manage the shared 4 Charges between Medicae and Purgator.
6. Keep enough weapon damage so the build does not become passive.

I do not build every skull option blindly. If the team is dying to failed rescues, Medicae is the priority. If the team is losing chokepoints and objectives, Purgator gains value. If the base skull is always active in my fights, Improved / Artificer Servo-Skull becomes more attractive.

<h2 id="common-servo-skull-mistakes">
  Common Servo Skull mistakes
</h2>

* **Using one input for everything:** base skull uses double-tap Tag; Medicae and Purgator use Blitz input.
* **Forgetting shared Charges:** Medicae and Purgator both use the same 4 Charges.
* **Spending Purgator before a rescue-heavy fight:** a flamer skull is useful, but a saved Medicae charge can rescue the run.
* **Sending the skull without line of sight:** move first if the ally or objective is blocked.
* **Expecting Data Interrogator skull use to be faster:** the value is staying free to fight, not always raw speed.
* **Ignoring Improved / Artificer Servo-Skull:** permanent empowered bonuses matter if the base skull is part of your build.
* **Placing Purgator behind the fight:** put fire where enemies are moving, not where the team already was.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I send Skitarii Servo Skull?

Double-tap your Tag input to send the base Servo-Skull at enemies or a Data Interrogator. Use Blitz / grenade input for Medicae and Purgator Servo-Skull actions.

### Why is my Servo Skull not working?

You are usually using the wrong input, targeting the wrong thing, missing line of sight, or out of shared Medicae / Purgator Charges.

### What does base Servo-Skull Blitz empower do?

For 8 seconds, it gives +100% Fire Rate, +25% Damage, applies 1 Burn stack per hit up to 8, and makes the target take +15% more damage for 5 seconds.

### What does Improved Servo-Skull do?

Improved / Artificer Servo-Skull makes the previously temporary empowered bonuses permanent, so the base skull contributes more consistently.

### Can Servo Skull do Data Interrogator objectives?

Yes. Double-tap Tag on a valid Data Interrogator to send the skull to solve it while you keep fighting.

### How does Medicae Servo-Skull work?

Target a knocked down, hogtied, or netted ally with Blitz input. It revives or injects them, then gives +75% Toughness Damage Reduction and 20% Toughness per second for 5 seconds.

### Do Medicae and Purgator Servo-Skull share Charges?

Yes. They share 4 Charges, so using Medicae can leave Purgator unavailable, and using Purgator can leave Medicae unavailable.

### What does Noospheric Command do?

Noospheric Command makes the base Servo-Skull attack faster for 2 seconds when you order it to attack an enemy, but it costs 25% Capacitance. I take it only if I actively use double-tap Tag for skull damage.

### What does Purgator Servo-Skull do?

It deploys a Flamer Servo-Skull at a targeted area. Use Primary Action to switch between Dispersed and Focused Fire Mode.

### Is Servo Skull better than doing objectives manually?

Not always. Servo Skull is better when staying free to fight is more important than doing the objective yourself.
