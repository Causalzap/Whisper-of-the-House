<h2 id="how-many-characters-are-in-sol-cesto">
  How many characters are in Sol Cesto?
</h2>

Sol Cesto currently has **7 playable characters**.

The most useful way to understand the roster is to split it into two groups:

### 1. Natural progression characters

These unlock through normal play, character-tree expansion, or clearing the game.

### 2. Hidden unlock characters

These require a specific in-run condition.

Right now, the clearest hidden example is **Lizard**.

<GuideCallout title="Simple roster rule">
If a character belongs to your normal home progression, character tree, or clear route, it is usually a **natural progression unlock**.

If it depends on a special item or a hidden run condition, it is a **hidden unlock**.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="starting-and-early-characters">
  Starting and early characters
</h2>

These are the characters most players will see first.

### Peasant

**Peasant** is the starting character.

There is no special unlock route here.

If you are just beginning the game, Peasant is the baseline roster slot that the rest of the progression gets compared against.

### Knight

**Knight** is an early progression character.

The important practical answer is not a hidden trick. Knight simply becomes available **very early through normal progression**, which is why most players treat Knight as their first real roster expansion rather than as a late unlock.

<GuideCallout title="What matters most here">
For Peasant and Knight, the practical question is not “what secret unlock path do I need?”

It is simply **how early they become available**, and the answer is: **very early**.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="character-tree-and-meta-progression-unlocks">
  Character-tree and meta-progression unlocks
</h2>

These are the characters that belong to Sol Cesto’s standard roster growth rather than a hidden item puzzle.

### Warrior and Wizard

**Warrior** and **Wizard** are best understood as **normal roster-expansion unlocks**.

The important thing they share is that they belong to the regular progression side of the game rather than to a hidden in-run condition.

That means:

- they are not Egg-style puzzle unlocks
- they are not secret biome-event unlocks
- they fit the broader home / character-tree expansion logic

At the moment, the most useful public answer is their **unlock type**, not a fake over-precise node-by-node path.

### Vampire

**Vampire** belongs in this same broad category, but her public route is cleaner:

she is bought from the **character-selection progression tree**, with player reports specifically placing her on the **right side of the tree**.

That is why Vampire should be thought of as a **meta-progression unlock**, not a hidden run puzzle.

<GuideCallout title="Most useful distinction">
If a character is bought or unlocked through the **roster / character tree side of progression**, it is a normal unlock.

If it depends on a special run item like the Egg, it is a hidden unlock.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="hidden-character-unlocks">
  Hidden character unlocks
</h2>

This is the category that causes the most confusion.

Right now, the clearest hidden-character route is **Lizard**.

### Lizard

Lizard is unlocked through the **Egg**.

The short version is:

1. buy the Egg from the shop
2. keep it in your inventory
3. reach **Biome 3**
4. let the Egg hatch automatically
5. return to the main menu after the run

That is why Lizard is different from Huntress, Warrior, Wizard, and Vampire.

Lizard behaves like a true hidden-character puzzle.

If you want the exact full route, use the <Link href="/sol-cesto/how-to-unlock-lizard/">full Lizard unlock guide</Link>.

<GuideCallout title="Hidden unlock rule">
If a route depends on a special run item and a specific condition inside that run, treat it as a **hidden unlock**, not a normal roster unlock.

That is exactly why Lizard stands apart from the rest of the cast.
</GuideCallout>
