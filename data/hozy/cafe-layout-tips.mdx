<h2 id="why-the-cafe-level-gets-cluttered-so-fast">
  Why the Cafe level gets cluttered so fast
</h2>

The Cafe feels easy at first because it has so much personality.

That is exactly what makes it tricky.

The room invites you to lean into charm, mixed furniture, wall pieces, and little decorative details. Each item can look good on its own, but once too many of them start competing for attention, the room stops feeling cozy and starts feeling crowded.

That is why Cafe is not difficult in the same way as Penthouse or Dreams.

- **Penthouse** is hard because it is easy to lose balance.
- **Dreams** is hard because the room feels strange.
- **Cafe** is hard because it tempts you to add too much too early.

If you do not need a full walkthrough and only want help making the Cafe feel more balanced, this is the better place to start.

<GuideCallout title="What makes Cafe tricky">
Cafe is not hard because it lacks style.  
It is hard because it has enough style to make almost every extra item feel tempting.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-to-build-a-balanced-cafe-layout">
  How to build a balanced Cafe layout
</h2>

The best Cafe layouts usually feel layered, not crowded.

A good way to approach the room is to think in three layers:

### 1. Main layout layer
This is the room’s structure.

It should answer:
- where the eye goes first
- where the main furniture grouping sits
- which areas feel active
- which areas stay lighter

### 2. Support layer
This is where secondary pieces help the room feel complete.

These items should reinforce the room’s flow, not fight it.

### 3. Detail layer
This is where the Cafe gets its personality.

Small decorations, wall accents, and extra touches belong here — but only after the room already feels stable.

If you reverse that order, the Cafe usually starts feeling cluttered before it feels finished.

<GuideCallout title="Best layout principle">
The Cafe usually looks better when the room feels designed first and decorated second.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-to-place-first-in-the-cafe">
  What to place first in the Cafe
</h2>

The most useful thing you can do in the Cafe is place the items that define the room before you place the items that decorate it.

That usually means starting with:

- the biggest furniture pieces
- the most important seating areas
- the room’s main visual center
- any furniture that controls how crowded the room feels

Once those pieces are in place, it becomes much easier to tell:

- which walls still need support
- which corners can stay quieter
- where smaller details will actually help
- when the room already has enough visual interest

### What to delay until later

Try not to rush into:
- small wall accents
- filler decor
- extra detail on already busy areas
- “cute” pieces that do not yet support a clear zone

Those items work best when the room already has a layout worth decorating.

<GuideCallout title="Simple rule">
If you are not sure where something should go in the Cafe, it probably belongs later in the process — not earlier.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-to-keep-the-room-eclectic-without-making-it-messy">
  How to keep the room eclectic without making it messy
</h2>

The Cafe works because it can handle a more mixed, lived-in, slightly busier look than some of the other Hozy rooms.

But “eclectic” only works when the room still feels intentional.

### Ways to keep the Cafe controlled

- let one area carry most of the visual personality
- do not make every wall equally busy
- leave at least one quieter section so the decorated zones can stand out
- use small items to echo the room’s style instead of constantly introducing new focal points
- check whether a detail adds character or just adds noise

### What usually makes the Cafe feel messy

The room starts feeling messy when:

- too many small objects are added without a clear furniture base
- wall decor piles up before the main layout is settled
- every zone tries to be the room’s most interesting zone
- there is no visual rest anywhere

The Cafe should feel charming, not overloaded.

<GuideCallout title="Best restraint advice">
A good Cafe layout often comes from stopping one step earlier than you want to.  
Leave a little breathing room so the style feels intentional instead of accidental.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Is this a full Cafe walkthrough?

No. This page is a layout guide, not a full step-by-step walkthrough. It is meant for players who already understand the room’s basic flow and want help making the Cafe feel balanced instead of messy.

### Is Cafe one of the hardest levels in Hozy?

Not in the same way as Penthouse or Dreams, but it is one of the easiest levels to over-decorate and make visually messy.

### What is the biggest Cafe layout mistake?

Adding too many wall items and small decorative pieces before the main furniture layout is stable.

### Should the Cafe look cluttered?

A little bit of visual richness works well in the Cafe, but it still needs control. The goal is eclectic, not chaotic.

### What should I place first in the Cafe?

Start with the pieces that define the room’s structure and seating flow. Smaller decor should come later.

### How do I make the Cafe feel more balanced?

Leave some walls lighter, keep one area as the main statement zone, and avoid making every part of the room equally busy.
