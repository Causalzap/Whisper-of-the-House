<h2 id="how-many-endings">
  How many endings does Directive 8020 have?
</h2>

Directive 8020 has **10 main ending variants** in the ending cleanup route:

- Everyone Lives,
- Forever and Ever,
- Docked,
- Beacon,
- Docked / Not Alone,
- Homeward Bound / Massacre,
- Homeward Bound / Masks Off,
- Hitchhiker,
- Game Over,
- Bad Ending / Everyone Dies.

The ending you get depends on more than one final choice. Your route can change based on who is alive, whether the correct Destinies are unlocked, whether the alien / intruder status is clean, which final message you send, and how the final escape plays out.

<h2 id="best-ending">
  What is the best ending in Directive 8020?
</h2>

The best ending for most players is **Everyone Lives**.

The reason is simple: it gives you the cleanest survival result before you start deliberately breaking branches for Beacon, Docked / Not Alone, Homeward Bound, Hitchhiker, Game Over, or Everyone Dies.

To work toward the best ending, focus on these conditions:

- keep every active crew member alive,
- complete the important Destinies,
- verify duplicates instead of guessing,
- resolve intruder status cleanly,
- survive the final boss and reactor escape,
- avoid carrying the alien threat forward into the ending.

The two most important things many players miss are **Destinies** and the **final message split**. If you only focus on QTEs and who is alive, you may still miss the ending or post-credit variant you wanted.

<h2 id="destinies-system">
  Do Destinies affect the ending?
</h2>

Yes. Destinies are not just labels at the end of a route. They are character-state outcomes, and they can change whether your ending setup is clean enough for Everyone Lives or which post-credit interpretation you receive.

For players, the useful question is not only “which Destiny did I get?” It is:

**What behavior pushed the character there, and what ending does that state support?**

The two Destinies you should track first are:

**Stafford → The Father**  
Push Stafford toward protective, mentor-style, crew-first decisions. This is one of the most important best-ending route checks.

**Eisele → Humanist**  
Push Eisele toward humanity-first choices rather than project-continuation or pure research logic. This is the important split behind the Humanitarian / Scientist reading.

For Cooper, Young, and Cernan, track their Destinies for completion and route-state cleanup, but do not treat every one of them as a confirmed hard gate for the best ending unless your route test proves it.

<h2 id="everyone-lives-ending">
  Everyone Lives ending
</h2>

The Everyone Lives ending is the clean survival outcome.

It requires more than simply reaching the finale. You need to bring a healthy route into the final stretch: active crew alive, duplicate mistakes avoided, key Destinies handled, and the final escape completed cleanly.

**How to reach this ending:** keep the active crew alive, complete the important Destiny outcomes, clear or avoid dangerous intruder conditions, and survive the final boss / reactor escape.

**How to avoid losing this ending:** do not guess during duplicate checks, do not let unresolved intruder status carry forward, and do not treat the final escape as a cutscene.

**Closest Turning Point to branch from:** start from the final boss / reactor escape or the late intruder-status checkpoint if your only issue is the ending route. If someone died earlier, you need to rewind to that character’s danger scene instead.

<h2 id="forever-and-ever-ending">
  Forever and Ever ending
</h2>

The **Forever and Ever** scene is one of the most important endings for understanding Directive 8020’s structure.

Instead of feeling like a normal escape result, it leans into the game’s loop, clone, and cycle themes. The scene returns to a familiar wake-up setup and makes the player question how much of the mission is repeating.

**How to reach this ending:** follow a route that exposes the cycle / clone structure and pushes the story into its repeating wake-up interpretation.

**How to avoid it:** use Turning Points to move toward a cleaner survival route, a final-message variant, or a more direct fail-state ending.

**Closest Turning Point to branch from:** rewind to the late ending-state branch before the final interpretation locks in.

<h2 id="cycle-13-explained">
  What does Cycle 13 mean?
</h2>

Cycle 13 is the key to understanding why Directive 8020’s endings feel stranger than a normal survival-horror finale.

The game is not only asking whether this crew survives. It is asking what kind of experiment, clone route, or repeated mission state has been created around the Cassiopeia and the life form.

That is why endings like Forever and Ever and the two Eisele post-credit scenes matter. They show that survival is not only physical. The project is also trying to turn repeated failure into usable knowledge.

In simple terms:

**Cycle 13 means the ending is part of a larger repeated experiment, not just one isolated mission.**

That is why Eisele’s final interpretation matters so much. She can frame the project as a moral failure, a scientific necessity, or a cycle that still needs another attempt.

<h2 id="docked-ending">
  Docked ending
</h2>

The **Docked** ending is a ship-state ending.

Its main value is as a comparison point for **Docked / Not Alone**. Docked is the cleaner version. Docked / Not Alone is the version where the escape state is contaminated by unresolved alien / intruder danger.

**How to reach this ending:** reach the docked outcome without letting the final route carry an active intruder condition into the result.

**How to avoid it:** push the route toward Beacon, Homeward Bound, Hitchhiker, or a bad ending through final-message and intruder-state changes.

**Closest Turning Point to branch from:** rewind to the final escape / docked outcome branch and change intruder status.

<h2 id="beacon-ending">
  Beacon ending
</h2>

The **Beacon** ending is tied to the final-message split near the end of the game.

The important choice is the distress / **Save Our Souls**-style message route. This is the branch to test if you are searching for how to get the Beacon ending or how final communication changes the post-credit result.

**How to reach this ending:** choose the distress / Save Our Souls-style final message instead of the warning-style route.

**How to avoid it:** choose the warning-style final message route instead.

**Closest Turning Point to branch from:** rewind to the final Andromeda / message choice before the ending sequence locks in.

If you only want to compare Beacon against the warning route, do not replay the whole game. Branch from the final-message Turning Point and change that one decision first.

<h2 id="eisele-final-choice">
  Eisele final choice and post-credit split
</h2>

Eisele’s ending is mainly about whether she lands on a **Humanist / Humanitarian** reading or a **Scientist / continuation** reading.

Do not treat this as a separate third ending path. Use this section as the bridge between Destinies and the two post-credit variants below: if Eisele’s choices lean toward humanity, compassion, and ending the moral compromise of the project, expect the Humanitarian interpretation. If her route leans toward research, control, and continuing the cycle, expect the Scientist interpretation.

<h2 id="eisele-humanitarian">
  Eisele the Humanitarian post-credit scene
</h2>

**Eisele the Humanitarian** is the post-credit variant tied to Eisele’s Humanist direction.

This route frames the ending around moral cost, compassion, and whether humanity can survive without sacrificing the part of itself that makes it human.

**How to reach this scene:** push Eisele toward Humanist choices, then use the final-message route that supports the Humanitarian interpretation.

**How to avoid it:** push Eisele toward the Scientist / continuation interpretation or change the final-message branch.

**Closest Turning Point to branch from:** Episode 8 - final message split plus Eisele’s final Destiny state.

<h2 id="eisele-scientist">

</h2>

This route frames the project as something that may still be necessary, even after everything the crew has suffered. It is colder than the Humanitarian route because it treats the cycle as a tool that might eventually save humanity.

**How to reach this scene:** push Eisele away from the Humanist interpretation and toward research, control, and continuing the project.

**How to avoid it:** push Eisele toward Humanist choices and test the alternate final-message branch.

**Closest Turning Point to branch from:** Episode 8 - final message split plus Eisele’s final Destiny state.

**How to avoid it:** do not let the alien threat travel forward in a state where it can massacre the crew or destination.

**Closest Turning Point to branch from:** rewind to the late homeward escape / intruder-state branch.

This ending is useful for completion and death-route testing, especially if you are also tracking all death scenes.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How many endings are in Directive 8020?

Directive 8020 has 10 main ending variants: Everyone Lives, Forever and Ever, Docked, Beacon, Docked / Not Alone, Homeward Bound / Massacre, Homeward Bound / Masks Off, Hitchhiker, Game Over, and Bad Ending / Everyone Dies.

### What is the best ending in Directive 8020?

The best ending for most players is Everyone Lives, where the active crew survives the final boss, reactor escape, and ending sequence with the cleanest route state.

### How do I get the Beacon ending?

To get the Beacon ending, branch from the final-message choice near the end and choose the distress / Save Our Souls-style message route. If you choose the warning-style route instead, you should compare the alternate post-credit result.

### Does the Destinies system affect the ending?

Yes. Stafford’s The Father and Eisele’s Humanist are the two Destinies you should check first for best-ending and post-credit cleanup. The Father comes from protective, mentor-style Stafford choices, while Humanist comes from Eisele choices that prioritize humanity over research or project continuation.

### Do Cooper, Young, and Cernan Destinies affect the ending?

They are useful for route-state and completion tracking, but Stafford’s The Father and Eisele’s Humanist are the first Destinies to check if your best ending or post-credit result is wrong. Track Cooper, Young, and Cernan after those two.

### What is Cycle 13?

Cycle 13 refers to the wider repeated experiment / clone route structure behind the mission. It helps explain why Forever and Ever and the Eisele post-credit scenes feel like more than normal escape endings.

### What is the difference between Eisele the Humanitarian and Eisele the Scientist?

Eisele the Humanitarian focuses on the moral cost of survival and the meaning of humanity. Eisele the Scientist focuses more on research, cycles, the clone project, and the belief that another attempt may eventually succeed.

### What is the difference between Homeward Bound / Massacre and Homeward Bound / Masks Off?

Massacre focuses on the bloody consequence of the alien threat carrying forward. Masks Off focuses more on duplicate identity and reveal conditions within the Homeward Bound route.

### What is Docked / Not Alone?

Docked / Not Alone is a darker docked variant where the crew may have escaped one danger but has not truly escaped the alien threat.

### How do I get the Game Over ending?

Game Over is a fail-state ending tied to having no playable characters left. Chase it only after your survival route.

### Should I chase Everyone Dies first?

No. Save Everyone Lives for your first route, then chase Everyone Dies after you understand the death checks, duplicate scenes, and ending structure.

### Are endings different on PS5, Xbox, and PC?

The ending structure should be the same across platforms. Platform differences usually affect performance, controls, or patches, not which story endings exist.
