  How choices work in Directive 8020
</h2>

Directive 8020 choices are dangerous because many consequences are delayed.

The game may ask you to route power, use the scanner, open a door, handle a sample, shape a trait, or send a final message. The real consequence may not appear until several episodes later.

This guide focuses on:

- what to choose,
- what happens if you choose wrong,
- and which earlier branch to check when a later route collapses.

<h2 id="ep2-power-routing">
  Ep2 power routing: Fire Control System or Landing Computer?
</h2>

The Ep2 power-routing choice is one of the most important delayed-consequence branches.

If you cut the **Fire Control System**, the later Episode 5 **Ice and Fire** rescue route can put Mitchell in serious danger. Young may be pushed toward saving Anders instead.

If you cut the **Landing Computer**, the later pressure can shift the other way, making Anders the higher-risk side.

That means this is not just a technical choice. It is an early setup for a later character rescue problem.

If Anders or Mitchell dies in Episode 5, check this branch before replaying the same scene repeatedly.

<h2 id="ice-and-fire-choice">
  Ice and Fire choice: save Anders or Mitchell?
</h2>

Episode 5’s **Ice and Fire** scene is one of the clearest delayed-consequence choices in Directive 8020.

At the moment, it looks like Young has to choose between two immediate emergencies: Anders is dealing with the freezing / sample-side danger, while Mitchell is tied to the fuel pump / fire-side danger. The trap is that the result is not only decided by the choice you make in Episode 5.

Your earlier Episode 2 power-routing branch can change who is actually in more danger:

- If you cut the **Fire Control System** in Episode 2, Mitchell can become the higher-risk side in Ice and Fire.
- If you cut the **Landing Computer** in Episode 2, the danger can shift toward Anders.
- If Anders or Mitchell dies here, do not only replay Ice and Fire. Rewind to the Episode 2 power-routing branch first.

The simple cleanup rule is:

**Ice and Fire is not a standalone choice. It is the payoff for your earlier system-routing decision.**

If you are trying to save both characters, treat Episode 2 and Episode 5 as one connected route chain.

<h2 id="wake-williams-choice">
  Should you wake Williams?
</h2>

Yes. Wake and stabilize Williams.

Williams can make the route more dangerous later because he pushes research, control, and project logic. But leaving him under during an emergency is not the safer survival choice.

The right play is:

**save Williams, but do not obey every Williams-driven priority.**

That distinction matters in the sample argument and later research / contamination scenes.

- the search-party split, because Cernan / Williams and Anders / Eisele move onto different pressure routes;
- the power-cell route, because damaged ship traversal separates the crew and slows regrouping;
- the sample argument, because Williams pushes the group toward contamination risk;

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Do choices matter in Directive 8020?

Yes. Choices can affect survival, route state, traits, Destinies, duplicate outcomes, final messages, post-credit scenes, and endings.

### Should I cut Fire Control System or Landing Computer?

Track this choice carefully. Cutting Fire Control System can put Mitchell at higher risk in Episode 5’s Ice and Fire route, while cutting Landing Computer can shift risk toward Anders.

### Should I authorize the weapon?

For the safer survival route, refuse the weapon. Test weapon branches later during cleanup.

### Should I shoot or test in the two Eisele scene?

Choose Test. The left Eisele is fake, and the newcomer is real.

### Is the scanner important?

Yes. Scanner / Test logic is the safest way to avoid mimic mistakes.

### Can you stop Williams from taking the sample?

Not cleanly. Cooper can push back, but Williams still drives the group toward taking the sample.

### Do traits affect Destinies?

Yes. Supportive, philosophical, heroic, and humanist-style responses can push characters toward different Destiny outcomes.

### Does Survivor Mode allow Turning Point cleanup?

Do not rely on it during the run. Treat major choices as permanent until replay access is available.

### Does the final message change the ending?

Yes. Distress / Save Our Souls-style and warning-style routes can change ending interpretation and post-credit direction.

### Can Turning Points fix a bad choice?

Yes, but only if you rewind to the earlier mistake, not just the scene where someone dies.
