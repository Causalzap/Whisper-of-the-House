<h2 id="eisele-survival">
  How to keep Eisele alive
</h2>

**Eisele’s survival depends on verification and route state. Do not treat her danger scenes as simple chase scenes.**

Her biggest risks come from identity pressure, scanner logic, Stafford-related pressure, and Episode 6 survival routing.

If Eisele dies later, check these in order:

1. Did you use Test / scanner logic in duplicate scenes?
2. Did an earlier Stafford-related route put her in a bad state?
3. Did Episode 6 become harder because of an earlier route mistake?
4. Did the final escape carry an intruder condition forward?

<h2 id="cernan-survival">
  How to keep Cernan alive
</h2>

**Cernan’s survival is tied to the Williams route and Episode 6 pressure.**

If Cernan dies late, do not assume the final scene is the only problem. His route can be shaped earlier when he is paired with Williams and pushed through pressure scenes.

Check these first:

1. The Episode 3 search-party split.
2. The Cernan / Williams route state.
3. Episode 6 Detour.
4. Any identity or mimic pressure before the death.

<h2 id="stafford-survival">
  How to keep Stafford alive
</h2>

**Stafford is safest when command stays practical instead of prideful.**

His highest-risk moments are not always monster attacks. They often happen when ship-system pressure, mission pride, and crew safety collide.

Check these first:

1. Episode 3 landing / crash route.
2. Landing computer and ship stabilization choices.
3. Command decisions where Stafford can prioritize the mission over people.
4. Late duplicate or intruder pressure.

Stafford’s survival is important for more than one route. He is tied to command stability, character survival, and some ending-state cleanup.

<h2 id="mitchell-survival">
  How to keep Mitchell alive
</h2>

**Mitchell’s danger scenes usually combine a system problem with a rescue problem.**

This is why players lose him: they solve only one side.

If Mitchell is in danger, ask two questions:

1. What ship system is failing?
2. Who is physically at risk while that system is failing?

Mitchell is most vulnerable around landing, fire, oxygen, and other system-pressure scenes.

To keep him alive, do not tunnel-vision on either the engineering task or the rescue. You need both.

<h2 id="williams-survival">
  How to keep Williams alive
</h2>

**Williams is the unusual case: you need him alive, but his priorities can make the route more dangerous.**

He is not just another person to rescue. Williams pushes the crew toward control, samples, research, and project logic. That means saving him does not mean obeying him.

His most important danger and influence points are:

1. Episode 3 wake-up and stabilization.
2. The sample argument.
3. Cernan / Williams route pressure.
4. Late conspiracy and Williams 2.0 material.

<h2 id="anders-survival">
  How to keep Anders alive
</h2>

**Anders is safest when science stays behind containment.**

Her danger scenes are tied to alien biology, sample collection, contamination risk, and science-team pressure.

Check these first:

1. Episode 3 sample route.
2. Alien growth scenes.
3. Episode 5 science / Drugs routes.
4. Any scene where research is prioritized over crew safety.

Anders is not usually saved by rushing toward more information. She is saved by limiting exposure and keeping the crew from treating the alien life form like a harmless discovery.

<h2 id="cooper-survival">
  How to keep Cooper alive
</h2>

**Cooper is the stabilizer. If Cooper’s route goes wrong, medical, rescue, and search scenes become harder to control.**

Cooper’s highest-risk value is not only her own life. It is what she helps prevent: panic, bad medical handling, and poor group coordination.

Check these first:

1. Williams wake-up and stabilization.
2. Medical pressure after the crash.
3. Search support during Episode 3.
4. Scenes where the group wants to split or rush.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Can everyone survive in Directive 8020?

Yes. The active crew can survive through the finale and reach the clean survival route.

### Can you save Carter?

No. Carter is part of the opening mystery and is not a normal active-crew survival target.

### Can you save Sims?

No, not like a normal crew member. Sims is tied to the sleep crew mystery, recordings, and mimic setup.

### Who is hardest to keep alive?

Eisele and Cernan are among the easiest to lose because later survival scenes can depend on earlier route states.

### How do I keep Eisele alive?

Use Test / scanner logic in duplicate scenes, avoid bad Stafford-related route states, and check Episode 6 pressure if she dies later.

### How do I keep Cernan alive?

Check the Cernan / Williams route and Episode 6 Detour before replaying only the final death scene.

### How do I keep Mitchell alive?

Treat his danger scenes as both system problems and rescue problems. Solving only one side can get him killed.

### Should I keep Williams alive?

Yes, but do not let Williams’ ambition override containment or crew safety.

### Why did I get a bad ending even though some characters survived?

A character can survive physically while the route still carries an intruder, duplicate, or contaminated escape condition.

### What should I do after saving everyone?

Use Turning Points for endings, monster scenes, and death-route cleanup.
