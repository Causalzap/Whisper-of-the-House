<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**To get the Two Queens Ending in Magical Princess, reach Loop 2 / your first NG+ or later, make Queen Cornelia your main route target, build her affinity through outings and Queen events, avoid committed companion partner endings, accept Cornelia's final-day invitation, and win the Red Moon Eve battle.**

Use two progress checks while building the route: **Royal Rendezvous** means you have gone on 5 outings with Cornelia, while **Side-by-Side with Her Majesty** means Cornelia's affinity is maxed. Two Queens gives you the **Inheritance Fragment**.

After Two Queens, the earliest practical Heir of the Stargazer attempt is **Loop 3 / your second NG+**, but only after you confirm the Inheritance Fragment in the next carry-over state.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

---

<GuideCallout title="Make a save before final-day route choices">
If Cornelia's final invitation appears, save before accepting it. If a companion confession, special date, or final partner prompt appears instead, save before answering. Two Queens can be blocked by another route taking priority even when Cornelia's affinity is high.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="two-queens-route-overview">
  Two Queens Route Overview
</h2>

Two Queens is Cornelia's NG+ secret ending. It is not a first-run career ending, and it is not the same thing as a normal companion partner ending.

<GuideTable
  caption="Two Queens route steps in Magical Princess."
  columns={routeColumns}
  rows={routeRows}
/>

The route has three checks: **Cornelia affinity**, **route priority**, and **Red Moon Eve victory**. If any one of those fails, Two Queens can fail.

---

<h2 id="loop-order">
  Loop Order: Two Queens, Inheritance Fragment, and Heir of the Stargazer
</h2>

Two Queens is the Cornelia half of the true-ending route, but it is not the final true ending by itself.

<GuideTable
  caption="Recommended loop order for Two Queens and Heir of the Stargazer."
  columns={loopColumns}
  rows={loopRows}
/>

The important rule is: **do Two Queens first, then check the Inheritance Fragment in the next loop before committing to Heir of the Stargazer.**

---

<h2 id="cornelia-route-checklist">
  Cornelia Route Checklist
</h2>

Cornelia is the most important part of this route. If her route state is not ready, the final-day invitation will not appear.

<GuideTable
  caption="Cornelia route checklist for Two Queens."
  columns={corneliaChecklistColumns}
  rows={corneliaChecklistRows}
/>

The two practical checkpoints are:

- **Royal Rendezvous:** 5 outings with Cornelia.
- **Side-by-Side with Her Majesty:** Cornelia affinity maxed.

Do not stop at 5 outings if you have not maxed her affinity yet. Royal Rendezvous is a useful milestone, but **Side-by-Side with Her Majesty** is the safer route target.

<h2 id="two-queens-vs-cornelia-partner-ending">
  Two Queens vs Cornelia Partner Ending
</h2>

This is the route confusion most players should watch for.

<GuideTable
  caption="How Two Queens differs from normal partner endings and other secret endings."
  columns={endingDifferenceColumns}
  rows={endingDifferenceRows}
/>

The simple answer: **Two Queens is Cornelia's secret Queen-route ending, not a normal partner ending.** Raising Cornelia affinity is required, but the route payoff is the secret ending that gives the Inheritance Fragment.

If another companion route takes priority, you can miss Two Queens even with strong Cornelia progress.

---

<h2 id="avoid-partner-route-lock">
  Avoid Partner Route Lock
</h2>

The biggest way to ruin a Two Queens run is to let a normal partner route take priority.

<GuideTable
  caption="Possible partner-lock signals to watch for before the Two Queens final day."
  columns={partnerLockColumns}
  rows={partnerLockRows}
/>

Do not treat every friendship scene as dangerous. The danger is commitment. If the game starts asking for final partner-style choices, stop and save before answering.

---

<h2 id="final-day-invitation">
  Cornelia's Final-Day Invitation
</h2>

The final-day invitation is the real route check.

If your Cornelia route state is ready, Cornelia invites Alice into the Two Queens ending sequence. Accepting that invitation moves you toward Red Moon Eve.

If the invitation does not appear, one of these is usually true:

1. Cornelia affinity is not high enough.
2. You stopped after the 5-outing milestone but did not max affinity.
3. A companion partner route has taken priority.
4. You are trying to force the route before the NG+ secret ending state is available.

Do not try to fix this at the last second. Reload from before the final stretch and check **Cornelia affinity → partner route lock → final invitation** in that order.

<h2 id="red-moon-eve-battle">
  Red Moon Eve Battle
</h2>

After accepting Cornelia's invitation, the route moves into the **Red Moon Eve** battle.

This is not the same as every normal Red Moon / Crimson Moon city event. Think of it as the Two Queens route's final combat gate. You can do the social side correctly and still fail if Alice cannot win the fight.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to prepare for the Red Moon Eve battle."
  columns={battleColumns}
  rows={battleRows}
/>

Late-route battles may include allies or route support, but do not rely on that to carry a weak file. If normal Red Moon / Crimson Moon fights feel risky, train more before committing to Cornelia's final invitation.

<h2 id="why-two-queens-is-not-unlocking">
  Why Two Queens Is Not Unlocking
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Common problems that block Two Queens."
  columns={troubleshootingColumns}
  rows={troubleshootingRows}
/>

Most failed Two Queens attempts come from one of three mistakes:

1. Cornelia route state is too low.

Check the route in this order: **Cornelia affinity → partner route lock → final invitation → Red Moon Eve battle → Inheritance Fragment.**

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Is Two Queens required for the true ending?

Yes. Two Queens gives the Inheritance Fragment, which is required for the Heir of the Stargazer true ending route.

### Can I get Two Queens on the first playthrough?

No. Two Queens is a NG+ secret ending. The earliest practical attempt is **Loop 2 / your first NG+**.

### What loop is Heir of the Stargazer?

If you get Two Queens in Loop 2 and confirm the Inheritance Fragment in the next carry-over state, the earliest practical Heir of the Stargazer attempt is **Loop 3 / your second NG+**.

### How do I raise Cornelia affinity?

Choose Cornelia outings, Queen visits, and Cornelia-related route events whenever they appear. Use Royal Rendezvous and Side-by-Side with Her Majesty as your progress checks.

### How many Cornelia outings do I need?

Five Cornelia outings are the Royal Rendezvous milestone. For Two Queens, keep going until Cornelia affinity is maxed and Side-by-Side with Her Majesty is secured.

### Is Two Queens the same as a Cornelia partner ending?

No. Two Queens is Cornelia's secret Queen-route ending. It uses Cornelia affinity, but it is not the same as a normal companion partner ending.

### Why did Cornelia's final invitation not appear even though her affinity was high?

Another route may have taken priority, or Cornelia may not be fully maxed. Check whether you accepted a confession, special date, romance activation, or final partner prompt from another companion.

### Can I date companions while going for Two Queens?

You can interact with companions, but do not accept confession, special date, or final partner commitment prompts if your target is Two Queens.

### What is Red Moon Eve?

Red Moon Eve is the final battle sequence after Cornelia's final-day invitation. Winning it completes Two Queens and gives the Inheritance Fragment.

### Does the Inheritance Fragment carry over to NG+?

After completing Two Queens, check your NG+ carry-over / inherited items screen before committing the next run. The Fragment is the key item for Heir of the Stargazer, so confirm it before starting the Library / Labyrinth half.

### Is Two Queens the same as Golden Ending?

No. Two Queens is Cornelia's secret route and leads toward Heir of the Stargazer. Golden Ending is tied to Cornet's necklace, Forbidden Research, and the Labyrinth special route.
