<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Burglin' Gnomes**, survival is about stopping panic from spreading. Drop heavy items before rescues, send one rescuer with one lookout, avoid fighting rats or roaches without the right tool, watch for Roomba and floor hazards, and stop starting new tasks when the tornado warning appears.

The old man is the main threat, but he becomes much more dangerous if he gets a gun. Try to take the gun before he does; if he already has it, stun him first before stealing it back. Evil gnomes can take over after the old man is killed, so do not kill him unless your team is ready to close routes, regroup, and extract.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="burglin-gnomes-survival-enemies-guide">
  Burglin' Gnomes survival and enemies guide
</h2>

**Burglin' Gnomes** is dangerous because most deaths do not happen from one big mistake. They happen when one mistake creates another: a player gets grabbed, two teammates rush the same room, someone keeps carrying a heavy item, then the tornado warning appears.

The survival rule is simple:

<strong>Drop the item, call the danger, rescue only when the route is safe, and stop adding new problems.</strong>

This guide covers the threats that ruin runs: the old man, old man with gun, evil gnomes, rats, roaches, Roomba, bees, sharp floor hazards, fall damage, traps, rescue mistakes, and bad rooms.

For task priority, read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/tasks-guide">Tasks Guide</Link>. For role splitting and callouts, read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/co-op-guide">Co-op Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="copy-paste-survival-card">
  Copy-paste survival card
</h2>

Use this in Discord, Steam chat, or your group message before starting a run.

```txt
BURGLIN' GNOMES SURVIVAL CARD

OLD MAN:
- Drop heavy items.
- Call the room.
- One rescuer, one lookout.
- Do not crowd the same trap room.

GUN:
- Take it before the old man gets it.
- If old man has gun, stun first, then steal.
- Do not carry gun through crowded rescue routes.

EVIL GNOME:
- Can take over after old man is killed.
- Close unused doors/windows before major chaos.
- Regroup and prepare to extract.

RESCUE:
- Empty hands before untie or CPR.
- Clear danger before revive.
- Skip far rescues during tornado.

FLOOR / FALLS:
- Watch for knives, forks, soap, traps, and clutter.
- Do not sprint through messy kitchens or bathrooms.
- Do not jump from medium heights without a safe landing.

TORNADO:
- No new tasks.
- Take only close or staged loot.
- Leave before one rescue becomes a full-team wipe.
```

This card is built for quick decisions. If the team can follow this, most wipes become recoverable.

Do not turn every old man sighting into a chase. Sometimes the correct play is to drop the item, hide, and resume after he leaves.

<h2 id="old-man-gun">
  Old man with gun: steal it before he does
</h2>

The gun route deserves special attention because it changes the old man from a chase threat into a ranged threat. If your team finds the gun first, taking it away from his route can prevent a much worse mid-run situation.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to handle the gun before or after the old man gets it."
  columns={gunColumns}
  rows={gunRows}
/>

The safest rule is:

<strong>If the old man gets the gun first, do not rush him directly. Stun him first, then steal it back.</strong>

Gun locations should be checked early, but do not build your whole route around one fixed spawn unless your current run confirms it. Drawers, bathroom storage, cabinets, weapon tables, and nearby surfaces are worth checking, but the task route still comes first.

  <li>watch its path like a moving trap,</li>

<h2 id="bees-outdoor-danger">
  Bees and outdoor danger
</h2>

Bees are easy to ignore until they ruin Jonathan, shed key, garden, or outdoor material routes. Outdoor danger is especially bad because the team is usually split between inside tasks and outside objectives.

For bees:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>slow down near outdoor routes,</li>
  <li>do not sprint through hive areas,</li>
  <li>call bees before the whole team enters the area,</li>
  <li>let a teammate help if bees attach,</li>
  <li>and do not combine bees with a far rescue or tornado escape.</li>
</ul>

If Jonathan or shed key tasks send you outside, send one scout first. The whole team should not move outside until the route is confirmed.

  Floor hazards, fall damage, and dismemberment

Not every danger is an enemy. Some of the worst deaths happen because players sprint over clutter, step on sharp objects, slip on soap, jump from unsafe heights, or pull heavy objects in a bad position.

  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

The risky objects include:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>knives and forks on the floor,</li>
  <li>soap or slippery objects,</li>
  <li>sharp tools near kitchen routes,</li>
  <li>heavy objects that can pull or twist your body,</li>
  <li>medium-height drops from shelves, counters, roofs, or furniture,</li>
  <li>and cluttered routes where you cannot see what your feet are touching.</li>

<h2 id="faq">
  Burglin' Gnomes survival and enemies FAQ
</h2>

### What is the most dangerous enemy in Burglin' Gnomes?

The old man is usually the main survival check because he can grab, carry, tie up, trap, and block routes. He becomes even more dangerous if he gets a gun.

### What should I do if the old man gets the gun?

Do not rush him directly. Use a stun option first, such as a taser, then steal the gun back when he is disabled or safely interrupted.

### What triggers evil gnomes?

Evil gnome pressure can begin after the old man is killed. Do not kill him casually; finish key tasks first, close unused routes, and prepare to extract.

### How do I rescue someone from the fridge?

Send one rescuer with empty hands while another player watches the old man's route. Untie the teammate, then leave the room immediately.

### When should I skip a rescue?

Skip a rescue if the tornado is active, the teammate is far away, the route is blocked, or saving them would likely turn one lost player into a full-team wipe.

### Are rats and roaches worth fighting?

Only if the task requires it or they block the route. Do not chase them into worse rooms, and do not fight while carrying heavy loot.

### Is Roomba dangerous?

Yes. Treat Roomba like a moving floor hazard. Watch its path, avoid dragging heavy items across it blindly, and do not sprint into it while looking backward.

### Why did I die without fighting anything?

You may have hit floor clutter, sharp objects, soap, fall damage, traps, or awkward item physics. Look down before sprinting through kitchens, bathrooms, and carry routes.

### Should I use guns, knives, or explosives?

Use them only for a clear job. Dangerous tools become a liability during climbing, rescue, crowded rooms, or tornado extraction.
