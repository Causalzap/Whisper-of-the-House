<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Burglin' Gnomes**, start materials with the two easiest early routes: **Plasto** and **Clonk**. Plasto comes from plastic-looking objects, with the bathroom, kids' room, and bedroom as the best first rooms to search. Clonk comes from metal items, and the kitchen route is usually the best place to start. For gather tasks, move or throw the items outside until the task updates.

For upgrades, buy based on your failure point. **Movement comes first** if you die before extraction. **Stealth** comes next if the house wakes up too early. **Rescue tools** matter when teammates keep getting tied or downed. **Carry capacity** is strong only after your team already extracts consistently and leaves materials behind.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="burglin-gnomes-materials-upgrades-guide">
  Burglin' Gnomes materials and upgrades guide
</h2>

**Burglin' Gnomes** progression is not just about stealing more. It is about stealing the right materials, getting them outside safely, and spending them on upgrades that fix the reason your runs fail.

The safest rule is:

<strong>Farm the material your next useful upgrade actually needs. Do not grab random objects and hope they solve the problem.</strong>

This guide focuses on material routes and upgrade priority. For task routing, read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/tasks-guide">Tasks Guide</Link>. For co-op role splitting and hauling, read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/co-op-guide">Co-op Guide</Link>.

```txt
UNCERTAIN MATERIALS:

UPGRADE ORDER:
- Movement if dying before extraction.

- Special gear only after the route is stable.
````

This template keeps material runs focused. The goal is not to touch every item in the house; the goal is to leave with the material your next upgrade needs.

<h2 id="uncertain-materials">
  Fraggles, Grabble, Scrapling, Marble: test carefully
</h2>

Not every material has the same clean early route as Plasto or Clonk. When the exact object mapping is uncertain, treat the material as a test route, not a first objective.

Use this rule:

<strong>Only test uncertain materials when the likely object is already close to your route.</strong>

Do not spend the final minute checking random shelves for Grabble or Scrapling when a confirmed task is still available. If the task list or Stockpile gives item images or wording in your current run, follow that over any generic route.

<h2 id="upgrade-priority">
  Best upgrade priority
</h2>

Some guides recommend **movement and carry weight first**. That advice makes sense for teams that already extract consistently and want better economy. This guide separates the two because new players often die before carry capacity matters.

<strong>Carry capacity feels tempting early, but it only matters after you already extract. Movement problems kill runs before carry problems do.</strong>

<GuideTable
caption="Burglin' Gnomes upgrade priority."
columns={upgradePriorityColumns}
rows={upgradePriorityRows}
/>

If your team is already surviving but leaving half the room behind, move carry capacity up. If your team keeps dying before the tornado, keep movement, stealth, and rescue above carry.

<h2 id="faq">
  Burglin' Gnomes materials and upgrades FAQ
</h2>

### How do I get Plasto in Burglin' Gnomes?

Start in the bathroom, kids' room, or bedroom. Look for plastic toys, toothpaste, cream tubs, nail polish, DVD covers, sink items, cabinet items, and objects under beds or on tables. Move or throw the items outside until the task updates.

### How do I gather Clonk?

Look for metal items, especially in the kitchen. Cans, toaster, kettle, cookware, tools, and other metal objects are good first tests. Move the metal items outside to count them.

### Is Plasto always in the same room?

No. Bathroom, kids' room, and bedroom are strong first places to search, but follow your current task list and item feedback.

### Is Clonk the same as Clunk?

No. Use **Clonk**. Wrong spellings make material notes harder to match with the Stockpile.

### Is Scrapling the same as Scraggle?

No. Use **Scrapling**. If your notes say Scraggle, update them to avoid confusion.

### Should I buy carry capacity first?

Buy carry capacity early only if you already extract consistently but leave loot or materials behind. If you die before extraction, movement, stealth, or rescue upgrades usually matter more.

### Why do some guides say movement and carry first?

That advice is fine for stable teams. For beginners, follow the upgrade priority section: movement fixes failed extractions first, and carry capacity moves up once you already leave safely.

### How do I know exact crafting costs?

Open the crafting menu and check the item or upgrade directly. Do not rely on memory because costs can vary by item, level, or update.

### Should I craft combat gear early?

Usually no. Early offensive gear often costs materials that could have improved movement, stealth, rescue, or carrying. Craft combat gear after the team can already extract safely.
