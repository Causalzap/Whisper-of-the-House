Your first goal is not perfect loot. Your first goal is to stop failing basic runs.

<h2 id="task-priority">
  Tasks: use LOW / MED / HIGH instead of random stealing
</h2>

If the task list looks overwhelming, do not complete it from top to bottom. Sort objectives into LOW, MED, and HIGH risk first.

LOW tasks are fast checkmarks such as opening windows, breaking small items, flushing small items, or stealing from a nearby confirmed room. MED tasks need a route, carry plan, or room check. HIGH tasks involve enemies, locked areas, tools, outdoor routes, or uncertain material testing.

The focused <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/tasks-guide">Tasks Guide</Link> covers exact routes for Break TV, flush item, frying pan, pot, metal can, Jonathan, rat, roach, shed key, firework, material tasks, task combinations, and Gnome Report review.

<h2 id="survival-enemies">
  Survival: old man, gun, evil gnomes, Roomba, and floor hazards
</h2>

Survival in Burglin' Gnomes is not only about avoiding the old man. The old man can grab, carry, tie, trap, and block routes. If he gets a gun, the run becomes much more dangerous. Evil gnomes can punish teams that trigger major chaos too early. Roomba, bees, floor clutter, fall damage, sharp tools, traps, and bad rooms can also ruin a clean route.

<h2 id="co-op-roles">
  Co-op: solo to 6 players, roles, rescue, and loot staging
</h2>

Burglin' Gnomes supports solo practice and online co-op. Solo is useful for learning maps, object handling, room names, and basic danger patterns. Co-op becomes stronger when players stop doing the same job at the same time.

A clean team usually needs:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Scout:</strong> checks routes, rooms, hazards, and task items.</li>
  <li><strong>Hauler:</strong> moves pots, frying pans, cans, materials, and heavy loot.</li>
  <li><strong>Distractor:</strong> pulls pressure only when the team needs an opening.</li>
  <li><strong>Anchor:</strong> watches the exit, stages loot, and handles rescue timing.</li>
</ul>

The <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/co-op-guide">Co-op Guide</Link> covers solo to 6-player setups, role lines, voice callouts, loot staging, rescue chains, movement tricks, and tornado extraction calls.

<h2 id="faq">
  Burglin' Gnomes guide FAQ
</h2>

### What guide should I read first?

Read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/beginner-guide">Beginner Guide</Link> first if you are new. It covers controls, crown target, safe entry, rescue basics, upgrades, and tornado extraction.

### How many tasks do I need to complete?

Use the Gnome Report crown target as your real pass target. Beginners can often plan around roughly three completed tasks, but do not rely on a fixed number if your current run shows a different target.

### What should I do if the task list looks too hard?

Read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/tasks-guide">Tasks Guide</Link>. Sort tasks into LOW, MED, and HIGH risk, finish safe checkmarks first, and avoid stacking multiple high-risk objectives so you reach the crown target with less risk.

### How do I survive the old man?

Drop heavy items, call your room, break line of sight, and avoid crowding the same rescue room. If the old man gets a gun or the run triggers evil gnome pressure, read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/survival-enemies-guide">Survival and Enemies Guide</Link>.

### Can I play Burglin' Gnomes solo?

Yes. Solo is useful for learning maps, movement, room names, object handling, and basic danger patterns. Co-op is stronger for rescue, hauling, callouts, and loot staging.

### How many players can play co-op?

A full group can scale up to a 6-player setup. The <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/co-op-guide">Co-op Guide</Link> covers solo, 2-player, 3-player, 4-player, 5-player, and 6-player role setups.

### How do I get Plasto?

Start with plastic-heavy rooms such as bathroom, kids' room, and bedroom. Look for plastic-looking objects and move or throw them outside until the task updates. The full route is in the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/materials-upgrades-guide">Materials and Upgrades Guide</Link>.

### How do I get Clonk?

Start with metal-heavy kitchen routes. Look for cans, toaster, kettle, cookware, tools, and other metal objects, then move them outside. Use **Clonk**, not Clunk, when writing notes.

### What upgrade should I buy first?

Buy based on your failure point. Movement comes first if you die before extraction. Stealth helps if the house wakes up too early. Rescue tools help if teammates keep getting trapped. Carry capacity becomes stronger after your team already extracts safely.

### Should I use this page as a wiki?

No. This hub is a route finder. Use it to choose the right focused guide, then read the spoke page that matches your current problem.
