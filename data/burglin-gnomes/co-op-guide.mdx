<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Burglin' Gnomes**, the best co-op setup is not everyone grabbing random loot. Use simple roles: a **scout** to find tasks and safe routes, a **hauler** to move heavy items, a **distractor** to pull pressure when needed, and an **anchor** to guard the exit, stage loot, and rescue teammates.

The game supports solo play and online co-op, with a full group able to include you and up to five friends. Solo is best for learning maps and object handling, while co-op is better for rescue, carrying, lookout duty, and controlled chaos.

<h2 id="burglin-gnomes-co-op-guide">
  Burglin' Gnomes co-op guide
</h2>

**Burglin' Gnomes** is funniest when everyone is panicking, but it is much easier to win when the team has a loose plan. You do not need perfect strategy. You just need fewer players doing the same mistake at the same time.

The best co-op rule is simple:

<strong>One player finds the route, one player moves the loot, one player handles pressure, and one player keeps the run recoverable.</strong>

If your team keeps failing because everyone gets caught in the kitchen, stuck in the fridge, or trapped after the tornado warning, this guide gives you a cleaner way to split jobs.

If you are still learning basic objectives, read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/tasks-guide">Burglin' Gnomes Tasks Guide</Link>. If enemies are the main problem, read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/survival-enemies-guide">Survival and Enemies Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="solo-vs-coop">
  Solo vs co-op: what each mode is good for
</h2>

Solo is useful, but it teaches a different skill set from co-op. Use solo runs to learn the house, object weight, window routes, room names, enemy behavior, and how long tasks take.

Solo is not the best place to learn rescue timing because no teammate can untie, revive, distract, or carry the second half of a route for you.

A simple way to think about it:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Solo:</strong> best for learning maps, movement, task wording, and object handling.</li>
  <li><strong>2-player:</strong> best for careful runs where one player handles route and the other handles rescue.</li>
  <li><strong>4-player:</strong> best for the clean scout / hauler / distractor / anchor setup.</li>
  <li><strong>5–6 players:</strong> best for chaos, split routes, and faster carrying, but only if voice calls stay short.</li>
</ul>

If you are playing solo, combine scout, hauler, and anchor decisions. That means you should move slower, stage fewer items, and leave earlier.

<h2 id="hauler-route">
  Hauler route: move heavy items in stages
</h2>

Carry objectives are where co-op helps the most. Pots, frying pans, metal cans, glass jars, books, tools, and kitchen items are much safer when one player carries and another player opens the path.

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>

A good hauler route looks like this:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Scout finds the item and the nearest exit route.</li>
  <li>Hauler grabs the item.</li>
  <li>Anchor opens the window, door, or path ahead.</li>
  <li>Distractor pulls pressure only if the old man enters the route.</li>
  <li>Hauler drops the item near a safe staging point.</li>
  <li>The team extracts it later when the route is clear.</li>
</ol>

Do not carry a heavy item during a rescue. Drop it first.

<h2 id="loot-staging">
  Loot staging and extraction piles
</h2>

Loot staging means you do not carry everything directly from the room to the tornado. Instead, you create small piles near safe windows, porches, tables, or exit routes.

<GuideTable
caption="How to stage loot in Burglin' Gnomes co-op."

The best staging points are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>near an open window,</li>
  <li>on a porch or outside step,</li>
  <li>beside a room you already cleared,</li>
  <li>near the anchor player,</li>
  <li>or on the route everyone will use when the tornado appears.</li>
</ul>

If the old man is active, do not keep dragging the item. Drop it, hide, and come back later.

  CPR and chain rescue
</h2>

A downed gnome can still be brought back if the team reacts fast. This is where co-op becomes more than just grabbing items together.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>

A clean chain rescue works like this:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Recover the closest teammate first.</li>
  <li>The revived player moves away from the danger.</li>
  <li>That player helps with the next rescue.</li>
  <li>The anchor keeps the exit route open.</li>
  <li>The team decides whether to continue or extract.</li>
</ol>

If two players are down and the tornado warning is active, do not automatically chase both bodies. Save only what is reachable without losing everyone else.

  Use a clear tornado extraction call
</h2>

The tornado warning should trigger one team rule: stop starting new tasks.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

When the warning appears:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Anchor calls the exit.</li>
  <li>Hauler grabs only staged or close loot.</li>
  <li>Scout stops exploring.</li>
  <li>Distractor only distracts if it protects the exit route.</li>
  <li>Everyone moves to the tornado.</li>
</ol>

Do not start a new rat task, TV task, bathroom task, or material test after the warning unless it is directly beside the exit.

The key is not to make the run complicated. The key is to make sure every phase has one job: enter safely, get early progress, stage items, rescue cleanly, and extract when the warning appears.

<h2 id="co-op-mistakes">
  Co-op mistakes to avoid
</h2>

Avoid these habits if your squad keeps wiping:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Everyone plays hauler.</strong> Six players carrying random objects means nobody is scouting, rescuing, or watching the exit.</li>
  <li><strong>No one reads the task list.</strong> The team wastes time on items that do not count.</li>
  <li><strong>Distraction happens too early.</strong> Noise before progress just makes the house harder.</li>
  <li><strong>Rescue turns into a crowd.</strong> One rescuer and one lookout is better than everyone rushing one fridge room.</li>
  <li><strong>Loot is never staged.</strong> Dragging every item directly to extraction wastes time and exposes the team.</li>
  <li><strong>No one calls extraction.</strong> The tornado appears and everyone does something different.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">
  Burglin' Gnomes co-op FAQ
</h2>

### How many players can play Burglin' Gnomes together?

The Steam page says you can enter houses with up to five friends, so a full online co-op group can have six players total.

### Is Burglin' Gnomes worth playing solo?

Yes. Solo is useful for learning maps, object handling, room names, task wording, and danger patterns. Co-op is better for rescue, carrying, lookout duty, and chaotic task recovery.

### Can we change roles mid-run?

Yes. Roles are jobs, not permanent classes. If the scout gets tied up, the anchor may become the rescuer, and a hauler may need to call the route.

### What should a 5-player or 6-player team do differently?

Add a second hauler first. In a 6-player group, add a second scout only if voice chat stays clear. Do not add more distractors unless the team already has clean callouts.

### What happens if the whole team wipes?

If everyone goes down, the run is usually unrecoverable. That is why the anchor role matters: one safer player can stop a bad capture from becoming a full-team wipe.

### Who should be the anchor?

Give anchor to the most patient player, not the loudest player. The anchor watches the exit, staged loot, and rescue route instead of chasing every funny object.

### Should beginners play distractor?

Only if they can escape after pulling pressure. A bad distractor brings the old man into the task room and makes the run harder.

### How should we handle voice chat with six players?

Use short callouts. Say the room, danger, and action: “Old man kitchen, drop loot,” or “Tornado, no new tasks.” Long explanations during panic make rescue harder.

### What should we do if two people are down at once?

Save the closest recoverable player first, then use that revived player to help chain the next rescue. Do not split into multiple unsafe rescues.
