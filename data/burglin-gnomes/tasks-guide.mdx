<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Burglin' Gnomes**, sort tasks into three tiers before you move: **LOW** tasks are fast checkmarks like opening windows, breaking small items, or flushing objects; **MED** tasks include room steals, carry objectives, and Jonathan-style routes; **HIGH** tasks include enemies, locked areas, tool routes, and uncertain material tasks.

Do LOW tasks first, combine MED tasks when they share a room or route, and attempt only one HIGH task at a time. If the tornado warning appears, stop starting new objectives and extract with whatever is already finished or staged.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="burglin-gnomes-tasks-guide">
  Burglin' Gnomes tasks guide
</h2>

**Burglin' Gnomes** tasks are not equal. Some tasks give fast progress with low risk. Some need a room route or carry plan. Some should wait until the team has tools, time, and a safe exit.

The best task strategy is:

<strong>Do LOW tasks first, combine MED tasks when possible, and never stack HIGH tasks before the route is stable.</strong>

This guide focuses on task priority and practical completion routes. For first-run controls and basic survival, read the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/beginner-guide">Beginner Guide</Link>. For enemy handling and rescue, use the <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/survival-enemies-guide">Survival and Enemies Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="task-tier-framework">
  Task tier framework: LOW, MED, and HIGH
</h2>

Use this tier system before the run starts. It gives the team a simple way to decide what to do first.

<GuideTable
  caption="Burglin' Gnomes task priority tiers."
  columns={tierColumns}
  rows={tierRows}
/>

Do not confuse **task tier** with **execution order**. Tier means risk level. Execution order means what your team does during the run. A room steal can be LOW if it is beside your entry route, but MED if it forces the team across the house.

This table is the task **risk tier**. The execution order below tells you how to move through a run after you know which tasks are LOW, MED, or HIGH.

<h2 id="copy-paste-task-template">
  Copy-paste task priority template
</h2>

Use this in Discord, Steam chat, or your group message before the run.

```txt
BURGLIN' GNOMES TASK PRIORITY

LOW:
- Open window
- Break small item
- Flush small item
- Nearby room steal

MED:
- Carry pot / frying pan / metal can
- Room-specific steal
- Jonathan / outdoor route
- Confirmed material task

HIGH:
- Rat / roach combat
- Locked area / shed key / firework
- Tool-required task
- Unconfirmed material test

RULES:
- Do 2 LOW tasks before chasing HIGH tasks.
- Combine MED tasks if they share the same room or exit.
- Attempt only 1 HIGH task at a time.
- Drop heavy items before rescue.
- When tornado appears, no new tasks.
```

This template is intentionally short. During a run, players need a decision tool, not a lecture.

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">

  <li>Leave immediately after the checkmark.</li>
</ol>

Bathrooms can become trap rooms. If the old man enters, drop the item and escape.

<h2 id="carry-objectives">
  Pot, frying pan, metal can, and heavy carry tasks
</h2>

Carry tasks are MED because they are not always dangerous, but they become slow if you carry the item through the whole house without staging.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/burglin-gnomes/burglin-gnomes-steal-frying-pan-task.webp"

    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Move frying pans, pots, metal cans, and other heavy objects in stages instead of dragging them across the entire house.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

For carry objectives:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>open the route before grabbing the item,</li>
  <li>move the item to a halfway point,</li>
  <li>drop it during rescue or danger,</li>
  <li>then finish the carry after the route is clear.</li>
</ul>

If you are playing co-op, one player should carry while another opens windows, doors, or drawers ahead. The <Link href="/burglin-gnomes/co-op-guide">Co-op Guide</Link> covers that setup in more detail.

<h2 id="enemy-tasks">
  Rat, roach, and enemy tasks
</h2>

Enemy tasks are HIGH because they require the correct tool, safe positioning, and calm movement. A rat or roach task is not automatically bad, but it should not be your first objective if easier checkmarks are available.

For enemy tasks:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>find the correct tool first,</li>
  <li>clear space before swinging, tasing, or stabbing,</li>
  <li>avoid fighting while carrying heavy loot,</li>
  <li>do not chase enemies into worse rooms,</li>
  <li>and stop if the fight pulls the old man into the route.</li>
</ul>

If the enemy is not blocking the route and the task is not required yet, leave it for later.

<h2 id="faq">
  Burglin' Gnomes tasks FAQ
</h2>

### What task should I do first?

Start with a LOW task: open a window, break a small item, flush a small item, or steal from a nearby confirmed room.

### Are all tasks required?

No. The task list can contain more objectives than you safely complete in one run. Prioritize the safest checkmarks first and use the Gnome Report to judge the result.

### What are LOW tasks?

LOW tasks are fast objectives with low movement and low tool requirements, such as opening a window, breaking a small nearby object, flushing a small item, or stealing from a nearby room.

### What are HIGH tasks?

HIGH tasks require tools, combat, locked areas, outdoor routes, or uncertain material testing. Rat, roach, shed key, firework, and unclear material tasks usually belong here.

### Should I do rat or roach tasks early?

Only if you already have the correct tool and safe space. If easier tasks are available, finish those first.

### How do I know if a room-specific steal will count?

Use the task wording. If it says kids room, kitchen, dining room, bathroom, or hallway, confirm the room before carrying the object out.

### How should I handle material tasks?

Use confirmed Stockpile names, test likely object types only when they are near your route, and verify through task or Stockpile feedback. Do not overcommit to unconfirmed material routes.

### When should I skip a task?

Skip or delay a task when it needs a missing tool, a locked route, an unsafe outdoor path, a far carry item, or long material testing while the tornado warning is close.

### What if my task list has no obvious LOW tasks?

Create a LOW route first. Open a window, secure an exit, confirm the closest room, and choose the task with the fewest tool, enemy, or outdoor requirements. If every task looks risky, start with the one that is closest to your entry route.
