<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
In **Tales of Seikyu**, your first week should focus on a stable loop: follow **Yui’s town tour**, open the **map**, start a small field with **Boar Form**, check **trash cans** for free materials, build storage, buy an early **backpack upgrade** at Musashi’s shop, unlock the **Basic Rod**, restore shrines, and avoid selling every useful item.

The biggest beginner mistakes are ignoring the map, skipping trash cans, delaying inventory upgrades, selling all wood or stone, searching for Bamboo Forest from the wrong side, and forcing water routes before **Slime Form** makes them practical.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="beginner-guide">
  Tales of Seikyu beginner guide
</h2>

Tales of Seikyu looks calm at first, but the first week can overload you quickly.

You have a farm, town tour, resident routes, requests, shrines, forms, fishing, materials, and early exploration all competing for the same day. The trick is to avoid treating every new system as urgent.

A strong first-week route is simple:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Learn the town layout from Yui.</li>
  <li>Use the map before guessing where to go.</li>
  <li>Start a small crop field.</li>
  <li>Check trash cans when you pass them.</li>
  <li>Build storage and buy backpack space.</li>
  <li>Unlock fishing from the Request Board.</li>
  <li>Restore shrines and use teleporting.</li>
  <li>Push ruins and water routes only when prepared.</li>
</ol>

If you want the full route hub, use the <Link href="/tales-of-seikyu">Tales of Seikyu Guide</Link>. If you are already inside the ruins, use the <Link href="/tales-of-seikyu/fox-ruins-guide">Fox Ruins Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="town-tour-map">
  Follow Yui’s town tour and open the map early
</h2>

The town tour is more useful than it looks. Yui is not only moving you through dialogue. She is showing you the places you will use all week:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>the general store,</li>
  <li>the fish shop,</li>
  <li>the tailor,</li>
  <li>the blacksmith,</li>
  <li>the shrine,</li>
  <li>the Request Board,</li>
  <li>and the main town paths.</li>
</ul>

After the tour, open the map often. The map is one of the best beginner tools because it shows NPC locations, building types, shop hours, and shrine teleport points.

This matters when a request says to find someone, when a shop is closed, or when you need to cross the island quickly.

<h2 id="first-farm-setup">
  Start with a small field
</h2>

Your first farm task teaches the most important form lesson: forms are tools.

Use **Boar Form** to cultivate soil, then switch back to **Human Form** to plant and water crops. Do not make the field too large on day one. A huge field looks productive, but it can drain time and energy before you have storage, food, or a good routine.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>
</figure>

A safe first field is better than a big messy one. Plant what you can water, then spend the rest of the day learning town systems.

<h2 id="trash-cans">
  Check trash cans for free early materials
</h2>

Trash cans are easy to ignore, but they are one of the best early habits.

When you pass through town, check trash cans. They can give free items such as ingredients, hardwood, bamboo, rare crafting materials, or other useful early supplies.

This is especially helpful before your farm income becomes stable. A free material can save you a day of gathering, help a request, or let you craft something earlier.

Use this rule:

<strong>If a trash can is already on your route, check it.</strong>

Do not spend your whole day hunting trash cans, but do not walk past free early resources either.

<h2 id="storage-backpack">
  Build storage and buy backpack upgrades
</h2>

Inventory fills fast in Tales of Seikyu.

You gather wood, stone, crops, forage, food, fish, quest items, and crafting materials long before you understand which ones are important. That is why storage and backpack upgrades matter early.

Musashi’s shop sells backpack upgrades. Early upgrades cost around **200 gold** and add one inventory row. Buy one or two early if you can spare the money.

<h2 id="request-board-basic-rod">
  Prioritize the Request Board and Basic Rod

The most important early example is fishing. Take the fishing request when it appears and unlock the Basic Rod. Fishing becomes useful for food, requests, Kappa progress, collection achievements, and downtime when crops are still growing.

<h2 id="upgrades">
  What should you upgrade first?
</h2>

Do not spend every coin the moment a new option appears.

Early upgrades should solve your current problem. If your bag is always full, buy backpack space. If your farm is disorganized, build storage. If you have no activity while crops grow, unlock fishing. If obstacles or movement are annoying, consider Boar upgrades.

<GuideTable
  caption="Early upgrades and systems to prioritize."

  rows={upgradeRows}
/>

A good first-week rule:

<strong>Buy the upgrade that fixes the problem slowing you down today.</strong>

<h2 id="bamboo-forest">
  How to find Bamboo Forest early
</h2>

Bamboo Forest can be confusing because the entrance is not where many players expect.

For the early route, look for the small path **behind the farmhouse**. Do not waste the whole day searching along the riverbank if the request or route sends you toward Bamboo.

This matters because Bamboo can appear in early request and crafting routes, and you may also get Bamboo from trash cans before you can route it comfortably.

If a Bamboo request appears, prepare like this:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>empty part of your bag,</li>
  <li>bring food if you are traveling far,</li>
  <li>check the map,</li>
  <li>use shrine teleporting if it saves time,</li>
  <li>and keep the Bamboo until the request is done.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="fox-ruins-prep">
  When should you enter Fox Ruins?
</h2>

Do not treat Fox Ruins as a day-one chore.

Enter when you have food, inventory space, a weapon, and time left in the day. The first run should be about learning the entrance, activating waypoints, reading clues, and checking offering gates.

<h2 id="time-management">
  Time management and the 3AM rule
</h2>

You do not need to panic every night, but you should still respect the clock.

If you stay out until **3AM**, the game forces you to sleep. In the current 1.0 behavior, you wake up with health and stamina restored, and there is no permanent penalty. The real cost is that you lose the rest of that in-game day and can miss time windows, shop hours, dates, festivals, or requests.

Use late night for nearby tasks:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>storing items,</li>
  <li>checking your next-day route,</li>
  <li>nearby gathering,</li>
  <li>quick crafting,</li>
  <li>or short map checks.</li>
</ul>

Do not start a long ruins, mountain, or water route close to the forced sleep time.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tales of Seikyu beginner FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Tales of Seikyu?

Follow Yui’s town tour first, then use the step-by-step first-week table at the top of this guide.

### Should I check trash cans?

Yes. Trash cans can give free ingredients, bamboo, hardwood, rare crafting materials, and other useful early items. Check them when they are already on your route.

### Where do I buy backpack upgrades?

Check Musashi’s shop. Early backpack upgrades cost around 200 gold and add one inventory row. Buy one or two early if inventory pressure is slowing you down.

### What happens if I stay out until 3AM?

The game forces you to sleep. In the current 1.0 behavior, you wake up with restored health and stamina and no permanent penalty, but you lose the rest of that in-game day.

### How do I find NPCs and shops?

Open the map. It shows NPC locations, building types, shop hours, and shrine teleport points, so you do not need to search blindly.

### Where is Bamboo Forest?

For the early route, look behind the farmhouse for the small path. Do not spend the whole day searching along the riverbank.

### Is Slime Form useful early?

Yes, after it unlocks. Slime Form can help with water, watering support, underwater movement, and routes that felt blocked before.

### Should I sell wood, stone, and early materials?

Sell extras, not your last useful stack. Wood, stone, clay, copper ore, crops, fish, food, bamboo, and hardwood can all become request, crafting, or offering blockers.

### When should I enter Fox Ruins?

Enter when you have food, inventory space, a weapon, and enough time. Your first run should focus on scouting, waypoints, clues, and offering gates.

### Can I ignore the Request Board?

No. The Request Board unlocks useful systems, especially fishing. Use it to guide early progress instead of only treating it as side money.

### Should I make a huge farm immediately?

No. Start small. A field you can water every day is better than a large field that drains all your time and energy.

### What is the best early upgrade?

The best early upgrade is the one solving your current problem. For most beginners, that means storage, backpack space, Basic Rod, or a practical form upgrade.
