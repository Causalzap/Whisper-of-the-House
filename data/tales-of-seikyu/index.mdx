<h2 id="what-to-do-first">
  What should you do first in Tales of Seikyu?
</h2>

Your first goal is not to min-max the whole island. Your first goal is to build a stable daily loop.

Start with this order:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Follow Yui’s town tour and meet the first residents.</li>
  <li>Pay respects at the fox shrine when the story sends you there.</li>
  <li>Reach your farmhouse and learn how the shipping box works.</li>
  <li>Use Boar Form to cultivate land and start your first crops.</li>
  <li>Make or place basic storage as soon as you can.</li>
  <li>Check the Request Board near the fountain.</li>
  <li>Take the fishing job to unlock your first rod.</li>
  <li>Prepare food before pushing deeper into the Fox Ruins.</li>
</ol>

That route gives you money, movement, farming, storage, and exploration access without turning the first week into a mess.

<h2 id="first-farmhouse">
  Your farmhouse is more than a home
</h2>

Your farmhouse is where the game starts to become practical.

At first, it is your bed, shipping box, and farm space. Very quickly, it becomes the place where you:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>plant crops,</li>
  <li>store materials,</li>
  <li>craft furniture and tools,</li>
  <li>plan requests,</li>
  <li>expand fields,</li>
  <li>and later unlock ranch facilities.</li>
</ul>

Do not treat your farm as something to clean perfectly on day one. Clear enough space to plant early seeds, keep useful materials, and return to town before the day disappears.

<h2 id="boar-form">

Boar Form is not just a funny first transformation. It is your first work form.

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

  Pay respects at shrines early
</h2>

Shrines are one of the most important early systems.

When you pay respects at a couple of shrines, the game explains that you can teleport between them. That matters because Seikyu spreads your time across town, farm, ruins, shops, requests, and fishing spots.

Early shrine habits:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>restore shrines when the route naturally brings you to them,</li>

  <li>use teleporting before long walks waste the day,</li>
  <li>and keep shrine offerings in mind for later achievement goals.</li>

Shrines also connect to long-term goals such as **Free to Roam** and **Inari is Pleased**, so do not ignore them after the tutorial.

<h2 id="request-board">
  Use the Request Board instead of guessing what to do
</h2>

The Request Board sits near the town fountain and gives you one of the cleanest ways to progress in the first week.

Yui explains that residents post requests there when they need something. Some requests are simple early jobs. Some ask for materials. Some can push you toward fishing, gathering, furniture, or exploration.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

Your first Request Board priorities should be:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>take the fishing request so you can unlock the Basic Rod,</li>
  <li>watch for easy material requests you can finish quickly,</li>
  <li>accept crop requests only if you can grow or already have the item,</li>
  <li>check whether a request has a time limit after accepting it,</li>
  <li>and keep common materials instead of selling everything overnight.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="achievement-priority">
  Which achievements should you think about early?
</h2>

Most early achievements unlock naturally through farming, cooking, fishing, combat, and exploration. The ones worth tracking early are the planning-heavy routes: shrine offerings, all waypoints, the 75-room Move Over, Yohji achievement, rare fish, ranch animals, giant crops, marriage, and completionist cleanup.

You do not need to chase all of these during the first week. Just avoid selling rare materials blindly, restore shrines when you find them, unlock fishing early, and keep enough notes to know which long-term goals are already started.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tales of Seikyu FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Tales of Seikyu?

Follow the opening town tour, reach your farmhouse, pay respects at the first shrine, use Boar Form to start farming, make storage, then check the Request Board.

### How do I unlock fast travel?

Pay respects at fox shrines. After you have restored a couple of shrines, the game explains that you can teleport between them.

### What is Boar Form for?

Boar Form is your first practical transformation. Use it to cultivate farm soil, smash obstacles, move around faster, and start learning the Forms upgrade system.

### Where is the Request Board?

The Request Board is near the town fountain. Yui introduces it early, and residents use it to post requests for items, jobs, and special needs.

### Should I accept every request?

No. Some requests have timers after you accept them, and some require materials you may not have yet. Check requirements before filling your quest list.

### How do I get the fishing rod?

Take the early fishing request from the Request Board. It leads you to the Basic Rod and starts the fishing loop.

### When should I go to the Fox Ruins?

Go after you have basic food, a weapon, inventory space, and shrine or waypoint access. The ruins include offerings, puzzles, mining, combat, bosses, and form progression, so rushing in underprepared can waste a day.

### What are relic waypoints?

Relic waypoints are teleport points you can unlock inside or near ruins routes. Activate them when you find them so you do not have to repeat long walks.

### How do I unlock Slime Form?

Slime Form comes from Fox Ruins boss progression. It helps with underwater routes, water-based blockers, water attacks, and crop watering.

### How do I unlock Tengu Form?

Tengu Form comes from later Fox Ruins boss progression. It helps with flight, high ledges, and aerial routes.

### How do I unlock Yuki-onna Form?

Yuki-onna Form unlocks later through Fox Ruins and story progression after earlier forms. It helps with later movement and route checks.

### How do I unlock Fox Form?

Fox Form is the final transformation route. Learn Boar, Slime, Tengu, and Yuki-onna first, then continue the Fox Clan story route.

### Is ranch content important?

Yes, but not immediately. Ranch facilities matter more after your basic farm, income, requests, and materials are stable.

### Does Tales of Seikyu have romance and marriage?

Yes. The 1.0 route includes loved gifts, birthdays, heart events, closer interactions, proposal, wedding, and spouse life at the farmhouse.

### Should I focus on achievements from the start?

Only the long-term ones. Simple achievements unlock naturally, but shrine offerings, all waypoints, the 75-room ruins achievement, rare fish, ranch animals, giant crops, marriage, and completionist goals are worth tracking early.
