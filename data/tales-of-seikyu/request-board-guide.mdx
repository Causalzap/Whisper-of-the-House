<h2 id="request-board-guide">
  Tales of Seikyu Request Board guide
</h2>

The Request Board looks simple, but it controls several important early routes.

It can give you money, unlock systems, point you toward NPCs, raise affinity, and teach you which materials the game expects you to keep. The mistake is treating every request as a quick cash job.

A good Request Board route is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Take system-unlock requests first.</li>
  <li>Check whether the request is timed or red stamp.</li>
  <li>Read the exact item and quantity.</li>
  <li>Keep common fish and building materials in storage.</li>
  <li>Use requests from important NPCs to support affinity goals.</li>
  <li>Skip requests that would cost your last useful stack.</li>
</ol>

If you are still setting up your first week, use the <Link href="/tales-of-seikyu/beginner-guide">Beginner Guide</Link>. If you are planning social achievements, use the <Link href="/tales-of-seikyu/romance-gifts-marriage-guide">Romance, Gifts, and Marriage Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="timed-vs-red-stamp">
  Timed requests vs red stamp side quests
</h2>

Not every request uses the same timer logic.

Normal short requests often have a **3-day limit**. These are the jobs where you must check the item, quantity, and your storage before accepting.

Red stamp requests are different. They are longer side quests and do not behave like the same short timed delivery jobs.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to read Request Board timers and request signals."
  columns={timerColumns}
  rows={timerRows}
/>

Use this rule:

<strong>If it has a short timer, check your storage first. If it has a red stamp, treat it as a longer progression quest.</strong>

This distinction matters because many players panic when a quest does not show the timer they expect. The board is mixing short jobs with longer side quest progress.

<h2 id="request-priority">
  Which requests should you accept first?
</h2>

The best request is not always the one with the biggest reward.

Early on, prioritize requests that unlock systems, are easy to finish, improve an important relationship, or use items you already have.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to prioritize Request Board jobs in Tales of Seikyu."
  columns={requestPriorityColumns}
  rows={requestPriorityRows}
/>

Avoid accepting a timed request just because it is available. The timer matters less than whether you can actually finish the request without hurting your farm, upgrades, or offerings.

<h2 id="fish-requests">
  Fish Please and same-species fish requests
</h2>

Fish requests deserve special attention because they can ask for **multiple of the same fish species**.

That means one-of-each storage is not enough. If you sell every duplicate fish, a simple Fish Please! request can become annoying.

After you unlock the Basic Rod:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>keep several common fish,</li>
  <li>keep at least one new fish species,</li>
  <li>check the exact species name before accepting,</li>
  <li>check the quantity,</li>
  <li>and avoid selling all duplicates overnight.</li>
</ul>

This habit also supports later fishing achievements and Kappa-related routes.

Request Board jobs are one of the main reasons you should not sell everything early.

The safest rule is simple: keep a reserve before selling extras.

This matters most for fish, wood, stone, bamboo, hardwood, clay, ore, crops, prepared food, and meat.

<h2 id="cooking-requests">
  Cooking item requests
</h2>

Cooking-related requests can be easy to misunderstand.

Some requests want prepared food items such as pickled or dried goods, not necessarily a normal stove-cooked dish. That means you should read the exact wording before assuming you already have the right item.

Use this checklist:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Check the exact item name.</li>
  <li>Confirm whether it is cooked, pickled, dried, or otherwise prepared.</li>
  <li>Do not eat or sell your last copy before checking the board.</li>
  <li>Keep ingredients if you are close to unlocking the needed preparation route.</li>
</ul>

This section becomes more important after your farm starts producing ingredient variety.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tales of Seikyu Request Board FAQ
</h2>

### What is the most important early Request Board quest?

Teach a Yokai to Fish is one of the most important early requests because it leads to Torleone on the docks and unlocks the Basic Rod.

### Where do I get the Basic Rod?

Accept Teach a Yokai to Fish, then go to Torleone on the docks. This starts your fishing route.

### How long do normal requests last?

Many normal short requests use a 3-day timer. Check the item, quantity, and your storage before accepting.

### What does a red stamp request mean?

A red stamp request is a longer side quest and does not behave like a normal short timed request. Treat it as progression content, not a quick timed delivery.

### Should I accept every request?

No. Accept system unlocks, easy deliveries, important NPC requests, and jobs you can finish without sacrificing your last useful materials.

### Do requests raise friendship?

Yes. Completing requests can raise affinity with the requesting character, which helps friendship and social achievement routes.

### How should I prepare for Fish Please requests?

Keep several common fish in storage. Fish requests can ask for multiple of the same species, not only one new fish.

### Should I sell duplicate fish?

Not all of them. Keep a few duplicates of common fish after unlocking the Basic Rod, especially if you are checking the Request Board often.

### What materials should I keep for requests?

Keep wood, stone, bamboo, hardwood, clay, ore, crops, ingredients, prepared food, meat, and several fish before selling extras.

### Are cooking requests normal cooked meals?

Not always. Some cooking item requests can ask for pickled, dried, or other prepared items. Read the exact item name before assuming a normal meal works.

### Can Request Board jobs help achievements?

Yes. Requests can support fishing, Kappa progress, friendship, ranch progress, material storage, and completion routes.

### What should I skip early?

Skip timed requests that need rare crops, rare fish, prepared food you cannot make yet, or materials you need for upgrades, offerings, or facilities.
