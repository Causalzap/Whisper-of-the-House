<h2 id="master-healer-kale-nightmare-mode-guide">
  Master Healer Kale Nightmare Mode guide
</h2>

Nightmare Mode is where **Master Healer Kale with Useless Party** stops pretending the normal story route is enough.

The normal **Demon King** already filters out a lot of runs. The achievement **Part Of The Journey Is The End** sits at **21.1%**, which means many players never even finish the standard clear. Nightmare Demon King is much lower: **Why So Serious?** is only **5.5%**.

That gap changes how I approach the post-game.

I do not see Nightmare as “same build, bigger enemies.” I see it as a new economy phase. I need more Ruby, facility upgrades, stronger major nodes, and a cleaner spell plan before I treat Nightmare Demon King as realistic.

<h2 id="after-demon-king">
  What changes after Demon King?
</h2>

After the first Demon King clear, the most important unlock is **Nightmare Mode**.

The key line is simple: dungeons can now be cleared in Nightmare Mode, and I can collect **Ruby again** for completing them. Before this point, Ruby feels limited and every mistake hurts. After this point, Ruby becomes the resource that drives the final build.

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">

  <li>refarm Ruby instead of spending it blindly,</li>

The main mistake is treating the unlock as permission to rush the hardest boss. It is not. It is permission to start building properly.

<h2 id="training-and-research-facility">
  Training Facility and Research Facility
</h2>

The endgame is not just more Tavern nodes.

Two achievement requirements make the facilities important:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Just Keep Swimming</strong> asks me to upgrade any stats in the **Training Facility** **30 times**.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Seize The Day, Boys</strong> asks me to upgrade any spells in the **Research Facility** **30 times**.
  </li>
</ul>

I do not leave those systems as final chores. I start using them once Nightmare farming begins.

**Training Facility** is where I fix party stat problems: max health, damage, and the kind of base scaling that makes repeated Nightmare clears less fragile. **Research Facility** is where I fix Kale problems: healing strength, spell reliability, cooldown pressure, and the cost of keeping the party alive.

If the party is getting one-shot or falling behind on stats, I lean Training. If the party lives but Kale cannot keep up, I lean Research.

<h2 id="angel-form-endgame">
  Angel Form in Nightmare fights
</h2>

I treat **Angel Form** differently in Nightmare than I do in the normal story route.

In the skill tree guide, the base numbers matter because I am deciding whether the node is worth buying. In Nightmare, I already care less about the tooltip and more about the timing window: Angel Form gives me a short burst where Kale can cycle spells faster and get more value from healing. If I activate it while the party is already collapsing, I usually waste the window.

For **Why So Serious?**, I wait until the account is clearly stronger than it was at normal Demon King. My minimum checklist is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Grandpa Bagel survives the opening pressure,</li>
  <li>Madeleine or Klepon has a clear damage route,</li>
  <li>Kale has enough mana recovery to keep casting,</li>

  <li>Raise, the revive spell, is ready as insurance,</li>

This is the point where I start treating Nightmare Demon King as a real attempt instead of a scouting run.

<h2 id="common-endgame-mistakes">
  Endgame mistakes I avoid
</h2>

The biggest endgame mistake is treating Nightmare as just bigger enemy numbers.

The bad habits I avoid are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>rushing Nightmare Demon King right after unlocking the node,</li>
  <li>spending Ruby without knowing the last failure point,</li>
  <li>ignoring Training Facility and Research Facility until the final grind,</li>
  <li>trying speed achievements with a defensive build,</li>
  <li>trying Shield achievements without Protection and mana ready,</li>
  <li>buying 100% Tavern filler before the farming engine is stable.</li>
</ul>

The better habit is to let each failed Nightmare run tell me what to buy next. If the tank dies, I buy tank. If the fight is slow, I buy damage. If spells are late, I buy Research or cooldown support. If the party gets clipped, I buy health or shields.

<h2 id="faq">
  Master Healer Kale Nightmare Mode FAQ
</h2>

### How do I unlock Nightmare Mode?

Beat the normal Demon King, then buy the Nightmare Mode node in the Tavern tree. The node unlocks Nightmare dungeons and lets you collect Ruby again from completed dungeons.

### How much does Nightmare Mode cost?

On the shown Nightmare Mode node, the cost is **43.2K gold** and **10.0K skill points**.

### Is Nightmare Mode just harder bosses?

No. The bigger change is the endgame economy. Nightmare Mode lets you refarm Ruby, then use that Ruby with Training Facility, Research Facility, and major endgame nodes.

### What should I do first after unlocking Nightmare Mode?

Clear safer Nightmare dungeons first. Use them to collect Ruby again, then buy the major upgrade that fixes your current wall.

### Should I rush Nightmare Demon King?

No. Normal Demon King clear is **21.1%**, but Nightmare Demon King is only **5.5%**. I treat Nightmare Demon King as the final check after Ruby refarming, facilities, and major endgame nodes.

### What is the best first Nightmare Ruby node?

It depends on the failure. **God Armor** helps if Grandpa Bagel dies, **Phoenix Armor** helps if Madeleine dies, **Arcana Armor** helps if Klepon dies, and **Phoenix Staff** is excellent if Madeleine already carries damage.

### Is Phoenix Staff worth it?

Yes, if Madeleine is already your main damage route. It gives Madeleine damage **x2**, which is much stronger when the magic route is already carrying.

### When should I buy God Armor?

Buy God Armor when Grandpa Bagel is still the reason Nightmare runs collapse. Grandpa Bagel maximum health **x3** gives the whole party more time to fight.

### Do I need Raise for Nightmare Demon King?

I like having Raise ready because it revives a KO'd ally and gives the run one emergency recovery point. I do not build the whole strategy around it, but for Nightmare Demon King it is useful insurance if one ally drops during a bad window.

### When should I use Angel Form in Nightmare?

Use Angel Form when the party is stable, mana is ready, and several useful spells are ready or close to ready. In Nightmare, I treat it as a prepared spell window, not a panic button.

### When should I do 100% Tavern cleanup?

I save 100% Tavern cleanup for late endgame, after Nightmare Ruby farming and facility upgrades make resource farming comfortable.
