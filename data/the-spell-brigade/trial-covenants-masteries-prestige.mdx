<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Trial Covenants, Masteries, Enchantments, Banish, rerolls, and Ascension are the systems that turn The Spell Brigade from “good run” into “planned run.”**

Use **Enchantments** for broad account power. Use **Masteries** to support a specific build identity, such as spell size for Solar Pulse or cast speed for high-frequency spells. Use **Trial Covenants** when the Devotion reward is worth the restriction. Use **Banish** and rerolls to protect focused builds from bad offers.

The simple rule:

**Pick the progression goal before the run starts. Then choose the covenant, mastery, spell, and offer-control tools that serve that goal.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="masteries-vs-enchantments">
  Masteries vs Enchantments
</h2>

Masteries and Enchantments are related, but they should not be planned the same way.

<GuideTable
  caption="Masteries, Enchantments, Covenants, and offer-control tools compared."
  columns={masteryVsEnchantColumns}
  rows={masteryVsEnchantRows}
/>

Use **Enchantments** to raise baseline power. Use **Masteries** to decide what kind of run this is going to be. A Solar Pulse run wants different Mastery support from a Falling Stars run, and both want different offer control from a high-Devotion Trial Covenant route.

<h2 id="trial-covenants">
  Trial Covenants and Devotion
</h2>

Trial Covenants increase difficulty and push Devotion-related progression, but every Covenant changes the run in a different way. Some slow your leveling, some reduce choices, some punish healing, and some turn the game into a near no-hit challenge.

Because unlock conditions can change with patches and should be checked in-game, this table focuses on the part that matters during the run: **what the Covenant does and how to build around it.**

  caption="Trial Covenants, effects, stacking pressure, and counterplay."

  rows={covenantRows}
/>

<GuideCallout title="Trial Covenant rule">
A Trial Covenant is only good if the run survives long enough for the Devotion value to matter.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="devotion-and-stacks">
  Devotion, stacks, and unlock planning
</h2>

The important detail is that some Covenants are not just on/off modifiers. Several of them scale with stacks, which means the same Covenant can move from manageable to run-breaking once you add another layer.

<GuideTable
  caption="How Devotion and Covenant stacking affect routing."
  columns={devotionColumns}
  rows={devotionRows}
/>

Do not unlock, stack, and farm with the same mindset. Unlocking a Covenant is a goal. Stacking it is a challenge. Farming with it is a route. Treat those as separate phases.

Abstinence is the cleanest example of how Covenants and Masteries work together.

The setup is simple:

**Pick one carry spell → use a Mastery that strengthens that spell → funnel upgrades into it → use rerolls / Banish to protect the route.**

The key question is not “is one-spell strong?” The key question is whether the chosen spell can handle three jobs by itself:

- clear enemies,
- protect your movement path,
- complete objectives safely.

If it cannot do all three, the Covenant turns from a focused route into a trap.

Luck matters most when the run will last long enough for offer quality to compound.

Take Luck when:

- the build is already safe,
- you are farming a long loop,
- you need legendary or rare upgrades,
- you are trying to hit a specific augment or spell route,
- your wizard or mastery already supports scaling.

Skip Luck when:

- you are dying now,
- the boss is coming and your damage is too low,
- your movement is failing,
- your main spell is not online yet.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>

Covenants and Masteries give you the plan before the run. Objectives and augments give you the power inside the run.

Do objectives when:

- your build is stable,
- the objective is nearby,
- the reward can unlock the next augment,
- you need +1 Projectile, extra beams, split, healing, or spell behavior changes.

Skip objectives when:

- the run is already unstable,
- the Covenant restriction makes the area too dangerous,
- a Hardcore portal is the safer decision,
- the objective would break the run goal.

<h2 id="level-pacing">
  Level pacing and upgrade pool dilution
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/the-spell-brigade/the-spell-brigade-achievements-level-15-rng-pool.webp"
    alt="The Spell Brigade upgrade screen used to explain upgrade pool dilution after level 15."
    width={1600}

    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Focused Covenant and Mastery routes can fail when the upgrade pool becomes too diluted before the key offer appears.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

For normal clears, leveling quickly is usually good. For Covenant and Mastery routes, the goal is more specific: reach the right offers before the pool becomes too noisy.

This matters most when you are:

- playing **Abstinence** and need one spell to carry the run,
- playing **Denial** and every upgrade choice matters more,
- playing **Mana Drought** and slower leveling makes bad choices more expensive,
- forcing one specific infusion,
- building around one Mastery instead of collecting random side power.

Do not slow down leveling on purpose unless the route needs precision. The real rule is:

**Level fast for power. Keep the pool compact for precision.**

Ascension farming is different from normal clearing. You are not only trying to win the run. You are trying to get consistent wizard progress without wasting time.

The best ascension routes usually have:

- one strong carry spell,
- a Mastery that supports that spell,
- a Covenant that increases progress without killing the run,
- enough pickup range to keep leveling,
- enough movement to survive loops,
- enough sustain to avoid random deaths.

A build can kill the whole screen and still scale badly if it cannot collect Mana. Pickup range is not a comfort stat. In long progression runs, it is part of the engine.

<h2 id="recommended-progression-order">
  Recommended progression order
</h2>

Use this order if you are still building your account:

1. **Stabilize normal clears.**  
   Do not stack hard Covenants before you can finish normal runs.

2. **Separate Enchantments from Mastery planning.**  
   Enchantments raise your floor. Masteries decide the build identity.

3. **Pick one reliable Mastery route.**  
   Spell Size with Solar Pulse is the easiest example.

4. **Learn Abstinence / one-spell routing.**  
   Use a spell that can actually carry the screen.

5. **Unlock and use offer control.**  
   Rerolls and Banish make focused builds much easier to force.

6. **Unlock Trial Covenants one at a time.**  
   Learn what each restriction changes before stacking it.

7. **Use objectives for augments.**  
   Build-changing augments are part of progression, not optional fluff.

8. **Farm ascension with long stable loops.**  
   Use pickup range, movement, sustain, and one-spell scaling.

9. **Cash out when the run has done its job.**  
   A clean completed progression run is better than a greedy dead one.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What do Trial Covenants do?

Trial Covenants add difficult run modifiers in exchange for Devotion and progression value. Each one changes the run differently, such as reducing Mana drops, disabling Enchantments, reducing upgrade choices, reducing healing, or adding extra enemy waves.

### What is Devotion?

Devotion is tied to Trial Covenant difficulty and long-term progression. Higher Devotion is useful when you are intentionally farming ascension-style progress, but it is not worth dying early for.

### Do Trial Covenants stack?

Some do. Abstinence, Denial, Torment, and Onslaught explicitly become more severe with stacks. Add stacks slowly and adjust your build around the new pressure.

### What is the difference between Masteries and Enchantments?

Enchantments are broad account upgrades. Masteries are build-defining passive focuses. Treat Enchantments as baseline power and Masteries as the thing that tells your run what it wants to become.

### What is the best Covenant for ascension farming?

Abstinence / one-spell routing is the easiest route to recommend when the chosen spell has strong AoE coverage. Solar Pulse is the safest starting example.

### Should I use rerolls aggressively?

Use rerolls when the current choices do nothing for the build or when you need a specific infusion. Do not reroll every imperfect offer.

### When should I use Banish?

Use Banish when an offer repeatedly appears but does not help your route. It is especially strong in one-spell, Denial, and achievement-focused builds.

### Does Luck help progression?

Yes, especially in long runs. Luck helps you see better rarity offers, but it should not replace immediate survival, damage, or movement when the run is unstable.

### Why does pickup range matter for ascension farming?

Long runs create more Mana than you can collect automatically. If Mana stays outside pickup range, your level flow slows down and the build stops scaling efficiently.

### Is Prestige the same as Ascension?

Players may use “prestige” loosely, but the current in-game progression language is better described through wizard rank, Devotion, Covenants, Masteries, outfits, and Ascension. Use the in-game term when naming the system.
