<h2 id="hardcore-solo">
  Hardcore solo priorities
</h2>

Hardcore solo is not just normal solo with bigger numbers. It changes what you should value.

In normal solo, you can sometimes fix mistakes with more damage, a potion, or a lucky upgrade. In Hardcore, bad positioning and greedy objectives punish you much harder.

Use this priority order:

1. **Movement and positioning**
2. **Dodge or sustain**
3. **AoE uptime**
4. **Spell size and cooldown**
5. **Boss safety**
6. **Damage greed**

Armor and HP still help, but they do not replace movement, dodge, or a safe route through the arena.

<GuideCallout title="Hardcore solo rule">
In Hardcore, do not ask “what gives me the most damage?” Ask “what keeps this run from ending when the screen gets crowded?”
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="dodge-crit-heal">
  Dodge Crit Heal for solo survival
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/the-spell-brigade/the-spell-brigade-solo-guide-dodge-heal-upgrade.webp"
    alt="The Spell Brigade Evasion Mend dodge healing upgrade that restores health when a dodge prevents damage."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Evasion Mend is the concrete card that turns dodge from chance-based defense into recovery.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Dodge Crit Heal is not one card. It is a survival shell.

You are looking for this combination:

1. **Dodge chance**
2. **Dodge healing**, such as Evasion Mend
3. **A high-hit spell**, such as Solar Pulse, Aether Beam, Rune Burst, or another spell that keeps triggering value
4. **Crit chance / crit damage**
5. **Healing on crit**, such as Vital Strike, if the run offers it

The loop is:

**Dodge avoids the hit → Evasion Mend restores health → crit clears enemies → Vital Strike turns critical damage into more healing**

That is why this shell is strongest on high-hit spells. Solar Pulse, Aether Beam, Rune Burst, and other frequent-hit spells create more chances for dodge, crit, and healing value to matter.

<h2 id="acid-solo-boss-build">
  Acid Multi-Hit for solo bosses
</h2>

Solo boss fights can feel bad when your build has damage but no shred.

That is where Acid Multi-Hit helps. Acid reduces resistance, and repeated-hit spells apply that value more often. This is especially useful when bosses and elites are the reason your run slows down.

Good solo Acid carriers include:

- Astral Orbs,
- Moonerang,
- Scepter Mesh zones,
- fast projectiles,
- any spell that keeps hitting while you dodge.

Use **Flux** if you want Lightning pressure plus Acid utility. Use **Corrosion** if you want to commit harder to Acid.

Scepter Mesh is good solo when you treat it like arena control.

With Lightning or Plasma, its walls become damage zones. With Acid or Flux, it can help soften tanky enemies. With enough size and cast speed, it gives you something solo players desperately need: space.

Use Scepter Mesh when:

- enemies are collapsing on you,
- you need safer objective zones,
- bosses move too much for narrow damage,
- you want to fight around a controlled area.

The mistake is taking Scepter Mesh and leaving it unsupported. Small, slow, under-infused walls will feel passive.

  Solo long loops and when to stop
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

Solo long loops are fun, but they are not automatically efficient.

Keep looping if:

- enemies are still dying quickly,
- XP pickup is keeping up,
- your positioning is safe,
- boss fights are controlled,
- your build is still scaling.

Stop or cash out if:

- enemies become damage sponges,
- XP is sitting outside pickup range,
- objectives become unsafe,
- your escape route is gone,
- the next boss will probably end the run.

A strong solo player is not the one who always loops forever. It is the one who knows when the run has already paid out.

<h2 id="recommended-solo-order">
  Recommended solo learning order
</h2>

If you are learning solo, use this order:

1. **Start with Solar Pulse Aura.**  
   It teaches focused spell scaling, AoE, movement, and safe objectives.

2. **Learn when to take safety upgrades.**  
   Solo runs fail when you keep taking damage after your positioning is already falling apart.

3. **Learn Dodge Crit Heal as a survival shell.**  
   Do not force it every run, but recognize Evasion Mend, Vital Strike, dodge, crit, and high-hit spells when they appear together.

4. **Try Acid Multi-Hit for boss problems.**  
   Use it when bosses and elites live too long.

5. **Try Scepter Mesh for space control.**

   Do safe objectives. Skip the ones that would probably kill the run.

   Falling Stars Lightning, Solitary Focus, and long loops are easier once you understand the basics.

  FAQ

### Can you play The Spell Brigade solo?

Yes. From the main menu, choose Play, then use Private Match or start without waiting for squad fill. If the UI shows Fill Squad or matchmaking, turn it off before launching.

### What difficulty should I start on solo?

Start on Moderate Threat if you are still learning enemy patterns, objective timing, and boss movement.

### What is the best solo build?

Solar Pulse Aura is the easiest solo build to recommend because it gives AoE damage, strong scaling, and room for sustain.

### Is solo easier than co-op?

Solo is cleaner but less forgiving. You avoid teammate chaos, but you also lose revives, role coverage, and co-op support.

### What is the best solo stat?

Movement speed is one of the best solo stats, but in Hardcore you should think in terms of a full safety plan: movement, positioning, dodge, sustain, and AoE uptime.

### Is Dodge Crit Heal good solo?

Yes. Dodge Crit Heal is excellent when Evasion Mend, Vital Strike, dodge chance, crit, healing, and a high-hit spell start coming together. It is usually a survival shell, not a forced opener.

### Should I do every objective solo?

No. Do objectives when they are safe and useful. Skip objectives that would probably kill the run.

### What is the best solo boss build?

Solar Pulse Aura and Acid Multi-Hit are both strong. Solar Pulse keeps damage active while you dodge, while Acid Multi-Hit helps shred bosses and elites.

### Is Scepter Mesh good solo?

Yes. Scepter Mesh is good when supported with Lightning, Plasma, Acid, Flux, spell size, and cast speed. It gives space control and damage.

### Can you farm gold solo?

Yes. Solar Pulse Aura is one of the easiest solo farming routes. Read the <Link href="/the-spell-brigade/gold-farm">Gold Farm Guide</Link> for Bounty, objectives, and farm timing.
