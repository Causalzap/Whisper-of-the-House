<h2 id="short-runs-vs-long-runs">
  Short runs vs long loops
</h2>

The biggest farming trap is thinking longer always means better.

Long loops can work if your build is still deleting enemies, collecting XP efficiently, and completing objectives. But after about **25-30 minutes**, many runs start slowing down because enemies become tankier and your kill speed drops.

That is the point where repeated fast clears can beat one long run.

Use this rule:

**If the run is still clearing fast, keep going. If enemies are becoming damage sponges, cash out and start another run.**

This is why Bounty objective runs are so strong. You are not trying to prove your build can last forever. You are trying to finish profitable runs quickly.

<h2 id="greed-breakables">
  Greed, crates, boxes, and breakables
</h2>

Greed is not the main gold farm by itself, but it is worth understanding.

When you have Greed, destructible objects such as crates, boxes, and breakable clusters become more valuable. These small sources are not worth ruining your route for, but they are worth collecting when they are already on your path.

Use Greed well by:

- breaking clustered objects near objectives,
- avoiding long detours for one small crate,
- pairing it with fast movement,
- treating it as bonus gold, not the whole farm.

The rule is simple:

**Break what is on your route. Do not turn the entire run into a box hunt.**

<h2 id="contracts">
  Contracts and bonus gold
</h2>

Contracts are easy to forget because they are checked before the run, not during it.

Sometimes the game gives a random wizard a contract bonus. If you run that wizard next, you can gain extra gold.

Before starting a dedicated farm run, check the wizard tab and ask:

- Which wizard has the contract?
- Can I clear safely with that wizard?
- Does that wizard have a strong farming build?
- Is the bonus worth changing my plan?

Do not force a weak run just because a contract exists. A safe Solar Pulse or objective run can still beat a bad contract run. But when the contracted wizard is already strong, the contract is free value.

Ascension and prestige-style systems are why gold farming becomes more important later.

Early on, you farm because you want upgrades. Later, you farm because you need to pay for expensive progression gates. That changes the way you should think about runs.

A late-game farm run should ask:

- Am I farming for Bounty value?
- Am I farming for a wizard unlock?
- Am I farming for enchantments or masteries?
- Am I farming for ascension?
- Am I farming for an achievement route?

<h2 id="map-choice">
  Best map for gold farming
</h2>

The best farming map is not always the hardest map.

Because gold and progression depend on survival, objectives, and clear speed, the best map is usually the one where your build can:

- move easily,
- finish objectives quickly,
- avoid too many terrain blockers,
- break useful object clusters without detours,
- keep enemies inside damage zones,
- survive boss phases consistently.

Open maps are usually better for farming than cramped maps with lots of obstacles. Terrain that blocks your route can lower your real gold per minute even if the map looks harder or more advanced.

<h2 id="solo-vs-coop">
  Solo vs co-op gold farming
</h2>

Solo farming is easier to control. Co-op farming can be faster if the team is organized.

A simple co-op farming template:

- **Player 1:** Main damage, such as Solar Pulse, Falling Stars, or Plasma.
- **Player 2:** Acid shred for bosses and elites.
- **Player 3:** Control or Scepter Mesh for safe objectives.
- **Player 4:** Support, healing, or objective runner.

The mistake is having four players all chase their own solo build. A co-op farm works best when the team covers different jobs.

<h2 id="recommended-farm-order">
  Recommended farming order for new players
</h2>

If you are still early in progression, use this order:

1. **Stabilize normal runs.**  
   Do not worry about perfect gold per minute until you can finish runs consistently.

2. **Unlock the path to Bounty.**  
   Bounty is the upgrade that turns normal clears into real gold farming.

3. **Respec into Bounty for dedicated farming.**  
   If your goal is gold, put points where the gold is.

4. **Run 25-30 minute objective clears.**  
   Fast clears are usually better than forcing every run into a long loop.

5. **Add Greed and breakables when convenient.**  
   Take free extra gold, but do not ruin the route for it.

6. **Check contracts before starting.**  
   A strong contracted wizard can be free value.

7. **Use Solar Pulse or another safe AoE route.**  
   Safe AoE makes objectives and boss phases easier.

8. **Save for late-game goals.**  
   Do not spend every bit of gold randomly once ascension or mastery costs start appearing.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best gold farm in The Spell Brigade?

Unlock Bounty, respec into it for farming, and run fast objective-focused clears. Solar Pulse Aura is the easiest build route to pair with this strategy.

### What does Bounty do?

Bounty gives 25% extra gold and doubles objective reward gold. It appears in the third row of upgrades and becomes the main gold-farming upgrade once unlocked.

### How much gold should a good run make?

A normal clear often lands around 500-550 gold. An optimized Bounty objective run can reach around 800-850 gold.

### Does gold drop during the run?

Gold is mostly calculated after the run. The important factors are objectives, kills, run completion, Bounty, contracts, Greed, and breakables.

### Are short runs better than long runs?

Often, yes. After about 25-30 minutes, enemies can become tanky enough that gold per minute drops. Keep looping only if your build is still clearing efficiently.

### What is Greed good for?

Greed improves gold from destructible objects such as crates and boxes. It is a bonus source, not the whole farming plan.

### What are contracts?

Contracts are random wizard bonuses that can give extra gold. Check the wizard tab before starting a farm run.

### Should I farm the hardest map?

Not always. The best farming map is the one your build can clear quickly and safely. If terrain slows objectives or you die early, your gold per minute drops.

### Why does pickup range matter?

If XP stays on the ground outside your pickup range, your leveling flow slows down. Long farms need pickup support or regular movement through XP.
