<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best element in The Spell Brigade 1.0 is usually either Lightning or Acid, depending on what your run needs.**

Pick **Lightning** when you want the safest first infusion. Pick **Acid** when bosses, elites, or tanky waves live too long, because Acid reduces enemy resistance and makes them take more damage from all sources.

After that, choose your advanced infusion by problem: **Plasma** for burst, **Flux** for Lightning plus Acid utility, **Corrosion** for full Acid commitment, **Wildfire** for Fire crit ticks, **Thunder** for double-Lightning impact, and **Ice** when you need control instead of more damage.

<h2 id="base-elements">
  Base elements: Lightning, Acid, Fire, Ice, and Dark
</h2>

The normal first-layer elements are **Lightning, Acid, Fire, Ice, and Dark**. Wildfire is not a base element; it is an advanced Fire route created by stacking Fire twice.

Your build usually comes online in this order:

**Main spell → first infusion → support stats → second infusion → advanced infusion.**

The first infusion usually appears around **spell level 7**. The second infusion usually appears around **spell level 14**. That second infusion is where advanced routes like **Plasma**, **Flux**, **Corrosion**, **Wildfire**, **Thunder**, **Brimstone**, **Hellfire**, or **Void** become possible.

<h2 id="acid">
  Acid: the S-tier resistance-reduction element
</h2>

Acid is the element that makes enemies take more damage.

The tooltip says:

**Afflicted target's resistance is reduced, meaning they receive more damage from all sources.**

That is why Acid deserves S-tier placement. It is not just another DoT. It changes how fast everything dies, especially when your spell can apply hits repeatedly.

Best Acid carriers include:

- **Astral Orbs**, because they can keep hitting enemies.
- **Moonerang**, because return paths can create repeated contact.
- **Fast projectiles**, because more hits mean more chances to apply Acid value.
- **Zone spells**, if enemies stay inside the damage area.
- **Co-op teams**, because every teammate benefits when enemies take more damage.

If your spell hits slowly and only once, Acid is less exciting. If your spell hits many times, Acid can become one of the best boss and elite tools in the game.

<h2 id="brimstone">
  Brimstone: Acid + Fire splash damage
</h2>

Brimstone is the advanced infusion shown when Acid meets Fire:

**Acid + Fire = Brimstone**

It is one of the best wave-clear routes because the initial hit inflicts splash damage on nearby enemies. This makes it much better when enemies are packed together.

Take Brimstone when:

- enemies are grouped,
- your spell already hits packs,
- your team needs wave clear,
- you want Acid utility to turn into AoE pressure.

Do not take Brimstone as your main answer to isolated boss phases unless your spell already has strong boss uptime.

<h2 id="plasma">
  Plasma: Fire + Lightning burst damage
</h2>

Plasma is the classic burst combo:

**Fire + Lightning = Plasma**

It is strongest when the spell already has good uptime. Solar Pulse, Rune Burst, Scepter Mesh, Aether Beam, and other frequent-hit spells can turn Plasma into a strong boss and elite damage route.

Take Plasma when:

- your spell hits often,
- you already have cast speed,
- you need burst damage,
- bosses are not dying fast enough,
- your build does not need Acid shred or Ice control more.

Plasma is excellent. It is just not the only best answer.

<h2 id="thunder">
  Thunder: Lightning + Lightning impact damage
</h2>

Thunder is the double-Lightning route:

**Lightning + Lightning = Thunder**

If Lightning is the safest first infusion, Thunder is what happens when you double down on that plan. It is best when you want bigger impact damage without changing into Fire, Acid, Ice, or Dark utility.

Take Thunder when:

- your first Lightning infusion already feels good,
- you want stronger front-loaded hits,
- your spell lands reliably,
- you do not need Acid resistance reduction,
- your team already has Fire, Acid, or Ice covered.

Do not force Thunder just because Lightning is S-tier. If elites are too tanky, Acid or Flux may solve more. If the horde is collapsing on you, Ice or Brimstone may be better.

<h2 id="wildfire">
  Wildfire: Fire + Fire, not a base element
</h2>

Wildfire is an advanced Fire route:

**Fire + Fire = Wildfire**

It should not be treated like a sixth base element. The base element is Fire. Wildfire is what Fire becomes when you double down on it.

Take Wildfire when:

- you already have Fire,
- you can scale critical damage,
- enemies are dense,
- your spell hits many targets,
- you are playing Solar Pulse or another AoE-heavy build.

Do not take Wildfire when the run urgently needs Acid resistance reduction, Ice control, or Plasma burst.

<h2 id="ice">
  Ice: control, not vulnerability
</h2>

Ice is for control.

It can slow and freeze enemies, which gives you time to reposition, revive teammates, finish objectives, or keep enemies away from a fragile damage build. The important correction is this: **Acid is the element that reduces resistance. Ice is the element that buys time.**

Use Ice when:

- the horde is closing too fast,
- you need space,
- your team needs safer revives,
- objectives are getting messy,
- your build already has enough damage.

Avoid overusing Ice if your team wants enemies to stay inside Solar Pulse, Scepter Mesh, Brimstone, or other damage zones.

<h2 id="dark">
  Dark, Hellfire, Void, Blight, and Wither
</h2>

Dark is the most plan-dependent base element.

It becomes strong when your build is intentionally about corruption. It feels weak when you take it randomly without supporting the route.

Dark routes include:

- **Hellfire**: Dark + Fire.
- **Void**: Dark + Dark.
- **Blight**: Dark + Acid.
- **Wither**: Dark + Ice.

Use Dark when your build or team is coordinating corruption. If you are solo and underbuilt, Lightning or Acid is usually safer.

Scepter Mesh is easy to underrate because it does not look like a normal damage spell. It creates walls, so new players often treat it like a panic button instead of a build core.

The trick is that enemies walk through the walls. That means any element with repeated contact value becomes much stronger.

Use these routes:

- **Lightning / Plasma** if you want the walls to become a damage grinder.
- **Acid / Flux** if enemies are tanky and you want the walls to help reduce resistance.
- **Ice** if your team needs space and safer repositioning.
- **Fire / Brimstone** if enemies are grouped and you want more splash pressure.

A simple Scepter Mesh route is:

**Scepter Mesh → Lightning or Acid → spell size → cast speed → second infusion → objective augments**

The biggest mistake is taking Scepter Mesh and then not building around it. If the walls are small, slow, and under-infused, the spell feels passive. If the walls are large, fast, and infused correctly, they become arena control plus damage.

<h2 id="recommended-learning-order">
  Recommended learning order for new players
</h2>

If you are new to The Spell Brigade, do not try to learn every element route at once. Learn them in the order that teaches the most useful habits first.

1. **Start with Lightning.**  
   Lightning is the safest first infusion because it works on almost any spell and does not require a perfect build.

2. **Try Acid on Astral Orbs or Moonerang.**  
   Acid teaches you how resistance reduction works. Multi-hit spells make the lesson obvious because enemies start dying faster after repeated hits.

3. **Build Fire into Plasma.**  
   Fire + Lightning teaches you why the second infusion matters. Plasma is a great way to understand burst damage and high-uptime spells.

4. **Try Acid + Fire for Brimstone.**  
   Brimstone teaches you how Acid can become wave clear instead of only boss shred.

5. **Try Fire + Fire for Wildfire.**  
   Wildfire teaches you how advanced Fire routes scale with crit ticks, dense packs, and repeated damage.

6. **Use Ice when damage is not the problem.**  
   Ice teaches control. Pick it when you need space, safer revives, or objective control.

7. **Try Dark last.**  
   Dark is powerful only when you understand corruption routes. Do not use it as a random filler element.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best element in The Spell Brigade?

Lightning is the safest general element. Acid is usually the best answer when bosses or elites live too long. Plasma is strong for burst, but it is not always better than Acid, Flux, or Corrosion.

### Is Acid good with Astral Orbs and Moonerang?

Yes. Acid is excellent on multi-hit spells such as Astral Orbs and Moonerang because repeated hits can apply resistance-reduction value much more often.

### What is the difference between Flux and Corrosion?

Flux is Lightning + Acid, so it keeps Lightning pressure while adding Acid utility. Corrosion is Acid + Acid, so it is the full Acid route for resistance reduction and safer elite / boss fights.

### Is Wildfire a base element?

No. Wildfire is Fire + Fire. It is an advanced Fire infusion, not a base element.

### Does Ice make enemies take more damage?

Ice is mainly control: slow, freeze, and space. Acid is the element that reduces resistance and makes enemies take more damage from all sources.

### What is Thunder?

Thunder is Lightning + Lightning. It is the double-Lightning route for stronger impact damage.

### What is Plasma?

Plasma is Fire + Lightning. It is a strong burst route for spells with good uptime, such as Solar Pulse or Scepter Mesh.

### What is Brimstone?

Brimstone is Acid + Fire. Its initial hit inflicts splash damage on nearby enemies, making it strong against clustered packs.

### Are advanced infusions guaranteed?

No. You need the spell to reach the right level and you need the correct element options to appear. Rerolls help, but you should not assume every run will offer the exact route you want.

### What element should beginners learn first?

Start with Lightning, then learn Acid, Plasma, Brimstone, Wildfire, Ice, and Dark in that order. See the <Link href="#recommended-learning-order">recommended learning order</Link>.
