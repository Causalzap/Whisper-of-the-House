<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
The best ability in **Librarian: Tidy Up the Arcane Library** is **Assemble**.

For most players, the best upgrade order is:

1. **Assemble** — best overall search and speed tool.
2. **Insight** — useful early for finding matching books.
3. **Sorting** — useful once you carry full series.
4. **Auto-Shelving** — good time-saver after you know the shelf.
5. **Shelf Guide** — optional if you already use maps.

For speed or **Efficiency Librarian**, rush **Assemble** and collect all key upgrades early, especially the carry capacity chests.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="assemble">
  Assemble: why it is the best skill
</h2>

**Assemble** uses the top book in your hand as a base and gathers books from the same series. This is the strongest ability because the main bottleneck in Librarian is not placing one book — it is finding every volume in a series across huge piles.

What Assemble does:

- gathers books from the same series
- helps locate missing volumes from a known series
- reduces random pile searching
- turns one known book into a repeatable cleanup route

When Assemble is most valuable:

- during midgame and late-game cleanup
- after you unlock extra carry capacity
- when preparing for Efficiency Librarian
- when clearing long 10-book series

<GuideCallout title="Important Assemble limitation">
Do not use shelves as temporary storage if you expect Assemble to collect those books later. If a book is already placed on a shelf, Assemble may not pull it back into your hands.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="insight">
  Insight: best early search helper
</h2>

**Insight** is useful when the library is still messy and you are trying to locate books inside piles. It makes matching books easier to spot, which is especially helpful before Assemble is upgraded.

Insight is best when:

- the floor is still covered in piles
- you know a series but cannot find the remaining volumes
- you are still learning book covers and colors
- you want to avoid checking every pile manually

Insight is weaker when:

- Assemble is already upgraded
- most loose books are cleared
- your main problem is shelf order, not finding books

<h2 id="sorting">
  Sorting: weak early, useful later
</h2>

**Sorting** automatically arranges the books in your possession in numerical order.

At first, this can feel unnecessary because you may only be carrying a few books. Sorting becomes worth upgrading once you have both carry capacity chests unlocked and can regularly hold **7 or more books** at once. Before that, you usually hold too few books for auto-ordering to save meaningful time.

Use Sorting when:

- you have many books in hand
- you are placing a 10-book series
- you keep misplacing volume order
- you are trying to reduce late-game mistakes

Do not overvalue Sorting early. It does not find the shelf, does not find missing books, and does not complete the row by itself.

<h2 id="auto-shelving">
  Auto-Shelving: good, but not first priority
</h2>

**Auto-Shelving** instantly places matching books from your hand onto the bookshelf you are targeting. This is useful, but only after you already know where the books belong.

Auto-Shelving is best for:

- rows where you already know the correct shelf
- repeated late-game placement
- reducing manual clicks
- turning gathered series into completed rows faster

Auto-Shelving is not the best first skill because it does not solve the bigger early problem: finding all volumes and learning sections.

<h2 id="shelf-guide">
  Shelf Guide: useful only if you do not use maps
</h2>

**Shelf Guide** highlights the correct bookshelf for the top book you are holding. It is useful for blind players, but it is not always worth heavy investment.

Use Shelf Guide if:

- you refuse external maps
- you are playing fully blind
- you keep forgetting section locations
- you want in-game routing help

Skip or delay Shelf Guide if:

- you already keep the stair map open
- you are following a guide
- you need more Assemble power
- you are trying to speedrun

The category maps are posted at the base of the main staircase. If you use those maps or an external screenshot, Shelf Guide becomes much less important.

<h2 id="efficiency-route">
  Best ability route for Efficiency Librarian
</h2>

For **Efficiency Librarian**, your goal is to finish the game under 3 hours. Do not attempt this blind.

Recommended setup:

1. Get the **Crimson Octagon Key** and unlock Jump.
2. Collect the other key upgrades before serious sorting.
3. Prioritize **Carry Capacity +3** and **Carry Capacity +2** because they improve Assemble routes.
4. Rush **Assemble** upgrades.
5. Add **Sorting** once you are carrying larger series.
6. Use **Insight** only when you are still losing time searching piles.
7. Use **Auto-Shelving** when you already know the target shelf.

With +5 total carry capacity, a 10-book series can take about two trips instead of several smaller trips. Across hundreds of rows, that matters more than it looks.

For the key route, use <Link href="/librarian/key-locations">All Key Locations and Chest Unlocks</Link>.

<h2 id="anti-magic-master">
  Anti-Magic Master and major magic
</h2>

For **Anti-Magic Master**, do not use active major magic.

Treat these as major magic tools to avoid:

- Assemble
- Insight
- Shelf Guide
- Sorting
- Auto-Shelving

Chest upgrades such as **Jump**, **Sprint**, and **Carry Capacity** are passive minor upgrades based on community testing, but the safest Anti-Magic route is still simple: avoid active sorting spells and do the run manually.

For the full no-magic run order and how Anti-Magic Master interacts with You Are Fired and other achievement routes, use <Link href="/librarian/achievements">All Achievements and Endings Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best ability in Librarian: Tidy Up the Arcane Library?

**Assemble** is the best overall ability because it gathers books from the same series and reduces pile searching.

### What ability should I unlock first?

Unlock **Assemble** first if you want efficiency. Choose **Insight** early only if you are still struggling to find matching books in piles.

### Is Insight better than Assemble?

Insight is better for visual searching early, but Assemble is stronger overall because it brings matching books to you instead of only highlighting them.

### Is Shelf Guide worth it?

Shelf Guide is worth it if you are playing blind or refuse external maps. If you already use a section map, it is usually lower priority than Assemble.

### Is Sorting worth upgrading?

Sorting is worth upgrading once you have both carry capacity chests unlocked and regularly hold **7 or more books** at once. Before that point, the books in your hand are usually too few for auto-ordering to save meaningful time.

### Is Auto-Shelving worth using?

Yes, but it is not a first priority. Auto-Shelving is best when you already know the correct shelf and want to place matching books faster.

### Why does Assemble not grab a book?

Assemble may not pull books that are already placed on shelves. Keep temporary books on the floor if you plan to gather them later.

### Does Assemble have a cooldown?

Yes. Assemble has a cooldown, and upgrades make it available more often. Upgraded Assemble becomes the core loop for fast cleanup.

### What is the best ability route for Efficiency Librarian?

Collect all key upgrades early, rush Assemble, add Sorting once you carry larger groups, and use Insight only when searching piles still costs time.

### Do carry capacity upgrades help Assemble?

Yes. Carry capacity upgrades let you hold more books from the series Assemble gathers, which reduces trips and speeds up full-row cleanup.

### What counts as major magic for Anti-Magic Master?

Avoid active spell tools such as Assemble, Insight, Shelf Guide, Sorting, and Auto-Shelving. Chest upgrades like Jump, Sprint, and Carry Capacity are passive minor upgrades based on community testing.

### Can I use abilities in an Anti-Magic Master run?

Do not use active major abilities in an Anti-Magic Master run. Keep that run manual.

### Should I max every ability?

For a normal magic run, yes, eventually. For efficiency, prioritize Assemble first, then only invest in tools that reduce real time loss.

### What is the biggest ability mistake?

The biggest mistake is spending too much early on Shelf Guide or Auto-Shelving before upgrading Assemble. Assemble solves the main problem: finding full series.
