<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
To sort books in **Librarian: Tidy Up the Arcane Library**, use this order:

1. **Find the section** from the title, map, or Shelf Guide.
2. **Group the same series** together.
3. **Place volumes left to right** in number order.
4. **Check the shelf indicator** — blue / lit means complete, dark means something is still wrong.
5. **Do not trust color alone.** White books can be Philosophy, Healing, Holy, or Priest-style books.

The fastest beginner route is to start with obvious Monsterology books, white-book groups, black/red necromancy books, and repeated title groups, then use Assemble once you have enough early rows.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="volume-order">
  Volume order
</h2>

After you find the correct section, the next common failure is volume order.

Books in the same series need to be placed from left to right by volume number. If the row looks complete but will not turn blue, check the numbers before moving the whole row.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I know where a book goes?

Use the title keywords first, then confirm the section on the map or with Shelf Guide. Color is only a clue.

### Can I sort books by color?

Not reliably. White books can be Philosophy, Healing, Holy, or Priest-style books, so always check the title and section.

### Where do white books go?

White books can go to several sections, including Philosophy, Healing, Holy, and Priest-style areas. Do not send every white book to 2J Philosophy.

### Where do black books with red text go?

Many black or dark books with red text are Necromancy or Curse-related books. Check 1E Necromancy and curse / dispel sections first.

### Where do monster books go?

Monster, beast, creature, and species-related books usually point toward **1A Monsterology**. This is one of the easiest early categories to check.

### What does it mean when a shelf does not turn blue?

Usually one book is missing, out of order, in the wrong section, or split into a neighboring row. Recheck the full series and volume order.

### I placed all the books but the shelf still will not turn blue — what am I missing?

The most common cause is a volume order mistake or a split series. Books in the same series must stay together and be placed left to right by volume number.

### Does a book snapping into the shelf mean it is correct?

It means the placement was accepted, not that the row is complete. The row only counts when the shelf indicator turns blue or lights up.

### Why is volume order important?

The game expects books in the same series to be ordered by volume number. A single wrong number can keep the shelf incomplete.

### What should I sort first?

Start with easy visual groups: Monsterology books, white books, black/red necromancy books, repeated title groups, and short series that are easy to complete.

### Are small shelves important?

Yes. Some 3-volume or 5-volume series belong on smaller shelf panels. Do not force every short series into a long row.

### Should I use Shelf Guide?

Use Shelf Guide if you are playing blind. If you already have the map open, prioritize Assemble and Sorting instead.

### Why does Assemble not find my missing book?

The book may already be sitting on a shelf. Assemble is strongest when books are loose in piles or on the floor.

### Is this a full 3,072-book list?

No. This is a sorting system and section guide. A full 3,072-book database should be a separate long-term page or interactive index.

### Will there be a full 1A-1N section index?

That should be a separate page. This guide gives confirmed section examples and sorting rules, while a full 1A-1N and second-floor index needs a dedicated searchable table.
