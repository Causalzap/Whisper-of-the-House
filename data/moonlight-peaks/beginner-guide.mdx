<h2 id="moonlight-peaks-beginner-guide">
  Moonlight Peaks beginner guide: first week route
</h2>

The first week of **Moonlight Peaks** is about opening systems, not making the farm perfect.

The game looks familiar if you have played other farming sims, but the nightly structure changes the pressure. You wake at **6:00 p.m.**, work through the night, and need to be back before sunrise. The early save feels best when I keep the route moving: first crop, town registration, Chester, map use, early money, then the purchase that fixes the next bottleneck.

My first-week rule is:

<strong>do the objective that unlocks more systems before doing the chore that only cleans one tile.</strong>

That means I do not spend the first night chopping every tree or picking up every piece of debris. I plant what the first quest needs, then go to town while there is still time.

<h2 id="first-night-route">
  First night route: Blood Grapes, Victor, Town Hall
</h2>

The first real objective comes from Orlock. He gives you Blood Grape seeds and points you toward Victor if you need farming help.

The key is to start the Blood Grape timer and leave the farm while the night is still young. Town progress matters more than a perfectly clean starting field.

<h2 id="planting-blood-grapes">
  Planting Blood Grapes: keep the first patch simple
</h2>

For the first Blood Grapes, I clear just enough space, dig the soil, equip the seeds, plant them, and water them before leaving.

I keep the patch simple for three reasons:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Time:</strong> the first night is better spent opening town systems.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Movement:</strong> blocked walking paths become annoying when you are rushing home.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Future layout:</strong> better tools and the well will change how the farm should be arranged.
  </li>
</ul>

If a crop blocks movement, I leave enough space to walk around it. I also keep the main line from the house to the farm exit open. That small habit saves time every night.

That is where the save actually opens up. You meet Brooke and Ridge, get pointed toward Town Hall, and start learning where shops and residents are.

<h2 id="stamina-mana-and-sleep">
  Stamina, mana, and sleep: what actually punishes you
</h2>

Moonlight Peaks has both stamina and mana.

Stamina is used for most tool actions. Mana is used for magic. Early on, stamina is the bar I manage every night because clearing, digging, chopping, watering, and mining can drain it quickly.

Running out of stamina does not instantly end the night. It simply stops stamina-based work. That is when I switch to tasks that do not need stamina, such as:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>foraging,</li>
  <li>talking to residents,</li>
  <li>checking the map,</li>
  <li>selling through Chester,</li>
  <li>or walking back home.</li>
</ul>

The real danger is time.

I treat **5:00 a.m.** as the warning and **6:00 a.m.** as the hard stop. If I am far from the farm, I do not start a new route. I go back.

<h2 id="save-system">
  Save system: sleep before you quit
</h2>

Moonlight Peaks saves when you sleep, so I do not quit after a long night until the character is safely back in the coffin.

My rule is:

<strong>no sleep, no safe stopping point.</strong>

That matters most after buying an upgrade, finishing a quest step, getting a rare reward, catching important fish, or unlocking a new feature. I treat sleep as the end of the route, not just a way to skip to the next night.

<h2 id="aquaflux-magic-and-hellkitten">
  Aquaflux, magic, and Hellkitten form: do not expect them on night one
</h2>

Magic is important in Moonlight Peaks, but it is not fully online at the start.

The early mistake is seeing the broken wand, mana stars, or magical crop hints and assuming you missed a button. You usually did not. The game introduces magic through story progress.

<GuideTable
  caption="Moonlight Peaks early magic and form systems."
  columns={magicColumns}
  rows={magicRows}
/>

When Aquaflux unlocks, I treat it as a watering tool with limits, not a free automatic farm solution. It still depends on the watering can and mana, so I do not build a giant magical crop field before I know how much mana I can support.

Hellkitten form is the opposite kind of upgrade: it helps movement and farm prep. Once I have it, I use the faster movement when the night clock is tight, and I use the form's dirt-prep action when it saves energy compared with normal tool work.

The important beginner takeaway is:

<strong>magic and shapeshifting are progression systems, not missing night-one controls.</strong>

<h2 id="storage-and-crafting">
  Storage and crafting: use the house storage first
</h2>

Your home has built-in storage, and I use it as my main storage early.

The reason is convenience. Home storage works cleanly with the makers and crafters placed on the home plot. I keep resources, food, furniture, clothes, and uncertain items there instead of spreading everything across outdoor bins too early.

My storage rule is:

<strong>main resources inside, convenience items outside.</strong>

Extra bins are still useful later, especially for flowers, gifts, or farm supplies near the field. I just do not let storage management become the first-week minigame.

<h2 id="relationships-and-gifts">
  Relationships and gifts: talk first, gift cheaply
</h2>

You can talk and give gifts from the beginning.

My early relationship routine is light:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>talk to residents when I pass them naturally,</li>
  <li>give spare flowers when I have them,</li>
  <li>check the relationship screen for liked or loved gift tracking,</li>
  <li>and save expensive items until I know they matter.</li>
</ul>

I do not turn the first week into a full romance spreadsheet. Flowers are useful because they cost no gold and no stamina to collect, but I do not assume every character loves every flower.

<h2 id="nokturna">
  Nokturna: know it exists, but do not route around it yet
</h2>

Nokturna is the card-game system you will hear about early through town characters.

For the first week, I only treat it as a system to remember. I do not plan my first purchases or nightly route around a full Nokturna collection. Cards can become relevant later, including through dig-spot rewards once the feature is active, but that is not the first bottleneck.

My beginner rule is:

<strong>learn the name, do not chase the whole card game yet.</strong>

If you are still trying to unlock fishing, Chester, copper, tools, or the well, those systems matter more immediately.

<h2 id="faq">
  Moonlight Peaks beginner FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Moonlight Peaks?

Wake Orlock, accept the Blood Grape task, plant and water the first crops, then go into town and register at Town Hall. Do not spend the whole first night clearing the farm.

### Should I clear the whole farm early?

No. Clear enough space for the first crop patch and the walking paths you actually use. Save full cleanup for later when better tools and clearer farm plans make it more efficient.

### How do I sell items?

Use Chester after the Town Hall route sends him to your plot. He works as the shipping bin, and you can remove items before sleeping if you change your mind.

### What time do I need to sleep?

When the 5:00 a.m. warning hits, start heading home. 6:00 a.m. is the pass-out point.

### Does Moonlight Peaks autosave?

The safe habit is to sleep before quitting, because the game saves when you sleep.

### What happens when stamina runs out?

You do not instantly pass out from stamina loss. You simply cannot keep doing stamina-based actions. Use the remaining time for walking, talking, map checks, foraging, or selling.

### When do I unlock Aquaflux?

Aquaflux comes later through the magic progression around Luna, the Dinner Party route, and the wand repair path. Do not expect full spell use on night one.

### How does spell casting work?

Spell casting uses the wand and a circular input pattern. If you forget the pattern, check your magic book instead of guessing during a busy night.

### What does Hellkitten form do?

Hellkitten form is a later shapeshift that helps movement and farm prep. Once unlocked, it can move faster and prepare dirt with lower energy cost than normal tool work.

### Should I buy the well first?

Often yes. The well costs 1,500 gold and helps watering, but use the Early Resources and Tool Upgrades Guide for the full purchase timing.

### Should I buy the barn in the first week?

Usually not as an impulse buy. The barn costs 4,000 gold and starts the animal loop, so use the Barn and Animals Guide before committing.

### When should I buy the inventory upgrade?

Remember the number: 2,000 gold for +10 slots. Buy timing depends on how soon you are doing long mine, fishing, forage, or dig-spot routes.

### Why do I see bugs but have no bug net?

That is normal. The bug net unlocks later through character progression. Keep meeting residents, checking map sparkles, and progressing town events.

### Should I worry about Nokturna immediately?

No. Nokturna matters later, but I do not route the first week around the card game while basic money, tools, town progress, and feature unlocks are still open.
