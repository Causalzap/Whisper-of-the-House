<h2 id="moonlight-peaks-tool-upgrades-guide">
  Moonlight Peaks tool upgrades and early resources guide
</h2>

The tool upgrade route in **Moonlight Peaks** has two parts.

First, you need the right shop timing. **Howling Hammer is not open all week.** If you show up on the wrong day or after midnight, the upgrade route feels broken even when you have the bars ready.

Second, you need the right upgrade order. The game lets you spend bars on several tools, but ore is the resource that feeds the whole chain. That is why I upgrade the pickaxe first.

My tool rule is:

<strong>upgrade the tool that unlocks the next resource loop, not the tool that only feels convenient tonight.</strong>

That keeps the early game clean. Pickaxe improves ore. Ore becomes bars. Bars become better tools. Better tools open better resources.

<h2 id="how-to-upgrade-tools">
  How to upgrade tools at Howling Hammer
</h2>

To upgrade tools, go to **Howling Hammer** and talk to **Ridge**.

The shop timing matters:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Open days:</strong> Monday to Friday.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Open hours:</strong> 6:00 p.m. to midnight.
  </li>
</ul>

The menu path is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>talk to Ridge,</li>
  <li>choose <strong>Shop</strong>,</li>
  <li>choose <strong>I want to buy something</strong>,</li>
  <li>scroll right to the <strong>third tab</strong>,</li>
  <li>pick the tool upgrade you can afford.</li>
</ol>

This is a real early trap. If I need an upgrade on the weekend, I do not keep wandering around town. I use the time for mining, foraging, fishing, crop work, or resource prep, then return on the next open night.

<h2 id="best-tool-upgrade-order">
  Best tool upgrade order: pickaxe first
</h2>

My default upgrade order is:

  caption="Moonlight Peaks early tool upgrade order."

The pickaxe is first because it controls the ore chain. A better pickaxe lets me break larger mineral rocks and reach the ore types needed for future upgrades.

The axe is usually second because wood, logs, and blocked paths affect the whole farm layout. The watering can becomes important once the crop patch is large enough that water use slows the night. Shovel and scythe are still useful, but I do not spend early bars on them before the mining loop is stable.

  Tool upgrade costs and materials

Each tool upgrade needs coins, the previous version of the tool, and **3 bars** of the matching metal. The main exception is the scythe, which stops at Iron, and the fishing rod, which has a single Premium upgrade.

The price table changes how I spend bars.

If I have only **3 Copper Bars**, I do not spread them around. I ask which upgrade opens the next material route. Most of the time, that means Copper Pickaxe first.

If I already upgraded the pickaxe and farm paths are slowing me down, the axe becomes the next clean choice. If I am farming heavily and water is the bottleneck, the watering can moves up. I only push shovel or scythe earlier when a specific task needs them.

<h2 id="when-to-stop-upgrading">
  When to stop upgrading and save money
</h2>

A good upgrade makes the next few nights smoother. A bad upgrade feels nice once and then leaves me short for the real blocker.

I stop spending when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Howling Hammer is closed and I would only be wandering around waiting,</li>
  <li>I am saving for the 2,000 gold inventory upgrade before a long mine route,</li>
  <li>I need charcoal and ore before bars can actually be made,</li>
  <li>I have only 3 bars and the pickaxe path is not finished,</li>
  <li>I am close to the well or barn threshold but the purchase is not supported yet,</li>
  <li>or the next upgrade adds comfort while a story route is still blocked.</li>
</ul>

The key question is:

<strong>Will this upgrade open the next route, or just make tonight feel nicer?</strong>

If it only makes tonight feel nicer, I usually wait.

<h2 id="faq">
  Moonlight Peaks early resources and tool upgrades FAQ
</h2>

### Where do I upgrade tools in Moonlight Peaks?

Upgrade tools at Howling Hammer by talking to Ridge. Choose Shop, choose I want to buy something, then scroll to the third tab.

### What are Howling Hammer's hours?

Howling Hammer is open Monday to Friday from 6:00 p.m. to midnight.

### What is the best first tool upgrade?

I usually upgrade the pickaxe first because better pickaxes unlock better mining, and mining supplies the bars needed for later upgrades.

### How much does a Copper tool upgrade cost?

Most Copper tool upgrades cost 1,000 coins, the Rusty version of that tool, and 3 Copper Bars.

### How much does an Iron tool upgrade cost?

Most Iron upgrades cost 2,000 coins, the Copper version of that tool, and 3 Iron Bars. The Iron Scythe is different: it costs 2,500 coins, the Copper Scythe, and 3 Iron Bars.

### How much does a Gold tool upgrade cost?

Most Gold upgrades cost 3,500 coins, the Iron version of that tool, and 3 Gold Bars. The scythe does not have a Gold tier.

### How do I make Copper Bars?

Use the Furnace with 4 Copper Ore and 1 Charcoal to make a Copper Bar.

### Where do I get Copper Ore?

Copper Ore comes from the Cave of Echoes route in Misty Shores, which opens through A Bridge Too Far.

### Should I sell ore or bars?

Not early. Ore becomes bars, and bars pay for tool upgrades. I keep them until the upgrade and quest route is clear.

### When should I buy the inventory upgrade?

Buy the first inventory upgrade before repeated long resource runs. It costs 2,000 gold and adds 10 slots.

### Should I buy the well or upgrade tools first?

Buy based on the bottleneck. If watering wastes time every night, buy the well. If ore is blocking upgrades, prioritize pickaxe and mining.

### Should I buy the barn before tool upgrades?

Not usually. The barn costs 4,000 gold and starts the animal route, so check the Barn and Animals Guide before spending that money.
