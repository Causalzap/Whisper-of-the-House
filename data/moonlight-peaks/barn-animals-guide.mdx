<h2 id="moonlight-peaks-barn-animals-guide">
  Moonlight Peaks barn and animals guide
</h2>

The animal system in **Moonlight Peaks** looks simple at first: meet Luna, see cute animals, build a barn, bring one home.

The actual decision is more important than that.

A barn is not a decoration purchase. It starts a daily ranch loop with feed, animal goods, fertilizer, space planning, and future animal choices. I only buy it when I can support that loop instead of just affording the building.

My ranch rule is:

<strong>build the barn when the farm can support animals, not just when the shop lets me buy one.</strong>

That keeps the first animal route from slowing down tools, mining, watering, or early money.

<h2 id="farm-animals-for-sale">
  Farm Animals for Sale: how the animal route starts
</h2>

The animal route begins through Luna and the **Farm Animals for Sale** step.

Before the first barn, Luna's yard is limited. You may only see Cheekens, which makes the system look smaller than it really is. After the barn is built, the selection opens up and starts rotating.

</h2>

The well matters only as a support check. If watering is still eating the night, fixing the crop route first usually makes saving for animals easier.

<h2 id="luna-animal-rotation">
  Luna's animal rotation: do not judge the shop from one night
</h2>

After your barn is built, Luna's animals rotate.

That means one visit is not enough to know what is available. The game can show different animals and color variants on different nights, so I do not spend all four barn slots just because the first day's selection looks limited.

<GuideTable
  caption="Moonlight Peaks Luna animal rotation and adoption details."
  columns={rotationColumns}
  rows={rotationRows}

The naming detail is small but useful. When I adopt an animal, I have a name ready because the game expects me to choose one. That avoids sitting on the naming screen trying to think of something while I am just trying to finish the night.

<h2 id="barn-capacity">
  Barn capacity: one barn holds four animals
</h2>

Each barn holds **4 animals**.

That number is the reason I avoid duplicate-heavy buying early. With only four slots, one bad impulse can crowd out better variety later.

If I want more than 4 animals, I plan for a **second barn**. I do not assume there is a cheap capacity upgrade waiting in the same barn menu.

My first-barn rule is:

<strong>use the first 4 slots to learn the ranch loop and cover different outputs.</strong>

That gives me eggs, milk-style products, wool-style products, fertilizer, and future request coverage instead of one repeated output.

<h2 id="animal-goods-and-fertilizer">
  Animal goods and fertilizer
</h2>

Animals are worth the cost because they add value beyond simple daily income.

They help in three ways:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Goods:</strong> animals produce items such as eggs, milk, wool, and other creature-specific products.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Fertilizer:</strong> animals help support fertilizer for better crop quality.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Route coverage:</strong> different outputs can help with requests, crafting, cooking, or future side jobs.
  </li>
</ul>

This is why I prefer variety over four copies of the same animal. A mixed barn gives me more answers when the job board, crafting, or cooking asks for something specific.

I still check the barn regularly. Some goods need a direct barn visit, and fertilizer is easy to forget if I treat animals as fully automatic.

<h2 id="money-route-for-barn">
  Money route: how I save for the barn
</h2>

The barn costs **4,000 gold**, but I do not fund it by selling upgrade-critical materials.

I use safer income first:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>sell extra fish after keeping request items,</li>
  <li>sell duplicate common forage,</li>
  <li>process crops when the route is available,</li>
  <li>use dig spots for money, seed bags, recipes, and blueprints,</li>
  <li>and keep ore or bars if tool upgrades still need them.</li>
</ul>

The bad version of a barn purchase is spending everything, adopting one animal, then realizing I have no feed, no money cushion, and no upgrade materials left. I would rather wait one night and build a barn I can actually use.

<h2 id="where-to-place-barn">
  Where to place the barn
</h2>

The barn takes **10x6** space, so placement matters.

I do not place it in the middle of the main crop route. I want it close enough to check nightly, but not blocking:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>the house-to-field path,</li>
  <li>the farm exit route,</li>
  <li>the well and crop area,</li>
  <li>future walking paths,</li>
  <li>or decorative space I already want to keep open.</li>
</ul>

If I have not cleared enough space yet, I wait. A bad barn location turns every night into extra walking.

<h2 id="faq">
  Moonlight Peaks barn and animals FAQ
</h2>

### How much does the barn cost in Moonlight Peaks?

The barn costs 4,000 gold at Howling Hammer.

### How big is the barn?

The barn uses a 10x6 placement area and takes 1 Night to build.

### How many animals does one barn hold?

One barn holds 4 animals.

### How do I get more than 4 animals?

Build another barn. One barn holds 4 animals, so a larger ranch needs another barn rather than a simple capacity upgrade.

### How do I start raising animals?

Follow Luna's animal route and watch for Farm Animals for Sale, then build a barn before adopting animals.

### What animals can I get early?

The key early names are Cheeken, Piggoat, Draculamb, and Cowcula. The selection can rotate after the barn is built.

### Why do I only see Cheekens?

Before the first barn, Luna's yard may only show Cheekens. After the barn is built, more animals can rotate into the daily selection.

### Do Luna's animals change every day?

Yes. After your barn is built, Luna's available animals can rotate daily, usually with 3 choices outside her house.

### Do I need to name animals myself?

Yes. When adopting, be ready to choose a name yourself instead of relying on a default suggestion.

### How does feeding work?

Use Fodder in the feeder or trough so animals can eat overnight. Premium Fodder is a higher feed tier, but normal Fodder is the first priority.

### How do I make Fodder?

Use the refiner with farm cleanup materials such as fiber. Once animals are planned, fiber becomes important feed material.

### What is Premium Fodder?

Premium Fodder is a higher-tier feed made from Fodder and Mana Essence. I wait on it until the basic animal route is stable.

### Should I fill the barn with the same animal?

Usually no. With only 4 slots, a mixed barn covers more goods, requests, and future crafting needs.

### Do animals help with fertilizer?

Yes. Animals help support fertilizer, which feeds back into crop quality.

### Should I buy the barn before tool upgrades?

Only if the ranch route is your next real bottleneck. If copper, tools, or inventory are blocking progress, solve those first.
