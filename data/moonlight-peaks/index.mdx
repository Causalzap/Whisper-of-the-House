<h2 id="moonlight-peaks-guide-hub">
  Moonlight Peaks guide hub: where to start
</h2>

**Moonlight Peaks** opens in layers.

The first layer is the basic night loop: plant the first crops, reach town, register, sell safely, and get back before morning. The second layer is route-gated: Misty Shores, Copper Ore, fishing, tool upgrades, animals, magic, the bug net, Soul Blobs, jobs, and side collectibles all depend on different character or resource paths.

That is why I do not treat the game like one giant checklist.

My early rule is:

<strong>find the bottleneck, then read the guide that owns that bottleneck.</strong>

This hub is intentionally focused on progression, money, tools, animals, and unlock confusion. It does not try to cover every relationship, recipe, decoration, museum item, or card route before those pages exist. If this cluster expands later, this page is where I would add new routes.

<h2 id="barn-animals-and-luna">
  Barn, animals, and Luna
</h2>

The barn is an animal-system gate, not a decoration purchase.

The barn costs **4,000 gold**, uses a **10x6** placement area, takes **1 Night** to build, and holds **4 animals**. Luna's animal route includes **Farm Animals for Sale**, and after the barn is built her available animals rotate daily.

<h2 id="what-not-to-over-optimize">
  What not to over-optimize from this hub
</h2>

A hub should not make you optimize every system at once.

I do not route the first week around perfect gifts, full Nokturna collection, every spell, all animals, every Soul Blob, full decoration, or complete farm cleanup. Those are later systems. Early progress comes from opening the right route, then following the focused guide for that route.

The practical rule is:

<strong>solve the current bottleneck, then move to the next guide.</strong>

That keeps the save moving without turning the first week into a checklist of systems that are not all available yet.

<h2 id="faq">
  Moonlight Peaks FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Moonlight Peaks?

Plant the first Blood Grapes, register at Town Hall, learn Chester selling, and start meeting residents. Use the Beginner Guide for the full first-week route.

### Why is Misty Shores still blocked?

Misty Shores is tied to Orlock's Wine Scheme and A Bridge Too Far. If the bridge is still cursed, follow the character route before looking for copper.

### Where is Cave of Echoes?

Cave of Echoes is on the lower-left side of Misty Shores. It becomes important when Copper Ore and Copper Bars enter the route.

### Which tool should I upgrade first?

The pickaxe is usually first because better mining supports Copper, Iron, and Gold Bar upgrades. Use the Tool Upgrades Guide for the full cost table.

### When is Howling Hammer open?

Howling Hammer is open Monday to Friday from 6:00 p.m. to midnight.

### How much does the barn cost?

The barn costs 4,000 gold, uses 10x6 space, takes 1 Night to build, and holds 4 animals.

### What animals should I know early?

Cheeken, Piggoat, Draculamb, and Cowcula are the key early animal names. Luna's available animals rotate after the barn is built.

### Why do I not have magic yet?

The broken wand and spells are story-gated. Follow the magic route through later story progress instead of assuming spells are ready on night one.

### Why do I not have the bug net yet?

The bug net comes through Death after enough route progress. Bugs can appear before the net is unlocked.

### How do I unlock longer nights?

Longer nights come from the Death and Soul Blob reward path. After the clock reward, check Night Duration in settings.

### Should I chase Nokturna cards immediately?

No. Nokturna matters later, but the first week should focus on Town Hall, Chester, Misty Shores, tools, animals, and feature unlocks.
