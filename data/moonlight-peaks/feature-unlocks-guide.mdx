In **Moonlight Peaks**, most missing features are not hidden shop items. They are gated by character and story progress.

The fishing rod comes from **Noel's 3-fish competition**. The broken wand and spells unlock later through the **Dinner Party / Night 18 magic route**, involving **Sabrina, Fiona, the Mana Extractor, Luna, and Aquaflux**. The bug net comes through **Death** after progress around **Orlock's Wine Scheme**, **A Bridge Too Far**, and **Misty Shores**. Soul Blobs are a longer collection route with **100 total**, and the clock reward path can unlock **Night Duration**, including longer 25-minute nights.

<h2 id="moonlight-peaks-feature-unlocks-guide">
  Moonlight Peaks feature unlocks guide
</h2>

The most confusing part of **Moonlight Peaks** is not knowing whether you missed something or whether the game simply has not unlocked it yet.

Most of the time, you did not miss it.

The game holds back several major systems: fishing, spellcasting, bug catching, Soul Blobs, longer nights, jobs, Vampsters, special shops, and some card-game rewards. These systems open through characters, story events, map sparkles, and later nights.

My rule is:

<strong>if a feature is missing, check character progress before searching every shop.</strong>

This matters because the fishing rod comes from Noel. The wand repair comes from the magic story chain. The net comes from Death. Longer nights come from the Soul Blob clock route. Jobs appear later through Alberta. Vampsters become their own collection route. Treating all of those as normal shop purchases wastes time.

<h2 id="fishing-unlock">
  How to unlock fishing: Noel's 3-fish competition
</h2>

Fishing does not start as a simple shop purchase.

The early route comes from **Noel**. He challenges you to a fishing competition, and the useful anchor is the **3-fish** requirement. Some routes also frame the challenge as a **250 gold** wager, so I do not start it with an empty plan.

<h2 id="wand-and-spell-unlock">
  Broken wand and spell unlocks: why magic is missing
</h2>

The broken wand is the biggest missing-feature trap.

You can see magical systems early. You can visit Web of Wonders. You may even see spells for sale. But that does not mean spellcasting is ready.

<GuideTable
  caption="Moonlight Peaks broken wand, spellcasting, and Aquaflux route."
  columns={spellColumns}
  rows={spellRows}
/>

The magic route belongs to later story progress. The useful timing anchor is the **Dinner Party / Night 18 stretch**, then the chain involving **Sabrina**, **Fiona**, the **Mana Extractor**, **Luna**, and magical crops.

Once the wand route is repaired and Luna teaches **Aquaflux**, magic becomes practical. Aquaflux is the first watering spell, but I still treat it as a limited tool because it uses mana.

My magic rule is:

<strong>do not buy around magic until the wand is fixed and mana is visible.</strong>

Before that point, I spend money on systems I can already use. After that point, I start thinking about which spell actually fixes the route: watering, harvesting, or another nightly bottleneck.

<h2 id="bug-net-death-unlock">
  How to unlock the bug net: Death, Misty Shores, and A Bridge Too Far
</h2>

The bug net is not a starting tool, even though bugs appear early.

The route anchor I use is:

<strong>Orlock's Wine Scheme → A Bridge Too Far → Misty Shores / Ambrosia progress → Death encounters → bug net.</strong>

Some players reach the net around the Night 12 range, but I treat quest progress as the safer trigger than the exact date. If Misty Shores is still blocked or Death has not appeared enough, the net is probably not ready yet.

<strong>visible bugs are not proof that the net should already be in your inventory.</strong>

Once Death gives the net, it opens two routes at once: normal bug catching and Soul Blobs.

<h2 id="soul-blobs-and-night-duration">
  Soul Blobs, Antique Clock, and longer nights
</h2>

Soul Blobs are not just random floating collectibles. They are tied to Death's route and the night-duration reward path.

The full Soul Blob route has **100 Soul Blobs**. The clock reward path includes the **Antique Clock**, and after the relevant reward you should check **Night Duration** in settings.

That setting matters because nights can be extended from **15 minutes** to **25 minutes**.

This is a major quality-of-life unlock. Longer nights make mining, fishing, gifting, errands, and late returns easier.

My rule is:

<strong>after a clock reward, check settings before assuming the longer night is automatic.</strong>

I do not try to finish all 100 Soul Blobs immediately. I fold them into normal routes once I have the net:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>catch Soul Blobs when they are already near my path,</li>
  <li>do not chase them when the clock is close to morning,</li>
  <li>combine them with town errands or map checks,</li>
  <li>and revisit the setting after Death's reward.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="job-board-unlock">
  Job board unlock: Alberta the Owl
</h2>

The job board appears later through **Alberta the Owl**.

I do not plan the first week around it. Early money should come from crops, safe selling, foraging, fishing once Noel opens it, and resource routes. Jobs become useful once the board exists and the request matches something I can finish without breaking the night.

A job is worth taking when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>the item is already in storage,</li>
  <li>the reward is better than my current route,</li>
  <li>the time limit is realistic,</li>
  <li>and the request does not pull me away from a key unlock.</li>
</ul>

I skip early jobs if they conflict with magic, Misty Shores, tool upgrades, or Death progress.

<h2 id="vampsters">
  Vampsters: the collectible route players miss
</h2>

Vampsters are a separate collectible route, and they are easy to miss if you only think about farming, mining, and fishing.

They are small hidden creatures you carry back to the cave near Orlock's mansion. The important detail is that they are not normal inventory items. You have to carry them in the proper form and physically bring them back.

My Vampster rule is:

<strong>mark the route, but do not let one Vampster derail the whole night.</strong>

If I find one early, I ask:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>am I in the right form to carry it?</li>
  <li>do I know the path to the cave?</li>
  <li>is there enough time before morning?</li>
  <li>and am I interrupting a more important unlock?</li>
</ul>

If the answer is no, I remember the location and come back later. Vampsters are a collection route, not a reason to miss a story step or pass out.

<h2 id="special-shops-and-nokturna">
  Special shops, weekend routines, and Nokturna cards
</h2>

Some features are easy to confuse with missing content because they depend on timing or side systems.

Yabis, the weekend midnight market, Sabrina's stock, snake merchants, and other rotating shop moments are not always available whenever you want them. I check the map and calendar before assuming something disappeared.

Nokturna is similar. It is a card-game system that connects to town life and collection progress. Fishing and exploration can feed into card rewards later, but I do not route my first week around card hunting.

My side-system rule is:

<strong>learn the system name early, chase the full collection later.</strong>

That keeps the first week focused on higher-impact unlocks: Town Hall, Chester, Noel, Misty Shores, Death, tools, and the magic route.

<h2 id="faq">
  Moonlight Peaks feature unlocks FAQ
</h2>

### How do I unlock fishing in Moonlight Peaks?

Fishing unlocks through Noel's competition. Find Noel, start the challenge, catch 3 fish, and return to him.

### Is Noel's fishing challenge a bet?

Some routes frame the competition around a 250 gold wager, so do not start it completely unprepared.

### Why is my wand still broken?

The broken wand is part of the magic story route. Keep progressing toward the Dinner Party / Night 18 stretch, then follow the Luna, Sabrina, Fiona, and Mana Extractor chain.

### How do I unlock Aquaflux?

Aquaflux is taught by Luna after the wand repair and magical crop route progress. It is your first watering spell.

### Where do I buy spells?

Spells are tied to Webb of Wonders, but buying spells before the wand and mana route is ready will not solve the missing-magic problem.

### How do I unlock the bug net?

Progress Orlock's Wine Scheme, A Bridge Too Far, Misty Shores / Ambrosia, and Death encounters. Death gives the net later in that route.

### Why do I see bugs but have no net?

That is normal. Bugs can appear before the net is unlocked, so keep progressing Death and Misty Shores route anchors.

### How many Soul Blobs are there?

The full collection route has 100 Soul Blobs.

### What does the Antique Clock do?

The Antique Clock is tied to the Soul Blob reward path. After earning the reward, check Night Duration in settings.

### How do I get longer nights?

Progress the Soul Blob and Death reward route, then check Night Duration in settings. Longer nights can extend the night from 15 minutes to 25 minutes.

### When does the job board unlock?

The job board appears later through Alberta the Owl and town progress. Do not plan the first few nights around jobs.

### What are Vampsters?

Vampsters are hidden collectible creatures. You carry them back to the cave near Orlock's mansion instead of putting them in normal inventory.

### Should I focus on Nokturna cards early?

No. Nokturna matters later, but it should not replace early unlock priorities like fishing, magic, the net, Misty Shores, and tools.

### Why is Misty Shores still blocked?

Misty Shores is tied to A Bridge Too Far and the cursed bridge route. Follow the story and character steps instead of trying to force the bridge.
