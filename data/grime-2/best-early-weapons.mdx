<h2 id="which-early-weapon-is-best-in-grime-ii">
  Which early weapon is best in GRIME II?
</h2>

If you want the safest answer instead of the flashiest one, the best early weapon in GRIME II is usually **Maul Axe**.

That does not mean the other early weapons are useless. It means Maul Axe asks less from your build while still giving you strong value immediately.

That matters a lot in the first several hours because GRIME II puts players in a very awkward early situation:

- enemies and bosses hit hard
- healing is restricted at first
- early respec resources are limited
- different weapons scale off different stat combinations

Because of that, the best early weapon is not just the one with the coolest moveset. It is the one that lets you survive, learn the game, and still do solid damage before your build has real flexibility.

<GuideCallout title="Best early-game rule">
If you are unsure which weapon to commit to first, stay with Maul Axe until you understand the game better.

It is much easier to branch out later than it is to fix an awkward early build.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="where-to-find-the-best-early-weapons-in-grime-ii">
  Where to find the best early weapons in GRIME II
</h2>

If you are comparing early weapons, location matters almost as much as power.

A weapon can be theoretically strong and still be a bad early recommendation if it shows up too late, asks too much from your build, or appears right when respec flexibility is still limited.

### Maul Axe location
You get the **Maul Axe** in **Birthplace**, where it appears as the first real weapon pickup before you push deeper into Temple of Hands.

### Knifehand location
You get **Knifehand** in **Temple of Hands**. From the Temple route, you climb through the upper section past early enemies and continue upward until you reach a higher platform where the weapon is waiting.

### Throwing Thumbs location
You get **Throwing Thumbs** from the **Temple of Hands – Halls** route. From the Halls Surrogate, head left, meet **Yearning Paint**, then climb the nearby ladder and collect the weapon from the upper-left platform.

<GuideCallout title="Why location matters">
Maul Axe is not just strong because of its stats.

It is strong because the game gives it to you immediately, before your build has enough room to support riskier weapon choices.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="why-maul-axe-is-the-best-early-weapon-in-grime-ii">
  Why Maul Axe is the best early weapon in GRIME II
</h2>

Maul Axe is the strongest early recommendation because it combines **range, stability, and growth** better than the faster alternatives.

### 1. It has the best early all-around value
Maul Axe does not need a fragile or specialized early build to feel useful.

That matters in GRIME II because the first several hours are awkward:

- healing is restricted
- enemies hit hard
- respec resources are limited
- different weapons scale off different stat combinations

### 2. Its range and stagger value are much safer early
A big reason Maul Axe feels stable is that you do not need to stand as close or commit as hard as you do with some faster options.

Its moveset gives you practical control and stronger early stagger pressure, which lowers the chance of getting trapped in bad trades.

### 3. Its special attack gives it real early-game value
Maul Axe’s special attack throws the axe forward, stops on the first enemy hit, and spins briefly in place.

That gives it a practical early pressure tool with real damage value, especially if you are already managing your Force well.

### 4. Its growth curve is smoother than the faster alternatives
One of Maul Axe’s biggest strengths is that it does not feel like a trap later just because you started with it.

It stays useful for long enough that early upgrades usually do not feel wasted.

Maul Axe is not just a good starter weapon.

It is the early weapon that asks the least from your build while still giving you strong practical value.

<h2 id="knifehand-vs-throwing-thumbs-vs-maul-axe-in-grime-ii">
  Knifehand vs Throwing Thumbs vs Maul Axe in GRIME II
</h2>

The easiest mistake here is assuming that the faster weapons are automatically better.

They are not.

### Maul Axe
**Best for:** most players, especially first-playthrough players

Maul Axe is the safest early all-rounder.  
It asks less from your build, has better practical range, and stays worth upgrading for longer.

### Knifehand
**Best for:** players who already know they want a faster melee style

Knifehand looks appealing because it is extremely fast, but that speed does not automatically make it better in the early game.

The problem is that fast weapons usually need more from your build before they feel efficient. If your damage stats and general survivability are still underdeveloped, a fast weapon can feel less rewarding than it should.

That makes Knifehand harder to recommend as your **first real commitment**, even if it becomes more attractive later.

### Throwing Thumbs
**Best for:** players who want a more specialized spacing-based weapon

Throwing Thumbs is one of the easiest early weapons to overestimate.

It is not weak in a vacuum. It is just much less stable for the average early-game player because:

- its base damage is lower
- it asks more from your positioning
- it is less forgiving when your spacing breaks down
- it feels worse if your build and survivability are still underdeveloped

That makes it interesting, but not the safest first choice.

### The real early-game difference
The difference is not just speed.

The real difference is how much each weapon asks from you **before your build is ready**.

- **Maul Axe** gives strong early value with low friction
- **Knifehand** gives speed, but wants more support before it feels worth it
- **Throwing Thumbs** asks for cleaner positioning and more discipline than most early players want

<GuideCallout title="Simple weapon rule">

<h2 id="what-stats-to-level-first-for-early-weapons-in-grime-ii">
  What stats to level first for early weapons in GRIME II
</h2>

If you are trying to make your early weapon choice feel better, the first thing to understand is this:

**your early stat priorities matter at least as much as the weapon itself.**

The safest early approach is to prioritize **Health first**, then start investing harder into the weapon stats that match the weapon you actually plan to keep.

A practical early rule is:

- get **Health to around 5**
- then invest more seriously into the damage stats for your chosen weapon
- do not respec casually just to test every new weapon you find

That approach is especially important because GRIME II weapons often scale off a **primary stat plus a smaller secondary stat**, and the smaller bonus is marked with a white plus sign in the weapon UI.

<h2 id="when-to-respec-and-when-to-upgrade-your-weapon-in-grime-ii">
  When to respec and when to upgrade your weapon in GRIME II
</h2>

This is where many early decisions go wrong.

### When to respec
Do **not** respec too casually in the first part of the game.

Early on, your respec flexibility matters more than your curiosity. It is better to test a weapon on weaker enemies first, then respec once you are sure it actually fits your playstyle.

### When to upgrade
You should be much less afraid to upgrade Maul Axe than to spend early respec flexibility.

That is because Maul Axe stays useful long enough that early upgrades usually feel justified, while an early respec can easily become regret.

That makes the early decision much simpler than many players expect:

- **respec carefully**
- **upgrade more confidently**

<GuideCallout title="Best upgrade rule">
In the early game, it is usually safer to upgrade Maul Axe than to burn respec flexibility on a weapon you are not sure about.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="who-should-not-stick-with-maul-axe-in-grime-ii">
  Who should not stick with Maul Axe in GRIME II?
</h2>

Maul Axe is the safest early answer, but that does not mean it is right for everyone.

### Players who want a huge heavy-weapon payoff
If your favorite feeling is landing a massive slow hit for huge burst damage, Maul Axe may feel too balanced instead of dramatic.

It is strong, but not in the “one giant punish solves everything” way some players want.

### Players who want a more specialized high-speed style
If you already know you dislike balanced all-round weapons and want something faster or more technical, Maul Axe may feel too safe and too general.

### Players building around a very different long-term direction
If you are already certain you want to push into a very different scaling path later, Maul Axe can still carry you early, but it may not be the weapon you want to commit to for long.

<GuideCallout title="What Maul Axe is really best at">
Maul Axe is best for players who want a strong, low-risk, early-game weapon that stays useful while the rest of the build is still unstable.
</GuideCallout>

### What is the best early weapon in GRIME II?

For most players, the best early weapon is Maul Axe because it offers the strongest mix of safety, damage, range, and early usability.

You get Knifehand in Temple of Hands, on an upper platform reached by climbing through the area’s upper route after pushing past the early enemy section.

You get Throwing Thumbs from the Temple of Hands – Halls route. After talking to Yearning Paint near the Halls Surrogate, climb the ladder and collect it from the upper-left platform.

Yes. Early Maul Axe upgrades are usually worth it because the weapon stays useful long enough to justify the investment.

You can, but it is usually less forgiving than Maul Axe early on. It becomes more appealing once your build has more room to support it.

It can work, but it is not the safest first commitment. It asks more from your positioning and feels less stable if your build is still weak.
