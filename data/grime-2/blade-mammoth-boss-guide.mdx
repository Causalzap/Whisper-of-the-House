<h2 id="why-blade-mammoth-is-worth-studying-in-grime-ii">
  Why Blade Mammoth is worth studying in GRIME II
</h2>

Not every GRIME II boss needs a full standalone guide.

Blade Mammoth does because it is one of the first fights that clearly punishes impatience instead of just weak stats or early confusion. The boss controls space well, pressures your timing, and becomes much less forgiving once Phase 2 starts expanding the attack coverage.

That is why this fight is worth studying properly:

- it uses wide attacks that take away space
- it punishes early dodges and bad counter timing
- it makes Force management matter
- it becomes much harder if you try to solve it with panic movement

This is not just a big boss.  
It is a boss that punishes players for assuming the first safe reaction is the whole answer.

<GuideCallout title="Best mindset for Blade Mammoth">
Do not think of Blade Mammoth as a “stay close and out-DPS it” fight.

Think of it as a boss that rewards clean survival first, then short punish windows second.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="grime-ii-blade-mammoth-boss-summary">
  GRIME II Blade Mammoth boss summary
</h2>

Blade Mammoth is a control fight built around three main problems:

- fast forward pressure
- heavy slam coverage
- a Phase 2 escalation where your old spacing becomes less reliable

The fight usually falls apart in the same way:

1. you dodge the first obvious hit
2. you assume the danger is over
3. you step back in too early and get caught by the follow-up

That is the rhythm you need to break.

### What this boss really tests

- whether you can wait for real commitment
- whether you can hold enough Force for real punishes
- whether you can stay calm once Phase 2 starts expanding the hitboxes

<GuideCallout title="What makes Blade Mammoth hard">
Blade Mammoth does not need a huge move list to be dangerous.

It only needs a few wide, punishing patterns that make players overreact.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="best-preparation-for-blade-mammoth-in-grime-ii">
  Best preparation for Blade Mammoth in GRIME II
</h2>

The best preparation for Blade Mammoth is not a gimmick.  
It is going into the fight with a clear rule for risk.

### 1. Use a weapon you can stop swinging quickly
A weapon that lets you take one or two hits and disengage is safer than one that traps you in long recovery.

### 2. Keep enough Force for your real openings
Do not spend too much Force on weak punishes.  
If you do that, the next real window becomes much less rewarding.

### 3. Expect the boss to punish early movement
A lot of deaths happen because players react to the red warning instead of the committed attack.

### 4. Decide your punish limit before the fight starts
A simple rule works well:

- one hit on uncertain openings
- two hits on obvious recovery
- no greed unless you already know the next pattern

<GuideCallout title="Simple preparation rule">
If you already decide “I am not going to greed,” Blade Mammoth becomes much easier.

That one rule fixes a lot of bad deaths by itself.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="grime-ii-blade-mammoth-main-attacks-and-how-to-handle-them">
  GRIME II Blade Mammoth main attacks and how to handle them
</h2>

The names below are descriptive names for clarity.

You do not need official move names to beat Blade Mammoth.  
You just need to recognize the patterns that actually matter.

### Red slam into ground wave
This is the most dangerous attack sequence in the fight.

Blade Mammoth raises one blade arm with a strong red warning, slams down, and sends a fast ground wave forward.

**Why it is dangerous:**

- the red telegraph makes players dodge too early
- the follow-up wave arrives fast
- close range gives you less time to recover
- surviving the slam does not mean the sequence is over

**How to handle it:**

- do not dash too early just because the arm is raised
- if you are close, prepare for the wave immediately after the slam
- the safest answer is usually to dodge the slam with intention, then react to the ground wave instead of rushing forward
- if you are still in front of the boss after the slam, be ready to jump or dash through the wave depending on your spacing

### Low-stance sweep
Blade Mammoth lowers its body and prepares a wide horizontal swing.

This is dangerous because it catches players who stand at comfortable-looking mid-range and assume they still have time.

**How to handle it:**

- treat the lowered posture as the real warning
- do not drift backward lazily and hope the range will miss
- if you are unsure, survive first and punish later

### Double-blade slam
This is one of Blade Mammoth’s clearest heavy-commitment attacks.

The boss brings both front limbs together and crushes the area in front of it.

**How to handle it:**

- do not panic backward by default
- move toward the actual safe side, not just away from the boss
- wait until the full danger ends before stepping back in

**Best punish window:**  
This is one of the safest places to punish Blade Mammoth in GRIME II. If you avoid the full slam cleanly, you usually have time for one strong punish or two fast hits.

### Burst roar / transition pressure
At transition moments, Blade Mammoth can create a large burst-like threat that pushes you out and breaks your rhythm.

**How to handle it:**

- respect the reset
- do not assume the visual pause means free damage
- use the moment to reset your spacing and Force, not to rush forward

<h2 id="grime-ii-blade-mammoth-phase-2-when-it-starts-and-how-to-handle-the-expanded-coverage">
  GRIME II Blade Mammoth Phase 2: when it starts and how to handle the expanded coverage
</h2>

### When does Blade Mammoth Phase 2 start in GRIME II?

Blade Mammoth Phase 2 seems to start at roughly 50% health.

The clearest sign is not the exact number on the health bar, but the change in how the fight behaves: attack coverage widens, spacing becomes less reliable, and the spin or extended sweep pressure starts becoming much harder to ignore.

Phase 2 is where Blade Mammoth starts feeling much worse for most players.

The biggest change is not just that the boss looks flashier.  
It is that the attack coverage expands enough that your Phase 1 spacing starts to fail.

### What changes in Blade Mammoth Phase 2

- sweeps look longer and feel wider
- old “safe” distances become less reliable
- panic movement gets punished much faster
- the arena starts feeling smaller because more space becomes unsafe at once

### The spin or extended sweep is the real panic check
This is the pattern that makes many attempts collapse.

If you overreact, you give up too much space and lose control.  
If you underreact, you get clipped by more of the sweep than you expected.

### Ground pressure matters more in Phase 2
Once the floor starts feeling less safe, you cannot retreat as comfortably.

That means your defensive choices have to stay cleaner, not faster.

<h2 id="best-punish-windows-against-blade-mammoth-in-grime-ii">
  Best punish windows against Blade Mammoth in GRIME II
</h2>

Blade Mammoth is beatable once you stop confusing “the boss has moved” with “the boss is punishable.”

Those are not the same thing.

### Safest punish window: after the full double-blade slam
This is one of the best places to punish in the whole fight.  
If you avoid the slam cleanly and do not rush the re-entry, you usually have time for a meaningful punish.

### Good punish window: after full commitment to a forward sequence
If Blade Mammoth clearly spends itself on a forward action and leaves itself extended, you can usually take a short punish.

### Situational punish window: after a fully resolved reset
Sometimes the best punish is not tied to one flashy attack.  
It comes after the boss finally ends a full sequence in a position you can trust.

<GuideCallout title="Best punish rule">
If you are not sure the punish is real, assume it is not.

Blade Mammoth gets much easier once you stop taking medium-value risks in bad space.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="most-common-blade-mammoth-mistakes-in-grime-ii">
  Most common Blade Mammoth mistakes in GRIME II
</h2>

### 1. Dodging the first hit but forgetting the follow-up
This is the biggest one.  
The slam is not the whole problem. The ground wave and follow-up coverage are what finish people off.

### 2. Emptying Force on bad openings
This makes later punishes weaker and turns good reads into low-value damage.

### 3. Treating dash-through as a universal answer
Sometimes it works.  
But Blade Mammoth covers too much space to reduce the whole fight to one automatic solution.

### 4. Panicking in Phase 2
Once the hitboxes expand and the floor feels less safe, panic movement creates more deaths than the boss’s raw damage does.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### How do you beat Blade Mammoth in GRIME II?

The safest way is to play for clean spacing, short punishes, and Force discipline. The fight gets much harder if you greed after the first safe-looking dodge.

### How do you dodge Blade Mammoth’s ground wave in GRIME II?

Do not focus only on the slam. The wave is part of the same sequence. If you are still in front after the slam, be ready to jump or dash based on your spacing instead of rushing back in.

### When does Blade Mammoth Phase 2 start in GRIME II?

It appears to begin at roughly half health. The clearest sign is that the attack coverage expands and your old spacing stops feeling as safe as it did in Phase 1.

### What is the safest punish window against Blade Mammoth?

The cleanest punish usually comes after the full double-blade slam recovery, not after the first visible impact.
