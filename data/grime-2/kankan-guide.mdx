<h2 id="what-to-do-first-after-entering-kankan-in-grime-ii">
  What to do first after entering Kankan in GRIME II
</h2>

The first thing to do after entering Kankan is **activate the city Surrogate**.

That matters because Kankan is the kind of area where players lose the thread quickly if they do not establish a safe reset point first.

Once that is done, your practical priorities are:

- stabilize the city route
- figure out where Breathsmith and Firstsmith fit into the area
- stop treating every NPC or side room as equal priority
- work toward the route that leads deeper into the city and then downward

A more practical early route looks like this:

- move into town and activate the Surrogate
- continue through the market layer instead of dropping into every side path
- talk to the NPCs tied to the Breathsmith / Firstsmith route instead of chasing every side conversation
- if your objective still says to find the Breathsmith, stay focused on the city thread instead of assuming the route is broken
- do not worry yet about solving every locked or interesting-looking path

If the city feels confusing immediately, that is normal. Kankan mixes progression, merchants, errands, and boss routing in a way that makes it feel larger than it really is.

<GuideCallout title="Best first rule for Kankan">
Do not try to solve all of Kankan at once.

Get the Surrogate first, then start following the smith-related route. That is the fastest way to stop the city from feeling random.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-to-find-breathsmith-and-firstsmith-in-kankan">
  How to find Breathsmith and Firstsmith in Kankan
</h2>

A lot of Kankan confusion comes from knowing **who** you are supposed to be looking for, but not **how** the city wants you to reach them.

The city thread is built around **Breathsmith** and **Firstsmith**, but you do not just walk straight to both of them immediately.

### Start in the Merchant’s Area
Once you are properly inside Kankan, head into the main city / merchant layer and start talking to the NPCs who actually move the smith route forward.

### Talk to Heimar first
If you are looking for Breathsmith, one of the cleanest ways to move the route forward is to speak to **Heimar** in the Merchant’s Area and ask about Breathsmith.

### Use the ladder beside Lak
After that, use the ladder beside **Lak** to reach the upper level. From there, go **left** to find the Breathsmith’s assistant.

### The assistant points you to the Porcelain Court
The assistant tells you that Breathsmith is being held in the **Porcelain Court** on the far right side of Kankan.

That is the big thing many players miss: “find Breathsmith” is not just a random flavor objective. It is the route prompt that stops Kankan from feeling like one long city detour.

### Firstsmith is tied to the city conflict
You do not fully resolve the Firstsmith line just by walking into town. Kankan wants you to deal with the city’s disorder first, especially the route pressure that eventually leads you toward Greatblade Preacher.

<GuideCallout title="How to read the Kankan city thread">
If you are still hearing “ask around to find the Breathsmith,” you are still in the city setup phase.

Do not assume you have missed the whole route. It usually just means you have not followed the right NPC chain far enough yet.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="where-tankard-warden-fits-into-the-kankan-route">
  Where Tankard Warden fits into the Kankan route
</h2>

Tankard Warden is one of the first real pressure points in Kankan.

The reason players remember this section is not just the boss. It is the fact that the lead-in can drain your run before the actual fight starts.

### Why this part trips people up
A lot of players are not really losing to Tankard Warden cleanly. They are reaching the boss in bad shape after the approach has already eaten too much health, Force, or focus.

That means the real problems are usually:

- arriving with poor resource control
- burning healing too early
- letting the route before the boss tilt the run
- treating the whole sequence like a simple duel instead of a boss plus setup check

### Practical Tankard Warden tips
If this part keeps stopping your run, the fastest fix is to make the whole sequence cleaner instead of only focusing on the boss.

- keep the pre-fight section as clean as possible
- do not burn too much just to rush the approach
- stay at a controlled mid-range instead of constantly drifting into panic distance
- use the boss’s larger, more committed attacks as your parry or punish learning windows
- do not spend the entire fight trying to out-trade it face-to-face

### Why this matters for the route
Tankard Warden sits in the exact kind of place where players can lose confidence in the whole city route. If you keep arriving in poor shape, Kankan starts feeling worse than it really is.

<h2 id="how-to-reach-greatblade-preacher-in-lower-kankan">
  How to reach Greatblade Preacher in Lower Kankan
</h2>

Greatblade Preacher is the point where Kankan finally stops feeling vague and starts behaving like a proper progression route.

The cleanest route is:

### 1. After Tankard Warden, go back to the Ground Floor and head right
Once Firstsmith Malik tells you to deal with the Greatblade Preacher in Lower Kankan, return to the main city floor and head right until you find the elevator down.

### 2. At the bottom, go left toward the lower city route
You can check the small pickups on the right first, but the real progression continues to the **left**, through the lower route.

### 3. Go up and left to activate the Savior Shrine Surrogate
Before pushing too deep, go up first and left to unlock the Surrogate. This is the safest reset point for the Lower Kankan route.

### 4. From the Surrogate, head right and drop down to the lower path
Take the lower route you saw earlier. Keep going through the enemy-heavy section until you reach the **Depth Traversal door** at the far end and go through it.

### 5. Keep advancing until you reach Ver
After the Depth Traversal section and the following climb, keep moving until you meet **Ver**. She tells you directly that to reach Greatblade Preacher, you need to go **left and up**.

### 6. Go left past Ver and enter the second Depth Traversal door
This is the important progression gate. Go through it, clear the enemies on the other side, then use the route on the right to begin the upward climb.

### 7. Climb to the top-right, then continue into the boss route
Once you have made the upper climb, keep pushing through the route until you reach the Greatblade Preacher arena.

### What is the traversal gate on this route?
The major progression gate here is a **Depth Traversal door**.

The safest way to recognize it is that it looks like a special traversal-linked doorway rather than a normal wall or ordinary side passage. If you reach one and cannot use it yet, do not assume you are off-route. It usually means you are still missing the required progression state or traversal requirement for that specific door.

### Why Greatblade Preacher matters
This is not just another boss.

It is the point where Kankan’s noisy middle section resolves into something much cleaner. Once Greatblade Preacher is done, you can return with a real sense of direction instead of still wondering whether you missed the actual path.

### Practical Greatblade Preacher route rule
If your goal is Greatblade Preacher, stop treating every Lower Kankan side room as equally important.

The safest path is:

- get down into Lower Kankan
- keep the leftward route
- take the real traversal gate
- climb upward into the boss approach

<GuideCallout title="Best Greatblade Preacher route rule">
If you are in Lower Kankan and still feel lost, simplify the question.

Do not ask, “What can I do in this whole lower area?”  
Ask, “Am I still following the lower route leftward, then upward, toward the second Depth Traversal door and the climb after it?”
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-to-do-if-you-get-lost-or-locked-out-of-kankan">

Kankan is one of those areas where confusion often feels bigger than the actual route problem.

These are the three biggest places players tend to lose the thread.

### 1. The city entry interaction
One of the easiest ways to get confused is forgetting how the city entry interaction actually works.

If you leave, die, or return later, the route back into Kankan can feel less obvious than it should. If the city suddenly seems inaccessible again, walk back to the major entry interaction and re-establish the route directly instead of assuming the map alone will solve it.

### 2. Lower Kankan feeling too open
This is where players start checking too many side rooms, lose the main thread, and convince themselves they missed some huge required step.

The safer answer is usually much simpler: keep following the route that clearly trends toward the boss objective.

### 3. Mistaking every blocked path for the immediate answer
Kankan teaches bad habits because it presents many interesting-looking routes at once. A blocked route or gated opening does not automatically mean “this is the thing I must solve right now.”
