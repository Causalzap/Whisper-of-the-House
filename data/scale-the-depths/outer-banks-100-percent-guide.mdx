To get **100% in Outer Banks**, collect **5 messages in bottles**, **6 artifacts**, **2 baits**, **2 cosmetics**, the **boat blueprint**, unlock the shortcuts, activate both **Blue Switches**, then catch and prep **Lost Dimetrodon**.

The most important hidden pickup is **Sonar Receiver**. If you are missing one artifact, jump to the Sonar Receiver section below and check the route before the southwestern path shortcut.

If **Point Nemo is still grey or locked**, recheck Outer Banks before assuming a bug. The usual blockers are the boat blueprint, Sonar Receiver, Spring bait, Spanish Doubloon or Lost Dimetrodon prep.

<h2 id="before-you-start">
  Before you start Outer Banks cleanup
</h2>

Outer Banks is not only a puzzle route. Several pickups are blocked by **line length, route efficiency and fish-fight time**, so if a side pocket or late item feels impossible to reach, your setup is usually the problem.

For a clean 100% run, prioritize line length first. If **Industrial Rod V6** is available in the shop, buy it before spending everything on knife upgrades. The extra reach matters more for Outer Banks cleanup than faster prep, especially when you are trying to reach late bottles, the boat blueprint and hidden side pockets.

caption="Recommended Outer Banks cleanup setup."

rows={gearRows}
/>

A better knife still helps, but it should not be your first answer to every problem. If you cannot reach the route, upgrade the rod. If Lost Dimetrodon takes too long to catch, upgrade the hook. If prep is slow after the catch, upgrade the knife.

<h2 id="lost-dimetrodon">
  How to catch Lost Dimetrodon
</h2>

**Lost Dimetrodon** is the Outer Banks legendary fish. You can spot its route during the middle section, but you should save the real catch and prep for the end.

If you still struggle after upgrading your hook and line, do not force the catch from a bad angle. Leave the dive, return through the cleanest shortcut route, and start the attempt with as much line and empty storage as possible.

Before trying it, make sure:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>You have enough line left to follow the fish without rushing.</li>
  <li>Your hook is upgraded enough that the fight does not drag on forever.</li>
  <li>Your storage is not full of random fish.</li>
  <li>You already collected the baits, artifacts, cosmetics, switches and boat blueprint.</li>
</ul>

When you reach Lost Dimetrodon, aim ahead of its movement instead of chasing every turn from behind. If you keep running out of line or dive time before landing the catch, leave, upgrade line length or hook damage, then come back.

If you still struggle after upgrading your hook and line, do not force the catch from a bad angle. Leave the dive, return through the cleanest shortcut route, and start the attempt with as much line and empty storage as possible.

### Why is Outer Banks not showing 100%?

You are probably missing a hidden or late-route item instead of a normal fish. See the <Link href="#final-check">troubleshooting table</Link> for detailed guidance, especially Sonar Receiver, Spring bait, Spanish Doubloon, cosmetics, the boat blueprint and Lost Dimetrodon prep.

### How many messages are in Outer Banks?

Outer Banks has **5 messages in bottles**. If your message count is incomplete, rerun the opening route, the blue-switch route and the late boat-blueprint route.

### Where is the Sonar Receiver?

The Sonar Receiver is before the southwestern path shortcut, after the first cosmetic and before the Jar of Dirt / Canned Bread chain. Clear the full bush pocket in that extra-line section.

### Where is the first cosmetic in Outer Banks?

The first cosmetic is after the Lost Dimetrodon route marker and before the Sonar Receiver bush pocket. If you reached Sonar Receiver without collecting a cosmetic, backtrack through that section.

### Why can’t I reach some Outer Banks pickups?

You probably need more line length. Prioritize rod upgrades for cleanup, especially Industrial Rod V6 when it becomes available, before assuming you missed a puzzle step.

### Does Lost Dimetrodon count as done when I catch it?

No. Catch it, then finish the prep step. If you only catch it and leave, your legendary fish progress may still feel incomplete.

### Why is Point Nemo still grey or locked?

Recheck Outer Banks completion. The boat blueprint, Sonar Receiver and Lost Dimetrodon prep are the first things to verify before assuming Point Nemo is bugged.

### Should I catch Lost Dimetrodon as soon as I find it?

Usually no. Find the route early, but save the real catch for the end after you clear baits, artifacts, cosmetics, switches and the boat blueprint.
