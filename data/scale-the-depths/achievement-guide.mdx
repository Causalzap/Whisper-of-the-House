<h2 id="perfect-prep">
  Perfection and Prep Expert
</h2>

**Perfection!** is only your first perfect fish. **Prep Expert** is the hard version: you need to perfectly prep every fish in the game. It is also one of the rarest achievements, so treat it as a full-run tracking task instead of a last-minute cleanup.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>

For Prep Expert:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Perfectly prep new species as soon as you catch them.</li>
  <li>Slow down during scale removal instead of scraping too aggressively.</li>
  <li>Move calmly when removing parasites so they do not hide repeatedly.</li>
  <li>Upgrade knives before trying to perfect larger fish.</li>
  <li>Be extra careful with legendary fish because repeating the catch can take time.</li>
</ul>

Do not save perfect prep for the very end if you can avoid it. It is much easier to track species as you clear each location.

<h2 id="customer-achievements">
  Customer achievements and variants
</h2>

Customer achievements are not just about feeding whoever appears first. You need normal customers, liked-fish progress and eventually customer variants.

A **customer variant** means an alternate or special version of a customer that still needs to be found and fed for **Elusive Customer Entertainer**. Treat variants as a late cleanup task after your normal customer counts are stable, because it is easier to spot what is missing once the regular customer list is mostly done.

<h2 id="deep-pockets">
  Deep Pockets and shop upgrades
</h2>

**Deep Pockets** asks you to get every shop upgrade in the game. This is easy to underestimate because upgrades feel like normal progression early on, but the full buyout becomes one of the rare completion achievements.

Do not treat Deep Pockets as your first cleanup goal. Buy upgrades that unlock routes first, then come back for the full shop buyout once the location data is under control.

Recommended approach:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Buy rod / line upgrades first</strong> when a location still has deep routes, hidden pockets or legendary fish access.</li>
  <li><strong>Buy hook upgrades</strong> before serious legendary fish attempts.</li>
  <li><strong>Buy knife upgrades</strong> before perfect-prep cleanup on larger fish.</li>
  <li><strong>Buy storage and utility upgrades</strong> when repeated cleanup runs are taking too long.</li>
  <li><strong>Do the final buyout near the end</strong> after you know which areas still need work.</li>
</ul>

Because shop prices vary by area and upgrade tier, treat **Deep Pockets** as a final buyout achievement instead of trying to finish it during the main route. Spend money first on upgrades that unlock routes or make legendary fish safer, then return for anything left in the shop once your location cleanup is nearly done.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How long does it take to 100% Scale the Depths?

The main route usually takes around **8–12 hours**. Full achievement cleanup is closer to **20–30 hours**, depending on how much you track during each location.

### What are the hardest achievements in Scale the Depths?

The hardest achievements are usually **Prep Expert**, **True Fisherbot**, **Fashion Fan**, **Decal Delighter**, **Elusive Customer Entertainer**, **Master of Bait**, **Boat Collector**, **Deep Pockets**, **Elite Explorer** and the later legendary fish/customer achievements.

### How do I unlock True Fisherbot?

Catch every fish in the game. Use each location guide and do not leave an area if fish entries are still missing.

### How do I unlock Prep Expert?

Perfectly prep every fish in the game. Prep new species carefully as you find them instead of saving all perfect prep for the end.

### How do I unlock Customer Connoisseur?

Feed every customer in the game. Keep checking location customer counts and do not stop feeding just because the next location unlocks.

### What are customer variants?

Customer variants are alternate or special versions of customers. They count separately for **Elusive Customer Entertainer**, so finish normal customers first, then cycle customers again and feed any variant appearances you have not handled.

### How do I unlock Master of Bait?

Obtain every bait in the game. Track bait pickups inside each location route.

### How do I unlock Boat Collector?

Collect every boat in the game. Pick up every boat blueprint during location cleanup.

### How do I unlock Decal Delighter?

Get every decal in the game. Do not skip boat decal pickups while cleaning messages and artifacts.

### How do I unlock Fashion Fan?

Get every character cosmetic. Cosmetics look optional, but they count toward achievement cleanup.

### How do I unlock Elite Explorer?

Get all data in every level. That means cleaning up every location's messages, artifacts, treasures, secrets and other tracked data.

### How do I unlock Deep Pockets?

Buy every shop upgrade in the game. Treat it as a late cleanup achievement: buy route-enabling upgrades first, then finish the full shop buyout when location cleanup is nearly done.

### Do I need legendary fish for 100%?

Yes. Each region has a legendary fish achievement, and each also has a legendary customer achievement.

### Should I catch legendary fish as soon as I find them?

Usually no. Treat legendary fish as final route targets. Open shortcuts, clear nearby collectibles and upgrade hook / line first, then catch and prep them in a clean run.

### Why did I finish the story but still miss achievements?

The story can finish before 100% completion. You probably still need location data, fish, perfect prep, customers, variants, baits, boats, decals, cosmetics or shop upgrades.

### What should I do first after finishing the main game?

Start with location cleanup. Finish Loch Ness, Outer Banks, Huatulco and Point Nemo 100% first, because that also progresses Elite Explorer, Master of Bait, Boat Collector, Fashion Fan, Decal Delighter, True Fisherbot and legendary achievements.
