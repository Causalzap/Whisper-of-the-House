<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Prestige in Fracture Field has two layers: World Fracture and Reality Shatter.**

Use **World Fracture** when your current run slows down and your pending **Core Fragments** can buy useful permanent upgrades. As a practical first-reset benchmark, **30+ Core Fragments** is a good target, while **under 20** often feels weak.

Use **Reality Shatter** later, after you can earn enough **Reality Shards** to buy several strong upgrades. As a community-and-upgrade-cost benchmark, **200–500 Reality Shards** is workable for a first serious shatter, while **500–1,000+** usually feels smoother.

As of **v1.1.0**, Reality Shatter is much less punishing than older guides suggest: **Drone Hub stays unlocked, drone rules are preserved, Drone upgrade levels reset, and Quarry Foreman fully persists with upgrade levels and automation settings.**
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="how-prestige-works">
  How prestige works in Fracture Field
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Fracture Field prestige systems at a glance."
  columns={prestigeOverviewColumns}
  rows={prestigeOverviewRows}
/>

Fracture Field is built around repeated escalation. You push into a new rock layer, hit a wall, reset, buy permanent upgrades, and return faster.

The two prestige systems do different jobs:

- **World Fracture** is the reset you use often.
- **Reality Shatter** is the deeper reset you use when Reality Shards can buy real long-term power.

For the full route from early upgrades to late-game cleanup, start with the <Link href="/fracture-field">Fracture Field Complete Walkthrough & Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="world-fracture-explained">
  World Fracture explained
</h2>

World Fracture resets your current run and gives **Core Fragments**. Core Fragments are earned by gaining resources from **Sandstone or higher**.

This is the first point where Fracture Field stops being about one long run and becomes a reset loop. A good World Fracture should make the next run faster through stronger damage, better Pierce, improved rock economy, stronger drones, or better automation.

The mechanism is simple:

1. Push into Sandstone, Quartz, or deeper layers.
2. Earn a Core Fragment reward.
3. Reset with World Fracture.
4. Spend Core Fragments on permanent upgrades.
5. Return to the same wall faster.

---

<h2 id="when-to-world-fracture-first">
  When to World Fracture for the first time
</h2>

**You should usually World Fracture for the first time when Quartz or the next layer becomes slow and you can reach around 30+ Core Fragments.**

Use this as a benchmark, not a hard rule:

- **Under 20 Core Fragments:** usually weak unless you are experimenting.
- **20–30 Core Fragments:** usable if the run is already slow.
- **30+ Core Fragments:** common first-reset target.
- **Much higher than 30:** fine if your run is still accelerating, but do not wait forever.

The first World Fracture is a tempo reset. It does not need to be perfect. It only needs to make your second run faster.

<GuideCallout title="First World Fracture rule">
Reset when the next 10–20 minutes would only add a small reward, but the Core Fragment upgrades you can buy now would noticeably speed up the next run.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="reality-shatter-explained">
  Reality Shatter explained
</h2>

Reality Shatter is the second prestige layer. It gives **Reality Shards**, which are earned from **Silver or higher** resources.

Reality Shatter is not just a stronger World Fracture. It is a deeper reset loop, and the early version of the game made it feel very punishing. v1.1.0 softened the pain by preserving Drone Hub and Quarry Foreman, but Reality Shatter still resets enough progress that you should use it with a plan.

<h2 id="what-do-you-lose-after-reality-shatter">
  What do you lose after Reality Shatter in v1.1.0?
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="What you lose and keep after Reality Shatter in Fracture Field v1.1.0."
  columns={loseKeepColumns}
  rows={loseKeepRows}
/>

The most important current-version answer is:

**You keep Drone Hub unlocked and you keep Quarry Foreman automation. Drone upgrade levels reset, but Quarry Foreman upgrade levels and automation settings are retained.**

This is the biggest difference between v1.1.0 and older launch-version advice. Reality Shatter is still a major reset, but it no longer destroys the core automation setup that made many players quit.

<GuideCallout title="Outdated guide warning">
If an older Fracture Field guide tells you that Reality Shatter removes Drone Hub or forces you to rebuild Quarry Foreman automation from scratch, it is probably using pre-v1.1.0 information.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="when-to-reality-shatter-first">
  When to Reality Shatter for the first time
</h2>

**Do your first Reality Shatter when the reward buys several useful upgrades, not when the button first appears.**

Use this rough range:

- **Under 100 Reality Shards:** usually too early.
- **100–200 Reality Shards:** possible, but often weak.
- **200–500 Reality Shards:** workable if your run is heavily stalled.
- **500–1,000+ Reality Shards:** much smoother for a first serious shatter.
- **Beyond 1,000:** good if you can still earn shards quickly before resetting.

The right moment is usually after Silver or Gold slows down and another World Fracture no longer gives enough progress.

<h2 id="achievement-warning-before-prestige-upgrades">
  Achievement warning before Reality Shatter upgrades
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Prestige upgrades that can make some Fracture Field achievements harder."
  columns={achievementWarningColumns}
  rows={achievementWarningRows}
/>

The biggest warning is capacity achievements.

Some achievements ask you to max the capacity upgrade for a specific rock, such as Gold. If you buy Reality Shatter quarry upgrades that increase the maximum number of rocks on the field, the number of capacity upgrades required can increase too.

If you care about 100% completion, finish important capacity achievements before heavily investing in quarry expansion upgrades.

<GuideCallout title="Achievement safety rule">
Before buying Quarry Expansion Mk. II or similar rock-capacity upgrades, check whether you still need capacity achievements for Gold or other layers.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="drone-hub-and-prestige">
  Drone Hub and prestige timing
</h2>

Drone Hub is now a complete release-version system, not just a demo preview. It matters for prestige because automation changes how quickly you can recover after each reset.

The current-version rules are simple:

- Drone Hub stays unlocked after Reality Shatter.
- Drone rules are preserved.
- Drone upgrade levels reset.
- Drone Core earnings resume immediately.
- Quarry Foreman fully persists, so automation recovery is much smoother.

That means your post-shatter goal is not to re-unlock Drone Hub. Your goal is to rebuild drone power quickly through upgrades and let automation resume the loop.

Shatter Drones and Collector Drones are especially important because they solve different problems: Shatter Drones help clear tough clusters, while Collector Drones reduce manual cleanup and keep the economy moving.

<h2 id="deep-layer-prestige-timing">
  Deep-layer prestige timing: Stone, Sandstone, Gold, Obsidian, Primal, and Chronite
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="How deeper layers change prestige timing in Fracture Field."
  columns={deepLayerColumns}
  rows={deepLayerRows}
/>

Sandstone starts the Core Fragment path, but that does not mean you should reset immediately. The first World Fracture usually feels better once the reward is large enough to buy visible power.

Silver unlocks Reality Shard generation, but the deeper layers decide whether Reality Shatter snowballs.

Gold through Chronite received deeper-layer health and hardness adjustments in v1.1.0, so late-game walls are not just bugs or bad routing. You need enough Mk. II scaling, rock economy, Pierce, and hardness handling to make those layers farmable.

Once you can consistently break Chronite, fixed shard benchmarks matter less. At that point, reset timing should be based on upgrade costs: shatter when the next reset buys the next meaningful level.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Is 30 Core Fragments enough for the first World Fracture?

Yes, **30+ Core Fragments** is a good practical benchmark for the first World Fracture. Under 20 often feels weak, while 20–30 can work if the run has clearly stalled.

### How many Reality Shards should I have before my first Reality Shatter?

For a first serious Reality Shatter, **200–500 Reality Shards** is workable and **500–1,000+** usually feels smoother. Under 100 is usually too early unless you are testing.

### Does Reality Shatter reset Drone Hub or Quarry Foreman?

No. In v1.1.0, Drone Hub stays unlocked, configured drone rules are preserved, and Quarry Foreman fully persists with upgrade levels and automation settings. Drone upgrade levels still reset.

### Which Reality Shatter upgrades should I buy first?

Prioritize high-impact scaling such as **Fractured Mind Mk. II**, **Geologist's Insight Mk. II**, **Core Saturation Mk. II**, Dust or rock economy, and then Drone Hub support.

### Can prestige upgrades make achievements harder?

Yes. Quarry expansion upgrades can make capacity achievements harder by increasing the maximum rock count. If you want 100% completion, finish important capacity achievements before heavily expanding quarry capacity.

### Should I follow older Reality Shatter guides?

Be careful. Any guide written before v1.1.0 may be wrong about Drone Hub and Quarry Foreman persistence. Always check whether the guide mentions the v1.1.0 Reality Shatter changes.
