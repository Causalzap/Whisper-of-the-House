<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Fracture Field progression is about knowing whether a new layer is farmable, not just whether it is unlocked.**

The full layer route is:

**Stone → Clay → Sandstone → Quartz → Iron → Silver → Gold → Diamond → Obsidian → Primal → Star Metal → Void Stone → Chronite**

The main walls are:

- **Quartz:** first reset check
- **Iron:** first Pierce / Hardness check
- **Silver:** automation and Reality Shard entry
- **Gold:** deep value wall
- **Obsidian:** late-game gate
- **Primal → Chronite:** Reality Shatter snowball phase

If a layer barely takes damage, check **Pierce vs Hardness**. If it breaks but upgrades are slow, improve **economy and Local Mastery**. If the quarry is full, fix **space control and drone targeting**. If the run is flat, reset around the next World Fracture or Reality Shatter purchase that will clearly speed up the next run.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="progression-route">
  Fracture Field progression route
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Complete Fracture Field layer route, wall type, and upgrade focus."
  columns={layerColumns}
  rows={layerRows}
/>

Fracture Field is not a straight line where every new layer should be unlocked immediately. A layer is only useful when it improves your income, reset value, or next upgrade path.

Use this route:

1. stabilize the current layer
2. unlock the next layer
3. test whether the new layer is farmable
4. identify the wall type
5. buy the matching upgrade category
6. reset if the next prestige purchase beats waiting

Sandstone starts Core Fragment generation. Silver starts Reality Shard generation. Gold through Chronite are tuned around deeper Reality Shatter scaling, so late-game walls should be handled with prestige upgrades, not only local grinding.

For reset benchmarks, use the <Link href="/fracture-field/prestige-guide">Fracture Field Prestige Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="why-new-layers-feel-impossible">
  Why new layers feel impossible at first
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Why you get stuck in Fracture Field and what each type of wall means."
  columns={stuckReasonColumns}
  rows={stuckReasonRows}
/>

A new layer can feel impossible for different reasons.

If the health bar barely moves, you are probably facing **damage falloff** from low Pierce against high Hardness.

If the layer breaks but upgrades take forever, you have an **economy stall**.

If useful rocks stop appearing or the field feels clogged, you may have a **quarry space** problem.

If drones keep attacking the wrong layer, you have a **targeting** problem.

The right fix depends on which problem you actually have.

<GuideCallout title="Layer unlock rule">
A layer is not truly unlocked when the button appears. It is unlocked when you can break it fast enough to improve your income, reset value, or next upgrade path.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="pierce-vs-hardness">
  Pierce vs Hardness explained
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Core progression mechanics behind Fracture Field layer walls."
  columns={mechanicColumns}
  rows={mechanicRows}
/>

**Pierce vs Hardness is the main reason Iron and later layers feel different from early rocks.**

When your Pierce is far below a layer's Hardness, your damage suffers heavy falloff. The v1.0.1 update softened that falloff, so Iron and similar walls are less punishing than launch, but Pierce is still the stat to check when the health bar barely moves.

Use this rule:

- if the layer takes damage but feels slow, buy Fracture and economy
- if the layer barely takes damage, buy Pierce and hardness tools
- if the layer is farmable but inefficient, buy Local Mastery and automation
- if none of those upgrades are affordable, reset

<h2 id="drone-hub-setup">
  Drone Hub setup for progression walls
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Basic Drone Hub setup by progression stage."
  columns={droneSetupColumns}
  rows={droneSetupRows}
/>

Drone Hub does not become powerful just because it is unlocked. It becomes powerful when your drone slots, targeting rules, and drone roles match the wall you are solving.

Before Silver, drones are support. Let them clean lower layers, collect resources, or help your current farmable layer while your strongest damage focuses the wall.

Around Silver and Gold, drones become part of the main engine. Shatter Drones help with clustered or tough layers, Collector Drones keep resources moving, and Supervisor support reduces downtime.

At Obsidian and later, your fleet should not blindly attack the deepest unlocked layer. The best target is the deepest layer that your current setup can damage efficiently.

<h2 id="quartz-and-first-reset">
  Quartz wall: when to reset
</h2>

Quartz is usually the first point where Fracture Field stops feeling like a simple clicker and starts feeling like a prestige game.

Do not reset the moment Sandstone appears. Sandstone starts Core Fragment generation, but the first World Fracture usually feels better once the reward can buy visible power.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to decide whether to reset at Quartz or keep pushing."
  columns={resetDecisionColumns}
  rows={resetDecisionRows}
/>

A practical reset test is:

1. **What can I buy immediately after resetting?**
2. **Will another 5–10 minutes change that purchase?**
3. **Is my deepest layer farmable or only technically unlocked?**

If resetting buys a key upgrade and another short push does not change the answer, World Fracture is usually better than waiting.

For exact reset numbers, use the <Link href="/fracture-field/prestige-guide">Fracture Field Prestige Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="iron-wall">
  Iron wall: how to break through
</h2>

Iron is the first real mechanics check.

If Iron barely takes damage, do not keep buying only raw Fracture. Iron is where the game starts testing whether your Pierce and reset upgrades can handle higher Hardness.

To break Iron:

1. add Pierce or hardness tools
2. keep Fracture high enough to support that Pierce
3. improve World Fracture upgrades if local costs stall
4. use drones to clear lower layers instead of wasting all attention on Iron
5. reset if Core Fragment gain is more useful than another slow Iron push

<h2 id="silver-wall">
  Silver wall: automation and Reality Shards
</h2>

Silver is where Reality Shards begin, but it is also where manual pushing starts to feel inefficient.

The jump into Silver can be sharp. If your drones, Quarry Foreman, and World Fracture upgrades are weak, Silver may technically unlock before it is actually farmable.

If Silver is too slow, focus on:

- Drone Hub slots and role balance
- Quarry Foreman automation
- Fracture and Pierce
- Core Fragment generation
- repeat World Fracture value

Reality Shatter begins here, but a tiny shard reward is usually not enough. Use Silver to build the scaling that makes the first serious Reality Shatter worthwhile.

<h2 id="gold-wall">
  Gold wall: deep value check
</h2>

Gold is where your first-layer prestige route starts to show whether it is strong enough.

If Gold is unlocked but barely farmable, the problem is rarely one missing upgrade. It is usually your whole scaling stack: World Fracture, Local Mastery, Drone Hub, automation, and Reality Shatter preparation.

<h2 id="obsidian-wall">
  Obsidian wall: the late-game gate
</h2>

Obsidian is the late-game wall that makes earlier layers feel misleading.

You may reach a point where Diamond breaks instantly, but Obsidian still barely takes damage. That usually means your late-layer multipliers are not ready yet.

Obsidian asks for:

- stronger Reality Shatter upgrades
- more Pierce and hardness handling
- better rock economy
- advanced Drone Hub support
- bomb usage for starts, clusters, or hardness support
- repeated resets for Mk. II scaling

Do not assume that easy Diamond means easy Obsidian. Obsidian is the gate into Primal, Star Metal, Void Stone, and Chronite progression.

---

<h2 id="primal-star-metal-void-stone-chronite">
  Primal, Star Metal, Void Stone, and Chronite progression
</h2>

After Obsidian, progression becomes less about one clean wall and more about Reality Shatter snowballing.

Primal matters more after v1.1.0 because its rock layer multipliers were buffed. Gold through Chronite also received health and hardness increases to preserve the depth of the Reality Shatter system, so late layers require real Mk. II scaling instead of only time.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the full Fracture Field layer order?

The full recommended layer sequence is **Stone → Clay → Sandstone → Quartz → Iron → Silver → Gold → Diamond → Obsidian → Primal → Star Metal → Void Stone → Chronite**.

### Why am I stuck at Iron in Fracture Field?

Iron is the first serious Pierce and Hardness check. If the bar barely moves, buy Pierce, hardness tools, Fracture, and World Fracture upgrades instead of only waiting longer.

### How much Pierce do I need for each layer?

Use the Quarry Stats panel in your current version to compare Pierce against the layer's Hardness. Exact thresholds can shift with patches and upgrades, so avoid relying on old static numbers.

### Why does my quarry stop progressing even when my damage is good?

Your quarry may be full or badly targeted. Use bombs, adjust drones, and make sure the field is cycling useful rocks instead of clogging.

### Which drones should I use for progression?

Before Silver, use **Hammer → Collector → Shatter if needed**. Around Silver and Gold, use **Shatter → Collector → Supervisor**. In late game, add **Shatter → Pierce → Boost → Sentry / filler** once you have enough slots.

### When exactly should I do my first Reality Shatter?

Do your first Reality Shatter when Silver, Gold, or a deeper layer has slowed down and the shard reward can buy several meaningful upgrades. As a practical benchmark, under 100 Reality Shards is usually early, 200–500 is workable, and 500–1,000+ is smoother.

### Why is Gold so slow?

Gold is a deep value wall. It tests your World Fracture routing, Local Mastery, Drone Hub scaling, and Reality Shatter preparation.

### Is Obsidian bugged?

Usually no. Obsidian is meant to be a major late-game gate, although early versions had rougher scaling. Check your version, then improve Pierce, Mk. II scaling, bombs, drones, and rock economy.

### How do I reach Chronite?

Build Reality Shatter scaling, improve Pierce and rock economy, push through Obsidian, Primal, Star Metal, and Void Stone, then reset around upgrade breakpoints instead of fixed layer milestones.
