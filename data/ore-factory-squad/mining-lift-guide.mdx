<h2 id="where-to-buy">
  Where I found the Mining Lift and what it costs
</h2>

I first used the Mining Lift during my **Level 3 progression**.

At that point, its components were available at the dig site through the
**Mine & Lift** section of the equipment selection:

- **Lift Head** starts the route at the surface;
- **Lift Extension** continues the structure downward.

This confirms when the equipment was available in my run. It does not prove
that reaching Level 3 alone is the exact unlock trigger in every save.

<GuideTable
  caption="Tested Mining Lift component costs and example Extension totals."
  columns={liftCostColumns}
  rows={liftCostRows}
/>

The component price was not the main obstacle.

The larger cost was the time needed to:

- clear a vertical shaft;
- leave enough room around the ladder;
- avoid underground electrical obstacles;
- and test every new Extension from both directions.

I do not buy a large stack of Extensions before the Head and first connection
are working.

If the Lift components are not visible yet, I continue the active tutorial and
level progression, then check the **Mine & Lift** section again rather than
assuming the marketplace sells the structure.

<h2 id="when-worth-building">
  When is the Mining Lift worth building?
</h2>

I build the Lift when repeated vertical travel has become the mining
bottleneck.

Before placing it, I check:

<div className="my-5 rounded-xl border border-slate-200 bg-slate-50 px-5 py-4 shadow-sm">
  <div className="font-mono text-sm leading-7 text-slate-700">
    Warehouse computer → Properties → Active Property → Remaining Resources
  </div>
</div>

<GuideTable
  caption="When I build a permanent Mining Lift and when I continue using a temporary ramp."
  columns={liftDecisionColumns}
  rows={liftDecisionRows}
/>

The Lift is strongest when the same property still contains several useful
resources at depth.

A **$35** starting setup is inexpensive, but it is not automatically efficient.
A badly placed Lift can require more excavation and repair time than the
purchase price suggests.

<h2 id="place-lift-head">
  Where to place the Lift Head
</h2>

I place the Lift Head along the **edge of the property**, not in the middle of
the area I intend to excavate.

A good position provides:

- diggable terrain directly below;
- open space on the ladder side;
- room to approach the Head from the surface;
- space for transferred mining boxes to appear nearby;
- a short route toward the truck;
- and distance from obvious electrical obstacles.

### Check the ladder side before excavating

The Lift Head is not symmetrical from a route-planning perspective.

The attached ladder occupies one side of the structure. I rotate the Head so
that side faces the area where I intend to create:

- the ladder approach;
- the first underground landing;
- and the shaft below.

A Head can place successfully while still creating a bad route.

I reposition it early when:

- the ladder faces a wall;
- the ladder faces the property boundary;
- an electrical obstacle blocks the first section;
- there is no room to step onto or away from the ladder;
- or the surface cargo area would be difficult to reach.

I could move a short Lift setup during my run.

I did not verify whether moving a completed deep structure carries every
Extension with it or returns the full purchase cost, so I correct a bad Head
position before extending far underground.

<h2 id="add-extensions">
  How to add Lift Extensions
</h2>

I build the shaft one tested section at a time:

<div className="my-5 rounded-xl border border-slate-200 bg-slate-50 px-5 py-4 shadow-sm">
  <div className="space-y-1 font-mono text-sm leading-7 text-slate-700">
    <div>Clear one vertical section</div>
    <div>→ Place one Lift Extension</div>
    <div>→ Clear the ladder face and landing</div>
    <div>→ Climb through the new connection</div>
    <div>→ Climb back to the previous level</div>
    <div>→ Add the next Extension</div>
  </div>
</div>

This exposes a blocked section immediately.

If I place several Extensions before testing, one narrow connection can make
the lower half of the structure difficult to reach.

For normal construction, I leave enough space:

- directly in front of the ladder;
- around the Extension joint;
- and at the upper and lower landing.

I save the detailed clearance checks for the troubleshooting process below.

### Test every section in both directions

A ladder can appear usable while descending and still fail during the return
climb.

After adding an Extension, I test:

1. climbing from the upper section to the new landing;
2. climbing from the new landing back upward;
3. movement across the Extension connection;
4. room to step away from the ladder.

Only then do I continue the shaft.

The completed route eventually provided access from the property surface to the
lowest useful excavation level.

<h2 id="move-resources">
  How to send packed mining boxes up the Lift
</h2>

The cargo function worked with **packed mining boxes** in my test.

This is the complete upward route I used:

1. mine until the backpack is full or the current load is large enough;
2. pack the collected resources into a mining box;
3. return to the lower Mining Lift position;
4. stand close enough for the Lift interaction to appear;
5. use the displayed **X** interaction in my keyboard layout;
6. continue mining or send the next packed box;
7. climb the attached ladder back to the surface;
8. collect the transferred boxes near the Lift Head;
9. move them to the truck.

The tested route was:

<div className="my-5 rounded-xl border border-slate-200 bg-slate-50 px-5 py-4 shadow-sm">
  <div className="space-y-1 font-mono text-sm leading-7 text-slate-700">
    <div>Full backpack</div>
    <div>→ Packed mining box</div>
    <div>→ Lower Lift interaction</div>
    <div>→ X to send upward</div>
    <div>→ Box appears near the surface Head</div>
    <div>→ Load the box onto the truck</div>
  </div>
</div>

During one mining session, I found **at least three transferred boxes** waiting
around the surface route after climbing back up.

That confirms that packed mining boxes work and that I can send several loads
during one underground trip.

It does not prove that three boxes are the Lift's simultaneous capacity. I sent
loads during the session and did not perform a maximum-capacity test.

### The player and cargo used separate routes in my test

I did not ride a visible moving cargo platform.

I sent the packed boxes upward, then used the attached ladder to return to the
surface myself.

I also did not see a floor-selection menu or choose a specific intermediate
Extension stop.

The tested behavior was a lower-to-surface cargo transfer, not a manually
driven elevator with selectable floors.

### What I have not confirmed

I have not completed controlled tests for:

- loose individual resources;
- product pallets;
- several boxes loaded simultaneously;
- sending cargo from the surface downward;
- a player riding with the cargo;
- or a maximum weight limit.

I therefore use packed mining boxes for the documented workflow instead of
assuming that every object placed near the structure can be transferred.

### How I fix `Ladder is blocked`

When the warning appears, I stop adding Extensions.

My repair order is:

1. identify the exact ladder section that fails;
2. clear terrain directly in front of it;
3. remove protruding terrain beside the ladder;
4. widen the upper landing;
5. widen the lower landing;
6. clear the joint between connected Extensions;
7. test from below;
8. test again from above.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/ore-factory-squad/ore-factory-squad-mining-lift-ladder-blocked.webp"
    alt="Ore Factory Squad Mining Lift displaying Ladder is blocked beside uncleared underground terrain"
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Clearing only the ground under the structure may not fix the warning. The
    ladder face, Extension joint, and landing also need room.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Adding another Extension does not repair an obstructed section above it.

The problem is normally physical clearance rather than the number of installed
parts.

### Working around electrical obstacles

An electrical obstacle near my shaft did not force me to abandon the route.

I opened space on the free side, kept the ladder path away from the obstacle,
and created a slightly offset landing.

The goal is not to destroy the cable. It is to preserve enough space for the
character to enter, climb, and leave the ladder safely.

<h2 id="how-deep">
  How deep should I extend the Mining Lift?
</h2>

I do not use one fixed Extension count for every property.

I continue downward while both conditions remain true:

1. **Remaining Resources** still lists useful Mid or Deep Layer materials;
2. Detector readings near the shaft justify opening another level.

The Lift acts as my permanent vertical main route.

At each useful depth, I scan again.

When a target is approximately **1–3 meters** away and the direction remains
stable, I open a short horizontal branch.

When a reading remains distant or changes sharply, I return to the main shaft
rather than widening the whole layer.

My route becomes:

```text
Surface Head
→ Main vertical Lift route
→ Short Mid Layer branch
→ Continue downward
→ Short Deep Layer branch
```

I stop adding Extensions when:

- the lowest valuable resource layer is already reachable;
- Remaining Resources no longer justifies another section;
- nearby targets can be reached through short branches;
- or truck and cargo handling have become the larger bottleneck.

Reaching the lowest possible terrain is not automatically the goal.

The useful endpoint is the lowest level that improves repeated mining,
packed-box transfer, and safe return travel.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ore Factory Squad Mining Lift FAQ
</h2>

### Can the Mining Lift carry packed mining boxes?

Yes. I sent packed mining boxes from the underground route to the surface and
found at least three transferred boxes waiting near the Head during one mining
session.

I then carried those boxes to the truck normally.

### Do I need to ride the Lift with the cargo?

No, not for the tested upward route.

I sent each packed box upward through the Lift interaction and climbed the
attached ladder separately. I did not ride a moving platform with the cargo.

### Why is my mining box still beside the ladder?

Placing a box near the Lift is not enough.

Bring the packed box close to the lower transfer point and use the displayed
interaction. In my keyboard layout, the successful upward action used **X**.

### Can the Mining Lift carry product pallets?

I did not complete a product-pallet transfer test.

The confirmed workflow uses packed mining boxes. I move factory product pallets
with the forklift rather than relying on the Lift until that payload has been
tested.

### Can I move the Mining Lift after placing it?

I could move a short Lift setup.

I still relocate a bad Head before building a deep route because I did not
verify whether every Extension moves with a completed structure or whether
dismantling refunds the full cost.

### Should I keep a backup ramp?

Yes, while the Lift is still being built.

I keep a temporary slope until every installed ladder section works downward
and upward. Once the full route has been tested, the permanent Lift can replace
most improvised vertical access.
