<h2 id="check-stock">
  Check stock and choose a contract I can finish
</h2>

I do not rank contracts by the largest displayed payout.

I rank them by **completion certainty**.

Before submitting an offer, I check:

- every exact finished-product name;
- the quantity required for each product;
- the delivery time;
- tracked finished stock;
- the machines needed for missing items;
- and whether the relevant raw resources are still available.

The Contracts screen does not always give me a convenient total for everything
in the warehouse, so I sometimes leave the computer and inspect the pallets
manually.

My stock check is:

1. write down each required product and quantity;
2. inspect the pallets inside the marked storage area;
3. separate finished goods from raw materials and intermediate products;
4. identify the slowest missing production chain;
5. accept only when that slow side is realistic.

A pallet visible on the warehouse floor may not count as tracked stock when it
is outside the marked storage zone.

Raw material also does not count as a finished contract item.

Having Clay in Factory Storage does not mean I already have Clay Bricks.
Having Limestone Blocks does not mean I already have Cement.

### My early contract priority

I usually work through offers in this order:

1. both finished products are already tracked;
2. only one short production queue is missing;
3. the raw materials exist and all required machines are ready;
4. a short mining trip can provide the missing material;
5. the order requires a new machine, license, or property.

I usually stop at step four.

A high-paying offer is not useful when one product requires another property
or a machine I cannot yet buy.

### Estimate the slowest product

For a two-product contract, I calculate the time needed for the harder side.

If I already have 20 Limestone Blocks but still need:

- raw Clay;
- Clay Block production;
- the Forming Press;
- and 15 Clay Bricks;

then Clay Bricks determine whether the order is safe.

I do not call the contract “almost finished” simply because the easier counter
is ready.

<h2 id="contract-negotiation">
  How contract negotiation works in my early runs
</h2>

The amount first shown on an offer is not always the highest price the customer
will accept.

My cleanest recorded comparison was a contract that accepted an offer worth
approximately **$20 more** than the initial displayed amount.

I also had higher offers accepted at roughly **$530** and **$760**, but I did
not record the original displayed amounts for those two contracts.

Because those starting values are missing, I do not use the examples to claim:

- a best markup percentage;
- a guaranteed accepted increase;
- or a fixed negotiation formula.

They prove only that a higher offer can succeed.

My practical rule is:

- raise the amount modestly when most products are already finished;
- stay closer to the displayed value when mining or several machines are still
  required;
- avoid spending too much of the working day testing aggressive offers when I
  still need time to produce and deliver;
- and stop treating one successful markup as a rule for every customer.

### Negotiation Rate is also an upgrade path

The factory progression includes an improvement to **Negotiation Rate**.

That means my opening contract results are not necessarily the final limit of
the system.

I treat the upgrade as a reason to retest offers later. Until I have recorded
the displayed amount, submitted offer, accepted amount, and final payout across
several upgraded contracts, I do not claim an exact benefit.

<h2 id="select-pallet-packer">
  Select the accepted contract in the Pallet Packer
</h2>

Accepting an offer on the computer does not automatically configure the
Pallet Packer.

I walk to the machine, open its interface, and select the accepted contract as
the active delivery.

The interface then shows:

- the required products;
- the amount already supplied;
- the amount still missing;
- and useful information about available stock.

This is my final check before moving any pallets.

### Factory Storage does not automatically complete the order

The Pallet Packer can show that a product exists in stock, but my manual setup
still requires a physical finished-product pallet at the machine input.

The route is:

<div className="my-5 rounded-xl border border-slate-200 bg-slate-50 px-5 py-4 shadow-sm">
  <div className="space-y-1 font-mono text-sm leading-7 text-slate-700">
    <div>Production machine</div>
    <div>→ Finished-product pallet</div>
    <div>→ Pallet Packer input</div>
    <div>→ Contract Output</div>
    <div>→ Completed delivery load</div>
  </div>
</div>

I lower the finished pallet into the input area and look for:

- **Pallet detected**;
- the correct product counter;
- and **Auto Start: On**.

A detected pallet can still be useless when it contains the wrong product
stage.

### Partial and oversized pallets

The input pallet does not need to be full.

I can use a partial pallet when it contains enough of the correct product to
advance the contract.

I can also place a pallet containing more than the order needs. The packer uses
the required quantity and sends the remaining items toward **Excess Output**.

### Foam Glass contracts

I treat Foam Glass as its own finished product.

Sand or ordinary Glass in storage does not prove that a Foam Glass order is
ready.

I accept one only when:

- finished Foam Glass is already tracked;
- or its production chain is already running with enough deadline remaining.

<h2 id="outputs">
  Keep Contract Output and Excess Output clear
</h2>

The Pallet Packer handles two results:

- products assigned to the contract;
- products remaining after the required amount is taken.

The completed delivery uses **Contract Output**.

Leftover items use **Excess Output**.

Those excess items are not automatically destroyed. I move them back into
storage or send them to another production stage.

### Fix Output blocked

When the machine displays **Output blocked**, I inspect the physical output
areas before changing the active contract.

Common causes are:

- a completed contract load still occupying Contract Output;
- an excess pallet that has not been removed;
- another pallet overlapping the output position;
- or an input pallet placed so badly that it traps the result.

I move the obstructing pallet first.

<h2 id="load-and-deliver">
  Load the contract and use the Delivery Gate
</h2>

I wait until every required counter is complete and the finished delivery load
has appeared.

I then lift the completed load, place it fully inside the truck bed, and confirm
that every required contract load is aboard before driving to the Delivery
Gate.

### Complete the final handoff

At the marked Delivery Gate, I drive the loaded truck into the delivery area
and use the available handoff prompt.

In my tested deliveries, I did not manually unload every product pallet at the
destination.

The contract was complete when the payout and XP appeared.

When no reward appears, I check:

1. whether every required delivery load is on the truck;
2. whether I entered the correct marked area;
3. whether every product counter was complete;
4. whether the contract was still active;
5. whether a second required load remained at the warehouse.

### Machines work at night

Active machines continued producing after dark.

This allowed me to finish a nearly complete queue without advancing the day.

New contract offers could not be sent at night, but the v1.0.1 launch hotfix
removed the restriction that prevented delivery calls at night.

I did not separately record a night-delivery reward screen, so I do not claim
whether delivering after dark changes the Fast Delivery Bonus.

### End Day can expire the contract

An active contract with **1 day remaining** expired when I used End Day.

My rule is:

- continue producing overnight when the missing products can be finished;
- attempt the delivery before ending the day;
- and sacrifice the speed bonus when the alternative is losing the entire
  contract.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ore Factory Squad contracts FAQ
</h2>

### Can I deliver a contract at night?

The v1.0.1 hotfix removed the restriction that prevented delivery calls at
night.

Active machines can also continue processing after dark. I did not separately
verify whether a nighttime handoff changes the Fast Delivery Bonus.

### Can I switch active contracts in the Pallet Packer?

The machine allows an accepted contract to be selected as the active delivery.

I clear the current outputs before changing selection. I did not verify what
happens to partially packed products when switching midway through an order.

### Why does the game say Contract limit reached?

The game limits simultaneous active contracts, and a later upgrade increases
contract capacity.

I did not verify the exact default solo limit, so I finish or cancel an
existing order before treating the warning as a bug.

### Can I carry multiple contract deliveries in one truck?

Multiple completed loads can physically fit when the truck has enough space.

I still verify and hand off each active order separately rather than assuming
one visit completes every contract automatically.
