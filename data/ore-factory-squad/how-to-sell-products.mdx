For stock to appear in Stock Sell, I use this route:

### Internal resource storage and pallet stock are different

Crates unloaded into the Sorting Station are separated and transferred into
the factory's internal resource storage.

That does not mean every stored material is automatically ready for Stock Sell.

For my manual sales:

- the Sorting Station handles incoming crate contents;
- machines or transfer controls place sellable stock onto physical pallets;
- the marked storage area makes those pallets visible to the warehouse stock
  system.

This distinction matters for raw Coal as well as processed products such as
Sand and Cement.

### A partial pallet can be sold

My first recorded Stock Sell used only **6 Sand**.

The listed price was **$11 per unit**, so the sale returned:

**6 × $11 = $66**

I did not need to wait until the pallet reached its maximum stack.

This makes Stock Sell useful when I need immediate cash from a small surplus
rather than a full production run.

<h2 id="read-stock-sell-offer">
  Read stock, Demand and price before selling
</h2>

The three values I check are:

- **Stock:** how many tracked units I currently have;
- **Demand:** how many units the buyer wants;
- **Price per unit:** the value of each unit included in the sale.

The buyer does not necessarily purchase everything I own.

### Do not treat one displayed price as a permanent value

I observed several different unit prices while checking Stock Sell, including
offers around **$5**, **$6**, **$8**, **$11**, and **$12** for different listed
stock.

Those examples prove that displayed offers can differ. They do not prove that
waiting until the next day always produces a better price.

I confirm a sale when:

- I need the cash now;
- the item is a genuine surplus;
- and selling the requested quantity will not block a contract or production
  chain.

### Reserve contract stock first

Before confirming a sale, I check:

- active contract quantities;
- products already waiting for the Pallet Packer;
- intermediate products needed for another recipe;
- and how difficult the item will be to replace.

For example, I do not sell every Limestone Block when the next order may
require Blocks directly or I still need them for Cement.

The same rule applies to:

- Clay Blocks and Clay Bricks;
- Stone Blocks and Stone Bricks;
- Glass and Foam Glass;
- raw Coal and Crushed Coal.

### Recovering from negative cash

After daily costs pushed my balance to **-$18**, Stock Sell was still available.

I had:

- **90 Coal** in tracked stock;
- a buyer Demand of **41**;
- and an offer of **$12 per unit**.

Selling those 41 Coal produced **$492** and restored usable cash without
requiring a contract delivery.

This is where Stock Sell is strongest: converting an existing surplus into
immediate operating money.

I still avoid selling every unit of a resource merely because the balance is
negative. I sell enough replaceable stock to recover, then preserve anything
needed by active production.

<h2 id="night-restrictions">
  Why Stock Sell is unavailable at night
</h2>

The factory can continue operating after dark, but the business applications
have daytime restrictions.

At night, I could continue:

- moving pallets;
- processing resources;
- arranging the warehouse;
- preparing tomorrow's stock.

I could not:

- complete Stock Sell transactions;
- send new contract offers;
- purchase properties.

When I reach the computer after dark, I use the remaining time to:

1. finish production;
2. move sellable pallets into the marked storage area;
3. reserve contract stock;
4. clear blocked machine outputs;
5. use End Day when I am ready for the next daytime window.

I do not use End Day solely because the current offer looks weak. I have not
established that every new day guarantees a higher unit price.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ore Factory Squad Stock Sell FAQ
</h2>

### What happens to the pallet after a Stock Sell transaction?

When I sold all 6 Sand, the products disappeared and the empty blue pallet
remained in the factory.

In the partial Coal sale, the unsold stock remained available rather than the
transaction clearing all 90 Coal.

### Can I complete a sale when buyer Demand is higher than my stock?

Yes. The Sand buyer wanted more than the 6 units I had, but the sale still
completed for the available six.

I did not need to produce the buyer's full Demand before confirming the
transaction.

### Does Stock Sell complete an active contract?

No. Even when Stock Sell lists the same product required by a contract, the
sale does not advance the contract counters.

It can instead remove stock that the Pallet Packer still needs.

### Why does Stock Sell open the wrong computer app with a controller?

Update to **v1.0.1 or later**.

The launch hotfix corrected a controller issue that could open the wrong
application during the Stock Sell tutorial.
