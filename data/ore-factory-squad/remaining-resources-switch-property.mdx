<h2 id="cannot-buy-property">
  Why can’t I buy a second property?
</h2>

When I attempted to purchase another listing while Greenpark Lot was still
active, the game stopped the purchase with:

> **Remove your first property first.**

  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    In the tested early-game system, buying a new property did not add a second
    active dig site. I had to remove Greenpark before purchasing the
    replacement.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

This means my tested progression allowed **one Active Property at a time**.

Buying a replacement is not the same as keeping Greenpark and adding another
site beside it.

I recover what I still need, remove the current property, and then purchase the
new listing.

### Buying and removing are separate actions

I do not expect the Buy button to remove Greenpark automatically.

My order is:

```text
Open the active Greenpark listing
→ Recover the site
→ Remove Property
→ Confirm that no Active Property remains
→ Open the replacement listing
→ Negotiate or buy
```

I did not see a separate removal charge during my tested switch.

I still needed cash for:

- the replacement property;
- the Metal Processing License;
- the Smelting Furnace;
- and the operating costs that followed.

A removal action without a visible fee does not make the full property switch
free.

After extensive mining, my Greenpark Surface Layer was depleted while the Mid

I use Remaining Resources to decide whether another search is worthwhile, then
use the Detector to locate the actual deposit.

<h2 id="leave-greenpark">
  When should I leave Greenpark Lot?
</h2>

I do not wait until every Remaining Resources value reaches zero.

The useful question is not:

> Does Greenpark still contain something?

It is:

> Does Greenpark still contain the resource my factory needs next?

<GuideTable
  caption="How I decide whether to keep mining Greenpark or replace it with another property."
  columns={greenparkDecisionColumns}
  rows={greenparkDecisionRows}
/>

### Why an empty Surface is not enough reason to leave

When my Surface values reached zero, Greenpark still contained useful Mid and
Deep Sandstone, Coal, Clay, Limestone, and Stone.

That made the property worth keeping while I still needed:

- Sand;
- Limestone Blocks;
- Cement;
- Clay products;
- Stone products;
- Crushed Coal;
- or contracts using those materials.

### Why resource type matters more than total quantity

Greenpark could still contain large amounts of Sandstone and Coal while being
the wrong property for my next objective.

Once the next contracts and unlocks required:

- Iron Ingots;
- Copper Ingots;
- Bronze;
- Steel;
- or later metal products,

another basic-material trip no longer solved the progression bottleneck.

Distance changes the calculation as well. A deposit may still exist, but a long
Detector search, another Lift Extension, and a difficult return route may make
a small basic-resource estimate poor value.

I switch when the remaining material no longer justifies the recovery time or
does not support the next production chain.

<h2 id="before-remove-property">
  What to recover before Remove Property
</h2>

I prepare as though anything left at the old dig site may be lost.

Before removing Greenpark, I recover:

1. packed mining boxes;
2. useful loose resources near the surface;
3. easy-to-reach Antiques and Scrap;
4. cargo waiting near the Mining Lift Head;
5. anything beside the site entrance;
6. the final backpack load.

My recovery route is:

```text
Underground load
→ Packed mining box
→ Mining Lift or manual return route
→ Truck
→ Factory
→ Sorting Station
→ Internal Factory Storage
```

### Keep a basic-material bridge stock

A Forest property advances the metal production chain, but I do not assume it
will immediately replace every Greenpark material in the quantities I need.

Before leaving, I keep one practical contract-sized reserve of the basic
products I still use, especially:

- Sand and Cement;
- Clay products;
- Stone products;
- processed Coal;
- and any intermediate product already required by a contract.

The goal is not to stockpile everything.

It is to prevent the new metal property from opening while an older basic
contract becomes impossible to finish.

### Check active Contracts and machine cash

Before removal, I review:

- accepted product quantities;
- remaining contract time;
- Fast Delivery Bonus time;
- products still waiting to be made;
- replacement-property cash;
- license costs;
- and Smelting Furnace costs.

I do not spend the entire reserve on the listing itself.

The new property is only useful when I can process the materials recovered
there.

### What I have not verified about Remove Property

I did not complete controlled tests for the following behaviors:

- whether packed boxes, loose resources, or Mining Lift parts left behind are
  permanently deleted;
- whether Greenpark can be purchased again with the same terrain or a new
  resource seed;
- whether removing a property cancels, preserves, or changes active Contracts.

Because those results were not verified, I recover useful objects and review
active Contracts before pressing Remove Property.

<h2 id="switch-and-test">
  How I switch and test the new property
</h2>

My complete replacement process is:

1. inspect the available property previews;
2. compare resource types, layers, price, and area;
3. recover Greenpark cargo;
4. return the final boxes to Factory Storage;
5. review active Contracts;
6. save the game;
7. remove Greenpark as the Active Property;
8. buy or negotiate the Woodland Lot during daytime;
9. drive to the Forest property;
10. set the Detector to **Detect All**;
11. clear a small test area;
12. pack one useful load;
13. return it to the factory;
14. verify that the expected metals appear in storage.

### My first Forest test

I did not begin by clearing the entire Surface or building a deep Mining Lift.

I opened a small test area and recovered both:

- **Iron**;
- **Copper**.

That was enough to confirm that the switch solved the metal bottleneck I had
identified before removing Greenpark.

I did not need to reach the bottom of the property before judging whether the
purchase was useful.

### Verify one load at the factory

I return the first box through:

Truck
→ Sorting Station
→ Internal Factory Storage

I confirm that Copper and Iron appear correctly before investing heavily in:

<h2 id="faq">
  Ore Factory Squad Property Switching FAQ
</h2>

### Does Remove Property cost money?

I did not see a separate removal charge during my tested switch.

The meaningful costs were the replacement listing, the processing license, and
the machines required for the new resource chain.

### Does 26% Copper and Iron mean 26 guaranteed ores?

No.

The Woodland Lot preview displayed a 26% metal-resource value beside Copper and
Iron, but I did not record enough UI detail to determine whether that number
applied separately or to a grouped preview value.

I use it only to compare listings. It does not provide a guaranteed chunk
count, fixed coordinate, drop chance, or universal Forest resource total.
