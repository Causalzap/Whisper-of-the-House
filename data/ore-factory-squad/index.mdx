<h2 id="opening-route">
  Greenpark, storage, sales and first contracts
</h2>

My first goal is to prove that every part of the basic factory loop works:

```text
Buy Greenpark
→ Collect 12 ore
→ Return the mining box
→ Sorting Station
→ Factory Storage
→ First machine
→ Finished product pallet
→ Marked storage
→ Sale or Contract
```

Raw resources become available to machines after the mining box passes through
the Sorting Station.

Finished products follow a different rule: they remain physical pallets and
must be placed inside the marked storage area before the warehouse computer can
reliably track them.

<h2 id="factory-and-contracts">
  Factory production, product stages and contract delivery
</h2>

The most important cross-guide rule is:

> Exact product names are not interchangeable.

For example:

```text
Clay ≠ Clay Block
Clay Block ≠ Clay Brick
Limestone Block ≠ Cement
Coal ≠ Crushed Coal
Copper ≠ Copper Ingot
Iron Ingot ≠ Iron Plate
```

A machine, buyer, or contract checks the requested production stage rather than
the material family.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ore Factory Squad guide hub FAQ
</h2>

### Do I need to read every guide in order?

No.

Use the Beginner Guide for chronological progression, then move to the
specialist page matching the machine, property, product, or contract currently
blocking progress.

Dependencies still matter. Raw Copper must reach Factory Storage before furnace
troubleshooting becomes useful, and the correct finished product must exist
before Pallet Packer troubleshooting becomes useful.

### How much money do I need for the first metal-processing setup?

The confirmed required purchases are:

- Metal Processing License #1: **$2,000**;
- Smelting Furnace: **$1,500**.

That creates a confirmed License-and-Furnace budget of **$3,500**.

It does not include property costs, operating cash, or any unverified output
pallet expense.

### Which guide should I use immediately after reaching Level 4?

Use the Property Guide when the active site does not contain Copper or Iron.

Use the Smelting Guide when raw metal is already in Factory Storage.

Use the Contract Guide when the Ingots are finished but the delivery will not
pack or complete.

### What should I do when a price differs from this guide?

Treat the current in-game interface as the source of truth.

The values here come from launch-week testing, while the dependency order and
troubleshooting routes are designed to remain useful when balance values
change.

### Does this hub cover full Warehouse Automation?

Not as a complete standalone guide.

Current coverage focuses on Greenpark through Level 5, including mining,
storage, selling, contracts, property switching, Cement, Dynamite, and the first
Copper and Iron processing stage.
