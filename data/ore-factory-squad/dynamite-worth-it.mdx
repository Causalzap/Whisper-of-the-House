<h2 id="is-dynamite-worth-it">
  Is Dynamite worth it? Best and worst uses
</h2>

Dynamite is not an upgrade that makes every mining trip more profitable.

I treat it as a specialized access tool.

<GuideTable
  caption="Situations where Dynamite solves a real access problem and situations where manual mining is usually safer or cheaper."

The strongest reason to unlock Dynamite is not the size of the crater.

The strongest reason is that Forest and Quarry can generate Secret Caves with
entrances sealed behind breakable walls.

In that situation, Dynamite is part of the access requirement rather than
merely a faster alternative to manual digging.

For ordinary ore deposits, I switch back to the Pickaxe as soon as the valuable
material becomes visible.

<h2 id="how-to-use">
  How I throw Dynamite safely
</h2>

I select Dynamite from the equipment wheel and use the visible **Throw**
action.

I aim at the ordinary terrain or barrier blocking my route rather than the
center of an exposed ore deposit.

After throwing, I move back and let the explosion finish before approaching the
opening.

A close blast caused a clear hit reaction in my tests, so I do not remain
beside the target or test an explosion with the exit blocked behind me.

I also keep the blast away from:

- the Mining Lift;
- vehicles;
- electrical cables;
- pipes;
- and other placed equipment.

I have not completed a controlled equipment-damage test, so I do not assume
those objects are protected.

<h2 id="secret-caves">
  Using Dynamite to open Secret Caves
</h2>

Forest and Quarry can both generate underground Secret Caves, but I do not
treat their entrances as identical.

Forest caves are confirmed to have breakable entrances that require Dynamite.

Quarry also contains procedurally generated Secret Caves, but I have not
verified whether its entrance uses the same wall appearance or blast behavior.

Because cave locations are procedural, I do not expect one fixed coordinate or
one universal tunnel route.

This is the clearest use case where Dynamite is not merely an optional
time-saving tool.

### How I approach a possible cave entrance

My cave approach is:

1. clear enough ordinary terrain to expose the suspected entrance;
2. keep a clear retreat route back toward the Lift or main tunnel;
3. throw one stick at the barrier;
4. move back and inspect the result before spending another $50.

I do not throw Dynamite randomly while searching.

Because cave locations are procedural, random blasts can consume several
hundred dollars without proving that I am close to an entrance.

### How I avoid wasting Dynamite while searching for a cave

I do not yet have a reliable visual rule that identifies every cave entrance,
so I avoid searching by random explosions.

Before spending $50, I check that:

- the property is Forest or Quarry;
- I am working below the Surface;
- ordinary soil has been cleared from the suspected barrier;
- the area resembles a sealed pocket rather than a visible resource vein;
- and I have a safe route back to the Mining Lift.

This does not prove the wall is a cave entrance, but it prevents me from
treating every underground rock face as one.

### What if one stick does not open it?

I do not immediately throw several more sticks.

I first check whether:

- ordinary soil still covers part of the wall;
- the first blast struck the edge of the barrier;
- the opening is hidden behind loose terrain;
- or I was facing an ordinary wall rather than the cave entrance.

Only then do I test another stick.

I have not completed a controlled Forest-versus-Quarry comparison, so I do not
claim that both cave types always require the same number of explosions.

### Forest and Quarry are not treated as identical

Both property types can contain procedurally generated Secret Caves, but that
does not prove that their:

- entrance appearance;
- typical depth;
- cave size;
- resource pool;
- or required blast count

are identical.

Until both are tested under comparable conditions, I use the same cautious
one-stick approach without presenting a false property-by-property formula.

### Does opening a cave unlock an achievement?

Blasting through a cave entrance with Dynamite and discovering the cave is the
action associated with the **Open Sesame** achievement.

The cave location is procedurally generated, so the objective is tied to
opening and discovering a cave rather than reaching one fixed map coordinate.

<h2 id="reduced-yield">
  Does Dynamite destroy resources?
</h2>

The in-game Dynamite description explicitly warns that explosions reduce total
resource yield.

That is the main reason I avoid throwing directly into a visible high-value
deposit.

A blast removes terrain quickly, but I do not assume that every resource inside
the affected area reaches my inventory.

### My two-Limestone result

During one early test, an explosion around a Limestone target produced only
**2 Limestone**.

This is one failure example, not a fixed Dynamite drop rate.

I did not measure the original deposit size or mine an identical Limestone
deposit manually as a control.

The test therefore proves only that:

- visual destruction does not measure profit;
- a blast can return very little relevant material;
- and Dynamite is a poor choice for a small contract shortage.

It does not prove that Dynamite normally gives two Limestone or that every
material loses the same percentage.

A reliable yield-loss test would require two similar deposits:

1. mine one deposit completely by hand;
2. blast one comparable deposit;
3. check inventory before and after;
4. inspect ground drops after the explosion;
5. compare Remaining Resources afterward.

Until that test is complete, I treat the reduced-yield warning as real without
inventing an exact percentage.

<h2 id="one-stick-routine">
  My six-step one-stick routine
</h2>

I use this process to control both cost and resource loss:

1. **Check the property and escape route.**  
   I confirm the property still contains something worth reaching and make sure
   the ladder, Lift, or tunnel behind me remains usable.

2. **Leave inventory space.**  
   My early backpack held **50 items**, so I clear enough room to compare the
   inventory before and after the explosion.

3. **Confirm the target with the Detector.**  
   I do not spend $50 on a wall when the relevant reading is still distant or
   moving unpredictably.

4. **Aim beside the target and throw one stick.**  
   I remove the obstruction around the resource rather than throwing directly
   into the center of the visible deposit.

5. **Move back and inspect the result.**  
   I check the opening, cash balance, inventory change, Detector distance, and
   whether the exit is still clear.

6. **Use the Pickaxe on exposed material.**  
   I spend another $50 only when a new obstruction still prevents access.

This process keeps each explosion measurable.

When I throw several sticks before checking the first result, I cannot tell
which explosion opened the route and which ones only removed unnecessary
terrain.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ore Factory Squad Dynamite FAQ
</h2>

### Can Dynamite hurt the player?

Yes. A close explosion caused a visible hit reaction in my test.

I move away immediately after throwing and keep a clear route behind me. I did
not measure the exact damage value or a guaranteed minimum safe distance.

### What should I do if one stick does not open the cave wall?

I clear ordinary soil around the barrier, inspect whether the blast struck the
center, and confirm that the wall is part of a possible Secret Cave rather than
normal terrain.

I only spend another $50 after those checks.

### Why is my Secret Cave empty?

Update to **v1.0.1 or later**.

The launch version had a problem that could prevent resources from appearing
inside randomly generated Forest and Quarry Secret Caves.

The fix does not mean every cave must contain the same quantity or type of
resource.

### Can Dynamite collapse an entire property?

Not reliably.

I saw connected and suspended terrain remain after excavation and explosions.
I use Dynamite to create a local opening rather than relying on a
property-wide collapse strategy.
