### The Smelting Furnace costs $1,500

The Marketplace displayed the Smelting Furnace at **$1,500**.

That is the confirmed machine price.

The confirmed setup costs are:

- **Metal Processing License #1: $2,000**;
- **Smelting Furnace: $1,500**;
- **confirmed minimum setup cost: $3,500**.

I did not verify whether the output pallet creates an additional purchase cost,
or whether selling or relocating the furnace provides a refund.

I therefore do not present `$1,500` as the complete metal-processing cost.

It is the machine price only.

### Activate the first License before running the furnace

I use this order:

```text
Level 4 unlock screen
→ Licenses
→ First Metal Processing License
→ Marketplace
→ Smelting Furnace
→ Place and configure the machine
```

I do not claim the Marketplace prevents the furnace from being purchased first.

The important tested requirement is that the first Metal Processing License is
active before I expect the furnace to process raw metals.

### Level 5 is not required for Copper or Iron Ingots

The later Level 5 screen showed **Metal Processing License #2**.

That is not the License I used to begin the first Copper and Iron Ingot stage.

I do not buy the Level 5 metal License merely because an Iron Ingot contract
appears.

<h2 id="make-ingots">
  How to make Copper and Iron Ingots
</h2>

Both products use the same basic machine workflow.

<GuideTable
  caption="Verified Copper and Iron Smelting Furnace routes. Exact input ratios and production times were not recorded."
  columns={ingotRecipeColumns}
  rows={ingotRecipeRows}
/>

My manual setup sequence is:

1. open the Smelting Furnace interface;
2. select **Copper Ingot** or **Iron Ingot**;
3. enter the quantity I want to produce;
4. confirm that the correct raw metal exists in Factory Storage;
5. provide an available output pallet;
6. press **Start**;
7. wait until the first correct Ingot appears before leaving the machine.

### Make Copper Ingots

For Copper, I verify:

```text
Factory Storage contains Copper
→ Active recipe says Copper Ingot
→ Output pallet is available
→ Quantity is set
→ Start
```

I watch the first output.

If Copper is stored but nothing appears, I do not immediately mine more. I
check the active recipe, output, and Start state first.

### Switch the furnace to Iron Ingots

Factory Storage containing Iron does not automatically change a Copper queue.

To switch metals, I:

1. inspect whether the Copper queue is still active;
2. stop or finish that queue;
3. select **Iron Ingot**;
4. enter the Iron quantity;
5. verify the output pallet;
6. press Start;
7. confirm that the first Iron Ingot appears.

I do not assume that Copper and Iron can run simultaneously in one queue.

### How much should I smelt?

I match the active contract first.

Until later production recipes are fully documented, I keep part of the raw
Copper and Iron instead of converting every stored unit automatically.

This avoids using the entire metal reserve before I know whether another recipe
or contract needs the raw material.

<h2 id="furnace-setup">
  Smelting Furnace input, output and pallet setup
</h2>

The furnace reads its raw input from **Factory Storage**.

I do not carry loose Copper or Iron directly into the machine.

The physical space around the furnace is mainly needed for:

- the machine itself;
- its finished-product output;
- a product pallet;
- forklift access;
- and enough room to remove a completed pallet.

### My output displayed room for 120 items

In one tested setup, the metal output displayed capacity for up to **120
items**.

I treat this as an observation from that output setup.

I do not claim that:

- every Ingot pallet always has a universal capacity of 120;
- every pallet type uses the same limit;
- Copper and Iron can be mixed on one pallet;
- an upgrade cannot change the displayed capacity;
- or a 121st Ingot was tested and rejected.

The practical lesson is simpler:

> One available pallet gave me enough room to begin a long metal queue, but I
> still checked the output before leaving the furnace unattended.

### Move finished Ingots into tracked storage

After production, the Ingot pallet is a physical finished-product pallet.

To make it available to warehouse systems, I move it fully inside the marked
storage area.

The handling difference is:

```text
Raw Copper or Iron
→ Factory Storage through the Sorting Station

Finished Copper or Iron Ingots
→ Physical product pallet in marked storage
```

A finished Ingot pallet left beside the furnace may not be counted correctly
for Contracts or Stock Sell.

<h2 id="ingots-vs-plates">
  Why the Smelting Furnace does not make Plates
</h2>

The Smelting Furnace completes this stage:

```text
Raw Copper
→ Copper Ingot

Raw Iron
→ Iron Ingot
```

It does not complete:

```text
Copper Ingot
→ Copper Plate

Iron Ingot
→ Iron Plate
```

Copper Plate and Iron Plate belong to a later production stage.

I did not complete a controlled test of the exact Plate machine, License,
recipe ratio, or production time, so this guide stops at Ingots.

I do not use several uncertain names interchangeably or imply that the
Smelting Furnace is broken when Plate recipes do not appear.

### Do not confuse the Level 5 unlocks with the first Ingot stage

The Level 5 screen included:

- Metal Processing License #2;
- Fabrication Station;

<h2 id="troubleshooting">
  Why the Smelting Furnace is not making Ingots
</h2>

<GuideTable
  caption="Checks for missing raw metal, wrong recipes, inactive queues, blocked output pallets, Plate confusion, and tracked-stock problems."
  columns={furnaceTroubleshootingColumns}
  rows={furnaceTroubleshootingRows}
/>

My fastest diagnostic order is:

1. confirm Copper or Iron in Factory Storage;
2. confirm the first Metal Processing License is active;
3. select the exact Ingot recipe;
4. enter a production quantity;
5. verify the output pallet;
6. press Start;
7. watch the first finished item;
8. move the finished pallet into marked storage.

### Do not mine another load before checking the active recipe

When Iron is available but the machine continues producing Copper, the problem
is not necessarily missing Iron.

The previous Copper recipe may still be active.

I reopen the furnace and verify the recipe label before returning to the
property.

### Do not rebuild the furnace because the output stopped

A full or obstructed output can stop production even when the machine has
enough raw material.

I first:

- inspect the output pallet;
- move completed Ingots away;
- provide a clear pallet;
- confirm the remaining quantity;
- restart the queue.

I rebuild or relocate the furnace only after the current input, recipe, and
output have been checked.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ore Factory Squad Copper and Iron Smelting FAQ
</h2>

### Can one Smelting Furnace make both Copper and Iron Ingots?

Yes.

I used the same Smelting Furnace for Copper and Iron by changing the active
recipe.

The machine did not automatically switch when Iron entered Factory Storage, so
I reopened the interface, selected Iron Ingot, confirmed the output, and
started the new queue.

### Does the Smelting Furnace keep running when I leave the warehouse?

In my tested run, I started metal production and returned to other work while
the furnace continued processing.

I did not separately verify whether an unfinished queue always survives:

- End Day;
- saving and reloading;
- moving the machine;
- or every later game update.

### Can the first Smelting Furnace process Scrap?

I did not find Scrap in the available first-furnace recipe list during my
tested setup.

I therefore do not treat Scrap as a confirmed Smelting Furnace input or promise
that it produces Ingots.

Until a later License or machine proves otherwise, I keep or sell Scrap instead
of building the Copper and Iron route around an unverified Scrap recipe.
