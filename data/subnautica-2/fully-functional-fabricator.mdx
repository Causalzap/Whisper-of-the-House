<h2 id="what-the-message-means">What “requires a fully functional Fabricator” means</h2>

That message means the **Lifepod Fabricator is no longer enough** for the recipe or material chain you are trying to use. The game is pushing you out of the temporary Lifepod workshop and into a powered base with real crafting stations.

You may see the blocker after finding new resources, scanning new fragments, or unlocking recipes that need a proper Fabricator, Processor, or vehicle station.

<h2 id="quick-route">Quick route: Lifepod to working Fabricator</h2>

Use this route if you just want the blocker gone.

1. **Craft the Scanner.** You need it before the Welcome Center route matters.
2. **Swim southeast from the Lifepod toward the Welcome Center.** Use the visible structure, signal, and bright open-water direction as your guide instead of relying only on a fixed bearing.
3. **Scan the first Habitat Builder fragment** near the Welcome Center entrance / front-right exterior area.
4. **Go inside, insert a Battery to power the Welcome Center, and scan useful rooms / stations.**
5. **Check below and around the Welcome Center route for the second Habitat Builder scan.**
6. **Return with Titanium, Quartz, and Copper.**
7. **Build the starter setup in the table below, then craft blocked recipes from the real Fabricator.**

<h2 id="power">How much power does the Fabricator need?</h2>

The Fabricator uses base energy while it is actively crafting. A safe working rule is to treat it as about **2 power per second while in use**.

For a tiny shallow base, **one Solar Panel** can get you started. Add a second Solar Panel if you craft repeatedly, attach a Scanner Station, build more rooms, or notice power dips at night.

Use this rule:

- **1 Solar Panel:** enough for a tiny starter base and short crafting bursts,
- **2 Solar Panels:** safer for repeated crafting, storage-heavy bases, and Scanner Station setup,
- **Hydroelectric / power transmitters later:** better once you start building a serious base or pushing deeper.

Place Solar Panels **outside, on top of the base, in open water with clear access toward the surface**. Do not hide them under terrain, tuck them into cramped cave geometry, or place them where expansion pieces will block your layout.

After placing the Solar Panel, enter the base and confirm it is powered and oxygenated before moving your main crafting loop there. If the base does not feel stable, fix power before adding more stations.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="beacon-tip">Put a Beacon on your first base</h2>

Subnautica 2 is easy to get lost in because you are often navigating by signals, landmarks, air pockets, caves, and memory. Once you unlock Beacons or base markers, put one on your first base immediately.

Name it something boring and useful, such as:

- **Starter Base**
- **Fabricator Base**
- **Moonpool Prep**

This is not just quality-of-life. If you swim out for Silver, Salt, Strong Acid, or Tadpole fragments, a visible base marker saves time and stops you from turning the Lifepod into your only mental anchor.

<h2 id="processor">Unlock the Processor next</h2>

The Fabricator fix is only the first half of the chain. The next important station is the **Processor**, because it opens advanced material crafting such as ingots and vehicle prep materials.

Look for it during the later habitat route with black boxes, wall lockers, orange ladders, oxygen pockets, and multiple scannable stations. After entering the main interior room, check the **right-hand side from the entry path** and scan the Processor before leaving.

<h2 id="moonpool">Prepare the Moonpool and Tadpole Deck</h2>

Once the Processor and advanced materials are moving, your base starts connecting to the **Moonpool → Tadpole Deck → Vehicle Fabricator** path.

The most common placement mistake is building the starter base too close to the seafloor. The Moonpool and Tadpole Deck need enough open vertical space below them.

A safe placement rule is:

**Put the Moonpool on the outer edge of your base, over visibly deeper water, with open vertical clearance below.**

If the placement preview says **blocked**, do not fight it. Extend the base one or two pieces toward deeper water and try again from the edge.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### What does “fully functional Fabricator” mean in Subnautica 2?

It means the damaged Lifepod Fabricator is not enough for the recipe or progression chain. Build a powered base and place a proper Fabricator there.

### How many Solar Panels do I need for a Fabricator base?

Use one Solar Panel for a tiny shallow starter base. Add a second Solar Panel if repeated crafting, Scanner Station use, nighttime dips, or a larger base makes power unstable.

### Where should I place Solar Panels for the best early power?

Place Solar Panels outside on the top or upper exterior of a shallow base, with clear open water above and as little terrain obstruction as possible. Avoid cramped caves, shaded overhangs, and spots you plan to cover with future rooms.

### Is the Fabricator shared in co-op?

A Fabricator placed inside a shared base should be treated as a team crafting station. In co-op, coordinate shared storage, scanning, and material use so one player does not spend the Copper, Quartz, or advanced materials another player needs for the next unlock.

### Why are advanced materials still missing after I build a Fabricator?

You probably need the Processor. Scan it in the later habitat route, then use it for advanced material chains such as ingots, Strong Acid chains, and vehicle prep.

### Why can’t I place the Tadpole Deck?

The Moonpool area is probably too shallow or blocked. Extend the base over deeper water and place the Moonpool on an outer edge with clear space below.

### Should I put a Beacon on my first base?

Yes. Once Beacons or base markers are available, mark your first base immediately. It makes resource runs and Tadpole preparation much easier in a game where navigation depends heavily on landmarks and signals.
