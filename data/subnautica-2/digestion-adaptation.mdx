<h2 id="why-digestion-matters">Why Digestion matters</h2>

Digestion is the point where early food stops feeling awkward.

Before you unlock it, your body is not adapted to the planet yet. You can catch fish and craft some meals, but local food can still show **digestive incompatibility**, which makes hunger feel harsher than it really is.

That is why I would not spend the first hour trying to brute-force food with random cooked meals. Use Lifepod supplies as a bridge, solve water separately, then unlock Digestion so cooked local fish actually becomes useful.

<h2 id="when-to-get-digestion">When should you get Digestion?</h2>

I would get Digestion **after your basic tools, but before longer resource runs**.

A good timing is:

- you have made the **Survival Multi-Tool**,
- you have or are about to make the **Scanner**,
- you have enough oxygen confidence to leave the Lifepod area,
- Anita’s signal has appeared, or you are ready to check the north-northeast route,
- hunger is starting to matter more than the first few emergency supplies.

Do not wait until you are starving. Digestion is not a late-game upgrade. It is an early survival fix.

<GuideCallout title="My timing rule">
If you are about to leave the safe Lifepod loop for silver, deeper caves, or longer scanning trips, unlock Digestion first.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="where-to-find-digestion">Where to find Digestion Adaptation</h2>

From the Lifepod, the Digestion route is roughly **north-northeast**, around **150–160m** from the pod in current early routes. Anita’s signal points you toward the same area once it appears.

The easiest visual clue is the **Angel Comb** itself. Look for a massive glowing pink-purple plant shaped like spiky angel wings. The part you need to interact with is the **central pink bulb at the base**.

There is also a useful navigation clue: a **black cable runs along the seafloor toward the Angel Comb area**. If the compass bearing feels vague, follow the cable instead of swimming around randomly.

A simple route check:

- Start at the **Lifepod**.
- Make sure your basic tools are ready.
- Follow Anita’s signal, or head roughly **north-northeast**.
- Watch the seafloor for the **black cable**.
- Look for the huge glowing **Angel Comb**.
- Interact with the **central pink bulb**.
- Check Anita’s black box and nearby scannables before leaving.
- Return to the Lifepod and test local cooked food.

<GuideCallout title="Do not miss the plant">
If you found Anita’s black box but still cannot digest local food, you probably skipped the Angel Comb interaction. Swim up to the central pink bulb and interact with it.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-to-bring">What to bring before going</h2>

You do not need a full expedition kit, but I would not swim there empty-handed either.

Bring:

- **Survival Multi-Tool**, because it is your first basic utility tool,
- **Scanner**, because nearby points of interest are worth checking,
- **Air Bladder** if you already have it,
- **one First Aid Kit**, because hostile creatures usually lurk around the Angel Comb area,
- enough open inventory space to grab fish or small materials on the way back.

Fins make the route feel much better, but the key is not speedrunning. The key is reaching the plant, getting the adaptation, and returning without burning every emergency ration.

<h2 id="what-to-do-after-unlock">What to do after unlocking Digestion</h2>

Once Digestion is unlocked, turn it into a better routine instead of swimming off randomly.

My first moves would be:

1. Return to the Lifepod.
2. Catch a few nearby fish.
3. Cook them in the Fabricator.
4. Save nutrient blocks for longer trips.
5. Start preparing for silver, Standard Air Tank, and base progression.

This is where the opening game gets much smoother. You are no longer treating every hunger drop like an emergency; you have a repeatable food source.

<h2 id="water-and-food-loop">Best early food and water loop after Digestion</h2>

Digestion fixes food, not water. Keep those two systems separate in your head.

For water:

- grab Water Slugs,
- take them back to the Fabricator,
- make bottled water,
- avoid eating them raw unless you are desperate.

For food:

- catch small fish near the Lifepod,
- cook only what you need,
- carry a little backup,
- save Nutrient Blocks for trips where you may not return quickly.

That is the difference between surviving and constantly panic-eating every item in storage.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Why can’t I eat local food in Subnautica 2?

Because you need **Digestion Adaptation** first. Until then, local cooked food may show digestive incompatibility instead of working like normal food.

### Where is the Digestion Adaptation?

It is near Anita’s early signal route, roughly north-northeast from the Lifepod. Look for the massive glowing Angel Comb and interact with the central pink bulb at the base.

### What does the Angel Comb look like?

It looks like a huge glowing pink-purple plant with spiky angel-wing shapes. The important interact point is the central pink bulb near the base.

### Is there an easier way to find the Angel Comb?

Yes. Follow Anita’s signal if it is active, and watch for the black cable running along the seafloor toward the area.

### Is the Digestion plant the same as Anita’s black box?

No. Anita’s black box / data point is nearby progression content, but the food fix comes from interacting with the Angel Comb bulb. Do both before leaving.

### Do I need the Scanner for Digestion?

The plant interaction is the key unlock, but I still bring the Scanner because the area has useful nearby story and progression objects.

### Should Digestion be my first objective?

Not literally first. Craft basic tools first, then get Digestion early before longer resource runs.

### What should I eat before Digestion?

Use Lifepod supplies, found food, and Nutrient Blocks. If you have the Processor route later, Biofuel Block plus Salt can also become an emergency Nutrient Block option.

### Are Water Slugs food or water?

Treat Water Slugs as water materials. Turning them into bottled water is usually the better habit.
