<h2 id="start-here">Start here if you feel lost</h2>

Subnautica 2 is not hard because the first tools are complicated. It is hard because the game drops you into an ocean and lets oxygen, hunger, inventory space, and fear all push back at once.

That is why I would not start by asking, “Where is everything?” I would start with this:

- **Can I breathe longer?** Work toward the Air Bladder and Standard Air Tank.
- **Can I unlock more recipes?** Craft the Scanner and scan everything useful.
- **Can I eat reliably?** Unlock Digestion before depending on local cooked food.
- **Can I store and craft cleanly?** Build a small powered base.
- **Can I travel farther safely?** Prepare for the Tadpole and forward outposts.
- **Can I get back home?** Use Signals, Beacons, landmarks, and the Lifepod as your zero point.

This page is the hub for that route. Use it to pick the next problem, then open the specific guide when you need details.

<h2 id="what-not-to-do-first">What not to do first</h2>

Before the checklist, here are the three things that waste the most early time:

- **Do not swim deep just because you can see something interesting.** If you do not have oxygen backup, you are turning curiosity into a death marker.
- **Do not treat local cooked food as solved before Digestion.** If you see digestive incompatibility, stop brute-forcing food and unlock the adaptation.
- **Do not leave the Welcome Center after one scan.** It is an unlock hub, not a sightseeing stop.

These are the mistakes that make the first hour feel unfair. The safer play is to build a short route, unlock one thing at a time, and return before your inventory, oxygen, or food plan collapses.

<h2 id="first-hour-checklist">First hour checklist</h2>

If you want the cleanest start, follow this order:

1. Open the Lifepod Fabricator and check your early equipment recipes.
2. Collect Titanium, Copper, Quartz, Fibrous Pulp, Lucifer Rotsack, Water Slugs, and Acidic Raion Pouches.
3. Craft the **Survival Multi-Tool** so you can harvest key materials.
4. Make a **Battery**, then craft the **Scanner** so you can unlock fragments and buildables.
5. Make **Fins** so every swim costs less oxygen.
6. Craft the **Air Bladder** before deeper cave routes.
7. Find Silver and craft the **Standard Air Tank**.
8. Head to the **Welcome Center**, southeast / south-southeast from the Lifepod.
9. Scan both **Habitat Builder** fragments and useful scannables before leaving.
10. Power the **Biolab** with a Battery.
11. Choose early Biomods, then unlock **Digestion** before relying on cooked local fish.
12. Build a small powered base once the Lifepod becomes a storage and crafting bottleneck.

This is not the only possible route, but it is the route I would give a new player who wants fewer death loops and less wasted swimming.

<GuideCallout title="Best early Biomods">
For most players, I would start with **Dash** as the active Biomod and **Oxygen Control** as the passive Biomod. Dash gives you an emergency movement option, while Oxygen Control helps when you stop to scan, build, read, or recover your route.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="early-access-note">Early Access note</h2>

Subnautica 2 is still in Early Access, so recipes, unlock order, map details, co-op rules, creature behavior, and biome balance may change.

The safest way to use this guide is to follow the **decision logic**, not memorize one perfect patch route:

- craft tools before long trips,
- scan before leaving structures,
- solve food with Digestion,
- solve storage with a small powered base,
- mark routes before going deeper,
- treat dangerous zones as planned dives, not curiosity swims.

<GuideCallout title="Best player habit">
Use the Lifepod as your zero point until Beacons are unlocked. Think in direction, distance, landmark, oxygen reset, and return path.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Can I play Subnautica 2 without playing the first game?

Yes. You will understand the survival loop faster if you played earlier Subnautica games, but this guide assumes you are starting fresh and focuses on what to do in the first hours.

### How long is the early game before it opens up?

The early game starts opening up once you have the Scanner, better oxygen, Digestion, Habitat Builder progress, and a small base. For most new players, that means the first couple of hours are about building a safe route rather than rushing deep.

### Should I follow signals immediately?

Follow signals when you have the tools and oxygen to survive the trip. Early signals are important, but swimming toward every marker with no Scanner, no oxygen backup, and a full inventory is how you create avoidable death runs.

### Is the Welcome Center required?

It is not just a landmark. The Welcome Center is your first major unlock hub because it gives Habitat Builder progress, Biolab access, oxygen safety, and useful scannables.
