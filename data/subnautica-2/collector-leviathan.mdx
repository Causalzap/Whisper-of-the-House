<h2 id="should-you-enter-collector-territory">Should you enter Collector territory right now?</h2>

Most players should **not** enter Collector territory just because they found it. Treat the area like a planned dive.

I would only go in if one of these is true:

- you need to cross the edge of the biome,
- you are specifically hunting for the scan entry,
- you are mapping the boundary from a safe distance,
- you have a vehicle, base, or oxygen route already planned,
- you have Distraction Flares ready on your hotbar.

If you are carrying rare resources, low on oxygen, missing healing, or just “checking it out,” leave first. The Collector is exactly the kind of threat that turns curiosity into a lost inventory.

<h2 id="collector-location">Where to find the Collector Leviathan</h2>

The Collector Leviathan is associated with **Sparse Plains** and **Outer Bounds** in current Early Access reporting. That matters because a lot of players only prepare for Sparse Plains, then relax too much near the outer edge of the map.

Ocean currents can pull you into **Sparse Plains** without warning. Check your position on the HUD before any deep dive near the biome edge.

My rule is simple:

- **Sparse Plains:** expect possible Collector activity.
- **Outer Bounds:** do not assume safety just because you left Sparse Plains.
- **Biome edge plus deep open water plus no clear exit:** leave or mark the route first.

Do not build a main base, park the Tadpole, or start a long resource run just because the area looks empty for the first minute. Scout from the edge, watch for movement, and keep enough distance that you can still turn around without panic.

<GuideCallout title="Location rule">
If you are going into Sparse Plains or Outer Bounds, pretend the Collector is already nearby until you prove otherwise.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="first-sighting-decision">What to do when you first see it</h2>

The first sighting is the safest moment because you still have distance. Do not waste that advantage.

Do this instead:

- stop moving forward,
- check oxygen,
- check inventory value,
- turn your camera toward the exit,
- decide whether you are leaving, crossing, scanning, or observing.

If you only came for navigation, leave wide space around it. If you came for a scan, wait for a better angle instead of swimming straight at its face. If you came for resources, ask yourself whether the item is worth a death run. Usually, it is not.

Low light and quieter movement can help before you are noticed, but do not overrate them. The Collector is not just a visual stealth puzzle. Once it starts reacting hard, you need to switch from “sneak” to “escape.”

<h2 id="lock-on-escape-plan">What to do if the Collector locks on</h2>

The Collector’s detection should be treated as **sonar-style lock-on**, not “I turned my lights off so I am safe.”

That changes the decision:

- before it notices you, reduce obvious attention and stay outside the route,
- once it turns, closes distance, or commits to you, stop hiding,
- throw a Distraction Flare away from your escape path,
- move toward open water, your Tadpole, or a sealed base,
- ignore dropped items and reset the dive after you are safe.

Once the encounter has flipped from scouting to chase, greed is the real danger. You are no longer optimizing the route. You are saving the run.

<h2 id="distraction-flare-strategy">How to use Distraction Flare against the Collector</h2>

**Distraction Flare is the specific tool you should name and use here.** Do not write this off as a vague “decoy.”

Use it like this:

1. Equip the flare before entering the biome.
2. When the Collector notices you, aim away from your escape route.
3. Throw the flare to the side or behind the threat.
4. Leave while the flare creates a distraction window.
5. Do not wait to confirm every animation.

The direction matters. If you throw the flare along your exit path, you may pull the danger toward the same route you need to use. If you throw it at your own feet, you have not created separation. The best flare is one that buys distance, not one that makes a cool light show.

<h2 id="scan-collector-safely">How to scan the Collector Leviathan safely</h2>

Yes, the Collector can be scanned, but I would not make your first sighting a scan attempt.

A safer scan plan looks like this:

1. **Scout first.** Find the edge of the area and identify a route out.
2. **Unload valuables.** Do not bring rare materials into the scan attempt.
3. **Bring at least one Distraction Flare.** Two is better if you are learning the timing.
4. **Approach from a side angle.** Do not swim straight at the head.
5. **Start scanning only when you have room to abort.**
6. **Abort the second it turns hard or closes distance.**
7. **Do not chase the scan.** If the window is bad, leave and reset.

The scan is not worth forcing. A clean failed attempt is better than dying with the Scanner in your hand and no route back.

<h2 id="tadpole-safety">Is the Tadpole safe near the Collector?</h2>

The Tadpole is useful near Collector territory, but it is not a panic room.

Use it for:

- getting to the edge of the biome,
- keeping oxygen and storage nearby,
- turning a failed scout into a faster retreat,
- carrying enough supplies for a second attempt.

Do not use it for:

- idling inside Collector patrol space,
- parking next to a scan attempt,
- assuming the vehicle can absorb every mistake,
- slowly turning around after the Collector reacts.

If you bring the Tadpole, park it facing the way out. That sounds small, but it matters. A vehicle pointed into danger is not an escape plan.

<h2 id="base-safety">Are bases safe from the Collector?</h2>

A **sealed base compartment** is the one place I would treat as a real reset point. If you make it through the hatch into a sealed base, use that moment to breathe, heal, and wait.

But that does **not** mean you should build your main base on the Collector’s route.

The smart use is a small emergency setup near the edge:

- small room,
- hatch,
- power,
- beacon,
- maybe one locker.

Use it as a panic room or oxygen stop, not as your beautiful permanent base. The danger is not usually “will the Collector break the base?” The danger is “can I reach the hatch before it catches me?”

<h2 id="pearl-warning-signs">Pearls as a Collector territory warning sign</h2>

Pearls are worth treating as a warning sign around Collector territory. Current creature data links the Collector with **Pearl Stash** interactions, so if you notice pearl-related objects in a suspicious deep zone, do not assume you found a peaceful loot pocket.

My rule:

- if you find a pearl stash, slow down,
- check your exit,
- do not farm with a full inventory,
- expect Collector attention until proven safe.

This does not mean every pearl guarantees an immediate attack. It means pearls should change your behavior from “loot mode” to “territory check.”

<h2 id="can-you-kill-collector">Can you kill the Collector Leviathan?</h2>

Do not plan around killing the Collector Leviathan.

For a practical player guide, the answer is:

- do not fight it,
- do not test damage with rare items on you,
- do not stay to see if it works,
- scan, distract, escape, and come back later if needed.

If a future Early Access update adds a reliable neutralization method, this section should be updated with the patch version, tool, and exact steps. Until then, the winning strategy is not bravery. It is leaving early enough that you do not need to recover a corpse marker.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Where does the Collector Leviathan spawn?

Current reporting places the Collector Leviathan in **Sparse Plains** and **Outer Bounds**. Treat both areas as dangerous until you have confirmed the route yourself.

### Can ocean currents drag me into Collector territory?

Yes. Ocean currents can pull you into Sparse Plains faster than you expect, so check your HUD position before deep dives near the biome edge.

### Should I go into Collector territory early?

Only if you have a clear reason and a way out. Do not enter with rare materials, low oxygen, no flare, or no exit marker.

### Does turning off lights make me safe?

No. Low light may help before the Collector notices you, but the important point is that its detection should be treated as sonar-style tracking. Once it locks on, escape and flare timing matter more than staying dark.

### Does Distraction Flare work?

Yes, Distraction Flare is the tool I would bring. Throw it away from your escape route so the attention moves off your path instead of following you.

### Can you scan the Collector Leviathan?

Yes, but do it as a planned scan attempt. Bring flares, keep an exit route, approach from an angle, and abort if it turns toward you.

### Is the Tadpole safe from the Collector?

No. The Tadpole is useful for travel and retreat, but it is not a guaranteed safe room. Keep it pointed toward the exit and do not idle near the Collector.

### Are bases safe from the Collector?

A sealed base compartment is a safe reset point once you are inside. The risky part is reaching the hatch, so do not build your main base directly in its route.

### Can you kill the Collector Leviathan?

Do not plan around killing it. Treat the Collector as an avoid, distract, scan, and escape threat unless a future patch adds a verified method.
