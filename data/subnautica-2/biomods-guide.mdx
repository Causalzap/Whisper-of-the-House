<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**For most players, the best Biomod setup in Subnautica 2 is Pathfinder or Dash as your active Biomod and Sea Skimmer as your passive Biomod.** Use Pathfinder when the route is confusing, Dash when you already know where you are going, and Sea Skimmer as your default passive for faster seafloor travel.

The upgrade you should not skip is the **Bioscanner**. Many later Biomods do not unlock from normal Scanner entries. The wreck is roughly **1,800m east of the Lifepod / starting area** if you count the full route: first to the Alien Ruins, then another long push to the wreck marked with **73**. Scan the Bioscanner fragment, craft the upgrade at a **Modification Station**, then rescan the required creatures or plants with the upgraded Scanner.

<h2 id="how-biomods-work">How Biomods work in Subnautica 2</h2>

Biomods are character upgrades you equip through the Bio Lab interface. I treat them like route tools rather than permanent perks: before a trip, ask what is most likely to end the run. Is it the exit route, oxygen, predators, food, water, or finding the right resource?

There are two types:

1. **Active Biomods** are abilities you trigger manually, such as Dash, Pathfinder, Sonic Echo, Electric Discharge, and Chum Cloud.
2. **Passive Biomods** stay active while equipped, such as Sea Skimmer, Oxygen Control, Camouflage, Water Secretion, and the food / water passives.

In the current Early Access build, plan around **one active Biomod and one usable passive Biomod**. Some UI / guide data shows room for up to **six passive slots**, but only **one passive slot is currently usable** for normal play. Until a later update unlocks more, treat every passive choice as a real tradeoff.

<h2 id="craft-bioscanner">How to craft the Bioscanner</h2>

After scanning the Bioscanner fragment, craft the upgrade at the **Modification Station**. This is not the basic Fabricator, and it is not something most players have at the start of the game. If you have the Bioscanner blueprint but cannot craft it, the missing piece may be the Modification Station itself; look for it during the second major / Alien Ruins-side progression and build that station before blaming the Bioscanner materials.

<h2 id="common-problems">Common Biomod problems and fixes</h2>

### I scanned the creature, but the Biomod did not unlock

You probably scanned it with the normal Scanner before crafting the Bioscanner. The old scan does not count for Biomod progress. Equip the Bioscanner upgrade and scan that same creature or plant again.

### I have the Bioscanner blueprint, but I cannot craft it

Check whether you have access to a **Modification Station**. The Bioscanner is crafted there, not at the basic Fabricator. You also need **1 Scanner, 2 Enamelled Glass, and 3 Conduit Crystal**.

### Where do I get the Modification Station?

Treat the Modification Station as a **mid-game / second-major-area unlock**, not a Lifepod-starter station. If you have not pushed into the Alien Ruins-side progression yet, you may be too early to craft the Bioscanner even if you already know the recipe.

### I cannot find the Bioscanner wreck

Use the Alien Ruins as the midpoint landmark. From the start, head east to the Alien Ruins side, then continue roughly east / bearing 120 for about 600m more. The full route is roughly 1,800m from the Lifepod / starting area, and the final visual target is the wreck door with the yellow bottom strip and 73 above it.

### Can I get the Bioscanner with only a Standard Air Tank?

Yes, if you use the interior oxygen bubble and already know the route. A High-Capacity Air Tank, Rebreather, Fins, and vehicle make the trip more comfortable, but the Repair Tool and Scanner are the real hard requirements.

### Why does the guide say one passive slot if the UI shows more?

Because that is the current practical limit for Early Access play: **one active Biomod and one usable passive Biomod**. The passive menu may show room for up to **six passive slots**, but only **one passive slot is usable right now**. Future passive-slot expansion is possible, but it is not something I would build this guide around until the game actually unlocks it.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### What is the best Biomod in Subnautica 2?

For most players, **Sea Skimmer** is the best passive Biomod because faster seafloor travel improves almost every early route. For active Biomods, use **Pathfinder** if you get lost and **Dash** if you want simple mobility.

### How do I unlock more Biomods?

Get the Bioscanner upgrade first, then scan the required creature or plant with the Bioscanner equipped. Normal Scanner records are not enough for most later Biomods.

### Where do I get the Bioscanner?

Scan the Bioscanner fragment inside the wreck beyond the Alien Ruins route, roughly **1,800m east of the Lifepod / starting area** in total. Bring a Repair Tool, a normal Scanner, and a safe oxygen / vehicle route. After scanning it, craft the upgrade at a Modification Station.

### What materials does the Bioscanner need?

The Bioscanner is crafted at a Modification Station with **1 Scanner, 2 Enamelled Glass, and 3 Conduit Crystal**. Enamelled Glass uses Glass plus Creature Enamel, and Creature Enamel is gathered from clump-like deposits around the Alien Ruins biome rather than from random starter-zone fish.

### Do I need to rescan creatures after getting the Bioscanner?

Yes. If you scanned a creature with the normal Scanner before you had the Bioscanner, scan it again with the upgraded Scanner. Earlier normal scans do not retroactively unlock Biomods.

### Which Biomod should I use for caves?

Use **Pathfinder** as your active Biomod. For the passive slot, choose **Sea Skimmer** if movement speed is the problem or **Oxygen Control** if you stop often to scan and read the cave.

### Which Biomod helps against predators?

Use **Electric Discharge** when a predator is already close, **Chum Cloud** when you want to distract one before contact, **Camouflage** when you can stop moving early, or **Threat Sense** if surprise attacks are the problem.

### Is Sonic Echo worth unlocking?

Yes, but not as your first risky scan. Sonic Echo is excellent for highlighting resources, but the Collector Leviathan scan is dangerous. Wait until you have a vehicle, oxygen plan, and escape route.

### Are food and water Biomods worth using?

Yes for long trips. **Water Secretion** is the easiest convenience pick, **Water Retention** stretches water items, **Dermal Garden** creates emergency food, and **Slow Metabolism** slows hunger loss without adding inventory clutter.
