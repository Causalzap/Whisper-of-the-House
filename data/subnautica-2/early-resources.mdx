<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The best early resource route in Subnautica 2 is a 15-20 minute Lifepod loop: collect 3 Titanium near the Lifepod, dive into the cave below for Copper and Acidic Raion Pouches, check coral domes for Quartz, harvest Lucifer Rotsack and Fibrous Pulp near the huge coral route, then push the green-flora Silver cave once you have Fins or an Air Bladder.**

Do not farm forever. Your first stopping points are **two Batteries**, **Scanner**, **Fins**, **Air Bladder**, and **Standard Air Tank**. Once one of those unlocks is ready, return and craft it before extending the route.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="pre-dive-checklist">Pre-dive checklist</h2>

Before you leave the Lifepod, check three things:

1. **Pin your recipes.** Pin Survival Multitool, Scanner, Fins, Air Bladder, and Standard Air Tank so every pickup has a purpose.
2. **Empty your inventory.** Store extra food, first aid, and spare ore before entering caves.
3. **Know your oxygen reset.** Your resets are the Lifepod, oxygen bubbles, air pockets, and interior rooms.

<h2 id="titanium-copper-battery">Titanium, Copper, and Battery materials</h2>

Start with **3 Titanium** for the Survival Multitool. After that, your first Copper goal is usually **two Batteries**: one for the Scanner path and one for early powered interactions such as the **Welcome Center**, the first major early POI where you scan important fragments and use powered systems.

The cave below the Lifepod is the best first stop because it bundles Copper, Acidic Raion Pouches, bubbles, and often a Portable Locker into one route. Cut the Acidic Raion Pouches with the Survival Multitool before you commit to deeper exploration.

<GuideCallout title="Decision point">
Once you can craft two Batteries, stop treating Copper as the main objective. Move to Quartz for Scanner progression, then Lucifer Rotsack / Fibrous Pulp for movement and oxygen safety.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="quartz-coral-domes">Quartz: learn the coral dome pattern</h2>

Quartz becomes easier once you stop hunting random rocks and start reading the terrain. Look for **coral dome** structures and check underneath or inside them. That visual pattern repeats, so learning the shape matters more than memorizing one exact spot.

<h2 id="fibrous-pulp-lucifer-rotsack">Fibrous Pulp, Lucifer Rotsack, Rubber, and Fins</h2>

Near the huge coral route, collect **at least 4 Lucifer Rotsack and 4 Fibrous Pulp**. That minimum gives you the Rubber and Fiber branch needed for Fins and Air Bladder progression.

Lucifer Rotsack is easy to spot because of the bright yellow-orange growths. Fibrous Pulp is easier to overlook because it looks like background plant life, but it matters for Fiber, mesh-style crafting, and food routes after Digestion.

<h2 id="silver-standard-air-tank">Silver and the Standard Air Tank breakpoint</h2>

Silver is the first resource that noticeably changes your oxygen economy. Once you have Fins or an Air Bladder, push toward the green-flora Silver cave north of the Lifepod route.

A good first target is **2-4 Silver**. Get enough for the Standard Air Tank or your next pinned Silver-gated recipe, then leave. Do not turn your first Silver trip into a full cave-clear unless you already know the air pocket and return path.

<h2 id="digestion-salt-food">Digestion, Salt, and why food changes after Anita</h2>

If local food feels punishing early, you are missing **Digestion**. Digestion is an early adaptation near Anita's signal that lets you safely rely on local cooked food and plant-based food routes. Until then, use Lifepod food, crafted water, and short trips instead of assuming every edible-looking item is safe.

After Digestion, Salt and food crafting become more valuable because longer routes are less about sprinting home and more about sustaining a trip. Salt appears around early caves and seafloor routes near habitat POIs, so pick it up when you are already nearby instead of making it your first dangerous detour.

<h2 id="salt-sulfur-gold">Salt, Sulfur, Gold, and when to stop pushing</h2>

Salt is reasonable to grab during early habitat routes. Sulfur and Gold are different: they matter for later chains such as Strong Acid, Power Cells, and Tadpole progression, but the hotter route is not worth forcing while you are still oxygen-starved.

When this guide says to wait for the right adaptation and oxygen plan, it means: unlock Digestion for food reliability, craft the Standard Air Tank, know your return route, and bring a mobility option such as Fins or Air Bladder. If the route feels like gambling on oxygen, leave and come back.

<h2 id="if-you-get-lost">If you get lost or run out of oxygen</h2>

Use these quick fixes instead of forcing a bad route:

- **Cannot find Silver:** return to the Lifepod, reset your direction, then look for the huge coral route and green-flora cave features rather than chasing random ore.
- **Running out of oxygen:** craft Fins first, carry Air Bladder before deeper dives, and treat bubbles or air pockets as checkpoints.
- **Copper but no Scanner:** you still need an Acidic Raion Pouch for the Battery and Quartz for the Scanner recipe.
- **Fins crafted but still stuck:** push Silver next. The Standard Air Tank is the bigger oxygen breakpoint.
- **Sulfur or Gold route feels dangerous:** back out and return later with Digestion, more oxygen, and a pinned recipe.

<h2 id="co-op-resources">Co-op resource split</h2>

In co-op, do not send the whole team into the same cave chasing the same Copper. Subnautica 2 supports up to four players, but the resource loop works better when each player has a different job:

- one player gathers Titanium and Water Slugs near the Lifepod,
- one player handles Copper and Acidic Raion Pouches below the Lifepod,
- one player checks coral domes for Quartz,
- one player scouts the huge coral / Silver route and returns with directions.

Because blueprints and progression are shared in co-op, the team gets more value from dividing routes than from duplicating the same scan or resource run. Store shared materials at the base or Lifepod so the group can craft the next unlock without asking who has the one missing item.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Can I skip Fins and rush the Air Bladder?

You can, but most players should craft Fins early because they improve every resource trip. Air Bladder is the better emergency oxygen tool; Fins are the better route-speed upgrade.

### When should I stop farming Copper?

Stop focusing on Copper once you can craft two Batteries. After that, Quartz for Scanner progression and Silver for Standard Air Tank usually matter more.

### Should I farm Silver before making Fins?

Only if you already know the cave and oxygen reset. For a first playthrough, Fins or Air Bladder make the Silver trip safer and less stressful.

### Should a co-op group split resources or stay together?

Split by route once everyone knows the Lifepod return path. One player can do Copper, one Quartz, one Lifepod basics, and one Silver scouting. Pool everything in shared storage.

### Do I need Salt before Digestion?

Not urgently. Salt becomes more useful after your food loop opens up. Before that, prioritize tools, oxygen, and the resources tied to pinned recipes.
