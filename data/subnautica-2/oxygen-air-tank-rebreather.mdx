<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The safest oxygen progression in Subnautica 2 is Air Bladder → Standard Air Tank → High Capacity Air Tank → Rebreather → Portable Oxygen Generator / vehicle support.** You start around **45 oxygen**, the **Standard Air Tank** pushes you to about **75**, and the **High Capacity Air Tank** pushes you to about **120**. The **Rebreather** does not raise the number; it makes deep dives cost less oxygen.

For the **High Capacity Air Tank**, this guide uses the early southwest / south-southwest colony-ruin data card route, about **250-290m from the Lifepod** and roughly **90m deep**. Some guides point to a farther **Research Center / Alien Ruins** data card after Tadpole Pens progression, so treat the southwest route as the closer early source and the Alien Ruins route as a later alternate. For the **Rebreather**, the cleanest early route is the **south / southeast scrap route around 320-330m**, with the southwest hidden bunker as a strong backup.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="oxygen-numbers">Oxygen numbers that matter</h2>

Before you chase another blueprint, know what each upgrade actually changes. The tanks increase your oxygen pool. The Rebreather changes how fast oxygen drains at depth. Oxygen support tools give you mid-route resets.

<GuideTable
  caption="Subnautica 2 oxygen values and what each breakpoint means."
  columns={oxygenNumberColumns}
  rows={oxygenNumberRows}
/>

Depth is the hidden killer. In shallow water, oxygen is roughly a one-second-per-point timer. Below **100m**, the drain jumps to roughly **3 oxygen per second**; below **200m**, it can jump to roughly **5 oxygen per second**. The Rebreather cancels that depth penalty, which is why a 120-oxygen tank can still feel bad in deep wrecks if you ignore the Rebreather.

<h2 id="high-capacity-crafting">High Capacity Air Tank crafting checklist</h2>

Once the data card unlocks the recipe, the practical crafting chain is:

```text
High Capacity Air Tank
  → Standard Air Tank
  → Plasteel Ingot

Plasteel Ingot
  → 2 Titanium
  → 1 Lithium
```

The main blocker is **Lithium**. Early on, look around the southwest Great Jaw / colony-ruin route. Later, after more progression, lithium becomes easier around the Tadpole Pens / hot-cave side. Do not swim into a lithium route with a tiny tank and no escape plan; you only need enough for the ingot, not a full inventory.

<h3 id="south-southeast-scrap">South / southeast scrap route</h3>

From the Lifepod, head between south and southeast. In compass terms, aim roughly between **150 and 165**. Pass the rock columns, then look for ground-level scrap rather than a big interior structure. The fragment sits in a box on the left side of the scrap area.

This is the route I would point most players to first because it lines up with the common 320-330m southeast descriptions and does not require Repair Tool or Heat Tolerance.

<h3 id="southwest-hidden-room">Southwest hidden colony room</h3>

The southwest hidden-room route is still useful. Head southwest / south-southwest until you see scrap hanging near a cliff and lights below. Drop toward the lights, enter the hidden colony room, and scan the Rebreather on the right.

<h2 id="rebreather-crafting">Rebreather crafting checklist</h2>

Once you scan a fragment, craft the Rebreather from:

```text
Rebreather
  → 2 Fiber Mesh
  → 1 System Chip

Fiber Mesh
  → Fiber
  → Strong Acid

System Chip
  → Wiring Kit
  → 2 Quartz
```

Do not expect your oxygen number to go up after crafting it. The Rebreather is valuable because it makes deeper routes drain less oxygen than they would without it.

<h2 id="portable-oxygen-generator">Where the Portable Oxygen Generator fits</h2>

The Portable Oxygen Generator is not the first thing I would chase, but it belongs in this oxygen spoke because it fills the gap between “I have a tank” and “I can safely operate far away from base.” You can scan broken generator pieces near the **Great Jaw** route, then build it later with the Habitat Builder once you have enough Lithium.

Use it for remote route support, not as a replacement for tanks. It needs a **Basic Battery** to function and refills oxygen over time through electrolysis, so it is best when you are staging a longer resource run or repeated wreck entry.

<h2 id="when-to-use-rebreather">Which setup should I use for each dive?</h2>

Use this decision rule:

```text
Short shallow dive
  → Air Bladder + Standard Air Tank

Long wreck or colony ruin
  → High Capacity Air Tank

Deep wreck, lower cave, or fast-drain route
  → High Capacity Air Tank + Rebreather

Remote repeated route
  → High Capacity Air Tank + Rebreather + vehicle or Portable Oxygen Generator
```

If you are still in the first hour and mostly scanning shallow fragments, the Rebreather can wait. If you are pushing 90m routes, southwest ruins, Repair Tool wrecks, or deeper story routes, it becomes much more valuable.

<h2 id="route-order">Recommended route order</h2>

For a clean first playthrough, use this order:

```text
1. Craft Survival Multitool and Scanner.
2. Make an Air Bladder.
3. Farm Silver and craft the Standard Air Tank.
4. Power the Welcome Center and slot Oxygen Control if you want safer scanning.
5. Build your first base so the full Fabricator / Processor path is available.
6. Grab the High Capacity Air Tank data card from the early southwest / south-southwest colony ruin.
7. Use the Processor to make Plasteel Ingot from Titanium + Lithium.
8. Craft the High Capacity Air Tank.
9. Scan the easiest Rebreather fragment.
10. Add Portable Oxygen Generator or Tadpole support for longer routes.
```

This order avoids the common trap: swimming to a deep upgrade before you have enough oxygen to leave with it.

For the base step, use the <Link href="/subnautica-2/welcome-center-habitat-builder">Welcome Center and Habitat Builder guide</Link>. For the resource step, use the <Link href="/subnautica-2/early-resources">early resources route</Link>. If your next blocker is lithium, cankers, or blocked mineral deposits, the <Link href="/subnautica-2/sonic-resonator-guide">Sonic Resonator guide</Link> helps open the routes that feed the oxygen upgrade chain.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### What is the best first oxygen upgrade in Subnautica 2?

Craft the **Standard Air Tank** first. Air Bladder is useful before that, but the Standard Air Tank is the first permanent oxygen breakpoint.

### How much oxygen do the tanks give?

You start around **45 oxygen**. The **Standard Air Tank** raises that to about **75**, and the **High Capacity Air Tank** raises it to about **120**.

### Where is the High Capacity Air Tank blueprint?

There are multiple reported data-card sources. This guide uses the southwest / south-southwest colony ruin route, around **250-290m from the Lifepod** and roughly **90m deep**. Some written guides point to the Research Center / Alien Ruins route after Tadpole Pens progression; treat that as a later alternate source if you do not use the early southwest route.

### What do I need for Plasteel Ingot?

Use the Processor to make Plasteel Ingot from **2 Titanium and 1 Lithium**. Early lithium can be found around the southwest Great Jaw / colony-ruin route; later lithium becomes easier around the Tadpole Pens / hot-cave side.

### Does the Rebreather increase oxygen capacity?

No. The Rebreather reduces deep-water oxygen drain. It is best paired with a larger tank.

### Where should I get the Rebreather first?

Try the **south / southeast scrap route around 320-330m** first. If you are already working the southwest colony-ruin route, the hidden southwest bunker is also a good one-scan option.

### Do I need multiple Rebreather fragments?

In the current Early Access build, one Rebreather scan is enough to unlock the blueprint. Extra fragments are backups.

### Should I get the High Capacity Air Tank or Rebreather first?

Get the **High Capacity Air Tank** first if your problem is total oxygen. Get the **Rebreather** when your problem is deep-water drain. The strongest setup is both together.
