<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**The fastest Sonic Resonator route is to scan two fragments in the Old Habitat, about 370-420m north / north-northwest of the Lifepod. Enter the lower habitat, scan one fragment immediately to your right, then scan the second in the next room near the floor / toppled table area. After the blueprint unlocks, craft the Sonic Resonator at a Fabricator in your own base, not the basic Lifepod Fabricator.**

The recipe is **1 Basic Battery, 2 Titanium Ingots, 2 Lead, and 1 Wiring Kit**. Build it before you keep pushing blocked wrecks, Bloom Biofilm, large mineral deposits, or the Feedback Resonator upgrade path.

<h2 id="why-sonic-resonator-matters">Why the Sonic Resonator matters</h2>

The Sonic Resonator is not just a mining tool. It is the point where Subnautica 2 starts letting you break the things the Survival Multitool cannot: large ore deposits, Bloom Biofilm, and several growths tied to adaptation routes.

Once you have the blueprint, your goal should be simple: craft it, test it on a useful deposit, then bring it on routes where the game keeps showing you blocked holes, infected growths, or big mineral nodes.

<h2 id="how-to-use-sonic-resonator">How to use the Sonic Resonator</h2>

Equip the Sonic Resonator, aim at a valid target, charge it, then release. On a large mineral deposit, the node breaks into collectible chunks. On Bloom Biofilm or certain infected growths, it clears the blockage or lets the objective continue.

<h2 id="what-can-sonic-resonator-mine">What can the Sonic Resonator mine?</h2>

The useful answer is: anything the game labels like a large mineral deposit or Resonator-only breakable target. In practice, that means you should start thinking of the Sonic Resonator as your bulk resource tool for materials such as Titanium, Lead, Copper, Silver, Quartz, Creature Enamel, and Celestine.

This is where the tool pays for itself. Mine the material that removes your current blocker instead of cracking every deposit you see.

<h2 id="bloom-biofilm-and-adaptations">Bloom Biofilm, cankers, and adaptation routes</h2>

The Sonic Resonator also matters because it clears growths tied to wrecks, caves, and adaptation objectives. If you hit a Bloom Biofilm wall or an infected Angel Comb route that keeps pointing you toward breakable growths, bring the Resonator with first aid, oxygen, and a clean exit path.

The tool opens the route, but it does not make the route safe. Bloom parasites, predators, and oxygen pressure are still the real danger.

<h2 id="feedback-resonator-recipe">Feedback Resonator recipe and material blockers</h2>

After scanning both Feedback Resonator fragments, upgrade the Sonic Resonator at the **Modification Station**.

<GuideTable
  caption="Subnautica 2 Feedback Resonator upgrade materials."
  columns={feedbackCraftColumns}
  rows={feedbackCraftRows}
/>

The material most players underestimate is **Strontium**. You do not pick up Strontium directly from a random early cave. Farm **Celestine** around the Alien Ruins biome, then process it into Strontium at your Processor. Creature Enamel works the same way as a late-zone commitment: you need the Sonic Resonator to break the deposits before you can make Enameled Glass.

<h2 id="recommended-progression-order">Recommended progression order</h2>

Use this order if you are trying to avoid wasted trips:

```text
Scanner and basic oxygen setup
  → Old Habitat Sonic Resonator fragments
  → base Fabricator + Processor setup
  → Basic Battery, Titanium Ingots, Lead, and Wiring Kit
  → craft Sonic Resonator at your base Fabricator
  → mine your current blocker: Lead, Titanium, Quartz, Copper, or Silver
  → clear Bloom Biofilm / blocked objective routes
  → progress far-east exploration and Alien Ruins-side objectives
  → scan Feedback Resonator fragments at Cicada Wreck and Metal Farms
  → gather Creature Enamel, Conduit Crystal, and Celestine / Strontium
  → upgrade at the Modification Station
```

If your current blocker is oxygen or navigation rather than mining, use the <Link href="/subnautica-2/navigation-guide">Navigation Guide</Link> and <Link href="/subnautica-2/tadpole-submarine">Tadpole Submarine guide</Link> before forcing the far-east route.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">Common mistakes</h2>

### Using the Lifepod Fabricator

The Sonic Resonator is crafted at a Fabricator in your own base. If you only check the Lifepod Fabricator, it can look like the recipe is missing even after you scanned the fragments.

### Ignoring Old Habitat and chasing scattered fragments

The scattered cave and Spider Dome fragments work, but Old Habitat is the cleaner route because it has two scans in one location.

### Forgetting the Wiring Kit

A Wiring Kit needs Copper Wire and Silver. If this is your missing piece, do a short Silver run instead of drifting deeper into unrelated objectives.

### Mining with a full inventory

Large deposits can drop enough resources to fill you quickly. Bring storage, clear your pockets, or mine only the resource you actually need.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### How many Sonic Resonator fragments do I need?

You need **two Sonic Resonator fragments**. The fastest route is Old Habitat, where you can scan both fragments in one trip.

### Where is the best Sonic Resonator fragment location?

Old Habitat is the best location because it has two fragments inside and sits roughly 370-420m north / north-northwest of the Lifepod. The north-northwest cave and southwest Spider Dome fragments are good backups.

### What is the Sonic Resonator recipe?

In the current Early Access build, the Sonic Resonator recipe is **1 Basic Battery, 2 Titanium Ingots, 2 Lead, and 1 Wiring Kit**.

### Why can I not craft the Sonic Resonator after scanning it?

Check two things: you need the required materials, and you need to craft it at a **base Fabricator**, not the basic Lifepod Fabricator. You may also need your Processor chain online for Titanium Ingots.

### What does the Sonic Resonator actually do?

It breaks large ore deposits, clears Bloom Biofilm / certain infected blockers, and becomes the base item for the Feedback Resonator upgrade.

### Where do I get Strontium for the Feedback Resonator?

Farm Celestine in the Alien Ruins biome, then process Celestine into Strontium with your Processor.

### Is the Sonic Resonator a weapon?

Not really. It can sometimes disrupt smaller threats, but its real job is mining and clearing breakable progression blockers. Use movement, flares, and your vehicle as your main survival tools.
