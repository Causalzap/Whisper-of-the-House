<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**In the first two hours of Subnautica 2, make the Survival Multitool first, then craft the Scanner, Fins, Air Bladder, and Standard Air Tank before pushing into longer routes.**

Use the Lifepod as your zero point. Start with Titanium near the pod, Copper and Acidic Raion Pouches in the cave below it, Quartz under coral domes, Lucifer Rotsack and Fibrous Pulp near the large coral route, then Silver after you have safer movement and air. Your first major destination is the **Welcome Center**, the large early structure southeast of the Lifepod where you scan Habitat Builder fragments, power the Biolab, and unlock early progression.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="early-access-note">
  Early Access note
</h2>

This guide is written for the opening Early Access build of Subnautica 2. Routes, recipes, and quest pacing may change during updates, so treat the timings as a practical player route, not a speedrun script.

<GuideCallout title="The core rule">
Do not gather randomly in the first two hours. Gather for the next unlock: Multitool, Scanner, Fins, Air Bladder, Standard Air Tank, Digestion, Habitat Builder, and your first powered base.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="first-30-seconds">
  The first 30 seconds: what to click before you swim away
</h2>

Before you leave the Lifepod, do three things:

1. **Open the Fabricator and pin your starting tools.**  
   You want to see what Titanium, Quartz, Copper, Rubber, Fiber, and batteries are actually for.

2. **Check Lifepod storage, but do not waste all food immediately.**  
   Keep emergency food and first aid for when you actually need them. Water Slugs should be turned into bottled water at the Fabricator instead of eaten raw.

3. **Make a simple plan before diving.**  
   Your first loop is not “explore everything.” It is: **3 Titanium → Survival Multitool → Copper / Acidic Raion Pouch → battery → Scanner**.

This tiny decision tree prevents the most common opening mistake: swimming away with no tool plan, filling your inventory, then returning with the wrong materials.

<h2 id="why-this-order">
  Why this order works
</h2>

The table above is not just a checklist. Each step removes a specific early blocker: the Multitool unlocks harvested materials, the Scanner unlocks fragments, Fins and Air Bladder make cave routes safer, the Welcome Center opens Habitat Builder and Biolab progress, and Digestion plus Silver stabilize food and oxygen before base crafting takes over.

<h2 id="survival-multitool">
  Make the Survival Multitool first
</h2>

The Survival Multitool should be your first crafted tool. You only need a small amount of Titanium near the Lifepod, and the payoff is immediate: you can harvest the plant materials that turn into Fiber, Rubber, batteries, and later crafting chains.

Do not treat the Multitool as a weapon-first item. In the opening route, it is mainly your harvesting key.

<h2 id="scanner-battery">
  Build the Scanner after your first battery
</h2>

The Scanner is the tool that turns exploration into progression. It unlocks blueprints, identifies scannable objects, and helps you stop guessing which wreck parts matter.

To make the Scanner route smooth:

- collect Copper from the Lifepod cave,
- cut Acidic Raion Pouches with the Multitool,
- craft a battery,
- then craft the Scanner.

From this point onward, scan aggressively. Scan fragments, room objects, useful machines, decorative pieces, and anything with a scanner prompt. Some items unlock immediately; others need multiple scans.

<h2 id="water-food">
  Handle water and food without wasting your start
</h2>

Water is usually easier than food in the first stretch. Grab Water Slugs, bring them back, and use the Fabricator to turn them into bottled water. Do not make a habit of eating them raw, because that can hurt your hunger situation.

Food becomes more reliable after Digestion. Until then, treat starting food and emergency blocks as backup, not snacks.

Once you unlock Digestion, local cooked fish becomes a real food plan instead of a risky experiment.

<h2 id="fins-air-bladder">
  Craft Fins and Air Bladder before risky caves
</h2>

Fins are not flashy, but they make everything safer. Faster swimming means less oxygen wasted, less panic, and better recovery when a route goes wrong.

The Air Bladder is your emergency button. Use it for extra air or a fast return to the surface when you dive too low too early.

This is why I do not recommend rushing Silver with only starter oxygen and no backup. You can do it, but new players lose more time dying or retreating than they save.

<h2 id="welcome-center">
  Find the Welcome Center and scan both Habitat Builder fragments
</h2>

The Welcome Center is the first major early structure after the Lifepod. From the Lifepod, swim **southeast toward the large visible structure in open water**. If you are unsure, look for the building silhouette rather than relying on one fixed compass number, because small route differences and signal visibility can make exact bearings feel inconsistent.

The first Habitat Builder fragment is near the **front-right exterior** of the Welcome Center. Scan it with the Scanner.

<h2 id="biolab-power">
  Power the Biolab with the red battery box
</h2>

Inside the Welcome Center, look for the red battery box on the left-side interior route. Insert a battery there to bring the Biolab and nearby systems online.

This matters because the Biolab gives you early Biomod choices such as movement or oxygen-support options. You do not need to understand the entire Biomod system yet. For the first two hours, just think of the Biolab as an early survival upgrade station inside the Welcome Center.

<h2 id="anita-digestion">
  Follow Anita’s signal when it appears
</h2>

After early NOA progression, Anita’s signal appears as one of your first important objective markers. It points you toward an adaptation plant and a black box.

Follow the signal north-northeast from the Lifepod area when it appears. Interact with Anita’s black box, then attune to the nearby adaptation plant. This unlocks Digestion, which lets local cooked food become safe and useful.

If you do not see Anita’s signal yet, continue your early tool route, scan useful objects, check NOA updates in the Lifepod, and make sure signals are enabled in your Signals tab.

<h2 id="silver-air-tank">
  Get Silver for the Standard Air Tank
</h2>

After you have better movement and a safer air plan, Silver becomes the next big target. Start from Anita’s area, look for the green-flora cave route, and use visible glowing landmarks to stay oriented.

Bring an Air Bladder and a first aid kit if you have one. If you picked up a distraction flare during the intro, bring one as an emergency predator decoy for routes that feel dangerous. Do not dive deeper just because you saw one more deposit. Mine enough Silver for the Standard Air Tank, then leave.

The Standard Air Tank is one of the first upgrades that changes how the game feels. More oxygen means less panic, better scanning, and safer cave routes.

<h2 id="first-death">
  What to do after your first death
</h2>

Your first death is not the end of the run. You reprint back at the Lifepod, and the game can mark a death container or beacon where your dropped items can be recovered.

Do this after dying:

1. **Do not panic-craft replacements immediately.**  
   Check whether your lost items are recoverable first.

2. **Open your Signals tab.**  
   Turn the death marker back on if it is hidden.

3. **Return with empty inventory and oxygen backup.**  
   Bring Air Bladder if you have it. Do not carry your whole resource stock while recovering old items.

4. **Loot the death container, then leave.**  
   Do not turn a recovery trip into a second deep exploration route.

This section is especially important if you drowned while trying to push a cave too early. The lesson is usually not “avoid the cave forever.” It is “come back with Fins, Air Bladder, Standard Air Tank, or a clearer route.”

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Can I skip the Welcome Center and build a base anyway?

Not realistically for a smooth early route. The Welcome Center gives you key scans, including Habitat Builder progress, and it also introduces the Biolab and early survival upgrades. You can wander first, but you will usually come back because the crafting chain needs those unlocks.

### Anita’s signal is not showing. What should I do?

Check the NOA in your Lifepod, keep scanning early objects, and open your Signals tab to make sure the marker is not hidden. Anita is an early objective, but if you have not progressed enough, finish your basic tool loop first.

### I found only one Habitat Builder fragment. Where is the second one?

The first fragment is near the front-right exterior of the Welcome Center. The second is lower, beneath the main Welcome Center area. Use oxygen bubbles or nearby air pockets, swim down from the center area, and scan the lower fragment before leaving.

### Can I go for Silver before Fins or Air Bladder?

You can, but new players should not. Fins reduce wasted oxygen, and Air Bladder gives a recovery option if the cave route takes longer than expected.

### What should I do if I die and lose my items?

Respawn at the Lifepod, turn on the death marker in Signals if needed, return with empty inventory and oxygen backup, open the death container, recover the items, and leave instead of continuing deeper.
