<h2 id="when-to-build-first-base">When should you build your first base?</h2>

The right time to build is not “after I find the perfect location.” That is how you stay trapped in the Lifepod for too long.

I usually start building my first base when one or more of these things happen:

- my Lifepod storage is full and I am dropping useful materials just to make space,
- I keep swimming back to the Lifepod only to craft one small thing,
- I am doing longer trips and need a safer oxygen stop,
- I am starting to think about Tadpole / Moonpool progression,
- I have enough basic materials that another resource run feels wasteful without storage.

That is the real trigger. Your first base is not about luxury. It is about stopping the early-game friction: no storage, awkward crafting, short oxygen runs, and no clear place to stage materials.

<GuideCallout title="Player rule">
If you are asking “Should I build a base yet?” because your inventory is full again, the answer is probably yes.
</GuideCallout>

<h3 id="lifepod-default-location">Safe default: near the Lifepod / Welcome Center route</h3>

This is the easiest recommendation for most players. You already know the route, the area is familiar, and you are close enough to early scans and materials that the base immediately feels useful.

The only thing I would not do is build directly on the shallowest patch of terrain. Give yourself one side that faces deeper water. That one decision saves a lot of pain when you start placing Moonpool or Tadpole-related pieces later.

<h3 id="east-current-location">Power-focused option: around 60m east of the Lifepod</h3>

If you want a stronger long-term starter spot, look around the area roughly **60m east of the Lifepod** where the current is easier to use. This is not required for a first base, but it makes Hydroelectric planning feel much cleaner once you unlock the right power pieces.

This is the kind of spot I like because it gives you a simple early workshop now and a better power route later. Just remember that Hydroelectric only helps if the turbine is actually in a current and the transmitters can link back to the base cleanly.


<h3 id="sparse-plains-base-location">Sparse Plains option: the safest biome-style first base</h3>

If you want a more open biome pick instead of building right beside the Lifepod, **Sparse Plains** is the safer first-base choice I would look at. The big reason is simple: it has lower threat density than the more dangerous biome edges, while still giving you enough open space to place rooms, storage, power links, and later Moonpool / Tadpole pieces without constantly fighting the terrain.

I still would not treat Sparse Plains as automatically safe. Scout the edge before building. If you see Collector, Wakaker, or any Leviathan-sized patrol activity nearby, do not force the location. Swim away from that boundary and give yourself roughly 300–400m of distance before committing to a base site.

A plains-style biome can feel great for building because it gives you more space and fewer awkward walls. But I would treat any “safe biome” advice as conditional. If the area is near a Leviathan boundary or patrol path, it is not a starter-base location anymore.

Do a short safety swim before placing the room. If you see a Collector, Wakemaker, or obvious large predator patrol, do not build there just because the terrain looks good.

<h2 id="where-not-to-build">Where not to build your first base</h2>

The worst first base is not the ugly one. It is the one you are scared to return to.

Avoid building:

- directly on a Leviathan patrol route,
- near a biome edge marked by major predator activity,
- in water so shallow that vehicle pieces get blocked,
- inside tight terrain where rooms and transmitters are painful to place,
- so far from the Lifepod that every early trip becomes a commute.

<GuideCallout title="Leviathan location warning">
If you spot a Collector, Wakemaker, or another Leviathan-sized threat while scouting a base site, leave. I would swim in the opposite direction and give the area roughly 300–400m of distance before thinking about building. You can always come back later with better tools.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="starter-layout">Minimum starter base layout</h2>

For the first version, I keep it simple:

```text
Basic room
→ Hatch
→ Power
→ Fabricator / station corner
→ Storage wall
→ Habitat Beacon
→ empty side for expansion
```

That is enough to stop using the Lifepod as a messy junk drawer. Once this is built, every trip feels cleaner because you come home, unload, craft, and leave again.

Do not fill every wall immediately. Leave one wall or one side of the base open so you can add more rooms, power links, or Moonpool access without tearing everything apart.

<h2 id="storage-setup">Storage setup: Wall Locker or Floor Locker?</h2>

Storage is where a first base either feels good or turns into chaos. I would rather have ugly, obvious locker names than waste five minutes checking every container.

<GuideTable
  caption="Storage choices for your first Subnautica 2 base."
  columns={storageColumns}
  rows={storageRows}
/>

My usual setup is simple:

- **Wall Lockers** for categories I check constantly,
- **Floor Lockers** for bulk titanium, overflow, and moving materials,
- one obvious **Do Not Spend** locker for rare pieces,
- one **Vehicle Parts** locker so Tadpole / Moonpool materials do not vanish into general storage.

In co-op, this matters even more. A messy solo base is annoying; a messy co-op base turns into four people asking, “Who used all the silver?”

<h2 id="fabricator-layout">Fabricator and crafting layout</h2>

Put your Fabricator / crafting stations right next to storage. This sounds obvious, but it is one of those small choices that saves time every single trip.

The layout I like is:

```text
[Storage wall]   [Fabricator / crafting]
[Bulk lockers]   [Rare materials]
[Vehicle parts]  [Open space to move]
```

The goal is to swim in, dump everything, craft what you need, and leave again. If you have to cross the room every time you need copper, the base is already too annoying.

<h2 id="power-setup">Power setup: Solar first, Hydroelectric later</h2>

Start with whatever early power gets the base online, but understand the limits.

Solar-style power is good near the surface because it is easy and cheap to start. The downside is that it gets worse the deeper you build, and it does not generate power at night. If your base is shallow and small, Solar is fine. If your base is deeper, larger, or running more equipment, you should plan for something stronger.

Hydroelectric power is the upgrade I would pay attention to early. The turbine needs to sit in a current, and the power has to be carried back to the base with transmitters. When the connection is right, you should see the transmitter link back toward the turbine / base instead of just sitting there doing nothing.

<h2 id="habitat-beacon">Do not skip the Habitat Beacon</h2>

A Habitat Beacon is not exciting, but I would build one early anyway. Subnautica 2 does not become easier because your base exists; it becomes easier because you can reliably get back to it.

Without a beacon, your first base can disappear into “somewhere near the Lifepod” surprisingly fast, especially at night, in bad visibility, or after a long resource run.

My rule is simple: if a base has storage worth protecting, it gets a beacon.

<h2 id="dismantle-move-base">Do not worry if you build in the wrong place</h2>

This is the part new players stress about too much: your first base is not a permanent contract.

If you hate the location, dismantle it and move. Base pieces can be taken apart and the materials are returned, so the real cost of a bad starter base is usually time, not a ruined save.

The clean way to move is:

1. build a Floor Locker or temporary storage at the new spot,
2. move the important materials first,
3. dismantle the old rooms and parts,
4. rebuild the core layout at the new location,
5. move the rare materials last so they do not get lost in the shuffle.

<GuideCallout title="Build mistake fix">
If you built too shallow, too far away, or too close to danger, move the base before decorating it. It is much easier to relocate a small workshop than a huge messy base.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="moonpool-planning">Plan for the Moonpool before you need it</h2>

This is the part I would not ignore. You do not need the Moonpool or Tadpole Deck on minute one, but you should build like they are coming.

The Tadpole Deck needs enough space below it. If your base is jammed over shallow ground, the placement can get blocked and you will have to rebuild or awkwardly extend the base out over deeper water.

Before I commit to a location, I check:

- Is there deeper water close to one side of the base?
- Can I add another room without hitting terrain?
- Can the Tadpole Deck hang below the base without clipping into the seafloor?
- Is there enough room to approach it comfortably?

<h2 id="co-op-base">Co-op base setup</h2>

For co-op, I would make the base even more obvious than usual. Nobody wants to spend a whole night sorting lockers while everyone else is asking where the copper went.

A good co-op starter base should have:

- one shared storage wall,
- one “rare materials” locker that players do not casually spend from,
- one crafting station area,
- one vehicle parts area,
- one food / water dump,
- one clear expansion direction.

The trick is to make the base readable at a glance. If everyone can swim in, unload, craft, and leave without asking questions, the base is working.

<h2 id="forward-outpost-guide">When to build a forward base or oxygen outpost</h2>

Your first base should carry the early game, but I would not force it to do everything forever. Once exploration pushes you into deeper or more dangerous routes, a tiny forward outpost starts making more sense than one giant central base.

I would build a forward outpost when:

- the swim back home feels longer than the actual resource run,
- you are repeatedly running out of oxygen in the same region,
- you keep dying or turning back before reaching deeper materials,
- you need a staging point near a future vehicle route,
- you want a safer place to dump loot before pushing farther.

A forward outpost does not need to be fancy. My minimum version is:

```text
Small room
→ Hatch
→ basic power
→ Habitat Beacon
→ optional Floor Locker
```

That is enough for oxygen, navigation, and emergency storage. If the route becomes important, then I add more lockers or a Fabricator later. If I only need the outpost as a breathing stop, I keep it tiny.

<GuideCallout title="Forward outpost rule">
A forward outpost is not a second mansion. It is a checkpoint. Build it small, mark it clearly, and only expand it if you keep using that route.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Should I build near the Lifepod?

Yes. A nearby starter base is fine, especially near the Lifepod / Welcome Center route. Just avoid building so shallow that Moonpool or Tadpole placement becomes annoying later.

### Is the east-of-Lifepod current worth building near?

It can be. Around 60m east of the Lifepod is useful if you want to plan for Hydroelectric power, because currents matter once you start using turbines and transmitters.

### What should my first base include?

At minimum: a room, hatch, power, Fabricator / crafting setup, storage, Habitat Beacon, and one clear expansion side for future Moonpool / Tadpole work.

### Should I use Wall Lockers or Floor Lockers?

Use both. Wall Lockers give 20 slots and are better for labeled categories. Floor Lockers give 30 slots and are better for bulk materials, temporary moving storage, and co-op overflow.

### Is Solar enough for the first base?

Solar is enough for a shallow starter base, but it becomes worse with depth and does not generate at night. If the base is deeper or more power-hungry, plan for stronger power like Hydroelectric.

### Why does deep water matter?

Because Moonpool / Tadpole Deck placement can be blocked if the base is too close to the seafloor. Leave one side of the base facing deeper water.

### What if I build in the wrong place?

Move it. Dismantle the base pieces, recover the materials, transfer storage into temporary containers, and rebuild the same small layout somewhere better.

### Should I build a second base?

Eventually, yes, but start small. A forward outpost can be just one room, a hatch, power, a Habitat Beacon, and maybe one Floor Locker.
