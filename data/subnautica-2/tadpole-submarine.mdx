<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**To unlock the Tadpole in Subnautica 2, scan 3 Tadpole Fragments, then build it through the Moonpool / vehicle construction chain using 2x Titanium Ingot, 1x Glass, 1x System Chip, and 1x Power Cell.**

The Tadpole is your first true submarine-style vehicle. It gives you safer oxygen routing, longer material runs, portable storage options, upgrade paths, and a better way to push toward deep routes. The big mistake is treating it as invincible: you still need power, repairs, safe parking, and depth awareness.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="tadpole-route-loop">
  The Tadpole route loop you are building toward
</h2>

This guide is not only about unlocking the blueprint. The real value of the Tadpole is that it turns dangerous routes into repeatable routes.

```text
Base
→ Tadpole parked near deep entry
→ scan / gather route
→ return to Tadpole for oxygen
→ unload at base
→ recharge / repair
→ repeat with better upgrades
```

That loop is why Fragment locations, Moonpool placement, Portable Lockers, Power Cells, repairs, and crush depth all matter. If the Tadpole is parked badly, unpowered, or overloaded with rare materials, it stops being a safety tool and becomes another recovery problem.

<h2 id="fragment-1-ruby">
  Fragment #1: Blackbox Ruby route
</h2>

The first useful Tadpole route is near the Ruby / black box progression area. Look around the wreck debris and nearby structure pieces instead of only following the objective marker.

This is a good first fragment because it naturally connects to the midgame material path. The risk is that players push too deep after the scan and lose the route back.

Take this approach:

1. follow the Ruby / black box route,
2. scan the Tadpole Fragment,
3. check only the nearby debris,
4. leave before oxygen becomes uncomfortable.

<h2 id="fragment-3-bunker">
  Fragment #3: Colonist Bunker route
</h2>

The third route points toward the colonist bunker direction, roughly southwest from the Lifepod and far enough away that you should not treat it like a casual shallow swim.

Before going:

- bring a clear oxygen plan,
- keep inventory space open,
- mark the route if Beacons are unlocked,
- avoid carrying rare materials you do not need for the scan.

<GuideCallout title="Do not miss the bunker reward">
The Colonist Bunker route can also include a Biobed unlock tied to inventory expansion progress. If you came all the way for Fragment #3, scan the room carefully before leaving.
</GuideCallout>

This is where your <Link href="/subnautica-2/navigation-guide">navigation habits</Link> matter. A fragment is not useful if you die trying to leave with it.

<h2 id="portable-lockers">
  How to attach Portable Lockers to the Tadpole
</h2>

This is one of the best Tadpole tricks and many players miss it. The Tadpole has rear attachment points that can support Portable Lockers or other utility attachments depending on your current build and unlocks.

Use Portable Lockers for planned material runs, not for permanent storage. They are strongest when the run has one clear purpose: Titanium, Quartz, Silver, Salt, base materials, or co-op drop-off.

Label your route mentally:

```text
Tadpole storage = current run only
Base storage = long-term materials
Rare materials = return immediately
```

If you overload the vehicle with important materials, then park it badly or lose power, you turn your best tool into your biggest problem.

<h2 id="power-cell">
  Power Cell management
</h2>

The Tadpole runs on power. When the Power Cell drains, you need to swap or recharge instead of assuming the vehicle will always be ready.

Good habits:

- check power before leaving base,
- keep a spare Power Cell when routes get longer,
- recharge old cells at a Power Cell Terminal when your base supports it,
- do not park your only vehicle deep with an almost-empty cell.

For co-op, assign one player as the vehicle lead. That person checks power, storage, parking, and upgrades before the group leaves.

<h2 id="repair-tool">
  Bring the Repair Tool before dangerous routes
</h2>

The Repair Tool matters because the Tadpole can take damage. If you are pushing into predator routes, tight wreck routes, or deeper water, do not treat repairs as optional.

Bring the Repair Tool when:

- predators are nearby,
- the route is deep enough that losing the Tadpole would strand you,
- you are carrying important materials,
- you are testing a new biome.

If you do not have the Repair Tool yet, play the Tadpole conservatively. Use it as oxygen support and transport, not as armor.

<h2 id="crush-depth">
  Crush depth and deep route limits
</h2>

The Tadpole has a safe depth limit. If you push beyond its current crush depth, the vehicle can take pressure damage. Depth upgrades make the Tadpole more useful later, but the first version should be treated as an early-to-mid route vehicle, not a free pass to every biome.

Before descending:

- check the vehicle depth warning,
- mark the entry route,
- keep enough power to return,
- leave before damage becomes a recovery problem.

<h2 id="how-to-use">
  How to actually use the Tadpole well
</h2>

The Tadpole makes you safer, but only if you use it as a route tool.

Use it for:

- oxygen relay between base and deep entry,
- material transport with Portable Lockers,
- safe return point near wrecks,
- co-op meeting point,
- scouting before building a forward base.

Avoid using it as armor, a junk drawer, or a reason to ignore crush depth. Keep the vehicle powered, repaired, and parked where it helps the return route.

<h2 id="tadpole-pens">
  What to do after building the Tadpole
</h2>

After building the Tadpole, your next goals are not just “go deeper.” Use the vehicle to open safer progression routes:

- revisit fragment and wreck routes you skipped,
- move materials for better base setup,
- work toward Tadpole Pens / later route objectives,
- build a forward outpost near a repeated deep entry,
- start collecting upgrade materials.

If you are still missing the base chain, go back to the <Link href="/subnautica-2/first-base-setup">First Base Setup guide</Link>. If you keep getting lost, use the <Link href="/subnautica-2/navigation-guide">Navigation Guide</Link> before pushing farther.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How many Tadpole Fragments do I need?

You need 3 Tadpole Fragment scans to unlock the Tadpole blueprint.

### Can I build the Tadpole without a Moonpool?

No. The Tadpole belongs to the vehicle construction chain, so you need the Moonpool / vehicle setup rather than only the Lifepod Fabricator.

### What materials do I need for the Tadpole?

The current recipe is 2x Titanium Ingot, 1x Glass, 1x System Chip, and 1x Power Cell.

### Can teammates use my Tadpole in co-op?

Yes. Treat the Tadpole as a shared vehicle in co-op. Agree on where it is parked and what materials are inside.

### Can I attach storage to the Tadpole?

Yes, the Tadpole can support utility attachments such as Portable Lockers through rear attachment points when available.

### What happens if the Tadpole exceeds crush depth?

The vehicle can take pressure damage if it goes beyond its safe depth. Upgrade depth before treating the Tadpole as a deep-biome vehicle.

### How do I recharge the Tadpole Power Cell?

Swap in a charged Power Cell or recharge drained cells through your base power / Power Cell Terminal setup when available.

### Do I need the Repair Tool?

Yes, if you plan to use the Tadpole in predator routes or deeper areas. It is your main way to recover from vehicle damage.
