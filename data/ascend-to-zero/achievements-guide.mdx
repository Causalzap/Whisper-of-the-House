<h2 id="missable-achievements">
  Are there any missable achievements?
</h2>

I have not found a one-run-only achievement that permanently locks itself out
on the current save.

The game is built around repeatable runs, boss retries, difficulty clears,
Bunker progression, and repeated equipment processing. A failed setup usually
costs time or materials rather than the achievement itself.

Several achievements are still easy to delay:

- spending Coins before `Millionaire`;
- using a Conversion chance above 45% while working on `Failed Again?!`;
- keeping Emergency Escape equipped during the Scarlet Pirate low-Health
  challenge;
- clearing the Stage 4 final boss before recording `All According to Plan`;
- or unlocking more equipment slots before preparing enough Mythic and Set
  items for `Collector of Rarities`.

These are planning traps rather than permanent missables.

<h2 id="best-achievement-route">
  Best route for all 58 achievements
</h2>

I use four phases instead of chasing each achievement as soon as it appears.

### Phase 1: Natural Institute progression

Finish:

- the tutorial;
- the survivor rescues;
- one Hyper Skill;
- Lv.10,000;
- the early special enemies;
- Javier's first successful Conversion;
- and Terrae Motus.

I do not interrupt a strong run to force an intentional death.

### Phase 2: Bunker and permanent systems

Concentrate resources on:

- one Avatar reaching Lv.5;
- one Avatar Tech Chip reaching Lv.8;
- all weapon slots;
- two Lv.5 Gadgets;
- one long-term item reaching Lv.20;
- Mai's upgrades;
- the Fate Editor;
- and one planned Mythic conversion.

These investments also improve the later difficulty clears.

### Phase 3: Controlled and Avatar-specific setups

Return to easier routes with more permanent power and complete:

- three elite deaths;
- the boss-at-10% death;
- the Glitch defeat;
- Blossom Blade's ten-kill Avatar Skill;
- Golden Gunslinger's five-kill projectile;
- Windborne Spearmaster's Dash kills;
- Scarlet Pirate's 10-Health Time Stop;
- and One Strike.

### Phase 4: Endgame and collection cleanup

Finish:

- Stage 3 contamination and its boss;
- Stage 4 on Easy, Normal, Hard, and Zero;
- Perfect Game;
- Multidimension Mode 12;
- BIVI's player, enemy, and item categories;
- then Fate Breaker during final cleanup.

This order lets one permanent upgrade contribute to several achievements
instead of creating a separate grind for every condition.

<h2 id="run-wasting-achievements">
  Three achievements that can waste an entire run
</h2>

These are the three conditions I plan before entering the Time Machine.

<h3 id="millionaire">
  Millionaire: hold the Coins instead of spending them
</h3>

`Millionaire` requires me to **own 1,000,000 Coins at once**.

I stop optional spending as the balance approaches the target. The biggest
delays usually come from:

- expensive Entry Stats;
- weapon and Gadget slots;
- shop rerolls;
- permanent weapons that are only small upgrades;
- and nonessential Bunker purchases.

I verify the current balance directly instead of treating total lifetime Coins
earned as progress.

<h3 id="failed-again">
  Failed Again?!: keep every attempt at 45% or lower
</h3>

The displayed Conversion success rate must be **45% or less** when the attempt
fails.

My setup is:

1. Use a disposable temporary item.
2. Add no valuable conversion materials.
3. Confirm the displayed chance is 45% or lower.
4. Attempt the Conversion.
5. Repeat until ten qualifying failures have occurred.

I never use:

- a Mythic item;
- a Set item;
- my main permanent weapon;
- or equipment needed for the current progression wall.

<h3 id="scarlet-low-health">
  Then I Took an Arrow to the Knee: remove Emergency Escape
</h3>

Scarlet Pirate must activate Time Stop at **10 Health or less**.

`Emergency Escape` automatically activates Time Stop and fully restores Health
when Health falls to 30% or below, preventing a controlled low-Health setup.

My route is:

1. Remove Emergency Escape.
2. Remove automatic healing and large shields.
3. Enter a low-level room.
4. Let one weak enemy lower Health gradually.
5. Watch the numerical Health value.
6. Activate Time Stop manually at 10 Health or less.

I avoid bosses and enemies capable of moving directly from safe Health to
defeat.

<h3 id="stage-four-order">
  Stage 4 order
</h3>

My order is:

1. Let the final boss defeat me once for `All According to Plan`.
2. Clear Stage 4 on Easy.
3. Repeat the route on Normal.
4. Move to Hard after the route is stable.
5. Leave Zero until the rooms and final boss are repeatable.
6. Attempt `Perfect Game` separately from the first clears.

I do not combine the no-damage condition with the first successful route. A
learning run should reveal the dangerous rooms instead of trying to satisfy
every condition at once.

<h3 id="multidimension-order">
  Multidimension Mode 12 order
</h3>

I clear Mode 12 in this order:

1. Easy.
2. Normal.
3. Hard.
4. Zero.

The Easy clear is where I identify:

- which rooms consume the most Limited Time;
- which elites are worth fighting;
- whether the permanent main weapon still carries the damage;
- and whether one mistake immediately ends the run.

I raise the difficulty only after the previous clear becomes repeatable.

For `Abyss of the Predator`, I prioritize the Terrae Motus kill during the
Multidimension run. I continue deeper only when the remaining Limited Time and
Build are still stable.

<h3 id="fate-breaker">
  Fate Breaker cleanup order
</h3>

`Fate Breaker` has the in-game description **Fight against fate**.

I leave it until the rest of the fate-related progression is already complete:

1. finish Stage 4;
2. complete the Fate Editor;
3. clear the available Multidimension difficulties;
4. revisit remaining late-game choices and fate-related interactions;
5. then check whether it remains as the final incomplete achievement.

This prevents an unclear late-game condition from distracting from achievements
with direct, trackable requirements.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ascend to ZERO achievements FAQ
</h2>

### How many achievements are in Ascend to ZERO?

The launch version has 58 achievements.

### Are any Ascend to ZERO achievements missable?

No permanently missable achievement has been identified. The repeatable run,
boss, Conversion, upgrade, and difficulty systems allow later cleanup.

### How difficult is the achievement completion?

My provisional launch estimate is 8/10. Most early achievements are
manageable, but Stage 4 Zero, Multidimension Mode 12, Perfect Game, and full
Handbook completion create a demanding endgame.

### How long does it take to unlock every achievement?

A provisional estimate is 60–100+ hours. Equipment RNG, Handbook collection,
late difficulty clears, and item-upgrade materials can extend the total.

### Are there any online or multiplayer achievements?

No launch achievement condition requires co-op, multiplayer, or competitive
online play.

### Does the game require multiple playthroughs?

A single save supports cleanup, but the game requires many repeatable runs and
separate difficulty clears.

### Can I clean up achievements after completing the Institute?

Yes. Bosses, stages, Avatar setups, Bunker upgrades, and equipment systems
remain available for later cleanup.

### Do the Stage 4 difficulty achievements stack?

The achievement list contains separate conditions for Easy, Normal, Hard, and
Zero. I plan one successful clear for each listed difficulty.

### Are the completion percentages live?

No. Every percentage on this page is a Steam global achievement snapshot
captured on July 14, 2026.
