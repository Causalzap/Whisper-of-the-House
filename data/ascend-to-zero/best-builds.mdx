<h2 id="avatar-tier-list">
  Verified Ascend to ZERO Avatar Tier List
</h2>

This is a progression Tier List for the Institute, Terrae Motus, and the first
Desert route.

I rank each Avatar by:

1. how consistently it reaches the next elite;
2. whether its core skill protects Limited Time;
3. how dependent it is on one weapon category;
4. and how safely it handles an unfamiliar boss.

### Why only four Avatars are ranked

Ascend to ZERO has six playable Avatars, but a Tier List is only useful when
the ranking is based on complete combat loops rather than one achievement
description.

Windborne Spearmaster and Frost Moon Hacker remain outside this ranking until
their Avatar Skill panels, passive bonuses, weapon interactions, and full boss
runs are verified.

Their character-specific achievement routes remain covered in the{" "}

  Ascend to ZERO Achievement Guide

### Why Chrono Child is S Tier

Chrono Child's base release deals **550% damage to nearby enemies**, and each
equipped weapon reduces Time Stop cooldown by **3%**.

The safest general upgrades also reinforce this same loop:

- slower Time Flow;
- shields after Time Stop;
- Avatar Skill damage;
- separate damage when Time Stop ends;
- and Limited Time recovery.

The Build remains functional even when one specific weapon does not appear.

### Why Blossom Blade and Golden Gunslinger are A Tier

Both can deal more practical damage than Chrono Child after their equipment
package is complete.

Blossom Blade needs Swords, close-range positioning, and enough sustain to
remain beside the target.

Golden Gunslinger needs actual Guns plus projectile and reload support. Without
those pieces, its safe range does not compensate for the lost damage uptime.

### Why Scarlet Pirate is B Tier

Scarlet Pirate can create enormous boss burst, but the Build has more failure
points:

- self-damage from Bombard effects;
- long reloads;
- unsafe explosion ranges;
- and greater dependence on Health and defensive support.

A completed Pirate Build is strong. An incomplete one can destroy its own run.

  Diagnose why the Build keeps failing
</h2>

I do not replace the Avatar after every failed run. First, I identify what
actually ended it.

### Use the end-of-run damage report

The final report shows which weapons, Avatar Skills, Gadgets, and triggered
effects actually contributed damage.

I remove a high-numbered item when:

- its attack pattern misses repeatedly;
- it spends too much time inactive;
- its range does not match the route;
- it receives no bonuses from the Avatar;
- or it remains near the bottom of the report over several attempts.

The displayed Attack Multiplier matters only when the attack consistently
connects.

<h2 id="weapon-tags">
  Check weapon tags before buying or converting
</h2>

The game separates weapon and attack tags such as:

- Sword;
- Spear;
- Gun;
- Cannon;
- Drone;
- melee;
- ranged;
- projectile;
- physical;
- AoE;
- and Bombard.

Tags can overlap, but they are not interchangeable.

A Budget Handgun and Budget Grenade Launcher are both ranged projectile
weapons. One is tagged as a **Gun**, while the other is a **Cannon**.

Golden Gunslinger receives passive scaling from equipped Guns. Scarlet Pirate
receives its passive scaling from equipped Cannons.

This is the only check I use before spending Coins or conversion resources:
read the exact item tag rather than judging the weapon by appearance.

### When I buy a permanent weapon

I buy from Seis when:

- the item has the correct tag;
- the current permanent weapon is clearly underpowered;
- the purchase fixes the problem shown in the damage report;
- and enough Zero Coins remain for the next Time Machine Entry Fee.

I skip it when the item is only a small numerical improvement or would force me
to remove Limited Time, XP Gain, or another important Entry Stat.

### My Chrono Child priority

1. Time Shield or another slower-Time-Flow effect.
2. Avatar Skill damage.
3. A separate damage effect when Time Stop ends.
4. Limited Time recovery.
5. XP from elites and bosses.
6. Health, Defense, or a release shield.
7. Movement Speed only when reaching the next elite is the problem.

### Time Residue, Time Shield, or Heavy Skill

**Time Residue** increases Time Stop charges by one and raises cooldown by 5%.

I take it when another freeze is more valuable than a small cooldown increase.

**Time Shield** slows Time Flow by 15% and increases Damage Reduction by 5%.

This is my most consistent choice while learning bosses.

**Heavy Skill** increases Skill Damage by 35% but raises Time Stop cooldown by
25%.

I take it when the Build already survives safely and needs a larger release
hit. I avoid it when repeated freezes are still necessary to cross boss
patterns.

### Base skill damage and Tech Chip damage are separate

Chrono Child's base Avatar Skill is the displayed **550% nearby damage**.

A Tech Chip that adds damage when Time Stop ends is a separate triggered
effect. I keep those values separate when comparing damage instead of replacing
the base 550% number with the Chip's percentage.

### When faster Time Flow is worth taking

Faster Time Flow can add strong Attack or Attack Speed bonuses, but the Limited
Time clock also moves faster.

I take it when the added damage shortens the fight more than the faster clock
shortens the run.

I avoid it when I already reach Terrae Motus or the next elite with fewer than
10 seconds.

### My Blossom Blade route

1. Equip the strongest practical permanent Swords.
2. Add nearby-enemy or melee damage.
3. Add Avatar Skill damage and AoE.
4. Take Blood Leakage or another reliable Bleed source.
5. Add maximum Bleed stacks or Bleed damage.
6. Add life steal, Health, or close-range Defense.
7. Use Dash to line up Battojutsu rather than replacing damage choices with
   unnecessary mobility.

`Blood Leakage` adds **10% Bleed chance on hit** and increases maximum Bleed
stacks by five.

`Spatial Slash` deals damage equal to **300% of Attack** along the Dash path.

`Area Deployment` creates a nearby damage zone whose damage increases closer
to its center.

These effects support the same close-range plan. I do not take one isolated
Bleed effect and call it a complete Bleed Build.

### Battojutsu must travel through the target

I freeze before the enemy attack, move to the side or behind the group, and aim
the forward movement through the target line.

The Build can look weak even with strong numbers when Battojutsu travels into
empty space.

### When a lower-numbered Sword is better

I replace a larger ranged weapon when it receives no Sword, melee, Bleed, or
Avatar Skill bonuses and contributes little in the damage report.

A weaker-looking Sword can produce more total damage after the passive Attack
Speed, nearby damage, and Bleed effects are included.

### Quick Reload is the core quality-of-life effect

Quick Reload causes Dash to reload the projectile weapon currently being
reloaded. It also grants a short Tactical Reload effect with **+50% Crit Chance
for one second**.

My combat loop becomes:

1. Fire until the current magazine is empty.
2. Activate Time Stop before the enemy pattern reaches me.
3. Dash to a safe firing angle.
4. Release Time Stop with the weapon reloaded.
5. Repeat.

This turns a movement input into damage uptime.

### Echo of Death and Armor Piercing Rounds

`Echo of Death` creates an explosion equal to **50% of the projectile's
damage** when the projectile ends.

I prefer it when projectiles finish inside a group or near a boss surrounded by
common enemies.

`Armor Piercing Rounds` increases Projectile Damage by **10% each time the
projectile pierces a target**.

I prefer it in narrow rooms where enemies naturally line up.

### How I tell whether reload is the real problem

I watch one complete attack cycle:

- If the weapon fires often but damage remains low, I need Attack, Pierce,
  Projectile Size, or boss damage.
- If the weapon deals good damage but spends most of the fight inactive, I
  need Quick Reload, Reload Speed, stamina, or magazine support.
- If the projectile misses, more reload speed will not fix the Build.

### My Scarlet Pirate route

1. Equip Cannons or Bombard weapons.
2. Add Bombard damage.
3. Fix the actual reload bottleneck.
4. Add direct Attack or boss damage.
5. Add maximum Health.
6. Add a shield or healing.
7. Keep the Avatar or Hyper Skill ready for the next major target.

### With Style is a calculated risk

`With Style!` increases Bombard damage by **90%**, but damages the player when
their own Bombard attack makes contact.

I take it when:

- the Cannon can attack from a safe distance;
- I understand the explosion radius;
- and the Build has Health, healing, or a shield.

I skip it when the Cannon explodes close to the character or the boss forces
close-range positioning.

### Single Cannon Shot

`Single Cannon Shot` fixes Ammo Capacity at one, increases Reload Speed by
**60%**, and increases Bombard damage by **50%**.

It works with a weapon already designed around one large hit.

I avoid it when the Cannon needs several shots to complete its damage pattern.

### Emergency Escape

`Emergency Escape` activates Time Stop and fully restores Health when Health
falls to 30% or below. It can activate once every 10 seconds.

This is excellent for progression and risky Bombard Builds.

It must be removed for the Scarlet Pirate achievement that requires manually
activating Time Stop at 10 Health or less. The complete achievement setup is in
the{" "}

<h2 id="time-stealing-enemies">
  How I adjust the Build for Time-Stealing enemies
</h2>

A wide AoE Build can waste valuable seconds clearing common enemies while the
special target continues removing Limited Time.

When a Time-Stealing enemy appears, I temporarily change target priority:

1. Stop clearing the room indiscriminately.
2. Activate Time Stop before the target moves out of reach.
3. Move into the correct attack angle.
4. Use aiming mode, a direct Avatar Skill, or the strongest single-target
   weapon.
5. Return to ordinary enemies only after the marked target dies.

This is one situation where one fast single-target weapon is more useful than
another large room-clearing effect.

<h2 id="difficulty-scope">
  How difficulty changes these recommendations
</h2>

This Tier List is written for launch progression and Normal first clears.

On Easy and Normal, specialist Builds can outperform Chrono Child once their
weapon package appears.

On harder settings, reliable Health, Defense, shielding, and time recovery
become more important. That generally favors Chrono Child's safe loop and makes
Scarlet Pirate's self-damage choices less forgiving.

I do not present this launch Tier List as a confirmed Stage 4, Zero difficulty,
or Multidimension Mode ranking without complete runs from those modes.

<h2 id="when-to-return">
  When to continue the run and when to rebuild
</h2>

I continue when:

- the latest elite dies quickly;
- one main weapon clearly contributes damage;
- the next boss is within a reachable progression bracket;
- and at least one recovery effect keeps Limited Time stable.

I return when:

- a same-level elite consumes most of the time it restores;
- the Build lacks a permanent main weapon;
- the first Desert enemy survives several complete attack cycles;
- one boss hit removes the rest of the clock;
- or a valuable temporary item needs storage or conversion.

A failed Build normally needs one clear fix:

- the correct weapon category;
- more direct damage;
- one recovery effect;
- enough Health for one mistake;
- or a shorter route.

It rarely needs every permanent upgrade at once.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Build mistakes that waste the most runs
</h2>

### Ranking a specialist without its required equipment

Blossom Blade without Swords, Golden Gunslinger without Guns, and Scarlet
Pirate without Cannons do not show the actual strength of those Avatars.

### Treating Starting Level as damage

Starting Level skips solved rooms. It does not increase the damage dealt after
reaching the boss.

### Filling slots with unrelated attacks

Every slot should add practical damage, survival, time, or a useful trigger.
More equipment is not automatically a better Build.

### Choosing faster Time Flow without checking kill speed

The damage bonus must shorten the fight more than the faster clock shortens the
run.

### Entering optional challenge rooms too early

I leave when the first enemy does not die quickly enough to repay the time
spent.

### Converting an item only because it is rare

I convert equipment because it completes the Build, not because its border has
a higher rarity.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ascend to ZERO Builds FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best Avatar for a first clear?

Chrono Child is the safest verified first-clear Avatar because its release
damage, slower-Time options, shields, and recovery all support the same combat
loop.

### What is the current Avatar Tier List?

For verified Institute and early Desert progression: Chrono Child is S Tier;
Blossom Blade and Golden Gunslinger are A Tier; Scarlet Pirate is B Tier.
Windborne Spearmaster and Frost Moon Hacker are not ranked without complete
Build data.

### Why are only four Avatars ranked?

A useful Tier List needs complete skill, weapon, Tech Chip, and boss-run
evidence. The remaining two Avatars do not yet have enough verified material
for reliable Build recommendations.

### Which Avatar is best for melee?

Blossom Blade is the strongest verified melee specialist when the Build has
Swords, nearby damage, Bleed, and enough sustain to remain close.

### Which Avatar is best for ranged damage?

Golden Gunslinger is the most consistent verified ranged option when equipped
with Guns, projectile scaling, and reload support.

### How do I check whether a weapon matches the Avatar?

Read the exact item tag in the inventory or shop. Do not judge it only by
whether the attack looks ranged or fires a projectile.

### How do I know reload is lowering my damage?

Watch one full attack cycle. When the weapon deals good damage while firing but
spends most of the fight inactive, solve reload, magazine, stamina, or Dash
availability before adding more Attack Speed.

### What is the safest Chrono Child Tech Chip?

Time Shield is the safest verified progression choice because it slows Time
Flow by 15% and adds 5% Damage Reduction.

### What is the best Terrae Motus Build?

Use one coherent Avatar and weapon package, boss damage, reliable time recovery,
and enough survival for one mistake. Elite kill speed matters more than matching
Lv.20,000 exactly.

### How should I deal with Time-Stealing enemies?

Stop clearing common enemies, use Time Stop to reach the marked target, and
commit direct burst before it removes more Limited Time.

### Are these final endgame Builds?

No. These recommendations cover the Institute, Terrae Motus, and early Desert
progression. Stage 4, Zero difficulty, and Multidimension Mode may produce a
different final Meta.
