reach Lv.20,000 before entering the Central Control Room or before entering the
Desert.

<h2 id="three-level-20000-checks">
  The three Lv.20,000 labels are not the same requirement
</h2>

This is the main source of confusion near the end of the Institute.

The game places three similar-looking pieces of progression close together:

1. the **Lv.20,000 Central Control Room** marker;
2. the **Terrae Motus** boss inside that route;
3. and the later objective to **Reach Lv.20,000 in the Desert Zone**.

Only one action opens Stage 2:

> Defeat Terrae Motus and use the EXIT after the fight.

<h2 id="do-not-return-to-bunker">
  Do not look for a Stage 2 button in the Bunker
</h2>

The first Level Two transition is route-based.

The Bunker contains the Time Machine, Entry Stats, Supply Shop, Fate Editor,
Avatar upgrades, and item processing, but none of those menus starts Stage 2.

After Terrae Motus dies, I:

1. let the victory effects finish;
2. activate Time Stop before opening the inventory when needed;
3. equip only an obvious upgrade;
4. close the inventory;
5. and walk through the visible EXIT.

I do not spend the remaining clock comparing every temporary item.

The progression trigger is the EXIT itself.

<h2 id="desert-level-objective">
  How the Desert Lv.20,000 objective works
</h2>

There are two normal outcomes when the new objective appears.

### You enter below Lv.20,000

Continue into the Desert and gain the missing levels there.

The objective does not send you back to the Institute. A run can enter the new
zone below the displayed objective and still progress by defeating the first
manageable Desert enemies.

### You enter at or above Lv.20,000

The objective can complete immediately.

When that happens, continue toward the next route. There is no second trigger
to find.

<h2 id="first-desert-room">
  What to do in the first Desert room
</h2>

The Desert is an immediate difficulty increase.

The first enemies can be around Lv.20,000 or slightly higher even when the
character enters below that range.

  <Image

  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-500 dark:text-slate-400">
    Entering below the nearby enemy levels does not mean the transition failed.
    Use the first manageable encounters to close the gap.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

My first-room order is:

1. Activate Time Stop before the room becomes crowded.
2. Check which nearby enemy can be killed fastest.
3. Equip an obvious post-boss upgrade when one is already available.
4. Kill enough normal enemies to close the level gap.
5. Target the first elite once its kill time is reasonable.
6. Ignore optional challenge rooms until the normal route feels stable.

### When I skip the first challenge room

I skip it when:

- the first normal enemy survives several complete attack cycles;
- the Limited Time clock is already close to empty;
- the main Avatar Skill or Hyper Skill is unavailable;
- or one enemy attack removes most of my Health.

The first Desert entry is a progression check, not a requirement to clear every
optional encounter.

### When I let the run end

I stop forcing the attempt when:

- ordinary enemies cannot be killed efficiently;
- the first elite consumes the entire clock;
- one hit repeatedly causes a defeat;
- or the Institute Build depended on temporary equipment that will disappear.

Stage 2 remains unlocked.

I can return with a better permanent weapon, more Attack, additional Limited
Time, or a stronger Entry Stat package.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ascend to ZERO Stage 2 and Desert FAQ
</h2>

### How do I unlock Stage 2 in Ascend to ZERO?

Reach the Central Control Room, defeat Terrae Motus, and use the EXIT after the
boss. Confirm that you want to continue beyond the Institute.

### Do I need to be Lv.20,000 before fighting Terrae Motus?

No. The displayed level is a recommendation for the final Institute route, not
an exact requirement for entering the Central Control Room.

### Do I need to be Lv.20,000 before entering the Desert?

No. Reach Lv.20,000 in the Desert Zone is an objective given after the Desert
has already opened.

### Is the Central Control Room boss Terrae Motus?

Yes. The Central Control Room is the Lv.20,000-marked boss route, and Terrae
Motus is the boss inside it.

### Where is the Stage 2 button in the Bunker?

There is no separate button for the first transition. Use the EXIT after
defeating Terrae Motus.

### Which dialogue option continues the route?

Choose “I will save the world from destruction” at the confirmation prompt
after the Terrae Motus EXIT.

### Why did the Desert objective complete immediately?

Your current level already met or exceeded Lv.20,000 when the objective
appeared.

### Why am I below Lv.20,000 in the first Desert room?

That is allowed. Gain the missing levels from enemies inside the Desert.

### Why can I not go back after entering the Desert?

The run has crossed the Level Two transition. Complete the new objective or
allow the run to end, then improve the next setup in the Bunker.

### Is One Strike required to open Stage 2?

No. Any successful Terrae Motus kill can open the post-boss route.

### What confirms that Terrae Motus counted?

The boss disappears, the EXIT becomes available, the route continues into
Level Two, and End of the Predator unlocks.
