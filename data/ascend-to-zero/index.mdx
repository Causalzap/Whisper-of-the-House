<h2 id="first-run-plan">
  Best Ascend to ZERO first-run plan
</h2>

The opening becomes easier once I stop treating it like a normal
bullet-heaven game.

I do not remain in a room until every common enemy disappears. I use the level
markers as a route ladder, kill enough enemies to approach the next useful
bracket, and then look for the elite, survivor, Area Expander, or boss that
provides permanent progress.

<GuideTable
  caption="A practical first-run route through the Institute."
  columns={firstRunColumns}
  rows={firstRunRows}
/>

The early route moves through low-level rooms before escalating into Institute
checkpoints around **Lv.1,800, Lv.4,800, Lv.10,800, and Lv.20,000**.

Those jumps look extreme, but the displayed number is not the only readiness
check.

I also ask:

- Can the current weapons still kill a nearby elite efficiently?
- Can I survive the first dangerous attack?
- Is there enough Limited Time to reach the target?
- Does the room contain permanent progression?

### When I continue the current run

I keep moving when:

1. the next route contains an elite, survivor, boss, or permanent unlock;
2. the current weapon still kills enemies near my level efficiently;
3. and the Limited Time clock is healthy enough to reach the next target.

### When I return to the Bunker

I return when:

- no nearby elite can restore time;
- the next objective greatly exceeds the current damage;
- I have just rescued a survivor or activated permanent content;
- I need to protect a valuable temporary item;
- or spending more time in the current run risks losing more than I can gain.

A survivor rescue is already a successful run. I do not risk permanent
progress for a few ordinary drops.

<h2 id="limited-time">
  How the Limited Time clock works
</h2>

Each loop begins with a small pool of **Limited Time**, initially around 30
seconds.

That does not mean the entire run lasts only 30 real-world seconds.

The clock pauses during Time Stop, and several systems can extend or recover
it:

- elite and boss kills;
- Tech Chips;
- Time Traces;
- Cubes;
- and Time Machine Entry Stats.

A run can continue far beyond its starting clock when the route keeps reaching
valuable targets.

Limited Time is also tied to survival.

When Health reaches zero, a defeat can consume part of the remaining clock
instead of ending the run immediately. The attempt finishes when Limited Time
is exhausted.

That makes both offense and defense valuable:

- damage kills elites before the clock disappears;
- Health provides room for mistakes;
- Defense reduces the cost of enemy attacks;
- shields protect the moment Time Stop ends;
- and time recovery repairs a run after an error.

A high-damage Build can still fail when one boss attack repeatedly removes
time. A defensive Build can fail because it cannot kill the elite before the
clock expires.

<h2 id="time-stop">
  How to use Time Stop without wasting it
</h2>

Time Stop freezes:

- enemies;
- enemy projectiles;
- normal weapon attacks;
- and the Limited Time clock.

While time is frozen, I can:

- move through a projectile pattern;
- reposition beside or behind an elite;
- collect experience and dropped equipment;
- inspect the room;
- open the inventory;
- equip a weapon or Gadget;
- and choose the next route.

Releasing Time Stop activates the current Avatar Skill.

The basic Chrono Child rhythm is:

1. Let normal weapons deal damage.
2. Freeze before a dangerous attack lands.
3. Move to a safe position near the main target.
4. Collect nearby drops.
5. Release Time Stop to trigger the Avatar Skill.
6. Freeze again before the next dangerous pattern.

I do not wait until Health is almost empty. An early Time Stop prevents damage,
protects Limited Time, and places the Avatar Skill where it can hit the target.

### When I remain inside Time Stop

I stay frozen when:

- a projectile wall still blocks the route;
- the elite is turning toward me;
- useful equipment remains on the floor;
- I need to inspect a newly dropped item;
- or I am still standing in a dangerous position.

There is no benefit to resuming combat before the room is safe.

<h2 id="elite-route">
  Why elites matter more than common enemies
</h2>

Elites are the main engine of a successful run.

They can provide:

- Limited Time recovery;
- large experience gains;
- Tech Chip choices;
- weapons, armor, and Gadgets;
- Zero Coins and Zero Cells;
- survivor progression;
- and access to later routes.

Common enemies are most useful when only a small amount of experience is
needed before the next elite.

I stop farming them when:

- the next elite is already reachable;
- the group no longer provides meaningful levels;
- the clock is becoming unstable;
- or backtracking would cost more time than the enemies can return.

### When common enemies are still worth killing

I clear them when:

- one nearby group will reach the next level bracket;
- they are tightly packed for an Avatar Skill;
- the current weapon or Tech Chip rewards repeated kills;
- I need to test the current damage;
- or the next elite is still far above my level.

I do not chase one surviving enemy across a completed room.

<h2 id="survivor-order">
  Which survivors should you rescue first?
</h2>

Survivor rescues provide permanent progression. I prioritize a survivor room
over an ordinary reward room whenever both are available.

The main Institute services are restored through **Seis, Gabriella, Javier,
Mai, and BIVI**.

<GuideTable
  caption="The main survivor unlocks and the Bunker services they restore."
  columns={survivorColumns}

### Seis: permanent shop equipment

Seis restores the Supply Shop.

I buy a shop item when:

- the current permanent weapon is clearly underpowered;
- the item matches the preferred Avatar or weapon direction;
- and the purchase leaves enough Coins for the next loop.

I skip it when:

- it is only a minor numerical improvement;
- the current temporary weapon is already carrying the run;
- or the purchase would remove the Entry Stats needed to reach the next elite.

### Gabriella: Entry Stats

Gabriella restores Time Machine Entry Stats.

A permanent improvement to future entries is often more useful than unlocking
several equipment slots while the inventory contains only one strong permanent
item.

### Javier: item processing and storage

Javier becomes important when temporary equipment starts outperforming the
permanent inventory.

His systems let me:

- store an item for later;
- attempt to convert it into permanent equipment;
- or extract unwanted gear into Zero Cells.

### Mai and BIVI: long-term progression

Mai strengthens equipment that is worth keeping.

BIVI records discoveries and turns broad exploration into permanent collection
progress.

I do not stop the first Institute push to complete every upgrade or Handbook
entry. These systems become more important after the main route is stable.

<h2 id="bunker-progression">
  How the Underground Bunker improves each run
</h2>

A productive Institute loop should improve at least one permanent system:

- a survivor service;
- an Area Expander;
- an Entry Stat;
- an equipment slot;
- a permanent weapon, armor piece, or Gadget;
- an Avatar level;
- a permanent trait;
- or the BIVI Handbook.

### Activate Area Expanders when the route is safe

Area Expanders can restore new Bunker content, enemies, challenges, and
services.

I prioritize one when:

- it is already on the current route;
- no nearby elite must be skipped to reach it;
- or it unlocks a system needed by the current equipment.

I leave it for another run when reaching it requires crossing a room that the
current Build cannot clear efficiently.

Gabriella lets me purchase bonuses for the next loop.

The available Entry Stats include:

- Starting Level;
- Limited Time;
- XP Gain;
- Attack;
- Coin Gain;
- Free Entry;

### Early priority: time first, then progression speed

My normal order is:

1. enough Limited Time to reach the first important elite;
2. XP Gain while several level brackets remain relevant;
3. Attack when same-level elites survive too long;
4. Starting Level after the opening rooms have become repetitive;
5. Coin or Entry Fee support when the preferred package becomes expensive.

Starting Level gives an immediate visible number, but XP Gain can produce more
levels across a complete run.

I move toward Starting Level only after the early Institute route is solved and
repeating it provides little useful practice.

### Check the Entry Fee before buying equipment

A stronger entry package can cost significantly more Zero Coins.

Before buying shop equipment or opening a new slot, I check:

1. the next Entry Fee;
2. the Coins remaining after the purchase;
3. whether the item is permanent;
4. and whether the next route needs time, damage, or levels.

A weaker permanent weapon with enough Limited Time to reach the elite is often
better than an expensive weapon that forces me to remove the entire entry
package.

<h2 id="equipment-processing">
  Temporary equipment, Conversion, and Extraction
</h2>

Temporary equipment appears faded and is destroyed when another entry begins.

It can still be used during the current run. Time Stop provides a safe moment
to open the inventory and equip a stronger temporary weapon or Gadget.

Javier adds four decisions.

### Use it during the current run

I equip the temporary item immediately when it:

- clearly improves damage or survival;
- helps reach the next survivor or elite;
- and does not break the current weapon synergy.

### Store it

I use the Warehouse when:

- the item is much stronger than the permanent inventory;
- the current conversion chance is uncomfortable;
- or more Cells and conversion materials are needed.

Storage is better than gambling away a rare upgrade immediately.

### Convert it

Conversion attempts to preserve a temporary item permanently.

Failure destroys the item.

I convert when:

- the weapon matches the Avatar;
- it fixes a real damage or survival problem;
- a similar permanent item is not cheaply available;
- and the success chance is worth the risk.

I do not convert every rare-colored item. A high-rarity item with the wrong
weapon tag or irrelevant modifiers still wastes resources.

### Extract it

Extraction destroys an item and returns Zero Cells.

I extract:

- outdated permanent equipment;
- duplicate weapons that do not improve the Build;
- items with poor modifiers;
- and temporary equipment I would never use or convert.

<h2 id="equipment-slots">
  When to unlock weapon and Gadget slots
</h2>

I unlock a slot only when a useful permanent item is ready to fill it.

My early order is:

1. obtain one reliable permanent weapon;
2. unlock a second weapon slot when a second useful weapon is available;
3. obtain one permanent defensive or time-focused Gadget;
4. unlock the second Gadget slot when the combination provides more value than
   another Entry Stat;
5. add more weapon slots after the permanent inventory can support them.

Empty slots provide no combat power.

One strong permanent weapon, one useful Gadget, and a stable Entry Stat package
can progress further than several empty or poorly matched slots.

</h2>

My basic rule is:

> Zero Coins start the next loop. Zero Cells improve what survives between

I delay random purchases until these essentials are stable:

- enough Limited Time to reach the first elite;
- one reliable permanent weapon;
- one defensive or utility Gadget;
- and enough Coins for the next entry.

<h2 id="tech-chips">
  Which Tech Chips should beginners take?
</h2>

Tech Chips are temporary upgrades for the current run.

I choose the effect that fixes the current bottleneck instead of selecting the
highest rarity automatically.

### When Limited Time is the problem

I prioritize:

- Limited Time recovery;
- maximum Limited Time;
- boss or elite time recovery;
- recurring time recovery;
- slower Time Flow;
- extra Time Stop uses;
- or Time Stop cooldown support.

### When damage is the problem

I prioritize:

- Avatar Skill damage;
- elite and boss damage;
- XP from elites and bosses;
- weapon-tag damage matching the current equipment;
- projectile, melee, or Bombard bonuses;
- or direct Attack scaling.

### When survival is the problem

I prioritize:

- shields after Time Stop;
- damage reduction;
- Health recovery;
- maximum Health;
- or defensive effects that fit the current Time Flow setup.

### Be careful with faster Time Flow

Faster Time Flow can provide strong offensive bonuses, but it also makes the
Limited Time clock drain faster.

I use it when the extra damage will shorten the fight more than the faster
clock hurts the run.

I avoid it when:

- learning an unfamiliar boss;
- reaching every elite with an unstable clock;
- or surviving longer is more important than increasing peak damage.

<h2 id="avatars">
  Which Avatar should you upgrade first?
</h2>

Chrono Child is the simplest starting Avatar because releasing Time Stop deals
area damage around the character and directly teaches the basic combat loop.

I stay with Chrono Child until I understand:

- when to freeze;
- where to stand before releasing time;
- how quickly an elite must die to repay the time spent reaching it;
- and which upgrades improve the Avatar Skill.

After that, I choose an Avatar based on permanent equipment:

- Blossom Blade for Sword and close-range Avatar Skill play;
- Golden Gunslinger for Guns and projectiles;
- Scarlet Pirate for Cannons and Bombard attacks;
- or another Avatar when its preferred equipment already exists in the
  permanent inventory.

I do not spread early Zero Keys across every Avatar.

One developed character provides more value than several characters that have
not reached their important Tech Chips or upgrades.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Beginner mistakes that waste the most time
</h2>

### Spending before checking the Entry Fee

I confirm the next Time Machine cost before buying shop equipment, random
rewards, or an empty slot.

### Unlocking equipment slots too early

A slot is useful only when a permanent weapon or Gadget is ready to fill it.

### Treating every rare drop as a conversion target

Rarity does not fix the wrong weapon tag, poor modifiers, or a Build that
cannot use the item.

### Expecting faded equipment to survive automatically

Temporary equipment disappears on re-entry unless Javier stores or converts
it.

### Continuing after permanent progress with no safe target ahead

After rescuing a survivor or activating a Bunker system, I return when no
nearby elite can restore the clock.

### Ignoring the end-of-run damage report

The damage report reveals whether a weapon, Avatar Skill, or Tech Chip actually
contributed. I remove impressive-looking effects that consistently provide
little real damage.

<h2 id="faq">
  Ascend to ZERO beginner FAQ
</h2>

### Does Ascend to ZERO really give you only 30 seconds?

Each loop starts with a small Limited Time pool, but Time Stop pauses the clock
and several enemies, items, Tech Chips, and Entry Stats can recover or extend
it.

### Does Limited Time decrease during Time Stop?

No. The Limited Time clock pauses while Time Stop is active.

### Can weapons attack while time is frozen?

Normal weapons also freeze. Use the pause to move, collect drops, manage
equipment, and reposition. Releasing Time Stop activates the current Avatar
Skill.

### What happens when Health reaches zero?

A defeat can consume part of the remaining Limited Time. The run ends when the
Limited Time clock is exhausted.

### Should I kill every enemy?

No. Prioritize elites, survivors, bosses, Area Expanders, and permanent
objectives. Kill common enemies when they provide needed levels or can be
cleared quickly.

### What should I upgrade first?

Begin with Limited Time and XP Gain. Add Attack when elites survive too long,
then add Starting Level after the early rooms become repetitive.

### Why did my equipment disappear?

Faded equipment is temporary and is destroyed on re-entry. Javier can store
it, convert it into permanent equipment, or extract it into Zero Cells.

### Should I convert every rare item?

No. Convert an item when it matches the preferred Avatar, fixes a real Build
problem, and is worth the risk. Store exceptional equipment when the current
conversion chance is uncomfortable.

### When should I return to the Bunker?

Return after permanent progress, when no nearby elite can restore time, when
the next route greatly exceeds the current damage, or when a valuable temporary
item needs to be stored.

### Which Avatar is best for beginners?

Chrono Child is the most direct starting choice because the Avatar Skill
reinforces the basic freeze, reposition, and release rhythm.

and continue into the Desert. The{" "}
