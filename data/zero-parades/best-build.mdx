<h2 id="best-build-short-answer">
  ZERO PARADES Best Build Short Answer

For most first playthroughs, use the **Safe First Run** spread below.

It gives you:

- strong dialogue through **Personalism** and **Cold Read**,
- practical investigation through **Blueprints**,
- clue interpretation through **Poetics**, **Records**, and **Grey Matter**,

<h2 id="best-starting-preset">
  Best Starting Preset or Custom Choice
</h2>

If you are choosing between preset and custom, pick **custom** if you want the safest first run.

If you do not want to think about numbers, pick the preset that leans most toward **Relation / social reads**. ZERO PARADES starts with people, pressure, memory, lies, and strange social context. A pure Action start can work, but it does not explain the opening as well.

**Pick Relation if you want the opening to talk back. Pick Intellect if you want clues to make sense. Pick Action if you want to survive tense scenes.**

The best first run mixes all three, but Relation should usually be your anchor.

<h2 id="best-skills-to-prioritize">
  Best Skills to Prioritize First
</h2>

For a safe first run, prioritize these skills first:

1. **Personalism** — your main people skill.
2. **Cold Read** — helps you read behavior before committing.
3. **Blueprints** — helps with routes, city logic, and practical leads.
4. **Poetics** — helps with lateral interpretation and cultural weirdness.
5. **Records** or **Grey Matter** — keeps the worldbuilding and clues more readable.
6. **Coordination** or **Instincts** — gives you Action backup.

Do not overinvest in only one of them before you understand the route. A 6 in Personalism is useful, but a 6 in Personalism with terrible clue reading can still leave you lost.

The best opening shape is:

**one social anchor, one clue anchor, one weird-reading skill, and one physical backup skill.**

<h2 id="how-exertion-changes-builds">
  How Exertion Changes Build Choices
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zero-parades/zero-parades-exertion-third-die.webp"
    alt="Exertion check in ZERO PARADES showing an extra die and a higher chance to pass."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Exertion can rescue an important check, but it adds pressure. A good build reduces how often you need it.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

A build is not only about passing checks. It is also about how often you need to **Exert**.

By default, checks use two dice plus your relevant skill and modifiers. Exertion improves your odds by adding a third die and keeping the better two dice, but it raises the related stress meter. In many checks, that cost is effectively **+5 stress** in the relevant category.

<GuideTable
  caption="How Exertion changes build decisions in ZERO PARADES."
  columns={exertionColumns}
  rows={exertionRows}
/>

This is why the safe build spreads points across common early checks.

If your build is too narrow, you will be tempted to Exert constantly. If you Exert constantly, you raise Fatigue, Anxiety, or Delirium. If a meter gets too high, you can become Pressured and lose access to Exertion when you need it most.

For the full mechanics page, read the <Link href="/zero-parades/stress-exertion">ZERO PARADES stress and Exertion guide</Link>.

<h2 id="gear-matters">
  Gear Matters: Use Items to Patch Weak Checks
</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zero-parades/zero-parades-gear-skill-bonus.webp"
    alt="Inventory screen in ZERO PARADES showing gear and skill modifiers."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Gear is not just cosmetic. A single item can be the difference between forcing Exertion and passing cleanly.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

This is the most important build habit most players miss:

**Check gear before spending stress.**

ZERO PARADES lets clothing and held items affect skills. That means your starting build does not need to solve every check by itself. If a roll matters and you have time, use your inventory first.

<GuideTable
  caption="Early items and gear that matter for ZERO PARADES builds."
  columns={gearColumns}
  rows={gearRows}
/>

Not every inventory item is a gear piece.

The **Sock Purchase Order** and **Mysterious Red Disc** matter because they move routes forward, not because they fix your skill sheet. Treat them as lead items: the purchase order points toward Carmuna and Bootleg Bazaar, while the red disc is an unresolved media clue you carry into later investigation.

The practical order is:

1. Read the check.
2. Check the skill it uses.
3. Open inventory.
4. Equip anything that improves that skill.
5. Look for more context or modifiers.
6. Exert only if the result still matters.

The **Operant Toolkit** is a good early example because it can give a Blueprints bump while also acting as a practical tool. The **Vostatok Blendcoat** is another useful example because it shows how clothing can carry a skill modifier.

Do not confuse clue items with gear, though. The **Sock Purchase Order** and **Mysterious Red Disc** matter, but they matter because they open leads, not because they fix your skill sheet.

<h2 id="disco-elysium-players">
  Build Advice for Disco Elysium Players
</h2>

If you are coming from Disco Elysium, do not try to rebuild Harry.

ZERO PARADES has fewer skills, different pressure rules, and Exertion adds a new risk layer. You can still bring over the good habits: read carefully, check clothes, accept some failed checks, and let the character become strange.

But the build logic changes.

In Disco Elysium, a weird internal voice could carry a whole roleplay identity. In ZERO PARADES, you also need to ask what kind of **stress** that identity keeps creating. A social Cascade can raise Anxiety. A mental Cascade can raise Delirium. A physical Cascade can raise Fatigue.

The best Disco-friendly first build is:

**Personalism 5, Cold Read 4, Blueprints 4, Poetics 4, Records 3, Grey Matter 3, Coordination 3, Instincts 3.**

That gives you the reading-heavy feel you probably want without making the opening too fragile.

For the full comparison, read the <Link href="/zero-parades/disco-elysium-guide">ZERO PARADES guide for Disco Elysium players</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

<h3 id="what-is-the-best-45-point-build">
  What is the best 45-point build in ZERO PARADES?
</h3>

Use the Safe First Run spread: Personalism 5, Cold Read 4, Blueprints 4, Poetics 4, Coordination 3, Instincts 3, Entanglement 3, Grey Matter 3, Records 3, Statehood 3, Nerve 3, with lower backup values in the remaining skills.

<h3 id="which-preset-is-best-for-beginners">
  Which preset is best for beginners?
</h3>

Pick the preset that leans closest to Relation or social reading. If you prefer clues and strange objects, pick the Intellect-style preset. Use Action only if you want a more physical first run.

<h3 id="can-a-bad-build-ruin-my-run">
  Can a bad build ruin my run?
</h3>

Usually no. Gear, Exertion, Conditioning, extra context, and alternate routes can all help you recover from weak checks. A bad build may make the opening rougher, but it does not automatically ruin the run.

<h3 id="should-i-use-exertion-to-fix-my-build">
  Should I use Exertion to fix my build?
</h3>

Use Exertion for important checks, not as your default build fix. If gear can raise the skill, try gear first. Exertion improves the roll but adds stress.

<h3 id="what-skills-should-disco-elysium-players-pick">
  What skills should Disco Elysium players pick first?
</h3>

Start with Personalism, Cold Read, Blueprints, Poetics, Records, and Grey Matter. That keeps the reading-heavy Disco Elysium feel while adapting to ZERO PARADES' spy-focused systems.

<h3 id="does-starting-build-lock-content">
  Does my starting build lock me out of content?
</h3>

Not in a simple all-or-nothing way. A low skill can close or weaken some options, but ZERO PARADES often gives other routes, gear fixes, or ways to accept the consequence and keep moving.

<h3 id="is-gear-important-for-builds">
  Is gear important for builds?
</h3>

Yes. Gear can change skill values and help you pass checks without spending stress. Always check clothing and held items before using Exertion on a major roll.

<h3 id="which-conditioning-is-best-for-relation-build">
  Which Conditioning is best for a Relation build?
</h3>

Favor Thoughts that raise Personalism, Cold Read, Nerve, Statehood, or other social-route potential without adding a drawback that punishes your normal dialogue or item habits.

<h3 id="should-i-min-max-second-playthrough">
  Should I min-max on a second playthrough?
</h3>

Yes, if you already understand the game. First runs are better with flexible builds. Second runs are where extreme Action, strange Intellect, heavy Relation, or drawback-heavy Conditioning becomes more interesting.
