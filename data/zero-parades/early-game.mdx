<h2 id="early-game-overview">
  ZERO PARADES Early Game Overview
</h2>

The opening of ZERO PARADES can feel messy because the game does not hand you one clean objective marker and tell you exactly what to do next.

You wake up in a safe house, your partner Pseudopod is incapacitated, your controller wants you to leave, and the room is full of objects that do not fully explain themselves yet. The best way through the opening is to treat the room like a spy scene, not a normal RPG tutorial.


The best way through the opening is to treat the room like a spy scene, not a normal RPG tutorial. Search first, turn objects into leads, then follow the person or place those leads point toward.

<h2 id="inspect-pseudopod">
  Inspect Pseudopod First
</h2>

Pseudopod is the center of the opening scene.

Start by checking his condition and the objects on him. The game is not just asking whether he is alive or dead. It is asking you to understand why your supposed partner is out of action before your mission has even started.

Search his clothing carefully. His jacket contains several items, but the most important one for early progression is the purchase order.

Inside Pseudopod's jacket, you can find a purchase order for socks linked to **Carmuna** and **Bootleg Bazaar**.

This is easy to underestimate because it sounds absurd. Do not ignore it.

In ZERO PARADES, strange small objects are often how the game gives you direction. The purchase order gives you:

- a vendor lead,
- a marketplace lead,
- a reason to ask a local,
- and a clear destination once Constance confirms it.

The purchase order is more immediately useful than the business card because it points to a person and a place you can actually reach.

</h2>

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zero-parades/zero-parades-red-disc-stereo.webp"

Follow the cord from Pseudopod's headphones to the stereo and inspect the disc deck.

You should find a single red disc inside. Play it, read the scene, then take the disc with you.

Do not expect this to solve itself immediately. The red disc is a loose end, not a one-click puzzle. Your goal in the safe house is to recognize that it matters and keep it in your inventory for later.

The important beginner rule:

**If the game turns an object into a loose end, do not treat it as flavor.**

<h2 id="phone-call">
  Answer the Phone, But Keep Investigating
</h2>

After the red disc, the phone call pushes the scene forward.

Your controller wants you out. That can make it feel like the safe house is done.

It is not.

This is one of the first moments where ZERO PARADES teaches you how to read against pressure. A character gives you an order, but the room still contains leads. Listen to the order, then finish the room.

Before going downstairs, make sure you have searched:

- Pseudopod,
- his jacket,
- the stereo,
- the bathroom trousers,
- and the desk area.

The other piece is still in the room.

The opening is teaching you this pattern:

**check the obvious object, then check the weird small object near it.**

After finishing the safe house search, go downstairs and speak with Constance.

She is important because she turns the purchase order from a strange item into a real destination. Pseudopod was connected to Bootleg Bazaar, and the purchase order gives you a reason to go there.

Constance also gives practical direction: the bazaar is near the old Esilk factory, and you should not confuse the route with Party Alley.

That makes this the cleanest early path:

**purchase order → Constance → Bootleg Bazaar.**

Once you reach Bootleg Bazaar, you should have two important reasons to be there:

1. Pseudopod had a purchase order connected to a vendor.
2. You have the red disc and need someone who may understand bootleg media.

That is enough to keep moving.

Do not worry if every name and faction is still confusing. ZERO PARADES throws a lot of worldbuilding at you early. Your job is not to memorize all of it immediately. Your job is to follow the strongest lead in front of you.

<h2 id="what-to-do-in-bootleg-bazaar">
  What to Do First Inside Bootleg Bazaar
</h2>

Do not stop at the entrance.

Your first real objective inside Bootleg Bazaar is to find **Carmuna / Manina Carmuna**, the vendor tied to Pseudopod's purchase order. The order is not just a location marker. It is your conversation hook.

<GuideTable
  caption="What to do first after reaching Bootleg Bazaar."
  columns={bazaarColumns}
  rows={bazaarRows}
/>

When you find Carmuna, ask about:

- Pseudopod,
- the sock purchase order,
- what he was doing in the Bazaar,
- whether she saw who he met,
- and any lead pointing toward the docks or Boatman.

Do not expect the red disc to be solved instantly here. It is still worth carrying because Bootleg Bazaar is the first place that feels connected to bootleg media, but the purchase order is your stronger first conversation anchor.

If Carmuna points you toward the west docks or a Boatman, follow that next. 
**Safe house evidence becomes a Bazaar contact. Bazaar contact becomes a dock lead. That is the opening investigation moving forward.**

<h3 id="what-should-i-do-first-in-zero-parades">
  What should I do first in ZERO PARADES?
</h3>

Inspect Pseudopod and search the safe house before leaving. You want the purchase order, red disc, half cipher, Rosetta cipher, and the Bootleg Bazaar lead.

<h3 id="where-is-the-purchase-order">
  Where is the purchase order in ZERO PARADES?
</h3>

The purchase order is inside Pseudopod's jacket. It points toward Carmuna and Bootleg Bazaar.

<h3 id="what-do-i-do-with-the-red-disc">
  What do I do with the red disc?
</h3>

Inspect the stereo, play the red disc, then take it with you. You do not fully solve it in the safe house, but it is an important media-related loose end.

<h3 id="where-is-the-half-cipher">
  Where is the half cipher?
</h3>

The half cipher is hidden in the bathroom trousers. Check the small extra pocket to find it.

<h3 id="where-is-the-rosetta-cipher">
  Where is the Rosetta cipher?
</h3>

The Rosetta cipher is hidden in the cigarette pack near the desk. Search the desk area before leaving the safe house.

<h3 id="should-i-leave-after-the-phone-call">
  Should I leave after the phone call?
</h3>

Not immediately. The phone call pushes you to leave, but the safe house still has important clues. Finish the room first.

<h3 id="where-do-i-go-after-the-safe-house">
  Where do I go after the safe house?
</h3>

Talk to Constance downstairs, then follow the Bootleg Bazaar lead toward the old Esilk factory.

<h3 id="who-do-i-talk-to-in-bootleg-bazaar">
  Who do I talk to first in Bootleg Bazaar?
</h3>

Find Carmuna / Manina Carmuna first. The purchase order from Pseudopod's jacket points to her fashion and garment business, so she is your first real Bazaar contact.

<h3 id="does-the-red-disc-matter-in-bootleg-bazaar">
  Does the red disc matter in Bootleg Bazaar?
</h3>

The red disc is still important, but it is not the strongest first conversation hook. Start with the purchase order and Carmuna, then keep the red disc in mind as a media or bootleg-related lead.

<h3 id="where-do-i-go-after-carmuna">
  Where do I go after Carmuna?
</h3>

Follow the next lead she gives you. If the route points toward the west docks or a Boatman, go there next.
