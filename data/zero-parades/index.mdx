<h2 id="zero-parades-guide-overview">
  ZERO PARADES Guide Overview
</h2>

ZERO PARADES: For Dead Spies is easiest to understand if you stop treating it like a normal checklist RPG.

Your first goal is not to pass every check, see every line, or build the perfect version of Cascade. Your first goal is to stay flexible enough to follow the mystery, survive pressure, and understand which failures are worth living with.

The game gives you several ways to solve problems: starting skills, gear, stress management, Exertion, Conditioning, and route choices during tense scenes. A bad roll is not always a disaster. Sometimes it is just the game pushing you into a different route.

<h2 id="when-to-use-exertion">
  When Should You Use Exertion?
</h2>

Exertion lets you push an important check for a better chance, but it raises the related stress meter. Do not use it just because the button is available.

Save Exertion for route-changing checks, major clue checks, or Dramatic Encounter moments where failure would actually change what you care about.

For the full breakdown, read the <Link href="/zero-parades/stress-exertion">ZERO PARADES Stress and Exertion Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="conditioning-and-thoughts">
  Conditioning and Thoughts
</h2>

Conditioning turns Thoughts into build commitments. A Thought can raise skill potential, add a gameplay effect, or change how Cascade plays, but many Thoughts also have violation rules or drawbacks.

On a first run, do not take a Thought just because it sounds strange. Take it only when it supports skills you already use and the drawback fits the way you actually play.

For confirmed Thoughts, effects, drawbacks, and build pairings, read the <Link href="/zero-parades/conditioning">ZERO PARADES Conditioning Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

<h3 id="what-should-i-do-first-in-zero-parades">
  What should I do first in ZERO PARADES?
</h3>

Inspect the safe house carefully. Check Pseudopod, his jacket, the stereo, the trousers, and the desk before leaving. You want the purchase order, red disc, half cipher, Rosetta cipher, and Bootleg Bazaar lead.

<h3 id="what-is-the-best-build-for-beginners">
  What is the best build for beginners?
</h3>

The best beginner build is flexible rather than extreme. Lean toward social and mental options for dialogue and clue reading, but keep enough physical ability to survive tense encounters.

<h3 id="should-i-use-exertion-on-every-check">
  Should I use Exertion on every check?
</h3>

No. Use Exertion when the check matters. If the roll is optional, low-value, or easy to improve with gear, save your stress.

<h3 id="what-do-fatigue-anxiety-and-delirium-do">
  What do Fatigue, Anxiety, and Delirium do?
</h3>

They are your main stress meters. They rise through pressure, mistakes, and Exertion, and they can make your run more difficult if you let them get too high.

<h3 id="is-conditioning-like-the-thought-cabinet">
  Is Conditioning like the Thought Cabinet?
</h3>

It has a similar role, but you should not treat it as a simple copy. Conditioning and Thoughts can improve your build, but many also come with drawbacks or rules that change how you play.

<h3 id="do-i-need-to-play-disco-elysium-first">
  Do I need to play Disco Elysium first?
</h3>

No. ZERO PARADES is its own game. Disco Elysium experience helps you understand the style of reading and roleplaying, but the systems are different enough that new players can start here.

<h3 id="should-i-reload-failed-checks">
  Should I reload failed checks?
</h3>

Not always. Reload if the failed check blocks a route or result you really care about. Otherwise, keep going and see how the game handles the failure.

<h3 id="where-do-i-go-after-the-safe-house">
  Where do I go after the safe house?
</h3>

Follow the Bootleg Bazaar lead. The purchase order and Constance's directions point you toward the next useful place to investigate.
