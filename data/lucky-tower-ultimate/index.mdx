<h3>
  1. <a href="/lucky-tower-ultimate/potion-recipes-and-permanent-buffs-guide/">Potion Recipes and Permanent Buffs Guide</a>
</h3>
<div>
  <strong>Why it matters first:</strong> Potions are not random. The colors are fixed across every run. Learning recipes allows you to reliably craft run-winning items.
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong> Drinking two identical buff potions back-to-back grants a <strong>Permanent Buff</strong> (e.g., Strength x2 lets you one-punch enemies). You will also learn the hidden mechanic of using the Aqua Blue Antidote to transform monsters back into human peasants.</li>
  </ul>

  <ul>
    <li><strong>Key Takeaways:</strong> Master the synergies, such as using Alva's Slime Potions to feed the Princess before her hunger makes her betray you. Learn how to trigger Sobu's trap warnings and how spending gold in the village unlocks the Castle route.</li>

<h2 id="faq">
  <a href="#faq">Frequently Asked Questions</a>
</h2>

<h3>Are potion colors randomized every run like in The Binding of Isaac?</h3>
<div>
  No. Lucky Tower Ultimate uses fixed potion colors. A bright yellow potion will <strong>always</strong> be Speed, and a dark green one will always be Poison. You can rely entirely on visual cues.
</div>

<h3>Do I need to rescue peasants to unlock the True Endgame?</h3>
<div>
  Rescuing peasants increases village population, but the True Endgame (Castle access) is strictly unlocked by <strong>spending gold</strong> to upgrade your house and buy citizenship, or by using a specific exploit with the Princess.
</div>

<h3>Is there a "Tight Wallet" achievement for not spending money?</h3>
<div>
  No, this is a common misconception. The game challenges you to hoard wealth with the <strong>"Loaded"</strong> achievement (having 300+ gold in the bank). You are actively encouraged to engage with the village economy.
</div>
