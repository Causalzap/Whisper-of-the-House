The pipeline system in **Lucky Tower Ultimate** looks like a side quest at first, but it is actually one of the smartest utility systems in the whole game. Once you understand the four broken pipes, connecting rings, activation cost, reclaim fees, and village buyback loop, the pipeline stops being “plumber content” and starts becoming a real strategic safety system.

<h2 id="quick-answer">
  <a href="#quick-answer">Quick Answer</a>
</h2>

<div>
  There are <strong>4 broken pipes</strong> total. After you repair all four, you still need to pay
  <strong> 100 gold</strong> to the Pipefitters to activate the network fully.
</div>

<div>
  Once active, each tower segment gives you <strong>one pipe slot</strong>, and that slot can hold
  <strong> one item at a time</strong>. You place an item into the slot, it gets transported to the next segment, and
  if it makes it all the way to the village end, you can buy it back there for <strong>8 gold</strong>.
</div>

<div>
  The biggest reason the system is so strong is simple:
  <strong> once an item is safely in the pipe network, it stays safe even if you die later in the run</strong>.
</div>

<h2 id="where-to-start-the-pipeline-quest">
  <a href="#where-to-start-the-pipeline-quest">Where to Start the Pipeline Quest</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The pipeline quest starts with the Pipefitters in the village:
  <strong> Marisha and Louise</strong>.
</div>

<div>
  If you are not sure where they are, look <strong>beside the pawn shop</strong>. That is the village location tied to
  the entire pipe network, including repairs, activation, and buying items back after they reach the village end.
</div>

<h2 id="how-the-pipeline-system-works">
  <a href="#how-the-pipeline-system-works">How the Pipeline System Works</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The pipeline system is not a permanent cross-run stash chest. It is better understood as an
  <strong> in-run transport network with village recovery</strong>.
</div>

<ol>
  <li>You place one item into the segment’s pipe slot.</li>
  <li>The item gets moved to the <strong>next segment</strong>.</li>
  <li>You can retrieve it at the next slot or keep sending it farther down.</li>
  <li>If it reaches the village end of the network, the Pipefitters can sell it back to you.</li>
</ol>

<div>
  The most important detail is that the network protects items from death loss. If the item already entered the system,
  it still reaches the village side even if you die before escaping.
</div>

<h2 id="how-many-broken-pipes-are-there">
  <a href="#how-many-broken-pipes-are-there">How Many Broken Pipes Are There?</a>
</h2>

<div>
  There are <strong>4 broken pipes</strong> total:
</div>

<ul>
  <li>one at the start of each new tower segment,</li>
  <li>plus one in the <strong>main hall</strong>, between the main entrance and the cellar entrance.</li>
</ul>

<div>
  This is the single most useful number to know, because many players stop after the first few repairs and assume the
  quest is finished. It is not finished until all four are repaired.
</div>

<h2 id="where-the-last-pipe-usually-gets-missed">
  <a href="#where-the-last-pipe-usually-gets-missed">Where the Last Pipe Usually Gets Missed</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The most commonly missed repair is the late one near the exit / final stretch. That is why so many players think they
  are done after the first three repairs.
</div>

<div>
  If the Pipefitters still say something is missing, go back to this checklist:
</div>

<ul>
  <li>did you count the main hall pipe,</li>
  <li>did you count all segment-start pipes,</li>
  <li>did you actually check the late-run exit area?</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="activation-cost-and-running-costs">
  <a href="#activation-cost-and-running-costs">Activation Cost and Running Costs</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The pipeline system has two costs you need to know from the start:
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>100 gold</strong> to activate the full system after all four repairs</li>
  <li><strong>8 gold per item</strong> to reclaim something from the village end</li>
</ul>

<div>
  These numbers are what make the system strategic. The question is not just “can I send this item?” The real question
  is “is this item worth the activation investment and the reclaim fee?”
</div>

<h2 id="connecting-ring-economics">
  <a href="#connecting-ring-economics">Connecting Ring Economics</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The connecting ring has one of the easiest-to-miss economic details in the entire quest:
</div>

<ul>
  <li>it sells for <strong>7 gold</strong>,</li>
  <li>but buying it back costs <strong>11 gold</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<div>
  That is why “do not sell the ring” is good advice. The spread is bad for you.
</div>

<div>
  The good news is that every time you return to the village, the Pipefitters give you a
  <strong> fresh connecting ring</strong>. So the ring is important, but you still should not casually dump it for
  short-term cash.
</div>

<h2 id="when-the-pipeline-is-most-worth-it">
  <a href="#when-the-pipeline-is-most-worth-it">When the Pipeline Is Most Worth It</a>
</h2>

<h3>1. You are about to die anyway</h3>
<div>
  If you are deep in the tower, carrying a great weapon or irreplaceable utility item, and sitting on almost no health,
  the pipeline is amazing. Once the item is inside the system, you no longer need to survive with it in your bag.
</div>

<h3>2. Your inventory is clogged</h3>
<div>
  If you already have a good immediate setup but find another high-value item, the pipeline solves the “which great
  thing do I throw away?” problem.
</div>

<h3>3. You are doing Tight Wallet</h3>
<div>
  The pipeline is especially good for <strong>Tight Wallet</strong>-style play because the
  <strong> wallet is a real item</strong>, not just an abstract coin counter. A normal wallet can hold
  <strong>20 gold</strong>, and you can send that wallet through the pipeline instead of physically carrying it through
  every trap and fight yourself.
</div>

<h2 id="when-carrying-it-yourself-is-better">
  <a href="#when-carrying-it-yourself-is-better">When Carrying It Yourself Is Better</a>
</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Basic consumables</strong> are usually not worth an 8-gold reclaim fee.</li>
  <li><strong>Immediate combat gear</strong> should stay with you if you still need it right now.</li>
  <li><strong>Boss or endgame push tools</strong> should not be piped away if you are already committed to using them in this run.</li>
</ul>

<div>
  A good rule of thumb is:
  <strong>if replacing the item would cost less than 8 gold or one easy room, do not pipe it</strong>.
</div>

<h2 id="best-items-to-send">
  <a href="#best-items-to-send">Best Items to Send</a>
</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>rare weapons</strong> you cannot afford to lose to a bad death</li>
  <li><strong>valuable wallets</strong> during money-focused runs</li>
  <li><strong>strong utility tools</strong> you do not need immediately but still want preserved</li>
  <li><strong>duplicate high-value items</strong> that would otherwise clog inventory</li>
</ul>

<div>
  The system is strongest when the item is valuable enough that <strong>8 gold is cheap insurance</strong>.
</div>

<h2 id="how-to-finish-the-pipeline-quest-efficiently">
  <a href="#how-to-finish-the-pipeline-quest-efficiently">How to Finish the Pipeline Quest Efficiently</a>
</h2>

<ol>
  <li>Count to <strong>four total broken pipes</strong>, not three.</li>
  <li>Do not forget the <strong>main hall</strong> pipe between the entrance and cellar.</li>
  <li>Keep your connecting ring instead of flipping it for bad short-term gold.</li>
  <li>Return to Marisha and Louise after the final repair and be ready to pay <strong>100 gold</strong>.</li>
  <li>Once active, use the segment slot for genuinely valuable items only.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="pipeline-related-achievements">
  <a href="#pipeline-related-achievements">Pipeline-Related Achievements</a>
</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Put a ring on it</strong> – fix the first pipe</li>
  <li><strong>All pipe systems go!</strong> – complete the full repair line</li>
  <li><strong>No hard fee-lings / Money down the drain</strong> – first time buying back an item from the Pipefitters</li>
</ul>

<div>
  These are the key new-player milestone achievements because they map directly to the actual system progression:
  first repair, full repair, first successful reclaim.
</div>

<h2 id="plumber-slumber">
  <a href="#plumber-slumber">Plumber Slumber</a>
</h2>

<div>
  <strong>Plumber Slumber</strong> is related by name, but it is <strong>not</strong> the same thing as the pipe
  infrastructure system.
</div>

<div>
  That achievement requires you to kill <strong>15 enemies in one run</strong> using
  <strong> Plumber's Helper</strong>, which is a separate combat item. Community discussion points to
  <strong> toilet monster / toilet door areas</strong> as the place to look for it.
</div>

<div>
  So if you are reading this page because of Plumber Slumber, the important clarification is:
  <strong> fixing the pipes does not automatically progress that achievement</strong>.
</div>

<h2 id="common-pipeline-mistakes">
  <a href="#common-pipeline-mistakes">Common Pipeline Mistakes</a>
</h2>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Stopping after three repairs.</strong> There are four total pipes.</li>
  <li><strong>Forgetting the 100-gold activation fee.</strong> Repairs alone do not finish the system.</li>
  <li><strong>Ignoring the 8-gold reclaim fee.</strong> Not every item is worth piping.</li>
  <li><strong>Selling the connecting ring.</strong> The 7g / 11g spread is bad.</li>
  <li><strong>Using the slot on junk.</strong> Save the system for items worth protecting.</li>
  <li><strong>Assuming you must survive for the item to matter.</strong> Once piped onward, it stays safe.</li>
  <li><strong>Assuming the slot is infinite.</strong> Each segment slot handles one item at a time.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">
  <a href="#faq">FAQ</a>
</h2>

<h3>How many broken pipes are there in Lucky Tower Ultimate?</h3>
<div>
  There are 4 broken pipes total: one in each new segment and one in the main hall between the entrance and the cellar.
</div>

<h3>Where are the Pipefitters?</h3>
<div>
  Marisha and Louise are in the village beside the pawn shop.
</div>

<h3>Do you need to pay after fixing the pipes?</h3>
<div>
  Yes. After repairing the full network, you still need to pay 100 gold to activate it fully.
</div>

<h3>How much does it cost to get an item back from the pipeline?</h3>
<div>
  Each reclaim from the village costs 8 gold.
</div>

<h3>If you die after sending an item through the pipe, is it lost?</h3>
<div>
  No. Once the item is successfully in the system, it still reaches the village side even if you die later.
</div>

<h3>How many items can one pipe slot hold?</h3>
<div>
  One item at a time. You insert it, move on, and then either retrieve it at the next slot or keep pushing it farther.
</div>

<h3>Should you sell the connecting ring?</h3>
<div>
  Usually no. It sells for 7 gold but costs 11 gold to buy back, so the trade is bad.
</div>

<h3>What is the best thing to send through the pipeline?</h3>
<div>
  A genuinely valuable item that is worth more than the 8-gold reclaim fee and that you do not still need immediately.
</div>

<h2 id="final-verdict">
  <a href="#final-verdict">Final Verdict</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The pipeline system is one of the smartest safety valves in Lucky Tower Ultimate. It costs real money, so it is not
  for junk, but once you understand the numbers, it becomes clear why it is so powerful:
  <strong> the pipeline turns fragile run loot into protected village-side value, even if the run itself falls apart</strong>.
  If you only remember one rule, remember this:
  <strong>pipe items that are worth more than 8 gold to save, and do not stop until all four broken pipes are fixed.</strong>
</div>
