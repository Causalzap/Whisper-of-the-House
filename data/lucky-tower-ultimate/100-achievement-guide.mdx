<h2 id="quick-answer">
  <a href="#quick-answer">Quick Answer</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The fastest way to 100% <strong>Lucky Tower Ultimate</strong> is to split the list into five phases:
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>escape basics</strong></li>
  <li><strong>village and citizenship</strong></li>
  <li><strong>companions</strong></li>
  <li><strong>potions and transformations</strong></li>
  <li><strong>endgame and prophecy</strong></li>
</ul>

<div>
  Do not try to do everything in one run. Build your save in layers and let overlap do the work.
</div>

<h2 id="total-achievements">
  <a href="#total-achievements">Total Achievements</a>
</h2>

<div>
  At the time of writing, the Steam global achievements page lists <strong>99 achievements</strong> for Lucky Tower Ultimate.
</div>

<h2 id="how-long-100-percent-usually-takes">
  <a href="#how-long-100-percent-usually-takes">How Long 100% Usually Takes</a>
</h2>

<div>
  Think in <strong>dozens of runs</strong>, not a handful. Some achievements unlock naturally while you learn the game,
  but the rarest ones are cleanup projects.
</div>

<h2 id="best-roadmap">
  <a href="#best-roadmap">Best Roadmap</a>
</h2>

<h3>Phase 1: Escape basics</h3>
<div>
  Get out of the tower reliably before you chase weird or strict challenge runs.
</div>

<h3>Phase 2: Village and citizenship</h3>
<div>
  Start building long-term progression. Citizenship matters because it opens later castle and prophecy routes.
</div>

<h3>Phase 3: Companions</h3>
<div>
  This is where Princess, Hubert, tavern population, vending machines, torture chamber rescues, and party-size runs
  overlap efficiently.
</div>

<h3>Phase 4: Potions and transformations</h3>
<div>
  Do monster, broom, poison, gold, and brewing cleanup in dedicated runs instead of mixing them into fragile progression
  attempts.
</div>

<h3>Phase 5: Endgame and prophecy</h3>
<div>
  Finish with castle, Evelius, prophecy, disguise, and the rarest update 5 routes.
</div>

<h2 id="escape-and-speed-achievements">
  <a href="#escape-and-speed-achievements">Escape and Speed Achievements</a>
</h2>

<h3>Lucky Tower</h3>
<div>
  Your first clean escape should come naturally once you stop bleeding runs to fake doors, greed, and trap impatience.
</div>

<h3>Lucky Towers</h3>
<div>
  This is a grind gate. Do not try to hard-farm it early. It is much more efficient to let it build in the background
  while you clean everything else.
</div>

<h3>Speedrun</h3>
<div>
  The 15-minute clear is your first real speed benchmark. Learn to skip optional rooms before you force it.
</div>

<h3>Speedrunner-er</h3>
<div>
  This should be treated as a dedicated speed route, not a normal run that happens to go well. Commit to moving fast
  from the start and stop looting once the run is live.
</div>

<h2 id="challenge-escape-achievements">
  <a href="#challenge-escape-achievements">Challenge Escape Achievements</a>
</h2>

<h3>Pacifist</h3>
<div>
  This is one of the most searched achievements because it sounds simpler than it is. Save it for a run where your room
  reading is already clean and you know how to avoid accidental kills.
</div>

<h3>Looper</h3>
<div>
  Easy to overlook because it sounds harmless. Treat it as a planned objective during a relaxed cleanup run.
</div>

<h3>Not a peasant</h3>
<div>
  Use this as your first serious difficulty benchmark.
</div>

<h3>Barenudle Beast</h3>
<div>
  Best attempted after your movement is already clean and you no longer depend on armor to survive mistakes.
</div>

<h3>No touching!</h3>
<div>
  The exact rule is:
  <strong>start the run with no items from the village, then never hold anything in your hand, equip anything, or insert teeth for the rest of that run</strong>.
</div>

<div>
  Treat it as a dedicated purity run. Do not bolt it onto a normal escape.
</div>

<h2 id="companion-achievements">
  <a href="#companion-achievements">Companion Achievements</a>
</h2>

<h3>Snackathon</h3>
<div>
  Princess needs to stay fed long enough to keep the run stable while the counter climbs.
</div>

<h3>Hammerfry!</h3>
<div>
  This is not a side bonus. Treat it as a dedicated Princess run with the right hammerer flow.
</div>

<h3>The Princess</h3>
<div>
  Natural companion milestone, but still worth planning if you are already doing Princess-related cleanup.
</div>

<h3>Now That&apos;s a Party</h3>
<div>
  Plan for this. Use rescue rooms, vending machines, and follower opportunities aggressively instead of hoping five
  companions happen naturally.
</div>

<h3>Sacrifices need to be made</h3>
<div>
  The cleanest way to get this is to bring multiple companions into dangerous rooms and let
  <strong>traps or enemies kill them</strong>. Do not rely on random bad luck.
</div>

<h3>Rent a friend</h3>
<div>
  Best done in a vending-machine cleanup run.
</div>

<h3>Freed, not fried</h3>
<div>
  Best done in the same general block as your rescue-heavy companion cleanup.
</div>

<h3>The more the merrier</h3>
<div>
  This does <strong>not</strong> mean “go to the tavern once and hope.” You need to have rescued and retained enough
  friends beforehand so that one tavern visit contains at least 10 of them.
</div>

<h3>True friend</h3>
<div>
  Start giving Hubert money earlier than you think. This is a long-tail achievement, not a last-minute cleanup.
</div>

<h3>Avenger</h3>
<div>
  If Hubert dies, be ready to immediately use his club or corpse against the killer.
</div>

<h2 id="faq">
  <a href="#faq">FAQ</a>
</h2>

<h3>How many achievements are there in Lucky Tower Ultimate?</h3>
<div>
  The Steam global achievements page currently lists 99 achievements.
</div>

<h3>Should I try to do all achievements in one run?</h3>
<div>
  No. Lucky Tower Ultimate 100% is much faster when split into dedicated phases and cleanup runs.
</div>

<h3>What should I do first for 100%?</h3>
<div>
  First get normal escapes stable. After that, build village progression and citizenship.
</div>

<h3>When should I attempt No touching!?</h3>
<div>
  Late. Start the run with no items from the village, then never hold or equip anything for the rest of that run.
</div>

<h3>When should I attempt Speedrunner-er?</h3>
<div>
  After you already know how to survive quickly, not just move quickly.
</div>

<h3>Is The chosen one one fixed gear list?</h3>
<div>
  Treat it as a prophecy-execution achievement, not a single permanent shopping list.
</div>

<h3>Which achievements usually finish naturally?</h3>
<div>
  Most segment-reach, first-escape, early rescue, and basic exploration achievements usually finish naturally while you
  learn the game.
</div>

<h2 id="final-verdict">
  <a href="#final-verdict">Final Verdict</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The best thing about Lucky Tower Ultimate’s achievement list is also what makes it intimidating: it is not a checklist
  of isolated tricks. It is a map of the whole game. If you respect the order — escape first, village second,
  companions and potions third, endgame last — the list becomes much more manageable and a lot less miserable.
</div>
