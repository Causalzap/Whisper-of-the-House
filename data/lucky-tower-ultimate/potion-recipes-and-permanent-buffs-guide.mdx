<h2 id="quick-answer">
  <a href="#quick-answer">Quick Answer</a>
</h2>

<div>
  Yes, potion colors in <strong>Lucky Tower Ultimate</strong> are fixed across runs, and yes, the game has a real
  <strong> permanent buff system</strong>. The exact trigger is:
  <strong> drink the same potion again while the first copy is still active</strong>.
</div>

<div>
  The most important potion routes are:
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Permanent Speed</strong> for safer movement and better jumps</li>
  <li><strong>Permanent Strength</strong> for maximum combat power</li>
  <li><strong>Permanent Monster</strong> for low-combat or no-kill routing</li>
  <li><strong>Antidote</strong> for poison recovery and buff cleanup</li>
</ul>

<div>
  The single biggest mistake players make is treating potions like random drinks. They are not random. They are
  fixed, craftable, and often route-defining.
</div>

<ul>

  <li><strong>Recipe:</strong> Frog + Mushroom</li>

  <li><strong>Recipe:</strong> Coin + Coin <strong>(or another cold object)</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Use:</strong> turns objects gold, can upgrade certain targets, instantly kills enemies when thrown, and briefly gives the player golden skin when drunk</li>

<h3>Slime Potion</h3>

  <li><strong>Color:</strong> fluorescent green</li>

<h3>Hair Potion</h3>

  <li><strong>Recipe:</strong> Coin + Brain</li>

<h3>Weird Potion</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Color:</strong> bubbling brown</li>
  <li><strong>Recipe:</strong> Pumpkin + Wood</li>
  <li><strong>Use:</strong> no major practical buff confirmed yet; repeated use can create purple spots</li>

<h3>Alcohol</h3>
<ul>

  <li><strong>Source:</strong> usually treated as a non-standard potion rather than a normal cauldron recipe</li>
  <li><strong>Note:</strong> can also be made permanent under the same active-effect stacking rule</li>
</ul>

<h3>Explosion Potion</h3>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Use:</strong> explosive utility and enemy interaction</li>
  <li><strong>Source:</strong> usually treated as a non-standard potion rather than a normal cauldron recipe</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="are-potion-colors-fixed">
  <a href="#are-potion-colors-fixed">Are Potion Colors Fixed Across Runs?</a>
</h2>

<div>
  Yes. Potion colors are fixed across runs.
</div>

<div>
  Lucky Tower Ultimate does not use a random identification system where the same color means different things in
  different runs. Once you learn the color language, you can trust it again in later runs.
</div>

<h2 id="alchemy-rules-and-tag-logic">
  <a href="#alchemy-rules-and-tag-logic">Alchemy Rules and Tag Logic</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The cauldron works by <strong>item tags</strong>, not only by one exact object.
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Strength = Pumpkin + any Steel-tagged item</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Speed = Banana + any Wood-tagged item</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Gold = Coin + Coin (or another cold object)</strong></li>
</ul>

<div>
  That broader tag logic is what makes potion crafting much more reliable once you know the categories.
</div>

<h2 id="stable-potion-sources">
  <a href="#stable-potion-sources">Stable Potion Sources</a>
</h2>

<h3>1. Cauldron crafting</h3>
<div>
  This is the most reliable source for standard recipes. If you have the right tagged ingredients and access to a
  cauldron, you can repeatedly craft the potion you want.
</div>

<h3>2. Shops and vending machines</h3>
<div>
  Potions can also appear as purchasable items. This is especially useful when you want to force a specific route,
  such as Monster or Speed.
</div>

<h3>3. Alva-related spaces</h3>
<div>
  Alva is one of the most important potion-support NPCs in the game. Her hut becomes a key utility space once she has
  been rescued.
</div>

<h3>4. Witches</h3>
<div>
  Witches are a stable source for special non-standard potions, especially Alcohol and Explosion, if you can make them
  drop what they are holding before they use it.
</div>

<h2 id="alva-guide">
  <a href="#alva-guide">Alva Guide</a>
</h2>

<div>
  Alva is one of the most useful potion-support NPCs in Lucky Tower Ultimate.
</div>

<ul>
  <li>Alva can heal poison <strong>unlimited times</strong>.</li>
  <li>Alva always carries one <strong>Antidote</strong> item.</li>
  <li>After Alva is rescued, her hut becomes an important potion and cauldron space.</li>
  <li>If Alva has not been rescued yet, players can run into the common issue where her hut or cauldron utility is not available yet.</li>
  <li>Alva also has a special disguise-related potion interaction that can make you look like Evelius.</li>
</ul>

<div>
  In practice, that makes Alva important for poison management, potion crafting, and several transformation-related
  achievement routes.
</div>

<h2 id="permanent-buffs">
  <a href="#permanent-buffs">Permanent Buffs</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The permanent buff rule is very precise:
  <strong> drink the same potion again while the first copy is still active</strong>.
</div>

<div>
  This is more accurate than saying “drink two quickly.” The overlap window matters.
</div>

<h3>Permanent Speed</h3>
<div>
  Permanent Speed gives you stronger movement, safer trap traversal, and much better jump routing.
</div>

<h3>Permanent Strength</h3>
<div>
  Permanent Strength turns standard combat into a much simpler problem, especially against normal unarmored enemies.
</div>

<h3>Permanent Monster</h3>
<div>
  Permanent Monster is one of the strongest routing states in the game because standard monsters will usually stop
  aggroing onto you.
</div>

<h3>Permanent Alcohol</h3>
<div>
  Alcohol can also be made permanent under the same active-effect stacking rule. It is much more niche than Speed or
  Monster, but it is real and worth understanding because players actively search for it.
</div>

<h2 id="how-to-trigger-permanent-monster">
  <a href="#how-to-trigger-permanent-monster">How to Trigger Permanent Monster</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The direct route is simple: drink a Monster potion, then drink another Monster potion while the first one is still
  active.
</div>

<div>
  But that is not the only path. Permanent Monster can also be triggered through mixed routes, including:
</div>

<ul>
  <li>getting transformed by a wizard and then drinking one Monster potion while that transformation is still active</li>
  <li>using an Evelius-provided transformation state and then adding one Monster potion</li>
</ul>

<div>
  These alternate routes matter because they are directly relevant to no-kill routing and Monster Schmonster-style
  achievement paths.
</div>

<h2 id="become-a-broom">
  <a href="#become-a-broom">Become a Broom</a>
</h2>

<div>
  Becoming a broom is a special transformation edge case, and it is more precise than simply saying “drink three
  potions.”
</div>

<div>
  There are two useful ways to understand it:
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Path 1:</strong> drink three transformation potions in a row</li>
  <li><strong>Path 2:</strong> reach permanent Monster state, then take one further transformation effect, which turns you into a lifeless broom</li>
</ul>

<div>
  The second explanation is the cleaner mechanical model. In other words, once the game already considers you fully
  locked into Monster form, one more transformation push can tip you into broom form.
</div>

<h2 id="how-to-remove-permanent-buffs">
  <a href="#how-to-remove-permanent-buffs">How to Remove Permanent Buffs</a>
</h2>

<div>
  Permanent potion effects do not last forever under every condition. The main removal or reset conditions are:
</div>

<ul>
  <li><strong>Death</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Poison</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Drinking Antidote</strong></li>
  <li><strong>Getting hit by another relevant transformation effect</strong></li>
</ul>

<div>
  A common point of confusion is Alcohol. Current community-tested understanding is that
  <strong>Alcohol does not remove permanent buffs</strong>. Poison is the more important danger.
</div>

<h2 id="best-potions-to-prioritize">
  <a href="#best-potions-to-prioritize">Best Potions to Prioritize</a>
</h2>

<h3>Best overall: Speed</h3>
<div>
  Permanent Speed is the most broadly useful potion state in the game.
</div>

<h3>Best for combat: Strength</h3>
<div>
  Permanent Strength is the strongest pure combat buff.
</div>

<h3>Best for low-combat routing: Monster</h3>
<div>
  Permanent Monster is the strongest “ignore normal monster hostility” plan.
</div>

<h3>Best safety tool: Antidote</h3>
<div>
  Antidote is what keeps strong runs from collapsing to poison or the wrong transformation state.
</div>

<h3>Best throwable utility: Gold</h3>
<div>
  Gold is one of the best examples of a potion that is often stronger thrown than drunk.
</div>

<h2 id="advanced-potion-tips">
  <a href="#advanced-potion-tips">Advanced Potion Tips</a>
</h2>

<ul>
  <li>Do not blind-drink black, brown, or unknown special potions during a good run.</li>
  <li>Always think about whether a duplicate should be saved for a permanent trigger instead of used immediately.</li>
  <li>If you are relying on a permanent state, avoid accidental poison at all costs.</li>
  <li>Keep Antidote in mind whenever your run depends on a high-value buff state.</li>
  <li>Monster routing is not just a gimmick; it is one of the strongest routing strategies in the game.</li>
  <li>Alcohol matters more than it first appears because it interacts with both permanent-state logic and companion behavior.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">
  <a href="#faq">FAQ</a>
</h2>

<h3>Are potion colors random in Lucky Tower Ultimate?</h3>
<div>
  No. Colors are fixed across runs.
</div>

<h3>How do permanent buffs actually trigger?</h3>
<div>
  Drink the same potion again while the first copy is still active.
</div>

<h3>Is Antidote the same as Human Transformation?</h3>
<div>
  No. They should be treated as separate items or potion functions.
</div>

<h3>Does Alcohol remove permanent buffs?</h3>
<div>
  No. Poison is the much more important permanent-buff remover.
</div>

<h3>How do you get permanent Monster form?</h3>
<div>
  The main route is two Monster potions with overlapping active effect, but wizard transformation or Evelius-linked
  transformation plus one potion can also trigger it.
</div>

<h3>What is the best potion in Lucky Tower Ultimate?</h3>
<div>
  For most players, Permanent Speed is the best all-around effect. Permanent Monster is the best routing cheese, and
  Permanent Strength is the best pure combat buff.
</div>

<h2 id="final-verdict">
  <a href="#final-verdict">Final Verdict</a>
</h2>

<div>
  The potion system in Lucky Tower Ultimate is much deeper than it first appears. The real power jump comes when you
  stop treating potions as random drinks and start treating them as a fixed, craftable, stackable system with permanent
  states and transformation edge cases. Learn the recipe tags, separate Antidote from Human Transformation, respect
  Alcohol and Monster interactions, and build around permanent Speed, Strength, or Monster whenever the run allows it.
</div>
