<h2 id="best-route">
  Best Route for Your First Playthrough
</h2>

Do not treat the first few hours like a full cleanup run.

LEGO Batman Legacy keeps adding systems as the story moves forward. The opening teaches the basics, early Gotham introduces larger objectives, and the Batcave slowly turns into your main base. If you stop to chase every reward too early, you can waste time before the game has given you the right tools.

Use this route instead:

The short version: **push story until the Batcave and Batgirl are active, then start caring more about cleanup, Red Bricks, and shop refreshes.**

<h2 id="what-to-do-first">
  What Should You Do First?
</h2>

Start by following the main story.

The early missions introduce movement, combat, stealth, gadgets, partner actions, and the first big Gotham objectives. These are not just tutorials. They decide how comfortable later missions, collectibles, and boss fights feel.

Focus on:

- learning counter timing and focus attacks,
- getting comfortable with gadget prompts,
- watching for partner actions,
- checking the Batcave when the story sends you back,
- and saving full collectible cleanup for later.

If the game gives you a new system, use it once so you understand it. Then keep moving.

- refresh Bat-Mite’s Shop only when you are prepared to spend studs.

Red Bricks in this game are mostly cosmetic color modifiers, not old-style Stud Multiplier cheats. Use the <Link href="/lego-batman/red-bricks">Red Bricks Guide</Link> for all 23 locations, effects, and shop refresh rules.

<h2 id="bosses">
  Bosses: Survive First, Attack Second
</h2>

Most boss fights in LEGO Batman Legacy are mechanic checks.

If you are stuck, the answer usually is not “farm more” or “restart the chapter.” It is usually one of these:

- you are attacking while the boss is protected,
- you are ignoring adds or hazards,
- you are missing the obvious damage window,
- or you are standing too close during a big attack.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

<h3 id="should-i-clear-every-collectible-right-away">
  Should I clear every collectible as soon as I see it?
</h3>

No. Follow the story first and return later when more characters, tools, Batcave systems, and replay options are available.

<h3 id="is-the-batcave-important-early">
  Is the Batcave important early?
</h3>

Yes. Once the Batcave starts adding systems, check it between major missions. It becomes your base for rewards, challenge progress, suits, props, shops, and story resets.

<h3 id="what-is-the-challenge-board">
  What is the Challenge Board?
</h3>

The Challenge Board tracks criteria you complete out in the field and reveals more of the mural over time. Treat it as a long-term checklist.

<h3 id="what-does-bat-mite-sell">
  What does Bat-Mite sell?
</h3>

Bat-Mite’s Shop sells rotating rewards such as suits, props, and shop Red Bricks. It is useful early, but more important during cleanup.

<h3 id="when-should-i-start-red-bricks">
  When should I start collecting Red Bricks?
</h3>

Start seriously during mission replay or cleanup. Grab mission Red Bricks when you are already replaying levels, then buy shop Red Bricks from Bat-Mite’s Shop.

<h3 id="what-do-red-bricks-do">
  What do Red Bricks do?
</h3>

Red Bricks unlock cosmetic color modifiers for suits and vehicles. They are completion and style rewards, not old-style Stud Multiplier cheats.

<h3 id="when-does-batgirl-unlock">
  When does Batgirl unlock?
</h3>

Batgirl joins during a major story sequence involving Barbara Gordon and Firefly. After that, her tech becomes more important for later progression.

<h3 id="why-am-i-stuck-in-carmine-falcone">
  Why am I stuck in Carmine Falcone?
</h3>

You are probably missing the correct route through the Iceberg Lounge, the basement clue, the food order, or the House of Fun route. Use the Carmine Falcone guide for the exact path.

<h3 id="are-boss-fights-hard">
  Are boss fights hard?
</h3>

They are mostly mechanic fights. Survive the attacks, wait for the real opening, then use the required prompt or attack window.

<h3 id="should-i-use-this-page-as-a-100-percent-map">
  Should I use this page as a 100% map?
</h3>

No. Use this page as a route hub. For exact sections, open the Red Bricks, Carmine Falcone, Batgirl, Firefly, Poison Ivy, or Bane guides.
