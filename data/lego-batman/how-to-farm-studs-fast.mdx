<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
The fastest way to farm studs early in **LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight** is the **Stud Cache loop** in the **Batcave vehicle garage**. After you redeem the LEGO Group Online Survey reward, open the red-and-yellow cache near the Batcave vehicles, fast travel away, return to the Batcave, and open it again.

With the right stud upgrades and x4 combo setup, the Stud Cache is worth around **30,000 studs per open**. Before those upgrades, it still works, but the payout will be lower.

The best active farming method is **Jim Gordon’s Rebound Launcher** in dense rooms like **Gotham Bank** or the **Batcave Trophy Room**. For open-world farming, follow **police radio crime events** whenever the red alert icon appears. After the story, **Party Man replay** and the **amusement park roller coaster** become strong late-game options.

<h2 id="stud-colors-and-values">
  Stud Colors And Values
</h2>

Before you farm, it helps to know which studs actually matter.

Most breakable objects drop **bronze**, **silver**, or **gold** studs. Those are useful for keeping your combo alive, but the big payouts come from **blue** and especially **purple** studs.

<GuideTable
  caption="LEGO Batman Legacy stud colors and base values."
  columns={studValueColumns}
  rows={studValueRows}
/>

This is why stud farming is not just about breaking everything. It is about keeping your combo alive long enough to pick up the high-value drops.

A single **purple stud** is worth **10,000** by default. At **x4**, that becomes **40,000**. That is more than many small rooms are worth by themselves.

<h2 id="best-upgrades-for-farming-studs">
  Best Upgrades For Farming Studs
</h2>

The most important farming upgrades are not bought with studs. They use **Skill Bricks**.

That detail matters because farming more studs will not directly buy the upgrades that make farming better. Spend your early Skill Bricks on stud collection and combo upgrades before comfort upgrades.

The basic priority is simple: first make studs easier to collect, then make the combo last longer, then improve Gordon’s room-clearing power.

<h2 id="stud-cache-loop">
  Stud Cache Loop: Fastest Early Stud Farm
</h2>

The **Stud Cache loop** is the best place to start because it is simple, repeatable, and does not require you to search for a perfect farming room.

To unlock it, pause the game, go to the **Extras** tab, and open **The LEGO Group Online Survey**. After completing the survey, redeem the code from the main menu. This adds a Stud Cache crate to the Batcave.

Once the reward is active, go to the **Batcave vehicle garage**. Look for the red-and-yellow crate near the vehicles. This is the Stud Cache.

Open the crate, collect the studs, then use the map to fast travel to another location. After that, use **Return to Batcave**. When you come back, the cache should be full again.

This method is strongest when you need money immediately for suits, vehicles, Batcave props, or early purchases.

<h2 id="jim-gordon-rebound-launcher-farm">
  Jim Gordon Rebound Launcher Farm
</h2>

Jim Gordon becomes one of the best active stud farmers once you upgrade the **Rebound Launcher**.

The key upgrade is the one that makes rebound bolts split after bouncing. Without that, Gordon is useful but not special. With it, one shot can bounce through clustered objects and clear a room far faster than normal melee attacks.

Gordon farming is not as passive as the Stud Cache loop, but it is better while you are already playing. Use it whenever you are moving through Gotham, replaying missions, or entering rooms full of props.

<h2 id="police-radio-crime-events">
  Police Radio Crime Events
</h2>

Do not ignore police radio crime events while exploring Gotham.

When a red police alert appears, follow it and stop the crime. These events can drop a large burst of studs, and they fit naturally into normal free-roam cleanup. You do not have to stop your entire plan to farm a single room.

The best way to treat these events is as a route interruption:

1. Travel toward your collectible, suit, or mission objective.
2. If a red crime alert appears nearby, follow it.
3. Clear the criminals quickly.
4. Grab the stud burst.
5. Return to your original route.

If you are using stealth-heavy characters or stealth takedowns, these events can become even more rewarding because special stud drops are more likely to matter when your combo is active.

Police radio events are not the best way to hard farm from zero to the cap, but they are one of the best ways to earn extra money while you are already clearing Gotham.

<h2 id="party-man-replay-catwoman-farm">
  Party Man Replay Catwoman Farm
</h2>

After you finish enough story content to use mission replay, **Party Man** becomes a strong manual farming route.

Open the Bat Computer, replay the **Flugelheim / Party Man** mission, and switch to **Catwoman** when you reach the display and glass-case sections. Catwoman can open or cut through several high-value areas that are easy to rush past during a normal story run.

The goal is not to slowly full-clear every corner. The goal is to hit the valuable Catwoman interactions while keeping your combo active.

A clean Party Man route looks like this:

1. Enter the mission through replay.
2. Switch to Catwoman as soon as the display-case route opens.
3. Break or cut the glass displays, then prioritize blue and purple studs before the combo drops.
4. Continue toward the safe and statue-head sections.
5. Skip low-value loose studs if they would make you lose the route rhythm.
6. Restart or replay once the valuable rooms are cleared.

This route is especially good if your stud combo can reach x4. A fixed “per run” number is hard to promise because it depends on your upgrades and how cleanly you keep the combo alive, but the route is valuable because the mission has concentrated high-value pickups instead of scattered outdoor props.

<h2 id="amusement-park-roller-coaster-farm">
  Amusement Park Roller Coaster Farm
</h2>

The amusement park roller coaster is a late-game option for players who want a more relaxed way to build toward the cap.

Once you can access the amusement park area, get on the roller coaster and let the loop run. Each loop continues adding studs until the counter stops increasing.

Each loop is short and easy to repeat, so this method works better as a relaxed cap-push than a strict per-minute farm.

<h2 id="when-to-switch-farming-methods">
  When To Switch Farming Methods
</h2>

The mistake most players make is sticking with one farming method for the entire game.

The better approach is to switch methods when your unlocks change. The Stud Cache is best as soon as it is available. Gordon becomes better once his Rebound Launcher is upgraded. Police events are best when you are already moving through Gotham. Party Man and the roller coaster are best after replay and late-game access open up.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

<h3 id="what-is-the-fastest-way-to-farm-studs">
  What is the fastest way to farm studs in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight?
</h3>

The fastest simple method is the **Stud Cache loop** in the Batcave vehicle garage. Open the cache, fast travel away, return to the Batcave, and open it again.

With full stud upgrades and x4 scaling, it can pay around **30,000 studs per open**.

<h3 id="where-is-the-stud-cache">
  Where is the Stud Cache in the Batcave?
</h3>

The Stud Cache is near the **Batcave vehicle garage / vehicle bay**. Look for the red-and-yellow crate near the vehicles after redeeming the LEGO Group Online Survey reward code.

<h3 id="is-the-stud-cache-one-time-use">
  Is the Stud Cache one-time use?
</h3>

No. The survey reward unlocks the Stud Cache, and the crate can be reopened after you leave the Batcave and return. The reward code is redeemed once, but the crate itself can be looped.

<h3 id="how-much-is-a-purple-stud-worth">
  How much is a purple stud worth?
</h3>

A purple stud is worth **10,000 studs** before multipliers. At **x4**, it is worth **40,000 studs**, which is why you should always prioritize purple studs during farming routes.

<h3 id="what-are-the-best-gordon-farming-locations">
  What are the best Gordon farming locations?
</h3>

The best Gordon farming locations are dense rooms such as **Gotham Bank** and the **Batcave Trophy Room**. Both have enough breakable objects for the upgraded Rebound Launcher to bounce through multiple targets quickly.

<h3 id="are-police-radio-events-worth-doing">
  Are police radio crime events worth doing?
</h3>

Yes. They are worth doing whenever they appear near your route. They give a strong burst of studs and fit naturally into Gotham cleanup, especially if your combo and stud magnet upgrades are already active.

<h3 id="what-is-the-stud-cap">
  What is the stud cap?
</h3>

The visible stud counter caps at **9,999,999**. If your counter stops increasing at that number, stop farming and spend your studs.

<h3 id="should-i-farm-studs-before-buying-upgrades">
  Should I farm studs before buying upgrades?
</h3>

Do not delay the key farming upgrades just to build a bigger stud pile. If you already have Skill Bricks, buy Stud Magnet and combo upgrades first, then farm. Your later Stud Cache loops, Gordon routes, and replay farms will pay more.

<h3 id="do-stud-farming-upgrades-cost-studs-or-skill-bricks">
  Do stud farming upgrades cost studs or Skill Bricks?
</h3>

The key stud-farming upgrades — **Stud Magnet**, combo multiplier, combo drain, and combo fill upgrades — use **Skill Bricks**, not studs.
