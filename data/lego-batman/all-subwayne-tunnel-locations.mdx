<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
There are **9 SubWayne stations** in **LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight**, but only **8 open-world SubWayne puzzles** if you exclude the Tricorner story/tutorial station.

The full station list is **Tricorner**, **Old Gotham South**, **Old Gotham North**, **Old Gotham West**, **The Cauldron South**, **The Cauldron North / ACE Chemical**, **Newtown**, **Gotham Village / Robinson Park**, and **East End / Amusement Mile**.

The easiest way to solve them is to use **Batman’s Detect / X-Ray Vision** near each shutter, follow the wires, and bring the right character: **Catwoman** for Old Gotham South and West, **Jim Gordon** for Cauldron North and fire/foam steps, **Robin** for Newtown, and **Vehicle Grapple** for The Cauldron South.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="nine-stations-vs-eight-puzzles">
  9 SubWayne Stations vs 8 SubWayne Puzzles
</h2>

This is the part that causes most of the confusion.

Some guides say there are **8 SubWayne puzzles**. That is not wrong if they are only counting the open-world puzzle stations. But the full fast-travel network has **9 SubWayne stations** because **Tricorner** is introduced as a story/tutorial station.

<GuideTable
  caption="Why SubWayne counts can appear as 8 or 9."
  columns={countColumns}
  rows={countRows}
/>

For a full completion route, track all **9 stations**. For open-world puzzle solving, expect **8 non-story puzzle stations** after Tricorner.

<h2 id="use-detect-xray-vision">
  Use Detect / X-Ray Vision Before You Solve Each Station
</h2>

If you arrive at a SubWayne marker and the solution is not obvious, do not start randomly breaking everything. Use **Batman’s Detect / X-Ray Vision** first.

Most SubWayne puzzles are built around wires, hidden battery slots, yellow interactables, rooftop objects, or small side paths. Detecting the area usually tells you which object the shutter is connected to.

<GuideTable
  caption="How Detect and X-Ray Vision help with SubWayne puzzles."
  columns={detectColumns}
  rows={detectRows}
/>

This is especially important for **Newtown**, where the battery machines are easier to confirm with X-Ray Vision, and for any station where a wire leads away from the entrance.

<h2 id="tricorner-subwayne">
  1. Tricorner SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **Tricorner SubWayne** is the story/tutorial station. It teaches the basic logic used by several later stations: follow wires, hit targets, use a character tool, then return to the shutter.

<GuideTable
  caption="Tricorner SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={tricornerStepRows}
/>

Once the final target is hit, the station is added to the SubWayne network.

<h2 id="old-gotham-south-subwayne">
  2. Old Gotham South SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **Old Gotham South SubWayne** requires **Catwoman**. The key clue is the **red wire** leading away from the shutter.

<GuideTable
  caption="Old Gotham South SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={oldGothamSouthStepRows}
/>

If the station still does not open, check whether you inserted both batteries. This is the easiest part of the puzzle to half-finish without realizing it.

<h2 id="old-gotham-north-subwayne">
  3. Old Gotham North SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **Old Gotham North SubWayne** is blocked by a crowd near a boombox. Your goal is not to fight them. You need to scare them away by using the plug and water valve setup.

<GuideTable
  caption="Old Gotham North SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={oldGothamNorthStepRows}
/>

If you only look at street level, the puzzle feels like it has no solution. Grapple up and check the roof above the entrance.

<h2 id="old-gotham-west-subwayne">
  4. Old Gotham West SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **Old Gotham West SubWayne** requires both **Catwoman** and **Batman**. Catwoman starts the puzzle, but Batman finishes it with Batarangs.

<GuideTable
  caption="Old Gotham West SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={oldGothamWestStepRows}
/>

Do not come here with Batman alone. You need Catwoman’s whip to reveal the targets above the crowd.

<h2 id="the-cauldron-south-subwayne">
  5. The Cauldron South SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **Cauldron South SubWayne** is the vehicle puzzle. Bring the **Batmobile** or another vehicle that can use the grapple / winch interaction.

<GuideTable
  caption="The Cauldron South SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={cauldronSouthStepRows}
/>

If the container is not moving, line up the vehicle and look for the grapple prompt instead of trying to push it by hand.

<h2 id="the-cauldron-north-subwayne">
  6. The Cauldron North / ACE Chemical SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **Cauldron North / ACE Chemical SubWayne** is blocked by a van. To move it, you need to restore power to the nearby billboard.

<GuideTable
  caption="The Cauldron North / ACE Chemical SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={cauldronNorthStepRows}
/>

The important tool here is **Jim Gordon’s Foam Sprayer**. If the fire pipe is still active, you have not started the real power route yet.

<h2 id="newtown-subwayne">
  7. Newtown SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **Newtown SubWayne** requires **Robin** and benefits heavily from **Batman’s X-Ray Vision**.

The confusing part is the battery placement. The batteries need to go into the correct power stations, not just any empty machine.

<GuideTable
  caption="Newtown SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={newtownStepRows}
/>

Use the **3rd** and **4th** power slots. If you are unsure, scan near the red toolbox and follow the green wires.

<h2 id="gotham-village-robinson-park-subwayne">
  8. Gotham Village / Robinson Park SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **Gotham Village / Robinson Park SubWayne** is one of the longer station puzzles. A truck blocks the entrance, and you need to clear several buttons before the worker moves it.

<GuideTable
  caption="Gotham Village / Robinson Park SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={gothamVillageStepRows}
/>

If the worker does not move the truck, you missed one of the buttons or the roof target.

<h2 id="east-end-amusement-mile-subwayne">
  9. East End / Amusement Mile SubWayne Solution
</h2>

The **East End / Amusement Mile SubWayne** is a battery-and-lever puzzle. The part players usually miss is the vent hidden behind the dumpster.

<GuideTable
  caption="East End / Amusement Mile SubWayne puzzle solution."
  columns={stationStepColumns}
  rows={eastEndStepRows}
/>

Once this station is open, you have a useful fast-travel anchor near Amusement Mile. If you are also farming money in this part of the map, use the <Link href="/lego-batman/how-to-farm-studs-fast">how to farm studs fast guide</Link> after your SubWayne network is active.

<h3 id="how-many-subwayne-tunnels-are-there">
  How many SubWayne tunnels are there in LEGO Batman: Legacy of the Dark Knight?
</h3>

There are **9 SubWayne stations** in total, including the **Tricorner** story/tutorial station.

Some guides count **8 SubWayne puzzles** because they exclude Tricorner and only count the free-roam puzzle stations.

<h3 id="what-do-subwayne-tunnels-do">
  What do SubWayne tunnels do?
</h3>

SubWayne tunnels expand Gotham’s fast-travel network. Every station you unlock gives you another travel point, which makes district cleanup much faster.

<h3 id="why-do-some-guides-say-there-are-only-8-subwayne-puzzles">
  Why do some guides say there are only 8 SubWayne puzzles?
</h3>

They are usually counting only the open-world puzzle stations. The full network has 9 stations, but Tricorner is introduced through the story, so some puzzle guides leave it out of the count.

<h3 id="which-subwayne-tunnel-is-story-related">
  Which SubWayne tunnel is story-related?
</h3>

The **Tricorner SubWayne** is the story-related station. It introduces the SubWayne system and uses a tutorial-style shutter puzzle.

<h3 id="which-subwayne-tunnels-require-catwoman">
  Which SubWayne tunnels require Catwoman?
</h3>

**Old Gotham South** and **Old Gotham West** require Catwoman.

Old Gotham South uses Catwoman’s whip on a yellow panel, while Old Gotham West uses her whip to reveal Batarang targets above the crowd.

<h3 id="which-subwayne-tunnels-require-jim-gordon">
  Which SubWayne tunnels require Jim Gordon?
</h3>

**The Cauldron North / ACE Chemical** requires Jim Gordon’s Foam Sprayer. Gotham Village also uses Gordon for fire-covered buttons and roof fire.

<h3 id="which-subwayne-tunnel-requires-robin">
  Which SubWayne tunnel requires Robin?
</h3>

The **Newtown SubWayne** requires Robin. Use Robin’s staff on the yellow circle on the roof to open the shutters before solving the battery puzzle.

<h3 id="which-subwayne-tunnel-requires-vehicle-grapple">
  Which SubWayne tunnel requires Vehicle Grapple?
</h3>

The **Cauldron South SubWayne** requires Vehicle Grapple or Batmobile winch access.

<h3 id="should-i-use-detect-vision-for-subwayne-puzzles">
  Should I use Detect / X-Ray Vision for SubWayne puzzles?
</h3>

Yes. Detect / X-Ray Vision is one of the best tools for SubWayne puzzles because it helps you spot wires, battery machines, hidden power routes, and interactable objects.

<h3 id="can-you-unlock-subwayne-tunnels-before-finishing-the-story">
  Can you unlock SubWayne tunnels before finishing the story?
</h3>

You can unlock some SubWayne stations as soon as the area and required character are available, but others should be saved until you have Catwoman, Robin, Jim Gordon, and a vehicle with grapple access.

<h3 id="are-subwayne-tunnels-missable">
  Are SubWayne tunnels missable?
</h3>

No. If you cannot unlock one yet, come back later with the required character, tool, or vehicle.
