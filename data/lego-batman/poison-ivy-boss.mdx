<h2 id="poison-ivy-boss-overview">
  Poison Ivy Boss Overview
</h2>

Poison Ivy is not a straight damage race.

The fight has three jobs:

- get through the overgrown lab entrance,
- survive the giant plant’s attack pattern,
- and finish the fight by tying up the plant mouths.

Most players get stuck because they keep attacking during protected plant animations. The boss only gives you short exposed windows, and the final step changes from attacking to using the mouth tie-up prompt.

<h2 id="extra-plants-and-arena-pressure">
  Extra Plants and Arena Pressure
</h2>

After the first opening, Ivy adds more plant pressure.

This is the part where the fight can become messy. The extra plants are there to distract you from the main plant’s timing. Clear the closest threats first, especially anything that blocks your movement or keeps you trapped near the boss.

Use this priority:

1. move away from active acid or mouth attacks,
2. clear nearby plant pressure,
3. watch the Queen of Thorns again,
4. attack only when the main plant exposes itself.

The goal is not to clear the whole arena perfectly. The goal is to keep enough room to read the next boss cue.

<h2 id="tie-up-the-plant-mouths">
  Tie Up the Plant Mouths
</h2>

Near the end, the fight tells you to tie up the plant mouths.

<figure className="my-8">

This is the most important mechanic in the fight.

At this point, normal attacks are no longer the main answer. Move to the prompted mouth interaction, tie up the active mouth, then repeat on any other active mouth prompt until the sequence resolves.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

<h3 id="how-do-you-beat-poison-ivy">
  How do you beat Poison Ivy in LEGO Batman Legacy?
</h3>

Cut through the green overgrowth to reach the lab, dodge the acid pressure, attack only when the plant exposes itself, clear extra plants, and tie up the active plant mouths at the end.

<h3 id="how-do-you-get-into-poison-ivys-lab">
  How do you get into Poison Ivy’s lab?
</h3>

Do not push through the wall of green directly. Use the nearby buildable / spare-parts route to cut through the dense overgrowth and reveal the hidden lab entrance.

<h3 id="when-can-you-damage-poison-ivy">
  When can you damage Poison Ivy?
</h3>

Damage the boss when the Queen of Thorns plant overextends and exposes itself after a pressure pattern.

<h3 id="how-do-you-avoid-the-acid-attack">
  How do you avoid the acid attack?
</h3>

Back away when the acid attack starts. Treat acid as a spacing check, then punish only after the plant becomes exposed.

<h3 id="what-do-you-do-when-ivy-summons-more-plants">
  What do you do when Ivy summons more plants?
</h3>

Clear the closest plant threats first so you have enough room to read the main plant’s next cue.

<h3 id="how-do-you-tie-up-the-plant-mouths">
  How do you tie up the plant mouths?
</h3>

When the prompt appears, move to each active mouth interaction and use the tie-up prompt. Do this until the sequence resolves.

<h3 id="why-is-the-poison-ivy-fight-not-ending">
  Why is the Poison Ivy fight not ending?
</h3>

You are probably still attacking normally instead of using the mouth tie-up prompt. The final step is an interaction check.

<h3 id="does-poison-ivy-have-exact-health-phases">
  Does Poison Ivy have exact health phases?
</h3>

The useful route is based on visible cues, not exact health percentages: acid pressure, exposed windows, extra plants, and the mouth tie-up finish.

<h3 id="do-you-need-a-later-character-for-poison-ivy">
  Do you need a later character for Poison Ivy?
</h3>

No. The story route gives you what you need. The key is following the plant mechanics and using the final mouth prompt.

<h3 id="what-should-you-do-after-poison-ivy">
  What should you do after Poison Ivy?
</h3>

Continue the story and return to the guide hub if you are planning the next boss route or cleanup path.
