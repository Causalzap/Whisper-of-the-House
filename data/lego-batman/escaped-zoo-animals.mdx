<h2 id="before-you-start">
  Before You Start Escaped Zoo Animals
</h2>

Escaped Zoo Animals are not just normal map icons. The activity depends on tracker-style cleanup, district access, and your ability to spot animals through Gotham’s dark streets, rooftops, docks, and cluttered industrial areas.

If an animal does not appear, do not immediately assume the marker is wrong. Continue the story, return after more of Gotham is open, and use Detective Mode around the marked area.

<h2 id="how-escaped-zoo-animal-tracking-works">
  How To Tag Escaped Zoo Animals
</h2>

Most Escaped Zoo Animal objectives follow the same basic loop: reach the marker, find the animal, get close, and place the tracker. The hard part is not always the location. It is often the animal behavior.

Some animals are passive and let you walk up to them. Others may move, hide above you, run around the marker area, or interrupt you if you approach directly.

  caption="Escaped Zoo Animal behavior and tracker tips."

  rows={behaviorRows}

Use this rule while clearing them:

**Map marker first → Detective Mode second → check height levels third → attach tracker only when the approach is safe.**

<h2 id="tricorner-island">
  Tricorner Island
</h2>

Tricorner Island has two Escaped Zoo Animal tasks: **Bye-Bye, Hiss American Python** and **Bear With Me**.

The snake marker is the main one to watch. The mission name says **American Python**, but some players may describe the animal model as a cobra-style snake. Either way, treat this as the Tricorner Island snake task.

Do not stay only on the road. The marker sits around the industrial dock side of the island, and the animal can be easy to miss if you never check the raised cargo platforms, numbered dock structures, or open walkways.

**Bear With Me** is more straightforward. The brown bear is around the western cargo route, but approach carefully because bear tasks can interrupt you if you rush straight in.

<h2 id="old-gotham-south">
  Old Gotham South
</h2>

Old Gotham South has four entries: the story-related **Polar Bear**, **Find Cobblenot**, **Zebra Crossing**, and **C’Meerkat**.

The story Polar Bear is different from the normal free-roam animals because it appears during a story route. If you are only doing open-world cleanup, do not confuse it with **Fur-ious**, which is the separate Polar Bear task near Robinson Park.

For **Find Cobblenot** and **C’Meerkat**, the main issue is size. Penguins and meerkats are easy to miss in dark streets, plazas, or busy environmental clutter. Use Detective Mode and sweep the full marker area instead of walking to the coordinate and stopping.

**Zebra Crossing** is simpler. Stay near the street-level crossing area around the marker and look for the zebra on the road route.

<h2 id="old-gotham-north">
  Old Gotham North
</h2>

Old Gotham North contains **Not So Wily** and **Under Koalafied**.

**Not So Wily** is mostly a street and nearby-building search. If the coyotes are not visible immediately, circle the marker and check nearby sidewalks, raised access points, and alleys.

**Under Koalafied** is the important task here. Koalas are easy to miss because they are often tied to elevated structures rather than the main road. Use Detective Mode, look upward, and check high walkways, building edges, ledges, and vertical surfaces around the coordinate.

<h2 id="old-gotham-west">
  Old Gotham West
</h2>

Old Gotham West has **Toucan Play At That Game** and **Yes. Yes. The Tiger Is Out.**

The tiger is the easier animal to identify because it is large and usually handled around ground-level plaza space. The challenge is the approach: if it reacts to you, use height, gliding, or a side angle to close the distance safely.

The toucan is easier to miss visually. Do not only search the street. Check raised plaza objects, statues, ledges, signs, and nearby structures around the marker.

<h2 id="the-cauldron-north">
  The Cauldron North
</h2>

The Cauldron North has one Escaped Zoo Animal task: **Making A Macawry**.

This is a bird task, so treat the marker as a vertical search area. Macaws may sit around rooftops, pipes, industrial platforms, vents, smokestacks, or ledges instead of obvious ground positions.

Use Detective Mode while looking upward. If you are at the coordinate and cannot see the final macaw, rotate the camera around nearby rooftop and factory structures before leaving the area.

<h2 id="newtown">
  Newtown
</h2>

Newtown has three animal tasks: **Cat-ch Up**, **Hip Hoppers**, and **Polly Want a Tracker?**

**Cat-ch Up** is usually the simplest of the three because the panther is large and easier to identify around the ground-level marker. Approach from a controlled angle if it reacts.

**Hip Hoppers** is more annoying because frogs are small. Use Detective Mode and scan low ground, edges, trackside details, and nearby structures around the marker.

**Polly Want a Tracker?** is the Newtown task most likely to waste time. Parrots may not all sit at the same height. Search the marker, then check nearby rooftops, building sides, signs, ledges, and elevated routes for the remaining birds.

<h2 id="gotham-village-robinson-park">
  Gotham Village / Robinson Park
</h2>

Gotham Village and Robinson Park have the largest Escaped Zoo Animal cluster: **Tree Trunks**, **Spotty Reception**, **Fur-ious**, **I Ain’t Lion!**, and **Pack It Up**.

Use the coordinates carefully here because the map can become crowded with nearby icons. This is the section where a table and coordinate check are most useful.

**Tree Trunks** is easier to identify once you reach the right park area because the elephants are large. **I Ain’t Lion!** is similar: the lion is visible, but approach with care if it reacts.

**Spotty Reception** and **Pack It Up** require a wider sweep. Leopards and wolves can be spread around paths, grass, stone edges, and open park sections. Keep Detective Mode active and circle the marker until every animal is tracked.

**Fur-ious** is the free-roam Polar Bear task. Do not confuse it with the earlier story-related Polar Bear in Old Gotham South.

<h2 id="east-end-amusement-mile">
  East End / Amusement Mile
</h2>

East End / Amusement Mile has the final two tasks: **Un-Bear-able** and **Some Slow Bros**.

**Un-Bear-able** is the black bear task. The bear can be aggressive, so avoid rushing straight into it. Wait for a safe angle, approach when it is not charging, and place the tracker quickly.

**Some Slow Bros** is one of the easiest tasks to miss even though sloths are passive. The problem is vertical placement. Sloths can blend into poles, scaffolding, ladders, rails, rooftops, and building-side structures. If you cannot find the last one, stop looking at the road and start scanning upward with Detective Mode.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

<h3 id="why-are-escaped-zoo-animals-not-spawning">
  Why are my Escaped Zoo Animals not spawning?
</h3>

You may be too early in the story or missing the tracker-related cleanup unlock. Continue the main story, open more districts, then return to the marker later.

<h3 id="do-you-need-detective-mode-for-escaped-zoo-animals">
  Do you need Detective Mode for Escaped Zoo Animals?
</h3>

You do not need it for every animal, but Detective Mode is the best tool for the harder ones. Use it for koalas, sloths, macaws, parrots, frogs, meerkats, and any marker where the animal is not visible immediately.

<h3 id="which-escaped-zoo-animals-can-attack-you">
  Which Escaped Zoo Animals can attack you?
</h3>

Large animals such as bears and big cats are the ones to treat carefully. If the animal reacts, avoid a direct approach and wait for a safer moment to attach the tracker.

<h3 id="why-cant-i-place-the-tracker">
  Why can’t I place the tracker?
</h3>

You are usually not close enough, approaching from a bad angle, or trying while the animal is moving or reacting. Get closer, use stealth or height if needed, and attach the tracker when the animal gives you a safe opening.

<h3 id="which-escaped-zoo-animal-is-easiest-to-miss">
  Which Escaped Zoo Animal is easiest to miss?
</h3>

The easiest ones to miss are **Bye-Bye, Hiss American Python**, **Under Koalafied**, **Making A Macawry**, **Polly Want a Tracker?**, and **Some Slow Bros** because they involve high platforms, rooftops, ledges, birds, or vertical structures.

<h3 id="is-the-story-polar-bear-the-same-as-fur-ious">
  Is the story Polar Bear the same as Fur-ious?
</h3>

No. The Old Gotham South Polar Bear is tied to a story route, while **Fur-ious** is the separate free-roam Polar Bear task near Robinson Park.

<h3 id="are-escaped-zoo-animals-missable">
  Are Escaped Zoo Animals missable?
</h3>

No. You can return to Gotham cleanup later and finish the Escaped Zoo Animal tasks after more story progress.

<h3 id="what-reward-do-you-get-for-escaped-zoo-animals">
  What reward do you get for Escaped Zoo Animals?
</h3>

Escaped Zoo Animals contribute to Gotham cleanup and the animal objective reward path, including the **Absolute Batman** costume reward.

<h3 id="do-coordinates-matter">
  Do coordinates matter for Escaped Zoo Animals?
</h3>

Yes. Coordinates help confirm the correct marker, especially around Robinson Park where several activity icons can sit close together. Use the coordinate first, then search the full area around it.
