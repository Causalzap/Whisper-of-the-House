<h2 id="who-they-are">
  Who are Person 12 and Person K?
</h2>

The two hidden profiles solve different problems:

- **Person 12** is an earlier victim missing from the numbered roster.
- **Person K** is a remote victim whose death can be detected even though the
  machine cannot follow her full route through Galley House.

The launch achievement snapshot shows how easy both are to leave unresolved.
Only **28.4%** of players had learned the fate of Person 12, and **28.0%** had
learned the fate of Person K. In practical terms, fewer than three in ten
players had completed either deduction at the time of verification.

The difficulty is not entering the final code. The difficulty is knowing when
the evidence has become strong enough to create a new profile instead of
forcing another known person into the scene.

<h2 id="when-to-solve">
  When to solve them and when to keep progressing
</h2>

I do not try to solve either profile as soon as an unfamiliar name appears.

For **Person 12**, I wait until I have all four of these:

1. the unidentified body in the Study;
2. the Amelia West piano program;
3. the Fox case note;
4. and the thunderclap at the beginning of Time 01.

For **Person K**, I wait until I have:

1. the Dining Room phone call;
2. confirmation that the caller is Annie's mother;
3. the Wintercote location;
4. the distant thunder before John's fall;
5. and the machine note explaining deaths outside its local range.

### Keep progressing when

- Rupert has only been mentioned by name;
- the Study body has not yet been connected to the piano;
- the phone caller has not identified her relationship to Annie;
- or Wintercote is not yet available as a location.

### Return to the machine when

- the Timeline begins one death earlier than the known 1–11 sequence;
- a thunderclap has no matching local victim;
- the machine manual explains remote deaths;
- or every known profile creates a contradiction.

This is the point where manual calibration becomes part of the puzzle rather
than an optional naming tool.

<h2 id="person-12">
  Person 12 solution: Rupert Galley
</h2>

The real trap is assuming the visible roster is complete.

The machine begins with numbered profiles **1 through 11**, so it is natural to
treat the dead man in the Study as one of those people. That creates a dead end:
the body is already present before the known chain starts, and nobody in the
room can identify him.

### Step 1: return to the first Study body

The useful reconstruction is the early Study scene where John, Martha, and
Damian find a dead man.

The body has no obvious injury. The item beside him is more useful: an old
program for a piano recital by **Amelia West**.

I do not identify him from the program alone. It establishes the piano

### Step 2: match the Fox case note

The **Fox** entry is the strongest physical match. It describes:

- a long-term injury to the left leg;
- an empty film canister;
- and a key to a piano.

A piano key by itself is not enough. The film canister and old leg injury make

### Step 3: use the opening thunderclap

The important Timeline observation is that a thunderclap occurs at the
beginning of the case, before the deaths assigned to the known numbered
victims.

That means the chain starts with someone before Person 11.

The roster counts down through the known victims. The missing earlier profile
is therefore **12**, not 0 and not another existing character.

### Step 4: connect Rupert to Amelia

When Rupert hears Amelia's music, he recognizes the piece and realizes he had
forgotten her.

music, and opening thunderclap** all point to the same person and moment.

<h2 id="person-k">
  Person K solution: Katherine Beaumont
</h2>

Person K is harder because the final scene is not inside Galley House.

The machine can detect her death, but it cannot reconstruct a normal sequence
of local scenes around her. That is why treating K like another numbered house
guest never works.

### Step 1: replay the Dining Room phone call

John answers the phone in the Dining Room.

The caller identifies herself, asks for Martha, and then asks whether her
daughter **Annie** is safe. She says she lives nearby and can send neighbors,
but she cannot come to the house herself.

The wider Beaumont family evidence also identifies Katherine as the mother of
**Victoria Beaumont**. The call directly confirms Annie, while the family
records connect both sisters to Katherine.

This tells me who K is, but it does not yet give the death time.

### Step 2: identify the Deer portrait

The **Deer** case note describes:

- a wheelchair user;
- a broken teacup nearby;
- and a photograph of two young girls.

Those details fit Katherine's situation and family connection.

I treat the two-young-girls photograph as the strongest relationship clue. The
wheelchair and teacup then support the physical scene at her home.

### Step 3: do not assign the distant thunder to John

Late in the route, John falls from the Attic.

A thunderclap occurs around this sequence, and the easy assumption is that the
thunder belongs to John because his death is the visible event.

That assumption creates the wrong chain.

John's fall is not the unmatched remote death. The distant thunder belongs to
Katherine.

This is when I stop following only visible bodies and read the final machine
instructions again.

### Step 4: read the remote-death rule

The machine note explains that it can normally follow someone who dies within
its local radius. A person who dies during the Timeline but outside that radius
may leave only one accessible moment: the moment of death.

It also points toward three types of evidence:

- distant thunder;
- the relative location;
- and who remembered the person last.

### Step 5: use Wintercote and the letter K

The caller's location is **Wintercote**, abbreviated **WI**.

Her profile uses the letter **K**, not the next unused number. The letter is
part of the solution and distinguishes her from the numbered Galley House
victims.

<h2 id="why-the-codes-work">
  Why 01-ST-12 and 25-WI-K work
</h2>

The two codes test whether I understand the machine's boundaries.

### Rupert is outside the starting roster

Rupert is local, but his death sits before the known 1–11 sequence. The machine
can reconstruct him normally once I create the missing numeric profile.

### Katherine is outside the local radius

Katherine belongs to the Timeline, but she is not moving through Galley House.
The machine reaches only her death moment, so the solution depends on remote
location and relationship evidence.

That difference explains why:

- Rupert uses a normal numbered ID and a Galley House room;
- Katherine uses a letter ID and Wintercote.

<h2 id="achievement-cleanup">
  Can I return for the two achievements later?
</h2>

I prefer solving both before moving deeply into the modern-day investigation
because their clues are still fresh.

If I continue first, I do not start a new save. I return to the original
profile set, add the missing person, and enter the exact code.

The achievement unlocks when the hidden scene plays:

- `01-ST-12` → **Inciting Incident**
- `25-WI-K` → **Not Over Yet**

Neither achievement requires rebuilding the entire Timeline after the correct
profile and location exist.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Common mistakes with Person 12 and Person K
</h2>

### Forcing Rupert into profiles 1–11

The unidentified Study body is the evidence that the roster is incomplete.

### Choosing Fox from the piano key alone

I wait for the film canister, leg injury, Amelia program, and thunderclap to
support the same identity.

### Using the Study discovery time for Rupert

The body is discovered later. Rupert's death belongs to **Time 01**.

### Treating Katherine as a house guest

She is on the phone from another location and never follows the ordinary room
route.

### Assigning the distant thunder to John

John's visible fall distracts from Katherine's off-site death.

### Adding another number instead of K

The final code requires the letter profile **K**.

### Entering a Galley House room for Katherine

Her location is **Wintercote (WI)**, not the Dining Room where John answers the
phone.
