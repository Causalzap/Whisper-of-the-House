<h2 id="part-1-the-revelation-and-john-the-baptist">
  Part 1 — The Revelation and John the Baptist
</h2>

The story opens in **Nazareth**, where Jesus is told to begin his public journey by finding **John the Baptist**.

### Step 1 — Talk to Joseph, then Mary

Start by speaking with **Joseph**. He sends you to **Mary**, who gives the first real lead:

- John was recently mentioned at the **market**
- the fruit seller there may know more
- your first route stays inside town, not outside it

The clean path is:

**Joseph → Mary → market**

### Step 2 — Follow the injured merchant lead

At the market, ask about the destroyed stall and then use **Divine Vision** to follow the blood trail.

This sequence gives you the real route forward:

- find the wounded merchant
- warn his friend at the **farm**
- get the direction toward the **Jordan River**

### Step 3 — Fastest route to John the Baptist

If you want the shortest route, do not over-search Nazareth.

Use this order:

1. **Joseph**
2. **Mary**
3. **market stall**
4. **blood trail**
5. **farm friend**
6. **cart ride**
7. **Jordan River**

<GuideCallout title="Fastest John route">
If you feel lost in the opening, the answer is not more wandering. Follow the market clue chain and the game will naturally point you to John.
</GuideCallout>

### Step 4 — Gate Puzzle order

At the Jordan section, you hit the first major puzzle wall.

Use this order:

1. **Light**
2. **Water and Sky**
3. **Land and Vegetation**
4. **Sun, Moon, and Stars**
5. **Sea Creatures and Birds**
6. **Land Animals and Humans**

The logic is simply the **Genesis creation order**.

### Step 5 — Finish the baptism scene

Once the Gate Puzzle is done, continue the dialogue with **John the Baptist** and complete the baptism sequence.

This closes the opening act and sends you into the next major section:

**the Wilderness**

This section is fairly linear at first. Just keep following the spirit marker until the temptation encounters begin.

### Step 2 — Clear the three temptation scenes

The sequence escalates through:

The dialogue is simple enough. The real choke point is the gameplay section.

### Step 3 — How to beat the Satan boss in the Wilderness

This is where many players fail.

Use this approach:

- do **not** spam attacks
- stay out of harmful zones
- refill with prayer before you are completely drained
- enter Holy Spirit mode only when you are ready to hit cleanly

### Holy Spirit energy tips for early game fights

If you are running out of Holy Spirit too fast, the main mistake is usually overcommitting during miracle or combat sections.

Use these habits instead:

- do not spam power use the moment a fight starts
- recover before your bar is nearly empty
- use prayer as a rhythm tool, not a panic button
- save cleaner shots for when the target or projectile path is obvious
- back out of poison or dark zones before trying to recover

This will not replace skill, but it makes the Wilderness and later exorcism sections much more stable.

### Why the Holy Spirit bar runs out so fast in the Wilderness

The correct rhythm is:

- back off
- recover
- re-engage
- destroy incoming darkness
- repeat

If you keep dying here, it is usually because:

- you drained your energy too early
- you stayed in poison or dark zones too long
- you treated the whole fight like constant DPS

- **fast travel / easier travel skipping**

<h2 id="part-6-midgame-story-miracles-before-the-ending-run">
  Part 6 — Midgame story miracles before the ending run
</h2>

This section is where the campaign can feel thinner if you summarize too hard, so here is the cleaner breakdown.

### Nazareth synagogue walkthrough

Back in **Nazareth**, Jesus reads publicly, is challenged, and the crowd turns hostile.

This is one of the clearest “rejected in his hometown” scenes in the game, so treat it as a real main-story beat, not a small side episode.

### Royal official’s son walkthrough

The game also includes the healing of the royal official’s son.

This matters because it reinforces one of the core themes of the campaign:

**distance does not block faith or healing**

### Legion exorcism walkthrough

This is one of the clearest exorcism set pieces in the game, and it is worth spelling out because the objective changes in the middle of the scene.

The basic flow is:

- approach the possessed man
- pursue him when he starts fleeing
- force the identity reveal
- trigger the exorcism sequence
- finish the aftermath conversation instead of leaving immediately

When the confrontation begins, the key line is **“My name is Legion, for we are many.”** After that, the fight becomes a standard exorcism action sequence rather than a dialogue-only scene. Keep pressure on the target, complete the exorcism cleanly, and pay attention to the follow-up dialogue, because the game treats the release of the possessed man as part of the story payoff, not just a combat check.

If you are writing this section for SEO, do not hide it inside a one-line midgame summary. Players really do search for this miracle as a distinct moment.

### Paralytic through the roof walkthrough

Back in Peter’s house, you get the rooftop paralytic scene.

This is worth covering separately because it is a well-known story beat and a distinct search intent.

### Feeding the 5,000 and walking on water

This midgame sequence is longer than it first looks because it combines **crowd healing**, **food multiplication**, and a separate **walking on water** set piece.

Start by helping the wounded people in the crowd and letting the teaching sequence finish. After that, the objective shifts into the feeding miracle:

- the disciples point out that the crowd is hungry
- a boy is found with **five barley loaves and two fish**
- you must place the food into the baskets
- then open the baskets and continue the miracle interaction until the crowd scene resolves

Do not treat this as a pure cutscene. The game expects you to keep moving between baskets and finish the interaction prompts cleanly.

Once the feeding scene ends, the story transitions into the **Sea of Galilee** section. This is where many players get slowed down, because the actual goal is not just “walk on water” in a straight line. You need to:

- use divine power to locate the disciples’ boat
- move through the dark water while avoiding the visible storm hazards
- stay patient when the tornadoes drift across your path
- keep correcting your angle instead of charging directly into the nearest hazard

If you are getting frustrated here, the problem is usually visibility rather than route logic. Slow down, re-center on the boat, and only push forward when the nearest waterspout gives you a clean lane. Once you reach the boat, the scene continues with Peter stepping onto the water and then sinking when fear overtakes him.

### How to walk on water and reach the boat

If you are stuck in the Sea of Galilee section, the real goal is **reaching the disciples’ boat safely**, not sprinting blindly through the storm.

Use this approach:

- activate divine power so you can re-locate the boat
- watch the tornado movement before committing to a lane
- move in short corrections instead of one long straight charge
- if a waterspout drifts over the boat, stop, wait, and re-angle
- push through only when you have a visible opening

This section feels harder than it should mostly because the storm effects are dark and easy to misread. Treat it like a timing section, not a pure endurance test.

### Mount Tabor transfiguration walkthrough

Later, the campaign shifts into the **Mount Tabor** revelation scene with Peter, James, and John.

This section is more cinematic than puzzle-heavy, but it is still a real progression beat and should be present in the full walkthrough.

### How to raise Lazarus in Bethany

The Lazarus section is one of the biggest late-story miracles.

The clean route is:

- answer the message from **Bethany**
- meet **Mary and Martha**
- go to the tomb
- choose the resurrection miracle
- complete the resurrection scene
- finish the fallout dialogue

This miracle also strongly accelerates the move toward the Passion.
