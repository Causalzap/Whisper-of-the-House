ZeroSpace Act 1 is easier to complete when I treat it as a series of mission
problems instead of one long story recap. Operation Moby tests production and
objective order. Trisaris tests colony defense and command choices. Eterus
requires five Koru Hearts, while later missions introduce access fragments,
signal jammers, Fabricator Blocks, protected bunkers, and reinforcement sites.

This walkthrough focuses on what must remain alive, which objective should be
completed first, when a detour is safer than a direct attack, and which choice
consequences I could actually confirm. It does not repeat every conversation or
summarize lore that does not change the route.

<h2 id="opening-landing">
  Opening Landing walkthrough: keep the first squad alive
</h2>

The Campaign begins with a damaged deployment and a small surviving force.
There is no economy available to replace careless losses, so I use the opening
as a movement and ability test rather than a race to clear the entire map.

My priorities are:

1. regroup the surviving infantry;
2. keep the squad behind Aster during the first engagement;
3. cross the river with the available movement ability;
4. approach enemy positions from open ground or high ground;
5. reach Kris Diaz and the surviving strike force;
6. use the Striders to continue toward Operation Moby.

pull the squad toward open terrain instead of allowing individual infantry to

Injured units remain behind Aster during the final approach. Reaching the next
phase with a larger surviving force is worth more than clearing an unnecessary
group at the edge of the route.

<h2 id="eterus">
  Eterus walkthrough: how many Koru Hearts do you need?
</h2>

The Eterus mission requires **five Koru Hearts** for the infant Leviathan.

Each extraction follows the same cycle:

1. reach the marked Koru Heart;
2. position or call the specialized Harvester;
3. secure the site before extraction begins;
4. defend the Harvester;
5. clear the resulting Grell attack;
6. regroup before moving to the next site.

The fourth Heart does not complete the mission. It advances the objective to
one final extraction, making the required total five.

A Shakru group offers a ritual extraction that avoids treating them as the
direct enemy.

- the previous attack has ended;
- the defense group has regrouped;
- injured units have repositioned;

Overlapping two extraction cycles creates more pressure without finishing the
objective faster.

<h2 id="city-uprising">
  City Uprising walkthrough: use the side streets
</h2>

The city operation combines Free Corsair resistance, civilians, barricades,
snipers, military vehicles, and a route toward the magistrate.

The safer approach is to use side streets to flank protected shooters, secure
the available secondary base, rescue useful Commandos, and capture vehicles
already on the route.

Barricades can create shortcuts, but destroying every obstacle is unnecessary.
The objective is opening a safe line to the magistrate with enough units left
to respond if negotiation fails.

<h2 id="arcadia-reservoirs">
  Arcadia Reservoir defense: fortify before chasing convoys
</h2>

The reservoir operation becomes difficult when the whole army leaves a fixed
objective to pursue one vehicle group.

I first place defenses where approaches overlap and keep a mobile group between
the protected sites. Convoys and data pads become worthwhile only during a gap
between attacks.

When a reservoir is already taking sustained damage, the structure takes
priority over a vehicle that can still be intercepted later.

<h2 id="deru-lab">
  Deru Laboratory walkthrough: 3 fragments and the ZeroSpace reactor
</h2>

The Deru laboratory cannot be opened until Phobos reconstructs an access key
from **three fragments** found in nearby alien structures.

The mission order is:

1. establish the Operating Tower;
2. secure the laboratory perimeter;
3. collect the three fragments;
4. rescue nearby survivors during safe gaps;
5. return when the third fragment triggers the major attack;
6. defend until access is restored;
7. enter the laboratory;
8. overload the ZeroSpace reactor;
9. escape before the countdown ends.

Once the final fragment is collected, I stop exploring. The following warning
is the mission's signal to return to the laboratory.

<h2 id="four-bunkers">
  Protect the four bunkers before chasing Mondar
</h2>

The later Celestari operation contains **four bunkers** that must survive.

It also includes Black Watch prisoners, armored attackers, and an apparent
Mondar transmission.

The priority is defending whichever bunker is currently threatened. Useful
anti-armor units handle the heavy targets, while prisoners are rescued only
after the active bunker is stable.

After all four bunkers survive, the apparent Mondar transmission is revealed
to be a recording. Reaching it faster does not replace the defense objectives.

  Arcadia Machine Works: destroy the signal jammer first
</h2>

Arcadia Machine Works contains **six Fabricator Blocks**.

Each Fabricator can add more pressure while a Sun Corp signal jammer disables
nearby automated defenses.

For each active section:

1. identify the Fabricator Block;
2. locate its supporting jammer;
3. destroy the jammer;
4. let the restored defenses engage;
5. neutralize the Fabricator;
6. regroup before the next block becomes active.

The jammer is usually the cheaper and safer first target.

<h2 id="when-to-restart">
  When I restart an Act 1 mission
</h2>

I continue after ordinary infantry losses when the required structure, hero,
economy, and route remain intact.

I restart when:

- the Operating Tower or another mandatory structure is about to fall before
  the economy is functional;
- a fixed objective such as a reservoir, bunker, or laboratory is lost;
- a permanent attack source remains active when the final wave begins;
- the required hero is trapped or nearly dead before the next phase;
- production placement blocks movement between defensive fronts;
- or the mobile army cannot return before the protected objective collapses.

A damaged army can recover through production, rescued units, allied support,
and mission reinforcements. A destroyed mandatory objective usually cannot.

<h2 id="faq">
  ZeroSpace Act 1 Campaign FAQ
</h2>

### How do I complete Operation Moby?

Wait until the objective counters show four completed Supply Platforms, two
completed Barracks, and eight trained Commandos. Commandos still inside the
production queue may not count yet. Retake the southern airfield before
attacking the entrenched Thresher, and keep the Operating Tower protected after
the production objective finishes.

### Should I answer the Trisaris distress signal?

Answering it starts the Arcadia colony-defense mission. Later dialogue refers
back to the no-diversion order, Sherman disapproves, and the Aegis Oath loses
standing. I have not completed the refusal branch far enough to compare every
later reward or mission.

### How many Koru Hearts are required on Eterus?

Five. The objective does not complete after the fourth extraction; it advances
to one final Koru Heart. Clear the previous Grell response before beginning the
next ritual so two attack cycles do not overlap.

### Can Kris survive the ship boarding mission?

I have not found a verified rescue route. The mission provides no optional
rescue objective during the cargo-route retreat, and completing the Detonator
section faster did not reveal another prompt in this route.

### What happens if Aster disobeys Sherman?

Sherman calls the pursuit of Mondar insubordination and attempts to remove
Aster from command. The Aegis Oath crew follows Aster and continues the
pursuit. Consequences after the current Act 1 ending remain unconfirmed.

### Does the peaceful Shakru option remove combat from Eterus?

No. It prevents a direct fight with the cooperating Shakru, but Grell still
attack during their ritual extractions. The peaceful route changes who causes
the conflict; it does not make the mission combat-free.

### Why can I not enter the Deru Laboratory?

Phobos needs all three access fragments from the surrounding alien structures.
The third fragment also triggers the mission's return phase, so searching for
more optional objectives at that point can leave the laboratory undefended.

### Should I chase Mondar during the four-bunker mission?

No. Protect the four bunkers in the order they are threatened. Mondar's apparent
transmission is later revealed to be a recording, so reaching it does not
replace the bunker-defense objective.
