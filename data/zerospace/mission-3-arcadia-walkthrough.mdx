<h2 id="why-arcadia-fails">
  Why the Arcadia defense keeps collapsing
</h2>

The final swarm is rarely the only reason the mission fails.

The real problem is allowing several pressure sources to survive together:

- normal waves continue hitting the main entrance;
- Bastion production stalls without Flux;
- Old Bessie keeps sending additional spawn;
- the southern source opens a second attack direction;
- and delayed side objectives leave the mobile army outside the base when the
  final warning appears.

The safer strategy is sequential:

1. stabilize the main entrance;
2. establish Flux income;
3. add Bastions and reinforcements;
4. kill Old Bessie;
5. destroy the southern source;
6. return before the final swarm.

Trying to solve all six problems at once spreads the army too thin.

<h2 id="old-bessie">
  How to beat Old Bessie in ZeroSpace
</h2>

Old Bessie is introduced about **6 minutes and 20 seconds** after deployment.

She has made a nest near the colony and continues sending spawn until she is
killed. Leaving her alive turns every later wave into a larger fight.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zerospace/zerospace-arcadia-old-bessie-location.webp"
    alt="ZeroSpace Mission 3 Arcadia Old Bessie location near the colony and the mobile force sent to kill her"
    width={832}
    height={620}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-500">
    I send the mobile group after Old Bessie while the turret line,
    reinforcements, and heavy gunners continue protecting the main entrance.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

My Old Bessie approach is:

1. wait until the entrance can survive without Aster;
2. select Aster, Mera, and the mobile damage group;
3. move directly toward the nest;
4. focus Old Bessie instead of clearing every small spawn first;
5. use the heavy damage abilities while the target is exposed;
6. return toward the colony as soon as she dies.

Old Bessie appeared at about **6:18** and was dead before **8:00** in my clear.

That window is useful because it removes her production before the southern
flank becomes the next priority.

Leaving every unit at the entrance feels safe, but it allows Old Bessie to
increase the pressure indefinitely. Sending the whole army is the opposite
mistake because the colony can fall while the boss group is away.

The southern threat became active at about **9 minutes and 20 seconds** in my

A safer approach is:

1. confirm that the main entrance is stable;
2. send the mobile group south;
3. ignore unrelated enemies outside the route;
4. destroy the permanent source;
5. turn back as soon as the objective is complete;
6. refill production and Supply at the forward base.

I do not scout south before the warning. That creates unnecessary travel during
the opening economy and may leave the base exposed before the mission has
identified the objective.

When the colony begins taking sustained damage during the trip, I return,
stabilize the entrance, and make a second attempt rather than losing the entire
mission to finish the side target.

<h2 id="final-swarm">
  How to survive the Arcadia final swarm
</h2>

The overwhelming-swarm warning arrived at about **16 minutes and 40 seconds**
after deployment in my clear.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zerospace/zerospace-arcadia-final-swarm.webp"
    alt="ZeroSpace Mission 3 Arcadia final swarm warning before the last colony-defense battle"
    width={1090}
    height={874}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-500">
    Once the overwhelming-swarm warning appears, I stop pursuing side targets
    and prepare every remaining approach around the colony.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Before that warning, I want the following conditions:

- Old Bessie is dead;
- the southern source is destroyed;
- the Harvester is still working;
- the Barracks queue is active;
- Supply can support one final reinforcement cycle;
- mobile units are already returning to the base.

My final defense setup is:

1. place the durable frontline in front of the ranged units;
2. keep heavy gunners behind the main line;
3. position Aster and Mera where they can respond to either approach;
4. use the reinforcement call-in before the entrance collapses;
5. focus large siege threats before chasing smaller enemies;
6. keep damaged units inside turret coverage rather than pursuing retreating
   Grell.

The final warning is not the time to finish Old Bessie or search for the
southern objective. Those sources should already be gone.

Removing both sources does not cancel the final swarm, but it prevents optional
pressure from stacking on top of the required last battle.

<h2 id="when-to-restart">
  When I restart Mission 3
</h2>

I continue after ordinary infantry losses when the Operating Tower, colony
defenses, Harvester, and production cycle are still functional.

I restart when:

- the main defense is collapsing before Flux income begins;
- the Harvester is lost and there is no practical way to restore Bastion
  production;
- Old Bessie remains alive when the southern source becomes active;
- the mobile army is trapped far south when the final-swarm warning appears;
- the colony entrance has no surviving frontline or turret coverage;
- or a mandatory structure reaches an unrecoverable failure state.

One lost squad is recoverable. Three permanent attack sources active at the
same time usually are not.

<h2 id="faq">
  ZeroSpace Mission 3 Arcadia FAQ
</h2>

### What should I build first in ZeroSpace Mission 3?

Start with the Supply Platforms covering the main entrance, but keep enough
resources available for the Harvester. Spending the entire opening economy on
turret upgrades delays Flux income and the first Bastions, leaving the army
without a mobile answer to Old Bessie.

### How do I beat Old Bessie in ZeroSpace?

Leave the turret line, reinforcements, and enough infantry at the main entrance.
Send Aster, Mera, and the mobile damage group to the nest, focus Old Bessie
before clearing every small spawn, and return as soon as she dies. The Moran
heavy gunners are especially useful when they can attack the large target
without being surrounded.

### How do I survive the Arcadia final swarm?

Finish Old Bessie and the southern source before the warning, then recall every
mobile unit. Keep ranged units behind the frontline, use reinforcements before
the entrance breaks, and avoid chasing small Grell outside rocket-turret
coverage. Saving one final Supply cycle is more valuable than adding a distant
structure.

### Why can’t I train Bastions in Mission 3?

Bastions require Flux. Create a Harvester from the Operating Tower and confirm
that it has an available discovered node. A Harvester accidentally included in
the army selection can also be pulled away from its economic route.

### Where is the southern Grell source in Arcadia?

The mission identifies it only after the southern-flank warning. Move south
from the colony with the mobile group that handled Old Bessie. There is little
value in searching for it during the opening because the objective is not yet
active and the main base still needs the army.

### Should I evacuate the Colonists or the Militia?

The verified route here evacuates the Colonists. The immediate result is that
the Militia remains behind. I have not completed the Militia branch far enough
to confirm different rewards, later support, relationship changes, or an
alternate ending.

### Can I save both the Colonists and the Militia?

No option to save both appears in the decision panel. The dialogue states that
the final shuttle has enough room for only one group, so the choice cannot be
avoided by completing the defense faster in this route.

### Do rocket-turret upgrades replace the Supply Platforms?

The mission presents the rocket turret as an upgrade fitted to an existing
Supply Platform, not as a separate replacement building. I use the upgrade to
add lane coverage while continuing to plan Supply around the same platforms.
