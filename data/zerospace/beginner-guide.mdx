New to ZeroSpace? The first systems I needed to understand were not advanced
units or perfect build orders. They were resource flow, Supply management,
Flux harvesting, production timing, and knowing when the first base was stable
enough to expand.

This guide focuses on those early decisions. It explains why units stop
building, how Harvesters and Flux nodes work, when to add Supply, how I use the
hero without throwing it away, and how I recover after a bad opening fight.

It is designed for the first Tutorial, AI match, or early Campaign mission—not
for optimizing one competitive faction build.

<h2 id="first-ten-minutes">
  Your first 10 minutes in ZeroSpace
</h2>

I do not use a strict second-by-second build order when learning a new faction.
Instead, I complete five checkpoints in sequence.

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-4 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Complete the Tutorial objectives.</strong> I learn where production,
    army selection, the build menu, and top-bar abilities are before entering a
    busier mode.
  </li>

  <li>
    <strong>Build one production structure.</strong> One active Barracks is
    more useful than two idle Barracks competing for the same resources.
  </li>

  <li>
    <strong>Create a Harvester.</strong> I start Flux income before filling the
    queue with units that require Flux.
  </li>

  <li>
    <strong>Capture the nearest safe objective.</strong> I use the hero and
    starting units to take a nearby Control Tower without pushing deep into the
    map.
  </li>

  <li>
    <strong>Expand after the economy is stable.</strong> I wait until the first
    base has working production, enough Supply, active Flux income, and a
    defendable route to the expansion.
  </li>
</ol>

My first ten minutes are successful when I can answer three questions:

- What resource is currently limiting production?
- Can the original base survive if the army moves away?
- Do I have a safe reason to leave the base right now?

<h2 id="tutorial-and-controls">
  Finish the Tutorial and learn four controls first
</h2>

I initially tried to learn ZeroSpace by opening the build menu in an
unrestricted match. I could see buildings, research, mercenaries, hero
abilities, and production categories, but I did not understand which part of
the interface controlled the next unit I needed.

The Tutorial presents those systems in a better order:

1. choose a hero;
2. place a defensive structure;
3. capture a Control Tower;
4. use a faction ability;
5. build production;
6. start Flux harvesting;
7. train basic units;
8. add Supply;
9. expand;
10. unlock advanced production and mercenaries.

I learn four controls before worrying about the full keybinding list:

- **A:** Attack Move;
- **B:** open the build menu;
- **Tab:** access or cycle through production;
- **top-bar ability:** use the currently available faction power.

I use normal movement when I need to:

- retreat without stopping to fight;
- leave an enemy ability area;
- move behind a frontline unit;
- or occupy one exact position.

My rule is:

> I use Attack Move when the route is uncertain and direct movement when the
> destination matters more than fighting anything on the way.

<h3 id="hexite">
  Hexite comes from active bases
</h3>

Hexite is the basic resource generated by active bases. I do not create a
normal worker and assign it to a visible Hexite mineral line before producing
my first units.

When Hexite income feels too slow, I check whether:

- the current Operating Tower is active;
- I added more production than one base can support;
- I spent too much on structures that are not helping the next fight;
- or I am ready for a second base.

I do not add another Barracks simply because the first queue is empty. I first
check whether I can afford to keep both queues active.

<h3 id="flux">
  Create a Harvester to get Flux
</h3>

Flux requires an active Harvester.

I create it from the Operating Tower. Once completed, it automatically moves
toward the nearest available Flux node and starts gathering.

  <Image
    src="/images/zerospace/zerospace-arcadia-harvester-flux.webp"

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    A Harvester automatically travels to the nearest Flux node. I keep it
    outside my combat selection and let it work without manual army commands.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

The Tutorial also states that I can create one Harvester for every discovered
Flux node.

That gives scouting an economic purpose. Finding another node can support
another Harvester and increase the amount of advanced production I can sustain.

<h3 id="supply">
  Supply controls how large the army can become
</h3>

Supply is a unit cap rather than spendable income.

A production queue may appear funded but still refuse to finish because the
army has reached its supported Supply.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zerospace/zerospace-tutorial-supply-platform-warning.webp"

    creates an avoidable gap in army growth.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

I normally place the next Supply Platform when:

- several units are already queued;
- a second production structure is coming online;
- or the next army cycle will approach the current cap.

<h2 id="first-base">
  My first base setup
</h2>

I keep the first base compact but leave a clear movement lane through it.

My opening setup is:

1. one Supply or defensive platform;
2. one Barracks;
3. one Harvester;
4. enough infantry to protect the hero and base;
5. another Supply Platform before the cap;
6. advanced production only after the first army is functional.

One Campaign Barracks tooltip shows:

- **100** resource cost;
- **30 seconds** to construct;
- **1,000 health**;
- Commando production.

I avoid placing every structure in one tight block.

Large units, infantry, Harvesters, and reinforcements can obstruct one another
when they all try to leave through the same narrow gap.

<h3 id="unit-queue-blocked">
  Why the unit queue is blocked
</h3>

When a unit will not complete, I check:

1. current Supply and maximum Supply;
2. whether the unit requires Flux;
3. available Hexite;
4. the selected production category;
5. whether the production structure is still alive.

I do not solve the problem by immediately adding another Barracks.

More production makes a Supply or Flux shortage worse because the additional
queues compete for the same limited resources.

<h3 id="no-flux-income">
  Why Flux income is not working
</h3>

The usual causes are:

- no Harvester was created;
- no Flux node has been discovered;
- the Harvester was pulled into an army selection;
- or the Harvester was destroyed while moving between the base and node.

I select the Operating Tower, confirm that the Harvester exists, and then check
the path to the node.

<h3 id="army-too-small">
  Why the army stays small
</h3>

An army can remain small even with several production buildings when:

- the queues are waiting for Supply;
- Flux income is too low;
- too many structures were built before units;
- reinforcements are walking alone into enemy-controlled ground;
- or the hero keeps dying before the main army arrives.

I would rather operate one continuous production queue than three queues that
start and stop.

<h2 id="hero-management">
  Use the hero early, but keep it with the army
</h2>

Heroes are strongest during the opening stage and become support pieces as the
army grows.

The Tutorial states that a dead hero respawns at the base for free after a
short delay. That prevents one mistake from permanently removing the hero, but
an early death still costs:

- map pressure;
- scouting;
- access to hero abilities;
- protection for the first infantry group;
- and time around the nearest Control Tower.

My opening route is:

<div className="my-6 rounded-lg border border-slate-700 bg-slate-950/70 p-4 font-mono text-sm leading-7 text-slate-200">
  <div>base</div>
  <div>→ nearest safe Control Tower</div>
  <div>→ clear the small defenders</div>
  <div>→ capture the objective</div>
  <div>→ retreat when badly damaged</div>
  <div>→ rejoin the first infantry group</div>
</div>

I stop using the hero alone once the enemy can surround it or field enough
damage to force a quick retreat.

The Protectorate Tutorial also demonstrates a top-bar **Beam Up** ability that
returns the hero to the base. I use it when the nearby objective is already
secured and walking home would risk losing the hero.

<h2 id="production-management">
  Manage production without returning the camera to base
</h2>

Once several production structures are active, clicking each building becomes
unnecessarily slow.

The Tutorial explains that production structures can be selected together
through **Tab**.

I keep four responsibilities separate:

- hero;
- main combat army;
- new reinforcements;
- Harvesters.

New units rally behind the frontline. I merge them into the army after they
arrive rather than sending them individually through contested ground.

<h2 id="expansion">
  When I take a second base
</h2>

Expansion locations are represented by blue Hexagons on the map.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zerospace/zerospace-tutorial-expansion-blue-hexagon.webp"
    alt="ZeroSpace Tutorial explaining that expansion locations are represented by blue Hexagons"
    width={924}
    height={616}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    I secure the route to the blue Hexagon before spending on another Operating
    Tower.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

I expand when:

- the first base has working production;
- at least one Harvester is active;
- the next Supply cycle is covered;
- the route is under control;
- and the original base can survive without the entire army.

I delay the expansion when:

- an enemy army has disappeared into the fog;
- the first base lacks defensive coverage;
- I am already Supply blocked;
- or the expansion would consume resources needed for replacement units.

An unfinished second base does not protect the first base from an attack already
in progress.

<h2 id="advanced-units">
  Advanced units and support systems
</h2>

Striders and Mercenaries are useful, but I learn them after the basic economy
and production cycle are stable.

<h3 id="strider">
  Use the Strider as protected transport
</h3>

The Strider is a large walker that can transport infantry.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zerospace/zerospace-strider-load-unload.webp"
    alt="ZeroSpace infantry and large transport units fighting around a mission objective"
    width={904}
    height={776}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    I use the Strider to move infantry across exposed routes, then unload before
    the formation becomes surrounded.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

My sequence is:

1. gather infantry beside the Strider;
2. load before crossing exposed ground;
3. stop outside the main enemy formation;
4. unload while there is room to deploy;
5. let the infantry form a line before pushing farther.

I do not drive directly into the middle of an enemy group and unload there.
The infantry can emerge surrounded while the Strider blocks the easiest retreat
route.

I have not confirmed that every infantry type deals full damage while loaded,
so I treat the Strider as transport rather than a permanent firing platform.

<h3 id="mercenaries">
  Save Mercenary charges for immediate pressure
</h3>

Mercenary hiring works differently from normal unit training.

Mercenaries deploy as soon as the resources are spent, but each hire consumes
a Mercenary charge that replenishes over time.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/zerospace/zerospace-mercenary-charge-deployment.webp"
    alt="ZeroSpace tutorial explaining that Mercenaries deploy immediately and consume a replenishing Mercenary charge"
    width={974}
    height={628}
    className="h-auto w-full rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    This example shows two available charges and another group returning in 14
    seconds. I preserve a charge when I expect an emergency defense.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

I hire Mercenaries when I need:

- immediate frontline units;
- a role the primary faction currently lacks;
- emergency defense before normal production finishes;
- or support for an attack already moving.

I do not spend every charge merely because it is ready. Normal production can
replace routine losses; immediate deployment is more valuable when a base or
Control Tower is about to fall.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Beginner mistakes I stopped making
</h2>

### Searching for a Hexite worker

Hexite comes from active bases. The worker-style unit I manage is the Harvester
collecting Flux.

### Adding production before stabilizing the economy

Several idle Barracks do not create a larger army. I fix Supply and income
before adding more queues.

### Pulling the Harvester into combat

I keep Harvesters outside broad army selections and let them move to Flux nodes
automatically.

### Moving normally through unexplored ground

I use Attack Move so the army responds to enemies encountered along the route.

### Sending the hero too far ahead

I use the hero to capture a nearby objective, then rejoin the first army instead
of pushing alone into the enemy side of the map.

### Expanding during an active attack

I stabilize the original base and route first. An unfinished expansion does not
defend structures already under pressure.

### Spending every Mercenary charge

I keep one available when the next mission wave or counterattack is likely to
require immediate reinforcements.

<h2 id="faq">
  ZeroSpace Beginner Guide FAQ
</h2>

### How do I get Flux in ZeroSpace?

Create a Harvester from the Operating Tower. It automatically travels to the
nearest discovered Flux node. The Tutorial allows one Harvester for each Flux
node discovered on the map.

### Why is my unit queue blocked?

Check Supply first, followed by Flux, Hexite, the selected production category,
and whether the production structure is alive. Building another Barracks does
not fix a resource or Supply block.

### How does Supply work in ZeroSpace?

Supply is the unit cap. Supply Platforms increase the number of units the army
can support. Add another platform before reaching the limit so queued units do
not stop completing.

### When should I expand in ZeroSpace?

Expand after the original base has active production, working Flux income,
enough Supply for the next army cycle, and a safe route to the blue expansion
Hexagon.

### How do heroes respawn in ZeroSpace?

The Tutorial states that a dead hero returns to the base for free after a short
delay. The real penalty is losing map pressure, abilities, and protection for
the army while the hero is unavailable.

### Why do I have Hexite but still cannot train a unit?

The unit may also require Flux, or the army may be at its Supply cap. Check both
before adding another production structure.

### Do Mercenaries use a normal production queue?

No. They deploy immediately after hiring, but each hire consumes a Mercenary
charge that replenishes over time.

### Can infantry attack while loaded in a Strider?

I have not confirmed that every infantry type continues dealing full damage
while loaded. I use the Strider as protected transport and unload before the
main fight.
