<h2 id="can-you-replay-missions-in-mouse-pi-for-hire">
  Can you replay missions in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?
</h2>

**No, not in the traditional way players usually hope for.**

MOUSE: P.I. For Hire does **not** give you a clean mission replay or chapter-select safety net in the launch version.

That means if you finish a mission and move on, you cannot simply jump back from a menu and clean up everything you skipped.

If you miss something important, your recovery options are usually limited to:

- **loading a manual save**
- **using the later buy-back system for eligible item types**
- **accepting the loss and moving forward**

<GuideCallout title="Replay rule">
If you care about 100%, assume **every chapter transition matters** until your save structure proves otherwise.
</GuideCallout>

---

<h2 id="the-main-point-of-no-return">
  The main point of no return
</h2>

The most important cleanup lockout is tied to **Jack Squat**.

Once you begin **Jack Squat**, the game stops functioning like an open cleanup route and starts acting like a one-way endgame push.

Before this point, you can still use the main hub to:

- check unfinished Side Jobs
- clean up shop-based recovery options
- finish Baseball Card / Prize D-Spenser goals
- review what you still need before the final stretch

Before you commit, make sure you have:

1. checked the **P.I. Office board and Side Jobs**
2. cleared any reward paths you still care about
3. reviewed shop recovery for missed Newspapers, Comics, and normal Baseball Cards
4. made a dedicated manual save
5. confirmed you still have enough money for unfinished card-grind trophies

<GuideCallout title="Point of no return rule">
Treat **starting Jack Squat** as your real endgame warning.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="what-you-should-treat-as-recoverable-vs-real-missables">
  What you should treat as recoverable vs real missables
</h2>

The simplest high-level rule is this:

### Usually safer to treat as later cleanup
- **Newspapers**
- **Comics**
- **many standard Baseball Cards**

These are the collectible lines most commonly tied to the later shop cleanup loop.

### Safer to treat as real missables
- **Weapon Schematics**
- **Secret Figurines**
- **Side Job-exclusive rewards**
- **optional clues you can pick up now**

That difference is the real key to staying calm and routing cleanly.

<h2 id="what-about-key-clues-and-optional-clues">
  What about Key Clues and optional clues?
</h2>

Public trophy routing still warns that you should try to collect **every clue you come across**.

The safe rule is simple:

**if you can pick up a clue, pick it up.**

Players often separate clues from “real collectibles” because they think clues only matter for story flow.

For completion routing, that is too optimistic.

<GuideCallout title="Clue rule">
Until every clue-related edge case is fully settled, treat **optional clues as important**, not disposable.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="bugged-or-still-risky-trophies-you-should-know-about">
  Bugged or still-risky trophies you should know about
</h2>

At the time of writing, public trophy routing still treats these as the main caution trophies:

- **Misfortunes of the Missing Magician**
- **Real Deal Gumshoe**
- **Our Lesser Brothers**

The current public warning around **Real Deal Gumshoe** is especially important because **Behind the Nuts** is still reported as unstable.

Older roadmap versions also treated **Card Shark** as potentially glitched, but current community reports now indicate it can unlock normally once you collect the missing **P.I. Office upstairs Baseball Card**.

<GuideCallout title="Bug warning">
If a trophy does not unlock when a guide says it should, search the trophy name directly before assuming your whole run is dead.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Does MOUSE: P.I. For Hire have mission replay?

No. The launch version does not provide a traditional mission replay or chapter-select cleanup system.

### What is the point of no return in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

The most important practical lockout starts when you begin **Jack Squat**.

### How many save slots does MOUSE: P.I. For Hire have?

The game supports **50 manual saves and 3 autosaves**.

### What should I save before the point of no return?

The most useful named safety save is **right after Glugging from the Deep**, before you start Jack Squat.

### Can you buy back missed collectibles later?

Yes, but only some of them. Newspapers, Comics, and many standard Baseball Cards can be recovered later. Schematics, Secret Figurines, and some Side Job rewards should be treated as non-buyable later.

### Are Schematics permanently missable?

In practical routing terms, yes: they are non-buyable later. But missing some does **not** automatically kill the upgrade achievement because only **63 of the 83 total** are required.

### Are Clues missable in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

Yes, in practical routing terms you should treat clues as missable content and collect every clue you can.

### How many Side Jobs are there in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

There are **14 Side Jobs** in total.

### Is Real Deal Gumshoe glitched in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

At the time of writing, public routing still treats it cautiously because **Behind the Nuts** is still reported as unstable.

### Does difficulty affect trophies in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

No. Everything can currently be done on **Easy**.

### Does MOUSE: P.I. For Hire have New Game+?

No. There is currently **no New Game+**.

### Is there free roam after finishing the story?

No. There is currently **no free roam or level-select cleanup after the story**.
