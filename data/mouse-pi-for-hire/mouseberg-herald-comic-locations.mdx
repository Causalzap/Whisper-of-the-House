<h2 id="why-heralds-and-comics-matter">
  Why Heralds and Comics matter
</h2>

Heralds and Comics are easy to underestimate because neither one is a raw combat-upgrade line like Schematics.

That does not make them filler.

### Why Heralds matter

Mouseberg Heralds are one of the clearest worldbuilding collectible lines in the game.

They give the city texture, political context, criminal background, and recurring issue framing that turn Mouseburg into more than a series of combat spaces.

### Why Comics matter

Comics matter for more than just “one more collectible.”

The practical reason to care is simple:

**collecting comic pages helps piece together a larger illustrated story thread.**

That gives the Comic line a real collection motive even for players who are not chasing full 100% immediately.

<GuideCallout title="The simplest motivation split">
  <div>
    Collect <strong>Heralds</strong> if you care about Mouseburg as a place.
  </div>
  <div>
    Collect <strong>Comics</strong> if you care about assembling a more complete illustrated side-story through the collectible line.
  </div>
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="mouseberg-herald-vs-comics">
  Mouseberg Heralds vs Comics
</h2>

Even though these two lines often get grouped together, they do not fail in the same way.

### Mouseberg Heralds

Heralds are usually easier to identify once you actually see them.

The real problem is **timing and room discipline**.

Players usually lose Heralds because they:

- push the main objective too aggressively
- leave clue rooms, offices, lounge areas, or furniture spaces too early
- assume the side container or reading surface is decorative

### Comics

Comics are usually the opposite.

The real problem is **visibility discipline**.

Players usually lose Comics because they:

- do not look behind the obvious route
- ignore darker side surfaces
- skip vent branches
- fail to look upward toward ledges, shelves, catwalks, and climbable geometry

<GuideCallout title="The simplest search rule">
  <div>
    When looking for a <strong>Herald</strong>, think <strong>did I clear the room before pushing the objective?</strong>
  </div>
  <div>
    When looking for a <strong>Comic</strong>, think <strong>did I really check the weird corner, vent branch, side surface, or elevated path before I moved on?</strong>
  </div>
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="completion-and-trophy-anchors">
  Completion and trophy anchors
</h2>

These collectible lines are tied to real completion milestones.

### Mouseberg Herald trophy anchors

- **Paper Person** — collect 5 issues
- **Cover to Cover** — collect 10 issues
- **Extra! Extra!** — collect every issue of the Mouseberg Herald

### Comic trophy anchors

- **Comic Relief** — collect 5 comic strips
- **Extremely Graphic Novel** — collect 10 comic strips
- **The Prequel** — collect all comic strips

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What are Mouseberg Heralds in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

They are the game’s newspaper collectible line and usually act as worldbuilding pickups.

### What are Comics in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

They are a smaller hidden collectible line that also helps build out a collectible story thread.

### Which is easier to miss: Heralds or Comics?

Heralds are easier to lose to fast objective triggers, while Comics are easier to lose to route discipline, vent branches, and poor vertical scanning.

### Does the shop show Comic prices?

Yes. The current hub shop image shows **Comic Strip #1** and **Comic Strip #2** at **50** each.

### I left a mission and only then realized I missed a Herald. What now?

You cannot replay missions normally, so you should treat the miss as permanent unless the item can appear through the shop cleanup economy. Check the <Link href="/mouse-pi-for-hire/buy-back-guide">buy-back guide</Link>, but do not assume every miss is recoverable.

### I can see a Comic but I cannot reach it. What does that usually mean?

It often means you either missed a route branch or you need a traversal option that you have not used correctly yet. Check vent branches, overhead ledges, and alternate paths before assuming the collectible is gone.

### What is Paper Person in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

**Paper Person** is the Herald milestone trophy for **collecting 5 issues** of the Mouseberg Herald.

### What is Cover to Cover in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

**Cover to Cover** is the Herald milestone trophy for **collecting 10 issues**, not the full-Newspaper completion trophy.

### What is the full-Newspaper completion trophy?

The full-Newspaper trophy anchor is **Extra! Extra!**

### What is the all-Comics completion trophy?

The all-Comics trophy anchor is **The Prequel**

### How many Mouseberg Heralds are there in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

There are **38 Mouseberg Herald issues** in total.

### How many Comics are there in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

There are **29 Comics** in total.
