<h2 id="can-you-buy-missed-collectibles-later-in-mouse-pi-for-hire">
  Can you buy missed collectibles later in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?
</h2>

**Yes, but only for specific collectible types.**

The cleanest way to think about the buy-back system is this:

**MOUSE lets you recover some standard collectible lines through shops, but it does not function like a universal cleanup safety net.**

That distinction matters because players usually hear “you can buy missed collectibles later” and mentally translate it into “I can clean up everything later.”

That is **not** the safe way to play this game.

The practical pattern looks like this:

1. **ordinary collectible lines can often be recovered later**
2. **special collectible lines still behave like true missables**
3. **side-job rewards create their own exception layer**
4. **shop access itself has a late-game time limit**

<GuideCallout title="Best way to use buy-back">
  <div>
    Treat buy-back as a <strong>safety net for standard exploration misses</strong>, not as permission to ignore collectible routes completely.
  </div>
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="which-collectibles-can-be-bought-back">
  Which collectibles can be bought back?
</h2>

The categories that are currently safest to treat as recoverable later are:

- **Newspapers**
- **Comics**
- **standard Baseball Cards**

These are the collectible lines most consistently tied to later cleanup through the shop loop.

In practical terms, that means if you simply missed a normal pickup while moving through a chapter, you may still see that item appear later in the shop inventory instead of losing it forever.

### Why these are the safer cleanup categories

These collectible types behave more like **ordinary story-exploration pickups** than one-off progression gates.

That makes them the most believable candidates for launch-version catch-up support.

It also explains why strong completion routes still tell players to collect everything on sight anyway:

- shop cleanup costs money
- shop stock still has timing pressure
- buy-back does not protect every collectible line
- late-game shop access is limited

<GuideCallout title="Practical rule">
  <div>
    If a missed collectible is a <strong>normal Newspaper, Comic, or non-special Baseball Card</strong>, it is the kind of item most likely to be recoverable later.
  </div>
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="which-collectibles-cannot-be-bought-back">
  Which collectibles cannot be bought back?
</h2>

The two most important non-buy-back categories are:

- **Schematics**
- **Secret Figurines**

If you miss one of these during normal progression, you should not assume the shop will save your run.

### Why this distinction matters

These items are closer to **special progression or special collectible tracks** than ordinary environmental pickups.

A missed Newspaper is annoying.

A missed Schematic or Secret Figurine is the sort of miss that can force you into:

- an older manual save
- a later cleanup run on another slot
- or accepting that the current file is no longer your clean completion file

### Secret Figurines: what you actually need to know

There are **10 Secret Figurines** total.

They are one of the smallest collectible lines in the game, which is exactly why players tend to underestimate them.

They also do **not** belong to the shop cleanup system, so the small total count does not make them safer.

The menu also does **not** tell you how many of them you have.

If you want to check figurine progress, go back to the **P.I. Office shelf between your caseboard and bedroom**.

<h2 id="shop-only-cards-versus-buy-back-cards">
  Shop-only cards versus buy-back cards
</h2>

Not every Baseball Card in the shop is there because you missed it.

Some are simply **shop-sold cards by design**.

The clearest currently confirmed example is the early P.I. Office / Roadhouse shop card set:

- **8 Baseball Cards are sold in the shop**
- **4 cost 500 Coins**
- **4 cost 1000 Coins**

That means you should separate Baseball Cards into three buckets:

1. **ordinary field pickups**
2. **shop-sold cards**
3. **side-job reward cards**

Those three buckets do **not** share the same logic.

<GuideCallout title="Baseball Card rule">

Some **side-job Baseball Card rewards** do **not** behave like normal missed Baseball Cards.

That means you cannot safely say:

> “Baseball Cards are shop recoverable, so I can ignore any card reward for now.”

The clearest currently confirmed examples are:

- **Oh Yes... They Float** → **Jeremiah Curd Baseball Card**
- **Friends in Deep Places** → **Jacob “Cheesehead” McQueen Baseball Card**

Those are the side-job card rewards you should treat as real missables, not as normal shop cleanup.

<h2 id="clues-and-locked-containers-still-change-routing">
  Clues and locked containers still change routing
</h2>

This page is about buy-back, not clue solving.

But two related systems still matter for your cleanup planning.

### 1. Clues are not disposable

Even though Clues are not part of the shop cleanup economy, they still belong in your broader missables checklist.

The safe rule is simple: **collect clues on sight.**

### 2. Timed safes and locks still need discipline

Buy-back logic does not erase the risk from locks, safes, and tailpick attempts.

If you fail a difficult time-sensitive lock, you may be creating extra cleanup pressure for yourself even before the shop system becomes relevant.

The practical rule is:

**save before attempting black safes or any tailpick sequence you do not feel confident about.**

<GuideCallout title="Practical lock rule">

    Do not treat locked collectibles as future shop problems by default.
  </div>
  <div>

<h2 id="how-late-game-lockout-changes-buy-back-planning">
  How late-game lockout changes buy-back planning
</h2>

Even if the buy-back system exists, it is still tied to your access to the normal game loop.

Public collectible routing is already explicit about the danger here:

**the last 2 missions are the point where you no longer have access to the shops.**

That means even buy-back-friendly categories become risky if you wait too long.

That is why the safest completion rhythm is:

1. collect as much as possible during each mission
2. finish side jobs early
3. check shops regularly
4. spend your cleanup money before the late-game squeeze
5. do not enter the final two-mission stretch while still assuming cleanup is for later

<GuideCallout title="Late-game cleanup rule">
  <div>
    Once you are entering the <strong>final two-mission stretch around Jack Squat</strong>, stop assuming the shops will save a lazy cleanup plan.
  </div>
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="should-you-rely-on-buy-back-for-100-percent-completion">
  Should you rely on buy-back for 100% completion?
</h2>

**No. Use it, but do not rely on it.**

The buy-back system is strong enough to rescue ordinary exploration mistakes.

It is **not** strong enough to replace good completion habits.

### Best way to use it

Use buy-back for:

- accidental missed Newspapers
- accidental missed Comics
- accidental missed standard Baseball Cards

Do **not** use it as your main plan for:

- Schematics
- Secret Figurines
- side-job reward cards
- late-game cleanup after you have already pushed too far

### Best clean-completion routine

If you want the least painful route:

1. collect as much as possible during each chapter
2. keep multiple manual saves
3. check the shop loop after major progress beats
4. finish side jobs before the late story lockout
5. treat buy-back as backup, not as strategy

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Can you buy back missed collectibles in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire?

Yes, but only for specific collectible categories. The safest currently confirmed buy-back lines are Newspapers, Comics, and standard Baseball Cards.

### How many Newspapers, Baseball Cards, and Comics are there?

There are **38 Newspapers**, **41 Baseball Cards**, and **29 Comics** in total.

### Are some Baseball Cards sold in shops by default?

Yes. There are currently **8 known shop-sold Baseball Cards**, with **4 costing 500 Coins** and **4 costing 1000 Coins**.

### Can you buy back missed Schematics later?

No. Schematics should not be treated as shop-recoverable cleanup items.

### Can you buy back missed Secret Figurines later?

No. Secret Figurines should be treated as non-buy-back collectibles.

### How many Secret Figurines are there?

There are **10 Secret Figurines** in total.

### How do you check Secret Figurine progress?

The menu does not track Secret Figurines. To check progress, go to the **P.I. Office shelf between your caseboard and bedroom**.

### Where do missed collectible items show up later?

The main places to check are the **P.I. Office** shop and the **Roadhouse** shop.

### Do all Baseball Cards follow the same buy-back rules?

No. Ordinary field pickups, shop-only cards, and side-job reward cards do **not** all follow the same rule set.

### Which side-job reward cards should I treat as true missables?

The clearest named examples are **Oh Yes... They Float** and **Friends in Deep Places**, because they reward unique Baseball Cards that should not be treated like ordinary shop cleanup.

### What should I do before trying a black safe or difficult lock?

Make a manual save first. Buy-back does not protect you from bad lock-routing decisions.

### When does shop cleanup stop being safe to leave for later?

The practical danger point is the **final two-mission stretch**, where collectible routing no longer treats shop access as safely available.
