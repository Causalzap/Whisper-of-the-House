<h2 id="how-to-use-this-collectibles-hub">
  How to use this Collectibles Hub
</h2>

Do **not** use this page like a single giant checklist.

Use it like a **routing map**.

The most efficient way to handle **MOUSE: P.I. For Hire** collectibles is:

1. learn the **lockout rules**
2. learn the **buy-back rules**
3. protect the **Side Job reward window**
4. then move into the specific collectible lines that still need cleanup

That order matters because not every collectible category behaves the same way.

Some are mostly a routing problem.

- **can I buy it later?**
- **is it tied to a Side Job reward?**
- **does Jack Squat make this harder or impossible to clean up?**
</GuideCallout>

### Baseball Cards

Baseball Cards are not just passive collectibles.

They also connect to:

- the bar minigame
- Prize Tokens
- the Prize D-Spenser
- the X1 D-Mousifier reward path

Use the <Link href="/mouse-pi-for-hire/baseball-card-locations">Baseball Card Locations guide</Link> for card sources, match rules, Prize Token routing, and the late-game cleanup risk.

These are the two largest “reading-style” collectible lines.

They are usually safer than Schematics or Secret Figurines because they overlap more cleanly with buy-back cleanup, but they still punish weak room-search habits.

Use the <Link href="/mouse-pi-for-hire/mouseberg-herald-comic-locations">Mouseberg Herald &amp; Comic Locations guide</Link> for search habits, trophy anchors, and the difference between Herald misses and Comic misses.

These are some of the most stressful collectible lines because they should **not** be treated as normal shop-cleanup content.

Use the <Link href="/mouse-pi-for-hire/weapon-schematics-secret-figurines-guide">Weapon Schematics &amp; Secret Figurines guide</Link> for the high-risk live-pickup logic, the schematic slack in the upgrade route, and how to track Secret Figurines correctly.

### Clues

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the safest page to read first for 100% completion?

The safest first page is the **Missables Guide**, because the biggest risks in MOUSE: P.I. For Hire come from progression rules, not just hidden-item placement.

### Can you do all collectible cleanup after the story?

No. You should not assume a clean endgame cleanup route because the game does not give you a normal mission replay or chapter-select safety net.

### Which collectible lines are safer to recover later?

Newspapers, Comics, and many standard Baseball Cards are the safer lines because they overlap with the buy-back system.

### Which collectible lines are riskier?

Schematics, Secret Figurines, and some Side Job reward items are much riskier because they should not be treated as normal shop cleanup.

### Why do Baseball Cards get special treatment in this hub?

Because they are not just a collectible line. They also feed the minigame, Prize Tokens, and the Prize D-Spenser / X1 D-Mousifier route.

### Where should I go for Heralds and Comics?

Use the **Mouseberg Herald & Comic Locations guide** if you want room-search habits, trophy anchors, and collectible-specific cleanup advice.

### Where should I go for Schematics and Secret Figurines?

Use the **Weapon Schematics & Secret Figurines guide** if you want the higher-risk live-pickup logic and upgrade-route planning.

### Should Clues have their own page right away?

Not necessarily. Early in the cluster, rules pages and the main collectible lines usually deliver more value than forcing a thin standalone clue page.
