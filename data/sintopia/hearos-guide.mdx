**Hearos** are the progression currency that turns a functional Hell into a scalable one. They unlock the routing, support, and administrative tools that make future runs smoother.

But Hearos are easy to misunderstand.

A lot of players assume Hearos are mostly about “punish more Souls.” That is only part of the story. Hearos are really about **clean outcomes**:

- get Souls below the **50 total Sin** threshold
- stop them from picking up extra Sin before they leave Hell
- spend Hearos on unlocks that make the next Hearos easier to earn

This guide is about those three things. It is not a full building-layout walkthrough. It is a **Hearos earning and spending guide**.

<h2 id="quick-answer">
  <a href="#quick-answer">Quick Answer</a>
</h2>

If you want the short version:

- A Soul becomes **Hearosable** at **50 total Sin or lower**
- Your first Hearos priority should usually be **Souls Dispatcher**
- Your best early support unlocks are usually **Entrance-Fee upgrades**, **Attrition Warfare**, and **Deep Pockets**
- **Saints** reduce Hearos efficiency because they occupy route capacity while producing no Hearos
- **Deviants** reduce Hearos efficiency because they clog broad treatment and slow the whole system
- If Souls are often close to clean enough but not quite there, the problem is usually route length, corruption exposure, or weak final filtering

If your Hell can reliably sort, protect, and release clean Souls, Hearos income becomes much steadier.

<h2 id="why-you-are-not-getting-enough-hearos">
  <a href="#why-you-are-not-getting-enough-hearos">Why You Are Not Getting Enough Hearos</a>
</h2>

Most Hearos problems come from one of five causes.

### 1. Souls are leaving above 50 total Sin
The most obvious problem. The Soul gets reincarnated, but it is still too dirty.

### 2. Souls are walking too far
Souls gain Sin from **Infernal Corruption** while exposed on roads. So a Soul that is almost Hearosable can still fail if it has to walk too far before leaving Hell.

### 3. Your filtering is too loose
If your route has no reliable final check, your Hell starts releasing Souls that feel “basically done” but are still over the threshold.

### 4. Saints are sitting on your best lanes
**Saints** do not produce Hearos while they remain Saints, so they quietly reduce the value of your strongest routes.

### 5. Deviants are slowing normal traffic
Once **Deviants** become common, they lower your broad-treatment efficiency and make normal Souls take longer to clean.

<h2 id="saints-are-a-hearos-tax">
  <a href="#saints-are-a-hearos-tax">Saints Are A Hearos Tax</a>
</h2>

**Saints** hurt your Hearos economy because they consume route capacity while giving you almost nothing back in Hell. They do not generate **Purgadollars** or **Faithcoins** when processed, and they do not give **Hearos** when reincarnated.

That means the real cost of Saints is not just “annoying traffic.” It is lost throughput on the routes that should be cleaning normal Souls instead.

If Saints are becoming a regular bottleneck, **Attrition Warfare** is worth mentioning here because it helps reduce **Holy Patience** faster and makes Saint handling less wasteful.

<h2 id="deviants-hurt-hearos-even-when-you-are-not-farming-them">
  <a href="#deviants-hurt-hearos-even-when-you-are-not-farming-them">Deviants Hurt Hearos Even When You Are Not Farming Them</a>
</h2>

**Deviants** hurt Hearos income indirectly. Once they become common, they stop being normal traffic, force specialist handling, and reduce the efficiency of your generic punishment loop.

That matters because every Deviant clogging the wrong lane is also delaying the normal Souls that could have become clean Hearos outputs sooner.

So from a Hearos perspective, Deviants are mainly a **throughput problem** before they are a specialist-treatment problem.

<h2 id="support-unlocks-vs-support-buildings">
  <a href="#support-unlocks-vs-support-buildings">Support Unlocks Vs Support Buildings</a>
</h2>

This distinction matters for article credibility.

Some things you spend Hearos on are **Liber Administratum unlocks**.  
Some things you place later are still **Hell buildings that cost Purgadollars**.

So for example:

- **Impish Resources** is best understood as a **support unlock-and-build path**, not a first-wave Hearos shopping item
- **Break Room** is a support building upgrade, not a primary Hearos target by itself

If upkeep is what is collapsing your run, those tools are still excellent. They just belong in the **support infrastructure** bucket rather than the “best first Hearos buys” bucket.

<h2 id="campaign-vs-sandbox-hearos-priorities">
  <a href="#campaign-vs-sandbox-hearos-priorities">Campaign Vs Sandbox Hearos Priorities</a>
</h2>

Hearos priorities are not exactly the same in every mode.

### Campaign
Campaign is objective-driven, so Hearos spending should stay practical.

That usually means:
- Dispatcher early
- economy support early
- Saint support only when it becomes a real bottleneck
- specialist expansion based on mission pressure

### Sandbox
Sandbox rewards stronger long-term scaling.

That usually means:
- build a stable Hearos route early
- invest in protection/support sooner
- add Hellpet Show support once the main route is already functioning
- buy for refinement, not just survival

Campaign asks, “What helps this mission?”  
Sandbox asks, “What makes this system better forever?”

<h2 id="faq">
  <a href="#faq">FAQ</a>
</h2>

### What is the Hearos threshold in Sintopia?

A Soul is **Hearosable** when it leaves Hell with **50 total Sin or lower**.

### What is the best first Hearos upgrade in Sintopia?

For most runs, **Souls Dispatcher** is the best first Hearos purchase because it gives you real routing control.

### Why am I not getting more Hearos?

Usually because Souls are still leaving above 50 Sin, or because they are gaining extra Sin on long exposed routes before they leave.

### Are Saints good for Hearos?

No. Saints do not give Hearos while they remain Saints, so they reduce the value of your best route space.

### Is Hellpet Show worth Hearos?

Yes, but usually not before your main Hearos engine is already stable. It is better as a second-wave Faithcoin support unlock than as an emergency first purchase.

### What should I buy after Souls Dispatcher?

Usually **Entrance-fee upgrades** or **Attrition Warfare**, depending on whether your bigger problem is money or Saints.

<h2 id="final-verdict">
  <a href="#final-verdict">Final Verdict</a>
</h2>

The best Hearos strategy in **Sintopia** is not just “clean more Souls.”  
It is:

- sort early
- protect outcomes
- spend Hearos on stability first
- treat Saints as dead weight until managed
- stop Deviants from stealing throughput
- only then expand into broader support systems

That is what makes a Hearos economy actually scale.

Your first Hearos should make the next Hearos easier.  
If an upgrade does not do that, it is probably not the right purchase yet.
