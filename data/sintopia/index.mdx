<h2 id="game-overview">
  <a href="#game-overview">Game Overview</a>
</h2>

Mastering **Sintopia** means understanding that the game runs on three tightly connected layers.

First, the **Overworld** creates your inputs. Your rulers, spells, shrines, corruption pressure, and demon problems all shape the exact quality of the Souls that get sent down to Hell.

Second, your **Hell layout** is the processing engine. This is where you route Souls through punishment lanes, specialist branches, fallback loops, and Hearos checks while controlling **Infernal Corruption** and preventing your whole system from jamming.

Third, the **Hearos** economy is your long-term progression layer. Hearos unlock the support tools, routing control, and quality-of-life upgrades that make both the Overworld and Hell more stable over time.

As your operation grows, you also need answers for high-friction exceptions like **Saints** and **Deviants**. Manage these layers separately, and the whole run becomes much easier to control.

<h2 id="quick-start">
  <a href="#quick-start">Quick Start</a>
</h2>

If you are just starting your descent, use this reading order:

1. **Fix your foundation** — build a Hell that can actually process Souls correctly.
2. **Understand the economy** — learn how Hearos work and what to unlock first.
3. **Control your inputs** — manage the Overworld so you stop feeding bad traffic into Hell.
4. **Solve high-friction traffic** — build a proper answer for Saints once they start wasting route space.

If you only read one guide first, start with the Hell layout guide. Most Sintopia problems become easier once your core routing is stable.

<h2 id="faq">

</h2>

### What is the single best guide for a brand-new player?

Always start with [How To Create The Best Hell Layout In Sintopia](/sintopia/best-hell-layout-guide). If your routing is messy, every other system in the game becomes harder to fix.

### How do I fix a broken Hell layout in Sintopia?

Go straight to the [Hell Layout Guide](/sintopia/best-hell-layout-guide). That is the best page for fixing bad routing, missed treatment steps, weak Dispatcher placement, and Souls that keep leaking out before they are ready.

### How do I fix not getting enough Hearos in Sintopia?

Read the [Hearos Guide](/sintopia/hearos-guide). It covers the 50-Sin threshold, why Souls keep missing Hearos, which unlocks to buy first, and how bad route length or loose filtering can quietly kill your Hearos income.

### How do I fix Imployees going on strike in Sintopia?

Imployee strikes are usually a symptom of weak routing, unstable upkeep, or scaling too early. Review the [Hell Layout Guide](/sintopia/best-hell-layout-guide) to stabilize your core system, then check the [Hearos Guide](/sintopia/hearos-guide) to make sure your unlock priorities are supporting the economy instead of dragging it down.

### How do I fix Saints clogging my lanes in Sintopia?

Use the [Saints Guide](/sintopia/saints-guide). It explains when to route Saints away immediately, when to break sainthood deliberately, and how Holy Patience, Waiting Lines, Waiting Booths, Attrition Warfare, and Time Dilatator fit together.

### How do I fix bad Souls coming from the surface in Sintopia?

That is usually an Overworld problem, not a Hell problem. Read the [Overworld Guide](/sintopia/overworld-guide) for help with rulers, shrines, corruption, demons, and the surface-side systems that affect Soul quality before processing even begins.

### Where can I find information on late-game topics?

Once you have mastered the four core modules above, the next topics worth expanding are **Deviants and specialist treatment**, **Campaign / Hard Mode optimization**, and **Faithcoins / Hellpet Show** planning. Those are the next logical additions to this hub.

Sintopia becomes much easier once you stop trying to solve everything in one place.
