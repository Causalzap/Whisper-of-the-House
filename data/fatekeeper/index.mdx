<h2 id="first-hour-route">
  First hour route
</h2>

Your safest first hour is simple:

```txt
Explore Haven
→ finish portal training
→ craft at the alchemy table
→ enter Ancient Hallways
````

That order matters because each step gives you something practical. Haven lets you check weapons and the skill tree. Portal training teaches block, kick, stamina, telekinesis, and search mode. The alchemy table gives you healing, mana, poison, or weapon support. Ancient Hallways is where keys, side paths, enemies, Ring of Life, and NPC rescue start to matter.

<h2 id="build-routes">
  Choose your build route
</h2>

The skill tree can feel huge when you first open it. Do not try to solve the whole tree immediately. Pick a simple direction and let your weapon choice, spell choice, and survivability needs guide the next few points.

For most first-time players, **safe melee** and **fire** are the easiest routes. **Alchemy**, **shatter / frost**, and **telekinesis** need more setup. **Wind** and **dagger / crit** are more demanding because they ask for mana support, clean dodging, or tighter timing.

<h2 id="bosses">
  Bosses and hard fights
</h2>

Fatekeeper's hard fights punish the same habits that get you through easier rooms: greedy attacks, late dodges, panic healing, and ignoring adds.

Before a boss or horde fight, check your healing, mana, weapon recovery, stamina, and whether adds need to die before you focus the main target.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Fatekeeper?

Use this route: explore Haven, finish portal training, craft at the alchemy table, then enter Ancient Hallways prepared. Check your weapons and skill tree before spending several points.

### Which Fatekeeper guide should I read first?

If you are still early, read the Beginner Guide first. If you already understand the basics, move to Best Builds, then Best Weapons, Best Spells, and Boss Guide.

### What is the safest beginner build?

Safe melee is the easiest first route for most players. Use enough health, stamina, and recovery to survive mistakes, then add fire or alchemy once your basics feel stable.

### Should I use Blade of the Sentinel or Axe of the Sentinel?

Blade of the Sentinel is the stronger listed starting weapon with 30 slash damage. Use Axe of the Sentinel if the one-handed feel is easier while learning.

### Is Burning Axe good?

Yes, if you want a fire / melee hybrid route. Burning Axe is a rare one-handed axe with 10 slash damage and 15 fire damage, so its value comes from fire support rather than pure slash damage.

### Is telekinesis worth using?

Yes. Telekinesis is useful early because it can pull enemies, create openings, control space, and interact with specific objects. It is not just a damage spell.

### Is alchemy important?

Yes. Alchemy gives you potions, vials, poison-style effects, mana support, fire support, Life Leech sustain, and weapon coatings. Prepare before hard rooms instead of waiting until you are already out of healing.

### Is Life Leech a spell route?

Life Leech is currently confirmed through alchemy sustain, especially Guards Vermillion. It is not confirmed as a complete standalone spell-tree route yet.

### Are there hidden paths and locked doors?

Yes. You will see side paths, locked doors, key requirements, chests, shortcuts, and NPC rescue opportunities. Track locked doors and return when you find the matching key or route.

### Is Sacred Roots the official boss name?

Not confirmed. “Sacred Roots” appears in dialogue, but this hub does not treat it as the official boss name. The boss guide calls the fight the corrupted roots fight or final Early Access fight until the official name is clear.

### Which guide should I read if a boss keeps killing me?

Read the Boss Guide. It covers preparation, adds, block timing, stomp, phase two, fire damage, short melee punish windows, and common mistakes.
