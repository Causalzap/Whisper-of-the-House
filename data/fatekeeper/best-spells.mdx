<h2 id="mana">

Mana decides whether a spell route feels good or frustrating. A strong spell route with no mana support can still collapse halfway through a room.

  </figcaption>

**Power** is the broader early magic node: it gives **+5 Mana** and **+3% Elemental Damage**, so it helps both your resource pool and spell scaling. **Greater Mana** is the cleaner mana expansion node: it gives **+15 Mana** without also acting as an elemental damage pick.

Use the two differently:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Take <strong>Power</strong> when you want mana plus elemental damage.</li>
  <li>Take <strong>Greater Mana</strong> when spell cost is the main thing stopping your build from working.</li>
  <li>Do not stack mana nodes blindly if your real problem is damage, stamina, or survivability.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="shatter-frost">
  Shatter / frost: use it as a planned route
</h2>

Shatter and frost are interesting because they can connect magic with weapon choice. This route makes the most sense when you are also thinking about blunt weapons, setup windows, and control.

Use shatter / frost if:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you like freezing or controlling enemies before punishing,</li>
  <li>you want to use clubs, maces, hammers, or other blunt weapons,</li>
  <li>you are comfortable waiting for setup windows,</li>
  <li>and you do not need the fastest beginner damage route.</li>
</ul>

A shatter-style route looks like this:

```txt
survivability
→ stamina
→ frost / ice support
→ blunt weapon comfort
→ shatter payoff
```

Do not force shatter if your weapon and build do not support it. Fire and telekinesis are easier to use early because they solve more immediate problems.

<h2 id="recommended-spell-progression">
  Recommended spell progression
</h2>

For a normal first run, use this progression:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Use telekinesis for pull, control, and object interaction.</li>
  <li>Use fire as your first real damage spell route.</li>
  <li>Take Power when you want mana plus elemental damage.</li>
  <li>Take Greater Mana if you run out of mana too quickly.</li>
  <li>Add fire nodes like Excessive Flames, Ricochet, Flame Spout, or MORE! if fire is carrying fights.</li>
  <li>Use Kutracite if you want short-window fire damage support from alchemy.</li>
  <li>Use Savant or Dark Arts only if telekinesis is part of your normal loop.</li>
  <li>Use wind nodes after you can handle the spell costs.</li>
  <li>Use shatter / frost when your weapon and build support the setup.</li>
  <li>Use Guards Vermillion when you want Life Leech sustain through alchemy.</li>
</ol>

### What is the best spell in Fatekeeper?

Fire is the safest damage spell route for most players. It has clear upgrades like +20% fire spell power, Excessive Flames, Ricochet, Flame Spout, and MORE! +2 Projectiles. Kutracite can also support fire through alchemy.

### Is telekinesis good?

Yes. Telekinesis is one of the best early tools because it can pull enemies, control space, and interact with objects. It is useful even if you are not a full magic build.

### Should I build around telekinesis?

Build around telekinesis only if you use it often. Savant reduces telekinesis spell costs by 50%, and Dark Arts adds arcane damage to lifted enemies, but those nodes are best when telekinesis is part of your normal loop.

### Is Greater Mana worth it?

Yes, if spell costs are stopping your build from working. Greater Mana gives +15 Mana and helps fire, wind, telekinesis, and other spell-heavy routes feel smoother.

### Should I take Power or Greater Mana first?

Take Power if you want both +5 Mana and +3% elemental damage. Take Greater Mana if your main problem is simply running out of mana, because Greater Mana gives +15 Mana.

### Is wind good in Fatekeeper?

Wind can be good, but it is more expensive to support. Storm Surge gives +50% spell power with wind spells but also adds +25% wind spell costs, so you need mana support first.

### Is fire better than wind?

For most beginners and hard fights, fire is easier to recommend because its damage plan is clearer. Wind can work, but it needs more support.

### Does Kutracite help fire builds?

Yes. Kutracite gives +6% Fire Damage for 15 seconds and +5 Fire damage, making it useful alchemy support for fire builds.

### Does Burning Axe work with a fire build?

Yes. Burning Axe is a strong fire / melee hybrid option if you like axe timing and want fire damage to support your weapon route. See the Best Weapons Guide for details.

### Is shatter or frost good?

Shatter and frost are good when you build around them. They make more sense with control, setup windows, and blunt weapon routes than as random side investments.

### Is Life Leech worth using?

Yes, as alchemy sustain. Guards Vermillion gives +5% Life Leech and +10% increased Stance for 15 seconds, so Life Leech can support longer fights. It is not confirmed as a full standalone spell-tree route yet, so use it as support for melee, fire, or alchemy builds.

### What is the difference between Potion, Vial, and Hand Bomb?

Potion results affect your body, Vial results can be applied to your weapon, and Hand Bomb results become throwable bombs. Choose the container based on whether you want a self-buff, a weapon effect, or a thrown alchemy tool.

### What spell should I use for bosses?

Use fire as your main spell damage route if your build supports it. Keep telekinesis for control, bring mana support if casting often, and use alchemy support such as Kutracite or Guards Vermillion when it fits your setup.

### Should I use WIP spell nodes?

Do not build your whole setup around WIP nodes. Treat them as future route hints, and rely on working effects like fire upgrades, Greater Mana, Savant, Dark Arts, Kutracite, and Guards Vermillion for the current version.
