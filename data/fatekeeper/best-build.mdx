</h2>

The best Fatekeeper build for your first run is not the highest damage setup. It is the build that keeps you alive while you learn enemy timing.

For most players, start with **safe melee**. It is the easiest route because it works before you understand every spell node, weapon route, or boss pattern.

<h2 id="safe-melee-build">
  Safe melee build
</h2>

The safest beginner build is a melee-first setup with enough defense and stamina to survive mistakes.

Start with this plan:

```txt
Fortitude / health
→ stamina
→ Wrath / melee damage
→ attack speed
→ comfortable weapon
→ fire, telekinesis, or alchemy as backup
````

This route is best if you are still learning:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>enemy windups,</li>
  <li>how much stamina your attacks cost,</li>
  <li>when to block,</li>
  <li>when to kick,</li>
  <li>and when it is safe to heal.</li>
</ul>

The safe melee route does not mean ignoring magic. It means your build does not collapse if you run out of mana or miss a spell. You can still use telekinesis for control, fire for safer damage, and alchemy for backup.

<h2 id="fire-build">
  Fire / pyromancy build
</h2>

Fire is the easiest magic route to recommend early because it gives you damage without forcing you to stand inside every enemy swing.

Fire also has a clear weapon bridge: **Burning Axe**. If you find it, it fits best as a fire / melee hybrid option instead of a random axe swap.

<GuideTable
caption="Fatekeeper fire, wind, frost, and telekinesis nodes."

/>

A practical fire build looks like this:

```txt
Basic survivability
→ Power or spell power
→ Greater Mana
→ Excessive Flames
→ Ricochet
→ MORE!
→ Burning Axe or alchemy backup
```

The key fire nodes are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Excessive Flames:</strong> burning enemies continuously lose poise.</li>
  <li><strong>Flame Spout:</strong> turns your fire spell into a ray of fire.</li>
  <li><strong>Ricochet:</strong> fire projectiles now ricochet.</li>
  <li><strong>MORE!:</strong> adds +2 projectiles.</li>
</ul>

These effects make fire more than raw damage. Fire can pressure enemies, punish groups, and help when melee windows are too dangerous.

<h2 id="shatter-build">
  Shatter / frost build
</h2>

Shatter / frost is a setup route, not the easiest first build. Use it when you want control before damage and you are willing to pair magic with the right weapon type.

Right now, the clearest frost-facing node in the current notes is **Herald of Winter (WIP)**, which says **Windblast is infused with ice**. That makes it an important route signal, but not something you should treat like a finished full build by itself.

A practical shatter / frost plan looks like this:

```txt
Fortitude
→ stamina
→ blunt weapon comfort
→ frost / ice support
→ Herald of Winter when supported
→ shatter payoff
→ alchemy backup
```

Use this route if:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>you like clubs, maces, hammers, or other blunt weapons,</li>
  <li>you want control before damage,</li>
  <li>you are comfortable setting up punish windows,</li>
  <li>and you do not mind a slower, more deliberate build.</li>
</ul>

If you want the easiest route, use safe melee or fire first. If you want a more technical route with room to grow across updates, shatter / frost is worth watching.

<h2 id="telekinesis-build">
  Telekinesis utility build
</h2>

Telekinesis is useful even without a full build. You can pull enemies, control space, and interact with certain objects. But if you want to turn it into a real route, look for nodes that reduce cost or add damage.

The two important nodes are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Savant:</strong> -50% spell costs with telekinesis spells.</li>
  <li><strong>Dark Arts:</strong> telekinesis also deals arcane damage to lifted enemies.</li>
</ul>

A telekinesis route looks like this:

```txt
Mana support
→ Savant
→ Dark Arts
→ survivability
→ melee or fire backup
```

Do not build around telekinesis damage unless you are already using it often. If you only cast it once in a while, keep it as utility and spend your main points elsewhere.

<h2 id="wind-build">
  Wind build
</h2>

Wind can scale hard, but it is less beginner-safe than fire because some of its stronger effects come with cost pressure.

The important wind nodes are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Lord of the Skies:</strong> +30% Windblast Arc.</li>
  <li><strong>Storm Surge:</strong> +50% Spell Power with Wind Spells, but +25% Spell Costs with Wind Spells.</li>
  <li><strong>Herald of Winter (WIP):</strong> Windblast is infused with ice.</li>
</ul>

Storm Surge is the big warning point. +50% spell power sounds great, but +25% spell cost can make the route feel bad if you did not invest in mana first.

Use wind if you enjoy spell coverage and can support the cost. If you just want safer damage right now, fire is easier to recommend than wind.

A bad build in Fatekeeper is not always about picking a bad node. Sometimes it is about picking a good node before your build can use it.

<GuideTable

<h2 id="boss-build">
  Best build for hard fights
</h2>

For hard fights, use a build that gives you three things:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Survival</strong> so one mistake does not end the attempt.</li>
  <li><strong>Safe damage</strong> so you do not need to stand inside every attack.</li>
  <li><strong>Prepared consumables</strong> so you are not relying on perfect play.</li>
</ol>

The safest hard-fight setup is usually:

```txt
Fortitude or health
+ stamina
+ Wrath or fire damage
+ healing
+ mana support if casting
+ poison or weapon coating if available
```

If you keep dying while dealing almost enough damage, do not add more damage first. Add the survival or stamina that lets you reach the end of the fight.

<h2 id="recommended-progression">
  Recommended early progression
</h2>

For your first six upgrades, use the priority table above. Fix the problem that is actually killing your run first: health, stamina, melee damage, attack speed, mana, spell power, or alchemy.

Once those basics feel stable, continue with:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Add fire nodes like Excessive Flames, Ricochet, or MORE! if fire is your main spell route.</li>
  <li>Add Insight, Horder, Azuremash, or weapon coating support if you craft regularly.</li>
  <li>Test shatter, telekinesis, wind, or dagger once your survival, stamina, and main damage route are stable.</li>
</ol>

This keeps the build flexible without repeating the same early upgrade logic. The table solves your first problem; this progression tells you where to branch after that.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best beginner build in Fatekeeper?

For your first Early Access run, the best beginner build is a safe melee hybrid with Fortitude or health, stamina, Wrath or melee damage, and enough attack speed to avoid getting punished after every swing. Add fire or alchemy support once your basics feel stable. For most players, this is also the easiest route.


### Should I take Fortitude, Wrath, Power, or Insight first?

Take Fortitude if you are dying too fast, Wrath if your main damage is melee, Power if you are building around spells or elemental damage, and Insight if you use alchemy often.

### Is fire good in Fatekeeper?

Yes. Fire is one of the safest early magic routes. Ricochet, MORE!, Excessive Flames, and Flame Spout all give fire a clear damage identity. Burning Axe also fits fire / melee hybrid builds.

### What build is Burning Axe best for?

Burning Axe is best for a fire / melee hybrid build. Use it if you already like axe timing and want fire damage to support your melee route.

### Is wind good?

Wind can be strong, especially with Storm Surge, but it needs mana support because Storm Surge increases wind spell costs. Fire is easier for most beginners.

### Is dagger a good beginner build?

Dagger can be strong, especially with crit chance and pierce damage, but it is not the safest beginner route. Use dagger if you can dodge cleanly and stay close without taking constant hits.

### Is shatter or frost good?

Shatter / frost is worth watching if you like setup damage and blunt weapons. Herald of Winter is the clearest current frost-facing node in these notes, but because it is marked WIP, safe melee or fire is easier for a first run.

### Is telekinesis worth building around?

Telekinesis is always useful as utility. Build around it only if you take nodes like Savant or Dark Arts and use it often in combat.

### Is alchemy worth investing in?

Yes, if you craft and use consumables. Insight, Horder, Azuremash, poison, vials, and weapon coatings can all support real builds. If you never craft, spend points elsewhere first.

### Can I respec in Fatekeeper?

Early respecs are free at the Sanctuary of First Flame. Later respecs cost a rare resource found through exploration. Spend carefully until you know where the sanctuary is in your current build.

### Should I use WIP nodes?

Do not make WIP or inactive nodes the center of your current build. Treat nodes like Herald of Winter or Flying Dagger as route signals for future updates, then build around working effects right now.

### What build should I use for bosses?

Use enough Fortitude or health to survive mistakes, enough stamina to dodge after attacking, a reliable damage plan like Wrath melee or fire, and prepared consumables such as healing, mana support, poison, or weapon coating.
