<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
The best early weapon in **Fatekeeper** is usually **Blade of the Sentinel** if you want stronger listed starting damage: it has **5 attack costs, 5 dash costs, and 30 slash damage**.

**Axe of the Sentinel** has the same **5 attack costs and 5 dash costs**, but lower listed damage at **20 slash damage**, making it a simpler one-handed baseline while you learn combat.

After that, use **Ornate Dagger** for crit and pierce damage, **Burning Axe** for fire / hybrid damage, and **clubs, maces, or hammers** for blunt or shatter-style builds.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

  Best early weapon in Fatekeeper
</h2>

The best early weapon in Fatekeeper is not automatically the rarest weapon you find. It is the weapon that lets you attack, recover, and still avoid the next hit.

For most players, start by comparing **Blade of the Sentinel** and **Axe of the Sentinel**, then use the early weapon table below to decide when to test Skull Club, Ornate Dagger, Burning Axe, or weapon coatings.

<h2 id="blade-vs-axe">
  Blade of the Sentinel vs Axe of the Sentinel
</h2>

Your first real weapon choice is between **Blade of the Sentinel** and **Axe of the Sentinel**. Both use the same listed attack and dash cost, so the decision is about damage, weapon type, rhythm, and comfort.

<GuideTable
  caption="Blade of the Sentinel vs Axe of the Sentinel."
  columns={startingWeaponColumns}
  rows={startingWeaponRows}
/>

Use **Blade of the Sentinel** if you want the stronger opening hit and can handle the two-handed rhythm. Use **Axe of the Sentinel** if you prefer a simpler one-handed weapon while learning spacing, kick, block, and stamina management.

Do not overcommit points before testing both. Fatekeeper punishes bad timing, so if one weapon feels safer in your hands, use that while you learn.

<h2 id="weapon-stats">
  Weapon stats that actually matter
</h2>

Do not judge weapons only by damage. In Fatekeeper, a weapon can look stronger on paper but feel worse if the cost or recovery gets you hit.

The most important beginner stat is not crit chance or fire damage. It is whether you can swing, recover, and still react.

If a weapon makes you spend all your stamina and then stand still, it is probably not your best beginner weapon yet.

<h2 id="blunt-weapons">
  Club, mace, and hammer: blunt weapon routes
</h2>

Blunt weapons are slower and more deliberate, but they matter if you want a heavier build or a shatter-style route.

The early **Skull Club** is your first reason to test this style.

Avoid blunt weapons if you are still panic-dodging or missing every timing window. Heavy weapons feel much better once your spacing improves.

<h2 id="burning-axe">
  Burning Axe: best early fire weapon
</h2>

The **Burning Axe** is one of the most interesting early weapon pickups because it combines axe pressure with fire damage. Its known stats are **5 attack costs**, **5 dash costs**, **10 slash damage**, and **15 fire damage**.

Use Burning Axe if you already like axe timing, you are investing in Power or elemental damage, or you want a hybrid melee / fire route. It is not a pure slash upgrade over Blade of the Sentinel; its value comes from the added fire damage.

Do not switch to Burning Axe blindly if you depend on attack speed. If the weapon makes you feel too slow, keep it for a fire route later instead of forcing it immediately.

<h2 id="weapon-coating">
  Weapon coatings, poison, and alchemy support
</h2>

Weapon coatings are easy to overlook, but they are one of the best ways to make your current weapon stronger without changing your whole build.

If you craft before fights, alchemy can add poison effects, vial-based bonuses, mana or healing support, and extra weapon pressure before longer fights.

Weapon coating is best if you are using Insight or alchemy support, have spare ingredients, are preparing for a boss or horde room, or your weapon feels good but needs more damage over time.

<h2 id="weapons-for-bosses">
  Best weapons for bosses and hard fights
</h2>

For bosses, your weapon needs to be safe more than flashy.

A boss weapon should let you attack once or twice, recover before the next hit, keep enough stamina to dodge or block, and back away before phase pressure starts.

Do not use a weapon that only works when the boss stands still. Hard fights punish long strings and panic healing.

If your weapon damage feels low, add support instead of forcing greed. For the full weapon choice breakdown, use the table above.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best early weapon in Fatekeeper?

Blade of the Sentinel is the stronger listed starting weapon with 30 slash damage. Use Axe of the Sentinel if the one-handed feel is easier while learning.

### Is Ornate Dagger good?

Yes, if you want a dagger / crit route and can dodge cleanly. Its crit chance and pierce damage make it the clearest early dagger option, but it is not the safest weapon if you are still trading hits.

### Is Burning Axe good?

Yes, if you want a fire or hybrid melee route. Burning Axe is a rare axe with fire damage, making it useful for players who already like axe timing or elemental pressure.

### Are clubs good in Fatekeeper?

Clubs are useful if you like blunt weapon timing or want to test shatter-style routes. They are less beginner-friendly if you are still struggling with slow attacks.

### Do maces and hammers matter?

Yes. Maces and hammers belong with blunt weapon routes and are worth watching for shatter or heavy melee builds, even if your first tested blunt weapon is a club.

### Do weapon coatings matter?

Yes. Weapon coatings let alchemy support melee builds through poison, vials, or other effects. They are especially useful before bosses or longer fights.

### What weapon should I use for bosses?

Use a weapon that lets you attack once or twice and still recover safely. Pair it with fire, poison, weapon coating, healing, or mana support instead of trying to win with greedy melee strings.

### Should I always use rare weapons?

No. A rare weapon is not automatically better if it does not fit your build or timing. Use rare weapons when their damage type, speed, or effect supports your route.

### What weapon should I pair with a fire build?

Burning Axe is the clearest early fire weapon option. You can also pair fire spells with a safe melee weapon if the axe timing does not feel comfortable.
