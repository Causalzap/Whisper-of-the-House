To make money early in **Starminer**, mine a useful load, return to **Reclamation 9**, open **Trade**, choose **Sell**, select the resource amount, confirm the trade, and wait for cargo transfer to finish.

Do not sell everything. Keep enough **iron**, **silicates**, **cobalt** and research materials for repairs, blueprints and Research Lab preparation. Sell surplus or high-value resources when you need credits for power, storage, licenses or safer expansion.

<h2 id="money-loop">
  The early money loop
</h2>

The safest early money loop in Starminer is not complicated. Mine a useful load, return safely, sell only the surplus, and spend credits on the bottleneck that makes the next trip safer.

The mistake is trying to mine everything. Your ship has mass, storage limits, power limits and travel time. A clean short trip is better than a huge overloaded trip that leaves you drifting, low on power or unable to stop near the station.

<h2 id="how-to-sell">
  How to sell resources at Reclamation 9
</h2>

Return to Reclamation 9 with a useful load, get close enough for cargo transfer, then open the trade screen. Use the **Sell** tab, choose the resource, set the amount and confirm the trade.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/starminer/starminer-reclamation-9-close-trade-range.webp"
    alt="Starminer ship positioned close to Reclamation 9 before selling resources."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Get close before selling. Shorter cargo movement makes the early trade loop more reliable and less confusing.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

If the trade does not seem to move anything, check the simple things first:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Are you close enough?</li>
  <li>Are you trading with the right station or merchant?</li>
  <li>Are you on the Sell tab, not Buy?</li>
  <li>Did you choose the correct resource and amount?</li>
  <li>Did you wait for the cargo transfer to finish?</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="wait-for-transfer">
  Wait for the cargo transfer
</h2>

After you confirm a trade, give the game time to move the cargo. Starminer is physical: resources do not always feel like instant menu numbers. Cargo pods and haulers are part of the loop.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/starminer/starminer-cargo-pods-transfer-short-trip.webp"
    alt="Starminer cargo pods transferring resources during a short trade trip."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Do not fly away the moment you hit Confirm Trade. Wait until the cargo movement finishes.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

A good habit is to stay near the trade target until the transfer is visibly complete. If you leave too early, you may think the sale failed when the transfer simply had not finished.

<h2 id="what-to-sell">
  What should you sell first?
</h2>

In the first hours, sell what you can spare, not what looks valuable in isolation.

A simple early rule is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Keep a reserve of iron and silicates.</li>
  <li>Keep cobalt until you know your next module or Research Lab costs.</li>
  <li>Sell high-value extras when you are saving for a specific upgrade.</li>
  <li>Do not sell materials that appear in your current objective.</li>
  <li>Do not fly around overloaded just because you cannot decide what to sell.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="do-not-sell-silicates">
  Do not dump all your silicates
</h2>

Silicates look easy to sell because they are common, but early repair and construction objectives can need them. If you sell all of them, you may immediately have to mine the same material again.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/starminer/starminer-silicates-repair-objective.webp"

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>
</figure>

This is the difference between a money guide and a trap. Credits are useful, but buying back materials you just sold is a bad early economy loop.

<h2 id="spend-credits">
  What should you spend early credits on?
</h2>

Do not spend credits just because the number went up. Spend them on whatever makes the next loop safer or faster.

<GuideTable
caption="Good ways to spend early Starminer credits."
columns={spendColumns}
rows={spendRows}
/>

In most early runs, your best credit targets are power stability, battery capacity, storage, hauler support, useful licenses and missing materials. Avoid spending everything on combat, fleet or flashy late-game systems before your mining and power loops are stable.

<h2 id="missions">
  Can missions make money too?
</h2>

Yes. Mining and selling is the most direct early credit loop, but missions and side quests can also provide credits and XP. They are useful when you are tired of repeating mining trips or when your next bottleneck is rank, not just cash.

<GuideTable
caption="Starminer early money routes: mining, missions and high-value sales."
columns={missionColumns}
rows={missionRows}
/>

Before taking a mission, check travel distance, power, cargo space and whether combat might be involved. A mission that pays credits is not worth it if your ship cannot safely reach the target, stop, fight or return.

<h2 id="sell-price-perk">
  Sell Price Perk is a strong early economy pick
</h2>

When you level up, watch for perks that improve your credit loop. A sell price bonus can be powerful because it improves every future sale instead of only helping one trip.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

This does not mean you should always pick trade perks over everything else. If your ship cannot stay powered, power is more important. If your ship cannot move safely, control and layout matter more. But if credits are the current wall, a sell price perk is one of the cleanest ways to snowball.

<h2 id="buying-supplies">
  Buying supplies is part of the money loop
</h2>

Selling is only half the economy. Sometimes the correct use of money is buying the thing that prevents a run from collapsing: water, materials, power-related parts or missing construction resources.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image

Do not spend your last credits unless the purchase solves an immediate problem. A small credit reserve can save you when you need emergency supplies or one missing material.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I sell resources in Starminer?

Return to Reclamation 9 or a valid trade target, get close enough, open Trade, choose Sell, select the resource and amount, confirm the trade, then wait for the transfer to finish.

### Why is nothing transferring after I sell?

You may be too far away, using the wrong trade target, on the wrong tab, or not waiting long enough for cargo movement. Move closer and confirm the correct Sell order.

### What should I sell first?

Sell surplus or high-value resources you do not need soon. Keep enough iron, silicates and rare materials for repairs, blueprints and Research Lab preparation.

### Should I sell silicates?

Not all of them. Silicates can be needed for repairs and construction, so keep a reserve before selling extras.

### Is Eonite good to sell?

Yes, Eonite is a strong credit resource when you need money. In one shown trade, 20 Eonite sells for 2,400 credits. Make sure it is not needed for your current objective or next build before selling.

### Should I sell Cobalt?

Be careful. Cobalt can be tied to more advanced module costs and Research Lab preparation, so check your next blueprint or research plan before selling it.

### Why does my ship handle worse after mining?

Cargo adds mass. A heavier ship takes longer to move and stop, so return slowly and unload or sell before taking risky maneuvers.

### How do I make credits faster?

Mine deliberately, sell surplus high-value resources, avoid wasting materials you need later, take suitable missions for credits and XP, and consider sell price perks if credits are your current bottleneck.

### Do missions give credits in Starminer?

Yes, missions and side quests can be another credit and XP route. Use them when you need variety or rank progress, but check travel distance, combat risk, power and cargo space before accepting.

### Is the sell price perk worth it?

Yes, if you are credit-blocked. A sell price perk improves future sales, but it should not come before power or survival if your ship is already unstable.

### What should I buy first with credits?

Spend early credits on the bottleneck that blocks your next loop: power, battery capacity, storage, licenses, hauler support, emergency water or missing materials.

### Should I buy water?

Buy water if your crew or ship operation needs it and you cannot safely solve the problem by mining or hauling. Keep enough credits for emergency supplies.

### Should I hoard every resource?

No. Hoarding makes your ship heavy and slows progression. Keep useful reserves, then sell surplus to fund upgrades.
