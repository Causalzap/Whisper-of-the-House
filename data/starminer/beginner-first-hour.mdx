<h2 id="start-mode">
  Start with Campaign, or use the Sandbox checklist
</h2>

If this is your first run, Campaign is the safest start because it teaches the systems in order: energy grid, orientation, dampeners, scanners, weapons, mining, hauling and selling.

If you jumped straight into **Sandbox**, do not treat it like a normal free-build mode. You still need the same survival loop, but the game gives you less guidance. Start by checking your ship vitals, scanning a nearby target, mining a small load, then finding where you can trade or unload.

<GuideTable
caption="If you started in Sandbox instead."
columns={sandboxColumns}
rows={sandboxRows}
/>

The important difference is that Campaign tells you the next step. Sandbox makes you diagnose the next step yourself. If something does not work, treat it as a power, range, angle, storage or transfer problem before assuming it is a bug.

<h2 id="presets-modifiers">
  Pick a preset, then choose 3 modifiers
</h2>

Starminer does have preset-style starts in this Early Access build. The preset list shows **Warmonger, Miner, Builder, Trader, Navigator and Custom**.

The important part is that a preset is not just a label. It changes the perk pool you see before starting, and the screen shows **Selected Modifiers 0/3**. That means you should read the available modifiers before confirming instead of assuming the preset automatically gives every listed perk.

<h2 id="ship-vitals">
  Read ship vitals before building anything big
</h2>

If your first question is “why is nothing working?”, start with the vitals. A weak grid, full storage, low water or rising heat can break the whole plan.

The main early vitals are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Credits</strong> — used for purchases, licenses and recovery supplies.</li>
  <li><strong>XP / Rank</strong> — unlocks perks and progression options.</li>
  <li><strong>Energy generation</strong> — tells you whether the grid is gaining or losing power.</li>
  <li><strong>Battery</strong> — protects you from short spikes.</li>
  <li><strong>Storage</strong> — limits how much you can mine or haul.</li>
  <li><strong>Water</strong> — matters for travel and ship operation.</li>
  <li><strong>Heat</strong> — tells you whether your systems are pushing too hard.</li>
</ul>

If two or more vitals are strained, stop expanding. Fix the bottleneck first.

<h2 id="rank-xp">
  Rank up before planning a serious expansion
</h2>

Early Starminer progression is tied to more than resources. You also need XP, rank and unlocked module options. One early rank screen shows **Ensign → Lieutenant Junior** with XP moving from **750 → 1,500**, plus an increase in max available officers from **3 → 4**.

That means you should not plan a huge station before the early systems are online. First, complete the basic objectives that teach:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Energy grid control.</li>
  <li>Orientation and dampeners.</li>
  <li>Scanner use.</li>
  <li>Mining laser alignment.</li>
  <li>Hauler collection.</li>
  <li>Trade and selling.</li>
</ul>

Once you have a rank-up and a working economy loop, expansion decisions become safer.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I do first in Starminer?

Start by checking energy, battery, storage, water and heat. Then scan a nearby target, mine a small load, wait for haulers, sell surplus and add power before building big modules.

### Should I start in Campaign or Sandbox?

Campaign is safer for a first run because it teaches systems in order. Sandbox is fine, but you need to self-direct: check vitals, scan, mine, sell and stabilize power before expanding.

### How do Starminer presets work?

In this Early Access build, the preset screen shows Warmonger, Miner, Builder, Trader, Navigator and Custom.Each preset changes the modifier pool you choose from. The screen shows Selected Modifiers 0/3, so choose 3 modifiers that help your first-hour plan instead of confirming by preset name alone.

### What is the best beginner preset in Starminer?

For a first run, choose a preset based on the modifier pool, not just the name. Miner or Builder-style starts are usually safer if you are learning mining, power, hauling and selling. Warmonger is more weapon-heavy and is better once you understand combat and power draw.

### What does Warmonger do in Starminer?

Warmonger shows a weapon-heavy perk pool. In the shown build, it includes Offensive perks such as Advanced Kinetic Weapons Training, Tactical Energy Weapon Training, Advanced Weapons Systems Training, Rapid Fire Deployment Training and Energy Weapons Research, plus Streamlined Construction Techniques as an Industry option.

### How many modifiers do I choose?

The preset screen shows Selected Modifiers 0/3, so you choose 3 modifiers before confirming your start.

### How much XP do I need to rank up?

One early rank screen shows Ensign moving toward Lieutenant Junior with XP increasing from 750 to 1,500. Treat rank and XP as part of progression, but check your current build because Early Access values may change.

### Why is my mining laser not working?

Mining may be offline, the laser may face the wrong direction, the target may be out of range, or your power may be low. Rotate the ship, move closer and check the grid.

### Why are my haulers not collecting?

You may have moved away too early, storage may be full, or the target may be awkwardly positioned. Stay near the target and wait for collection to finish.

### How do I sell resources early?

Return to Reclamation 9 or another valid trade target, open the trade screen, choose Sell, select the amount and confirm the trade. Wait for the transfer to finish.

### Should I sell all my early resources?

No. Keep materials needed for repairs, blueprints and Research Lab preparation. Sell surplus or high-value resources when you need credits.

### Why does my ship keep drifting?

Your ship may have dampeners off, low energy, too much cargo mass or the wrong orientation. Turn on dampeners, stop thrusting, hold Space and let power recover.

### What should I build first?

Build what fixes the current bottleneck. If low energy appears, add solar and battery. If storage fills too fast, add storage. If research is your goal, prepare power, heat, colonists and materials first.

### Should I build the Research Lab in the first hour?

Usually no. Build it after mining, selling, power, battery and heat are stable. In one run, the Research Lab cost shows 71 Iron, 36 Silicates, 8 Cobalt, 10 Colonists and 500 Energy.

### What should I spend early credits on?

Spend credits on the bottleneck: power, battery, storage, emergency water, useful licenses or materials. Do not spend credits on flashy upgrades while the grid is unstable.

### What is the biggest beginner mistake?

Overbuilding too early. Extra modules add mass, power draw, heat and material costs. Build a stable loop first, then expand.
