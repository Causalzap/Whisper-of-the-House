<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
If your ship keeps drifting or spinning in **Starminer**, stop thrusting, check your orientation, turn on **linear dampeners** for straight-line drift, turn on **rotational dampeners** for spin, and hold **Space** when you need a hard stop.

Starminer uses Newtonian-style movement, so releasing the keys does not automatically stop the ship. Your first control loop is:

**read orientation → brake early → use dampeners → manage mass → keep enough energy**. 
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="why-controls-feel-hard">
  Why Starminer controls feel hard at first
</h2>

Starminer movement feels strange because your ship behaves like a heavy machine in space, not like a flat arcade cursor. You are flying with six-direction, **6DOF-style movement**: forward/back, left/right, up/down, plus rotation for aiming tools and lining up modules.

That is why new players often think the controls are broken when the real problem is usually one of these:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Your ship is facing a different direction than you think.</li>
  <li>Your ship still has velocity after you release movement keys.</li>
  <li>Linear dampeners are not stopping straight-line drift.</li>
  <li>Rotational dampeners are not stopping spin.</li>
  <li>Your power grid is too weak for the current maneuver.</li>
  <li>Your ship is heavier after mining, salvage or expansion.</li>
</ul>

The fix is not to spam more thrust. Slow down, read the orientation, stabilize the ship, and move with smaller inputs.

<h2 id="newtonian-movement">
  Why doesn't my ship stop when I release the keys?
</h2>

In Starminer, your ship keeps moving because space has no air resistance to slow it down. Releasing the keys only stops adding new thrust. It does not erase the velocity you already built up.

Dampeners are the tools that fight that velocity for you. Linear dampeners counter straight-line drift, while rotational dampeners counter spin. If they are off, underpowered, or fighting a heavy ship, your rig will keep moving longer than you expect.

<h2 id="center-of-mass">
  Why does my ship spin after I add modules?
</h2>

A sudden spin is often a layout problem, not just a control problem. When you attach a heavy module on one side of the ship, the center of mass can shift. If your thrusters no longer push cleanly through that center of mass, the ship can start rotating instead of moving straight.

That is the simple version of the “death spin” problem: uneven mass plus off-center thrust creates torque.

<h2 id="linear-dampeners">
  Linear dampeners stop drift
</h2>

Linear dampeners counter straight-line movement: forward, backward, sideways, up or down. Leave them on while learning, especially near stations, asteroids, debris fields, unmanned vessels and docking targets.

Once you understand the controls, you can turn them off to coast across open space. Early on, turning them off near obstacles is one of the fastest ways to ram something.

<h2 id="rotational-dampeners">
  Rotational dampeners stop spinning
</h2>

Rotational dampeners counter angular movement. If your ship keeps rolling, pitching or yawing after you rotate, this is the system that helps calm it down.

Use them whenever you need stable tool alignment, especially when lining up scanners, weapons, mining lasers, docking angles or merge targets.

<h2 id="space-brake">
  Hold Space when you need to stop
</h2>

If the ship is moving too fast or drifting toward danger, use Space as your emergency braking habit. Do not just tap it once and assume the rig is safe. Hold it until velocity actually drops.

  <Image

  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">

  </figcaption>

A good approach rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Start slowing down earlier than you think.</li>
  <li>Use small taps near objects.</li>
  <li>Keep dampeners on while learning.</li>
  <li>Do not point full thrust at the station and hope the ship stops in time.</li>
</ul>

Starminer punishes panic movement. If you are unsure, stop first, rotate second, then move.

<h2 id="shift-rotation">
  Shift controls rotate the ship
</h2>

WASD and Q/E move your ship. Holding Shift changes those inputs into rotation. This is powerful, but it is also how many new players accidentally spin too far.

Use Shift inputs in short taps. Long presses can rotate you past the angle you need, especially if rotational dampeners are off, low energy is active, or your ship has an uneven mass layout.

A clean aiming habit looks like this:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Stop or slow the ship.</li>
  <li>Turn on rotational dampeners.</li>
  <li>Use small Shift inputs.</li>
  <li>Wait for the rotation to settle.</li>
  <li>Only then move or activate the tool.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I stop my ship from drifting in Starminer?

Turn on linear dampeners, stop thrusting, and hold Space if you need stronger braking. If Low Energy is showing, let the grid recover before trying more maneuvers.

### How do I stop my ship from spinning?

Turn on rotational dampeners, stop giving rotation input, and use smaller Shift taps. If the spin started after adding modules, check whether the new layout shifted your center of mass.

### Why doesn't my ship stop when I release the keys?

Starminer uses Newtonian-style movement. Releasing the keys stops adding thrust, but your ship keeps its existing velocity until dampeners, braking or opposite thrust slow it down.

### What does 6DOF movement mean in Starminer?

6DOF means six degrees of freedom. In Starminer, you can move forward/back, left/right, up/down, and rotate the ship to aim tools, weapons and docking angles.

### What do linear dampeners do?

Linear dampeners counter straight-line movement. They help stop forward, backward, sideways, upward and downward drift after you release thrust.

### What do rotational dampeners do?

Rotational dampeners counter spin and angular movement. They help the ship stop rotating after you turn.

### What does Space do?

Space helps brake the ship by using dampening systems together. Hold it until your velocity drops instead of tapping it once.

### Why do WASD controls feel reversed?

Your ship may be facing a different direction than you think. Use the orientation tool to check forward direction before thrusting.

### How do I rotate the ship?

Use Shift with the movement keys to rotate instead of only moving in a straight line. Use short taps so you do not over-rotate.

### Why does my ship handle worse after mining?

Cargo adds mass. A heavier ship takes longer to slow down and can strain power while maneuvering.

### Why does low energy affect movement?

Dampeners and other active systems need power. If your grid is strained, the ship may not stop, rotate or respond as cleanly.

### Should I turn off dampeners to travel faster?

Only when you understand how to brake safely. While learning, keep dampeners on near stations, asteroids, debris and docking targets.

### Why do my weapons or mining lasers not point at the target?

Many modules are directional. Rotate the ship until the correct side has line of sight.

### How do I avoid crashing into stations?

Slow down early, keep linear dampeners on, cancel risky navigation near the target, and finish the final approach manually with small inputs.
