  Campaign, Sandbox and Survival modes
</h2>

Starminer currently has three main ways to play: Campaign, Sandbox and Survival. Campaign is the easiest place to learn because it introduces systems through objectives and missions. Sandbox gives more freedom but less direction. Survival is better once you understand movement, power, heat and combat.

<h2 id="worth-playing">
  Is Starminer worth playing in Early Access?
</h2>

Starminer is worth considering in Early Access if you want a technical space mining and ship-building sim where movement, mass, power, heat and logistics all matter. It already has a full tutorial plus Campaign, Sandbox and Survival-style play, so there is enough structure to learn the systems and enough freedom to experiment.

The tradeoff is that it is still Early Access. Balance values, UI labels, module costs, mission flow and heat behavior may change. The development team is small, and public Early Access messaging has framed the EA period as roughly a year, so expect iteration rather than a finished 1.0 experience.

If you want polished frictionless mining, wait. If you enjoy learning deep systems, solving power and heat problems, and turning a fragile rig into a working space operation, Starminer already has the kind of hard mechanics that make good guides necessary.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is the best Starminer guide to read first?

Start with the Beginner First Hour Guide. It gives you the safest first route through presets, modifiers, Campaign vs Sandbox, ship vitals, mining, selling and early building decisions.

### Should I start Starminer in Campaign or Sandbox?

Campaign is easier for a first run because it teaches systems through objectives. Sandbox is fine if you want freedom, but you must self-direct the same survival loop: check vitals, scan, mine, sell, add power and expand slowly.

### Why is Starminer hard at the beginning?

Starminer combines Newtonian movement, ship mass, directional tools, energy, battery, heat, cargo transfer, research costs and colonists. If one system fails, several other systems can feel broken too.

### What should I do first in Starminer?

Pick a preset and 3 modifiers, check ship vitals, bring the energy grid online, learn dampeners, scan before mining, mine a small load, wait for haulers, sell surplus and add power before overbuilding.

### How do I make money in Starminer?

Mine useful resources, sell surplus at Reclamation 9, keep repair and research materials, use suitable missions for credits and XP, and spend credits on the bottleneck that blocks your next loop.

### What should I research first in Starminer?

Research by bottleneck. For many early runs, power, batteries, heat control, mining efficiency, mobility or colonist support matter more than fleet, hangar or nuclear tech.

### Is G2 Solar worth it early?

Yes, if power is blocking progress and you can afford it. In one shown Early Access build, Energy Solar costs 5,000 credits and requires 0 RP, making it a strong credit-based power target.

### Is Starminer worth it in Early Access?

It depends on what you want. Starminer is a good fit if you enjoy complex space engineering, physics-heavy movement and logistics problems. It may be frustrating if you want a finished, polished, low-friction mining game.
