  How research works in Starminer
</h2>

Research in Starminer is not just a list of tech boxes. It is a chain of money, rank, materials, Research Points, power, heat and colonists. If one layer is missing, the unlock will not help.

This is why new players often get stuck. You may have the credits but not the materials. You may have the tech visible but not the rank. You may have the Research Lab built but not the power to run it. You may have enough energy but not enough heat control or colonists.

A good research route solves the current bottleneck instead of chasing the highest-tier icon on the screen.

<h2 id="do-before-research-lab">
  Do this before building the Research Lab
</h2>

Before you build the Research Lab, make sure the station can survive it.

You should already have:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>A basic mining route you can repeat safely.</li>
  <li>A selling route that gives you credits without desperate trips.</li>
  <li>Enough solar to recover after normal activity.</li>
  <li>Enough battery capacity to survive spikes.</li>
  <li>Thermal Dumper or Radiator support if heat is already rising.</li>
  <li>Enough materials, colonists and energy for the lab and the first follow-up upgrade.</li>
</ul>

If your ship still flashes Low Energy during mining or docking, do not rush the lab yet. Fix the grid first.

<h2 id="research-lab">
  The Research Lab can crash a weak grid
</h2>

The Research Lab starts your Research Point loop, but it is also one of the first modules that really tests your station. If the grid is weak, the lab can pull the whole setup into Low Energy. If thermal support is weak, heat becomes the next problem.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/starminer/starminer-research-lab-power-drain.webp"

    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Do not build the Research Lab just because it is available. Build it when the grid can run it.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Treat the lab like a stress test:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>If energy drops immediately, you built it too early or need more generation.</li>
  <li>If batteries never recover, you need more storage or less drain.</li>
  <li>If heat rises while the lab runs, add thermal support.</li>
  <li>If RP does not increase, check staffing, colonists, power and lab uptime.</li>
  <li>If you cannot afford the next unlock after building it, your economy is still too weak.</li>
</ul>

For power recovery, use the dedicated power guide.

<h2 id="colonists-staffing">
  Colonists and staffing can block research
</h2>

The Research Lab is not only a materials problem. It can also be a colonist and staffing problem. In this run, the lab cost includes **10 Colonists**, so you should not spend all your attention on iron, silicates and cobalt while ignoring crew support.

<GuideTable
caption="Research Lab colonist and staffing checks."
columns={staffingColumns}
rows={staffingRows}
/>

If the lab is built but not producing Research Points, do not only add more power. Check whether the lab is staffed, whether you have enough colonists, and whether your station can support those people while the lab is running.

<h2 id="g2-solar">
  G2 Solar / Energy Solar is the safest early power target
</h2>

If your next bottleneck is power, look at G2 Solar / Energy Solar before chasing more complicated systems. The shown Energy Solar unlock requires **level 1**, **0 RP**, and a **5,000-credit license cost**.

That matters because it gives you a way to improve power through money instead of waiting for Research Points. If your mining and selling loop is working, credits may be easier to solve than lab output.

Use this order:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Mine and sell enough to build a credit reserve.</li>
  <li>Buy the solar license when power is the bottleneck.</li>
  <li>Add battery capacity so spikes do not empty the grid.</li>
  <li>Only then run high-drain systems for long periods.</li>
</ol>

Do not buy it just to spend money. Buy it because low energy is slowing down mining, movement, construction or research.

<h2 id="battery">
  Battery upgrades protect the grid from spikes
</h2>

Solar generation and battery capacity solve different problems. Solar helps your grid recover. Batteries help the station survive sudden demand.

Battery capacity becomes important when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Mining drains the grid in short bursts.</li>
  <li>Dampeners struggle during heavy movement.</li>
  <li>Construction creates temporary dips.</li>
  <li>Research Lab power draw keeps the station unstable.</li>
  <li>Weapons and combat stack on top of normal systems.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="heat-control">
  Add heat control before long research sessions
</h2>

Power is only the first half of research prep. Heat is the second half.

A station can have enough energy and still overheat if research, mining, industry or weapons run too long.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/starminer/starminer-thermal-dumper-radiator.webp"
    alt="Starminer build menu explaining Thermal Dumper heat storage and venting."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Thermal Dumpers help with heat spikes. Radiators help with passive heat control over time.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Use this simple rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li><strong>Thermal Dumper</strong> for temporary heat spikes.</li>
  <li><strong>Thermal Radiator</strong> for heat that stays high over time.</li>
  <li><strong>Both</strong> before long research, industry or combat sessions.</li>
</ul>

Do not assume power upgrades alone solve research. If the lab overheats, you need thermal support, not just more credits.

<h2 id="by-bottleneck">
  Research by bottleneck, not by tier
</h2>

The best early unlock is the one that fixes the thing actually stopping you.

<GuideTable
caption="How to choose Starminer research by bottleneck."
columns={bottleneckColumns}
rows={bottleneckRows}
/>

This is the key mindset shift. You do not need the most exciting tech. You need the unlock that keeps the next loop running.

If you are low on power, research or buy power. If you are traveling too far, consider mobility. If mining is slow but your grid is fine, mining upgrades make sense. If everything is overheating, fix heat before adding more load.

<h2 id="nuclear">
  Do not rush nuclear power
</h2>

T2 Nuclear Reactor looks tempting because it sounds like the big answer to power problems. Early on, it is usually not the safe answer.

The reason is not that nuclear is bad. The reason is that nuclear asks more from your station. You need fuel, supporting modules and heat control. If you are already struggling with solar, batteries and thermal systems, nuclear can create a bigger mess.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/starminer/starminer-nuclear-reactor-requires-fuel.webp"

    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Nuclear power is not a simple early fix. It can involve fuel production and extra heat support, so delay it until the station is ready.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Use nuclear later when:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>You understand your fuel route.</li>
  <li>You can handle the heat.</li>
  <li>Your economy can absorb the cost.</li>
  <li>You have enough batteries to buffer the grid.</li>
  <li>You are not relying on nuclear to rescue a failing early setup.</li>
</ul>

Until then, solar and batteries are cleaner.

<h2 id="fleet-hangars">
  Delay fleet and hangar upgrades until the station is stable
</h2>

Fleet upgrades, hangars and ship support are exciting, but they are not free. They add resource cost, mass, power draw, logistics and sometimes combat pressure.

If your station is still failing at:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Power recovery,</li>
  <li>Heat control,</li>
  <li>Cargo storage,</li>
  <li>Mining income,</li>
  <li>Basic movement,</li>
  <li>Crew and staffing support,</li>
</ul>

then fleet tech is probably too early.

Build the machine that funds the fleet before you build the fleet.

The biggest trap is thinking research is automatically progress. Research is only progress if the unlock solves a real problem and your station can actually use it.

A good early research path is not flashy. It is stable: credits coming in, solar and batteries online, heat under control, colonists available, the Research Lab running cleanly, and first RP spent on the bottleneck that actually slows you down.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What should I research first in Starminer?

Research whatever fixes your current bottleneck. For many early runs, that means power, batteries, thermal support, mining efficiency, mobility or colonist support before fleet and nuclear tech.

### Should I build the Research Lab early?

Usually no. Build the Research Lab after your mining, selling, power, battery, heat and colonist support are stable enough to support it.

### How do I get Research Points?

Research Points come from running the Research Lab after it is built, powered and staffed. If the lab drains the grid or lacks staffing, fix those problems before trying to rush more RP.

### How fast does the Research Lab produce Research Points?

A fixed RP-per-minute rate is not clearly shown in this build. Treat RP output as an uptime problem: the lab needs stable power, heat control and staffing to keep producing.

### Why is my Research Lab not producing RP?

Check power, heat, staffing, colonists and lab uptime. If any of those are missing, the lab may be built but not producing useful Research Points.

### Why is my Research Lab causing Low Energy?

The Research Lab adds continuous power demand. If your solar and batteries are weak, it can pull the station into Low Energy.

### Why is my Research Lab heating up?

The lab can add heat pressure while it runs. Add Thermal Dumpers for spikes and Radiators for ongoing heat control.

### What are Colonists used for in Starminer research?

Colonists can be part of Research Lab requirements and staffing. In one run, the Research Lab cost includes 10 Colonists, so research can be blocked by people as well as materials, power and heat.

### How do I staff the Research Lab?

Check whether the lab has enough colonists, crew support and stable station operation. If the lab is powered but not producing RP, staffing is one of the first things to inspect.

### Why does the Research Lab cost look different?

Costs may vary by version, perks or balance changes. In this run, the build menu labels it as T2 Research Lab and shows 71 Iron, 36 Silicates, 8 Cobalt, 10 Colonists and 500 Energy.

### Is G2 Solar worth it early?

Yes, if power is the bottleneck and you can afford the license. The shown Energy Solar unlock costs 5,000 credits and requires 0 RP.

### Should I get T2 Battery early?

Get battery capacity early if your grid dips during mining, movement, construction or research. Batteries protect you from power spikes.

### Should I rush nuclear power?

Usually no. Nuclear can require fuel, support modules and heat management. It is safer after solar, batteries, economy and thermal systems are stable.

### Should I research fleet or hangar tech early?

Usually not first. Fleet and hangar systems are powerful, but they add cost, logistics, power and heat pressure. Build the economy and grid first.

### Is a sell price perk good?

Yes, if credits are your bottleneck and you are selling often. If the ship is failing because of power or heat, fix that first.

### Is research speed worth it?

Only after your Research Lab can run safely. Faster research does not help if the lab keeps crashing your grid.

### Should I sell cobalt before research?

Be careful. Cobalt and other rarer materials can appear in advanced module or lab costs, so check your next build before selling it.
