<h2 id="basic-loop">
  The basic mining loop
</h2>

Mining in Starminer is not just clicking an asteroid. Your ship position matters, your scanner angle matters, your mining laser range matters, and your haulers need time to bring the resources home.

If you try to skip the scan and just mine everything nearby, you can easily fill storage with the wrong material, overload your ship, or waste energy while chasing a resource you did not need.

<h2 id="scanner">
  Scanner not working? Check angle, distance and power
</h2>

Turn on your scanner when you need information, then point your ship toward the target. In the early game, you will often see targets marked with distance and unknown composition. That is your cue to scan first.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/starminer/starminer-scanner-composition-unknown.webp"
    alt="Starminer asteroid target showing Composition Unknown before scanning."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    If the target still says Composition Unknown, treat it as unconfirmed. Do not waste a long mining trip until you know what you are looking at.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

If the scanner does not start, check:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Is the scanner turned on?</li>
  <li>Is your ship facing the target?</li>
  <li>Are you close enough?</li>
  <li>Is the target inside the scanner angle?</li>
  <li>Is your power already too low?</li>
</ul>

The exact scanner range was not shown clearly enough in the captured panel, so do not guess a fixed number yet. For now, play it by the target distance and scanner behavior. If Composition Unknown does not resolve, move closer, slow down, and point the scanner more directly at the target.

When the scan is done, turn scanners off if your power grid is weak. Early Starminer rewards players who toggle systems deliberately instead of leaving every tool online.

</h2>

After scanning, enable mining and line up the ship so the mining laser has a clean path to the target.

Do not panic if the laser does not fire instantly. Starminer is physical. You may need to rotate, move closer, stop the ship, or recover power so the tool can stay in range.

<h2 id="asteroid-resources">
  What resources come from different asteroid types?
</h2>

Do not assume every asteroid is worth the same trip. Some resources are common building materials, while others are more valuable or tied to later systems.

In early runs, treat the scanner as your source of truth. Debris and wreckage can often be useful for repair or build materials, while asteroid classes may point toward different raw resources. Competitor and player notes have mentioned cobalt from debris-style targets and thorium from C-class asteroid routes, but you should still confirm composition in your own build before committing cargo space.

The practical rule is simple: scan first, mine what solves the next bottleneck, and do not fill storage with random raw material just because the asteroid was close.

<h2 id="raw-vs-processed">
  Raw resources are not always ready to use
</h2>

Early on, pay attention to the difference between scrap-style targets and asteroids. Debris can give processed or immediately useful materials tied to objectives, while asteroids may provide raw resources that need a larger production loop later.

Resources are not all equal in the first hours. Some are tempting to sell, but you may need them for repairs, modules or research preparation.

The safest habit is to sell deliberately. If a material appears in your current objective, repair cost or module blueprint, keep a reserve before turning the rest into credits.

<h2 id="power">
  Low energy while mining? Stop stacking systems
</h2>

Mining is one of the first places where weak power shows up. You may be moving, running dampeners, keeping scanners online, powering haulers, and firing mining lasers at the same time.

  <Image

If Low Energy appears:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Stop unnecessary movement.</li>
  <li>Turn off scanners if the scan is done.</li>
  <li>Turn off weapons if no threat is nearby.</li>
  <li>Wait for battery recovery.</li>
  <li>Return to sell or unload if cargo is making the ship too heavy.</li>
</ul>

Do not fix low energy by adding more mining lasers. Fix the grid first.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I mine in Starminer?

Turn on scanners, face the target, move close enough, scan it, enable mining, line up your mining laser, then wait for haulers to collect the resources.

### Why does the asteroid say Composition Unknown?

It has not been scanned properly yet. Turn on scanners, point your ship toward the asteroid, move closer if needed, and wait for the scan to complete.

### What is the scanner range in Starminer?

A fixed scanner range value was not clearly captured in this build. If the target remains Composition Unknown, move closer, face the scanner toward the target, and check whether your current scanner panel shows a range value.

### Why is my scanner not working?

The scanner may be off, pointed the wrong way, too far from the target, or your ship may be low on power. Check angle and distance first.

### Why is my mining laser not firing?

Mining may be offline, the laser may not face the target, the target may be out of range, or your grid may not have enough energy.

### Does the mining laser need line of sight?

Treat it that way. The mining laser is mounted on your ship, so angle and module position matter. Rotate the ship until the laser has a clean path.

### What does the T1 Mining Laser cost to run?

In the example shown here, the T1 Mining Laser uses 20 MW, has 10t mass, and shows 182.5m range with bonuses active. Your exact numbers may change with perks or updates.

### Why are my haulers not collecting resources?

They may still be traveling, storage may be full, the target may be awkwardly positioned, or you may have moved away too quickly. Stay close until the transfer finishes.

### Should I mine debris or asteroids first?

Mine the debris or target the Campaign points you toward first. Use asteroids when you know which resource you need and have scanned the composition.

### Where do I find cobalt, thorium or Eonite?

Use the scanner before committing to a trip. Debris-style targets can be useful for rarer build materials, while some asteroid classes may point toward resources like thorium. Confirm composition in your own build before filling cargo.

### Should I sell everything I mine?

No. Sell what you can spare, but keep materials needed for repairs, construction, blueprints and early progression.

### Why does my ship handle worse after mining?

Cargo adds mass. A heavier rig drifts more, slows down worse, and can strain power while maneuvering. Sell or unload before making risky moves.

### Why do I get Low Energy while mining?

You may be moving, scanning, running dampeners, powering haulers and firing lasers at the same time. Turn off unused systems and let the battery recover.

### What should I do after my first mining trip?

Return to Reclamation 9, sell only spare resources, check your next objective, then decide whether you need power, storage, repairs, research preparation or more mining.
