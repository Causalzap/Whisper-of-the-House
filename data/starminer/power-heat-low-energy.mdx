<h2 id="why-low-energy-happens">
  Why low energy happens
</h2>

Low energy usually means you are asking the ship to do too much at once. In the first hours, your grid is small, your batteries are limited, and every active system competes for power.

The usual causes are:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Moving a heavy ship while dampeners are working.</li>
  <li>Leaving scanners online after the scan is done.</li>
  <li>Mining while constantly adjusting position.</li>
  <li>Running weapons during or after combat.</li>
  <li>Building modules while the battery is already low.</li>
  <li>Adding the Research Lab before your power grid is ready.</li>
</ul>

When Low Energy appears, do not add more tools immediately. Stabilize first, then expand.

<h2 id="heat-basics">
  Heat is the second bottleneck
</h2>

Once your power is stable, heat becomes the next limit. You can have enough energy and still run into heat problems if too many systems are active for too long.

Heat also matters because high heat signature can attract alien attention. That means heat management is not only about keeping modules online. It can also affect how much danger your station invites while mining, researching, fighting or running heavy industry.

caption="Starminer heat problems and fixes."

Heat usually rises when you scale:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>More mining.</li>
  <li>More weapons.</li>
  <li>More industry.</li>
  <li>More research.</li>
  <li>More continuous operation without downtime.</li>
</ul>

Do not solve heat by pretending it is not there. Add the right thermal modules before you push the station harder.

<h2 id="thermal-dumper-radiator">
  Thermal Dumper vs Thermal Radiator
</h2>

Thermal Dumpers and Thermal Radiators do different jobs.

A **Thermal Dumper** acts like a heat buffer. It absorbs excess heat, stores it, then vents when filled.

A **Thermal Radiator** is for passive heat dumping over time.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/starminer/starminer-thermal-dumper-radiator.webp"
    alt="Starminer build menu showing Thermal Dumper and Thermal Radiator options."
    width={400}
    height={300}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    Dumpers help absorb heat spikes. Radiators help with ongoing passive heat control.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

The simple rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Use dumpers if heat spikes during short bursts.</li>
  <li>Use radiators if heat stays high over time.</li>
  <li>Use both if research, industry or combat runs for long periods.</li>
</ul>

Do not add only one dumper and assume the heat problem is solved forever.

</h2>

The biggest trap is trying to solve every slow system by adding another module. If mining is slow, you add lasers. If haulers are slow, you add hangars. If research is slow, you run the lab harder. That only works if the grid, batteries and heat systems can support the extra load.

<h2 id="recovery-route">
  Recovery route when the grid is already collapsing
</h2>

If you are already stuck in Low Energy, use this recovery route:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Stop moving unless you are about to hit something.</li>
  <li>Turn off scanners, mining and weapons if they are not needed.</li>
  <li>Wait for the battery to recover.</li>
  <li>Sell or unload heavy cargo if the ship is hard to control.</li>
  <li>Add cheap solar panels if you can.</li>
  <li>Add energy battery capacity if spikes keep draining the grid.</li>
  <li>Add thermal support if heat is climbing.</li>
  <li>Delay Research Lab, extra lasers, nuclear power and combat expansion until stable.</li>
</ol>

Do not keep forcing the same failing action. If the ship says Low Energy five times in a row, the current plan is too heavy for the grid.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do I fix Low Energy in Starminer?

Stop moving, turn off unused scanners, mining lasers and weapons, wait for battery recovery, then add more solar and battery capacity before expanding again.

### Why does Low Energy keep happening?

You are probably running too many active systems at once. Movement, dampeners, scanners, mining, weapons, construction and research can all pull from the same weak early grid.

### Should I turn off scanners after scanning?

Yes, if power is tight. Scan the target, confirm what you need, then turn scanners off until you need them again.

### Do mining lasers use power?

Yes. Mining lasers add power demand, and mining often happens while you are also moving, using dampeners and running haulers.

### Why does my ship control worse during Low Energy?

Dampeners and other active systems need power. If the grid is strained, stopping, rotating and recovering from bad movement can feel worse.

### What should I build first for power?

Start with more solar panels and energy battery capacity. They are the safest early way to stabilize the grid before bigger modules.

### Is G2 Solar worth it early?

Yes, if power is your bottleneck and you can afford the license. In this build, the shown Energy Solar unlock costs 5,000 credits and requires 0 RP.

### What does a Thermal Dumper do?

A Thermal Dumper absorbs excess heat, stores it, and vents it when filled. Treat it as a heat buffer.

### What does a Thermal Radiator do?

A Thermal Radiator passively dumps heat over time. It is better for ongoing heat control than a one-time buffer.

### Does heat signature attract aliens in Starminer?

Yes, high heat signature can attract alien attention. Keep long high-heat activities under control, especially when running research, weapons, mining or heavy industry.

### Should I build the Research Lab early?

Usually no. Build the Research Lab after your solar, battery and thermal support are stable, because the lab can drain power and produce heat.

### Should I rush nuclear power?

Usually no. Nuclear power can require fuel and heat support, so it is safer after your early solar, battery, economy and thermal systems are already stable.

### Why does my station overheat?

Too many active modules may be producing more heat than your dumpers and radiators can handle. Reduce load and add thermal support before expanding again.
