<h2 id="burn-after-nerf">
  Burn after the Flare and Wildfire nerfs
</h2>

The biggest reason to play Burn right now is still the same: it kills slow boards before they finish scaling.

The difference after the nerf is that Burn is less autopilot. Flare is slower and more expensive, and Wildfire applies less base Burn. That does not kill the build, but it changes the way you read the first few triggers.

<GuideTable
  caption="Recent Burn changes that affect the build."
  columns={patchColumns}
  rows={patchRows}
/>

The most important number is **4.0s vs 4.1s**.

Molten Bow triggers at 4.0s. Flare now triggers at 4.1s. That 0.1s gap is small, but it is enough to make Molten Bow the first Burn source and Flare the follow-up scaler.

<h2 id="build-plan">
  The Burn build plan
</h2>

Burn is a tempo build. Your job is to start the damage race early and force the opponent to die before their engine stabilizes.

A clean Burn run has five jobs:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>
    <strong>Find a real carry.</strong> In this guide, that means Molten Bow, not a vague “Firebow.”
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Use Flare as the follow-up scaler.</strong> After the 4.1s nerf, it should usually fire after Molten Bow.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Add Wildfire or Fire Orb when they connect the chain.</strong> Do not buy them as random Fire tags.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Hit the right star breakpoints.</strong> Flare and Wildfire upgrades are much more valuable than generic filler.
  </li>
  <li>
    <strong>Buy just enough survival.</strong> One defensive slot can beat mirrors, Poison, and burst without turning the build into a tank board.
  </li>
</ol>

The default order to think about is:

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Is Burn still good after the Flare nerf?

Yes. Burn is still good, but the timing changed. Flare moved from 3.9s to 4.1s, while Molten Bow is 4.0s, so Molten Bow usually applies Burn first and Flare follows as the scaler.

### What is the best Burn build in Oaken Tower?

The best Burn build is usually a Molten Bow carry supported by Flare, Wildfire, Fire Orb, cooldown, star upgrades, and one defensive flex slot. The exact board changes by shop, but the trigger order matters.

### Is Firebow a real item?

Use the official item name Molten Bow. “Firebow” can be used casually to mean a Burn bow, but the item this route is built around is Molten Bow.

### Why does Flare's 4.1s cooldown matter?

Because Molten Bow has a 4.0s cooldown. That 0.1s gap means Molten Bow can apply the first Burn stack before Flare triggers, so Flare should usually be treated as a follow-up scaler.

### Is Wildfire still worth buying?

Yes, but it is not an autopick. Wildfire now applies 4 Burn instead of 5, but it costs 20g and has a 3.7s cooldown, so it can still provide early Burn pressure and useful scaling at higher stars.

### What are the best perks for Burn?

Merge and Flash Sale are the safest general Burn perks because they help with star breakpoints and expensive roll-downs. Artillery, Flame/Burn support, and economy perks are good when the opening shop supports them.

### Should I force Burn from Day 1?

No. Buy early Burn only when the shop gives you real tempo or a clear Fire package. If the shop gives random filler, level, freeze, save, or play the strongest temporary board.

### What beats Burn?

Poison tempo, Burn mirrors, Dark burst, shield / healing boards, and late scaling engines can all beat Burn if your first wave is too slow or too weak. The answer is usually earlier activation, a stronger carry, or a small defensive buffer.

### Do I need a 3-star Molten Bow?

No, but Molten Bow upgrades are valuable when it is already your carry. Do not chase 3-star unless the upgrade actually helps the active Burn chain.

### Why does my Burn build fall off late?

Burn falls off when it is only fast, not scalable or safe. Add Flare upgrades, Wildfire scaling, Fire Orb positioning, cooldown, direct damage, or a defensive flex slot before enemies survive your first wave.
