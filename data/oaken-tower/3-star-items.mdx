<h2 id="how-stars-work">
  How item stars work
</h2>

Item stars are Oaken Tower's upgrade system. When you get matching copies of an item, you can merge them upward and improve the item.

The important part is not just “higher star is better.” The important part is **which item gets the upgrade**.

A 3-star item is strongest when it is:

- your main damage source,
- your main Poison, Burn, Bleed, Shock, or scaling engine,
- the support item that makes your carry trigger,
- the defensive core that lets your carry survive,
- the exact item your build is already built around.

A 3-star item is weak when it is just an expensive trophy sitting next to the real carry.

<h2 id="natural-merges">
  Natural merges: the safest 3-star path
</h2>

The safest 3-star upgrades usually start naturally.

If the shop keeps offering the same carry item, that is a signal. You can buy duplicates, freeze the important copy, and build around the item without destroying your economy.

Natural merging is good when:

- the item is already winning fights,
- you already have duplicates,
- the next upgrade is close,
- your board survives while you chase it,
- you are not ignoring a better level timing.

Natural merging is bad when:

- you have only one copy,
- the item is not your carry,
- you are rolling from nothing,
- the rest of your board is falling behind,
- the upgrade does not solve your next matchup.

<h2 id="upgrade-rewards">
  Upgrade-to-3-star rewards
</h2>

Some reward choices can push an item straight toward 3-star or offer a direct upgrade path.

These rewards are powerful, but they are not always correct. In one run, the player saw an **upgrade item to 3-star** choice but passed because the board did not have a good 2-star target for it yet.

- Do I already have a 2-star item worth upgrading?
- Is that item my carry?
- Does the 3-star version solve my next fights?
- Would another reward keep me alive more safely?
- Am I upgrading because it wins, or because 3-star looks exciting?

<h2 id="crown-forge">
  Crown Forge potion: use it on the right 2-star item
</h2>

Crown Forge is one of the cleanest ways to reach 3-star, but it has a key rule: **you need a 2-star item first.**

That means Crown Forge is not a magic fix for a weak board. It is a payoff for a board that already built the correct 2-star item.

Use Crown Forge on:

- your main damage carry,
- a Poison / Burn / Bleed engine,
- a shield or heal core that keeps your carry alive,
- a trigger item that makes the whole board work,
- a support piece only if it creates a major breakpoint.

Do not use Crown Forge on:

- random temporary items,
- support that barely matters,
- a piece you are about to sell,
- an item that is not part of your current build,
- a 2-star item that exists only because you happened to find copies.

<h2 id="level-vs-reroll">
  Level vs reroll when chasing upgrades
</h2>

If you want 3-star items, your instinct may be to reroll. That is not always right.

Reroll when:

- you are one copy away,
- the item is your carry,
- the shop tier can actually show what you need,
- your board survives while rolling,
- one upgrade changes future fights.

Level when:

- your current shop quality is too low,
- you need higher-rarity items,
- your board is already stable,
- rerolling now would only find weak copies,
- better future shops matter more than this shop.

For the full decision tree, read the <Link href="/oaken-tower/level-vs-reroll">Oaken Tower Level vs Reroll Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### How do you get 3-star items in Oaken Tower?

You get 3-star items by merging matching item copies, using upgrade rewards, copying important item lines, or using shortcut effects such as Crown Forge on an eligible 2-star item.

### Does Crown Forge work on any item?

No. Crown Forge-style upgrades are best used on a 2-star item. Do not treat it as a fix for a weak 1-star board.

### Is Mirror Elixir good for getting 3-star items?

It can be, but only when it copies an item that matters. Use copy effects on your carry path, not on random support pieces.

### Should I always chase 3-star items?

No. Chase 3-star only when the item is your carry or the upgrade creates a major breakpoint. Many 10-win runs do not need a 3-star item.

### Is Doom Guide worth 3-star?

Only if Doom Guide is your real carry or scaling engine. If another item is doing the work, do not spend the run chasing Doom Guide just because it looks powerful.

### Is Petal Staff worth 3-star?

Petal Staff can be worth 3-star if it is already your Poison or scaling engine. It is not worth forcing if the rest of your board cannot survive while you roll.

### Is Venom Bow worth 3-star?

Yes, if your board is built around Poison or bow pressure and Venom Bow is carrying fights. If you have no bow support or Poison payoff, do not chase it blindly.

### What item should I upgrade to 3-star?

Upgrade the item that is actually winning fights: your damage carry, debuff engine, trigger engine, or defensive core that keeps your carry alive.

### Is a 3-star support item worth it?

Sometimes, but only if that support directly improves your carry. A 3-star support item is bad if your main damage is still weak.

### Should I reroll for 3-star copies?

Reroll when you are close to the upgrade and your board can survive. Do not roll from zero copies just because you want a 3-star item.

### Should I freeze duplicates?

Yes, if the duplicate is part of a real carry upgrade. Freezing is often safer than panic-rolling or spending all your gold immediately.

### Can I win without 3-star items?

Yes. Many runs reach 10 wins with strong 1-star or 2-star items plus good support. A 3-star item is a tool, not a requirement.
