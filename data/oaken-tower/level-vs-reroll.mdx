<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Level when your board is already strong enough to survive. Reroll when you can name the exact item, duplicate, support piece, or matchup fix you are looking for. Freeze when the shop has a key item but the timing is wrong.**

A level is not just “better shops.” It also costs real gold now. The useful shortcut is: each next level costs 4 more XP than the last, and each level adds 5 income. Level when that future value is worth more than buying power for the next fight.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="quick-rule">
  The rule in one sentence
</h2>

If your board is strong, **level**.  
If your board has a target, **reroll**.  
If the shop has a key item at the wrong time, **freeze**.  
If the next fight kills you, **buy power**.  
If nothing helps and you are already winning, **save**.

That sounds simple, but most players lose because they break this rule emotionally. They level because the shop is boring. They reroll because they are tilted. They freeze because an item looks rare. They buy because they have gold.

Do not spend gold because you can. Spend it because it changes the run.

<h2 id="level-numbers">
  Level numbers: what leveling actually gives you
</h2>

Leveling is an economy decision, not just a power decision. The basic pattern is simple:

- The next level cost increases by **4 XP** each level.
- Your income increases by **5 gold** each level.
- Higher levels also improve the kind of items you can realistically shop for.

The income shown in the table is the income after you reach that new level. For example, Level 1 shows 45 income in-game, so leveling from 1 to 2 puts the next income point at 50.

<h2 id="when-to-level">
  When should you level?
</h2>

Leveling gives you access to better shops, more income, and more health, but it does not automatically make your current board stronger.

That means leveling is best when you are already stable.

<GuideTable
  caption="Good reasons to level in Oaken Tower."
  columns={levelColumns}
  rows={levelRows}
/>

The biggest trap is treating level as a panic button. If the enemy is already killing you, spending gold on XP may leave you with the same weak board and fewer chances to fix it.

<h2 id="risky-level">
  Example: risky early leveling
</h2>

Sometimes you can level early and accept that the first fights might be ugly. This is not always wrong. It is a gamble.

The key question is whether the loss is affordable and whether the level gives you enough future value.

Use risky leveling only when at least one of these is true:

- early losses are still affordable,
- your perk or economy makes the level valuable,
- you have enough health to take the hit,
- the current shop is truly bad,
- you have a plan for the next shop.

Do not copy greedy leveling blindly. The same play that wins one run can throw another.

<h2 id="when-to-reroll">
  When should you reroll?
</h2>

Rerolling is good when you have a target. It is terrible when you are just asking the shop to save you.

Before you reroll, say the target out loud:

- I need one more copy of my carry.
- I need cooldown support.
- I need multicast.
- I need shield or healing.
- I need a specific payoff item.
- I need a pivot because my current build has no future.

If your target is “something good,” do not roll yet.

<h2 id="when-to-freeze">
  When should you freeze?
</h2>

Freeze is one of the strongest tools in Oaken Tower because it lets you separate timing from value.

Sometimes the item is correct, but buying it right now is wrong. Maybe you need to level first. Maybe you need income. Maybe the item is a duplicate you cannot use yet. Maybe the support piece only matters if you also buy the carry next day.

That is when freeze wins games.

<h2 id="freeze-then-level">
  Example: freeze then level
</h2>

One of the cleanest plays is to roll first, find a useful shop, then freeze and level because buying immediately does not improve the board.

This works when the item is good, but not useful this second.

Freeze then level is good when:

- the item completes your next shop, not your current fight,
- leveling gives health or shop access now,
- buying the item immediately would leave it unused,
- you want to preserve a duplicate or carry option,
- you are strong enough to survive without buying it now.

Freeze then level is bad when:

- you need immediate power,
- the frozen item is not important,
- you are too weak to invest,
- the next fight can end the run.

<h2 id="buy-vs-save">
  Buy power vs save gold
</h2>

Buying and saving are both correct in different spots.

Buying power is correct when the next fight is the problem. Saving is correct when your board already wins and the shop does not improve it.

<h2 id="three-star-chasing">
  Level vs reroll when chasing 3-star items
</h2>

Chasing 3-star items is where reroll discipline matters most.

Rerolling for a 3-star is good when:

- the item is your actual carry,
- you already have duplicates,
- one upgrade changes your damage, defense, or trigger speed,
- your board can survive while rolling,
- you are not sacrificing a better level timing.

Rerolling for a 3-star is bad when:

- the item is only support,
- you are already losing,
- the rest of your board is weak,
- you are rolling because upgrades feel good,
- leveling would give you better shops and more health.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Should I level or reroll in Oaken Tower?

Level when your board is already strong enough to survive and better shops, more income, or more health matter. Reroll when you can name the exact item, duplicate, or support piece you need.

### What does leveling give you?

Leveling costs XP, increases your income by 5 gold per level, improves your future shop quality, and gives more health. The next level cost also rises by 4 XP each level, so leveling is best when your board can survive the investment.

### Is early leveling good?

Early leveling can be good if you can afford the loss or already have enough tempo. It is bad when you skip power and leave your tower too weak to survive.

### When should I stop rerolling?

Stop rerolling when you no longer have a target, when your board needs immediate power, or when leveling gives better value. If you are rolling just to see something exciting, stop.

### Should I freeze before leveling?

Yes, when the shop has a key item you want later but leveling first is better right now. Freeze then level is one of the strongest shop patterns when your current board can survive.

### Should I buy weak items or save?

Buy weak items only if they help the next fight or support your carry. Save if your board already wins and the shop does not improve it.

### Should I reroll for 3-star items?

Only when the item is your actual carry or the upgrade creates a major breakpoint. Do not roll for 3-star support items while your board is falling behind.

### Why do I lose after leveling?

Usually because you leveled while too weak. Leveling improves future shops, but it does not fix a board that cannot survive the next fight.

### Why do I lose after rerolling?

Usually because the rerolls had no target. You spent gold without gaining power, upgrades, health, or a clear pivot.
