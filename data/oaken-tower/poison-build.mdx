<h2 id="when-to-play-poison">
  When should you play Poison?
</h2>

Play Poison when at least one of these is true:

- you rolled **Toxic** and can use the Poison Slot,
- your opening shop shows **Poison Dagger** or **Petal Staff**,
- you see early bow support for **Venomstrike**,
- you find **Toxins** or **Venom Ring** as a scaling payoff,
- you already have fast triggers, multicast, or cooldown support,
- an encounter gives you enough rerolls, upgrades, or shop value to search for a real Poison piece.

The important word is **real**. One random Poison item is not a build. A Poison item plus repeated triggers, scaling, or a strong support path is a build.

Poison starts before the shop. Your selected perks decide what kind of run you are allowed to play.

You do not need every Poison-friendly perk at once. You need one perk that gives a direction and enough backup value to survive if the first shops do not support Poison.

The short version:

- **D4-D6:** commit only if your Poison actually has a carry path.
- **D7-D8:** level, upgrade, or search for a specific payoff.
- **D9+:** solve the next opponent, not your dream final board.

<h2 id="mid-game-scaling">
  Mid game: what you are actually searching for
</h2>

In the mid game, your shop decisions should be boring and specific.

You are usually searching for one of these:

- **Venom Ring** if you already have several Poison items.
- **Toxins** if your Poison exists but needs to trigger harder.
- **Venomstrike** if your board has bow or projectile support.
- **Petal Staff duplicates** if it is already carrying your scaling.
- **Potion support** if your run has potion synergy.
- **Cooldown or multicast support** if your Poison engine is too slow.
- **Shield or healing** if you are dying before the stacks matter.

Do not reroll because you are bored. Reroll because one of those targets changes the next two fights.

<h2 id="placement-and-support">
  Placement and support rules
</h2>

Poison gets much better when the right item is doing the poisoning.

Good Poison holders usually have:

- fast cooldown,
- repeated triggers,
- multicast,
- projectile support,
- item-triggering effects,
- a Poison slot,
- enough protection to survive long fights.

Bad Poison holders are slow items that trigger once, apply a little Poison, and then watch the enemy kill you.

If you have to choose between “more Poison” and “faster Poison,” faster Poison is often the better first fix.

<h2 id="matchups">
  Poison matchups: what to do against common builds
</h2>

Poison is not played the same way into every opponent. The next fight should change what you buy.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to play Poison against common Oaken Tower builds."

  rows={matchupRows}

Against shield and heal, Poison needs enough application speed to finish the enemy. Against Burn or Dark burst, you need enough early tempo to avoid dying first. Against bow carries, the key is surviving their burst window so your damage-over-time can actually finish the fight.

<h2 id="final-board">
  What a strong Poison board looks like
</h2>

A good final Poison board should be easy to explain:

- this item applies most of my Poison,
- this item makes it trigger faster,
- this item helps me survive,
- this item is my scaling payoff,
- this item finishes fights if Poison is too slow.

If you cannot point to those jobs, the board is probably too scattered.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What are the best Poison items in Oaken Tower?

Start with Poison Dagger or Petal Staff if the shop offers them early. Look for Venomstrike if you have bow support, Toxins if you already apply Poison, and Venom Ring when you have enough Poison items to scale around it.

### Is Toxic the best perk for Poison?

Toxic is the cleanest Poison opener because it gives a Poison Slot, but it is only strong when your shop gives you fast triggers or Poison items to use with it.

### Is Venom Ring worth building around?

Yes, when you already have enough Poison items or can quickly add them. Venom Ring is much weaker if it appears in a board with no Poison base.

### Is Poison good against shield builds?

Poison can be good into shield and heal builds, but only if you apply enough of it. A tiny Poison source may not beat heavy shielding, healing, or debuff cleanse.

### What should I reroll for in a Poison build?

Reroll for a named target: Venom Ring, Toxins, Venomstrike, Petal Staff duplicates, potion support, cooldown support, multicast, or defense. If you cannot name the target, you probably should not roll yet.
