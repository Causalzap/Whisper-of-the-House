<h2 id="tremor-evacuation-guide">
  Frostpunk 2 Breach of Trust tremor and evacuation guide
</h2>

Breach of Trust has two environmental threats that players often blur together: **tremors** and **Volcanic Night**.

They are not the same problem.

Tremors are repeated pressure checks. I can see them coming, lower steam output, build Emergency Shelter Hubs, and save prefabs for repairs. Volcanic Night is the late-game phase shift. Once it begins, New Edinburgh is no longer a city I am trying to perfect. It becomes a city I am trying to empty before the eruption.

My route is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Lower steam output before the first tremor timer becomes dangerous.</li>
  <li>Research Emergency Shelter Hub early.</li>
  <li>Protect housing before comfort buildings or extra expansion.</li>
  <li>Keep prefabs ready for repairs after every tremor.</li>
  <li>When Volcanic Night starts, rush Evacuation Hub research.</li>
  <li>Prepare repeated airship cycles instead of relying on one late transport.</li>
  <li>Build Aurora housing before evacuees arrive.</li>
  <li>Start Generator Core extraction only when the evacuation loop is already moving.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="tremor-vs-volcanic-night">
  Tremor vs Volcanic Night
</h2>

The first half of the scenario is about surviving repeated tremors without letting the city fall apart.

A tremor is a recurring event. It can damage districts, create injuries, kill people if shelter coverage is poor, and force repairs. The main controls are steam output, Emergency Shelter Hub coverage, and prefab reserves.

Volcanic Night is different. It is the eruption phase. Ash pressure rises, squalor becomes harder to contain, trust becomes fragile, crime can increase, and the objective shifts toward evacuation. At that point, repairing every old district in New Edinburgh is no longer the right instinct.

I use this rule:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>For tremors, build prevention and repair capacity.</li>
  <li>For Volcanic Night, build evacuation capacity and Aurora-side survival.</li>
</ul>

That distinction helps avoid the common mistake of spending late-game resources to make New Edinburgh comfortable instead of loading the next evacuation cycle.

<h2 id="steam-output">
  Lower steam output before the first serious tremor
</h2>

The opening objective points toward reducing steam output to **200 or less**. I treat that as a survival target, not a side objective.

If I leave steam output high, the city feels safer for a few weeks because heat is easy. The problem is that the next tremor arrives sooner. That means damaged housing, broken production, injured workers, and another prefab bill before the economy is ready.

I lower steam output in this order:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>turn down overheated districts first,</li>
  <li>disable excess geothermal extraction when the city can survive it,</li>
  <li>use coal as a controlled heat buffer,</li>
  <li>avoid burning coal so fast that Aurora trade or evacuation stockpiles suffer later,</li>
  <li>and demolish dead or empty districts instead of repairing them out of habit.</li>
</ul>

I do not freeze everyone just to hit 200. The useful move is to remove wasted steam, then watch whether cold, disease, or housing starts becoming more dangerous than the tremor timer.

<h2 id="emergency-shelter-hub">
  Research Emergency Shelter Hub early
</h2>

Emergency Shelter Hub is the tech that turns tremors from a death spiral into a repair problem.

The hub is expensive early. In my run, the build screen showed **50 workforce**, **40 materials demand**, **50 heatstamps**, **40 materials**, and **100 prefabs**. Treat those as a screenshot reference, not a permanent universal cost. Recheck the build screen in your own run if difficulty or patches have changed.

Coverage matters more than simply placing the hub somewhere nearby. I look at the radius and protect housing first. If housing gets hit without enough coverage, the tremor can turn into homelessness, cold exposure, disease, and trust loss.

My coverage priority is:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Housing districts.</li>
  <li>Prefab and industrial production.</li>
  <li>Research districts.</li>
  <li>Food or transfer infrastructure.</li>
  <li>Everything else only after the survival layer is covered.</li>
</ol>

If I choose the war route, I still try not to delay Emergency Shelter Hub too long. Barracks and platoons help against Aurora. They do not protect New Edinburgh from the ground opening under housing.

<h2 id="prefab-reserve">
  Covered districts can still be damaged
</h2>

The most important shelter lesson is simple: **covered does not mean untouched**.

Emergency Shelter Hubs reduce casualties, but districts can still be damaged. That damage still costs prefabs to repair. If I spend every prefab right before the hit, the tremor leaves me unable to fix the districts that keep the city alive.

After each tremor, I repair in this order:

<ol className="my-6 list-decimal space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>Housing if people become homeless or cold.</li>
  <li>Prefab production if the damage blocks future repairs.</li>
  <li>Research if the next tech is time-sensitive.</li>
  <li>Food or transfer systems if New Edinburgh is still starving.</li>
  <li>Comfort buildings only after the chain reaction is stopped.</li>
</ol>

This is why I keep a prefab reserve. It is not only for new buildings. In Breach of Trust, prefabs are damage insurance.

<h2 id="pure-dead-brilliant">
  Pure Dead Brilliant checklist
</h2>

The **Pure Dead Brilliant** achievement requires a no-deaths clear on at least **Steward** difficulty.

I do not treat it as a first-run goal. A deathless run can fail from tremors, cold, hunger, disease, crime, bad evacuation housing, or poor Generator Core timing. I use the first clear to learn where deaths happen, then replay with a tighter route.

My no-deaths checklist is:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>reduce steam output early enough to delay the first tremor,</li>
  <li>research Emergency Shelter Hub before risky comfort or military detours,</li>
  <li>cover housing before industry,</li>
  <li>keep enough prefabs for instant repairs,</li>
  <li>avoid food gaps that can become starvation deaths,</li>
  <li>control disease before it turns lethal,</li>
  <li>control crime during Volcanic Night,</li>
  <li>and prepare Aurora housing before evacuation waves arrive.</li>
</ul>

The final deaths may not come from the volcano itself. They can come from ordinary problems that are easy to ignore when the eruption timer is on screen.

I do not plan this phase around resources I have not secured yet. Volcanic Night can limit new Frostland routes, and the timer is already running. I want the evacuation economy ready before the city starts falling apart.

The practical lesson is not only “build more hubs.” The real lesson is to keep the whole chain moving:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>food must be produced or transferred fast enough,</li>
  <li>coal must stay available for both launch loading and heat,</li>
  <li>prefabs must not be drained by late comfort projects,</li>
  <li>workers must remain in New Edinburgh long enough to load the next cycle,</li>
  <li>and Aurora must have housing before the evacuees arrive.</li>
</ul>

If any part of that chain breaks, the airship sits idle while the eruption timer keeps falling.

Before starting or continuing the final core window, I check:

<ul className="my-6 list-disc space-y-2 pl-6">
  <li>how many people are still in New Edinburgh,</li>
  <li>whether the next airships are loaded or nearly loaded,</li>
  <li>whether enough workers remain to keep loading resources,</li>
  <li>whether Aurora has housing for arrivals,</li>
  <li>and whether the core timer fits the remaining eruption window.</li>
</ul>

If the airships are idle or resources are missing, I fix the loading chain first. If the eruption timer is too tight, I stop spending on anything that does not support the final launches.

<h2 id="faq">
  Tremor and evacuation FAQ
</h2>

### Will my Volcanic Night timer and evacuation target match yours?

Not always. My run showed a **79-week** eruption timer, a **10,450** majority evacuation target, and a **6,966** casualty cap. Your numbers can change based on difficulty, population size, earlier deaths, route choices, and patches.

### How do I reduce tremor danger?

Lower steam output, especially excess geothermal use. The opening objective points toward **200 or less**, which is a useful target if the city can survive it.

### What is the difference between Tremors and Volcanic Night?

Tremors are recurring damage events that you manage with lower steam output, shelter coverage, and repairs. Volcanic Night is the later eruption phase where ash, squalor, crime, evacuation, and Aurora housing become the main problem.

### Should I research Emergency Shelter Hub before Barracks?

For a first clear, usually yes. If you rush Barracks and the first tremor hits uncovered housing, people can die before the war route matters.

### Which districts should I protect first?

Protect housing first, then prefab production, research, food, or transfer infrastructure. Housing damage can create homelessness and cold deaths quickly.

### Why do I still need prefabs after building shelters?

Shelters reduce casualties, not all damage. Tremors can still damage districts, and repairs can cost a lot of prefabs.

### What does each Evacuation Hub airship need?

In my run, each launch needed **5,000 food**, **5,000 coal**, and **500 prefabs**, then saved **1,000 people**. Recheck your own hub panel if balance patches change costs.

### How many Evacuation Hubs should I build?

More than one if your resources allow. The real goal is not just hub count; it is keeping food, coal, prefabs, workers, and Aurora housing ready for repeated launches.

### When should I extract the Generator Core?

Extract it after your evacuation system is already running and the core timer lines up with the final eruption. Do not start it while airships are idle or unloaded.

### How do I prepare for Pure Dead Brilliant?

Do not chase it blind. Learn the scenario first, then replay with strict shelter coverage, no starvation, no housing gaps, low crime, stable Aurora housing, and clean Generator Core timing.
