<h2 id="hard-requirement">
  Hard requirement: you need the Chapter 4 Weird Route state
</h2>

The first check is not inside Chapter 5. It is your save file.

Chapter 5 Weird Route requires a Completion File that already continued the Weird Route through Chapter 4. If you start Chapter 5 from a normal file, this lake route will not appear in the same way. You cannot simply decide at the festival that you want Side B.

If this requirement is missing, stop here and use a normal Chapter 5 guide instead. The file-management warning is important, but I cover that in the permanent save-file section below so this route requirement stays focused.

<h2 id="permanent-save-file">
  Does the Weird Route Permanently Affect Your Save File?
</h2>

This is the main question to answer before starting the Chapter 5 Weird Route.

The safe answer is: **do not treat this like a normal chapter branch that you can casually overwrite later**. Chapter 5 Weird Route progress is tied to carried Completion File / route-state data, and a successful run creates a Side B continuation state instead of a normal Chapter 5 continuation.

It is not best described as “every save file is permanently ruined” in the same fear people often associate with Undertale-style Genocide Route discussion. The risk is more practical: if you overwrite your only clean normal file with a Weird Route / Side B state, you may lose your easy path back to normal Chapter 5 cleanup on that file.

Use this setup before finishing the sequence:

* **Normal file** — Flowery, regular ending, Chapter 6 continuation, and first-clear story context.
* **Cleanup file** — Pink Coins, Pink / Miumu, foxes, Secret Egg, flower gear, and optional Chapter 5 collection.
* **Weird Route file** — Chapter 5 Weird Route / Side B continuation only.

<GuideCallout title="Safe save rule">
If you care about seeing both the normal Chapter 5 route and the Weird Route later, do not overwrite your only normal completion file after finishing the Side B sequence.
</GuideCallout>

The reason players worry about this route is that it feels closer to a long-term file-state decision than a normal optional scene. I would only finish the Chapter 5 Weird Route on a file I am comfortable carrying forward as the Weird Route / Side B file.

<h2 id="get-out-of-bed">
  Step 1: force Kris out of bed
</h2>

Chapter 5 Weird Route opens with Kris refusing to move normally.

Toriel calls from outside the room, but Kris stays in bed. I mash directional inputs until Kris finally reacts, rolls, crawls, and stands up. Movement is slow, text is sparse, and the whole opening is meant to feel wrong.

Do not assume the game is frozen. Keep pressing directions until Kris gets out of bed.

This is also where I avoid optional room messing around unless I am deliberately testing flavor text. The route has no normal pacing, and the file already feels fragile.

<h2 id="sans-bread">
  Step 3: get Bread from Sans
</h2>

Before going to the lake, go to **Sans's ice cream stand outside his grocery store** and talk to him.

Sans gives Kris **Bread** while the ice cream machine is working. This is not optional flavor. Bread is the item that lets the route move toward Noelle at the lake.

If I try to head to the lake without doing this, Susie redirects the route and pushes me back toward finding ice cream / Sans first. That is why I treat Sans as a required checkpoint, not a side NPC.

My check is simple:

1. Leave the Dreemurr house with Susie.
2. Go south toward the grocery store area.
3. Talk to Sans at the ice cream stand.
4. Confirm that Kris receives Bread.
5. Only then go to the lake.

Once Bread is handled, the route becomes much more direct.

<h2 id="stop-proceed">
  Stop and Proceed: what actually matters
</h2>

The Stop / Proceed section has two different stages.

In the first lake prompts, choosing **Stop** or **Proceed** does not safely undo the scene. If Stop is chosen, Noelle forces the route forward anyway. This is why I do not tell players to “just pick Stop” once the lake scene is already underway.

The real execution check starts later, when Kris and Noelle are submerged.

At that point, the options become a fast Proceed sequence. The prompt may become visually confusing, and the text can stop being useful. I keep my cursor discipline simple: select the visible Proceed option immediately, and do not wait to read every box. If two Proceed choices appear, I keep confirming quickly instead of chasing the text.

This is the part that decides whether I get Side B or an aborted route.

---

<h2 id="submerged-timer">
  The submerged Proceed timer: roughly 70 prompts
</h2>

Once Kris and Noelle are underwater, a hidden timer starts.

This is the biggest mechanical correction: the lake is not just a slow story scene. It becomes a timed input challenge. You need to select Proceed through roughly **70 prompts** before the timer expires.

The timer starts more forgiving, around several seconds, then gets shorter as the sequence continues. Near the end, the screen becomes harder to read, the audio pressure rises, and the timer becomes much less forgiving.

My practical method:

* put one hand on confirm before the submerged sequence starts;
* do not pause to read every repeated Proceed box;
* keep confirming the Proceed option immediately;
* if the screen starts fading white, speed up;
* if the static gets louder and higher, treat it as a timer warning;
* do not relax until the CRT ending screen appears.

This is why players fail even when they think they “picked Proceed.” The problem is usually not the choice. It is speed.

<h2 id="thorn-ring-twisted-sword">
  Thorn Ring, Twisted Sword, and Why the Weird Route Matters
</h2>

The Chapter 5 Weird Route matters because it may affect later route-state content, not because it gives you a normal Chapter 5 item reward.

The two item names players usually connect to this route are **Thorn Ring** and **Twisted Sword**.

The Thorn Ring is tied to the Weird Route discussion, and the Twisted Sword is usually discussed because of its connection to **Thorn Ring** and **Pure Crystal**. As of Chapter 5, I would treat this as future-route setup and theory territory, not as a normal Chapter 5 reward you can freely craft during this route.

That is why I keep the file advice strict:

* a clean Side B file is for continuing the Weird Route state;
* an aborted Weird Route file is a different experiment;
* a normal Chapter 5 file is still better for Flowery, Pink Coins, Pink / Miumu, foxes, and Secret Egg cleanup.

<h2 id="how-to-avoid-abort">
  How to avoid aborting the lake sequence
</h2>

Most failures happen because the player thinks the sequence is still a normal dialogue scene.

I prepare for it like this:

1. Finish reading Noelle’s monologue before the water sequence begins.
2. Get ready when the prompts become repetitive.
3. Keep the cursor stable.
4. Confirm immediately.
5. Do not stop to think once the submerged timer begins.
6. Watch for screen whitening and static as danger signs.
7. Continue until the CRT screen, not until the scene merely “feels long.”

The approximate 70-prompt length matters because it keeps me from panicking. If it feels like it is going on too long, that is normal. It is supposed to keep going.

<h2 id="when-to-turn-back">
  When You Can Still Turn Back Safely
</h2>

The safest time to turn back is **before starting this Chapter 5 Weird Route file**.

Once the lake scene is active, I do not treat Stop as a reliable “change my mind” button. Once the submerged timer begins, failure means an aborted route state, not a clean undo.

My rule is:

* not ready for Side B: do not start this file;
* want normal Chapter 5: use a normal save;
* want Pink / Miumu or Pink Coins: use a cleanup save;
* already at the lake: finish the route or reload;
* failed the timer: reload if you wanted Side B.

This is stricter than the normal route, but it prevents accidental file confusion.

<h2 id="normal-vs-weird">
  Normal route vs Weird Route
</h2>

The normal route and Weird Route are not two small variations of the same Chapter 5 ending.

In the normal route, Chapter 5 continues into the Flower Kingdom, Flowery, the fountain route, and the Chapter 6 setup.

In the successful Weird Route, Chapter 5 ends early with **Insert Chapter 7 Side B** and skips the normal Dark World progression.

In an aborted Weird Route, the chapter keeps going, but with altered route-state consequences.

That is why I do not recommend doing this before a normal first clear unless I specifically want Side B first. The route is short, but the file consequences are large.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Common Chapter 5 Weird Route mistakes
</h2>

* **Starting from a normal save:** Chapter 5 Weird Route needs the previous Weird Route chain, especially the Chapter 4 Weird Route state.
* **Skipping Sans:** Bread from Sans is required before the lake.
* **Thinking the festival walk is the main objective:** Susie’s festival comments are context, but Bread is the hard checkpoint.
* **Treating Stop as a safe exit at the lake:** early Stop does not cleanly undo the route once the lake scene is active.
* **Reading every underwater prompt slowly:** the submerged section has a hidden timer.
* **Underestimating the prompt count:** expect roughly 70 Proceed prompts.
* **Relaxing when the screen gets hard to read:** the end is the most dangerous part of the timer.
* **Continuing after an abort when you wanted Side B:** reload and complete the timed sequence.
* **Mixing Weird Route with Pink cleanup:** use separate files.
* **Expecting Chapter 6 normal continuation on the Side B file:** successful Weird Route points to Chapter 7 Side B instead.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Can I start Chapter 5 Weird Route from a normal save?

No. You need a save file that already continued the Weird Route through Chapter 4.

### Does Chapter 5 Weird Route start at the beginning?

Yes. If the save file qualifies, Chapter 5 starts differently immediately: Kris refuses to get out of bed and the route is active from the start.

### How do I get Kris out of bed?

Mash directional inputs until Kris finally leaves the bed. It is intentional resistance, not a softlock.

### Do I need to talk to Sans?

Yes. Go to Sans's ice cream stand outside his grocery store and get Bread before going to the lake.

### What is Bread for?

Bread is the required Light World item that lets the Weird Route progress toward Noelle at the lake.

### Can I go straight to the lake?

No. If you try to go before handling the Sans / Bread step, Susie redirects you.

### Can I leave the lake room?

Once the route puts you into the lake room, do not count on leaving freely. Treat it as the commitment zone.

### Should I pick Stop or Proceed at the first lake prompts?

The first prompts do not safely stop the route. Stop and Proceed both continue the scene, with Noelle forcing the direction forward.

### What do I do once Kris and Noelle are underwater?

Select Proceed quickly through roughly 70 prompts. This part has a hidden timer.

### What happens if I am too slow?

The route aborts. The chapter continues in an altered state, and Kris can later carry the Shattered Rose title in the Dark World.

### What is the successful ending?

The successful route ends with the CRT message **Insert Chapter 7 Side B** and returns to file select.

### Does Chapter 5 Weird Route have SAVE points?

No normal SAVE points are available during this Chapter 5 Weird Route sequence. Use the correct carried save before starting.

### Does the Chapter 5 Weird Route permanently affect my save file?

Treat it as a long-term route-state decision, not a casual branch. It does not mean every file is automatically ruined, but a successful Side B file should be kept separate from your normal Chapter 5 file.

### Should I delete or overwrite my Weird Route file?

No, not if you want to continue the Weird Route later. Keep the Side B file separate and use a normal or cleanup file for Flowery, Pink Coins, Pink / Miumu, foxes, Secret Egg, and regular Chapter 5 cleanup.

### Are Thorn Ring and Twisted Sword part of the Chapter 5 Weird Route?

They are part of the larger Weird Route discussion, but I would not treat Twisted Sword as a normal Chapter 5 reward. The important thing for this page is the file state: clean Side B, aborted Weird Route, and normal Chapter 5 cleanup should stay separated.

### Should I do this before Flowery?

Only on a separate Weird Route file. For normal Flowery, Pink / Miumu, Pink Coins, foxes, and Secret Egg cleanup, use a normal or cleanup file.
