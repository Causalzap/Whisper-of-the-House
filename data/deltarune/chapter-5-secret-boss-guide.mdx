<h2 id="equipment">
  Best equipment: reduce CAT damage
</h2>

Pink’s attacks are CAT-element style damage, so equipment matters more than it first looks.

The most useful defensive pieces to check are:

* **Mannequin**;
* **FrayedBowtie**;
* **Dealmaker**;
* **TrueTie**.

If I still have Mannequin from Chapter 2, I put it on Kris for extra survivability. Dealmaker is also strong if I have it because it gives real defense and damage reduction. TrueTie and FrayedBowtie are worth checking if they fit my current setup.

I do not over-optimize attack stats here because Pink is not a damage race. I care about surviving Purple Soul patterns, bombs, dummies, tunnel pressure, and dating sim mistakes long enough to fill DOKI.

<h2 id="purple-soul">
  Purple Soul: collect hearts without chasing every heart
</h2>

Pink uses a Purple Soul style where the heart moves along lines in the battle box. At phase breaks, the fight can reduce the TP cap, and pink hearts become the way to recover the resource loop.

Pink hearts matter because they can restore HP, TP, and Max TP pressure. That does not mean I chase every heart. If a heart is safe, I take it. If it is sitting inside a bad bomb line or cat-face pattern, I let it go and survive the turn.

My Purple Soul rule is:

* move early when the lane is obvious;
* do not cross bomb lines late;
* collect hearts that do not cost more HP than they restore;
* prioritize survival before DOKI if Kris or Ralsei is low;
* use the next menu turn to convert the recovered TP into ACT progress.

<h2 id="doki-acts">
  How I spend DOKI ACTs
</h2>

The basic DOKI ladder is simple:

**Flirt** is small progress.
**MegaFlirt** is a +5 push.
**MegaFlirtX** is the better +5 push when available.
**GigaFlirt** is the +10 phase finisher.

I do not press the biggest ACT just because I can. Filling DOKI immediately sends the fight into the next dating sim or harder phase, so I check HP first. If everyone is healthy and Pink is close to the next DOKI target, MegaFlirt or GigaFlirt can skip an incoming attack and push the fight forward. If the party is weak, I heal before crossing the threshold.

<h2 id="lovelyboy-gigaflirt">
  LovelyBoy and GigaFlirt: why the 80 TP cap matters
</h2>

GigaFlirt gives **+10 DOKI**, but it needs **80 TP available**. In this fight, that is not only a current-TP problem. Pink can reduce the party’s Max TP, so I may not even be able to hold 80 TP until I rebuild the cap.

That is why Ralsei’s **LovelyBoy** matters. LovelyBoy raises Max TP by **14%**, and safe pink hearts during Purple Soul attacks help restore the TP loop. Once my TP cap can reach 80 and I have enough current TP, GigaFlirt becomes available as the big phase-push button.

My clean setup is:

1. Survive the early Purple Soul attacks.
2. Collect safe pink hearts without taking worse damage.
3. Use LovelyBoy to raise Max TP.
4. Build enough current TP.
5. Use GigaFlirt when the 80 TP requirement is actually reachable.
6. Heal first if GigaFlirt will trigger a harder date or phase while the party is low.

The best GigaFlirt is not the earliest one. It is the one that ends a dangerous phase after I have rebuilt both the TP cap and the party’s HP.

<h2 id="dating-sim-one">
  Date 1 answers: Yes, Yes, No
</h2>

After the first 15 DOKI, the fight enters its first dating sim section.

The important answer pattern is:

1. Walk-home / school-style question: **Yes**.
2. Cute-body question: **Yes**.
3. “Wish you never met me” style question: **No**.

This first date is the warm-up. It teaches that the fight is not only a bullet pattern boss. You also have to read Pink’s emotional prompts correctly.

---

<h2 id="dating-sim-two">
  Date 2: body and ghost split
</h2>

The second date is where many attempts fall apart because Pink’s body and ghost start pulling in different directions.

The rule is that the paired prompts are asking for the same answer in different tones. One side may sound rude or absurd, while the other gives the cleaner clue. I do not answer by mood. I answer by matching the cue.

Examples I watch for:

* bike / tired cue → choose the tired answer;
* honest / frank cue → choose the frank answer;
* Susie jeans / dog cue → choose the husky answer;
* letter / tear cue → choose the tear-up answer;
* holding / chicken cue → choose the tender answer;
* gift / rotten cue → choose the stinky fish answer;
* metal / scrap cue → choose the tin can answer;
* outfits / match cue → choose the matching-style answer;
* attention / Noelle cue → choose the fawn-over-Noelle answer;
* hike / mountain cue → choose the mountain answer;
* date / fruit cue → choose the date answer.

If I pick wrong, I expect damage and a repeated prompt. I slow down here because guessing turns the dating section into unnecessary HP loss.

<h2 id="phase-three">
  Phase 3: 20 DOKI and the hardest push
</h2>

Phase 3 raises the target to **20 DOKI**, and the attacks become heavier.

This is where I stop spending resources casually. A few regular Flirts are fine when the party is safe, but this phase is where **MegaFlirtX** and **GigaFlirt** matter most. If I have built enough Max TP and saved 80 TP, GigaFlirt can cut the phase dramatically with +10 DOKI.

The danger is entering the final date with everyone hurt. If the DOKI meter is close to full but Kris, Susie, or Ralsei is low, I heal before pushing. The next section is not a victory screen. It is another execution check.

---

<h2 id="die-maze">
  Final dating sim: DIE maze and heart targets
</h2>

The final dating sim turns into a maze with red **DIE** bullets and heart targets.

My goal is not to rush through it. I move from safe point to safe point, use checkpoints when the route gives them, and stop inputting if I can see that I am about to get caught. Getting hit can reset position and cost HP, but panic movement causes more failures than patience.

<h2 id="share-food">
  Share Food: useful, but do not throw away your healing
</h2>

Share Food is one of the strongest parts of the fight because it can add DOKI without the same TP pressure as Flirt ACTs.

The catch is obvious: food is also healing.

Common healing items can still add DOKI, and rarer food can add more. That means Share Food is best when the item is not needed for survival or when the DOKI push prevents a worse attack. It is bad when I give away the exact item I needed to keep Ralsei alive.

My Share Food rule:

* use it when the party is stable and I need DOKI;
* avoid it when two characters are low;
* do not give away my only strong heal before Phase 3;
* use it as a phase-push tool, not as my default action every turn.

This is still one of my favorite ways to push the fight, but only when the inventory can afford it.

<h2 id="downed-party-flirt">
  Downed party Flirt bonus: useful knowledge, risky plan
</h2>

There is one advanced mechanic worth knowing: regular Flirt can gain more DOKI when party members are down. With one teammate down, regular Flirt can gain more than usual; with more down, it can increase again.

I treat this as a recovery detail, not my main plan.

If Susie or Ralsei drops and Pink is one tiny push away from a DOKI break, a regular Flirt may be enough to force the transition. But if the next attack can kill Kris, I revive or heal instead. This bonus does not make a dead party safe, and it does not improve MegaFlirt in the same way.

The practical rule is:

**Use the downed-party Flirt bonus only when it clearly ends the section. Otherwise, stabilize first.**

<h2 id="when-to-heal">
  When to heal before pushing DOKI
</h2>

The old version repeated “heal first” too many times, so here is the clean rule.

I heal before pushing DOKI if:

* Kris can die to the next pattern;
* Ralsei is down before a hard section;
* two party members are low;
* DOKI is about to trigger a harder phase;
* I am entering Date 2 or the final maze;
* my only plan depends on surviving one more Purple Soul attack.

I push DOKI instead if:

* everyone is healthy;
* I have enough TP for MegaFlirtX or GigaFlirt;
* the DOKI gain will skip the next attack;
* the next section is safer than the current attack pattern;
* I still have emergency healing after the push.

That is the whole decision loop. I do not need a separate safe-clear route and fast-clear route. The safe route is fast when the DOKI pushes are timed correctly.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Common Pink secret boss mistakes
</h2>

* **Trying to attack Pink:** attacks are not the way to win this fight.
* **Not tracking DOKI targets:** Phase 1 needs 15, Phase 2 needs 15, and Phase 3 needs 20.
* **Using GigaFlirt without 80 TP planning:** build Max TP first with LovelyBoy and hearts.
* **Forgetting that GigaFlirt is +10 DOKI:** save it for a real phase push.
* **Ignoring CAT damage equipment:** Mannequin, FrayedBowtie, Dealmaker, and TrueTie can matter.
* **Chasing every pink heart:** take safe hearts, not suicidal ones.
* **Entering a date section at low HP:** DOKI breaks can lead into more pressure, not instant safety.
* **Guessing the dating sim answers:** read the prompt pair and pick the matching cue.
* **Panicking in the DIE maze:** use safe spots and slow movement.
* **Giving away key healing with Share Food:** DOKI is good, but survival still decides the clear.
* **Treating downed-party Flirt as a main strategy:** it is a clutch tool, not a reason to let everyone die.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### Who is the Chapter 5 secret boss?

The Chapter 5 secret boss is Pink / Miumu, found behind the Mystery Key and Pink Door.

### How do I unlock Pink?

Collect enough Pink Coins, buy the Mystery Key from the Cliffs shop, then use it on the Pink Door near the late castle route.

### Can I beat Pink by attacking?

No. Attacks do not meaningfully solve this fight. Fill the DOKI meter with ACTs and clear the dating sim sections.

### How much DOKI do I need?

Phase 1 needs **15 DOKI**, Phase 2 needs **15 DOKI**, and Phase 3 needs **20 DOKI**.

### How much DOKI does Flirt give?

Regular Flirt gives **+1 DOKI** and costs **8 TP**. If party members are down, regular Flirt can gain more DOKI, but that is risky.

### How much DOKI does MegaFlirt give?

MegaFlirt gives **+5 DOKI** and costs **60 TP**.

### How much DOKI does MegaFlirtX give?

MegaFlirtX gives **+5 DOKI** and costs **40 TP** when available.

### How much DOKI does GigaFlirt give?

GigaFlirt gives **+10 DOKI** and costs **80 TP**.

### How do I get enough TP for GigaFlirt?

Use LovelyBoy to raise Max TP, collect safe pink hearts during attacks, and avoid wasting TP before Phase 3.

### What equipment should I use?

Use defensive equipment that helps against Pink’s CAT-style damage. Check Mannequin, FrayedBowtie, Dealmaker, and TrueTie.

### What are the first dating sim answers?

For the first date, use **Yes**, **Yes**, then **No**.

### What is the body / ghost split?

During the second dating sim section, Pink’s body and ghost split into conflicting prompt styles. Read the paired cues and choose the matching answer instead of guessing.

### What do I do in the DIE maze?

Move slowly, use safe spots, avoid red DIE bullets, and reach the hearts to unlock the final response route.

### Should I use Share Food?

Yes, but only when you can spare the item. Share Food can add DOKI, but giving away your best healing item can make Phase 3 or the final maze much harder.
