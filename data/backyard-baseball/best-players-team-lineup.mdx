<h2 id="tier-list">
  Backyard Baseball 2026 S and A Tier player stats
</h2>

This is a **League usefulness Tier List**, not a ranking based only on the sum
of four ratings.

I value players who solve more than one roster problem:

- a hitter who can also pitch;
- a fast runner who can cover center field;
- an ace who does not completely weaken the batting order;
- or a fielder who repairs a lineup already overloaded with sluggers.

### What S Tier means

An S-tier player can be drafted early without already knowing the rest of the
roster.

Pablo, Pete, Angela, Kenny, and Kiesha each solve a high-value problem
immediately:

- Pablo provides elite offense and emergency pitching;
- Pete provides leadoff speed and center-field range;
- Angela and Kenny provide the two primary starters;
- Kiesha adds offense, speed, and shortstop coverage.

A Cold condition can still move one of them out of the starting lineup for a
single game.

### Competitive tournament cross-check

The Welcome Backyard Tournament provides a useful competitive check on this
Tier List because its players drafted for short, high-pressure matchups rather
than a full casual League season.

Tournament selections do not prove that one character is universally better
than another. They are most useful when the same player appears repeatedly
across independent teams, especially in high-value roles such as starting
pitcher, leadoff hitter, shortstop, or center field.

I use the tournament results as supporting evidence alongside base ratings and
my own League games—not as a replacement for either one.

### What A Tier means

A-tier players are strong enough to start, but their value depends more on the
role and surrounding roster.

Examples:

- Mikey is excellent when the team needs cleanup power and third-base fielding.
- Luanne is valuable when speed and bullpen depth are both missing.
- Ernie is better when defense matters more than adding another power hitter.
- Ashley and Sidney move up when used together.
- Maria moves up when the team is built around her pink-uniform condition.

### B Tier

Annie Frazier, Tony Delvecchio, Sally Dobbs, Dante Robinson, Lisa Crocket,
Vicki Kawaguchi, Jorge Garcia, Dmitri Petrovich, and Jocinda Smith can all fill
a useful role.

I usually choose an S- or A-tier player first because the higher-tier option
solves the same problem with fewer tradeoffs.

### C Tier

Billy Jean Blackwood, Gretchen Hasselhoff, Kimmy Eckman, Marky Dubois, Reese
Worthington, Ricky Johnson, and Ronnie Dobbs are better suited to themed teams,
challenge runs, favorite-character rosters, or a very specific active boost.

C Tier does not mean unusable. It means I would not select the player early
when building the safest all-purpose League team.

<h2 id="draft-plan">
  How to draft the team
</h2>

I draft by roster need rather than by choosing nine favorite characters.

The priority order is:

1. elite all-around production;
2. leadoff speed and center-field coverage;
3. two starting pitchers;
4. middle-order power;
5. shortstop and third-base coverage;
6. additional relief pitching;
7. conditional boosts after the base roster works.

### Start with Pablo and Pete

Pablo is the safest first selection.

His base ratings are:

- 10 Batting;
- 10 Running;
- 9 Pitching;
- 9 Fielding.

He can produce runs, cover an outfield position, and enter as a high-quality
relief pitcher.

Pete is the movement engine of the team.

Pete does not need 10 Batting to be an S-tier player. His value comes from
reaching base, pressuring the defense, taking extra bases, and covering the
largest part of the outfield.

Do not move him into a low-movement position merely because another character
can stand in center.

### Secure Angela and Kenny before adding another slugger

Angela and Kenny both have 10 Pitching, but the rest of their ratings are very
different.

Angela:

- 7 Batting;
- 4 Running;
- 10 Pitching;
- 1 Fielding.

Kenny:

- 3 Batting;
- 8 Running;
- 10 Pitching;
- 4 Fielding.

Angela provides more offense. Kenny provides more running and slightly more
fielding.

I alternate them as starting pitchers rather than exhausting one ace and
treating the other as an emergency replacement.

Pablo is the first relief option. Luanne, with 9 Pitching, gives the roster a
fourth usable arm.

### Solve shortstop and third base before adding more power

Kiesha belongs at shortstop because her 9 Batting and 9 Running are supported
by 6 Fielding.

Mikey moves to third in the final alignment. His 10 Batting and 7 Fielding make
him a better corner-infield fit than using Kenny there only because Kenny has a
high Pitching rating.

Pitching and Fielding are separate player-card values. A 10-rated pitcher is
not automatically the best option for long throws across the diamond.

Kenny at catcher is a practical way to protect the alignment, not a permanent
rule. Against a team that steals aggressively, I am willing to test a stronger
defensive catcher and move Kenny to another low-risk position.

<h2 id="rotation">
  Starting rotation and bullpen plan
</h2>

My pitching order is:

1. Angela Delvecchio
2. Kenny Kawaguchi
3. Pablo Sanchez
4. Luanne Lui
5. Amir Khan when the score allows a lower-pressure option

Angela and Kenny alternate starts.

Pablo enters when I need a strong replacement without introducing a weak hitter
into the lineup. Luanne provides another high-rated arm and can remain useful
in right field when she is not pitching.

### When to change pitchers

I prepare the next pitcher when:

- visible fatigue appears;
- the selected pitch location begins drifting;
- a useful pitch option becomes unavailable;
- the opponent's strongest hitters are approaching;
- or the current lead is large enough to preserve the starter for the next
  League game.

With a safe lead, I do not automatically use Angela or Kenny to finish the
game. A weaker relief option can protect the ace for the next matchup.

That decision is more useful than relying on one universal pitch count.

### Achmed and Amir Khan

Achmed provides 10 Batting and Amir provides 8 Pitching.

That makes the pairing easier to justify than a boost combination made from two
players with the same narrow role.

The balanced lineup already has space for both:

- Achmed at first base and second in the order;
- Amir at second base and as a secondary pitcher.

### Hot and Cold players

When Hot and Cold Players is enabled, check the condition icons before every
League game.

I review:

- the starting pitcher;
- the first five batting positions;
- shortstop and center field;
- and any player selected mainly for one elite rating.

The interface does not clearly expose one universal numerical formula for every
Hot or Cold state. I use the icon as a lineup decision signal rather than
claiming that every player receives the same fixed modifier.

### Birthday Boost

Birthday Boost is another temporary condition, not a permanent change to the
base stats table.

When a player receives a birthday-related condition:

1. check the icon before confirming the lineup;
2. compare the player with the normal starter;
3. identify which role is actually improved;
4. use the boost only when the position still makes sense.

A temporary boost does not automatically make a low-fielding player the best
shortstop or a weak pitcher the best starter.

<h2 id="custom-kid-builds">
  Best Custom Kid builds
</h2>

A Custom Kid receives **20 skill points** to distribute across Batting, Running,
Pitching, and Fielding.

Because each category begins at 1, the final visible ratings total 24.

Do not spend the points until the rest of the team reveals its missing role.

### Leadoff center fielder

Use:

- 6 Batting
- 10 Running
- 1 Pitching
- 7 Fielding

Choose this build when Pete or another true center-field runner is missing.

It reaches base often enough, creates speed pressure, and has enough fielding
to use the 10 Running rating in the largest outfield area.

### Power corner infielder

Use:

- 10 Batting
- 4 Running
- 1 Pitching
- 9 Fielding

Choose this build when the roster already has speed and pitching but needs
another middle-order hitter.

Use the player at first or third rather than center field.

### Two-way pitcher

Use:

- 7 Batting
- 3 Running
- 10 Pitching
- 4 Fielding

Choose this build when Angela or Kenny is missing and the team needs another
ace without adding a completely empty batting slot.

Do not create this build when Angela, Kenny, Pablo, Luanne, and Amir already
provide enough pitching depth.

### Balanced utility player

Use:

- 6 Batting
- 6 Running
- 6 Pitching
- 6 Fielding

This player can fill several positions and works well for a themed roster or a
first Custom Kid experiment.

The tradeoff is that the build does not outperform an S-tier specialist at any
one job.

<h2 id="alternative-teams">
  Alternative team builds
</h2>

The balanced lineup is the safest general answer, but a different goal can
justify a different roster.

### Power-focused lineup

Start with:

- Pablo Sanchez
- Kiesha Phillips
- Mikey Thomas
- Achmed Khan
- Angela Delvecchio

Then add:

- one 10-rated runner;
- a second elite pitcher;
- and enough fielding to protect shortstop and center field.

Do not draft nine slow power hitters. The extra offense can be lost through
poor outfield coverage and weak baserunning.

### Speed and defense lineup

Start with:

- Pete Wheeler
- Luanne Lui
- Kiesha Phillips
- Stephanie Morgan
- Ernie Steele

Then add Pablo for offense and Angela or Kenny for the primary pitching role.

This build is useful on larger fields, for stolen-base objectives, and for
players who prefer pressure and defense over repeated home-run attempts.

### Sibling-focused lineup

Useful pairings include:

- Achmed and Amir Khan;
- Ashley and Sidney Webber;
- Tony and Angela Delvecchio;
- Sally and Ronnie Dobbs;
- Kenny and Vicki Kawaguchi.

Check the positions before committing.

A themed team can activate useful relationships while still lacking a natural
center fielder, shortstop, or second ace.

<h2 id="common-mistakes">
  Team-building mistakes
</h2>

### Ranking players only by total rating points

The location of the points matters more than the sum.

A 10-rated skill can still be wasted when the player is placed in the wrong
role.

### Drafting only one elite pitcher

Fatigue can force a change within one game and can affect the next League
matchup.

### Filling the batting order before solving defense

Another power hitter does not repair shortstop, third base, catcher, or center
field.

### Treating the Tier List as permanent

Hot and Cold status, boosts, opponent speed, and field dimensions can change
the correct starter for one game.

### Forcing a teammate boost without a position plan

A relationship bonus is not worth creating two duplicated or weak defensive
roles.

### Building a Custom Kid before identifying the missing job

Spend the 20 points after the rest of the roster shows whether the team needs
speed, power, defense, or pitching.

### Replacing every standard player with an MLB pro

The unlocked player should improve a specific role. A famous power hitter does
not automatically repair pitching depth or center-field coverage.

<h2 id="faq">
  Backyard Baseball best players FAQ
</h2>

### Who is the best overall player?

Pablo Sanchez is the safest overall first selection. He has 10 Batting,
10 Running, 9 Pitching, and 9 Fielding.

### Who is the best leadoff hitter?

Pete Wheeler is the best standard leadoff option because he combines
10 Running with 8 Batting and 7 Fielding.

### Who are the best pitchers?

Angela Delvecchio and Kenny Kawaguchi both have 10 Pitching. Pablo Sanchez has
9 Pitching and is the strongest emergency relief option in the recommended
lineup.

### Who is the best shortstop?

Kiesha Phillips is the best offensive shortstop option. Stephanie Morgan is a
stronger defense-first alternative when the lineup already has enough power.

### Does the Tier List include all player stats?

The detailed table includes all S- and A-tier standard Backyard Kids. B- and
C-tier players remain listed without a full stats table to keep the page focused
on competitive team building rather than becoming a complete wiki database.

### Is Maria Luna S Tier with a pink uniform?

The pink condition makes Maria substantially more attractive, but I keep her in
conditional A Tier because the team must be designed around that requirement.

### What is the best Custom Kid build?

Use 6/10/1/7 for a leadoff center fielder, 10/4/1/9 for a power corner
infielder, or 7/3/10/4 for a two-way pitcher.

### Should I add an MLB pro immediately after unlocking one?

Only when the player improves a specific role without removing essential
pitching, fielding, or center-field speed.
