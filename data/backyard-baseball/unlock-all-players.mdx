stat-based unlock.

selectable characters that are not part of the normal starting roster.

<h2 id="easy-unlocks">
  Mr. Clanky, Vinnie the Gooch, and Sunny Day
</h2>

These are the three fastest character unlocks and should be completed before a
long League season.

### How to unlock Mr. Clanky

Open the Clubhouse and look for Mr. Clanky lying on the floor.

Hold `Shift`, then click him.

    his player card elsewhere is not the unlock interaction.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

If nothing happens:

- make sure the pointer is directly over Mr. Clanky;
- keep Shift held before clicking;
- and return to the Clubhouse rather than trying from a player-selection menu.

### How to unlock Vinnie the Gooch

Complete the entire Tutorial.

Do not leave after the first batting section. Finish the baserunning section as
well and return to the Clubhouse so the profile can register the completion.

This is the best first unlock because the Tutorial also teaches:

- selecting runners;
- advancing and retreating;
- sprinting;
- and sliding near the base.

Those same controls are needed for Kenny Lofton.

### How to unlock Sunny Day

Use:

1. Home Run Derby;
2. Parks Department Field #2;
3. the center-field Jumbotron.

A normal hit on the board is not enough.

Use a hitter whose contact you can control.

The most powerful character is not automatically the best choice because a
maximum-distance home run can pass over the target. Square the hitter toward
center and prioritize repeated contact with the board.

<h2 id="challenge-unlocks">
  Amy Bostwick, Julie Dunkel, Jay Green, and Kenny Lofton
</h2>

These four unlocks depend on the game counting a specific result.

An attempt is not always progress.

### How to unlock Amy Bostwick

Complete **15 tag outs**.

A tag out occurs when the fielder holding the ball physically touches the
runner.

A force out does not count.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/backyard-baseball/backyard-baseball-amy-bostwick-tag-out.webp"
    alt="Backyard Baseball 2026 fielder tagging a runner for an out"
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    The fielder must touch the runner with the ball. Merely standing on a force
    base records a different type of out.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

The easiest setup is a rundown:

1. encourage or allow the runner to move between bases;
2. throw ahead of the runner;
3. move the receiving fielder toward them;
4. finish the play with physical contact.

Wiggle Ball is useful because the smaller field and reduced roster create more
direct tag situations.

### How to unlock Julie Dunkel

Break **10 windows or other glass objects**.

Tin Can Alley in Home Run Derby gives repeated access to visible windows, but
the unlock is not limited to Derby. Glass broken during normal games can also
contribute.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/backyard-baseball/backyard-baseball-julie-dunkel-window-break.webp"
    alt="Small broken window pane after a hit at Tin Can Alley in Backyard Baseball 2026"
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    The broken-pane effect is small and easy to miss. Watch the glass itself
    rather than waiting for a large unlock animation after every hit.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

Do not count every ball that reaches the building.

Look for:

- a missing pane;
- a brief crack or shatter;
- or another visible glass reaction.

### How to unlock Jay Green

Win **20 local multiplayer Home Run Derbies**.

The main profile must win.

Use a second controller or local input for the guest player, then keep the
matches short. Do not allow the guest side to win while assuming that simply
finishing 20 Derbies is enough.

This is a win requirement, not a participation requirement.

### How to unlock Kenny Lofton

Complete **five successful stolen bases as a team in one game**.

The five steals can be divided between multiple runners. For example:

- Pete steals two;
- Luanne steals two;
- Kenny steals one.

Use Pickup Game or Quick Play when you want to retry without risking League
standings.

For each attempt:

1. select a fast runner;
2. start the steal before the delivery is complete;
3. hold the sprint input;
4. slide near the destination base;
5. confirm that the runner is called safe.

An `OUT` does not count.

Do not use a screenshot of a failed steal as proof of the unlock route. The
important result is five successful steals before that game ends.

<h2 id="mlb-pros">
  How to unlock all seven MLB pros
</h2>

The current MLB roster is:

- Vladimir Guerrero
- Jason Giambi
- Sammy Sosa
- Chipper Jones
- Kenny Lofton
- Mark McGwire
- Jeff Bagwell

Their conditions do not use one shared system.

### Vladimir Guerrero: pull his card

Earn Tokens, buy a pack, and open a pack containing a Vladimir Guerrero card.

The important event is pulling Vlad's card—not merely opening any random first
pack.

Open packs during normal League progression so the following goals move
together:

- Vladimir Guerrero;
- one-, ten-, and thirty-pack achievements;
- card rarity ownership;
- and the full card collection.

### Jason Giambi: lifetime Token progress

The strongest current route is approximately **2,000 or 2,001 lifetime
Tokens**.

The exact final number is not clean-profile confirmed.

The practical advice is still clear:

- do not save 2,000 Tokens in the current balance;
- do not avoid spending Tokens on packs;
- and do not restart the profile.

The condition appears tied to total Tokens earned over time.

Quick Play, Pickup, Wiggle Ball, Derby, and League can all contribute to profile
earnings, so Jason should be treated as a passive long-term unlock.

### Sammy Sosa: 66 Wiggle Ball home runs

Hit **66 home runs in Wiggle Ball**.

Do not assume Derby or League home runs contribute to this condition.

I combine this route with Amy Bostwick:

- hit for Sammy while batting;
- create tag opportunities for Amy while fielding;
- and continue until the Wiggle Ball-specific total is complete.

Use strong contact before chasing maximum distance. A foul ball or ordinary hit
does not move the home-run count.

### Chipper Jones: reach the League postseason

The strongest current condition is qualifying for the postseason and entering
the first tournament.

This is marked `Strongly supported` because several profile milestones may occur
near the same time during the first League.

Do not start a separate weak team solely to miss the playoffs and test the
negative condition. Continue the main League, qualify normally, and check the
roster after entering the first postseason stage.

### Kenny Lofton: five steals in one game

Kenny's condition is covered in the challenge section above.

The critical details are:

- five total team steals;
- one game;
- successful attempts only;
- and multiple runners may contribute.

### Mark McGwire and Jeff Bagwell

Mark McGwire and Jeff Bagwell are two of the seven unlockable MLB Pros.

The developer has confirmed that the Pro challenges reference milestones from
the players' real MLB careers, including at least one unlock tied to a specific
home-run total. That explains the design of the challenges, but it does not
identify Mark or Jeff's exact threshold.

Mark's famous 70-home-run season makes **70 home runs** a plausible theory, but
there is not enough evidence to claim that Derby, League, or profile-wide home
runs unlock him at that number.

Jeff's career totals also do not prove that the game requires 44 or 45
extra-base hits, 449 home runs, 488 doubles, or four Sluggers packs.

For normal progression:

- continue accumulating home runs and extra-base hits;
- continue the League postseason;
- open Sluggers packs when spending Tokens normally;
- and check the selectable roster after major milestones.

Do not create a separate 70-home-run, 45-extra-base-hit, or four-pack grind
until one of those conditions produces a repeatable unlock.

### Chico Pappas

Complete the full postseason on **Playground Pro** and win the:

**Ultra Grand Championship of the Universe Series**

This also opens the next difficulty needed for the remaining route.

Do not stop after the first playoff or national tournament. Continue through
the final championship and return to the Clubhouse before creating the next
League.

### Leah Wayne

After completing the Playground Pro championship:

1. create a new League on **Backyard Legend**;
2. qualify for the postseason;
3. win the first playoff stage;
4. win the **Super Entire Nation Tournament**;
5. return to the Clubhouse and check the roster.

The current route places Leah at the second postseason victory of the
Backyard Legend season.

Do not confuse reaching the tournament with winning it.

### Star Moonbeam

Use the same Backyard Legend progression, but the League should be configured
for **night games before the season begins**.

Then win the final:

**Ultra Grand Championship of the Universe Series**

Do not wait until the final game to look for a day/night toggle. The time-of-day
condition belongs in League creation, so choosing day can force another full
season if night is required.

### Recommended two-League plan

Use:

**League 1**

- Difficulty: Playground Pro
- Goal: win all postseason tournaments
- Expected route: Chico Pappas and the next difficulty

**League 2**

- Difficulty: Backyard Legend
- Time: Night
- Goal: win the second and final postseason tournaments
- Expected route: Leah Wayne and Star Moonbeam

This plan avoids creating separate Backyard Legend seasons for Leah and Star.

Promotional wording has left room for unlockables that were not revealed

- a visible locked roster slot;
- an in-game achievement or record entry;
- a reproducible interaction;

reference, or legacy character mention does not prove that the character is
playable.

<h2 id="not-working">
  Why a player unlock is not working
</h2>

### Mr. Clanky does not respond

Return to the Clubhouse, hold Shift before clicking, and place the pointer
directly over Mr. Clanky.

The player card is not the unlock screen.

### Sunny Day did not unlock after hitting the Jumbotron

A normal impact is not enough.

Look for the glitch animation and Sunny's appearance. Continue aiming at the
center-field board until that effect occurs.

### Amy Bostwick is still locked

Force outs do not count as tag outs.

The fielder holding the ball must touch the runner. Create rundowns rather than
standing on the destination base.

### Julie Dunkel is still locked

The glass effect can be very small.

Do not assume that every ball hitting a building breaks a window. Watch for the
pane or crack animation and continue until ten confirmed glass objects have
been broken.

### Jay Green did not unlock after 20 Derbies

Check who won.

The main profile needs 20 victories. Guest wins and completed matches without a
main-profile win should not be included in your count.

### Kenny Lofton did not unlock

Check all three parts of the condition:

- five steals;
- all in one game;
- each runner called safe.

The five steals do not need to use one player, but failed attempts do not count.

### Jason Giambi is still locked

Continue on the same profile until the Token total has passed **2,001**, then
return to the Clubhouse and check the selectable roster.

Do not restart the profile when the unlock does not appear at exactly 2,000.
The practical safe target is 2,001, and the unlock may register after the next
game or progression screen.

### Sammy Sosa is still locked

Only count home runs produced in Wiggle Ball.

League, Derby, and Pickup home runs should not be assumed to contribute.

### Chipper Jones is still locked

Finish the regular season, qualify for the playoffs, and enter the first
postseason tournament.

Simply being high enough in the standings before the schedule ends may not be
enough.

### Leah Wayne or Star Moonbeam is still locked

Confirm:

- Backyard Legend was selected at League creation;
- the required tournament was won, not merely reached;
- and the Star Moonbeam League was configured for night games before the
  season began.

### Mark McGwire or Jeff Bagwell is still locked

Do not assume the game is bugged because one launch-week theory failed.

The developer has confirmed that MLB Pro unlocks reference real-career
milestones, but the available evidence does not prove that Mark requires 70
home runs or that Jeff requires a specific extra-base-hit total or four
Sluggers packs.

Continue normal batting, League, Token, and card progression and check the
roster after major milestones instead of repeatedly restarting one speculative
grind.
