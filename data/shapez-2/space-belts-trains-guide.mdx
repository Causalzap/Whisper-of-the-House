<GuideQuickAnswer title="Quick Answer">
**Use Space Belts for short- to mid-distance high-frequency platform logistics. Use trains for long-distance bulk transport.**

A practical rule is:

- **Space Belts**: nearby platforms, continuous flow, several small or medium routes, easy visual debugging
- **Trains**: very long distances, bulk raw shapes, bulk paint, repeated single-resource imports, cleaner map-wide logistics
- **Train Launcher, unlocked late game around Milestone 10**: launches trains directly into the Vortex, eliminating the return trip and changing final-delivery throughput math

For Space Belt capacity, use the in-game tooltip. Community explanations often describe the layers, lanes, slots, and belt-equivalent capacity differently, so this guide treats the tooltip as the source of truth.

Most Space Belt problems are not input problems. They are **output extraction problems**. Most train problems are not rail problems. They are **unloader and downstream belt problems**.
</GuideQuickAnswer>

<h2 id="what-are-space-belts">
  What are Space Belts in Shapez 2?
</h2>

Space Belts are the main early way to move shapes between platforms. They let you take output from one platform, launch it into space, and catch it on another platform.

They unlock early because the game quickly asks you to move beyond a single platform. Once your factory spreads out, Space Belts become the default tool for nearby logistics.

<figure className="my-8">
  <Image
    src="/images/shapez-2/space-belt-basic-connection.webp"
    alt="Shapez 2 Space Belt basic connection between platforms using launcher and catcher."
    width={1600}
    height={900}
    className="rounded-xl"
  />
  <figcaption className="mt-3 text-sm text-slate-400">
    A Space Belt is a platform-to-platform bridge, not a magic infinite-throughput pipe.
  </figcaption>
</figure>

A good Space Belt setup should make three things obvious:

1. what enters the launcher
2. which layer and lane carries it
3. how the catcher extracts it on the receiving platform

If you cannot see those three things, the belt will be painful to debug later.

One extra behavior is worth testing before you rely on it: **Space Belt branches can distribute shapes across each branch**. This can be useful for balancing, but verify the split visually before using it in a critical production chain.

<h2 id="extract-full-space-belt-throughput">
  How to extract full throughput from a Space Belt
</h2>

To extract full throughput, design the catcher side from the tooltip value backward: whatever the Space Belt can deliver must also be extracted and consumed.

<GuideTable
  caption="How to extract full Space Belt throughput in Shapez 2."
  columns={extractionColumns}
  rows={extractionRows}

The important mindset is:

```text
tooltip capacity
must match
catcher output capacity
must match
downstream machine capacity
```

If any one of those is lower than the others, that is the real bottleneck.

<h2 id="what-are-trains">
  What are Trains in Shapez 2?
</h2>

Trains are long-distance logistics tools. They are not just faster belts. They are moving buffers that can carry large amounts of cargo between distant areas.

They usually unlock around the point where your factory has grown beyond comfortable Space Belt routes.

<h2 id="train-loading-unloading">
  Train loading and unloading bottlenecks
</h2>

Train capacity is impressive, but capacity is not the same as throughput.

A wagon can carry a large amount, but the route is only as strong as the loader input, train cycle time, unloader output, and consuming factory.

For mixed-cargo problems, use the troubleshooting table below; the safest first train route is still one clean cargo type per loader.

<h2 id="best-cargo-for-trains">
  What should trains carry?
</h2>

The best early train cargo is **bulk raw shapes** or **bulk paint**.

These are good because they are:

- high volume
- easy to identify
- useful in many places
- relatively safe to buffer
- easier to flush or reroute than complex outputs

Avoid using trains early for fragile mixed complex shapes, operator shapes, or high-layer outputs unless you already have a clean system. If the wrong complex shape enters a wagon, cleanup becomes annoying and error-prone.

<GuideCallout title="Train cargo rule">
Ask: **Would I be comfortable filling a giant buffer with this item?**

If the answer is no, keep it on a more visible Space Belt route until your cleanup plan is reliable.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="space-belts-and-trains-in-manufacture-mode">
  Space Belts and Trains in Manufacture Mode
</h2>

Manufacture Mode gives Space Belts and trains different jobs.

Use **Space Belts** inside the Trade Station chain: waste processing, nearby station inputs, refined-shape handoffs, and short platform-to-platform routing where continuous flow and visual debugging matter.

Use **trains** for upstream supply: distant raw-shape sources, bulk paint, repeated imports into MAM-style modules, and long routes that would otherwise consume too much platform capacity.

That split matters because Trade Stations need continuous upstream supply. If a distant raw input starves the first station, every later refined station can starve as well. Keep the station chain local and readable, then bring bulk resources to it with trains when distance becomes the bottleneck.

For the full 1.0 progression path, read the <Link href="/shapez-2/manufacture-mode-guide">Manufacture Mode Guide</Link>. For station-specific routing, read the <Link href="/shapez-2/trade-station-guide">Trade Station Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### When do Space Belts unlock in Shapez 2?

Space Belts unlock early, around Milestone 2-3, when you start expanding beyond the initial platform and need to connect platforms across space.

### When do trains unlock in Shapez 2?

Trains usually appear in the mid- to late game, around Milestone 7, when your factory needs long-distance bulk transport.

### How much can a Space Belt carry?

Check the Space Belt launcher / catcher tooltip in your save. Space Belt capacity is best planned from the tooltip because layers, lanes, floor access, and global speed upgrades all affect the practical output you can extract.

### Why is my Space Belt clogged?

The receiving side is probably too slow. Check catchers, output belts, machine count, storage, trash, and downstream capacity before changing the input.

### Why is my Space Belt only partially used?

You may only be extracting from part of the Space Belt. Read the launcher / catcher tooltip, then use belt lifts or ramp pieces to access the relevant floors and lanes before merging output.

### How do I match Space Belt output to downstream machines?

Confirm the belt-equivalent output in the tooltip, give each extracted layer enough output belts, and build enough downstream machines to consume that confirmed capacity.

### Should I use Space Belts or trains?

Use Space Belts for short- to mid-distance continuous flow. Use trains for long-distance bulk raw shapes, bulk paint, or repeated single-resource routes.

### What should trains carry?

Trains are best for bulk raw shapes and bulk paint. Avoid mixed complex shapes until you have a strong reason and a clean cleanup plan.

### What is Train Launcher / Vortex delivery?

Train Launcher or Vortex delivery is a late-game train upgrade that sends trains directly into the Vortex with their cargo. Use it for final delivery after the unlock because it removes the normal return-trip loop.

### Why did my train cargo disappear while loading?

Different shapes entered the same loader floor or package. Keep each loader input clean, or use separate loaders / wagons for different cargo types.

### Why is my train stuck at the unloader?

The unloader output or downstream belt is probably full. Clear the receiving side before adding more trains.

### Are Train Loaders and Unloaders the main train buildings?

Yes, they are the core cargo buildings for normal rail logistics. Depending on unlocks, you may also use train stops, wait stops, wagons, depots, rails, routing pieces, and late-game Train Launcher / Vortex delivery.
