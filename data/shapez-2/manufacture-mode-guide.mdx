<h2 id="what-is-manufacture-mode">
  What is Manufacture Mode in Shapez 2?
</h2>

Manufacture Mode is a 1.0 mode where the Vortex is no longer the simple central delivery target from Classic Mode. The Vortex has imploded, and your job is to process damaged shapes into refined shapes, trade them upward, and rebuild toward the Grand Vortex Assembler.

The important difference is permanence. In Classic Mode, many milestone factories can be deleted after their quota is complete. In Manufacture Mode, earlier production often feeds later station tiers, so deleting an old block can break the chain several steps later.

For station ratios, rocket issues, alternate recipes, and refined-shape troubleshooting, read the <Link href="/shapez-2/trade-station-guide">Shapez 2 Trade Station Guide</Link>.

<h2 id="trade-stations-in-manufacture-mode">
  How Trade Stations fit into Manufacture Mode
</h2>

Trade Stations turn exact input shapes into refined shapes used by later tiers.

The key difference from Classic Mode is that earlier stations often stay useful. A first real Trade Station may feed a second station, which feeds a third, which eventually feeds the final assembler. That makes station layout, Space Belt routing, and refined-output separation more important than compactness.

<h2 id="donation-station-and-research-station">
  What do Donation Stations and Research Stations do in Manufacture Mode?
</h2>

Donation Stations and Research Stations are the research layer of Manufacture Mode.

A **Donation Station** accepts shapes and turns production into operator progress and research points. This makes it the bridge between factory output and alternate recipe unlocks.

A **Research Station** uses refined inputs for research progress and can provide useful output or byproducts that help scale earlier production.

<h2 id="faq">
  FAQ
</h2>

### What is Manufacture Mode in Shapez 2?

Manufacture Mode is a 1.0 progression mode where you process Dimensional Waste, create refined shapes, feed Trade Stations, use research infrastructure, unlock alternate recipes, and work toward the Grand Vortex Assembler.

### Should I play Classic Mode before Manufacture Mode?

Yes, if you are new. Manufacture Mode assumes you already understand basic Shapez 2 production, belts, platforms, Space Belts, trains, paint, crystals, and throughput.

### What should I do first in Manufacture Mode?

Start with the Dimensional Waste Receiver, prepare the waste variant required by the first Polishing Station, run one Polishing Station, then scale the starter polishing block before connecting later Trade Station tiers.

### What does Dimensional Waste look like?

Dimensional Waste behaves like a shape stream, not a fluid; check the Polishing Station preview because the required waste variant may be different from the raw receiver output.

### Does the Polishing Station accept any waste shape?

No. Feed the specific waste variant shown in the station tooltip or input preview. If the station is idle, check shape mismatch before assuming it is a throughput problem.

### How many Polishing Stations do I need?

Start with one to prove the route, then scale. Early guidance points toward at least 8 Polishing Stations when the first refined-output step is too slow.

### What do Trade Stations do in Manufacture Mode?

Trade Stations consume exact input shapes and output refined shapes that feed later Trade Station tiers, research loops, rockets, or the Grand Vortex Assembler.

### What do Donation Stations and Research Stations do?

Donation Stations generate operator progress and research points. Research Stations use refined inputs for research progress and can provide useful outputs or byproducts that help scale earlier production.

### What are alternate recipes?

Alternate recipes are research-unlocked Trade Station routes with different inputs for same-tier refined output variants. Use them when they simplify routing or input supply.

### What is the Grand Vortex Assembler?

The Grand Vortex Assembler is the final destination of the refined-shape chain. It consumes high-tier refined outputs and turns the Manufacture Mode supply chain into the final objective.

### Why is my Manufacture Mode factory stuck?
