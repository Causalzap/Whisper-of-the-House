<h2 id="what-players-are-talking-about-right-now">

</h2>

The Retro Rewind community keeps circling around the same few topics.

### 1. Roadmap features
Players are closely following upcoming features such as:
- backroom storage
- VHS repair systems
- controller support
- game rental features

### 2. Custom videos
This is one of the biggest advanced-use topics in the community. Players regularly discuss:
- channel files
- encoding settings
- format problems
- converter tools
- what actually causes playback to fail

### 3. Save files and Steam Cloud
Save safety matters a lot in this game, especially while the community is still discovering edge cases and early-version issues.

### 4. Black Market questions
Many players want to know:
- how often the dealer appears
- whether bootleg stock is worth it
- whether risky inventory can create other store problems

### 5. Store layout and identity
A lot of player conversation is not about pure profit. It is about:
- how stores look
- how shelves are themed
- how people organize genres
- how they build a memorable video-store vibe

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Does Retro Rewind have an official Discord?

Yes. Retro Rewind has an official Discord, and it is one of the best places for updates, bug help, and custom-content discussion.

### Where should I report bugs?

The best places are:
- the Discord channels `faq` and `game-feedback-and-bugs`
- Steam Discussions, if you want a searchable public thread

### Where do players share custom videos, mods, and content?

Discord is the best starting point, especially the `modding` and `share-your-content` channels.

### Where do players share store screenshots and layouts?

Discord is useful for this, especially `share-your-store`. Reddit is also a good place for player showcases.

### Is there an official Retro Rewind wiki?

At the moment, the most useful public information is still spread across Discord, Steam Discussions, guides, and community pages rather than one clearly dominant official wiki.

### What is the best community link for a new player?

The official Discord is the best first stop. After that, Steam Discussions is the best place for threads you may want to revisit later.
