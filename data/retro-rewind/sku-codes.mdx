<h2 id="what-are-sku-codes-in-retro-rewind">
What are SKU codes in Retro Rewind
</h2>

SKU codes are unique IDs attached to every VHS tape in the game.

Normally, inventory is semi-random. But when you use a SKU code in the Black Market, you can order a **specific movie directly**, bypassing randomness.

This turns the Black Market into a **precision tool** instead of a gamble.

<h2 id="how-to-find-sku-codes-in-retro-rewind">
How to find SKU codes
</h2>

![Retro Rewind SKU code example showing VHS inspection screen](/images/retro-rewind/sku-inspect.webp)

Press **F** while holding a VHS tape to view its SKU code.

Finding a SKU code is simple:

- Pick up a VHS tape
- Press **F** to inspect it
- Look at the info panel on the left

You will see:
- movie title
- genre
- rating
- profit level
- **SKU code**

<GuideCallout title="Important">
You do not need to collect every SKU code.

In most cases, you only need codes for **high-performing tapes you actually want to replace**.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-to-use-sku-codes-in-the-black-market">
How to use SKU codes in the Black Market
</h2>

![Retro Rewind Black Market SKU search screen](/images/retro-rewind/black-market-sku.webp)

Enter the SKU code in the Black Market tab on your store computer.

To use a SKU code:

1. Open your in-game computer
2. Go to the **Black Market**
3. Enter the SKU code
4. Order the tape

This lets you instantly restock a specific title instead of waiting for random inventory.

<h2 id="when-sku-codes-are-actually-worth-using">
When SKU codes are actually worth using
</h2>

SKU codes are most useful in a few specific situations:

- a high-profit tape breaks
- a top shelf loses its best performer
- you need to fix a strong demand category quickly

In these cases, exact replacement is worth the extra cost.

---

<h2 id="when-you-should-not-use-sku-codes">
When you should NOT use SKU codes
</h2>

Most players overuse SKU codes — and lose money because of it.

You should NOT use SKU codes when:

- you are trying to grow inventory
- you are replacing low-profit tapes
- you are chasing “perfect shelves”
- you are early in the game

In most cases, **random stock is more efficient than exact replacement**.

<GuideCallout title="The biggest mistake">
SKU codes feel powerful, but they are not a growth system.

They are a **repair tool**.
</GuideCallout>

<h2 id="how-to-decide-if-a-tape-should-be-replaced-exactly">
How to decide if a tape should be replaced exactly
</h2>

Before using a SKU code, ask:

- Is this tape a top earner?
- Is it driving demand on this shelf?
- Will replacing it immediately recover revenue?

If yes → use SKU  
If not → let it go

This is the difference between efficient stores and struggling ones.

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Do I need a full SKU list?

No. Most players only need a few high-performing tapes.

### Are SKU codes worth it early game?

Usually not. They are better used later as a precision tool.

### Is the Black Market the best way to get tapes?

Not always. It is best used for **targeted replacement**, not general growth.

### What is the biggest mistake with SKU codes?

Using them too often. Most players lose money by overusing exact replacement.

### How do I check a SKU code in Retro Rewind?

Pick up a VHS tape and press **F** to inspect it. The item panel will show the tape's SKU code.
