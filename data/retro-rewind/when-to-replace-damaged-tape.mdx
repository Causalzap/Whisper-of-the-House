<h2 id="why-damaged-tapes-feel-so-punishing">Why damaged tapes feel so punishing</h2>

In Retro Rewind, damaged tapes are one of the most punishing systems in the game.

Players consistently run into three problems:

- The **broken fee is too low** and only covers a small part of the replacement cost  
- The fee feels **flat** and does not scale with expensive or rare tapes  
- Replacing a tape can **consume your daily order capacity**, slowing your entire store

<GuideCallout title="Player Insight">
“The broken fee feels entirely flat—it doesn’t scale at all with those expensive titles.”
</GuideCallout>

<GuideCallout title="Player Insight">
“It’s not just the money — replacing it eats into my daily order limit.”
</GuideCallout>

Every damaged tape is not just a loss — it is a **decision problem**.

---

<h2 id="why-replacing-the-exact-title-is-often-the-wrong-move">Why replacing the exact title is often the wrong move</h2>

Most players try to replace the exact same tape.

That is usually inefficient.

A damaged slot is not about the title — it is about what that shelf can earn.

<GuideCallout title="Core Strategy">
Don't get stuck chasing an exact replacement.  
A dead slot is a dead slot — fill it with what earns the most.
</GuideCallout>

If the original tape was weak, replacing it often just recreates the same problem.

---

<h2 id="replace-revenue-not-the-title">Replace revenue, not the title</h2>

This is the key concept that separates beginner play from strong store management.

Instead of asking:

> “Should I replace this exact movie?”

Ask:

> “What should I put here to recover the lost revenue?”

There are three approaches:

- Replace the **exact title**
- Replace with a **same-genre alternative**
- Replace the **revenue entirely** with a better-performing tape

<h2 id="when-to-replace-immediately">When to replace immediately</h2>

Replace immediately when:

- it is a **New Release**
- it has **high rental frequency**
- it belongs to a **top-demand genre**
- a **busy period is coming (especially rainy Friday)**

> Rainy Friday nights are extremely busy and can massively increase rentals.

<GuideCallout title="Player Experience">
“One blockbuster missing right before a rainy Friday weekend completely wrecked my profits.”
</GuideCallout>

---

<h2 id="when-to-wait-or-skip-replacement">When to wait or skip replacement</h2>

Skip or delay replacement when:

- the tape had **low demand**
- it was **filler stock**
- early-game **cash is tight**
- it wastes a **daily order slot**
- a better-performing alternative exists

<h2 id="when-this-advice-does-not-apply">When this advice does not apply</h2>

### Completion play
You may replace more aggressively to finish collections.

### Late game
Cash and order limits matter less.

### Critical shelves
High-demand categories may justify immediate replacement.

---

<h2 id="final-verdict-by-player-type">Final verdict by player type</h2>

- **Early game:** Don’t replace everything  
- **Profit-focused:** Replace only strong performers  
- **Advanced play:** Think in revenue slots  
- **Completionist:** Replace aggressively

<h2 id="faq">FAQ</h2>

### Do broken tape fees cover the replacement cost?
Usually not. They only cover a small portion.

### Should you replace every damaged tape?
No. Many are not worth replacing.

### Is it worth replacing a New Release before a rainy Friday?
Yes. That is one of the strongest cases.

### Replace exact tape or buy a better one?
Often better to replace the revenue.

### Should early-game players replace tapes?
Only selectively.

### Does replacing a tape use order capacity?
Yes. It can limit future orders.
